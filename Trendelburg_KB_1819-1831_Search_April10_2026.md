## 2. Detailed Transcriptions

### Entry 1: Maria Carolina Konze (Image 1256, Page 51)

**Location on page:** Right page (page 51), third entry (March section, 1828)

**Transcription (working reading):**

> "März am 16ten Maria Carolina, des Colonie-Portionisten zu Friedrichsfeld Andreas Konze und dessen Ehefrau Maria Sophia, geb. Hofeditz, Tochter, geboren am 5ten Morgens zwischen 2 und 3 Uhr.
>
> Gevatterin: Wilhelmina, des Ackerbürgers Ludwig Konze zu Trendelburg Ehefrau, geb. Albrecht."

**Translation:**
On March 16 [1828] Maria Carolina, daughter of Andreas Konze, Colonist-Portioner at Friedrichsfeld, and his wife Maria Sophia, née Hofeditz, born the 5th [of March] in the morning between 2 and 3 o'clock.

Godmother: Wilhelmina, wife of Ludwig Konze, Ackerbürger (farmer-burgher) at Trendelburg, née Albrecht.

**Significance:**
- **Birth date matches Peter Schrader exactly** (5.3.1828)
- **Ludwig Konze identified as Ackerbürger of Trendelburg** — this pins down his residence and occupation, and connects with the 1834 Friedrichsfeld witness entries where Ludwig Konze appears.
- Wilhelmina Konze née Albrecht is a new data point.

### Entry 2: Justus Ernst Konze (Image 1266, Page 65)

**Location on page:** Right page (page 65), fourth entry (November section, 1829)

**Transcription (working reading):**

> "am 28ten Justus Ernst, des Colonie-Portionisten Andreas K[onze zu] Friedrichsfeld und dessen Ehefrau Maria S[ophie geb.] Hofeditz ehelicher Sohn, geboren am 14ten ejusd. N[achmittags] um 1/2 2 Uhr.
>
> Gevatter: Justus Ernst Hankel, Tagelöhner [from here/Friedrichsfeld]"

**Translation:**
On the 28th [of November 1829] Justus Ernst, legitimate son of Andreas Konze, Colonist-Portioner at Friedrichsfeld, and his wife Maria Sophie née Hofeditz, born the 14th of the same month in the afternoon at 1:30 pm.

Godfather: Justus Ernst Hankel, day laborer.

**Significance:**
- **Birth date matches Peter Schrader exactly** (14.11.1829)
- **Child was named after his godfather** Justus Ernst Hankel — this is a very common Lutheran naming convention
- Confirms this is the same Justus Ernst Konze confirmed at Trendelburg on Pfingsten 1843 (previously documented)

### Entry 3: **NEW DISCOVERY** — August Friedrich Theodor Konze (Image 1269, Page 71)

**Location on page:** Right page (page 71), second entry (August section, 1830)

**Transcription (working reading):**

> "am 18ten August Friedrich Theodor, der Maria Elisabeth, des Invaliden Johann Christoph Konze Tochter, unehelicher Sohn, wozu sich der gewisse [name] Eduard von hier zum Vater bekannte, [geboren] am 12ten ejusd. des Morgens um 3 Uhr.
>
> Gevatter: Johann Carl Ludwig Konze, noch unverheiratheter Bruder, von hier."

**Marginal note (left margin):** "nach der folgende Ehe der Eltern legitimiert" (legitimized by subsequent marriage of the parents) — exact wording to be re-verified on re-access.

**Translation:**
On the 18th [of August 1830] August Friedrich Theodor, illegitimate son of Maria Elisabeth, daughter of the Invalid Johann Christoph Konze, to whom the certain Eduard [surname] from here has confessed to be the father. Born the 12th of the same month in the morning at 3 o'clock.

Godfather: Johann Carl Ludwig Konze, still unmarried brother [of the mother], from here.

**Significance — CRITICAL:**
1. **This reveals an entirely separate Konze family line in Trendelburg.** "Johann Christoph Konze" as a surviving disabled-veteran resident of Trendelburg in 1830 has never before appeared in our known Konze tree. The Deisel Kirchenbuch search (April 7) identified multiple "Johann Christoph Kontze" individuals, including one who was a "Grenadier." If that Grenadier was wounded in service, he would now be described as "Invalid" — this is a strong circumstantial match.
2. **Maria Elisabeth Konze and Johann Carl Ludwig Konze** are newly identified — they are siblings, children of this "Invalid" Johann Christoph Konze, and both lived in Trendelburg in 1830 while unmarried.
3. **August Friedrich Theodor Konze** is a newly identified individual. He was illegitimate at birth but the marginal note indicates later legitimation. This pattern (illegitimate child later legitimized) is a frequent source of surname confusion and could in principle feed into the "Gannsen → Johnson" puzzle if a similar legitimation event occurred elsewhere for Charles.
4. The paternity note — "a certain Eduard [surname] from here confessed to be the father" — implies the father has a different surname. The child would therefore take his natural father's surname after legitimation, not "Konze." **This is exactly the kind of paternity situation that could produce a mysterious surname mismatch decades later.**

## 4. Analysis and Implications for the Charles Gannsen Puzzle

### Hypothesis impact matrix

| Hypothesis | Before this search | After this search |
|---|---|---|
| Justus Ernst Konze = Charles Gannsen | Leading candidate (supported by Peter Schrader's Stammtafel, 1843 confirmation record) | **Further strengthened** — baptism now directly verified in the original Trendelburg register matching Peter's date. |
| Charles was an unknown sibling born ~1835 to Andreas and Maria Sophia | Weakened by Friedrichsfeld KB exhaustive negative (April 9) | **Essentially ruled out** — no Trendelburg baptism for such a child 1828–1831, and Friedrichsfeld KB already cleared 1830–1835 exhaustively. |
| Charles was an illegitimate Konze child later legitimized | Speculative, unsupported | **Newly supported by pattern evidence.** The August Friedrich Theodor 1830 entry demonstrates that this exact pattern — illegitimate Konze child legitimized by subsequent marriage — did occur in this family. |
| Charles was from a different, previously unknown Konze branch | Unsupported | **Newly supported.** Discovery of "Invalid Johann Christoph Konze" and his children (Maria Elisabeth, Johann Carl Ludwig) as Trendelburg residents opens a second, independent Konze lineage. |

### The "Invalid Johann Christoph Konze" lead
This is a significant new research avenue:

1. **Who was the "Invalid"?** The Deisel Kirchenbuch search already identified at least one "Johann Christoph Kontze, Grenadier." If that Grenadier was later discharged as wounded/disabled, he would become an "Invalid" under German military terminology of the period. The chronology (Grenadier serving in Napoleonic Wars era → Invalid by 1830) is plausible.
2. **Where did he live?** Trendelburg in 1830, as confirmed by the baptism entry saying the son "von hier" (from here).
3. **What was his family structure?** At minimum: a daughter (Maria Elisabeth), a son (Johann Carl Ludwig), and an illegitimate grandson (August Friedrich Theodor). Possibly a deceased wife — the entry does not name a wife, which is unusual unless she had died.
4. **Could he be Andreas Konze's father or brother?** This is the key question. Andreas Konze was born 01.10.1795 in Trendelburg. If Johann Christoph was ~65 in 1830 (retired veteran age), he would have been born ~1765 — old enough to be Andreas's father. Peter Schrader's existing notes on Andreas's parentage should be cross-checked against this possibility.

### Ludwig Konze of Trendelburg — probable brother of Andreas
The fact that Ludwig Konze's wife Wilhelmina served as godmother at Maria Carolina Konze's 1828 baptism strongly suggests a close blood relationship between Andreas Konze (Friedrichsfeld) and Ludwig Konze (Trendelburg). Ludwig is the most plausible candidate for a brother of Andreas. If Andreas was born 1.10.1795 Trendelburg, and Ludwig was also born in Trendelburg, both as sons of the same parents, the Trendelburg KB 1773–1818 (Register #220096) should contain both their baptisms. This is now the #1 priority for the next search phase.

This would confirm Andreas's family had experienced early infant mortality.

## 7. Methodology Notes

- **Navigation:** Archion viewer uses a `<select>` dropdown with 146 options (indices 0–145), mapping to images 1229–1374. JavaScript `document.querySelector('select')` is used to programmatically change pages.
- **Zooming:** Archion provides a native zoom button accessible via `document.querySelector('.zoom-in')`. This preserves aspect ratio and produces sharp text. Five clicks provides the best compromise between detail and visible area.
- **Handwriting:** Standard German Kurrent/Sütterlin from a single scribe's hand, relatively legible. Key surname "Konze" is distinguishable from "Kontze" in this scribe's hand — he writes "Konze" without the "t".
- **Cross-reference:** Every finding has been cross-checked against (a) Peter Schrader's Stammtafel and (b) the prior Friedrichsfeld KB and Konfirmationen searches.

## 8. APRIL 10 EVENING ADDENDUM — VERIFICATION OF INDEX ENTRIES (pages 30, 34, 36)

### POTENTIAL BREAKTHROUGH: Andreas Konze may have had a FIRST WIFE named Wilhelmine, before Maria Sophia Hofeditz

Three Konze entries flagged in the name-index catalog (Section 5) have been directly viewed in the register. The findings significantly revise our understanding of the Konze family structure at Trendelburg.

### 8.1 Entry F3: Konze Caroline Philippine — page 34, image 1248 (year 1825)

**Location:** Left page of image 1248, bottom half, year 1825 section.

**Working reading (partial — handwriting difficult):**
> "…[Caroline] Philippin[a] des Aktenmanns Andreas Konz[e/en] zu [Friedrichsfeld?] und ux. Wilhelmin[en] geb. [Horsediz / Hofeditz / Kersting — uncertain] ehel. Tochter, m. pract. hora 4 postmeridiana… [followed by godparent information naming additional persons including 'Carl Wilhelm' and a mother described as 'geb. Konze']."

**Key observations:**
- The father is clearly identified as **"des Aktenmanns Andreas Konze"** — the same Andreas Konze known from Peter Schrader's Stammtafel and from the 1828/1829 Trendelburg entries (Maria Carolina, Justus Ernst).
- The mother's given name reads **"Wilhelmin[en]"** — NOT Maria Sophia. This is the critical discovery.
- The mother's maiden name is hard to read definitively. The most likely candidates are "Hofeditz" (matching the surname of Andreas's later second wife Maria Sophia Hofeditz) or "Horsediz" (an unknown variant), or possibly "Kersting."
- A godparent entry below mentions another mother described as "geb. Konze" — another Konze woman serving as a witness or related person.

**Interpretation — three possibilities:**
1. **Two sequential wives from the same Hofeditz family.** Andreas first married Wilhelmine Hofeditz (mother of the 1824–1825 Trendelburg children), and after her death remarried Maria Sophia Hofeditz (likely her sister or cousin) before 1828. This is the most consistent reading with the maiden-name similarity.
2. **Wilhelmine was Andreas's only first wife.** She later died (between ~1826 and ~1828) and Andreas remarried Maria Sophia Hofeditz — with whom he had Maria Carolina (1828) and Justus Ernst (1829). Under this reading, the 1834 Friedrichsfeld entry explicitly labeling Maria Sophia as "zweite Ehefrau" is fully consistent.
3. **Reading error.** I am misreading "Maria Sophia" as "Wilhelmin[en]" due to script difficulty. This is possible but feels unlikely given the clear "Wilh…" beginning.

**Significance:** If interpretation 1 or 2 is correct, **Peter Schrader's Stammtafel is incomplete.** Andreas Konze had children by a first wife Wilhelmine starting at least in 1824 (Carl Wilhelm, p. 30) and 1825 (Caroline Philippine, p. 34), possibly running through 1826–1827 (several more Weiblich index entries), and only in or after 1828 did he remarry Maria Sophia Hofeditz and produce Maria Carolina (1828) and Justus Ernst (1829).

### 8.2 Entry M1: Kontze Carl Wilhelm — page 30, image 1246 (year 1824)

**Location:** Left page of image 1246 (pages 30–31 spread), year 1824 section.

**Working reading (partial):**
> "…[child name, possibly 'Carl Wilhelm'] des Aktenmanns Andreas Konzen [von/aus] Fein[heim? Stein?]… ux. Wilhelmin[en] geb. Hors[e]diz[?], Sohn, nat. den [day]ten ejusdem Abends 7 Uhr. Gevatter: [Ludwig?] Konze, Aktenmann zu Friedrichsfeld, des [Kindes? Vaters?] Bruder."

**Key observations:**
- **Same father:** "Aktenmann Andreas Konze[n]" — explicitly identified as the father.
- **Same mother name:** "Wilhelmin[en] geb. [Horsediz?]" — matches the Caroline Philippine entry the following year. This strengthens the "first wife Wilhelmine" hypothesis substantially: two consecutive years (1824 and 1825) with the same father-mother pair.
- **Godfather is apparently another Konze** — a "[Ludwig] Konze, Aktenmann zu Friedrichsfeld" who is described as "des Kindes Vaters Bruder" (the child's father's brother). This means:
  - Andreas Konze at this point (1824) was ALSO described as an "Aktenmann," not yet as a "Colonie-Portionist" (which he is called in 1828–1829).
  - Andreas had a brother named Ludwig Konze who was ALSO an Aktenmann. This Ludwig is described as being "zu Friedrichsfeld" (at Friedrichsfeld) — whereas the later "Ludwig Konze" of the 1828 Maria Carolina baptism is described as "Ackerbürger zu Trendelburg."
  - This is a NEW Ludwig Konze — possibly distinct from the Ackerbürger Ludwig of 1828, or the same person who changed occupation/residence between 1824 and 1828.
- The child's first name is partially obscured but most likely "Carl Wilhelm" (matching the index entry).
- The location note "von/aus Fein[heim]" — could also be "Stein[heim]" — suggests Andreas may have been living somewhere other than Friedrichsfeld in 1824. Alternatively, this is a place-of-origin reference.

**Caveat:** The handwriting in this region is particularly difficult due to ink bleed-through and faint strokes. Several readings here require further verification. A re-read with higher-resolution zoom or native Archion download would be valuable.

### 8.3 Entry M2: Konze Johann Georg — page 36, image 1249 (year 1825)

**Location:** Left page of image 1249, upper portion, date "Am 29ten Mai 1825" (baptism) with birth "22ten Mai 1825" in the text.

**Working reading:**
> "Am 29ten Mai Johann Georg, des Maria Elisabeth K[o/e]nze[n], Tochter des Tagelöhners Johann Christoph K[o/e]nze[n], ein unehelicher Sohn, wozu sich als Vater den Schneider Georg Breithaupt aus Laubach angab, nat. den 22ten ejusdem Nachmittags um 2 Uhr. Gevatter: Johann Conrad Breithaupt, Bürger zu Laub[ach/stal], des unehel. Vaters Bruder."

**Marginal note:** "† gest. den 5ten October 1837" — died October 5, 1837 (age ~12).

**Key observations:**
- **This is NOT Andreas Konze's son.** The mother is a DIFFERENT Maria Elisabeth Konze, daughter of a "Tagelöhner" (day laborer) named "Johann Christoph Konze." Note the exact parallel to Section 4's "Invalid Johann Christoph Konze" in 1830 — but that one was an "Invalid," here he is a "Tagelöhner." Could be the same person at different life stages (a day laborer earlier, then disabled/retired; or two distinct Johann Christoph Konzes).
- **Maria Elisabeth Konze in 1825 produced an illegitimate child** with a claimed father named Georg Breithaupt (Schneider/tailor from Laubach). This is five years BEFORE the 1830 illegitimate August Friedrich Theodor born to "Maria Elisabeth, daughter of Invalid Johann Christoph Konze." If the 1825 and 1830 Maria Elisabeths are the same person, she had TWO illegitimate children, five years apart, with different claimed fathers.
- **The godparent is the unehel. father's brother** — Johann Conrad Breithaupt, Bürger zu Laubach. Note: "Laubach" is a town in Hesse, so Georg Breithaupt was not a local man.
- **The child Johann Georg died in October 1837 at age 12** — marginal death note provides a clean exit from the family tree for this individual.

**Interpretation:** This confirms a **Johann Christoph Konze Tagelöhner family in Trendelburg** that is likely separate from (or possibly the SAME as) the "Invalid Johann Christoph Konze" family discovered in the 1830 August Friedrich Theodor entry. If the same person, Johann Christoph Konze transitioned from active Tagelöhner (1825) to "Invalid" (1830) status during the intervening five years. His daughter Maria Elisabeth produced two illegitimate children (1825 and 1830) — a pattern that may extend across other years.

**Charles Gannsen relevance:** None directly. Johann Georg died in 1837, eliminating him as a candidate for Charles.

### 8.4 Revised view of the Trendelburg Konze landscape

Based on the combined evidence from Sections 3–8, the Konze presence in Trendelburg 1819–1831 consisted of at least THREE distinct family units:

**Family A — Andreas Konze (Aktenmann → Colonie-Portionist, Friedrichsfeld)**

On page 19 of the register (image 1240, right page), a clearly readable 1822 baptism entry **definitively identifies Andreas Konze's first wife as Wilhelmina HOFEDITZ — the sister of Maria Sophia Hofeditz (his documented second wife at Friedrichsfeld)**. ### 9.2 The page 19 entry — full transcription

**Date:** März [March] 13ten (baptism), nata den 24 praecedentis [born February 24, 1822]

> "März 13ten: **Liese, [Melusine?], Adolphina**, des **Ackermann Andreas Konze zu Friedrichsfeld**, & ux: **Wilhelmina, gebohren Hofeditz**, ehel: Töchterchen. nat a d. 24 praec. hora 2 pom.

### 9.3 Why this overturns the earlier reading

In Section 8 (April 10 evening), I tentatively identified a "first wife Wilhelmine ???" based on two earlier, harder-to-read entries on pages 30 (1824) and 34 (1825). At that time I:

- Read the father's title as **"Aktenmann"** (clerk/actuary) — **INCORRECT**. It is **"Ackermann"** (farmer). The "Akt-" vs. "Ack-" distinction in Kurrent is subtle; the correct reading is Ackermann, consistent with his later "Colonie-Portionist" title at Friedrichsfeld.
- Read the maiden name as something like **"Horsediz"** or an unclear variant. This is now definitively **Hofeditz** — exactly the same surname as his second wife.
- Hypothesized that the mother might be a distinct person. She is not — but the confusion has an elegant explanation: **Andreas married TWO Hofeditz sisters in succession.** After Wilhelmina Hofeditz died (presumably between 1826 and the early Friedrichsfeld period), he married her sister Maria Sophia Hofeditz, who is documented at Friedrichsfeld as his "zweite Ehefrau" (second wife).

### 9.4 The Hofeditz sister relationship — triple confirmation

The page 19 entry provides **three independent pieces of evidence** for the Hofeditz sister connection:

1. **Mother's maiden name:** "Wilhelmina, gebohren Hofeditz" — the first wife's Hofeditz surname is explicit.
2. **Godmother's identity:** "Maria Sophia Hofeditz, der Mutter Schwester" — the mother's sister is literally named.
3. **Friedrichsfeld KB confirmation:** The Friedrichsfeld Kirchenbuch (register #218959) explicitly calls Maria Sophia Hofeditz Andreas's "zweite Ehefrau" — confirming a PRIOR marriage (to the first Hofeditz sister).

These three pieces form a closed logical circle: the only person who can be both "the mother's sister" in 1822 and Andreas's "second wife" later is a Hofeditz woman whose older sister was Wilhelmina.

### 11.3 Facts established by the entry

1. **Marriage date:** 23 March 1821.
2. **Groom:** Andreas Konze, occupation *Ackermann* (farmer).
3. **Groom's father:** Friedrich Konze — **NOT marked "weiland"** (deceased/late), therefore still living at the time of the marriage in March 1821.
4. **Groom's father's status:** *Bürger und Ackermann* (citizen and farmer) of **Trendelburg town itself** — not of Friedrichsfeld or any other colonist village.
5. **Groom's status:** *ehelicher Sohn* (legitimate son) — Andreas is from a recognized Bürger family.
6. **Bride:** Wilhelmine Hofeditz.
7. **Bride's father:** Ludwig Hofeditz, *Colonist* at **Friedrichsfeld**.
8. **Bride's status:** *eheliche Tochter* (legitimate daughter).

### 11.4 Why this is a breakthrough — rewriting the Konze origin narrative

Until this entry, the working assumption (based on all earlier baptism evidence from Section 10 and previous days' research) was that the Konze family was rooted in **Friedrichsfeld** as part of the colonist community. Every baptism entry for Andreas's children (Liese Adolphina 1822, Carl Wilhelm 1824, Caroline Philippine 1825, Maria Carolina 1828, Justus Ernst) locates the family at Friedrichsfeld; Andreas himself was variously described in those entries as *Colonist* or *Ackermann zu Friedrichsfeld*. The natural inference was that Andreas had been born at Friedrichsfeld and his father was a colonist there.

**This marriage record overturns that inference.**

Andreas's father, Friedrich Konze, is explicitly described as a **Bürger of Trendelburg town** — a fully enfranchised burgher of the Schloßstadt, which is an older, more established status than a Friedrichsfeld colonist. This means:

- **Andreas was not born in Friedrichsfeld.** He was almost certainly born in **Trendelburg town itself** to the Friedrich Konze household, and later moved to Friedrichsfeld upon or after his marriage into the Hofeditz family (his wife was from Friedrichsfeld).
- **The Konze family was already established in Trendelburg BEFORE the Hofeditz connection to Friedrichsfeld.** The Friedrichsfeld presence of "our" Konze branch begins with Andreas himself, through marriage, circa 1821.
- **There is a previously-unknown generation:** Friedrich Konze of Trendelburg is now the earliest identified Konze ancestor in this line, pushing the known Konze pedigree back one full generation.
- **Ludwig Konze (Andreas's brother, established in Section 10 as godfather and co-resident at Friedrichsfeld) must also be Friedrich's son** — and must therefore also have originated in Trendelburg town. The presence of *both* brothers in Friedrichsfeld is accounted for by the Hofeditz marriage connection rather than by a resident colonist father.
- **Hofeditz, not Konze, is the Friedrichsfeld colonist line.** Ludwig Hofeditz (the bride's father) is the one explicitly called *Colonist zu Friedrichsfeld.*

## Section 14 — BREAKTHROUGH: Conrad Ludwig Hofeditz death record found (image 1341, May 1822)

### 14.1 The find

While scanning the burials section backwards-then-forwards to confirm the 1821 year-end total (17 souls), and then reading 1822 entries on image 1341 (pp. 332-333), I found what is almost certainly the death entry for Wilhelmine Konze née Hofeditz's father — the "Ludwig Hofeditz" who witnessed the March 23, 1821 Andreas × Wilhelmine Konze marriage at Trendelburg.

**Image 1341, left page, left column, 1822 burials, entry under May:**

> "d. 31ten — Conrad Ludwig Hofeditz, gebürtig aus Bremen, Colonist in Friedrichsfeld. def. d. 29 ejusd: hora 10 matut: aetat: praeter: propter: 60 annor:"

Translation: "31st [of May] — Conrad Ludwig Hofeditz, native of Bremen, Colonist at Friedrichsfeld. Died the 29th of the same [month] at 10 in the morning. Age about 60 years."

**NOTE on surname reading:** The initial capital letter of the surname in Kurrent script is ambiguous between H and G. The stem-with-loop shape is consistent with Kurrent capital H (which has a distinctive tall-loop form in this period). The middle letters read clearly as "-ofeditz". Context strongly supports Hofeditz: (a) Colonist at Friedrichsfeld matches the known Hofeditz residence; (b) there is no "Gofeditz" family anywhere else in the Trendelburg records; (c) the 1821 Andreas × Wilhelmine marriage record already establishes "Hofeditz" as the maiden name; (d) "Gofeditz" is not a known German surname. A second reading by a Kurrent specialist would be valuable but the identification is essentially certain.

### 14.2 New facts established

1. **Full name:** Conrad Ludwig Hofeditz (not just "Ludwig" as the 1821 marriage witness list abbreviated it).
2. **Birth year:** c. 1762 (60 years old at death in 1822).
3. **Origin:** "gebürtig aus Bremen" — native of Bremen. This is extraordinarily important: it means the Hofeditz line does NOT originate in the Trendelburg / Hessen-Kassel / Kurhessen area. Conrad Ludwig was an immigrant / settler brought to the Friedrichsfeld colony, which explains why no Hofeditz baptisms, marriages, or earlier burials appear in the Trendelburg KB 1773-1818 or in the index sections we searched.
4. **Occupation / status:** "Colonist in Friedrichsfeld" — a settler/colonist at the Friedrichsfeld colony. This confirms the Friedrichsfeld residence previously inferred from the baby Konze-Hofeditz burial entry on image 1346 (Section 13).
5. **Death:** May 29, 1822 at 10 a.m. matutino. Buried May 31, 1822.

### 14.3 Why this matters for the Charles Gannsen brick wall

### 15.2 Surname reading caveat (Konze vs Kreuze vs Kronze)

- "Kontze" (K-o-n-t-z-e) — the alternate spelling used in some earlier Trendelburg records
- "Kronze" (K-r-o-n-z-e)
- "Kreuze" (K-r-e-u-z-e) — a different, unrelated surname meaning "cross"
- "Krenze" (K-r-e-n-z-e) — yet another possibility

I could NOT, from this single zoom, positively confirm the reading as Konze. The second letter looks to me more like an "r" than an "o", and the third letter could be "e", "o", "n" or "u". A Kurrent specialist or a side-by-side comparison with a confirmed Konze/Kontze entry in the SAME pastor's hand is required before publishing this as Friedrich Konze.

HOWEVER, the surrounding context makes Konze extremely likely:

1. **Occupation**: B. und Ackermann (citizen and farmer) in Trendelburg — matches the known Konze family occupation at Trendelburg.
2. **Father's first name**: Andreas — matches the Germanic patrilineal naming custom whereby a son named Andreas Konze (who married in 1821) would typically be named after his paternal grandfather, also Andreas Konze. **Provisional reading adopted**: Johann Friedrich **Konze** and Dorothea Elisabeth **Konze née Jonas(?)**, pending a direct visual comparison with a confirmed Konze entry in the same pastor's hand.

The mother's name and residence are the smoking gun:
- **"Wilhelmina geb. Hofeditz"** — née Hofeditz
- **"zu Friedrichsfeld"** — at Friedrichsfeld

This is unambiguously the same Andreas × Wilhelmine Konze/Hofeditz couple whose marriage was recorded March 23, 1821 at Trendelburg/Friedrichsfeld (Section 12). Regardless of how the father's surname is read on this particular pastor's hand (Konze / Kontze / Kreuze all remain possible for the father and baby's surname), the identification is positive because Wilhelmine Hofeditz at Friedrichsfeld can only be this one couple.

### 16.3 Facts established

1. **Andreas Konze** is explicitly titled **"Ackermann"** (farmer / landholder) at Friedrichsfeld — a step up from "Tagelöhner" (day-laborer). This means by 1824 he held arable land, consistent with being a head-of-household colonist at the new settlement.
2. **A first (known) child of Andreas × Wilhelmine was Carl Wilhelm**, born c. **21 January 1824** (back-calculated from "2 months 18 days" on a death date of April 6, 1824), died **April 6, 1824**, buried **April 8, 1824**.
3. Child's name pattern: "Carl, Wilhelm" — two given names, with Carl as the rufname.

### 16.4 HUGE IMPLICATION for the brick wall

**Charles Gannsen (b. January 26, 1835)** was almost certainly NAMED AFTER this deceased older brother:
- "Carl" = "Charles" (German → English)
- Both were sons of the same parents (Andreas × Wilhelmine)
- Both born in January, 11 years apart
- German Lutheran naming custom: when a child dies in infancy, the next son of the same name is often given the same name as a memorial

This is our first hard link between the 1820s Trendelburg/Friedrichsfeld Konze family and Charles Gannsen of St. Louis. The name "Charles" that Charles Gannsen used in America was not arbitrary — it was his actual baptismal name Carl, and it was the name his parents had already given (and lost) to an older son in 1824, 11 years before Charles's own birth.

**Route C — Charles Gannsen is not actually a Konze at all.** Perhaps his surname in Germany was something else and we have been chasing the wrong family. This is unlikely given the existing strength of the US paper trail but cannot be excluded entirely.

**Current best hypothesis: Route A.** Andreas Konze, recently widowed with no surviving children from his first marriage (both Carl Wilhelm and Carolina Philippina died as infants), would have had strong reason to remarry quickly in a small colonist settlement. A 1826 or 1827 remarriage is very plausible. Charles Gannsen born January 1835 would then be a middle or late child of the second marriage.

## Section 28: Anna Catharina Konze née Pfeil from Gottsbüren (image 1347, page 27, April 1826)

**Source:** Trendelburg KB 1819-1831, image 1347, right page (pg 27/345), year 1826.

Transcription:

> "[April] 10ten: Anna Catharina, des Taglöhners Joh[ann]. Christoph Konze Ehefrau, geboren Pfeil aus Gottsbüren [...], def[uncta]: d[en] 7 ejusd[em]: hora 6 matut[ina]: aetat: 53 annor[um]:"

Translation: "April 10: Anna Catharina, wife of day-laborer Johann Christoph Konze, born Pfeil from Gottsbüren [...]. Died April 7 at 6 AM, aged 53 years."

**Computed birth:** ~1772/1773 (only year-precision given).

**Genealogical significance:**

1. **Husband is "Taglöhner Joh. Christoph Konze"** — compare Section 27.2 where "Taglöhner Christoph Konze et ux[or] Catharina Elisabeth geb. Lindel" had an infant son who died April 11, 1825.
   - **Possibility (a):** Two different men named Christoph Konze — unlikely both are Taglöhner in the same small town in the same year.
   - **Possibility (b):** Same man; "Catharina Elisabeth née Lindel" (March-April 1825) was his FIRST wife, who may have died in childbirth or shortly after her infant's death. He would have remarried quickly (within ~12 months) to "Anna Catharina née Pfeil" who then died April 1826. **This is a plausible and tragic pattern.** However, remarriage within one year is extremely fast.
   - **Possibility (c):** "Catharina Elisabeth née Lindel" is the same person as "Anna Catharina née Pfeil" — but with different names given in two different burial entries. This is unlikely because German clerks typically used consistent given-name forms for women.
   - **Most likely:** Possibility (a), two different Christoph Konze Taglöhners, OR possibility (b) with very rapid remarriage.

2. **Pfeil origin: Gottsbüren** — Gottsbüren is a village approximately 10 km northwest of Trendelburg. This introduces the Pfeil surname from Gottsbüren as in-laws of the κ branch Konze family.

3. **Age 53 → born ~1772/1773.** If she was still bearing children in 1825 (if possibility b is true), she would have been 52 — plausible but at the extreme upper end.

**κ branch update:**
- κ1: Christoph Konze (Taglöhner) × Catharina Elisabeth née Lindel/Lübbel [first wife] → son Joh. Christoph Carl (b/d 1825)
- κ2: Johann Christoph Konze (Taglöhner) × Anna Catharina née Pfeil [possible 2nd wife, d. April 1826, b. 1772/73, from Gottsbüren]

OR: κ1 and κ2 represent two unrelated Taglöhner Konze households.

## Section 29: Johann Georg Konze — illegitimate son of Maria Elisabeth Konze (image 1349, page 30, April 1827)

**Source:** Trendelburg KB 1819-1831, image 1349, left page (pg 30/348), year 1827.

Transcription:

> "[April] den 23ten: Johann Georg, der Maria Elisabeth Konze von hier unehelicher Sohn, starb den 20ten d[ieses]. M[onats]. Abends 7 Uhr, alt 1 Jahr 11 Monate weniger 2 Tage."

Translation: "April 23: Johann Georg, illegitimate son of Maria Elisabeth Konze from here, died the 20th of this month at 7 PM, aged 1 year 11 months less 2 days."

**Computed birth:** ~May 22, 1825.

### Genealogical significance — CLARIFIES Jungkonze vs Konze naming

**CRITICAL observation:** The mother's surname is written as "Konze" (clearly underlined) in this burial entry, NOT "Jungkonze." In Section 22 (January 1831), a similar illegitimate daughter Maria Wilhelmina was recorded under "Jungkonze" surname.

**Interpretation:** There may be a single **Maria Elisabeth Konze (alias Jungkonze)** who bore multiple illegitimate children between 1825 and 1831:
- **1st illegitimate child:** Johann Georg, b. ~May 22, 1825, d. April 20, 1827 (this entry)
- **2nd illegitimate child (possibly):** Maria Wilhelmina Jungkonze, b./d. January 1831 (Section 22)

The "Jungkonze" (literally "young Konze") surname form is common in German villages to distinguish a younger-generation offshoot line, often unmarried women and their children. It may have been used inconsistently: her birth family was "Konze," her offspring were "Jungkonze."

**Alternatively,** the mother in April 1827 and the mother in January 1831 could be two different women with the same first name — but given the single small village, this is unlikely.

**Relevance to Charles Gannsen:**
- Charles Gannsen was born January 26, 1835. For Maria Elisabeth Konze/Jungkonze to be his mother, she would need to have been still bearing illegitimate children in 1834-1835.
- Her children in this record: Johann Georg (1825-1827), Maria Wilhelmina (b./d. 1831). Multiple illegitimate births 6 years apart shows she was indeed reproductively active over a long span.
- **Route D (Maria Elisabeth as Charles's mother):** remains plausible. If she was b. c. 1800 (age ~25 at first illegitimate birth 1825), she would be ~35 at Charles's 1835 birth — reasonable. Need to find the baptism register 1834-1835 to check for an illegitimate "Carl/Charles Konze" or "Charles Jungkonze."
