# Charles' Birth Date Across the American Records — Sourced & Confidence-Ranked

**Subject:** Charles Konzen — recorded in U.S. records as Gannsen / Gonson / Johnson (project-canonical 26 January 1835 – 16 December 1903)

Framing caveat: **no independent birth or baptism record for Charles has ever been found** (the German parish baptism is still unrecovered). Every figure below traces to Charles' own testimony or, at his death, to his family's. So none is a true "original, firsthand" record *of the birth event* — they are derivative attestations, and the right way to read them is as a cluster, not as competing facts.

## Confidence ranking, strongest → weakest (and why)

From a genealogical-evidence standpoint, "confidence" here means *how much weight the record deserves as evidence of Charles' true birth date.* Two axes drive it: **originality / informant proximity** (was the recorder present, and who supplied the fact) and **precision** (full date vs. round age).

1. **Church burial register — 26 Jan 1835** *(highest)*
   The only record carrying a full day-month-year, and it is internally self-checking: the stated "68 y 10 m 20 d" back-calculates to the same late-January-1835 birth. It is an original parish register entry written contemporaneously by the clergyman. *Why not higher still:* the birth fact is secondhand and post-mortem — supplied by the family after Charles died — not an independent birth record, and its 1835 year runs a year or two older than his own later statements.

2. **1900 census — Nov 1836**
   The only *other* record that fixes a month, and an original federal record taken in Charles' lifetime. *Why mid-pack:* he was a **boarder** in the Kuhn household, so the informant may have been the head of household rather than Charles himself; a stated month from a non-self informant is suggestive, not firm.

3. **9 Feb 1891 pension declaration — age 53 (~1838)**
   Charles' **own sworn statement**, under oath and penalty of perjury, on an original federal document — high informant proximity. *Why not higher:* age only, no month/day, and it conflicts with his *own* 1890 filing by three years, which shows he did not actually know his precise age.

4. **15 Jan 1890 pension declaration — age ~56 (~1833–34)**
   Also Charles' own sworn original statement. Ranked just below the 1891 one precisely *because* the two disagree: a person who swears 56 one year and 53 the next is estimating, not reporting a known date.

5. **1880 census — age 39 (~1841)**
   Original federal record, but age-only, household-reported (any adult could have answered), a round number, and the youngest outlier of the set.

6. **2 Dec 1862 enlistment — age 23 (~1839)**
   Original military record, but the age was **self-reported at enlistment**, a context where men routinely rounded or shaded their age, and it is age-only.

7. **17 Dec 1903 death certificate — age 69 / "born 1834"**
   *Lowest for birth precision.* The informant was necessarily **not Charles** (he was dead); a survivor gave a round "69," and "1834" is merely that age subtracted from 1903. Authoritative for the death; weak for the birth.

8. **18 Jan 1890 — age 65 (~1825)** *(disregard as a birth estimate)*
   An apparent clerical error. Age 65 contradicts every other record by roughly a decade and even contradicts Charles' own declaration filed three days earlier (~56). Cite it for completeness, but it should not weigh in the birth-date estimate.

## Bottom line

The firm, multiply-attested conclusion is a birth **in the mid-1830s in Kurhessen.** The precise **26 January 1835** rests on a single post-mortem parish entry (Doc N) and is best held as the **working anchor** until the German baptism is recovered. The only independent month-level support comes from the 1900 census ("Nov 1836"), and Charles' own sworn pension figures cluster around 1833–1838 — consistent with the mid-1830s, but never pinning the day.
