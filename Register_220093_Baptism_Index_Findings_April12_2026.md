## 1. Summary

The baptism alphabetical index of Register 220093 yielded an extraordinary number of Kontze entries — **12 male names with ~24 page references** and **8 female names with ~25+ page references**. Combined, roughly **50 individual Kontze baptism entries** span the entire register (1637–1772). No "Contze" (C-spelling) entries were found in the C-section of the index — confirming that this register consistently uses the K-spelling.

**Critical etymological discovery:** "Nies" (the nickname of the Generation 0 patriarch "Nies Kontze Senior" from the marriage section) is a German diminutive of **DIONYSIUS** — directly linking Nies Kontze Senior to the baptism index entry "Dionysius" on page 90.

## 3. Key Genealogical Connections

### 3.1 Dionysius = Nies Kontze Senior

The name "Nies" is a standard German diminutive of Dionysius (via Denis/Denies → Nies). This means:
- **Nies Kontze Senior** (deceased by February 1752, Ackermann) = **Dionysius Kontze** on page 90 of the baptism register
- Page 90 of the register likely dates to the 1720s–1730s, which would be consistent with a man who had marriageable daughters in the 1750s
- His baptism entry on page 90 should name his FATHER — potentially revealing the Kontze family's origin in Trendelburg

### 3.2 Arnold Philipp on Page 1

Page 1 of the baptism register covers the earliest entries (~1637–1640). If this is correctly read as page "1," it raises significant questions:
- An Arnold Philipp baptized ~1637–1640 would be ~115 years old at the time of his 1754 marriage — IMPOSSIBLE
- This may therefore be a **GRANDFATHER** or **GREAT-UNCLE** of our Arnold Philipp Kontze (b. ~1710–1720, d. 1774–1780)
- Alternatively, the page number may have been misread (could be "11" or "71" or another number)
- **This must be verified by visiting the actual page**

### 3.3 Joh. Heinr(ich) on Page 165

Page 165 likely dates to the 1740s–1750s. This could be:
- The baptism of **Joh. Henrich Kontze the Younger** (eldest son of Arnold Philipp, married 1773)
- Or the baptism of a son of **Joan Henrif Kontze** (Generation 0 patriarch, d. 1762)
- The entry should name the father, resolving which Joh. Henrich this is

### 3.4 Andreas — Three Entries (Pages 48, 117, 185)

Three "Andreas Kontze" baptisms spanning the register suggests:
- Multiple generations of the name (grandfather → father → son)
- Page 48 (~1660s?) could be Andreas the Generation 0 patriarch himself
- Page 117 (~1730s?) could be a son of one of the other patriarchs
- Page 185 (~1750s?) could be a grandson

### 3.5 Philipp — Three Entries (Pages 114, 68, 223)

Three "Philipp Kontze" baptisms:
- Page 68 (~1690s?) — earliest, possibly an ancestor
- Page 114 (~1720s?) — could be Arnold Philipp's generation
- Page 223 (~1760s?) — could be a son of the current generation
