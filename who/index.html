Charles & the Gannsen-Kreichelt household

Charles, his wife Auguste, and her immediate Kreichelt family in St. Louis. The Kreichelts converged with the Gannsens twice over: Auguste's father and brother both served in Battery B alongside Charles before the 1866 wedding.

  • Charles Gannsen Subject b. 26 January 1835, Hesse-Cassel · d. 16 December 1903†, St. Louis. Civil War sergeant, Battery B 2nd Missouri Light Artillery. Married Auguste Kreichelt 7 January 1866. → Charles in America tab
  • Auguste Kreichelt Wife Charles's wife. Married at St. Marcus German Evangelical Church 7 January 1866 — eighteen days after his discharge. Mother of seven. FamilySearch LZKV-9F5
  • Georg Kreichelt Father-in-law Auguste's father. Battery B veteran. Walks his daughter down the aisle on 7 January 1866. → Civil War tab
  • August Kreichelt Brother-in-law Auguste's brother. Battery B veteran. Sponsors Erwin Carl Konsen's 1875 baptism — the surviving Hesse-spelling of the family name.

The seven children

Each baptized at St. Marcus German Evangelical Church, St. Louis. Across the seven baptisms the surname spelling drifted through five distinct baptism-register renderings (Gonnson, Gansen, Gonsen, Jansen, Konsen) — the children's-baptism subset of the nine-attestation surname trail that is one of the central pieces of evidence in the surname-variant theory. → Open the Children index

  • Friedrich Wilhelm Gonnson Child 1 1866–1867. Named for the deposed Elector of Hesse. Died in infancy. → Profile
  • Maria Josephine Gonnson Child 2 1868–1890. Died at age 22. → Profile
  • Friedrike Catharina Caroline Amalia Gansen Child 3 1870–1949. Later "Kate Ruddy". → Profile
  • Auguste Gonsen Child 4 1872–1890. Died at age 18. → Profile
  • Louise Jansen Child 5 1873–1947. Later "Louise Foster". → Profile
  • Erwin Carl Konsen ★ Child 6 1875–1950. Later "Charles Johnson Jr." — the surname-preservation evidence. A key child for the German-side investigation: his baptism preserves "Konsen", the closest phonetic American capture to "Konze" (the verbatim Hesse-parish form Konzen appears in his sister Auguste's 1890 death record, as re-read in May 2026). → Profile
  • Bertha Jansen Child 7 1880–1959. Baptism surname “Jansen”; later recorded as “Bertha Johnson.”→ Profile

People who knew Charles in St. Louis

Eleven men who swore an affidavit, witnessed a signature, or stood as wedding sponsor for Charles between his Civil War enlistment in 1862 and the Pension Bureau's 1898 family-roster reply — plus his first treating physician. → Open the People index

  • Adam Herweck Battery B · Sgt Senior groomsman at Charles's 1866 wedding. Recruited Charles into Co. B in November–December 1862. Same rank trajectory as Charles. b. 1831 Lampertheim, Hessen. → Profile
  • August Derr Battery B · 1st Sgt Charles's direct NCO during the 1863 New Madrid station. Two affidavits in 1891. → Profile
  • Christian Meyer Battery B · Pvt May 1891 affidavit on cannonading and exposure at New Madrid. → Profile
  • Henry Niemeier Pension witness 26 years' acquaintance with Charles (since 1865). October 1891 affidavit on deafness and rheumatism. 1300 N. 9th Street. → Profile
  • Henry Stoll Pension witness Knew Charles since 1867. 29 September 1890 affidavit. 1232 S. 3rd Street. → Profile
  • Michael Nolan Co-worker witness Worked alongside Charles since 1867. October affidavit on rheumatism flare-ups and continuous rupture. → Profile
  • Theodore Gottlob Sr. ★ Neighbor · ancestor Charles's next-door neighbor at 2409 DeKalb. 1890 declaration witness. Jed Johnson's direct ancestor (FamilySearch KG17-D3H). Two ancestral lines converge on the same DeKalb Street block in 1890. → Profile
  • John Heitzenberger Declaration witness Co-witness with Theodore Gottlob on the 1890 detailed declaration. 2412 Arsenal Street. → Profile
  • John Hebbeler Declaration witness Witness on the 9 February 1891 Declaration. 20-year acquaintance. 916 N. 6th Street. → Profile
  • John P. Thurbecker Declaration witness Co-witness with John Hebbeler on the 9 February 1891 Declaration. 638 N. 7th Street. → Profile
  • Dr. Charles Hess Physician Charles's first post-war treating physician. Treated him for the rupture and rheumatism after the 1865 discharge. → Profile

Candidate families investigated (German side)

Twenty family lines investigated as possible identities for Charles's German parentage. Most have been eliminated through Archion parish-register searches and Schräder cross-checks; three remain open.

  • Konze / Kontze / Konzen Open Trendelburg & Deisel · Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis. The Konzen surname pool is the project's Working conclusion (May 2026); the specific Konze father / baptism remains Open (Peter Schräder's 12 April 2026 verdict: not a legitimate Konze son). → Family page
  • Hildebrand Open Deisel · Heisebeck · maternal Konze line + chain migration. → Family page
