## 1. What StLGS is, and why it fits this case

StLGS is an all-volunteer society that has been digitizing and indexing St. Louis City/County records for decades. Its core value for this project is that it holds **local German-Protestant congregational and civil records for exactly the population Charles belonged to** — a German immigrant, Evangelical/St. Marcus family in 1860s–1900s St. Louis. Two structural facts shape everything:

- **Missouri kept no state birth/death records until 1910.** Before that, registration was voluntary. So for Charles (d. 1903) and Augusta (d. 1896), the substitutes are church baptism/death registers, city death registers (1850–1908), burial permits, cemetery records, funeral-home records, and obituaries — all areas where StLGS has dedicated collections.
- **The richest databases are members-only** and run through the site's own search engine. You are already logged in and have swept the surname databases (Sessions 96/98), so the gaps below are mostly *non-searchable* finding aids and *off-site pointers* the site provides.

## 3. Untapped or under-exploited resources, mapped to your priorities

### A. Probate — directly serves Priority P13 (Charles d. 1903 estate file)
The probate page is the most actionable untapped lead. Coverage:
- **St. Louis probate 1802–1900** is digitized at the Missouri State Archives (Judicial Records collection) — free, off the StLGS pointer.
- **St. Louis City probate alphabetical card index 1890–1989** (covers Charles's 1903 death) — at the Missouri State Archives and the Emerson Center, St. Louis County Library Clark Branch. Each card gives deceased name, file number, filing date.
- StLGS's own **1804–1876 Index to Probate Abstracts** and **St. Louis County Will Book 1876–1889** are on-site sub-pages.
- *Why it matters:* a Charles Gannsen/Johnson estate file could name heirs and, critically for the German-origin question, any Hessian correspondents or birthplace references. This has not appeared in your files.
- Page: https://stlgs.org/research-2/government/probate

### B. Congregation Index + Clergy By Name — serves the church-of-origin path
You have records from Holy Ghost and St. Paul UCC, plus St. Marcus burials. Two finding aids you haven't logged as worked:
- **Clergy By Name** — if you have a clergy signature on any St. Louis vital record (e.g., the 1866 marriage officiant) but don't know the congregation, this reverse-lookup identifies the church and neighborhood. Directly useful for placing Charles's family in a specific German Evangelical congregation whose Taufbuch may state the *Heimatort*. https://stlgs.org/research-2/congregations/clergy-by-name
- **Congregation Index (alphabetical)** — full list with formation dates, addresses, denomination notes, and links to record holdings. Use it to enumerate every German Evangelical/UCC and Lutheran congregation operating near 2130 De Kalb St. in the 1860s–90s, then check each for a Gannsen/Gonnson/Konze baptism naming the German village. https://stlgs.org/research-2/congregations/congregation-index
- Note the active **Congregations Project** — they are still digitizing church books, so collections grow; worth a periodic re-check.

### C. Funeral Homes — independent of church and civil records
Funeral-home ledgers often record birthplace, parents, and informant when the death register does not. The Deaths page explicitly flags these as a separate source. Charles (1903) and Augusta (1896) each generated a funeral record somewhere. Worth checking which St. Louis German funeral homes StLGS has indexed. https://stlgs.org/research-2/life-death/funeral-homes

### D. Deaths page — sub-collections beyond the 1850–1908 register DB
The register DB you've used is only one item. Also present:
- **Deaths Reported in St. Louis Globe-Democrat, 1881–1891** — a separate newspaper-death index; Augusta died 1896 (just outside) but collateral Konze deaths 1881–91 fall inside.
- **Missouri Death Records Online** pointer (Missouri Archives death certificates 1910+ — relevant for Charles's children).
- https://stlgs.org/research-2/life-death/deaths

### E. Obituaries — German-newspaper emphasis
The obituary page stresses: *if your ancestor was German, look in a German-language newspaper.* For an 1896/1903 German St. Louisan, the German press (e.g., *Westliche Post*, *Anzeiger des Westens*) is more likely to carry a substantive notice — and a German obit is the kind of source that names the *Geburtsort*. Sub-pages: Globe-Democrat Morgue, Post-Dispatch obituaries. The State Historical Society of Missouri holds the fullest newspaper microfilm. https://stlgs.org/research-2/life-death/obituaries

### F. City Directories — annual residence/occupation tracking 1821–1980
Not a quick database, but a year-by-year Gannsen/Gonnson/Johnson trace (1866–1903) can pin Charles's occupation, confirm the De Kalb St. address span, reveal a widow listing for Augusta after 1896, and surface adult children. Useful to corroborate the pension-file timeline and to disambiguate the Catholic Koncen line (which stays put). https://stlgs.org/research-2/publications/directories

### G. First Families of St. Louis — a possible *output*, not just input
The "Immigrant Families (1822–1865)" category fits Charles exactly (arrived by 1865, married 1866). If your line is proven, you (as an StLGS member) could document a lineage application — and the existing First Families files may already contain a vetted Konze/Gannsen lineage submitted by another descendant. Worth searching the program's accepted-surnames list. https://stlgs.org/resources/services/first-families-of-st-louis

### H. Surname Files & Reference Room (office, in-person/by-request)
Physical **Surname Files** and a **Reference Room** at the Sunnen Drive office hold donated family research, Bible records, and clippings not on the website. A direct query to the office (or a volunteer-assistance request) for "Gannsen / Gonnson / Konze" could surface another researcher's file. Office hours: Tue/Thu/Sat 9–noon. https://stlgs.org/resources/office-research/surname-files

### I. Quarterly Back Issues + Surname Collections
You already hit the Quarterly surname index (the 1853 passenger find came from vol. 32). Worth a complete pass of the **Quarterly back-issue** and **Surname Collections** indexes for any Konze/Gannsen/Kreichelt article beyond the single passenger-list reference. https://stlgs.org/research-2/publications/quarterly-back-issues
