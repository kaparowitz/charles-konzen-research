The eight evidence cards · in chronological order

Card 1 · Project conclusion

Trendelburg burial register 220096 · K-section / Kontzen entries

1773–1818 · Trendelburg, Churhessen

The convergent-evidence basis for the May 2026 surname conclusion: KONZEN. Trendelburg parish surname-pool documented continuously from 1773; the parish-internal cross-reference note "siehe auch K. Contze, Cortze, Cordes, Contz" establishes orthographic interchangeability. The nine distinct American Charles-family renderings all converge on this German source.

Card 2 · 1st Kurhessen reference

Civil War enlistment record

2 December 1862 · St. Louis

The first mention of a town and state anywhere in Charles's documentary record: "Freundburg Kurhessen." Peter Schräder's 26 February 2026 phonetic re-reading decoded "Freundburg" as a clerk's mishearing of Trendelburg. The foundational geographic anchor of the entire investigation.

Card 3 · Marriage register

St. Marcus marriage register · Pastor Wall

7 January 1866 · St. Louis

Charles's own statement of origin: "aus Deisse, Kreis Cassel, Churhessen" — the village of Deisel inside the Electorate of Hesse-Kassel. Surname recorded as Gonnson. With Card 2's "Freundburg" decoding, this triangulates the answer to a 5×5 km square inside the Kreis Hofgeismar.

Card 4 · Baptisms

Seven St. Marcus baptisms · 1866–1880

1866–1880 · St. Louis

Charles & Auguste's seven children. The baptismal records carry the surname trail (Gonnson, Gansen, Gonsen, Konsen, Jansen) and the Pastor Braschler "smoking gun" pair (1870 vs 1872). Sponsor reconciliation across all seven baptisms reveals not a single Charles-side blood relative — the structural confirmation of "immigrated alone."

Card 6 · Konsen attestation

1875 St. Marcus baptism — Erwin Carl Konsen

1875 · Pastor Hoffmann

The strongest direct surname link between Charles in St. Louis and the Konze line in Hesse: Pastor Hoffmann recorded his sixth child as Erwin Carl Konsen, preserving the German -en form. Fifteen years later, the 1890 St. Marcus death record of his daughter Auguste reads Konzen — the single American attestation of the full Hessian z-form documented in the Trendelburg parish since 1773. Two scribes, two parallel K-initial orthographic variants, both preserving the Hessian -en suffix.

Card 7 · Pension file

Civil War Pension File F41-523680269E

1890–1903 · NARA

The richest U.S. document — birthdate, addresses, witnesses. No family-side affidavits — Charles emigrated alone. Page-by-page audit confirms: the pension file does not contain any Charles-self-stated birthplace declaration; its Kurhessen attribution is solely via the embedded 1862 service record.

Card 8 · Wife's death record

1896 St. Marcus death record · "Auguste Conson"

1896 · Pastor Eilt Heeren Eilts

The strongest single Charles-self-attestation of his surname. Charles, age 61, gave Auguste's name to Pastor Eilts at the funeral; Eilts wrote what he heard — the C-initial form (Conson) that ties straight back to the Trendelburg Contzen line.

For researchers — the address chronology

Researcher resource · directory-mined

Charles's St. Louis addresses — 1865–1903, chronological & sourced

Pension file + 1880/1900 censuses + St. Louis directories · updated June 2026

Every documented residence in chronological order, each with an outside link to verify it. A June 2026 FamilySearch Full-Text sweep of the St. Louis directories added two entries the project did not previously have: the 1872–73 and the 1876 "718 Gate" listings — the 1876 "Gonson Charles, driver, 718 Gate" entry (the Gonson spelling matching the 1880 census) extends that tenure to 1876 and re-places the long-lost Gate Street at Carondelet Avenue near Pestalozzi (Benton Park). The sweep also pinned the 1895 "Gannsen Charles, watch, r. rear 2407 DeKalb" entry. Together with Charles's own handwritten pension address-letter (c. 1898) and the 1880/1900 federal censuses, this gives a fully-sourced residence timeline across 38 years.
