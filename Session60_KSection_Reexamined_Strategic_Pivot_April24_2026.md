### Why the Session 59 reading was likely incorrect

The Kurrent script for "Köſterin" (Köster + feminine -in suffix) and "Kontze" share several glyph features:
- Both start with K
- Both have "ſ" (long s) in middle that resembles "f" or "n"
- The terminal "-in" of Köſterin and "-ze" of Kontze can look similar at low zoom

Session 59's zoomed crops (region [1000, 580, 1230, 700] of Image 1887 at zoom 12) showed entries reading "...mann, Wilhelmine — 68" and similar surnames ending in "-mann" or "-min" — which on review are Köſter family entries, NOT Konze. The Konze surname has a distinctive z that wasn't visible in the zoomed view.
