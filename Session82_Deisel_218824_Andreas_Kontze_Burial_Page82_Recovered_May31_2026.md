## Result — recovered

The entry that the April session searched for across images 1864–1871 without success was located and read with the high-zoom approach. Two corrections to the prior estimate, then the entry:

1. **Seite 82 is the 1828 burials**, not 1826–1827 as the April note estimated. (Image 1865 right page = Seite 77 / 1826; image **1868 left page = Seite 82 / 1828**; right page = Seite 83.)
2. The Andreas Kontze entry is **§26 on Seite 82**:

> **§26. Andreas [Kontze], *Einlieger*** (lodger) … died **the 24th**, ~7 Uhr morgens, **alt 29 Jahr 7 Monat 25 Tag**.

- **Surname:** confirmed as **Kontze** by the register's own alphabetical index (K-section → "Kontze, Andreas — Seite 82"); the in-page Kurrent is dense but consistent.
- **Status:** *Einlieger* (lodger) — a new civic-status detail.
- **Death:** the **24th** (February 1828, per the documented date and the 1828 page), morning.
- **Age:** **29 years 7 months 25 days → born ~30 June 1798.**

## Significance — which Andreas this is

The prior session flagged page 82 as ambiguous: *"Could be Andreas Kontze (I), the father figure … or the younger Deisel Andreas Konze."* The recovered entry settles it: a **29-year-old who died in 1828** is **neither** Andreas (I) the elder **nor** Andreas (II) (b. 1787, d. 1830). It is the **young Andreas Konze, b. ~1798 (Identity 5 in the disentanglement; previously "b. ~1799, d. 24 Feb 1828, age 29")** — now confirmed from the register, with his birth refined to **~30 June 1798** and his status as an *Einlieger*. None of this bears on Charles directly (this Andreas died seven years before Charles's 1835 birth), but it closes a long-standing "unreadable" item on the Konze-family reconstruction.
