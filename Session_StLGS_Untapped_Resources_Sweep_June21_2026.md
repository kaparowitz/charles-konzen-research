## Follow-up D — St. Louis probate for Charles (21 June)
**What's online vs. offline.** St. Louis City probate **1802–1900** is digitized free at the Missouri State Archives (Judicial Records DB). Charles died **Dec 1903**, just past that cutoff. For 1890–1989 the finding aid is the **St. Louis City probate alphabetical card index** (name + file number + filing date), held at **Missouri State Archives–St. Louis (MSA-STL)** and the **Emerson Center, SLCL Clark Family Branch** — **not online**.

**Online check done (free): St. Louis City *Wills* Index, Books M–J2 (1877–1904).** Read the full **I–J** letter list (SLCL). **No Charles/Carl Johnson** appears (Johnson wills indexed: Alfred, August, Barton W, Benjamin, Catherine, Demas, Ellie, Frances, George E, Henry A, John, John B, Laura B, Mary, Nancy, Nickolas, Patrick, Peter, Samuel P, Sanai Bell, Silas, Thomas B — none is Charles). Nor any Carl/Charles **Jansen**. (G = Gonnson/Gansen and K = Konzen letter-pages not separately pulled, but moot — see below.)

**Conclusion: Charles died intestate.** Absent from the wills index + modest circumstances (watchman/boarder, died at City Hospital) ⇒ no will. Any probate would be an **intestate administration**, found only via the offline **card index**, almost certainly filed under **"Johnson"** (the name on his 1903 death cert and 1900 census). So the G/K wills pages add nothing.

**Two honest caveats before chasing it:**
1. He may have **no probate at all** — an intestate man of little property often generated no administration. A real chance the search comes back empty.
2. Even if an administration file exists, it documents **heirs and assets**, and 1900s Missouri probate papers **rarely state a foreign birthplace**. So this is valuable for confirming family/heirs, but unlikely to deliver the Hessian **village** — that answer realistically lives only in the German parish record (the original project goal).

**How to pull it (turnkey):**
- **SLCL free email lookup** (History & Genealogy Dept, Emerson Center) — limit **3 searches/request**. Ask them to check the St. Louis City probate card index ~1903–1905 for: (1) **Johnson, Charles** (d. 16/17 Dec 1903); (2) **Gonnson, Charles**; (3) **Konzen, Charles/Carl**. Request the **file number + filing date** from the card. Lookup policy + contact: https://www.slcl.org/research-learn/genealogy/lookups-other-services · phone (314) 994-3300.
- Or contact **MSA-STL** directly (holds the same card index): https://www.sos.mo.gov/archives/resources/msastl/holdings
- **Then the actual file:** with the file number, request the estate/administration packet. MSA-STL does **not** hold the Probate Court case files/record books — those are with the **City of St. Louis Circuit Court (Probate Division)**; the card index tells you what to order.
