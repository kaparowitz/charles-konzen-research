Visualize

The project at a glance. Four visual entry points before you read the prose: life timeline, surname trail, candidate funnel, parish map. Each diagram links into the relevant evidence narrative.

A research site this dense rewards patient reading — but a first-time visitor often wants the shape of the argument before the argument itself. These five figures are the project's working diagrams. Each one stands alone; together they trace the case from the open question (where in Hesse?) to the working answer (the Trendelburg Konze pool), the gaps that remain, and the path the investigation itself took to get here.

1 · Life timeline · 1835–1903

Sixty-eight years across five chapters. Hover for detail; click any event to open its evidence card or section page.

2 · Surname trail · the convergent-evidence argument

Nine distinct American renderings across at least five scribes between 1866 and 1896 (the 17 May 2026 rigor pass expanded the canon from seven to eight with the Konzen z-form re-reading of the 1890 daughter's death record; the 21 June 2026 pass added the 1895 Gould’s-directory Gannsen). Five of the nine cluster on the [ˈkɔn.sən] sound; one is the Hessian z-form (Konzen, 1890); three are vowel/onset outliers (Gansen, Jansen, Gannsen). Konsen and Konzen together bracket the trail — two -en-preserving forms fifteen years apart, by two different scribes.

Open Key Evidence Card 1 → The Konzen puzzle deep dive →

3 · Candidate funnel · 16 families investigated, 1 surviving hypothesis

Two analytic filters — birth-year window and surname/parish congruence — eliminated all but the Trendelburg / Friedrichsfeld Konze pool of Churhessen. Each elimination is logged on its section page with the specific contradicting parish entry.

4 · Hessian parish map · the geographic search

Every parish within walking distance of Charles's stated home village (Deisel, ~5 km south of Trendelburg). Colour-coded by status: working hypothesis (gold), eliminated (red), open / undigitised (blue), Charles's stated origin (amber).

A meta-timeline. Charles Research — a multi-month transatlantic genealogical investigation into Charles Konzen (recorded in U.S. records as Gannsen / Johnson; 1835–1903), Hessian-born Civil War sergeant of Battery B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery, and his probable Trendelburg / Deisel origins in Churhessen (a working conclusion — the German baptism remains unrecovered).

      
Compiled by Jed Johnson with Peter Schräder and James Gunnis.
