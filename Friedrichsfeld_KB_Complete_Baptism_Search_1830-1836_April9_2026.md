## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

A **complete, page-by-page search** of the Friedrichsfeld Kirchenbuch baptism pages 1-17 (images 1326-1342) covering **October 1830 through May 1835** has been conducted. Every single entry was examined for the surname Konze/Kontze.

### Key Findings

**Three Konze baptism entries were found — and ONLY three:**

| # | Child | Born | Died | Page | Image |
|---|-------|------|------|------|-------|
| 1 | Heinrich Wilhelm Kontze | 12 Feb 1832 | 19 Feb 1858 | 5 | 1330 |
| 2 | Maria Sabina Kontze | 10 Jan 1834 | 18 Feb 1834 | 10 | 1335 |
| 3 | August Kontze | 15 Oct 1834 | 17 Oct 1834 | 13 | 1338 |

**CRITICAL NEGATIVE FINDING: There is NO Konze baptism entry for January 1835.** Page 15 (image 1340) covers "Jahr 1835, Monat Januar" and contains only one entry — Caroline Henriette Marie Dolfer. Charles Gannsen was NOT baptized in the Friedrichsfeld Kirchenbuch.

### Additional Discoveries
- Maria Sophia née Hofeditz was Andreas Kontze's **SECOND wife** (explicitly stated: "zweite Ehefrau")
- Both Maria Sabine and August died as infants (ages 39 days and 2 days respectively)
- A **separate Kontze connection** exists: Maria Magdalena née Kontze from Gelmanshausen married into the Schleiffer/Pfründer family at Friedrichsfeld
- **Johann Friedrich Kontze**, Schuhmachermeister in Gelmanshausen, served as godfather — described as "der Mutter Bruder" (the mother's brother)

## 4. RELATED KONTZE CONNECTIONS (Non-Andreas Family)

### Maria Magdalena née Kontze — Schleiffer/Pfründer Family

Two baptism entries on pages 5 and 10 involve a different Kontze connection:

**Entry #12 (Page 5, Image 1330):** Emilia Elmandine Philippina Charlotte Schleiffer
- Father: George Wilhelm Pfründer/Schleiffer, Schuhmacher (shoemaker)
- Mother: **Maria Magdalena geborne Kontze, von Gelmanshausen**

**Entry #29 (Page 10, Image 1335):** Johann Heinrich Schleiffer
- Father: George Wilhelm Schleiffer, Schuhmachermeister
- Mother: **Maria Magdalena geborne Kontze von Gelmanshausen**
- Godfather: **Johann Friedrich Kontze, Schuhmachermeister zu Gelmanshausen, Amt Liebenau, der Mutter Bruder** (the mother's brother)

**Significance:** This reveals a Kontze family branch in **Gelmanshausen** (near Trendelburg, in Amt Liebenau). Johann Friedrich Kontze was a master shoemaker there — he was the brother of Maria Magdalena. This is a DIFFERENT family branch from Andreas Kontze (Colonist from Trendelburg), but they may share a common ancestor.

## 5. CHRONOLOGICAL ANALYSIS — WHY CHARLES CANNOT BE FROM THIS FAMILY

### Timeline of Andreas Kontze's Known Children at Friedrichsfeld

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 12 Feb 1832 | Heinrich Wilhelm born |
| 10 Jan 1834 | Maria Sabina born |
| 18 Feb 1834 | Maria Sabina DIES (age 39 days) |
| 15 Oct 1834 | August born |
| 17 Oct 1834 | August DIES (age 2 days) |
| **26 Jan 1835** | **Charles Gannsen reportedly born — NO ENTRY FOUND** |

### Biological Impossibility

Maria Sophia Hofeditz gave birth to August on **October 15, 1834**. For her to give birth again on **January 26, 1835** — just **103 days later** — is **biologically impossible**. Even accounting for potential date errors of a few months, it would require an impossibly short inter-birth interval.

### Conclusions

1. **Charles Gannsen was NOT born to Andreas Kontze and Maria Sophia Hofeditz at Friedrichsfeld in January 1835.** The biological timeline and the absence of any baptism entry both confirm this.

2. **If Charles is connected to this family, the birth date of January 26, 1835 must be significantly wrong** — by at least 8-9 months (the next biologically possible birth would be no earlier than ~July 1835).

3. **The name index confirms only three Kontze entries** in the register for the entire 1830-1836 period. There are no missed or overlooked entries.

## 6. IMPLICATIONS FOR THE CHARLES GANNSEN INVESTIGATION

### Hypothesis Evaluation

**Hypothesis 1 — Justus Ernst Konze (b. Nov 14, 1829, Deisel) = Charles Gannsen:**
- STRENGTHENED. The absence of any January 1835 Konze birth at Friedrichsfeld makes the identification of Charles with an earlier-born Konze child more plausible. Justus Ernst was born at Deisel, which could be described as "Freundburg/Trendelburg area." His birth year (1829) is only 6 years off from the claimed 1835.

**Hypothesis 2 — Charles was an illegitimate child of an unmarried Konze woman:**
- WEAKENED for Friedrichsfeld. No such entry appears in the baptism register. However, illegitimate births might be recorded in a different parish if the mother was living elsewhere.

**Hypothesis 3 — Charles was a later legitimate child born after the family left Friedrichsfeld:**
- POSSIBLE. Andreas Konze was described as "verschollen" (disappeared) by 1844, and we know he died in Mainz on October 6, 1854. If the family left Friedrichsfeld after 1834, a child could have been born elsewhere — Trendelburg, Mainz, or another location.

**NEW Hypothesis 4 — Charles was from the Gelmanshausen Kontze branch:**
- The discovery of Johann Friedrich Kontze (Schuhmachermeister in Gelmanshausen) opens a new avenue. This is a previously unknown Kontze branch in the immediate Trendelburg area. A child from this family could also claim "Trendelburg" origins.

### Recommended Next Steps

1. **Search Deisel Kirchenbuch** for Justus Ernst Konze's baptism entry and any siblings
2. **Search Trendelburg civil/church records** for Konze entries 1835-1840 (the family may have returned there)
3. **Search Gelmanshausen church records** for the Johann Friedrich Kontze family
4. **Search Mainz civil records** for any Konze births 1835-1854 (period when Andreas was living there)
5. **Re-examine the 1844 Carl Wilhelm Konze entry** (image 1367) — born to "Adolphine Konze, daughter of the disappeared Andreas Konze" — for any additional clues about Andreas's movements

### Search Completeness
- Every baptism entry from page 1 (image 1326, Oct 1830) through page 17 (image 1342, May 1835) was individually examined
- Child names, parent names, and godparent names were all checked for Konze/Kontze variants
- The name index (located at the end of the baptism section) was previously confirmed to show Konze entries only on pages 5, 10, and 13 — this search independently verified that finding
