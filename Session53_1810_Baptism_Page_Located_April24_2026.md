# Session 53 — April 24, 2026
## 1810 Baptism Page Located; New Hillebrand-Konze Family Discovered; Thilin Confirmed as Deisel Surname

---

## Headline

Session 53 located the 1810 baptism page in Deisel KB 218824 at **Image 1685 (Jan–early/mid 1810) and Image 1686 left page (late 1810 through start of 1811)** — disproving the Session 52 estimate of Image ~1737 (which turned out to be 1827 baptisms). The 1810 page is in the older register adjacent to the 1796–1831 register on the same microfilm, near the 1809 baptisms found at Image 1683.

**Three significant findings during the 1810 scan:**

1. **A NEW Konze family appears in 1810:** Johann Christoph Konze × Anna Margaretha **geb. Hillebrand**, with daughter **Anna Catharina** baptized day 12 of a month in 1810 (Image 1685 right page). The mother's maiden name "Hillebrand" is the same surname (likely a spelling variant) as Wilhelmine geb. **Hildebrand** — Entry 195's mother. This implies a **second Konze-Hillebrand/Hildebrand couple** in Deisel, parallel to Joh. Heinrich Konze × Wilhelmine Hildebrand (Entry 195's parents).

2. **"Thilin" confirmed as a real Deisel surname.** Image 1685 left page shows another 1810 baptism — twins Johann David and Ferdinand, parents Andreas **Grebe** × Caroline geb. **Thilin**. The Thilin family was active in Deisel in 1810 with marriages into the Grebe family. This independently supports the Klaus Andreas Kontze × Catharina Elisabeth née Thilin record from 1799/1800.

3. **The Andreas Konze × Johannes b. 26 Oct 1810 baptism was NOT located in the initial scan.** Image 1686 left page (late 1810) was searched and the visible Konze/Andreas entries are: Charlotte (parents Schneider Johann Conrad — different family); Wilhelmine (parents Andreas **Köster** — different family, Köster not Konze); Ludwig (parents Johannes **Schildknecht** — different identity, "Johannes" not "Andreas"). A more careful page-by-page Kurrent reading is needed — or the entry may be in a less obvious portion of the page, or under a name variant.

### What was NOT found

- **The Andreas Konze × Johannes b. 26 Oct 1810 baptism.** No entry on Image 1685 right or Image 1686 left was clearly labeled with father "Andreas Konze" and child "Johannes." Multiple "Andreas X" entries exist on these pages (Andreas Grebe, Andreas Köster) but none read as Andreas Konze. The Schildknecht entry (Johannes Schildknecht × Anna Catharina Goebelin, child Ludwig) does NOT match either, since the father is Johannes (not Andreas) and the child is Ludwig (not Johannes).

**Hypotheses for the missing entry:**
1. **Misread surname.** "Andreas Köster" on Image 1686 left could conceivably be "Andreas Konze" with a Kurrent K-confusion (the same kind of D/K issue Session 25 corrected). A focused Jed-side read of that specific entry would settle it.
2. **Different image.** The October 26 baptism may be on Image 1686 right (1811 — but then he was born late 1810 with a delayed baptism into 1811) or potentially elsewhere.
3. **Different register.** Klaus Andreas may have moved his baptism to a neighboring parish in 1810. Worth checking Trendelburg or Friedrichsfeld registers for an October 1810 Konze baptism.
4. **Not yet baptized in this register.** If the child died young (we know he died in 1835 unmarried at 25), the baptism still should be recorded — but the entry could be in an unusual location.

## Connection to the broader Konze investigation

### The Hillebrand/Hildebrand axis (Major Session 53 finding)

Two Konze fathers in Deisel are now linked to the Hillebrand/Hildebrand family by marriage:

| Couple | Year | Source |
|--------|------|--------|
| Johann Christoph Konze × Anna Margaretha geb. **Hillebrand** | 1810 (Anna Catharina baptism, Image 1685) | NEW Session 53 |
| Joh. Heinrich Konze × Wilhelmine geb. **Hildebrand** | 1835 (Entry 195 baptism, Image 40 of 218842) | Session 25/26 |

The two surnames "Hillebrand" and "Hildebrand" are spelling variants of the same German name. This means:

- **Joh. Christoph Konze and Joh. Heinrich Konze are likely brothers** (or close cousins) who BOTH married into the Hillebrand/Hildebrand family.
- **Anna Margaretha Hillebrand (1810) and Wilhelmine Hildebrand (1835) are likely sisters** (or close relatives) — both Hillebrand/Hildebrand daughters who married Konze men.
- This creates a documented Konze-Hillebrand alliance across at least two generations (1810 children + 1835 child = same extended family tree).

### Joh. Christoph Konze appears with TWO wives in 1810–1811

- 1810: Joh. Christoph Konze × Anna Margaretha Hillebrand (daughter Anna Catharina)
- 1811: Joh. Christoph Konze × Maria Elisabeth Schrage (daughter Wilhelmine)

This implies Anna Margaretha Hillebrand died between Anna Catharina's 1810 birth and 1811. Joh. Christoph then quickly remarried Maria Elisabeth Schrage. Maria Elisabeth Schrage is the documented mother of Philipp Konze #1 (1849 marriage), confirming Joh. Christoph Konze as Philipp Konze #1's father.

This places Joh. Christoph Konze firmly in the Deisel Konze patriarchal line.

### Implication for Joh. Heinrich Konze, Kaufmann (Entry 195's father)

If Joh. Christoph and Joh. Heinrich were brothers, Joh. Heinrich is part of the same generational cohort as the man who married Anna Margaretha Hillebrand. This narrows the search for him: he should be findable in Deisel KB 218824 either as:
- A baptism child of one of the Andreas Konze patriarchs (likely 1780s–1790s)
- A marriage groom in the marriage section
- A confirmation candidate in the confirmation section

Given the surname pairing pattern (both Konze men married Hillebrand women in adjacent generations), Joh. Heinrich's marriage to Wilhelmine Hildebrand likely happened c. 1825–1834 in either Deisel or a neighboring parish.
