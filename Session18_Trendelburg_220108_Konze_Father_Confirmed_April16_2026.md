# Session 18 — April 16, 2026
## Register 220108 Trendelburg Taufen Konze Father-Surname Confirmed in Alphabetical Index; 220099 1828 Baptism Section Sampled; Jungkonze Trendelburg Pattern Retained; Seite-vs-Nro. Question Opened

## Headline

Session 18 delivers the **first definitively identified Konze father-surname baptism entry in Trendelburg's post-1831 Taufen register 220108**: the alphabetical surname index at option 270 (image 1645) records **"Konze, Johann Friedrich Wilhelm — 243"** in the K section, positioned between "Küchemann Joh. Heinr. Ludw. Aug. 241" and "Küchemann Karl Adolf 244". This confirms that at least one Konze-father-surname child was baptised in Trendelburg proper between 1830 and 1863, ending Session 17's inability to locate any Konze family heads in either Deisel or Trendelburg post-1831 registers.

Session 18 also opened a secondary structural question — whether the "243" in the index is a **Seite (page number)** or a **Nro. (running entry number)** — without definitively resolving it. A brief navigation to Nro. 243 on option 81 (image 1456) showed a different family (Johann Heinrich Küchnemüller, March 1840, died 16 März 1840), arguing that the index "243" must instead be a Seite page number pointing to a later portion of the register. This question is carried forward to Session 19 as the primary Move 1.

Register 220099 Kirchenbuch 1819-1831 was sampled for 1828 baptisms at options 27-30 (images 1256-1259), with the header "Getäufte im Jahr 1828" visible on multiple spreads. No obvious Konze father-surname or Konze mother-maiden-name entries were identified at subscriber zoom. The three previously confirmed 1828-1829 Konze-mother baptisms are therefore Deisel-register-only findings; Trendelburg's 1828 pages show different surname clusters (Martini, Dammermann, Dietholz, Hanger, Henze, Fischart, Lüchsing, Halmbrecht, Albrecht, Heitmann).

## Move 2 — Seite vs Nro. Interpretation Investigation (opened, not resolved)

Two hypotheses tested:

### Hypothesis A — Index "243" = Nro. (running entry number)

Tested by navigating to option 81 (image 1456). This option shows three entries numbered 243, 244, 245 in the Nro. column. Entry 243 reads:
- Wohnort: Trendelburg Nro. 81
- Geburt: [obscured date in early March]
- Tauf: 4ten März [likely 1840]
- Names des Kindes: männlich, "Johann Heinrich" + surname consistent with Küchnemüller/Küchenmüller (NOT Konze)
- Eltern: Christian Ludwig Küchenmüller (repeated surname) + wife Johanna Anna Christina geb. [Maasen?]
- Bemerkungen: "starb den 16. März 1840" (died 16 March 1840 — baby died ~12 days after baptism)

Conclusion: Nro. 243 is NOT the Konze baptism. Hypothesis A falsified.

### Hypothesis B — Index "243" = Seite (page number)

Tested partially. Navigation to option 22 (image 1397) showed a page spread with the right-page header legibly reading "Monat Februar/Dezember Seite 72". This demonstrates that Seite numbers DO exist and are printed on the pages themselves. The register uses both Nro. (running entry numbers in the first column) AND Seite (page numbers in the page header corners).

Relationship between Seite and option: Seite 72 is at option 22, suggesting Seite ≈ 3.3 × option, OR the baptism section starts later than option 3 and Seite numbering increments differently.

If Seite ≈ 3 × option is roughly linear, then Seite 243 ≈ option 73-77. This is near the option-81 range where Nro. 243-245 are visible — which could explain why the navigation confusion arose. **The Konze baptism at Seite 243 should be within a small range of options 73-83.**

**Deferred to Session 19**: Navigate to options 73-83 at maximum zoom and look at the right-page Seite-header of each spread to locate Seite 243 definitively, then read the single Konze entry on that page.

**No Konze mother or Konze father** observed on 1828 Trendelburg baptism pages at subscriber zoom.

### Implications

1. Trendelburg 1828 baptism cohort is distinct from Deisel 1828 — Deisel had three Konze-born mothers in 1828-1829, but Trendelburg shows a different surname cluster with NO Konze surname visible in the 1828 pages scanned.
2. The Konze-mother pattern (Maria Elisabeth, Wilhelmina, Carlotta geb. Konzen) is a **Deisel-specific phenomenon** in 1828-1829. If the same Konze family male line continues in Trendelburg 220108, the Trendelburg Konze-father entry (Johann Friedrich Wilhelm) is likely a **separate branch** that either never intermarried into Deisel or did so before 1828.
3. The Deisel vs Trendelburg separation of Konze surname lines strengthens the interpretive landscape: there may be **two distinct Konze branches** in the Kurhessen region circa 1800-1840 — a Deisel-resident female-to-male-out-of-surname branch, and a Trendelburg-resident male-line-continuing branch.

## Move 4 — Trendelburg 1829 Jungkonze Re-confirmation

While navigating 220099, confirmed the Session 17 finding that the **"Jungkonze"** compound surname appears in 1829 Trendelburg baptisms (option 38, image 1267). The 1829 year-end summary for Trendelburg is "Sa: 33 Kinder" (33 children), significantly smaller than Deisel's "Sa: 38 Kinder" for the same year — suggesting Trendelburg was slightly less populous or had a slightly smaller baptism catchment.

The Jungkonze entry (father "Ludwig Jungkonze, Schneider" = tailor) suggests the Konze root exists in multiple compound Kurhessen surnames (Jungkonze, possibly Altkonze, Klein-Konze, Groß-Konze pattern typical of rural Hessen naming). The Trendelburg Konze-father entry at 220108 Seite 243 may represent a later generation of the SAME family that spawned "Jungkonze" branch.
