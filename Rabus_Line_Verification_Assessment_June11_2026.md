## 2. The good news — the old generations check out surprisingly well

- Gen 2 Jakob Rabus is a documented historical figure: court preacher to Elector Ottheinrich, pastor of Monheim 1552, superintendent over 30+ parishes, † 8 Feb 1581 Monheim — matching Kugler's chart exactly ([Oberdeutsche Personendatenbank](https://oberdeutsche-personendatenbank.digitale-sammlungen.de/Datenbank/Rabus,_Jakob_(1522)), [Deutsche Biographie](https://www.deutsche-biographie.de/sfzR0114-4.html)).
- Kugler's treatment of the famous Catholic convert **Johann Jakob Rabus** (b. ~1545 Strasbourg, "disowned by his father," died as a priest in Straubing) matches the [ADB biography](https://www.deutsche-biographie.de/sfz75458.html) in detail, including placing him correctly as **Ludwig's** son — i.e., Kugler knew the scholarship and didn't graft your line onto the celebrity branch carelessly.
- Gen 3 rests on a quoted **1583 visitation protocol** ("M. Jakobus Rabus, the previous pastor M. Jakob Rabus's son, is 22 years old…") — an unusually strong source for a 16th-century link. Worth re-finding the original protocol citation.
- Kugler was honest about what he didn't know: on page 2 of his chart, **generations 8–12 are all marked "wann? wo? Beruf?"** — question marks. He never established the Bavaria→Gotha bridge.

## 3. The weak links (in priority order)

1. **Gen 9 — the Nuremberg→Gotha jump (Johann Wolfgang Martin Rabus, KZFV-DRQ).** This is the single load-bearing link for the whole Bavarian line, and it's exactly the part Kugler left blank — someone filled it into FS later. What proves that the J.W.M. Rabus who married in Gotha 1801 and died there 1850 is the boy baptized 17 Jun 1778 in Nuremberg? Verify via: his Gotha death entry (1850 — should state age/birthplace), the 1801 marriage entry (should name father), and Gotha *Bürgerbuch*/residence records. If any of these say "born Nuremberg, son of Johann Adam," the line holds. **Until then, treat gens 1–8 as unproven for your ancestry.**
2. **Gen 8 — infant-death ambiguity.** Memory #42 is titled "Rabus, Johann Adam **B and D 1743**" — if a Johann Adam was born *and died* 1743, the ancestor may be a misattached infant, or a later same-named brother. Read that extract carefully against memory #74 ("Johann Adam D 1808, LHX9-6PP") and the Bergen register; also confirm the 1775 Nuremberg marriage names a father in Bergen.
3. **Gen 6 — marriage discrepancy.** Kugler: ∞ 31 May 1697; FS tree: "about 1704" with son Mathias born 1700 — internally inconsistent. Also FS gives this Philipp a birthplace of **"Zwickau, Saxony"** — almost certainly a botched place-standardization of *Reuth*; Kugler says b. 17 Mar 1674 Reuth. Fix on FS.
4. **Wrong Walting in FS.** Gens 3–4 are standardized to "Walting, Weiding, **Cham**" (Oberpfalz, ~150 km away). The correct place is **Walting bei Pleinfeld** (Lkr. Weißenburg-Gunzenhausen), the parish lost to the Counter-Reformation — which is *why* the family relocated to Protestant Enhofen/Ettenstatt. Fix the place names on L9CX-KNW and LC2Q-TBY.
5. **Possible duplicate of Philipp 1606 in the sync.** L9CX-KNW appears twice in your GEDCOM with two different parent families (F793 and F800) — check on FS whether he has two sets of parents attached, or whether this is a sync loop.
6. **Minor date conflicts to settle from the register:** Mathias's marriage 5 Jun vs 4 Jul 1729; gen-3 Jakob b. 1559/1560/1561.

**New findings from reading the originals:**
- **Baptism date is ☉ (Sunday) 12 July 1778, not 17 July.** The 1957 certified Abschrift's "17. Juli" appears to be a Kurrent misreading of "12": the page's entries run in order 8, 10, 12, 14, 15, 18, and the weekday symbol ☉ (Sunday) fits 12 July 1778 (17 July was a Friday ♀). The tree's christening date was corrected on FS to **12 July 1778** and the birth date to **12 June 1778** (June 12, 2026), each with a reason statement citing the records. On re-reading, the 1957 Abschrift records ONLY the Tauftag ("Tauftag ☉ d. 17. Juli" — it even copies the ☉ Sunday symbol while typing 17, contradicting itself), so the old birth date 17 June had no documentary basis. The burial entry's exact age (71 Jahre 8 Monate before 12 Feb 1850) computes to 12 June 1778 — the same Kurrent 12/17 confusion. Caveat noted in the FS reason: a one-month birth-to-baptism gap is unusual; the birth day should be treated with caution until a record states it explicitly.
- **Cause of death deciphered: "Schlagfluß" (stroke/apoplexy), physician Dr. Blau** — closes the open item in §9.
