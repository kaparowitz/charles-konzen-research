## Analysis and Interpretation

### The J.H. Konze #2 Question

Two male Konze entries exist in the burial index. The key question is whether either represents Johann Heinrich Konze #2 (married to Wilhelmine Hildebrand, father of Charles Gannsen's Entry 195).

**"Konze, Joannes — Seite 358"** is the strongest candidate:
- "Joannes" is the Latin/ecclesiastical form of Johann/Johannes
- German church index clerks frequently abbreviated or Latinized names
- The index typically records only the first given name — "Johann Heinrich" would appear as just "Joannes"
- To confirm, the actual burial entry on Seite 358 must be located and read for full name, death date, occupation, and family details

**"Konze [no first name] — Seite 380"** cannot be ruled out:
- The absence of a first name is unusual and could indicate the entry was abbreviated
- Seite 380 is a higher page number than 358, suggesting a later death
- Could be any male Konze

### The Wilhelmine Konze Question

**"Konze, Wilhelmine — Seite 461"** is potentially the death record of Wilhelmine geb. Hildebrand:
- The high Seite number (461) suggests she died later in the register's timeframe (closer to 1876)
- As the wife of J.H. Konze, she would be listed under "Konze" in the index
- This would be a separate entry from "Konze, Maria Wilhelmine — Seite 328" (which is either a different person or has an additional given name "Maria")

### Konze Family Burial Network

The burial index reveals a substantial Konze presence in Deisel's death records:
- **2 male** deaths
- **5 female** deaths under the Konze surname
- **1 female** death recorded under married name (Kuhlmann) with Konze as maiden name
- **Total: 8 Konze-connected burials** in Deisel 1830–1876

This is consistent with the Konze family being a well-established Deisel family with multiple branches, as documented in the baptism register scan (Session 36).
