## 8. Immigration check — Justus Ernst Konze (21 June)

Tested whether Justus Ernst emigrated (the alternative to a German death). **Negative across every indexed source:**
- **US passenger lists (today's pass):** Konze surname, all arrivals 1850–1866 (108 hits) = entirely Kunze/Kunz/McKenzie/Rhineland-Catholic Konzen; **no Hessian Konze, no Justus/Ernst Konze.** Confirms/extends the project's earlier exhaustive manifest work (NY collection 7488 etc. searched under Gannsen/Gonnson + Konze/Konsen + Justus/Ludwig/Lorenz — no match).
- **Mainz Emigration Register 1856–77:** no Konze from the Trendelburg/Hofgeismar area (project, 8 Apr 2026).
- **HESAUS / "Hessische Auswanderer" (LAGIS) — the Kurhessen official emigrant index:** searched in full 25 May 2026 under all Konze variants → **no match**; the *only* Konze hit is **Ludwig Konze, Deisel, 1864 → Frankfurt/Main** (= the Ludwig b.1836 of this reconciliation; he relocated within Germany, did not emigrate to the US). The Best. 16 "Periodische Übersichten" summary emigration registers (1856–61) are name-indexed in HESAUS and thus already covered. **Re-verified live 21 Jun 2026 (lagis-hessen.de):** `Konze` = 2 records, both the same **Ludwig Konze, Deisel, 1864 → Frankfurt/Main** (HStAM Best. 180 Hofgeismar Nr. 841, Bl. 49, *Entlassung aus dem Staatsverband*); `Kontze` = 0; `Gansen` = 0. No Justus Ernst, no US-bound Konze, no Gannsen/Gonnson variant.

**Conclusion:** Justus Ernst Konze has **no traceable emigration record** anywhere indexed — just as he has no German death record. The two remaining, un-searched possibilities both sit **offline**:
1. **He emigrated without an officially-indexed record** — e.g., unauthorized emigration / military-service evasion (a real pattern for young Kurhessen men, and explicitly flagged in the drafted Marburg inquiry). Such a case would appear only in **HStAM Best. 180 Hofgeismar** district case-files (release-from-citizenship / Steckbrief records), **not** in HESAUS — the target of the project's drafted-but-unsent paid Marburg archive request (`Marburg_HStAM_Auswanderer_Query_May25_2026.md`).
2. **He died in Germany pre-1876** — most likely a **Stammen** burial (Archion 220072, no name index), where two siblings died (1858, 1865).

So the documentary state for Justus Ernst is symmetrical: **no German death AND no emigration record** — exactly what you'd expect for someone who either died young in an unindexed Stammen burial, or emigrated quietly under a new surname (→ Charles). The two decisive, still-open tests are both offline: (a) **Stammen Beerdigungen 220072** page-by-page (Archion/Peter/Jim); (b) **send the drafted HStAM Best. 180 Hofgeismar inquiry** (Marburg) for an individual emigration/release file.
