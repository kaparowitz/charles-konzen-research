## 2. P1 — "Kontze Maria" on page 397 (penance register, image 915)

**Finding:** Maria Christina Kontze appears in a **public-penance** entry dated **12 January 1748** tied to her upcoming marriage to **David Kerstens** (Perstens/Herstens — surname K/P/H ambiguous in the hand). The entry is under the standard *"Nomina publice deprecantium"* heading.

**Genealogical meaning.** In 18th-century Lutheran practice, a woman appearing in the *publica poenitentia* register immediately before her marriage almost always indicates **premarital pregnancy (fornicatio ante matrimonium)**: the couple was required to do public penance (*Kirchenbuße*) at the altar before being married. So Maria Christina Kontze and David Kerstens were married in or around January 1748, with an expected child.

**Open questions:**
- Who was Maria Christina's father? She is of Kontze-female line; she could be a daughter of Andreas Kontze Jr. (Gen 1) or of Andreas Kontze × Eva Rosina Engel (Gen 2 marriage found Session 7, img 860).
- A corresponding marriage entry for Kerstens × Kontze should exist in the 1748 marriage register (pages ~357–358, approximate image 879–881). This is a **new Session 9 priority**.

## 3. P2 — Marriage register pages 363, 373, 374 (Jungkontze / Kontze)

### 3.1 Page 374 (image 898 right) — TWO Kontze marriages in 1763

Header reads *"Copulati 1763"* with the August entries visible.

**Entry dated 5 August 1763:**
> *"copulirt der ledige Gesell und gewesener Sergeanten/Bader, des verstorbenen Meisters **Joan Henrich Kontzers** nachgelassener Sohn — **Philip [Kontze]** — mit **Martha Elisabeth Steidin**, des Accis-Schreibers **Joannes Steids** Tochter."*

**Decoded:**
- **Groom:** Philip Kontze, bachelor, Sergeant / former barber (*Bader*), "surviving son of the deceased Master Johann Henrich Kontzer."
- **Bride:** Martha Elisabeth Steidin, daughter of Joannes Steid, excise-scribe (*Accis-Schreiber*).

**Genealogical significance — MAJOR.** This establishes **Johann Henrich Kontzer** (Session 6's "Joan Henrif" Gen-0 candidate) as the **father of Philip Kontze**, and dates his death to BEFORE August 1763. This is the first direct filiation tying the Joan Henrich line into a specific son. Philip is almost certainly the "Sergeanten Philip Kontze" seen elsewhere (Kontze military service appears to be a recurring pattern — cf. the soldier-Kontze witnesses of the 1740s baptisms).

A second Kontze entry appears on the same page (visible mid-page, partial read) — ongoing.
