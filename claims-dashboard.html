Identity — who Charles was
The American-life identity spine: the 1862 enlistee “Charles Gannsen” ≡ the 1875 baptism informant ≡ the 1903 decedent “Charles Johnson” — one man across the U.S. record set. This is the part of the identity equation carried by a continuous American documentary chain. It does not by itself name the German-side surname or origin — those are the separate Working claim below. Confirmed Pension file F41-523680269E + St. Marcus parish registers + 1900 census (continuous documentary chain). See Bibliography › Military & pension. A documented second “Charles Gannsen” of military age in 1862 St. Louis — none surfaced in NARA, Fold3, or Missouri-state indexing.
…and that same American man is identified as Charles Konzen of the Trendelburg-complex Konze family. The German half of the identity equation. Because it depends on the surname conclusion and the Trendelburg/Deisel origin — both held Working below — the equation to Konzen can be no stronger than its weakest link, and is stated as Working rather than folded into the Confirmed American spine above. Working Convergent indirect evidence: the surname trail (Card 1), the “Freundburg”/“Deisse” origin decodings (Cards 2–3), and the Konze-pool parish concentration. No German baptism naming Charles has been recovered. DNA testing (Y-DNA + autosomal) is consistent with a Hessian origin but has produced no Konze-line match; the Y-line's closest matches are predominantly Ashkenazi lineages, raising a deep-paternal-origin question. See also negative-results audit. A Hessian baptism naming Charles in a Konze / Kontzen family, or a Y-DNA / autosomal match to a documented Konze descendant (see Open Questions · DNA), would promote this to Confirmed.
Birth ~26 January 1835, in the Kurhessen / Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis territory. Day and month from the parish death-register burial entry; the year is reported inconsistently across the records — the 1880 census gives age 39 (→ b. 1841), the 1900 census b. Nov 1836, and Charles's pension declarations ages 53–56 (→ 1834–1838); only the burial register lands on 1835. Informant caution: these records are independent documents but not independent information — the age/birth data on the censuses, pension, and burial all trace to Charles himself or his household, so they corroborate consistency of his own testimony rather than confirm the year from a second source. Working St. Marcus burial register #3393 (26 Jan 1835; age 68 at death; informant the family); 1900 U.S. Census (b. Nov 1836; household informant); 1880 U.S. Census (age 39 → b. 1841); pension-file declarations (Charles, ages 53–56). See Doc A–O crosswalk and the full record-by-record comparison, birth date across the U.S. records. (Census figures FamilySearch-verified against profile LZXC-6GB, 19 June 2026: 1880 indexed age 39 / est. 1841; 1900 indexed birth Nov 1836.) A Hessian baptism entry naming Charles directly with a date — an independent source rather than Charles's own restated testimony — would promote the claim to Confirmed. None recovered; see negative-results audit.
Surname is concluded as KONZEN, the Kontzen-family form documented in the Trendelburg parish complex. The conclusion replaces the earlier “Konze line” hypothesis (retracted 4 May 2026 after Peter Schräder's verdict on Entry 195). Working Nine distinct spellings across 14 U.S. records (1862–1896) by 5+ scribes — two signed by Charles himself (enlistment, pension) — plus all seven children's baptisms. See Surname across the records and Card 1. A Hessian baptism record naming Charles in a Konze / Kontzen / Contze family. Would promote to Confirmed. See errata · 25 May 2026 rigor pass.
Geography — where Charles came from
Parish of origin is TRENDELBURG (Kreis Hofgeismar, Kurfürstentum Hessen). Decoded from “Freundburg” on the 1862 enlistment register via clerk's-ear phonetic transposition (Trend → Friend → Freund). Working Card 2 · 1862 enlistment register (“Freundburg, Kurhessen”); independently anchored by the 1866 St. Marcus marriage register “Deisse, Churhessen.” A different decoding of “Freundburg,” or a baptism naming Charles outside Trendelburg. The Trendelburg reading is an interpretation, not a recorded fact — competing readings are ranked on Origin across the records (AncestryPro's Freudenberg/Schierstein and Dies/Diez eliminated; James Gunnis's Dissen open).
Village of origin is DEISEL (a village inside the Trendelburg parish). Independent attestation from the 1866 St. Louis marriage register (“Deisse, Churhessen”), four years after the 1862 “Freundburg” attestation. Working Card 3 · St. Marcus marriage register 1866. A different decoding of “Deisse.” Three independent researchers (Blumenstein, Schräder, AncestryProGenealogists) read it as Deisel; James Gunnis's Dissen / Grifte remains an open alternative (interpretations). A baptism naming Charles in any adjacent parish would also revise it.
No baptism-register record naming Charles in the Trendelburg-parish Konze family lines has been recovered. The German baptism entry — the would-be primary source for parents and exact birth date — remains the central unrecovered document. A June 2026 FamilySearch-tree reconciliation narrowed the field by negative evidence: of every documented Konze male of Charles's generation, all but one stayed and died in the parish complex (incl. the same-named Carl Wilhelm Konze, b. 1831, eliminated by his death at Trendelburg 30 Oct 1869). The sole male leaving no adult German footprint is Justus Ernst Konze (b. 14 Nov 1829) — but at ~5–6 years older than Charles's stated 1835 birth he falls outside the birth window, and an exhaustive emigration search found no record of him either, so he is a low-likelihood lead, not a confirmed identity. Open Documented negative searches across 36 parishes and 16 candidate families (incl. Peter Schräder's repeated database queries and a Jewish-line check); the June 2026 widened confirmation-register sweep read the Hofgeismar classes for any Carl b. 1833–1841, surname open, either confession — several local Carls, but none Konze / *n-s-n; and the FamilySearch-tree reconciliation showing Justus Ernst Konze absent from every searchable German death and emigration index. See research completed at a glance, candidate families, and parish search coverage. Two decisive offline tests now lead: a page-by-page read of the un-indexed Stammen burial register (Archion 220072, ~1843–1875, where two of Justus Ernst's siblings died) and an individual emigration / release-from-citizenship file in HStAM Bestand 180 Hofgeismar. Behind them, the surface review of Heisebeck (on-site only), the 1855 Stammrolle (HStAM Bestand 4h), and the 1859 Auswanderer-Beleg. See open questions.
American life — service, family, death
Emigrated to the U.S. in 1859, settling in St. Louis. 1900 census “Year of immigration: 1859”; the 1866 marriage register's “in St. Louis seven years” points to the same year. Both, however, are Charles's own reported statements — one informant, two records — and the crossing itself is undocumented. Working 1900 U.S. Federal Census (Peter Kuhn household, 170 Sidney St.); 1866 St. Marcus marriage register residency statement. No passenger manifest or Auswanderer-Beleg recovered — see Immigration Journey. A passenger-manifest entry or an Auswanderer-Beleg (HStAM Marburg) attesting the year independently of Charles's own testimony — would promote to Confirmed. Neither surfaced to date.
Civil War service: Battery B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery (Union), 2 Dec 1862 – 20 Dec 1865; highest rank Sergeant (reduced to Private 15 Mar 1865). The strongest single chain of records on the American side: enlistment, muster rolls, Compiled Service Record, pension file all attest to the same regiment and dates. Confirmed Compiled Service Record G11-422819443E; Pension File F41-523680269E; Fold3 muster rolls. See Civil War tab. No realistic scenario; the documentary chain is multi-source and internally consistent.
Married Auguste Kreichelt at St. Marcus German Evangelical Church, St. Louis, on 7 January 1866. Seven children, two surviving descendant lines. Confirmed St. Marcus marriage register entry (1866); imaged in Card 3 · Key Evidence. No realistic scenario.
Died 16 December 1903, St. Louis · stomach cancer · age 68. The parish burial register (Doc N) is the canonical source; the death certificate (Doc A) reads 17 December — a known 1-day discrepancy logged in errata. Working St. Marcus parish burial register #3393. See Doc A–O crosswalk for the 1-day source disagreement. Either a re-reading of Doc A or new evidence preferring 17 Dec. The 1-day variance is recorded, not resolved. See errata · 17 May 2026 death/burial.
No documented Charles-side blood relative appears among his children's baptism sponsors or his 1866 wedding witnesses — corroborating that he immigrated alone. Baptismal sponsorship was a kin-default convention; across 24 sponsor/witness slots (the seven baptisms 1866–1881 plus the 1866 marriage — itemised on the Sponsor reconciliation), every identified kin traces to Auguste's side. AncestryProGenealogists (2020) opened this thread; the 2026 audit executed it. Working St. Marcus baptism & marriage registers; each sponsor/witness cross-walked to Auguste's kin or the St. Louis / Battery B circle. High confidence none is Charles's blood kin; the “immigrated alone” inference is Working. See Sponsor reconciliation. Identification of any sponsor or witness as a Charles-side blood relative, or a German baptism / manifest revealing kin who joined him in America.
Naming evidence — the surname trail
The 1875 baptism rendering “Konsen” (Pastor Hoffmann) preserves the homeland-style K + -en suffix. One of two -en-preserving forms (the other being Auguste's 1890 death record “Konzen”); together they bracket the Charles-as-informant span. Confirmed St. Marcus baptism register, Erwin Carl Konsen, 27 August 1875; imaged in Erwin Carl Konsen profile. No realistic scenario; the entry is unambiguous and imaged.
1890 pension-declaration co-witness “John Heitzenberger” is a member of the Pennsylvania–Maryland Hitzelberger family; specific individual identity remains open. The clerk's reading “Heitzenberger” is a misreading of the surname Hitzelberger (L → N substitution). Two competing readings remain for the specific individual: A. J. Hitzelberger himself, or a brother / brother-in-law. Working Pension File F41-523680269E · 1890 detailed declaration; surname-family identification anchored by three censuses (1860 CA, 1900 MO, 1880 CA). See people · John Heitzenberger. A death record for A. J. Hitzelberger in California 1860s-70s would shift the identification toward a brother / brother-in-law as the actual 1890 witness.
The firstborn “Friedrich Wilhelm” (20 June 1866) carries an evocative overlap with the deposed Elector of Hesse, Friedrich Wilhelm I — read here as context, not proof of origin. The parsimonious explanation is on the record: the child was named for his godfather, Friedrich Wilhelm Pillmann, per ordinary Lutheran naming convention. That the name also matched the Elector's — in the very year Hesse-Kassel fell to Prussia — is a genuinely suggestive coincidence and possibly a deliberate gesture, but it cannot be settled from the record. See the two competing readings on the child's profile. Interpretive St. Marcus baptism register 1866 (godfather Friedrich Wilhelm Pillmann); period-context analysis. See Friedrich Wilhelm profile for Readings A and B. Nothing promotes an interpretation of motive to a fact. Direct testimony from Charles about why he chose the name would settle it; absent that, the page presents both readings and prefers the parsimonious one.
Methodology — disentanglements and frame decisions
The five (probably six) men named Andreas Kon(t)ze in the Trendelburg and Deisel parish records 1718–1830 are separable identities; none is Charles's father. Round-2 narrative section walks through the burial-register age statements, marriage-entry spouse names, and grandfather attribution at baptism that distinguish each Andreas. The framework was corroborated against the FamilySearch shared Family Tree in June 2026. Of the Konze males of Charles's generation, only Justus Ernst (b. 14 Nov 1829, son of Andreas 1795 × Hofeditz) now leaves no provable German death or emigration — the cousin who briefly shared the lead, Carl Wilhelm Konze (b. 1831), was eliminated (d. 1869 Trendelburg). Flagged as a low-likelihood candidate — he is ~5–6 years older than Charles's stated 1835 birth (outside the window) and no emigration record was found for him — not promoted. Confirmed Peter Schräder's correction of 3–5 April 2026; Archion 220096 / 220114 / 220120 / 218842 burial & marriage registers. Walked through at germany-and-origins · Andreas Konze disentanglement. A previously unread parish entry naming a sixth Andreas in the same period, or a baptism record fitting Charles's January 1835 birth window inside one of these five Andreas's documented households.
The 1890 pension-declaration co-witness “John Heitzenberger” / Hitzelberger reframe is a working surname-family identification, not a confirmed individual identity. The audit-trail entry that records the reframe was missing until 27 May 2026; the new errata entry closes the loop between live claim and promotion history. Working Pension File F41-523680269E + 1860 / 1880 / 1900 censuses. Promotion logged at errata · 3 May 2026. A documented California death record for A. J. Hitzelberger 1860s–1870s would shift the identification toward a brother / brother-in-law as the actual 1890 St. Louis witness.

How this page is kept honest

The dashboard is not a separate source. Each row's claim wording matches the canonical phrasing on its evidence page; if a claim is recalibrated, the errata entry is the authoritative record of the change-from / change-to and this page is updated to match. The pre-commit grep harness (scripts/check_canon.sh) audits the most-shared surfaces — meta descriptions, Open-Graph cards, JSON-LD, alt text, SVG <text> — to keep canonical phrasing aligned across the site. The primary-source apparatus is checked the same way: a full Archion citation audit on 21 June 2026 re-opened every cited register in the viewer and confirmed each Seite/image locator against the original manuscript page — all 19 registers with a checkable anchor validated, zero genuine citation errors.
