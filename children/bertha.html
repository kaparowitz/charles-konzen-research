Bertha Jansen

Seventh-born and youngest child · also recorded on FamilySearch as "Bertha Johnson"
Born 25 December 1880 · Died 1959 · lived to 78  (see errata · 17 May 2026 for 1879→1880 correction)

★ At a Glance

CHILD 7 of 7 · YOUNGEST · LATER "BERTHA JOHNSON" FS KGM3-RDK

Charles and Auguste's seventh and youngest child, born Christmas Day 1880. She lived to seventy-eight, married into the Johnson family, and outlived all of her siblings.

Bertha Jansen (b. 25 December 1880, d. 1959) was the seventh and youngest child of Charles & Auguste — later known as Bertha Johnson. Charles's 1898 pension declaration lists her as "Bertha Gannson, born 20 December 1880" (5-day pension drift from the church record). Christened 4 September 1881 at St. Marcus with the sole named sponsor Bertha Barbara "Babette" Kreichelt (her aunt and namesake). Surname rendered Jansen — the J-form persists into the final baptism, anchoring the eventual "Johnson" Anglicization.

Why this matters for understanding Charles: The 1880 baptism is the terminal entry in the seven-baptism series and the latest documented "Jansen" rendering before the family fully Anglicized to Johnson. The pension declaration's "Bertha Gannson" spelling shows that even by 1898 — twenty years later — Charles himself or his pension-clerk still used a German-form variant. The Jansen → Johnson resolution happens entirely in the post-1881 American civil-registration record.

Baptism record & sponsors

St. Marcus Baptism Doc L — page 574, entry no. 10269  ★ ANGLICIZATION-BRIDGE ENTRY

Name: Bertha Jansen  — the J-initial form recorded by Pastor Johannes Nollau is the project's documented Anglicization-bridge: Jansen → Johnson requires only a vowel-shift and the -en→-on swap to become the family's eventual American name. This 1881 entry is the textual inflection point at which the German-Hessian form began to give way to the American "Johnson" — but even at the inflection, the -en suffix is still preserved.
Born: 25 December 1880
Baptized: 4 September 1881
Parents: Karl Jansen and Auguste Kreichelt
Pastor / Scribe: Pastor Johannes Nollau (St. Marcus Dec 1877 – May 1885)

Baptism sponsors

  • SponsorBabette Kreichelt — Auguste's youngest sister Bertha Barbara "Babette" Kreichelt, then age 20. The infant Bertha Johnson is named directly after her aunt Bertha Barbara — the third of three Gannsen children with a single-named-sponsor namesake (alongside Katherine Carolina ← Catharina Albrecht in 1870 and Erwin Carl Konsen ← Erwin Spraul in 1875). Babette had stood as junior co-sponsor at the 1873 Louise baptism alongside her mother (recorded then as "Barbara Kreichelt"); now in 1880 she is the single named sponsor. She would die at age 32 in St. James, Phelps County, Missouri (1892), buried at Holy Ghost Cemetery in St. Louis. The "Barbara/Babette = Bertha Barbara" reconciliation was confirmed via the FamilySearch tree person L5FH-BRP's documented Alternate Name "Babette" (May 2026).  ·  FamilySearch L5FH-BRP

Bertha is the only child of the seven with a single named sponsor. The simplification of the sponsor pool from three or four sponsors in the 1860s and early 1870s to just one in 1880 reflects the contraction of the Kreichelt-Battery-B network: Auguste's brother August had died in 1877 (age 32), her father Georg's health was declining (he would die three years later in 1883), and the broader family social orbit was shifting away from the deep St. Marcus involvement of the early years. From the 2020 research journal, Doc L; sponsor identity resolved by Jed × Peter Schräder, December 2025 + May 2026 FamilySearch tree confirmation.

Confirmation record (1894) ✓ Confirmed primary source

Bertha was confirmed at St. Marcus Evangelical Church on Palm Sunday, 18 March 1894, taking first communion at Easter, 25 March 1894. She is recorded in the daughters' (Töchter) list as entry 16, under the family's Anglicized surname “Bertha Johnson” — the same Johnson form she carried for the rest of her life (FamilySearch KGM3-RDK). This is an independently located primary record, found in a live read of the register on 13 June 2026; it is not a change to any canonical fact — it adds her confirmation and supplies a second, conflicting reading of her birth year (see the note below).

⚠ Birth-year conflict (footnote). The confirmation register records Bertha's birth as 20 December 1879. The day (20 Dec) matches Charles's 1898 pension declaration exactly (“Bertha Gannson, born 20 December 1880”); the year (1879) conflicts with the canonical baptism record, which gives 25 December 1880 (St. Marcus Baptism p. 574, no. 10269). The canonical birth date remains 25 December 1880 — sourced to the contemporaneous baptism entry. The confirmation is logged here as an independent attestation that happens to carry the older “1879” reading the project corrected to 1880 (see errata · 17 May 2026); it does not overturn the baptism-sourced canon.

Located 13 June 2026 in a live signed-in read of the St. Marcus confirmation register; full context in Session99_StMarcus_Registers_Bertha_Confirmation_June13_2026.md. Note: a separate Bertha Jansen (b. 30 June 1879) confirmed in the 1893 class is an unrelated Janson family, not this Bertha.

📂 Surname-spelling research note — additional surname-drift detail

Surname-spelling note

The drift sequence: Gonnson (1866) → Gonnson (1868) → Gansen (1870) → Gonsen (1872) → Jansen (1874) → Konsen (1875) → Jansen (1881). Bertha's baptism returns to the Jansen spelling — the same surname her older sister Louise was baptized under in 1874. Across the seven baptisms, the parish clerks fell back into the J-leaning forms more often than the K-leaning ones, which is why the 1875 Konsen entry stands out as anomalous.
