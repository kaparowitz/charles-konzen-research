Erwin Carl Konsen ★

Sixth-born child · later "Charles Johnson Jr." · the only baptism that preserved the Hesse-style surname Konsen
Born 27 August 1875 · Died 1950 · lived to 74

★ At a Glance

CHILD 6 of 7 · LATER "CHARLES JOHNSON JR." · ★ KEY EVIDENCE FS KL18-V2Y

Charles and Auguste's sixth child and the one who, as an adult, carried his father's name into the next generation as Charles Johnson Jr. He lived to seventy-four and married Katherine Misch. His 1875 baptism is the keystone phonetic evidence record for the surname investigation: the parish clerk wrote his surname as Konsen — the closest American phonetic capture of the German Hessian spelling found anywhere on the U.S. record trail, and the phonetic-s sibling of his sister Auguste's 1890 death record reading Konzen (the verbatim Hesse-parish form and the closest American match overall).

Erwin Carl Konsen (b. 27 August 1875, d. 1950 at age 74) was the sixth-born child of Charles & Auguste — later known as Charles Johnson Jr. Christened 3 October 1875 at St. Marcus with the sole named sponsor Erwin Spraul (Anheuser-Busch Superintendent), with Charles Sr. listed as listed co-sponsor — the only Gannsen baptism with no Kreichelt-side sponsor at all. Surname rendered Konsen — the unique Hesse-style spelling that breaks the J-form / G-form drift pattern of the other six baptisms.

Why this matters for understanding Charles: This baptism is the strongest American phonetic surname-preservation evidence in the project, paired with sister Auguste's 1890 death record (the verbatim Konzen entry) as the project's strongest current surname-preservation evidence (the 1890 Konzen reading was itself revised across May 2026 re-examinations — see errata). The "Konsen" spelling is the only one of the seven baptisms that ties Charles back to the Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis Konze families in Hesse by sound — together with the 1890 Konzen they are the strongest pair tying the U.S. record to the Trendelburg / Deisel parish-register hypothesis. The 1875 entry is uniquely "German-side-tilted" — sole non-Charles-side sponsor, Hesse-style surname, father-as-co-sponsor — reading as Charles's deliberate identity statement at this particular baptism.

★ Why this baptism matters

Among the seven children, this one entry is unique. The St. Marcus parish clerk recorded the surname as Konsen — the only Hesse-style spelling of the family name in the entire American record. Every other St. Marcus baptism uses an Anglicised or J/G drift form (Gonnson, Gansen, Gonsen, Jansen). Only this one preserves the German Kons-/Konz- root that ties Charles back to the Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis Konze families.

This is the strongest surname-preservation evidence in the project, tying the U.S. record to the parish-register hypothesis (held ★ Working pending the still-unrecovered German baptism). It is the reason the Konze line in Trendelburg / Deisel has been worked so intensively.

Baptism record & sponsors

St. Marcus Baptism Doc K — page 453, entry no. 8854

Name: Erwin Carl Konsen
Born: 27 August 1875
Baptized: 3 October 1875
Parents: Carl Konsen and Auguste Kreichelt

Baptism sponsors

  • SponsorErwin Spraul — Superintendent of the Anheuser-Busch Brewing Association per his June 1889 obituary — the most senior trade title at one of St. Louis's largest German-immigrant employers. Born ~1848 Baden, Germany (per the obituary; FS tree currently records 1841 — a 7-year discrepancy the obituary likely resolves). Married Anna Maria Grassner 12 November 1868 St. Louis (one son Frank b. 1869); lived at 1340 S 9th Street in Soulard / Frenchtown, walking distance from the brewery and ~2 miles from the Gannsen home; died 19 June 1889 aged 41 of post-operative complications from a shoulder abscess. Predeceased Charles by 14 years. The infant Erwin Carl Konsen is named directly after him following the standard Lutheran namesake-godfather custom — the third of three Gannsen children with a single-named-sponsor namesake (alongside Katherine Carolina ← Catharina Albrecht in 1870 and Bertha ← Bertha Barbara "Babette" Kreichelt in 1880). Per Jed × Peter Schräder, December 2025: Charles chose Spraul "because he was a successful fellow immigrant" — sharpened by the 1889 obituary to "successful at the very top of the brewing trade." The Spraul surname appears in German records only post-WWII per Peter, so Spraul himself is not a useful German-origin lead. Multi-generational Anheuser-Busch connection: this child's own son — Charles Sr.'s grandson — Frederick Charles Johnson (1905–1947) · KLBX-VR1 later worked as a Bottler at Anheuser Busch, sustaining the family-Anheuser-Busch employment chain across two generations.  ·  FamilySearch KLP1-N62
  • SponsorThe father of the child — i.e. Charles Gannsen Sr. himself, listed by the parish clerk as a co-sponsor. This is unusual: the father standing as godfather to his own child violates standard Lutheran practice. Two competing readings:
    1. The parish clerk's shorthand for "no second sponsor present" — Erwin Spraul stood alone, and the entry is filled out with the father's name as a placeholder.
    2. A deliberate choice by Charles to mark the child as the carrier of his Hessian surname identity — the only Gannsen baptism with the spelling Konsen. The "father as co-sponsor" pattern reads as Charles personally guaranteeing the surname continuity.
    Either way, this is the only Gannsen baptism with no maternal-Kreichelt-side sponsor at all — a sharp break from every other entry in the series.

This is the most "German-side" of the seven Gannsen baptisms: no Kreichelts present, an outside sponsor (Erwin Spraul) chosen as the child's namesake, and the use of the Hesse-style surname spelling Konsen. The 1875 entry is the strongest direct surname link between Charles in St. Louis and the Konze line in Hesse — which is why it remains the most evidentially loaded line in the seven-baptism series. From the 2020 research journal, Doc K; sponsor analysis and Session 78 follow-up (Jed × Peter Schräder, 15–16 December 2025; Session 78, 27 April 2026). → Session 78 detail.

📂 Surname-spelling research note — additional surname-drift detail

Surname-spelling note

The drift sequence: Gonnson (1866) → Gonnson (1868) → Gansen (1870) → Gonsen (1872) → Jansen (1874) → Konsen (1875) → Jansen (1881) → Konzen (1890, sister Auguste's death record). Konsen is the closest American phonetic cognate of the German surname Konze / Kontze, and the phonetic-s sibling of the 1890 Konzen entry — which is itself the verbatim Hesse-parish form (as re-read in May 2026) and the project's strongest surname evidence on the main site.
