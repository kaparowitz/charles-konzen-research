Friedrike Catharina Caroline Amalia Gansen

Third-born child · later known as Katherine Carolina Johnson · pension file calls her "Kate Ruddy" (married name)
Born 5 April 1870 · Died 1949 · lived to 79

★ At a Glance

CHILD 3 of 7 · LATER "KATE RUDDY" FS L5N2-98F

Charles and Auguste's third child. She lived to seventy-nine, married into the Ruddy family, and was known to her family as Kate. Her 1870 baptism is the famous “Braschler smoking-gun” record — the same pastor who would baptise her sister Auguste two years later wrote two different spellings of the family name, evidence that the spelling came from Charles's own voice.

Friedrike Catharina Caroline Amalia Gansen (b. 5 April 1870, d. 1949 at age 79) was the third-born child of Charles & Auguste — later known as Katherine Carolina Johnson, then "Kate Ruddy" after marriage. Charles's 1898 pension declaration lists her as "Kate Ruddy, born 8 April 1870" (3-day pension drift from the church record). Christened 22 May 1870 at St. Marcus with sponsors Friedrich Pillmann, Catharina Albrecht, Carl Kreichelt, and Amalia Münke (Maria Emma Mincke). Surname now rendered Gansen — first vowel-shift away from Gonnson.

Why this matters for understanding Charles: The 1870 baptism is the densest in-law-network sponsorship in the seven-baptism series — Pillmann had married Albrecht's sister 7 weeks earlier; Catharina Albrecht would marry Carl Kreichelt 10 months later; Amalia Münke was already August Kreichelt's wife. Four sponsors stitch the Pillmann / Albrecht / Kreichelt / Mincke families into a single sponsor pool. The infant carries multiple namesakes (Catharina Albrecht, Amalia Münke).

Baptism record & sponsors

St. Marcus Baptism Doc H — page 285, entry no. 6644  ★ BRASCHLER SMOKING-GUN PAIR

Name: Friedrike Catharina Caroline Amalia Gansen  — note the "a" vowel; the same Pastor Braschler would record the same family as "Gonsen" with an "o" vowel just two years later at sister Auguste's 1872 baptism. Same scribe, two encounters, two different vowels — this is the linchpin of the surname-trail argument that the spelling came from Charles's own speech, not the pastor's pen.
Born: 5 April 1870
Baptized: 22 May 1870
Parents: Carl Gansen and Auguste Kreichelt
Pastor / Scribe: Pastor Heinrich Braschler · Swiss-born 1818; emig. PA 1848 → TX 1852 → MO 1857; St. Marcus Sept 1867 – April 1875 (this was Braschler's first Gannsen-family baptism)

Baptism sponsors

  • SponsorFriedrich Pillmann — corrected from "Billmann" (parish-clerk B/P misread); husband of Fredericka Albrecht (Catharina's sister) — m. 5 April 1870, just seven weeks before this baptism.  ·  FamilySearch L17N-8Q6
  • SponsorCatharina Albrecht — fiancée of Carl Kreichelt; she would marry into the Kreichelt family ten months later on 5 March 1871. The child's middle name Catharina is her direct namesake.  ·  FamilySearch L5FV-3Z1
  • SponsorCarl Kreichelt — Auguste's brother; born Clausthal, Hannover 1846. Still engaged at the time of the baptism.  ·  FamilySearch L5FW-5LD
  • SponsorAmalia Münke — the "Maria Emma Mincke" identified by Jed in Dec 2025; wife of August Kreichelt (Auguste's other brother & Battery B veteran), making her the only sponsor here who was already Auguste's sister-in-law at the time. The child's middle name Amalia is her namesake.  ·  FamilySearch L52N-1PL

All four 1870 sponsors are now resolved to a single tightly-intermarried Kreichelt ⟷ Albrecht ⟷ Pillmann ⟷ Mincke kin network on Auguste's side — none from Charles's German side. The child's full baptismal name Friedrike Catharina Caroline Amalia Gansen mirrors all four sponsors directly (Friedrike → Friedrich Pillmann or his wife Fredericka; Catharina → Catharina Albrecht; Caroline → Carl Kreichelt; Amalia → Amalia/Maria Emma Mincke). Originally documented in the 2020 AncestryProGenealogists journal, Doc H; sponsor identities resolved by Jed × Peter Schräder, 16–18 December 2025.

📂 Surname-spelling research note — additional surname-drift detail

Surname-spelling note

This baptism uses Gansen — one of five distinct baptism-register renderings across the seven children's baptisms (the children's-baptism subset of the nine-attestation surname trail). The drift sequence: Gonnson (1866) → Gonnson (1868) → Gansen (1870) → Gonsen (1872) → Jansen (1874) → Konsen (1875) → Jansen (1881). Same parents, same family, five distinct surnames — central evidence for the J/G/K spelling-drift framing of Charles's identity.
