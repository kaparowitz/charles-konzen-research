Auguste Gonsen

Fourth-born child · also recorded on FamilySearch as "Auguste Johnson"
Born 4 March 1872 · Died 1890 · age 18

★ At a Glance

CHILD 4 of 7 · DIED YOUNG FS L5N2-M8Y

Charles and Auguste's fourth child, named directly after her mother. She lived 18 years and died young in 1890 — the same year as her older sister Josephine. Her 1890 death record is the single American attestation of the full Hessian z-form spelling “Konzen”, and the bridge to the German parish-register surname pool.

Auguste Gonsen (b. 4 March 1872, d. 1890 at age 18) was the fourth-born child of Charles & Auguste — named directly after her mother. Christened 23 June 1872 at St. Marcus with sponsors Georg Kreichelt, Louise (Henriette) Kreichelt, and August Kreichelt — the most "in-family" baptism of the seven (all three sponsors are Auguste's parents and eldest brother). Surname rendered Gonsen — distinct from sister Katherine's 1870 "Gansen". The same scribe (Pastor Braschler) wrote two different vowels for the same family in two different baptisms two years apart — the project's strongest evidence of unstable surname-rendering.

Why this matters for understanding Charles: Her 1890 death record records her maiden name as "geb. Konzen" — born Konzen, with a z — making this the single American attestation of the full Hessian z-form Konzen documented in the Trendelburg parish complex. Together with her brother Erwin Carl's 1875 baptism reading Konsen (with s, Pastor Hoffmann's phonetic capture), the two records bracket the surname trail with the two closest matches to the documented Hessian Kontzen / Konzen pool. The 1890 death record is keystone evidence that the family kept the older Hessian -en suffix in continuous use 1875–1890, in two parallel orthographic variants. (The 1890 reading was previously transcribed Konzen → Konsen → Konzen across May 2026 re-examinations; per the 17 May 2026 user re-examination the canonical reading is Konzen with z. See errata for the full supersession chain.)

Marriage & child

Auguste married James E. Somers (FS LRYQ-HC9), a St. Louis–born man (born 4 January 1869), on 12 July 1890 in St. Louis. Their only child, James Victor Somers (FS LTML-7BY), was born and died in 1890. Auguste herself died barely five months after the wedding, on 31 December 1890 at age 18 — the death register names the cause Unterleibsleiden (an abdominal/pelvic ailment), and the sequence (marriage July 1890 → infant born and lost → her own death that December) points to a childbirth-related cause. Her death record (below) is the project's key attestation of the Konzen maiden surname.

Baptism sponsors

  • SponsorGeorg Kreichelt — Auguste's father; Charles's father-in-law; Battery B veteran; born Clausthal, Hannover 1819. This is his second appearance as Gannsen baptism sponsor (after the 1866 firstborn) and one of only two recurring sponsors across the seven-baptism series. He was 53 at this baptism.  ·  FamilySearch LZXC-NH9
  • SponsorLouise Kreichelt — almost certainly Auguste's mother Louise Friederike "Henriette" Weiss using her formal first name "Louise" rather than her family call name "Henriette". The 1873 St. Marcus parish clerk recorded her as "Louise Henriette Kreichelt née Weis" using the same formal first name. Born Clausthal 1820. She was 52 at this baptism.  ·  FamilySearch LZXC-NG7
  • SponsorAugust Kreichelt — Auguste's eldest brother; Battery B veteran (served alongside Charles); born Clausthal 1844, full baptismal name Carl Friedrich "August" Kreigelt. Husband of Maria Emma Mincke. The infant Auguste Gonsen is named after both her mother (Auguste Kreichelt) and her uncle (August Kreichelt) per the standard masculine-→-feminine namesake convention. He was 27 at this baptism. He died young at 32 in 1877, five years later.  ·  FamilySearch L5FW-572

All three sponsors are immediate Kreichelt family — both maternal grandparents plus the eldest maternal uncle. This makes Auguste's 1872 baptism the most "in-family" of the seven Gannsen baptisms. The full name Auguste is itself a triple-namesake (mother + uncle + likely maternal-aunt-line Juliane Auguste Henriette Kreigelt who was Georg's elder sister). From the 2020 research journal, Doc I; sponsor identities resolved by Jed × Peter Schräder, December 2025; the Louise/Henriette name reconciliation confirmed via the FamilySearch tree person LZXC-NG7's full baptismal name Louise Friederike Henriette Weiss.

Death record (1890)  ★ KEY EVIDENCE

St. Marcus Death Register · Entry 1961 · "Auguste Somers geb Konzen"

Auguste's St. Marcus death-register entry — page 18, entry 1961 (running №1) — recorded shortly after her death on 31 December 1890, with funeral on 2 January 1891. The maiden-name reading is "geb Konzen" — with a z, the full Hessian z-form documented in the Trendelburg parish (→ Card 6 of Key Evidence). The 1890 register thus gives the single American attestation of the Konzen rendering — by a different scribe, 15 years after the brother Erwin Carl's 1875 baptism reading Konsen (with s, Pastor Hoffmann's phonetic capture). Together the two records bracket the surname trail with the two closest matches to the Hessian Kontzen / Konzen pool, in two parallel orthographic variants. The 1890 reading is one of the most carefully captured forms in genealogical practice (maiden-name attestations on death records are used for cemetery, probate, and inheritance verification). (The reading was previously transcribed Konzen → Konsen → Konzen across May 2026 re-examinations; canonical per the 17 May 2026 user re-read is Konzen with z.)

Why this single record matters so much (click to expand)

Maiden-name attestations on death records are among the most carefully captured forms in genealogical practice — they are used for cemetery, probate, and inheritance verification. The 1890 register's "geb Konzen" reading (with z) is therefore a high-fidelity capture of the family's German-Hessian self-identification at the moment of Auguste's funeral — and the single American record carrying the full Hessian z-form documented in the Trendelburg parish, the form Pastor Hoffmann's 1875 baptism (which used the s-form Konsen) captured phonetically rather than orthographically. (The reading was previously transcribed Konzen → Konsen → Konzen across May 2026 re-examinations; per the 17 May 2026 user re-examination the canonical reading is Konzen with z, superseding the 5 May 2026 errata.)

For transparency, this entry's reading was revised more than once: an earlier transcription read Konzen; a 5 May 2026 re-examination briefly re-read it as Konsen; the 17 May 2026 user re-examination then restored Konzen (with z) as the canonical reading carried site-wide today. The full supersession chain is logged in the errata.

Why the sibling 1875 Konsen baptism still points to the same surname pool: only the German tz cluster collapses to s in American transcription; the K-initial and terminal -en are preserved exactly, and American clerks regularly captured the German tz [ts]-affricate as a simple s. So Erwin Carl's 1875 baptism (Konsen, phonetic) and this 1890 death record (Konzen, orthographic) converge on the same Hessian Konzen / Kontzen form from two directions.
