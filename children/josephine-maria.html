Maria Josephine Gonnson

Second-born child · also recorded on FamilySearch as "Josephine Maria Johnson"
Born 20 February 1868 · Died 1890 · age 22

★ At a Glance

CHILD 2 of 7 · DIED YOUNG FS LTSG-XWW

Charles and Auguste's second child and the first to survive infancy. She lived 22 years and died young in 1890. Her 1868 baptism is one of the eight U.S. records that preserve a phonetic spelling of the family surname.

Maria Josephine Gonnson (b. 20 February 1868, d. 1890 at age 22) was the second-born child of Charles & Auguste — the first to survive infancy. Christened 1 June 1868 at St. Marcus, with sponsors Jakob Reck & his first wife Josephine née von Delien, Maria Agnes Götz, and Georg Kreichelt. The infant carries Josephine née von Delien's first name as her middle namesake. Surname still rendered Gonnson — same as her older brother's 1866 baptism.

Why this matters for understanding Charles: This is the only one of the seven baptisms with non-Kreichelt-side sponsors dominating (3 of 4 sponsors are non-kin: the Reck couple + Götz). It signals a meaningful Charles-side parish-circle friendship — the Pfalz-Reformed Reck couple plus the Pfälzer / Hessen-Darmstadt / Baden Götz network. Josephine's individual identity is the only open sponsor ID in the project.

Marriage & children

Josephine married Edward “Ed” (Eduard) Herr (FS L2YN-98D), a St. Louis–born German-American (born 1 March 1866 in St. Louis; died 12 April 1947, buried Park Lawn Cemetery). They had three sons. Josephine died young, on 5 May 1890 (age 22), and was buried 7 May 1890 at Old St. Marcus Cemetery; her death is recorded in the St. Marcus death register under the surname Konzen (“Josephine, nee Konzen, wife of Eduard Herr”). Edward long outlived her and raised the boys.

Baptism sponsors

  • SponsorJacob Reck (FS KLPB-52X) and his first wife, Josephine née von Delien — Jacob is identified as Pfalz-born 1837 (Freinsheim, Bavaria), Evangelisch-Reformierte (Calvinist), arrived NY 20 Nov 1865, buried Old St. Marcus Cemetery alongside Charles. Surname "Delien / Delian" confirmed via three-source convergence (AncestryPro 2020 Doc G + 2 May 2026 high-res image reread + StLGS Concordia Cemetery Delien-surname cross-reference) — only Josephine's individual identity remains open. The child's middle name Josephine is her direct namesake. Peter Schräder's instinct (16 Dec 2025): "Which person would you choose as godparents to your first child? A complete stranger? There must be a reason for this." With Jacob's Pfalz origin (~250 km south of Charles's Trendelburg) locked in, the Reck-Charles bond reads as a St. Louis-formed parish-circle tie, not a pre-emigration old-country tie. Josephine likely died June 1868 → mid-1869 (no surviving children); Jacob remarried Emma Weingardt 27 Jul 1869.

    ★ Adjacent Battery-C observation (not a conflation): a May 2026 NPS Soldiers and Sailors search returned one Reck in Charles's regiment — Frederick Reck, Private, Company C, 2nd Regiment Missouri Light Artillery (NARA M390, Roll 39). This is the same regiment as Charles (Company B), one company over. Frederick may be Jacob's brother, cousin, or wholly unrelated; nothing yet documents the connection. → Jacob Reck profile · → Josephine profile      ·  ★ husband identified, wife's individual ID still open
  • SponsorMaria Agnes Götz — ★ Strong candidate identified (3 May 2026): likely Agnes Maria Goetz, b. ~1830 Baden, wife of Andrew Goetz (b. ~1816 Bavaria), 1870 St. Louis Census Ward 2, sons Wm (1850) and Julius (1857). Also appears 1865 St. Louis directory as "Agnes Goetz, wife of John Goetz." Plausibly the same person as Anna Goetz, the third named witness at Charles & Auguste's 7 January 1866 wedding — "Anna Maria Agnes" was a common triple-name in 1820s Pfälzer/Hessen records. Buecher's St. Marcus indexes confirm a sizable Pfälzer/Hessen-Darmstadt/Baden Götz network at the parish — same regional cluster as the Reck family.  ·  → Maria Agnes Götz profile
  • SponsorGeorg Kreichelt — Auguste's father; Charles's father-in-law; Battery B veteran; born Clausthal, Hannover 1819; emigrated 1853. He had stood as sponsor at the firstborn's 1866 baptism alongside his wife Henriette; he sponsors solo here in 1868, and again in 1872. He was 49 at this baptism.  ·  FamilySearch LZXC-NH9

Of these three sponsors, Georg Kreichelt and Jacob Reck (FS KLPB-52X; b. 1837 Freinsheim Pfalz, Calvinist; arrived NY 20 Nov 1865; buried Old St. Marcus Cemetery alongside Charles) are now positively identified. Only Jacob's first wife Josephine née von Delian[?] remains open — likely died young 1868–69. With Jacob's Pfalz origin locked in (~250 km south of Charles's Trendelburg), the Reck-Charles bond reads as a St. Louis-formed parish-circle tie, not a pre-emigration old-country tie. From the 2020 research journal, Doc G; sponsor identities partially resolved by Jed × Peter Schräder, 16 December 2025.
