# Memories Audit — Charles Johnson Sr. (FamilySearch LZXC-6GB)

**Reviewed:** 9 June 2026 · **Tab:** Memories (38 items) · **Contributor of record:** Jed S Johnson

---

## Summary

The Memories section is strong and unusually complete for a 19th-century immigrant — 38 items spanning
military service, naturalization, marriage, every child's baptism, census/residence, occupation, pension,
death, burial, obituaries, a studio portrait, and his personal signature. Most **record-type** memories
are well-curated: descriptive title, event place, event date, a substantive description (often with an
archival citation), and a face/region tag.

The main weaknesses are (1) the single most important item — the only photograph of Charles — is
unlabeled, and (2) dozens of memories that primarily depict relatives are tagged only to Charles, so they
never surface on those relatives' profiles. Both are quick, high-value fixes.

---

## What's working well

- **Life-cycle coverage is essentially complete** — birth/origin (German records), Civil War (enlistment,
  unit, fort service, pension, soldiers' home), 1866 marriage, children's baptisms, census and city-directory
  residence, occupation, 1903 death certificate / notice / obituary, and St. Marcus burial.
- **Metadata quality on documents is high.** Example: the naturalization record carries title, place
  ("St. Louis, Missouri"), exact date ("21 October 1868"), a sourced description ("C.C.C. St. Louis,
  Volume 10, Page 126 — name misread and filed as 'Gausen'"), and a region tag on his signature.
- **The surname thesis is documented in the memories themselves** — the Konzen → Gannsen/Gansen/Gonnson/
  Jansen → Johnson evolution is visible across the baptism titles, which is exactly the project's core claim.
- **Context items enrich the story** — the New Madrid brochure, Fort #2 drawing, residence-to-church map,
  and St. Marcus church photo give the documents a sense of place.

---

## Recommendations (priority order)

### 1. Title and describe the portrait — highest value
The studio portrait opens as **"No title."** It has no date, no place, no description, and a generic upload
filename (`584FCF3B…jpg`). It is the only photograph of Charles and is also the profile portrait, so it is
the most-seen item on the whole profile.
- Add a **title** (e.g., "Charles Johnson Sr. — studio portrait").
- Add **photographer/place** visible on the mount: **"S. Roth, 906b South Broadway, St. Louis, Mo."**
- Add an **approximate date** (the mustache/dress and address suggest a range — note it as "circa" if unsure).
- Add a one-line **provenance** note (who held the original, how it was digitized).

### 2. Cross-tag memories to the relatives they depict
Many memories are primarily about other people but are tagged only to Charles, so they do not appear on
those profiles. Tag each to its subject (FamilySearch allows multiple person tags per memory):
- **Wife, Auguste Kreichelt** — marriage record, her birth/baptism, her obituary ("Age 49").
- **Her parents** — Georg Kreichelt (obituary + death notice, "Age 64"), Louise "Henriette" Weiss Kreichelt (obituary, "Age 74").
- **Brother-in-law** — August Kreichelt birth/christening.
- **Children** — Charles Jr. (the *Erwin Carl Konzen* baptism), Catherine, Auguste, Bertha baptisms;
  Augusta Johnson Somers obituary ("Age 18"); Katherine Johnson + John Ruddy marriage.

### 3. Fix the one inconsistent maiden-name spelling
"Obituary for Auguste **Kreigelt** Gannsen" spells the maiden name differently from every other item
("Kreichelt"). Standardize the title to **Kreichelt** and keep documented variants in the description.
(The Gansen/Gannsen/Ganssen/Gannson variation across titles is fine as faithful transcription — optionally
add the standardized surname in parentheses.)

### 4. Summarize the large "download to view" PDFs
Three memories are heavy multi-page PDFs — the Ancestry ProGenealogists Research Journal, the Compiled
Service Record, and the Full Civil War Pension File. Add a description to each summarizing what's inside
(page count, key findings) so a viewer knows the contents without downloading.

### 5. Label the context/illustrative items as context
The New Madrid brochure, Fort #2 architectural drawing, residence-to-church distance map, "Old St. Marcus
Cemetery Information," and the St. Marcus church photo are illustrative rather than primary evidence for
Charles. Keep them, but make the description say so, so future researchers don't cite them as record evidence.

### 6. Consider one narrative item
There is no written life story or audio. A short biographical **story memory** (or the "Create StoriedBook"
tool) would tie the 38 items into a single readable narrative and is the natural capstone for a collection
this complete.

## Follow-up: on-site verification & fixes (9 June 2026)

After the grid-level audit above, each flagged item was opened and inspected directly. Findings:

- **Portrait — FIXED.** Was genuinely "No title" with no date/place/description. Now titled
  "Charles Johnson Sr. — Studio Portrait," dated "about 1895," placed at St. Louis, Missouri,
  described (incl. photographer **S. Roth, 906b South Broadway, St. Louis**), and reclassified
  Document → **Photo**.
- **Cross-tagging — already complete; no change needed.** The grid view doesn't show tags, which led
  the audit to overstate this. Inspection of the actual records shows the contributor already tags
  comprehensively. Verified examples: the marriage record is tagged to wife Auguste Kreichelt (LZKV-9F5)
  and witness Johann Adam Herweck (K4DW-PXM); daughter Auguste's baptism is tagged to the daughter
  herself (L5N2-M8Y) plus wife and the Kreichelt grandparents; Georg Kreichelt's obituary is tagged to
  Georg (LZXC-NH9) and his wife Henriette Weiss (LZXC-NG7). In-laws and children already appear on their
  own profiles.
- **PDF & document descriptions — already present; no change needed.** E.g., the Compiled Service Record
  PDF already reads "Compiled Service Record from the Civil War obtained from the national archives —
  download to see the full file"; the naturalization record cites "C.C.C. St. Louis, Vol. 10, p. 126";
  the Georg Kreichelt obituary cites "Anzeiger des Westens, 22 Sep 1883."

**Net result:** the portrait was the one substantive gap and has been fixed. Recommendations 2–5 in the
list above were largely already satisfied; they remain valid as light, optional polish (e.g., adding
page counts to PDF descriptions, a one-line "context" note on the illustrative items) but are not
required. Recommendation 6 (a narrative story memory) still stands as an optional capstone.
