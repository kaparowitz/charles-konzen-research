### 2D. CRITICAL: German Surname KONZEN Confirmed

The **1875 birth record** for Charles's son at St. Marcus German Evangelical Church is titled in the FamilySearch sources as:

> **"Erwin Carl Konzen's birth record: St. Marcus Evangelical Church records, 1847-1981"**

The child registered as **Charles Johnson Jr.** (KL18-V2Y, 1875-1950) in the family tree was baptized at St. Marcus under the surname **KONZEN**. This is the German church's recording of the father's German surname — not "Gannsen" or "Johnson," but the original German: **KONZEN**.

**KONZEN = KONZE = KONTZE** — variant spellings of the same Deisel/Trendelburg family surname. This is the strongest direct link between Charles Gannsen and the Konze family of Deisel.

### 2E. "L." Initial — Critical Clue for German Birth Record

From the FamilySearch profile note (written by Jed S Johnson):

> "L. Carl Gannsen, (aka Charles), was born on January 25, 1835 - see church death record. He never wrote out the first name of the 'L' initial. He preferred to be called Charles. The 'L' initial is a clue to finding his vital records in Germany. It may have been Ludwig, which was a common name."

**Confirmed name structure:** The German name was "**[L-name] Carl**" — a compound name where:
- First name: begins with L (possibly Ludwig, Lorenz, Leopold, Leonhard, or another L-name)
- Use name (Rufname): Carl/Charles — the name he actually used in daily life

This is consistent with 19th-century German naming conventions where a child was baptized with multiple names and went by the second (or third). In American records, the first name was reduced to the initial "L."

**Implication for Entry 195:** Entry 195's baptismal name is recorded as "Justus." The "L." initial does NOT directly match "Justus." Two possible reconciliations:
1. Entry 195's name reading may be uncertain ("most probable" qualifier applied in earlier sessions) — could the name be "Lorenz" or "Lorenz Carl" misread as "Justus" in Kurrent?
2. The child had two baptismal names (compound name), with "Justus" as first and an L-name as second — and used the L-name in daily life.
3. Entry 195 is NOT Charles Gannsen, and his German birth record has not yet been found.

## Part 3: Research Implications

### 3A. German Birth Record — Status and Next Steps

**Confirmed:** Charles Gannsen = L. Carl Konzen, born ~January 26, 1835, Deisel/Trendelburg area.

**Still missing:** The German baptism record. The FamilySearch profile notes that "no church birth, baptism or confirmation records have been found yet in the town to confirm the link."

**Strongest candidate:** KB 218842 Entry 195 (Deisel Baptisms 1830-1861) — child "Justus" born January 23-24, 1835, father "Joh. Heinrich Konze Kaufmann," mother "Wilhelmine geb. Hildabrand." 
- Match points: Deisel ✓, January 1835 ✓, surname Konze ✓ (= Konzen in church records)
- Discrepancy: Name "Justus" ≠ "L. Carl" structure. Must investigate whether: (a) the Entry 195 name reading is fully certain, (b) the child had a compound name with an L-name component, or (c) there is another birth record in Deisel or nearby registers.

**Entry 195 name re-examination is now the highest priority German register task.**

### 3B. FamilySearch Tree Gap

Charles Johnson Sr. (LZXC-6GB) has NO parents linked in FamilySearch. The German identity link is the research gap this entire project is attempting to fill.

### 3C. The "Kaufmann" Question

If Entry 195 IS Charles, then his father was "Joh. Heinrich Konze Kaufmann" (merchant). This figure does NOT appear in the documented FamilySearch Konze family branches — he is not Johann Henrich (1790-1865, Ackermann) and is not any documented son of Johann Christoph (1774-1814). He likely belongs to an undocumented branch, possibly the Andreas (1768-1800) or Johannes (1771-1838) sub-families which show 0 children in FamilySearch despite known marriages.
