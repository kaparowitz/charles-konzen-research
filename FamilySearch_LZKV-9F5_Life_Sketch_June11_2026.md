Auguste Kreichelt was born 12 May 1847 in Clausthal, a silver-mining town in the Harz mountains of the Kingdom of Hanover, a daughter of Georg Andreas Kreichelt (1819–1883) and Louise Friederike Henriette Weiss (1820–1894). The family's German emigration record lists her as Caroline Juliane Auguste Kreigelt among the Kreichelt family members leaving Clausthal for St. Louis. In September 1853, when Auguste was six, the family sailed from Bremen and arrived at the port of New Orleans on 28 November 1853, continuing up the Mississippi to St. Louis, where she would spend the rest of her life. The 1860 census records thirteen-year-old Auguste in her parents' household in St. Louis.

On 7 January 1866, at age eighteen, she married Charles Gonnson — Charles Johnson Sr., a Hessian-born immigrant and recently discharged Civil War artillery sergeant — at St. Marcus German Evangelical Church in St. Louis, Pastor Georg Wendelin Wall officiating (register entry No. 676). The marriage lasted thirty years, and St. Marcus remained the family's church throughout: all seven of the couple's children were baptized there, with Auguste's Kreichelt relatives — her brother August, sister Bertha Barbara, brother-in-law Carl, and her parents — serving again and again as baptismal sponsors.

Her seven children were Friedrich Wilhelm (1866–1867), Maria Josephine (1868–1890), Katherine Carolina (1870–1949), Auguste (1872–1890), Louise (1873–1947), Erwin Carl (1875–1950), and Bertha (1880–1959). She knew deep loss as a mother: her firstborn died in infancy, and in 1890 she buried two grown daughters, Maria Josephine and Auguste, in a single year. Four children survived her.

The 1880 census shows the family at 1534 Jackson Street, St. Louis, where Auguste — recorded as "Guissie Gonson" — kept house. The records of her life spell the family surname many ways — Gonnson, Gansen, Gonsen, Jansen, Conson — and these very variants later became key evidence in tracing the family's Hessian origins.

Auguste died 19 December 1896 at her home at 2130 DeKalb Street, St. Louis, at age 49. Her death notice appeared in the St. Louis Post-Dispatch on 21 December 1896, and the St. Marcus death register entered her on 22 December 1896 as "Auguste Conson." She was buried in the family plot at Old St. Marcus Cemetery. Charles survived her by seven years, dying in 1903. Through her four surviving children, she is the maternal ancestor of every documented descendant of this family.
