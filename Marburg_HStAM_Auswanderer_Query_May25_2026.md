## English version (for the researcher's reference — not for sending)

Subject: **Genealogical research request — emigration of Carl/Charles Konze(n),
Kreis Hofgeismar, around 1859**

Dear Sir or Madam,

As part of a multi-month transatlantic family-history investigation, I am looking for
an emigration record for a man who, on current evidence, emigrated from the former
Kreis Hofgeismar (Electorate of Hesse / Kurhessen) to North America around 1859. I
would like to request a paid search and am of course willing to cover the applicable
fees; a cost estimate would be welcome.

**Person sought**

- Name (German spellings): Konze, Kontze, Kontzen, Contze, Contzen, Konzen, Konsen —
  the family appears in Kurhessen parish registers under several of these forms.
- Given name: probably Carl (later Charles in U.S. sources).
- Born: 26 January 1835 (calculated from U.S. sources).
- Origin: Kreis Hofgeismar, Kurhessen — probably Trendelburg, Deisel, or a neighbouring
  village in the Hofgeismar church district. An 1866 U.S. marriage entry gives the
  birthplace as "Deisse, Kreis Cassel" (= Deisel); an 1862 enlistment record gives
  "Freundburg, Kurhessen" (probably Trendelburg).
- Religion: Evangelical Lutheran.
- Emigration: around 1859, destination St. Louis, Missouri (USA).
- In U.S. sources the surname was variously written Gannsen, Gansen, Gonson, and later
  Johnson.

**What I am asking for**

Please check whether an emigration or release-from-citizenship process can be
documented for this person — or for a young man of the same surname group from the
Hofgeismar church district / Kreis Hofgeismar — in roughly 1856–1861, in particular:

1. **Bestand 180 Hofgeismar** (district office of Hofgeismar), classification
   "Admission and release of subjects / release from citizenship" — emigration
   applications and release files of the Hofgeismar district. (For comparison: a
   record for Ludwig Konze of Deisel, 1864, is held there at HStAM Best. 180
   Hofgeismar Nr. 841, fol. 49.)

2. **Bestand 17b** — regional administrative records of the Kassel government on
   emigration/immigration matters, insofar as Hofgeismar-district cases for the
   period are included.

3. Because it cannot be ruled out that the emigration took place **without official
   permission** (possibly to evade military service), I would also be grateful for
   any indication of records on unauthorised emigration, evasion of military duty,
   or wanted notices (Steckbriefe) for the Hofgeismar district in this window.

**Already searched** (to avoid duplication): the HESAUS / "Hessische Auswanderer"
database (LAGIS) was searched in full on 25 May 2026 under all the surname variants
above, with no match; the single "Konze" hit is Ludwig Konze, Deisel, 1864, who moved

therefore already covered. The request is therefore aimed at the case-level district
and administrative records (Best. 180 Hofgeismar, Best. 17b) that are not, or not
fully, indexed in HESAUS.

## Notes for the researcher

- **Why this letter is still worth sending even though HESAUS came back negative.**
  HESAUS indexes the *summary* emigration registers (the Best. 16 "Periodische
  Übersichten"). It does **not** comprehensively index the underlying case files of
  the district offices (Best. 180 Hofgeismar) or the Regierung Kassel administrative
  series (Best. 17b). An individual emigration *application* or *correspondence* —
  or a record of an *unauthorised* departure — could exist there without ever
  appearing in HESAUS.

- **The illegal-emigration angle is the highest-value ask.** If Charles left without
  registering (the project's working hypothesis, given his absence from every indexed
  emigration list), then a *positive* record would most likely be a punitive one —
  a Steckbrief, a military-conscription loss notice, or a property-confiscation note
  for emigrating without leave. Point 3 of the letter is therefore not a throwaway.

- **Cross-reference:** this query also bears on the open *Hessian Stammrolle*
  (1855 conscription class) research log — a single Marburg visit could address both
  the Auswanderer-Beleg and the Stammrolle questions; consider asking the archive to
  treat them together.

- Sender details and the Marburg e-mail address (marburg@hla.hessen.de) are filled
  in and verified (25 May 2026). The letter is ready to send as-is.
