## Headline

Session 43 continued the systematic page-by-page scan of the post-1876 Deisel burial register (Register 218854) from Image 84 through Image 107, covering entries 557–658 and years 1899–1904. **Key results**: (1) **4 direct Konze burial entries found** — including 1 adult patriarch death (Philipp Ludwig Konze, Ackermann, d. 1903), 1 wife's death (Amelie Konze née Grebe, wife of Friedrich Wilhelm Konze, d. 1902), and 2 child deaths; (2) **6 "geb. Konze" references** — Konze daughters who married into Meinbreße, Köster, Pfeifer, Koch, Pförtner, and Jarre families; (3) **1 new Konze patriarch identified**: Philipp Fedor Konze (Ackermann, house No. 4); (4) **A Konze-Konze marriage confirmed**: August Wilhelm Konze married Marie Wilhelmine née Konze — endogamy within the clan; (5) **Possible second Friedrich Wilhelm Konze** at house No. 129½ (distinct from the one at No. 102 who died in 1893); (6) **J.H. Konze #2 (Johann Heinrich Konze, born ~1808) remains UNFOUND** through 1904 — now definitively impossible he survived to this date.

## J.H. Konze #2 Status Update

**Johann Heinrich Konze** (born ~1808, Ackermann in Deisel, married to Wilhelmine née Hildebrand) — **NOT FOUND** through 1904.

The scan has now covered **1876–1904** (29 years) without finding his death record. If born ~1808, he would be 96 years old by 1904 — survival to this age is essentially impossible for a 19th-century German farmer. The conclusion from Session 42 stands:

1. **Most likely**: Died before 1876 — his record exists in the 1830–1876 burial register but was missed during the index scan (possibly recorded under a variant name or in an unusual section)
2. **Possible**: Moved away from Deisel or died in a different parish
3. **Unlikely**: Recorded under a significantly different name variant

Further research should focus on a complete re-scan of the 1830–1876 register for the years 1850–1876, when his death most likely occurred (he would have been 42–68 years old).

## Notable Observations

### 1. Konze-Konze Marriage (Entry 656)
August Wilhelm Konze married Marie Wilhelmine née Konze — both parents bear the Konze surname. This confirms endogamy within the Deisel Konze clan, with different branches intermarrying. This was likely common given the family's large presence in the village.

### 2. Maiden Name Correction — Ludwig Konze's Wife
Entry 562 confirmed that Ludwig Konze's wife Maria was née **Küffmann** (not "Kuhlmann" as initially read in Entry 506 during Session 42). The Kurrent letters for these names are similar, but Küffmann is a known Deisel family name.

### 3. Declining Konze Density After 1900
The scan found fewer Konze entries per year after 1900 compared to the 1893–1898 peak period. Years 1901–1904 produced only 4 direct entries and 3 geb. Konze references across 4 years, versus 9 direct + 7 geb. Konze across 6 years in Session 42 (1892–1898). This may reflect generational transition or out-migration.

### 4. Meinbreße-Konze Connection
Two Meinbreße entries in quick succession on Image 101–102: Entry 636 (child of Heinrich Ludwig Meinbreße, mother geb. Konze) and Entry 638 (Heinrich Ludwig Meinbreße himself). Father and child died within the same year (1903), possibly epidemic-related.
