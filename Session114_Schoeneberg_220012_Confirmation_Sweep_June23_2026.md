   p/6a60e0d34a. **NOT a new lead — already eliminated.** This is the focal

## Conclusion

The Schöneberg confirmation classes across the candidate window — **1847–1853,
covering births ~1832–1839** (with 1854–1856 glanced for births 1840–41) —
contain **no boy named Carl who matches Charles** (b. 26 Jan 1835; American
surname → Gannsen / *…n·s·n*). Crucially, the **anchor classes are clean**: the
**1848 class has no Carl**, and the **1849 class — the natural class for a boy
born 26 Jan 1835 (age 14) — has no Carl at all** (its 4 boys are Johann Peter,
Wilhelm Friedrich Nolte, Friedrich Uffelmann, Christian Klein). **No Carl is born
in 1834 or 1835 anywhere in the register.** Every Carl that appears is born 1833,
1836, 1836, or 1839 and carries a **local Schöneberg surname** (Siebert, Küthen,
Grandjot, Wagner) — never a Konze / Kontze / Gannsen / *n·s·n* variant. (And the
1836 Grandjot Carl was **already eliminated** as focal candidate PCXG-W9M in March
2026 — see note above — so the sweep adds three incidental Carls plus a
corroboration of a known elimination, not four new leads.)

This **resolves the previously jumble-blocked / "Not yet" status** that Session
109 had to hand off, and **strengthens the ELIMINATED verdict** for Schöneberg
(already eliminated as Grandjot territory with no Konze; the post-1822 index had
returned no Konze entry). The pre-1822 Schöneberg volume remains **undigitised**
and is still an on-site / Kassel-archive target.

## Reliability notes
- Given names (Carl vs Johann / Conrad / Georg) read reliably; **surnames and
  exact birth-year numerals are best-readings**, not certified transcriptions
  (Siebert, Küthen, Grandjot, Wagner, Donnerfass especially).
- Image numbers are the viewer's displayed values; the register renders leaves
  with stale-canvas/duplication, so they are approximate — **the permalinks are
  the authoritative anchors.** The 1849-anchor permalink (p/669e6bc394) was
  re-opened and confirmed to resolve to Bild 1708 (Seite 19/20, 1849).
