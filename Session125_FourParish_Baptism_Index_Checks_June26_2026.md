# Session 125 — Baptism-register index checks: Heisebeck, Liebenau, Lamerden, Eberschütz (26 June 2026)

**Prompt:** "check the indexes for Heisebeck, Liebenau, Lamerden, and Eberschütz for the 1833–1841 window."

Same method as the Helmarshausen baptism re-read (Session 124): open each parish's
**Taufen** register on Archion, find its built-in alphabetical name-index ("… mit
Register," located at the end of the Taufen section), and read the **K** (Konze/Kontze),
**I/J** (Jungkonze/Jungkontz) and **G** (Gans/Ganß/Ganson) sections for the in-pool
surnames. All four parishes were already ELIMINATED on the confirmation track (1847–1855
classes = births ~1833–1841) and on Jan–Feb 1835 baptisms; this index pass is the
surname-level cross-check across the whole window.

## Result — NO Konze / Kontze / Jungkonze in any of the four; one Ganß family (Heisebeck, out of window)

### Heisebeck (219175) — clean, sparse index, high confidence
- **K** (Bild 1507–1508): Koch, Klinge, Küster, Klemme, Kassau — **no Konze/Kontze**.
- **I/J** (Bild 1506): Jacobi, Jaspe, Jericke — **no Jungkonze**.
- **G** (Bild 1505): Gerland, **Gans / Ganß**, Gerling. ⚠️ A **Ganß** surname IS present — but
  every G entry sits at **Seite 165–253**, and the register runs Seite ≈ img − 1237
  (img 1256 = Seite 19 = 1835). So the earliest G entry, **"Wilhelm Ganß" (Seite 165 =
  Bild 1402)**, is **1866** — an illegitimate, later-legitimised child; grandfather/father =
  Tagelöhner **Ernst Wilhelm Ganß** of Heisebeck. All Ganß baptisms are **~1866–1885, OUTSIDE
  the 1833–1841 window** (the family's baptisms begin only after ~1844). G-index permalink
  **p/8ed0eccb8b**. → A real in-pool surname in Heisebeck, but post-dates Charles; noteworthy
  for the Gans geography, not a 1835 lead.

### Liebenau (219745) — surname-sorted, difficult hand, medium-high confidence
- **K** (Bild 1706): local K-names (Köster, Kümpel, Kniep, Plate, Bock, Plößmann, + a repeated
  "Bunge" B-form) — **no Konze/Kontze**. The hand is hard; surnames confirmed by checking the
  neighbouring H/I pages (index is surname-alphabetical: H = Hagedorn/Hartmann, I/J = Jungheim).
- **I/J** (Bild 1703): **Jungheim** dominant — **no Jungkonze**.
- **G** (Bild 1702): **empty / near-empty** — **no Gans/Ganson**.

### Lamerden (219676) — dense, hard hand, partly shuffled index pages
- **K** (Bild 451–452, both pages = one large K block): Plate, Klage, Klö-, Krote, Rückert —
  **no Konze/Kontze** in the legible entries.
- **G** (Bild 448): **near-empty** — **no Gans/Ganson**.
- The K block is large and the hand difficult; a letter-certain transcription would need Peter
  Schräder. Corroborated by the **complete** Lamerden confirmation sweep (219673, 1846–1855,
  all classes negative).

### Eberschütz (218887) — readable hand, medium-high confidence
- **K** (Bild 599, both pages): Kraft, Küster, Klappe, Roß, Reichel — **no Konze/Kontze**.
- **I/J** (Bild 597): Jaeger, Jorden, Jagne — **no Jungkonze**.
- **G** (Bild 596): **empty** — **no Gans/Ganson**.

## Bottom line
**No Konze / Kontze / Jungkonze / Gans / Ganson baptism in the 1833–1841 window in any of the
four parishes.** The only in-pool surname surfaced anywhere is the **Heisebeck Ganß** family,
whose baptisms begin only ~1866 — three decades after Charles's claimed birth, and outside the
window. All four parishes remain ELIMINATED, now corroborated on the baptism-index track in
addition to the confirmation and 1835-baptism tracks.

**Method caveat:** the Liebenau, Lamerden and (less so) Eberschütz indexes are written in dense,
hard Kurrent hands; the K-section reads are best-effort-by-eye and scanned specifically for the
distinctive short "Konze/Kontze" and "Gans" shapes, none of which appeared. The cleaner G and
I/J pages (where Gans and Jungkonze would sort) were the more decisive checks and were uniformly
negative (empty G pages at Liebenau/Lamerden/Eberschütz; Jungheim/Jacobi/Jaeger at I/J).
