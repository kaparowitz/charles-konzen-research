## Headline results

1. **⭐ Garrison-congregation map.** Garrison soldiers' families were enrolled in
   **dedicated military congregations**, not scattered through civilian parishes.
   Of the five garrison towns, **only Kassel's covers the pre-1866 window**:
   - **Kassel (Garnisongemeinde)** — Taufen 1833-42 / 1843-51 / 1852-65; Trauungen
     1833-65; Beerdigungen 1833-65; + Militärfriedhof. ✅ covers window.
   - **Marburg (Garnisonsgemeinde)** 1896-1945; **Marburg (Soldaten)** 1939-1950 — ✗ too late.
   - **Bad Hersfeld (Militärgemeinde)** 1866-1945 (incl. "…und Fulda 1866-1887") — ✗ post-1866.
   - **Ziegenhain** — no separate garrison congregation (fortress garrison in the town parish).
   - **Hanau (Stadt)** — no separate garrison congregation (garrison used civilian town churches).

2. **✅ Kassel (Garnisongemeinde) Trauungen 1833-1865 = NO Gastmann marriage.**
   Reg. **174029**. The marriage volume has a **complete, thumb-tabbed A-Z name
   index** (Bild ~565-585). The **G leaf (Bild 568, "G H" tab) is blank** — no
   G-surname grooms at all 1833-65. F=568L, H=569L are consecutive (G between =
   empty). The H leaf carries a genuine "Hartmann Conrad" (a real H groom — NOT the
   OCR-confused Gastmann). ⇒ **Feldwebel Michael Gastmann did not marry in the
   Kassel garrison church.** This is the single most diagnostic check (the only
   pre-1866 dedicated garrison register), and it is negative.

3. **✅ Hanau (Marienkirche, reformiert) Taufen 1837-1842 = NO Gastmann.**
   Reg. **285709**. Full G index read (Bild **1006-1007**; index bound out of order
   + duplicated, ~Bild 1005-1024): Geyer, Gerung, Geil, Gernichel, Geßner, German,
   Geßler, Gräf, Gärtner, Galgenbach, Großeklang, Göschel, Götschel, Groß, Gelf/Golf,
   Gräßel, Gerlach, Göbel, Grünwald, Gönnerheim. H leaf (1008/1012) also read — no Hartmann.

4. **Kassel (Garnisongemeinde) Taufen 1833-42 (174005) & 1843-51 (174008):**
   indexes present but **sparse + broken by X-separator cards** (only scattered
   letters, e.g. W and Z, populated; A-V mostly blank/missing in scan). No Gastmann
   surfaced. Karl was baptised at Fulda, not Kassel. Not certifiable as a clean
   clearance from the index alone — a direct entry-read remains a low-value option.

## Method facts (govern feasibility — log for future sessions)
- **Archion has NO cross-register name/full-text search.** Search is place-based;
  the advanced search filters by record TYPE only. "komplett transkribiert" gives a
  per-page recognised-text panel but nothing searchable across a book.
- Pre-1866 Kurhessen church-book **alphabetical indexes are routinely bound out of
  order AND duplicated**, and interleaved with Archion "X" separator cards; some are
  only partly filled. Locating a single letter-leaf takes ~10-15 viewer steps, and
  G/H/K are hard to tell apart in Kurrent at fit-zoom.
- ⇒ A literal exhaustive G-index read across all civilian town parishes of all five
  towns (~60 volumes) is a multi-hour, multi-session manual grind with no shortcut.
  The garrison-congregation map collapses it to the few military registers — of
  which only Kassel's covers the window, and it is now negative on marriages.
