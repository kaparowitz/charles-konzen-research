Charles Gannsen — Death Certificate (no. 10619)

City of St. Louis Certificate of Death · filed 17 December 1903 · Doc A
PRIMARY SOURCE Civil vital record · 1903

About this document

The City of St. Louis Certificate of Death no. 10619 records the death of Charles Johnson — the American name of the man this project reconstructs as L. Carl Konzen (Gannsen / Konsen / Gonson in the U.S. records). It is the civil counterpart to the St. Marcus parish burial entry (Doc N, #3393) and is indexed as Doc A on the 2020 AncestryProGenealogists journal crosswalk.

For this project the certificate matters on two counts. First, it is the only record that states Charles's occupation at the end of his life — watchman — corroborating the 1887–88 city-directory listing ("Johnson Charles, watch") and the pension-era self-description. Second, it carries the death date and age that the project weighs against the parish register (see the discrepancy note below).

Citation. City of St. Louis, Certificate of Death no. 10619, Charles Johnson, filed 17 December 1903; digitised in “Missouri, Death Records, 1834–1910,” Missouri State Archives (Ancestry collection 1172). Quoted in the 2020 AncestryProGenealogists journal appendix as Doc A.

A one-day discrepancy: death date

The certificate dates the death 17 December 1903. The St. Marcus parish burial register (Doc N, #3393) records the death as 16 December 1903 and the burial as 19 December. The project holds 16 December as canonical — the parish entry is contemporaneous and internally consistent with the recorded age (68 y) — and flags the certificate's 17 December as a one-day source discrepancy. Likewise the certificate's round age “69” (a survivor's estimate, post-mortem) is treated as weak for birth-year precision; the 26 January 1835 birth date rests on the burial register, not on this certificate. See errata · 17 May 2026 (death vs. burial) and the Doc A–O crosswalk.

Examine the document firsthand

The certificate image is held by the Missouri State Archives and served through Ancestry's “Missouri, Death Records, 1834–1910” collection. It is behind Ancestry's subscription wall and is not reproduced locally, in keeping with the holding archive's rights.
