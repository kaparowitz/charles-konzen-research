Entry 195 — Deisel KB 218842 Baptism

High-resolution image · January 1835 · ELIMINATED 4 May 2026
PRIMARY SOURCE Archion image · Deisel KB 218842 Sent to Peter Schräder · 26 April 2026 ELIMINATED · 4 May 2026

★ Eliminated · Peter Schräder reading · 4 May 2026

Peter Schräder re-read the original Kurrent script of Entry 195 in May 2026 and corrected the project's prior interpretation on multiple counts. The Entry 195 child cannot be Charles Gannsen. Per Peter's email of 4 May 2026 ("Re: Quentins" thread):

  • The child received no first name — the field previously transcribed as "Justus" actually reads "männlich" (male), the convention used in Lutheran registers when an infant dies without baptism.
  • The child was born and died three days later, without baptism. Charles, by contrast, lived to age ~60 in St. Louis. Entry 195 cannot be him.
  • The father's family-name was Küster / Kuester (Köster), not Konze; profession Tagelöhner (day-laborer), not Kaufmann. The mother is therefore not the same Wilhelmine geb. Hildebrand who appears at the 1849 Confirmation Entry 261 (which records the Joh. Köster Kaufmann household — a separate Deisel Köster family).
  • Peter notes the absence of baptism is itself unusual: sick infants were typically baptised quickly. He infers the boy was healthy and died suddenly.

This page is preserved as the historical record of how the project reached and then resolved this hypothesis. The corrected entry on the home page is in Section "Key Evidence" #6; the Open Question A around the child's name is also now resolved.

About this document

This is the high-resolution scan of Entry 195 in Deisel Kirchenbuch KB 218842 — for several weeks in April 2026 the leading-hypothesis entry for Charles's birth registration. The image was sent to and discussed with Peter Schräder on 26 April 2026 in the course of the Andreas Konze identity disentanglement, and Peter's definitive reading on 4 May 2026 closed it out (see eliminated banner above).

Archion is the German Protestant church-records archive (archion.de). KB 218842 is the Deisel parish baptism register covering the relevant 1830s window. The "Entry 195" reference comes from the project's HTR-assisted reading of the page; that HTR reading has now been superseded by Peter's Kurrent reading.

Why this entry mattered (historical record)

The January 1835 Konze baptism cohort — the working hypothesis (now retracted)

Charles consistently gave his birth date as 26 January 1835 (the St. Marcus burial entry and the Civil War pension affidavits agree). When the Deisel KB 218842 register was re-walked in April 2026, the project's HTR-assisted reading produced two candidate Konze male births in January 1835 — entries 194 and 195 — and Entry 195 became the leading candidate after marginalia and Trendelburg burial-index cross-references appeared to identify its father as Joh. Heinrich Konze, Kaufmann × Wilhelmine geb. Hildebrand. Peter's 4 May 2026 re-read shows that interpretation was incorrect: the surname was Küster (a day-labourer), the child died at three days, and there was no first name. The hypothesis is closed. See the breakthrough write-up in Session 25 and the higher-zoom reverification in Session 26 for the original analysis path.
