U.S. records about Charles Gannsen

Civil War record · 1862

Charles Gannsen Military Record

Enlistment record · Co. G (as written on the enlistment card; Battery B in all later records) · "L. Charles Gannsen, age 23, born Freundburg, Kurhessen." Doc O on the 2020 journal index.
Civil War record · NARA M405 · 25 pp.

Fold3 / NARA M405 — Compiled Service Records

Complete carded-record series for Charles 1862–1865 — bi-monthly muster cards, promotion cards, muster-out card. Companion to Doc O. Fold3 image #228767500.
Civil War pension file · 40 pp.

F41-523680269E — Pension File

The richest U.S.-side primary source. Service, family roster, address chain (1865–1903), medical exams, death notification. Doc M on the 2020 journal index.
Naturalization · image

Naturalization Record

Image scan of Charles's naturalization document. Cross-referenced by the 1900 census's "naturalized" notation.
Civil vital record · 1903

Death Certificate — no. 10619

City of St. Louis Certificate of Death for Charles Johnson, recording death 17 Dec 1903 — age 69, occupation watchman, 40 yrs in U.S., stomach cancer, last residence 168 Sidney St. Doc A; civil counterpart to the St. Marcus burial #3393, whose register records the death as 16 Dec 1903 — the project adopts the parish date as its working anchor and logs the 1-day discrepancy on the death-certificate crosswalk.
Civil War service · Fold3 image

Project Attachment 3

Ancillary Civil-War-era scan referenced in early research papers; sourced from Fold3 image 303326269.

German records — Deisel parish

Archion image · Deisel KB 218842

Entry 195 — January 1835 Baptism

High-resolution scan of Deisel KB 218842 Entry 195 — once the leading-hypothesis baptism record, but retracted 4 May 2026 (re-read as a Köster day-labourer's child who died unbaptised); retained for the record. Sent to and discussed with Peter Schräder 26 April 2026. See errata · 25 May 2026.

Charles Research — a multi-month transatlantic genealogical investigation into Charles Konzen (recorded in U.S. records as Gannsen / Johnson; 1835–1903), Hessian-born Civil War sergeant of Battery B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery, and his probable Trendelburg / Deisel origins in Churhessen (a working conclusion — the German baptism remains unrecovered).

      
Compiled by Jed Johnson with Peter Schräder and James Gunnis.
