Jewish Citizens in Trendelburg, 1740

Earliest documented list of Jewish citizens in Trendelburg · supplied by Peter Schräder from local archive sources
SECONDARY SOURCE Peter chart · PDF

About this chart

The earliest documentary list Peter Schräder has provided of Jewish citizens in Trendelburg — drawn from a 1740 register — together with a 1863 funeral note from neighbouring Eberschütz. Compiled during the now set-aside Jewish-paternal-line inquiry.

— — —

Funeral Eberschütz 1863
Jeanette, nee Gans, widow of the merchant Falk Blankenberg in Eberschütz

Why this chart matters

The first name on the 1740 list — Seelmann (Seligmann) Gandt — is a 95-year predecessor of the surname-drift candidate "Gans / Gansen" attached to Charles. The chart anchors the Jewish-Trendelburg surname pool a century before Charles's birth. The 1863 Eberschütz funeral entry adds a Gans presence in the immediate region.

Charles Research — a multi-month transatlantic genealogical investigation into Charles Konzen (recorded in U.S. records as Gannsen / Johnson; 1835–1903), Hessian-born Civil War sergeant of Battery B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery, and his probable Trendelburg / Deisel origins in Churhessen (a working conclusion — the German baptism remains unrecovered).
