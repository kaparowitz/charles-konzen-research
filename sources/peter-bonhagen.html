Bonhagen — Peter Schräder's Family Chart

Deisel Bonhagen family · sent by Peter Schräder as part of the Schräder Correspondence
SECONDARY SOURCE Peter chart · PDF

About this chart

One of the family-overview charts compiled by Peter Schräder from his work in the Deisel parish registers and shared with the project as part of the Schräder correspondence. It documents the Ludwig Bonhagen × Katharina Elisabeth Leimbach family of Deisel, including baptism, confirmation, and emigration dates for their nine children — two of whom emigrated to the United States in 1857 and 1865.

Original chart

If the PDF doesn't render, open the original file in a new tab ↗.

Transcribed content

Bonhagen, Ludwig, * 10.11.1804, + 3.6.1854
oo Deisel 3.2.1833
Leimbach, Katharina Elisabeth, * 18.9.1807, + 12.10.1848

Children:
  1. Ludwig, * 3.8.1833, + 24.12.1835
  2. Wilhelm Heinrich, * 24.1.1835, confirmation 1849, 1857 to the US, in census 1860
  3. Marie Wilhelmine, * 17.4.1837, confirmation 1851, in census 1860
  4. Andreas, * 14.1.1839, + 18.2.1840
  5. Friedrich, * 14.1.1839, confirmation 1853, in census 1860
  6. Amalie, * 21.8.1841, confirmation 1855
  7. Phillip, * 4.9.1843, confirmation 1858, 1865 to the US
  8. Marie, * 22.2.1846, + 24.3.1848
  9. Katharina Elisabeth, * 12.1.1848, + 6.8.1848

Another Bonhage family had the nick-name lineweaver at a confirmation 1847.

Notation: * = born · + = died · oo = married · "to the US" = emigrated to the United States.

Why this chart matters

Wilhelm Heinrich Bonhagen, born 24 January 1835 in Deisel, is a near-exact birth-date match for Charles Gannsen (26 January 1835). The Bonhagen family was one of the candidate families investigated and ultimately ruled out — see the Bonhagen family page in the Schräder Correspondence.
