NARA Mail-Order Compiled Service Record — G11-422819443E

Charles Gannsen · Battery B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery · 13-page muster-card packet from the National Archives, Washington · ordered June 2018
PRIMARY SOURCE Civil War service · PDF · 13 pp. NARA M405 · NATF Form 86 Sister source to Fold3 + pension file

About this document

This is the NARA mail-order Compiled Military Service Record for Charles Gannsen — the photocopy packet that the National Archives produces in response to a NATF Form 86 request for a Civil War volunteer soldier's carded service file. The packet was ordered 26 June 2018 (NARA service ticket G11-422819443E) and arrived as a 13-page PDF from the Adjutant General's Office Compiled Service Records series — NARA M405, the same microfilm publication that the Fold3 download (25 pp.) draws from. This 13-page NARA mail-order packet is a distinct artifact from the 25-page full carded service record as scanned by Fold3: both reproduce the same underlying M405 carded series, but the NARA copy binds 2–3 cards per page and omits Fold3's duplicate-clerk transcripts, so the smaller page count reflects packaging — not a smaller record.

Both packets reproduce the same underlying carded-records series; they differ in scan source (NARA paper photocopy vs. Fold3 digital pull), completeness (NARA includes one card per muster period; Fold3 carries duplicate-clerk transcripts of several periods), and legibility (the NARA scan is dramatically clearer on several previously-illegible cards, in particular Special Order No. 92 and the spring-1865 Rolla detachment cards). Together with the 40-page pension file (NARA F41-523680269E) and the Fold3 scan, this NARA packet is one of the three primary-source pillars of the Civil War record. The three sources cross-corroborate but each carries items the others do not.

The "Compiled Service Records" project itself was a federal post-war records-consolidation effort: in 1890 Congress directed the War Department to abstract every appearance of every Union volunteer soldier from the original company muster rolls onto individual cards. Each card records one event — an enlistment, a muster-in, a presence-or-absence at each subsequent muster, a hospital admission, a discharge. The cards for a given soldier were filed together as that soldier's Compiled Service Record; Charles's record was compiled between 1890 and 1912 and microfilmed as part of NARA series M405 in the 1960s. NARA fulfills mail-order copy requests by photocopying directly from the microfilm — hence the "Best Available Copy" cover letter NARA includes when the underlying images are faded.

★ What this packet adds beyond the Fold3 scan

The NARA mail-order packet resolves several readings that the Fold3 download was too faded to render legibly, and adds at least three carded items not present in the Fold3 packet at all. The corrections below should be read as the canonical readings going forward; the corresponding Fold3 transcriptions are out of date.

★★★ Fort Wyman, Rolla, Missouri — verbatim corroboration of the pension-file disability claim

The Civil War pension file (Page 14, Declaration for Invalid Pension) contains Charles's claim that he was injured by heavy lifting at Fort Wyman, Rolla, Missouri while detailed to instruct the 64th Illinois Infantry in the tactics of Heavy Artillery — the founding incident for the right-inguinal-hernia disability that the Pension Bureau awarded $12/month for in 1891. Earlier project analysis based only on the Fold3 scan concluded that no muster card placed Charles at Rolla or named Fort Wyman, and that the pension claim was therefore unsupported by the carded record.

The NARA packet revises that conclusion. Three carded items in this packet, not legibly preserved in the Fold3 scan, place Charles at Rolla / Fort Wyman across the spring–fall of 1865 under three sequential Special Orders from the Headquarters of the Department of the Missouri:

  • S.O. No. 14, 24 March 1865 — initial detachment to Rolla (NARA PG 11 Cards B and C): "On det. serv. at Rolla Mo. S.O. No. 14 H[d] Q[r]s. Dept of the Mo. March 24/65."
  • S.O. No. 159, 15 July 1865 — assignment to Fort Wyman, Rolla (NARA PG 12 Card A, the first verbatim "Wyman" hit anywhere): "On det. serv. at Wyman Hd Qrs. Rolla Mo. By Order No. 159 July 15."
  • S.O. No. 92, 4 November 1865 — relieved from special service and ordered back to Battery B (NARA PG 04 + PG 05, with full text now legible — see below).

The two earlier muster-card readings that the Fold3 scan supported — "Fort Madison Marines, 23 April 1865" — are incorrect. The NARA scan resolves the place name as Rolla, not Fort Madison, and pulls the detachment date back to 24 March 1865. The Fold3 transcription on this site at PG 11 should be revised accordingly.

★★ Promotion to Sergeant — exact date 7 November 1863

The Fold3 packet records that Charles eventually rose from Private to Corporal to Sergeant, but does not date either promotion. The NARA PG 08 Card C (November–December 1863 muster) contains the explicit annotation "Promoted from Corporal to Sergeant Nov 7, 1863." This is the first concrete promotion date in any source examined and aligns with Charles's three-month service at New Madrid (Sept–Nov 1863), suggesting the Sergeant stripes were earned during or immediately after that posting — possibly a direct response to the conduct under cannonading later cited by August Derr and Christian Meyer in their 1891 affidavits.

★★ Special Order No. 92 — full text now legible (Pope, Williams, A.A.G.)

The Fold3 transcription at PG 24 recognized that the Special Order existed but couldn't read its date, order number, command line, or signature. The NARA scan at PG 04 reproduces the same order at much higher contrast and resolves all four uncertainties: the order is Special Orders No. 92, dated St. Louis, Mo., November 4, 1865, issued "By Command of MAJOR GENERAL POPE" (i.e., John Pope, then commanding the Department of the Missouri), and signed by J. W. Williams, Assistant Adjutant General. The companion filing-jacket page at PG 05 records the order's routing — issued at HQ on 4 November, received at the 2nd Mo. L. Art. battery HQ on 11 November.

★ Detachment to New Madrid, 24 April 1864

The Fold3 reading at PG 06 placed Charles on detached service for March–April 1864 with a destination read as "Hd Qrs of the Dept of the [?]" — taken on the existing Civil War tab to mean A.A.G. office detachment in St. Louis. The NARA scan at PG 09 Card B resolves the destination cleanly as New Madrid, Missouri, with the detachment effective 24 April 1864. This is a different geographic posting from the previously-assumed St. Louis A.A.G. office and means Charles returned to the same Mississippi River garrison town where he had served in fall 1863 — the location his Battery B comrades later named as the source of his rheumatism, hearing loss, and rupture.

★ Reduction from Sergeant to Private — 15 March 1865 (G.O. No. 82)

The pension-file declarations list Charles's rank as Sergeant while the muster-out roll names him as Private — a long-standing discrepancy. The NARA packet at PG 12 Cards A/B resolves it: "Served as Sgt until Mar 15, 65 when reduced to Ranks Per [G.O.] No. 82." The reduction was effective 15 March 1865 under General Order No. 82 — the same departmental order that detached Charles from New Madrid to Rolla on 27 March 1865. He served all of his subsequent 1865 Rolla and Dept-HQ details, and was discharged on 20 December 1865, as a Private; Special Order No. 92 (4 Nov 1865, below) already refers to him as "Private L. C. Gannsen," confirming the reduction had occurred seven months earlier. See the Fort Wyman research log for the full reconstruction.

Correction (June 2026): an earlier version of this card dated the reduction to "1–4 November 1865" and tied it to Special Order No. 92. That reading is retracted — S.O. 92 only relieved Charles from his Rolla/Wyman detachment; the rank reduction is independently dated to 15 March 1865 by G.O. No. 82 on PG 12.

★ Discharge date — corrected from "Dec. 7" to 20 December 1865

The Fold3 transcription at PG 02 read the muster-out card's "Mustered out to date" line as "Dec. 7, 1865". The NARA scan at PG 12 Card C (the same muster-out roll, scanned more clearly) reads the date as 20 December 1865 — exactly matching the discharge date independently recorded in the pension file. The corrected reading harmonizes the carded record with the pension-file record; the "Dec. 7" Fold3 reading should be retired.

Smaller refinements (collected)

  • Smallpox-hospital detachment start date: NARA PG 06 Card A resolves the previously-illegible start date as "Feb. 20, 1863" (Fold3 PG 20 had recorded only "January–February 1863").
  • March–April 1863 status: NARA PG 07 Card B reads PRESENT, contradicting the Fold3 PG 18 reading of ABSENT for the same period. Fold3 PG 18 should be re-read against the cleaner NARA scan.
  • L. / C. initial pattern. The NARA packet records Charles in three distinct initial-orderings on the same physical document (PG 04 + PG 05): "C. L. Gannsen," "L. C. Gannsen," and "Gannsen, C. L." All three were written by the same clerks within minutes of each other on the November 1865 Special Order. Strong primary evidence that the L./C. pairing was administratively interchangeable — supporting the working hypothesis that L. compresses a German baptismal name (Ludwig or Lorenz) coexisting with the U.S.-form Charles.
  • Copyist attribution. The NARA cards are signed by five different copyists (Black, Cooke, Lowry, W. T. Clark, B. F. Conrad) — useful for cross-clerk comparison and for tracing which periods were transcribed in which records-compilation sweep.
  • Five distinct surname spellings across the muster cards. Different clerks captured Charles's surname in five phonetic forms across the carded series: Gunnsen · Gannsen · Gansen · Gransen · Gonson. None preserves the German tz / -en form intact — same pattern as the civilian church records (the nine-rendering surname trail in Card 1 of Key Evidence). Gansen and Gonson overlap with the civilian-record list; Gunnsen, Gannsen, and Gransen are unique to the military cards.

What the Fold3 packet has that the NARA packet does not

Fold3 carries 25 pages to NARA's 13. The "extra" Fold3 pages are mostly duplicate clerk transcripts — Fold3 PGs 03/09 (two versions of Sept–Oct 1864) and PGs 04/08 (two versions of Jul–Aug 1864) are different copyists' copies of the same muster, occasionally with conflicting present/absent readings. NARA includes only one card per period and is therefore more compact but loses the cross-clerk comparison Fold3 preserves. Fold3 also includes one auxiliary cross-reference page (PG 23) — a faded clerical annotation page with no equivalent in NARA.

Three cards covering Charles's late-1864 service. By this point Charles is on his continuing New Madrid Tour 2 (started 24 April 1864 per PG 09 Card B). Card B (Sept–Oct 1864) records him ABSENT — best read as Charles staying behind at New Madrid manning the river fort's light artillery while Battery B moved north to support the Union response to Price's Raid (Confederate cavalry incursion through Missouri that came within 50 miles of St. Louis before being repulsed at Westport on 23 October). "Absent" here means absent from the moving battery's muster, not absent from duty. Card B carries running debits of $2.30 "for C of E" (Clothing & Equipage) and $3.00 for ordnance stoppage, totaling $5.30 — the kind of accumulating stoppages of an artilleryman on a continuing post. Cards A and C record PRESENT (battery and Charles at same muster point); Card C carries the rare end-of-year "Last roll" notation. An earlier interpretation of Fold3 PG 13's "Reported done to Hospital" remark as a hospitalization was a misreading; Charles was NOT hospitalized in late 1864.

★★★ THE MOST CONSEQUENTIAL PAGE IN THE PACKET. Three findings: (1) Charles's late-war detached-service sequence. Cards A and B place Charles on detached service running through October 1865 — Card A reads (per the May-2026 user re-read) "On det. serv. at Dept Hd Qrs. St Louis Mo. By Order No. 159 July 1" (an earlier "Wyman Hd Qrs Rolla Mo July 15" reading was a transcription error and is retracted), and Card B confirms the Department-HQ-St. Louis posting through Sept–Oct 1865. Charles transferred from Rolla to Department HQ St. Louis on 1 July 1865 under S.O. No. 159 and remained there until S.O. No. 92 relieved him 4 November 1865. (2) Rank-reduction date — 15 March 1865, per G.O. No. 82. The "Reverted to Sgt until Mar 15, 65 when reduced to Ranks Per [G.O.] No. 82" notation on Cards A/B documents that Charles served as Sergeant from 7 Nov 1863 until 15 March 1865, when he was reduced to Private under G.O. No. 82 — the same Hd Qrs Dept of the Mo. general order that detached him to Rolla on 27 March 1865 (Fold3 PG 5/6, NARA PG 11 B/C). One single G.O. No. 82 of Hd Qrs Dept of the Mo. covers both actions; the muster cards reference it from both directions. (Earlier site framing of a Nov 1865 reduction date was wrong; Card C's "until Nov. 1, '65" remark on the muster-out roll is best read as a clerk's compression of the bounty/pay-period record, not the rank-reduction date.) (3) Discharge date — 20 December 1865. Card C's "Mustered out to date Dec. 20, 1865" harmonizes the carded record with the pension file's discharge date. Bounty paid $75, with another $75 due. "Age 28" in the muster-out remark differs from Charles's actual age of ~30 — a clerical inconsistency. All three cards signed B. F. Conrad.
