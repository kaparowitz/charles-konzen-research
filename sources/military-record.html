Charles Gannsen — Civil War Military Record

Missouri AGO service-abstract card · Battery B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery · 1862–1865
PRIMARY SOURCE Civil War record · PDF · 1 page Doc O on the 2020 journal index

About this document

This is a Missouri Adjutant-General's Office service-abstract card (Form No. 242, A.G.O., Mo.) headed “2d (New) Reg't Art. Vols.” for “Gunnsen, Chas. L. or Gannsen, Chas.” It is a one-page summary of his service — company, rank, enlistment, promotion, reduction, and muster-out. The card is reproduced inline below; the transcribed key facts follow.

Correction (June 2026): in the 2020 AncestryProGenealogists journal the Civil War service is indexed as Doc O, and this page previously transcribed it as carrying “age 23” and the “Freundburg, Kurhessen” birthplace. That is wrong: this AGO abstract card's Age field is blank and it names no birthplace. The age-23 physical description and the “Freundburg, Kurhessen” notation are on the federal Regimental Descriptive Book card, reproduced on Key Evidence Card 2. See errata.

What the record means in context

"Freundburg, Kurhessen" — the long-read mishearing

(The "Freundburg, Kurhessen" birthplace is recorded on the Regimental Descriptive Book card — Card 2, not on this AGO service-abstract card. It is discussed here because "Doc O" is the 2020 journal's umbrella citation for the Civil War service.)

No place named "Freundburg" has been located in 19th-century Hesse-Nassau gazetteers. The 2020 AncestryProGenealogists journal's first instinct was to read it as Freudenberg, a small place near Schierstein on the Rhine — but the Schierstein parish baptism index for the 1830s contains no candidate matching Charles. The current working interpretation reads Freundburg as a Civil-War clerk's phonetic guess at Trendelburg, the parish town immediately north of Deisel inside the Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis. This Trendelburg decoding is one of the project's load-bearing ★ Working claims, not a confirmed identification, and would be settled by the still-unrecovered German baptism. Trendelburg is also where the only documented post-1830 male Konze line continues — see Session 18.

The "Kurhessen" portion is consistent across both the military record and the 1866 St. Marcus marriage entry (which says "Churhessen"). Both readings point at the same political unit — the Electorate of Hesse-Kassel, dissolved by Prussia later in 1866.
