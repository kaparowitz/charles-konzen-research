Person — Peter Schräder's Family Chart

Wehrden / Deisel Person family · Carl Ludwig Person sr. (1777–1839) and his Deppe second-marriage line
SECONDARY SOURCE Peter chart · PDF

About this chart

Peter Schräder's family-overview chart for the Person family — Carl Ludwig Person sr. (b. Wehrden 16 January 1777, d. 12 June 1839) and his two marriages: first to Catharina Elisabeth Weller of Deisel, second to Charlotte Deppe in Deisel. The Person family was one of the candidate families investigated and ultimately ruled out — see the Person family page.

Transcribed content

A   Person, Carl Ludwig, * 16.1.1777, Wehrden in Westfalia,
    son of Carl Friedrich Person and Maria Sophie Schmit, + 12.6.1839
    oo Deisel 12.4.1812
    Weller, Catharina Elisabeth, * Deisel 19.5.1791,
    daughter of Johannes Weller and Catharina Elisabeth Hofeditz, + 30.1.1825

    Children:
      1. Carl Ludwig, * 9.10.1812, oo Deppe, + 8.6.1854, gf. Heinrich Person
      2. Marie Elisabeth, * 8.7.1815, + 'in Kassel', gm. Wilhelmina Hofeditz
      3. Maria Sophia, * 2.2.1819, + 10.12.1819, gm. Elisabeth Bauer, nee Person
      4. Friedrich Karl, * 18.3.1821, + 20.7.1821, gf. Martin Lauterbach
      5. Marie Louise Catharina, * 2.7.1822, + 20.10.1823,
         gm. Catharina Bauer, nee Niemeier
      6. Andreas, * 13.9.1824, + 26.2.1825, gf. Andreas Weller


B   Person, Carl Ludwig, son of A, + 8.6.1854
    oo Deisel 14.4.1836
    Deppe, Charlotte

    Children:
      1. o-o Marie Amalie, * 6.4.1833, oo 1854 Drönner, gm. Amalie Deppe,
         mother's sister
      2. Karl, * 14.9.1836, oo Martha Elisabeth Schaub,
         + 31.10.1898 Kassel, gf. Peter Karl
      3. Friedrich, * 21.12.1838, oo 12.4.1863 in Sielen Hennemann,
         + 3.5.1915, gf. Johannes Bölte
      4. Phillip Heinrich, * 31.8.1841, + 1849, gf. Phillip Koch
      5. Maria Magdalena, * 7.4.1845, + 1848, Sophie Deppe, nee Köster

Notation: * = born · + = died · oo = married · o-o = illegitimate · gf. = godfather · gm. = godmother · nee = born (maiden name).

Why this chart matters

The Person family was one of the more deeply-investigated candidate families during the Schräder correspondence — Peter raised the possibility that an illegitimate Person son around 1835 could have been the missing biological connection. Carl Ludwig Person sr. became a widower in 1825 and died in 1839, which fits a "father not acknowledged" structure. The chart was central to that conversation; the family was ultimately ruled out.
