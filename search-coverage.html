Parish Search Coverage — German Confirmation Registers

What has been searched for Charles's birth in the Hessian parishes, and what has not. The June 2026 widened sweep: any boy named Carl, born 1833–1841, surname open — read straight off the confirmation registers.

A conclusion is only as strong as the searches behind it. This page is the at-a-glance ledger for the 2026 confirmation-register sweep across the Kirchenkreis Hofgeismar — one row per parish, with the register searched, the birth window covered, the method, and the result. It is the companion to the longer-running negative-results audit (which logs the earlier Konze-focused baptism searches) and feeds the GPS test of reasonably exhaustive research.

Open-web recheck — 23 Jun 2026 (all parishes)

Automated open-web pass across all 36 parishes (open sources only — Archion / Ancestry / FamilySearch gated images remain on the in-browser workflow). No new confirmation-class match for a Konze-type Carl surfaced, and no parish status changed. Two compiled-index data points stand from the open web:

• Heisebeck (new, 23 Jun 2026). The published Ortssippenbuch Heisebeck und Arenborn (Klaus Kunze, Uslar 2000 — a full evaluation of the local church books) has its complete name register online; it lists no Konze / Konzen / Kontze / Gansen / Gonsen surname (the established Heisebeck families — Schulze, Klinge, Thiele, Wienecke, Helwig, Schäfer — are all present, so the index is genuinely there). This corroborates that the surname was not native to Heisebeck — consistent with the standing Heisebeck candidate being the illegitimate Wehmann child, not a Konze. The open Wehmann forward-trace (a Carl/Charles Wehmann b. ~1835) again returned nothing on the open web.

• Helmarshausen (carried). The published Ortssippenbuch Helmarshausen surname register lists Konze (family nos. 67, 96, 302, 406) and Jungkontz / Jungkonze (nos. 297, 507, 468) — an open-web corroboration of the surname in Helmarshausen. Both are secondary compiled indexes; their entries must still be checked against the original registers before any status changes. Detail in the Heisebeck and Helmarshausen logs.

What this sweep was looking for

The earlier searches (logged in the negative-results audit) filtered on the surname — they looked for a Konze-family child, and outside the core parishes they narrowed to roughly January–February 1835. A negative-results re-audit exposed two blind spots in that approach, so this sweep deliberately widened all four criteria — given name as the anchor, surname open, the full 1833–1841 window, and both confessions — as set out in the search-terms card above.

Why confirmations, not baptisms

Confirmation registers are a fast filter: each class lists every ~13–15-year-old by name, birth date, and father. A child born 1833–1841 surfaces in the 1847–1855 confirmation classes regardless of which parish baptised him or what his surname was — so one class-list does the work of scanning years of scattered baptisms. This sweep reads the confirmation books; the baptism registers remain the deeper Tier described on Open Questions.

The boys named Carl that turned up

The forename Carl is common in these villages — which is the point. Across the now-saturated 1848–1855 confirmation classes the name recurs more than a dozen times; the given name does not narrow the field. The surname is the discriminator, and not one of the Carls found carries a surname resembling Charles's American names (Gannsen / Gonson / Konzen) or an *n-s-n ending.

Closest to Charles's date — the prime window, births ~1834–1836:

  1. Carl Schäfer — Gudensberg, b. 1835 non-matching surname

    The one primary Carl in Gudensberg's 1849 class (entry 9 of ~33 boys); born 1835, the canonical year. Surname Schäfer — not an *n-s-n ending. Two further classes (1850, 1851) carried only middle-name Carls (Friedrich Carl Hildebrand, Philipp Carl Junge).

  2. Carl Wilhelm Wilmes — Hümme, b. 12 March 1834 non-matching surname

    Read clearly off the “Geburts Jahr und Tag” column of the 1849 class. Father David Wilmes, a day-labourer; mother Margarethe Elisabeth née Tille. Squarely in the window, but the surname is Wilmes.

  3. Carl Julius Bringemann — Helmarshausen, b. 1836 non-matching surname

    From the 1849 class; father Johannes Bringemann, a farmer. In the window, but Bringemann is a local family with several confirmands in the same book.

  4. Carl Wilhelm Tiltner — Gudensberg, b. 1836 non-matching surname

    The single Carl in Gudensberg's 1850 class (boys 1–20, read in full); father Daniel, a master mason. In the window, but the surname reads Tiltner — nothing like an *n-s-n ending. This was the most probative class to read for a boy born 26 Jan 1835, and it yields no Konze-type Carl.

  5. Carl Schäfer — Helmarshausen, b. 19 Nov 1836 non-matching surname

    From Helmarshausen's 1851 class (read live 22 Jun 2026). A second, distinct Carl Schäfer — in the window, but again surname Schäfer.

Wider window — births ~1838–1841, from the residual 1852–1855 classes read 22 Jun 2026:

  1. Carl August Hofmeister — Helmarshausen, b. 1838 non-matching surname

    Entry 8 of the 1852 class. Older than any date Charles gave, and surname Hofmeister.

  2. Carl Ludwig Bößler — Sielen, b. 19 Mar 1839 non-matching surname

    The 1853 class, entry 5 (Seite 49). This resolves the earlier unverified “Carl Ludwig” at Sielen — logged tentatively as “Dößler / Basser” before the Sielen calibration slip was corrected. Re-read live 22 Jun 2026 (Session 112) it is Carl Ludwig Bößler, son of Friedr. Wilhelm Bößler (Leineweber), b. 19 Mar 1839 — a B-form surname, no match, and too young. Permalink archion.de/p/edc4e8bcbe.

  3. Sielen 1854 class — two more Carls, b. 1840 non-matching surnames

    Found on the same 22 Jun 2026 pass (Session 112), Seite 51: Heinrich Carl Storch and Carl Ludwig [Stein], both b. 1840, local surnames. Confirms the pattern — the only Carls in Sielen’s window are born 1839–40; the anchor-core 1848–1850 classes (births 1834–36) have no Carl at all. Permalink archion.de/p/00ce7acdf9.

  4. Carl Christian Weinrich — Gudensberg, b. 1841 non-matching surname

    Boys 1–5 of the 1855 class (the read that closed the Gudensberg sweep). Surname Weinrich; b. 1841, 5–6 years younger than any record Charles gave.

  5. Helmarshausen 1854–1855 cluster non-matching surnames

    The window-edge classes carried several more primary Carls — Carl Eduard Wagner, Carl Heinr. Mayer, Ferdinand Carl Gunge, Carl Eduard Hülse (b. ~1839–1841). All confirm the pattern: the forename is everywhere, the surnames never match.

  6. Schöneberg — four Carls across 1847–1853, b. 1833/1836/1836/1839 non-matching surnames

    The Schöneberg Konfirmationen (reg. 220012) were read leaf-by-leaf on 23 June 2026 (Session 114), clearing the previously jumble-blocked “Not yet” cell. The four Carls are Carl Wilhelm Siebert (1847, b. 8 Apr 1833; p/b11e520893), Carl Heinrich Jacob Theodor Küthen (1850, b. 1836) and Carl Ludwig Grandjot (1851, b. 13 Oct 1836; both p/6a60e0d34a), and Carl Wilhelm Wagner (1853, b. 23 Aug 1839; p/02153ef938) — all local surnames. Carl Ludwig Grandjot is not a new lead: he is the focal candidate already eliminated 4 March 2026 (FS PCXG-W9M; d. 12 Feb 1901 Schöneberg, never emigrated) — the confirmation entry (father Johann Daniel Grandjot, mother Maria Elisabeth Martin, b. 13 Oct 1836) merely corroborates his vitals. Decisively, the anchor 1848 and 1849 classes hold no Carl at all (the 1849 cohort — the exact group for a boy born 26 Jan 1835 — has 4 boys: Johann Peter, Wilhelm Friedrich Nolte, Friedrich Uffelmann, Christian Klein; p/669e6bc394, verified to Bild 1708), and no Carl is born in 1834 or 1835 anywhere in the register.

Method & reliability — read this before trusting a row

The registers were read in Archion's high-resolution viewer. Two limits shape how far the by-eye reads can be trusted:

• The given name is reliable; the exact birth year is not. Distinguishing Carl from Johann or Conrad in Kurrent script is dependable, so “is there a boy named Carl in this class?” is a sound result. Reading the year numerals is not — a calibration slip of several years was caught and corrected mid-search at Sielen. Exact birth dates here should be confirmed against the AI transcription or by a Kurrent reader.

• Surnames in Kurrent warrant a second eye. Given names and dates read more cleanly than surnames; any surname above is a best reading, not a certified transcription.

For these reasons the precise page-maps (register, image, class) are kept in the project's working log and are best handed to Peter Schräder (Kurrent specialist) or read on the September archive trip, rather than treated as final from the by-eye pass alone.

What the sweep means so far

As of the live reads of 22–23 June 2026, the tabular confirmation books are now saturated: Helmarshausen 1848–1855, Sielen 1847–1855, Hümme 1848 + 1850, Gudensberg 1848–1855, and Schöneberg 1847–1853 have all been read — the full span of classes where a boy born in the 1830s would be confirmed. The result is uniform: the forename Carl recurs in nearly every class, but never once on a Konze-type or *n-s-n surname, and no Carl with Charles's surname appears in any prime (births ~1834–1836) cohort. The most probative cohorts — the Sielen and Hümme 1850 classes and the Schöneberg 1849 class, the exact groups for a boy born 26 Jan 1835 — were each read in full and held no Carl at all; Gudensberg's 1850 class (strongest of James Gunnis's three “Deissen” candidates) produced one Carl on the wrong surname. That is a meaningful, well-saturated negative. The only residual unread cells are jumble-blocked window-edge or duplicate leaves. It points away from these already-digitised parishes and toward the emigration side, the un-digitised gaps (Heisebeck, Liebenau, Schöneberg pre-1822), and the remaining Dissen baptism scan (Grifte is now read in full, 1833–1841 — negative) — the natural work for an on-site visit.

What remains — the gaps this sweep does not close

Confirmation transcriptions — read, none outstanding

Both queued books have now been read and are negative for Charles. Deisel (218836, images 946–948) and Trendelburg (220102, images 2259–2261) were read in full (Deisel via AI transcription, Trendelburg live on 22 June 2026) — no Carl with a Konze / *n-s-n surname; “Konze” appears only as a mother’s maiden name. Nothing is awaiting transcription. The daily Archion monitor has been repointed at the remaining unread 1835-window confirmation registers (Liebenau and the Session 114 queue).

Tabular classes — now read

The residual Helmarshausen (1848–1855), Sielen (1847–1855), Hümme (1848 + 1850) and Gudensberg (1848–1855) classes were read live on 22 June 2026. The only cells left unread are jumble-blocked window-edge or duplicate leaves (the registers render leaves out of Seite order); not a coverage gap of substance.

Tier-3 baptism scans — now complete

Dissen (253403) and Grifte (253444) have no confirmation book for the window; their baptisms are online instead, and both are now read in full. Grifte 1833–1841 (~157 baptisms; per-year permalinks in its log) — one incidental third-name “Carl” (1834) on a non-matching surname, otherwise none. Dissen 1834–1841 (images 113–136, ~110 baptisms; per-year permalinks in its log, added 22 June 2026) — not one male Carl/Karl (the lone near-miss is a female “Maria Carolina,” 1841). No Konze / *n·s·n surname in either. Tier-3 baptism scans are closed.

Schöneberg — now read

Schöneberg's 1847–55 confirmations are in reg. 220012 (the combined Tauf-/Konfirmations-/Trauungs-/Todten-Buch; tables from ~image 1701), not 220009 — whose Konfirmationen list ends at the 1835 class. Read leaf-by-leaf on 23 June 2026 (Session 114) with per-class permalinks: the 1848 and 1849 anchor classes hold no Carl, no Carl is born 1834/1835, and the four Carls present (b. 1833–1839) all bear local surnames. The pre-1822 Schöneberg volume remains undigitised — an on-site target.

Not digitised — the on-site Tier

Heisebeck (pre-1845), Liebenau (1835), and Schöneberg (pre-1822) are not on Archion — core targets for the archive trip. (The Tier-3 “Deissen” parishes Dissen, Grifte & Gudensberg turned out to be on Archion — now mapped and partly read above.) See Open Questions.
