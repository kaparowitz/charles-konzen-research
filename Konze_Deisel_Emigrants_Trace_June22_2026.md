# Tracing the Deisel Konze emigrants to America — the Lüdicke "2 Brüder"

**Date:** 22 June 2026
**Trigger:** Lüdicke's *Verschwunden ist der Strand…* (Deisel emigrants) lists, p. 89,
"**KONZE, 2 Brüder**" under *Auswanderung aus Deisel nach Amerika nach 1876 → ohne
Jahresangabe.* This note traces them (and the wider Deisel-Konze emigration) in US records.
**Method:** FamilySearch Family Tree + Historical Records (logged in as Jed). The Deisel
Konze clan is densely reconstructed in the FS Tree from the Archion registers.

---

## Headline — the Deisel Konze family DID emigrate to America (≥4 documented men)

A FS Tree + records sweep of **Konze born in Deisel** who carry **US records** turns up a
clear chain migration in the 1880s–1900s — exactly the "post-1876" window Lüdicke flags.
None went to **St. Louis** (where Charles was); they settled **New York City** and
**Milwaukee/Chicago.** They are a *generation younger* than Charles (b. 1835) — collateral
kin, not his household — but they document the family's American emigration directly.

### ★ Best fit for "KONZE, 2 Brüder" — the NYC pair
**Sons of Ludwig Theodor Konze (FS 94CF-XBD) × Maria Christiane Schaefer (94CF-XBK), Deisel:**

| Brother | FS ID | Born | US trace |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Heinrich Philipp Konze** | 94CF-XYV | **1 Jul 1876, Deisel** | **m. 6 Feb 1898 Manhattan** (Josephine Ermeling); **naturalized 1906, Queens NY**; **d. ~9 May 1929 Manhattan** |
| **Friedrich Ludwig Konze** ("Frederick") | PWXP-LNC | **20 Jan 1887, Deisel** | **NYC**; US WWI draft card 1917–18; naturalizations 1919 & 1925 NY (wife Margaret); **d. Oct 1977** |

Two Konze brothers, same Deisel parents, both → New York City. This is the closest match to
Lüdicke's undated "2 Brüder."
- *Data caveat:* the FS Tree gives the father Ludwig Theodor Konze a **1839–1870** lifespan,
  yet lists him as father of children born 1874–1889 — internally impossible. Either his
  death year is wrong, or these are the mother **Maria Christiane Schaefer's** children
  (a Deisel Konze-household woman) recorded under the Konze name. Their **shared parentage
  and the brother relationship are consistent in the tree**; the father's death date needs
  an Archion check. (One sister, Wilhelmine Luise Auguste Konze b.1874, had children
  surnamed *Hillebrand* — i.e. the household has illegitimate births, consistent with the
  mother-line reading.)

Ludwig-Theodor × Schaefer (→ NYC, 2 brothers), Heinrich-1835 × Thiele (→ Milwaukee/Chicago),
and Gottlieb × Schneider (→ NYC). Plus assorted later US-record Konze born Deisel
(e.g. "Frederick Konze b. 1887," "Phillip Konze b. 1877 Dumont NJ 1920 census").

## Relevance to Charles (stated carefully)
- **Confirms the Deisel-Konze → America migration the whole Charles hypothesis rests on** —
  now with named, dated, documented individuals, not just Lüdicke's roster line.
- **But none is Charles, his child, or his sibling.** They were born 1871–1887 (Charles
  b. 1835) and settled NYC / Milwaukee / Chicago, **not St. Louis** (Charles d. 1903 St. Louis).
  They are **great-nephews / collateral cousins** if Charles is a Deisel Konze.
- **A recurring "Carl/Charles" in the emigrating Konze family** (Carl Konze b. 1881 → Manhattan;
  Charles "Carl Konzen" b. 1835 → St. Louis) reinforces that the forename travelled with the line.
- **Net:** circumstantial reinforcement — the Deisel Konze clan was an *emigrating* family,
  so Charles emigrating as one of them is fully in character — but this trace adds **no direct
  documentary bridge** to Charles's St. Louis household. The bridge remains his missing
  German baptism / a record naming his parents.
