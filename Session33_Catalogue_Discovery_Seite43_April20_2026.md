## Critical Analytical Update: New Research Avenues

### The Trauungen 1830–1901 Register — Highest Priority
The discovery of a separate marriage register beginning in 1830 fundamentally changes the search strategy for J.H. Konze × Wilhelmine Hildebrand:

**Previous understanding**: The marriage was absent from BOTH Deisel Kirchenbücher (218821 covering to 1796, and 218824 covering 1796–1831), so it likely occurred in another parish.

**Updated understanding**: The Kirchenbuch 218824 marriage index only covers entries IN that specific register (through ~1831). But a separate Trauungen register starting 1830 exists. If the marriage occurred in 1831 or later, it would NOT be in the 218824 index — it would be in the Trauungen 1830–1901 register instead.

**New hypothesis**: J.H. Konze × Hildebrand may have married in Deisel after 1830, recorded in the Trauungen register rather than the Kirchenbuch. The marriage must have occurred before January 1835 (Entry 195's child's birth). This gives a window of approximately 1830–1834.

**Previous hypothesis (bride's parish)** remains valid but now has an alternative: the marriage may still be in Deisel, just in a different register.

### The Taufen 1830–1861 Register
This register should contain:
- Baptism entries for Konze children born after 1830
- Potentially Entry 195's baptism (Jan 23–24, 1835) in its original form, separate from the 218824 compilation
- Any additional Konze children not yet identified

## Ludwig Konze Mystery

The index entry "Konze, Ludwig — 43" did not match any clearly readable entry on Seite 43. This creates an unresolved discrepancy. Possible explanations ranked by likelihood:

1. **Misread entry**: One of the entries I identified as non-Konze (particularly the §25 "Ludwig Minneigner") might actually read differently — the surname's initial letter could be a highly stylized Kurrent "K" rather than "M" or "W"
2. **Index column misalignment**: The "43" might refer to a page in the burial or confirmation section rather than the marriage section
3. **Different handwriting for index vs. entry**: The index was compiled separately and the indexer may have used a different page numbering system
4. **Page number error in index**: The index entry might have "43" as a scribal error for another page
