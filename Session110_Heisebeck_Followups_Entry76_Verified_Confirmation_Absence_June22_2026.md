## 1. Entry-76 birth-month reading — VERIFIED (live Archion)

Register **219175 — Heisebeck Taufen 1830–1887, image 1256**
(permalink https://www.archion.de/p/34e1ddfe6e/), entry **76**, re-read live at
high zoom while signed in to Archion as Jed.

- **Birth-date cell:** "den neun und zwanzigsten … [Januar], sieben Abends" —
  i.e. **the 29th, 7 in the evening.** The page header reads
  "Gemeinde Heisebeck … Jahr 1834–35 … Monat December, November, **Januar** …
  Seite 19"; the baptism cell reads "den 8ten Februar." A 29 Jan 1835 birth with
  an 8 Feb 1835 baptism is internally consistent. **The 29 January 1835 reading
  stands** (confirms Session 108).
- **Name cell:** "Männlich **Carl Friedrich August** … geb. **Wehmann**."
- **Parents cell:** "**Dorothea Henriette Charlotte Wehmann**, die unverehelichte
  [unmarried daughter of] … Wehmann, Köster … und dessen [Ehefrau] Marie
  Charlotte geb. [Roß…]." Matches the Session 108 reading exactly.
- **Remarks (Bemerkungen) cell:** **CONFIRMED BLANK.** Zoomed directly: the
  entry-75 remarks one row above read "**confirmirt 1849**"; the entry-76 remarks
  cell is empty paper. This is the crux — Heisebeck back-references confirmations
  into the baptism remarks column, so a blank remarks cell means the child was
  never confirmed in Heisebeck.

## 2. Confirmation absence — CORROBORATED (live Archion)

Register **219172 — Heisebeck Konfirmationen 1831–1955.** Confirmation classes
are annual; back-references to the baptism register are confirmed (entry 75
Georg Müller, b. 25 Nov 1834, is "confirmirt 1849"). A child born 29 Jan 1835
would confirm at ~14 in the **1849** class (or at 15 in 1850).

- The **b.1835–36 cohort** (≈ the 1849–1850 classes) was read in full: 6 boys +
  3 girls — Heinrich Schäfer, **Carl Schild** (s/o Johannes Schild, Zimmermann),
  Heinrich [Wehmann, *legitimate*], August Wenzel, **Carl Sommer**, Christian
  Lewendich; girls Elise Klinge, Dorothea Louise Charlotte, Christine Katharine
  Koch. **No "Carl Friedrich August Wehmann."** The two "Carl"s present are
  Schild and Sommer — both legitimate sons of married craftsmen, different
  surnames and families.
- **Caveat (important):** Archion's combined-viewer canvas is **stale-canvas
  prone after page jumps** (a known issue noted in the project memory). Year
  headers and image numbers did not map cleanly this session — e.g. image 849
  redrew from an apparent "1849" to **Jahr 1853** once forced to re-render. So
  **exact image→year pinning of the confirmation pages is treated as unreliable,
  and the decisive evidence is the blank baptism-remarks column** (position-
  independent), with the confirmation-register read as corroborating.

**Net:** Carl Friedrich August Wehmann was **not confirmed in Heisebeck** in any
year — consistent with the "left before confirmation age / emigrant" pattern.
