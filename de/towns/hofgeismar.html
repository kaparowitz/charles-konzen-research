Historical overview

Hofgeismar is the historic Kreisstadt (district seat) for the entire northwestern corner of Kurhessen. The town was founded in the early 13th century around a Cistercian-affiliated monastery (the Hof zu Geismar from which it takes its name) and received Stadtrechte in 1255. Through the 18th and 19th centuries it served as the administrative, judicial, and ecclesiastical center for ~50 surrounding villages including Trendelburg, Deisel, Friedrichsfeld, Helmarshausen, Hümme, and Schöneberg.

The town has two distinct Lutheran parishes:

  • Altstadt — the medieval old-town parish, centered on St. Peter's Church (built 13th c.). The dominant parish for old Hofgeismar families.
  • Neustadt — the post-1700 expansion parish, formed when the town outgrew its medieval walls.

The Hessian state archive HStAM Marburg holds Bestand 180 Hofgeismar, the Landratsamt records — a primary source for emigration permits, military rolls, tax registers, and 19th-c. census schedules covering every village in the Kreis. The town also has a long-standing Jewish community with surviving cemetery stones from the early 18th century onward — relevant to the regional Jewish community's history.

During the Austro-Prussian War of 1866, Hofgeismar was occupied by Prussian forces in mid-June. After annexation it became a Prussian Kreisstadt within Regierungsbezirk Kassel, Provinz Hessen-Nassau. The Standesamt Hofgeismar opened on 1 January 1875 under the Personenstandsgesetz.

Significance to the investigation

Hofgeismar is the administrative seat for the entire Kreis covering all the parishes in this town list. The Kirchenkreis Hofgeismar (Lutheran ecclesiastical district) is the level above individual parishes — Trendelburg, Friedrichsfeld, Helmarshausen, Hümme, Schöneberg, Heisebeck, and the two Hofgeismar parishes themselves all sit under it. This means HStAM Marburg's Bestand 180 Hofgeismar is the cross-cutting state-archive collection for every village in this list.

Both Hofgeismar Lutheran parishes were searched for a Joh. Heinrich Konze × Wilhelmine Hildebrand marriage — a parent pair drawn from the Entry 195 reading later retracted (corrected 4 May 2026): both negative. The two volumes searched were the Altstadt 1815–1840 marriage register and the Neustadt 1815–1840 marriage register (Sessions 63 + 64, April 2026).

Hofgeismar Jewish community

Hofgeismar's Jewish community was documented during the now set-aside Jewish-paternal-line inquiry. The Kleeburg / Stern families have Hofgeismar Jewish cemetery anchors, and surviving cemetery stones are recorded in the LAGIS Jüdische Friedhöfe database.
