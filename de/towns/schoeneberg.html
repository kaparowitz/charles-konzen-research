Historical overview

Schöneberg is a small village just north of the town of Hofgeismar, in Kreis Hofgeismar, overlooking the Esse and Diemel valleys. It is closely linked to the neighboring Carlsdorf, a French Huguenot refugee colony founded in 1686 by Landgraf Carl von Hessen-Kassel for refugees fleeing Louis XIV's revocation of the Edict of Nantes. Many Schöneberg families have French Huguenot surnames (Grandjot, Person, etc.) that thread through the candidate-family search.

The Lutheran parish church serves both Schöneberg and surrounding villages. Parish records exist back to the late 17th century but Archion has only digitized the post-1822 volumes; pre-1822 books remain at the Landeskirchliches Archiv Kassel and require direct inquiry.

Schöneberg passed to Prussia along with Kurhessen in September 1866. In modern administrative consolidation it became part of Stadt Hofgeismar.

Significance to the investigation

Schöneberg was searched as part of the seven-parish sweep for a Joh. Heinrich Konze × Wilhelmine Hildebrand marriage — a parent pair drawn from the Entry 195 reading later retracted (corrected 4 May 2026). The post-1822 volume returned negative; the pre-1822 volume — which would cover Wilhelmine Hildebrand's likely birth window — is not on Archion (Sessions 65–67, 26 April 2026).

The village is also the lead-figure home for the eliminated Carl Ludwig Grandjot candidate (b. 1836 Schöneberg) — see the Schräder Stammtafel. The Grandjot French Huguenot context remains a methodological reference for surname-resemblance theories, even though the specific Carl Ludwig Grandjot was eliminated 4 March 2026.
