Bad Karlshafen — harbour town ~9 km north of Deisel

Diemel/Weser harbour town ~9 km north · both confessions searched · one legitimized illegitimate, not Charles

Historical overview

Bad Karlshafen is a baroque harbour town where the Diemel meets the Weser, north of Deisel — a Huguenot/Waldensian foundation with both a Lutheran and a Reformed (French and German) congregation, the latter merged from 1830.

Significance to the investigation

Both confessions were searched. The Lutheran book (218677) is clean; the Reformed book (218713) holds the region’s one window-compatible illegitimate male, Johann Heinrich Thielemann, b. 8 Feb 1835 — but he was legitimized in 1838 by a documented father (Schlosshahn) and stayed locally recorded; not Charles. Eliminated June 2026.
