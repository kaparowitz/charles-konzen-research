Liebenau (+ Grimelsheim) — Parish Research Log

51.496 N · 9.276 E · ~12.7 km SW of Deisel · compiled 21 June 2026
ELIMINATED · baptism window + confirmation sweep on Archion (23 June 2026; prime leaves certified 24 June 2026)

Map-changing finding (21 June 2026). The June 2026 sweep recorded Liebenau as a coverage hole — Archion holding the registers only to 1777. That is no longer true: Archion now carries Taufen 1830–1890 (register 219745), which covers January 1835. It was opened live and the 1835 window read.

The 1835 baptisms (Jahr 1835, Januar–März, Seite 27 — image 1462, entries 87–90) are all legitimate males with named fathers — Justus Klüte (b. Oct 1834, bapt. Jan 1835), Ludwig Wilhelm Winnefeld (b. 12 Jan 1835), Carl [Ackermann] (b. 15 Mar 1835), Johann Heinrich Taubel (b. Mar 1835). No Konze-pool surname, no illegitimate male, and no birth on or near 26 January 1835 (the nearest January birth is Winnefeld, 12 Jan, legitimate). Positively eliminated — now from the image, not merely flagged as an undigitised gap.

Confirmation-register sweep added (23 June 2026). Liebenau’s Konfirmationen 1831–2013 (register 219742) is also on Archion — overturning the earlier “on-site only” note. Every confirmation class 1847–1855 (the years in which a boy born 1833–1841 would be confirmed) was read leaf-by-leaf for any boy named Carl/Karl, surname open. The forename Carl recurs about nine times across the window, but never once on a Konze / Kontze / Gansen / Gonsen / *n·s·n surname — every Carl carries a local non-matching surname (Büster, Krumpe, Lindorf, Lange, Schäfer, Pfennig, Reinhardt). The two prime cohorts for a boy born 26 Jan 1835 are decisive: the 1849 class held only Carl August Büster (b. 11 Jun 1835 — wrong surname and wrong date), and the 1850 class had no Carl at all (six boys: Wilhelm August, Wilhelm, Wilhelm, Georg, Johann Christian, Reinhard). A well-saturated negative, in the same pattern as Helmarshausen, Sielen, Gudensberg and Hümme.

Prime leaves re-read & certified (24 June 2026). The two probative leaves were re-opened on the live Archion viewer at high zoom and transcribed entry-by-entry, certifying the by-eye reads. 1849 class (Seite 55–56, img 1990) — five boys: (1) Konrad Ackermann, (2) Georg Heinrich Schalk b. 10 Dec 1834, (3) Friedrich Wilhelm Winnefeld b. 18 Jan 1835, (4) Justus Klüte b. 30 Dec 1834, (5) Carl August Büster, father Friedrich Büster, Maurer, mother Anna Margarethe geb. Rüngzig, b. 11 Juni 1835, conf. 1 April 1849 — surname Büster, non-matching. (Entry 3 Winnefeld is the only January-1835 birth among the boys: legitimate, surname Winnefeld, not 26 Jan; entry 7, b. 26 Jan 1835, is a girl, Mathilde Christine Elisabeth.) 1850 class (Seite 58–59, img 1991) — six boys, all certified, none named Carl: Wilhelm August Obermann (b. 20 Jun 1836), Wilhelm Obermann (b. 25 Aug 1836), Wilhelm Jung (b. 29 Aug 1836), Georg Lange (b. 9 Nov 1835), Johann Christian Ludwig Riehl (b. 17 Sep 1835), Reinhard Lüdicke (b. 26 Feb 1836); surnames Obermann · Obermann · Jung · Lange · Riehl · Lüdicke — none a Konze/*n·s·n variant. Both prime cohorts confirmed from the image, not by eye alone.

Baptism-register index check — K / I-J / G (26 June 2026)

The alphabetical name-index of the Taufen register (reg. 219745) was read for the in-pool surnames — negative. K (Bild 1706) = local K-names (Köster, Kümpel, Kniep, Plate, Bock, Plößmann, a repeated “Bunge”) — no Konze/Kontze. I/J (Bild 1703) = Jungheim — no Jungkonze. G (Bild 1702) = empty/near-empty — no Gans/Ganson. The index is surname-alphabetical (confirmed off the H/I pages) but written in a dense, difficult Kurrent hand, so the K read is best-effort-by-eye; the cleaner G (empty) and I/J (Jungheim) pages are the decisive checks. Corroborates the prior confirmation (219742) + Jan–Feb 1835 baptism eliminations. Detail: Session125 log.
