Hofgeismar-Kelze — Parish Research Log

51.483 N · 9.347 E · reg. 219634 · ~10.7 km SW of Deisel · compiled 21 June 2026 · confirmation sweep 24 June 2026
ELIMINATED

Small Huguenot colony parish. Eliminated on both tracks: the only Jan–Feb 1835 baptism is female (no Konze-pool surname, no in-window illegitimate male), and the full confirmation register 1847–1855 (the exact span for a boy born 1833–1841) was read class-by-class on 24 June 2026 — no confirmand with a Konze / *n-s-n surname, and the 1849 class (the precise cohort for a boy born 26 Jan 1835) holds no Carl at all.

Confirmation-register sweep, 1847–1855 (read 24 June 2026)

Kelze keeps no separate Konfirmationen book: baptisms, confirmations, marriages and burials all sit in one combined volume, Archion reg. 219634 (“Kelze KB 1830–1971”). The Konfirmationen run Seite 1–119 (1831–1970); the candidate window (confirmation classes 1847–1855 = births 1833–1841) sits at Bilder 558–563, Seiten 14–24, and was read leaf-by-leaf. The register is well-ordered (one opening per image, each Seite headed “Seite N — Jahr 18XX” with a year-end “Sa. der Confirmierten” tally), and surnames are underlined.

Every confirmand 1847–1855 bears a tight, overwhelmingly French-Huguenot-colony surname set — Bonnet, Benoit, Morell, Monard, Morin, Croll, Briede (maiden names Seguin, Paschal, Ruddenklau), plus Homburg, Weidemann, Stalknecht, Siebert, Finis/Tinnes, Jäger, Martin, Schöning, Rudolph. No Konze / Kontze / Gansen / Ganssen / Gonsen / Gonson / Konzen / Konsen / Jansen surname appears in any class — and in a Huguenot colony a German Diemel-valley “Konze” would be conspicuous by its presence. The whole window holds exactly one Carl: Carl Wilhelm Homburg, b. 4 Dec 1835 (1850 class, son of a Homburg Tagelöhner/Schullehrer) — wrong surname, and born December 1835, not Charles’s 26 January 1835. The 1849 class — the precise cohort for a boy born 26 Jan 1835 — has no Carl at all (4 Knaben, 1 Mädchen: F. W. Homburg, Ludwig Finis, Catharina Benoit, Maria Weidemann, Cath. Elis. Monard).
