Lippoldsberg — Parish Research Log

Wesertal, Landkreis Kassel · on the Weser N of Gieselwerder · Taufen reg. 219778 · Konf. reg. 219775 · compiled 26 June 2026 · baptism per-year + confirmation per-class permalinks added 28 June 2026
GANS CLAN CONFIRMED — but no Charles match in the baptisms (1831–1836)

New Landkreis Kassel target surfaced from Klaus Kunze’s Ortssippenbuch. Lippoldsberg (Wesertal, on the Weser north of Gieselwerder) is not among the 36 previously-searched parishes. Kunze’s OSB surname register attests Kontze, Jungkontze, Gans, Ganß and Ganson here — the last a direct match to one of Charles’s American surname spellings.

Confirmation read — the Gans surname IS present (3 confirmands). A first pass (Session 19) sampled a mis-dated displaced leaf and reported “all local, negative.” A corrected re-read (26 Jun 2026) of the ordered leaves (Seite 40–51 = Bild 151–157, ages 13–14 throughout) found three Gans boys across the 1846 and 1849 classes: Heinrich Wilhelm Gans (b. 1831), Konrad Gans (b. 1832) — both confirmed 1846 — and Friedrich Wilhelm Gans (b. 24 Jun 1835), confirmed 1849, son of the deceased Tagelöhner Johann Daniel Gans (widow Karolina née Haßler). They come from at least three distinct Gans households; boy 4’s mother was herself née Gans.

What this means. This is the first time a pool surname (Gans → the “Ganson” American spelling) appears in a confirmation class anywhere in the search — overturning the earlier “entirely local” reading. None of the three is named Carl, and none was born 26 Jan 1835 (Friedrich Wilhelm = 24 Jun 1835), so Charles himself is not among them. But Lippoldsberg now shows a dense, multi-household Gans family producing confirmand-age sons across exactly Charles’s generation — making it a priority parish for the Gans = Gannsen branch. Surname reading confirmed against the Kunze OSB register (live): “Ohaus” = 0 in the parish, “Gans” one of the most prolific surnames.

Baptism register (Taufen 1830–1870) — year→Bild index

The next step toward Charles is the baptisms. Lippoldsberg’s Taufen 1830–1870 is Archion reg. 219778. The baptism section opens with a title leaf at Bild 1411; entries begin Bild 1414. Baptisms are numbered cumulatively (not reset per year), so the year header (“Gemeinde Lippoldsberg — Jahr 18xx”) and the Monat column are the chronological guide. Indexed live on 26 Jun 2026; per-year Archion permalinks minted & each year-verified from its written “Jahr 18..” header on 28 Jun 2026 (table below). Re-reading the headers established that the reel runs cleanly in chronological order with no shuffle — Seite = Bild − 1411 holds throughout 1830–1841 — correcting an earlier “shuffled tail” reading that had come from year-numeral misreads.

Reel is linear: contrary to the earlier note, Bild 1414–1474 (years 1830–1841) all run cleanly in chronological order, with Seite = Bild − 1411 across the whole window (verified 28 Jun 2026 by reading each year’s written header). The eleven permalinks above each point to the first leaf bearing that year’s header; jump within a year by Bild/Seite in the viewer.

Spot-read at Charles’s date — Bild 1435 (Jan–Feb 1835, Seite 24): three baptisms — #111 Melusine Auguste Helene Pape (b.25 Jan 1835, female), #112 a stillborn male (b.11 Jan 1835), #113 Helene Auguste Adolphine Pape (b.31 Jan 1835, female). A child born 26 Jan 1835 would fall on exactly this opening — and there is no Gans and no male “Carl” here, only the two Pape girls and the stillborn boy. So Charles does not appear at his exact anchor date in the Lippoldsberg baptisms (this opening; the rest of 1835 — Seite 24–31, Jan–Dec — has now also been line-read, see below).

Reconciliation (26 Jun 2026 live read): the full 1835 baptism year (entries #111–140, Bild 1435–1442) was read line by line in the logged-in viewer and yielded three Gans baptisms in 1835 — the March girl (read on the image as Justine Wilhelmine Friederike Charlotte Gans, b. 1 Mar 1835), August Gans (b. 12 Jun 1835, above), and Friedrich Wilhelm Gans (b. 24 Jun 1835). None is a Carl/Karl; none was born 26 Jan 1835. The 1831–1834 and 1836 years were subsequently read the same way (also 26 Jun 2026): the “Carl Eduard Gans 1831” line is confirmed (Bild 1417, father Johann Wilhelm Gans the weaver, mother Anna Maria née Spierlmann; the child died 1832), as are Heinrich Wilhelm (Benjamin Gans, Bild 1417) and Konrad (Johannes Gans, Bild 1422). A previously-missed 1831 twin — Heinrich Gans, son of Johann Christian Gans (Bild 1419) — was added. 1833, 1834 and 1836 contain no Gans baptism whatsoever.

Bearing on Charles — full 1831–1836 sweep. The Gans baptisms have now been line-read for every year 1831 through 1836. Result: 1833, 1834 and 1836 contain no Gans baptism at all; the only Gans children are those of 1831, 1832 and 1835 above. The given name Carl is attested in the clan exactly once — Carl Eduard Gans (b.1831), who died an infant in 1832 — and the hoped-for “replacement Carl” was never baptised here: the Johann-Wilhelm-Gans (weaver) household never reappears after that 1832 death in the read window. There is no Carl Gans born 1833, 1834, 1835 or 1836, and the Jan–Feb 1835 opening (Charles’s exact date) carries no Gans at all. So Charles (Carl Gannsen, b. 26 Jan 1835) has no matching Gans baptism in Lippoldsberg. The clan is real and used the name Carl, but Lippoldsberg does not produce a direct match — the lead is down-graded from “expect a hit” to “clan of interest, no match found.” (Caveat: handwriting read from the register images; spellings and exact days should be double-checked against the originals per project standard.)

1837–1841 baptisms line-read (27 Jun 2026) — window now complete 1831–1841

The remaining tail was line-read on 27 Jun 2026 (reg. 219778, entries No. 167–306, every year carried to its end-tally), completing the full 1831–1841 baptism window. New Gans entries:

  • Justine Caroline Gans — bapt. 1 Oct 1837 (b. 16 Sep), Nr. 74; Johann Christoph Gans × Marie Wilhelmine née Hartung.
  • Carl August Gans — bapt. 18 Mar 1838 (b. 26 Feb), Nr. 40; Johann Daniel Gans × Karoline née Gela of Drüssel; godf. Johann Christian Gans — marginal note records a death in 1840.
  • Rosine Auguste Gans — bapt. 22 Dec 1839 (b. 6 Dec), Nr. 74; same Joh. Christoph Gans × Hartung couple.
  • Gans as parent/godparent: Sophie Wilhelmine Charlotte Gans (mother in the illeg. baptism of Dorette Elise Siebrecht, 14 Jul 1839) and Maria Wilhelmine Gans née Hartung (godmother, Sep 1839).

Bearing on Charles. A Carl Gans does exist in-window — Carl August Gans, b. 26 Feb 1838 — but he is not Charles (b. 1838 not 1835; infant death 1840; Tagelöhner-Gans of Nr. 40). No Konze/Kontze/Contzen child and no −nzen/−nsen child surname appears in 1837–1841 (the −hausen names — Wellhausen, Bardenhausen — are not −nsen). Borderline, flagged for a Kurrent eye: a mother née “Gontz” (1840) and a “Gauß/Gaus” family (1841) — distinct spellings from Gans. NB: the “1835 Justine / August” Gans entries noted above may overlap these 1837–38 records given the known year-numeral calibration risk — reconcile against the AI transcription / Peter Schräder.

Remaining ways Charles could still be a Gans: a Gans baptism in a neighbouring parish; birth elsewhere with later family migration; or that he carried a different surname in Germany and “Gannsen” is American.

Remaining follow-ups

Lippoldsberg yields no direct Charles match.
  • Neighbour ring — now COMPLETE (27 Jun 2026): the adjacent Weser/Diemel parishes were all swept for a Carl on a pool surname b. 1833–41 — Gieselwerder, Oedelsheim, Vernawahlshausen, Gewissenruh, Bodenfelde, Bursfelde/Glashütte (=Kirchspiel Fürstenhagen) and Wahmbeck, all negative. Gottstreu (its own Waldenser-colony register, Archion reg. 219073) was the last gap and is now searched & ELIMINATED (Session 134): the 1835 Taufen has no male b. 26 Jan (only twin Bock girls), and the alphabetical Tauf-index shows no Gans/Konze/Kontze/Contze anywhere 1830–~1855. Wülmersen needs no separate sweep — it is a manor estate in the parish of Deisel (Kirchspiel Trendelburg), already exhaustively searched. The Gans clan plainly moved between these villages, but none yields a direct Charles match.

  • Re-read the 1851–1855 confirmations (corrected leaves) for younger Gans sons b. ~1837–41.
  • Shortcut: Kunze’s printed Lippoldsberg OSB (~€40) lists the Gans households with dates.
