Schierstein — Parish Research Log

50.042 N · 8.192 E · Wiesbaden (Rhine) · ~170 km S · compiled 21 June 2026 · updated 27 June 2026 (Archion confirmation-track sweep)
ELIMINATED

Schierstein is the Rhine parish (a district of Wiesbaden) nearest to the hamlet of Freudenberg — the 2020 gazetteer match for an alternative “Freundburg → Freudenberg-on-Rhine” reading of the enlistment place-name, ~170 km from Deisel. No Konze/Kontze pool. Already eliminated on geography and on the 2020 baptism search; now also closed on the confirmation track for the years Archion holds.

On 27 June 2026 the Wiesbaden-Schierstein Konfirmandenregister 1854–1875 (Zentralarchiv der EKHN, Dekanat Wiesbaden-Rheingau) was swept. Key caveat: Schierstein’s standalone confirmation book on Archion jumps 1817 → 1854 — the confirmations of 1818–1853 are not digitised, so for the widened 1846–1856 window (births 1833–1841 confirmed at 13–15) only the 1854, 1855 and 1856 classes can be read here (1846–1853 rests on the 2020 baptism elimination + geography). All three classes were read entry-by-entry: negative. The surname pool is uniformly local Rheingau/Nassau (Birk, Ehrengart, Zipperle, Thiele, Merten, Georg, Nicolai, Stiller, Genßler, Schleim, Bleichenbach, Esch, Wehnert, Baum, Otto, Seipel, Mahl, Segert, John, Klett, Wieland, Schade, Reimann, Sack, Wittelobe, Schäfer, Maus, Bein, Strauch, Eichhorn, Reutter, Grimm…); no Konze / Kontze / Gansen / Gonsen, and the forename Carl appears only on local families (Carl Ehrengart, Carl Thiele, Carl Schade).
