Hümme — Parish Research Log

51.544 N · 9.407 E · ~2.8 km SSW of Deisel · compiled 21 June 2026 · updated 23 June 2026
REOPENED · Kontze fathers documented

Correction (22 June 2026): the earlier “no Kontze-fathered children” finding is overturned. A page-by-page read of every confirmand in the Hümme Konfirmationen register (Archion 219529) for the classes of 1833 through 1841 found two Kontze-surnamed boys, each with a Kontze father resident in Hümme: Heinrich Kontze (confirmed 1837; b. 22 May 1823), son of Johannes Kontze, Leinenweber, recorded “gebürtig von Stammen” (the neighbouring village; an earlier read had “Naumann” — see the follow-up on settling this); and Wilhelm Kontze (confirmed 1841; b. 29 Jan 1827), son of Heinrich Kontze and Marie Elisabeth geb. Büngener. A third, Marie Wilhelmine Laubert (confirmed 1836), is a child of the Laubert × “geb. Kontze” marriage — the same couple whose son Johannes was confirmed in the 1831 class. A full sweep of the Hümme Taufen 1830–1863 register (completed 23 June) pulled all ten Kontze baptisms it indexes, resolving into five Kontze father-households (two distinct men named Johannes, plus Georg Friedrich, Friedrich, and Wilhelm) — see the baptism table below.

No confirmand born 26 Jan 1835 appears in this window (such a child would be confirmed c.1849, beyond the 1833–41 classes searched). But the Hümme Kontze line was not extinct as previously stated: Johannes, Heinrich and Georg Friedrich Kontze were householders here into the 1840s, and Johannes Kontze’s origin note (“gebürtig von Stammen,” contested with “Naumann”) is a fresh lead to the adjacent parish. The earlier-confirmed entries still stand — “Johann Conrad Kontze” (burial 1849) and “Friedrich Konze aus Trendelburg” (marriage 1843). The surname is written with a looping ‑z/‑e flourish (can read as “Kontye”).

Kontze in the 1833–1841 confirmation classes — searched 22 June 2026

Distinct from the “any Carl” sweep above: a page-by-page read of every confirmand in the Hümme Konfirmationen register (Archion 219529 ↗) for each class from 1833 to 1841. Three Kontze records surfaced — two of them Kontze-surnamed boys, i.e. resident Kontze fathers. The classes of 1833, 1834, 1835, 1838, 1839 and 1840 contained no Kontze of any kind.

US-trail follow-up — worked 21 June 2026

The standing follow-up “Christoph & Friedrich Kontze US trail” was taken back to the open web (FamilySearch full-text, the NARA AAD Germans to America index and HESAUS had already been exhausted in earlier sessions). Result: one emigrant candidate is now eliminated, one remains cold, and the “Friedrich → New York City” lead is confirmed a phantom.

Christoph Kontze (b. 13 Feb 1814, Hümme) — the genuine emigrant

Two unverified US candidates were on file from the 7 June 2026 sweep. They now resolve differently:

CandidateOpen-web finding (21 Jun 2026)Verdict
“Christian Kontze,” 1850 census, Atlanta, DeKalb Co., Georgia This is Ernst Christian Kontz, 1814 – 2 Apr 1881, a documented Atlanta pioneer (settled 1848): born in Frankfurt am Main, apprenticed as a bootmaker, spent the early 1840s in France, then married Elizabeth Trabert (1821–1878, of Ostheim vor der Rhön) in New York; daughter Emelie b. New York 1846; Atlanta shoe merchant & co-founder of the City Brewery; trilingual (German/French/English), Liederkranz and Odd Fellows member; Lutheran; buried under the Egyptian-revival KONTZ monument at Oakland Cemetery. His 1848 declaration of intent and 1866 citizenship certificate survive in the Atlanta History Center’s Kontz family papers (MSS 545). Sources: Cooper, “The Kontz Family Home,” pt. 1 & pt. 2; AHC finding aid MSS 545; Find a Grave 80746375. ✗ ELIMINATED (high confidence). Born Frankfurt am Main, not Hümme (~140 km N); given name Ernst Christian, not Christoph; an educated urban merchant, not an orphaned Hümme farm boy. The only overlaps — birth year 1814, German-Lutheran, shoemaking — are coincidental. (The Session-96 census reading “wife Mary” was wrong — the wife was Elizabeth Trabert; “daughter Amelia b. NY ~1845” = Emelie, b. NY 1846 ✓.)
“Christopher Kontze” / “Christopher Kuntz,” Hardin Co., Ohio tax rolls 1840–47 No further open-web trace: he is gone from the county by the 1850 census, with no Find a Grave, FS-tree, or open record fixing his age or birthplace. The spelling “Kontze” (1840–44) is exact and rare, but nothing names him. STILL UNVERIFIED — cold. Resolving him now needs gated/offline US records (Hardin Co. tax & deed originals; a post-1850 census walk under Kuntz/Koontz variants).

Net: the open-web US trail for Christoph is essentially exhausted — the Atlanta candidate is removed, the Ohio candidate is cold. He remains the one Hümme Kontze male who vanishes; no name-faithful US record yet proves where he went.

Friedrich Kontze (FS GPK7-JYP) — “NYC post-1870” refuted

The follow-up’s “→ NYC post-1870” is the unsourced FamilySearch-tree note by user GeneDana (“d. after 1870, New York City”). It is refuted by the Hümme burial register: GPK7-JYP is the Leineweber Friedrich Kontze, b. 19 Sep 1816 (the log’s “b. ~1825” was loose), who died at Hümme 5 Aug 1873 (Magenkrebs) — 219538 S.255, img 1234, entry 1235. He never emigrated. Resolved.

Confirmation sweep 1833–1841 — worked 22 June 2026

Per a follow-up request, the Hümme Konfirmationen 1831–1934 register (Archion churchRegister 219529 — a book not previously cited in this project; register in the viewer) was read in full for the confirmation classes of 1833–1841 (images 9–23; confirmands born ~1819–1827). The aim was to catch any unaccounted Kontze male of that cohort — a second “vanishing” emigrant in the mould of Christoph. Result: none. No Kontze/Konze confirmand in 1833–1841 is new to the reconstruction, and no Gansen/Gannsen variant appears at all. Every Kontze entry in the window is already documented, and every male stayed in Germany:

Hümme baptisms 1830–1863 — Kontze children (searched 22 June 2026)

Following the confirmation sweep, the Hümme Taufen 1830–1863 register (Archion 219532 ↗) was opened at its own alphabetical name-index. This overturns the “no Kontze-fathered baptism occurs in Hümme 1830–58” statement in the confirmation-sweep note above. The K leaf of the index (image 1476, permalink) carries roughly ten Kontze baptisms across the period — about six boys and four girls (given names as transcribed from the index, pending page-by-page confirmation): Conrad, Friedrich, Heinrich Friedrich, a second Conrad, Christoph Heinrich and Wilhelm; Anna Maria, Wilhelmine Friederike, Maria and Elise Amalie. A Kontze family was plainly resident and baptising children in Hümme throughout the register, not extinct.

One entry was read in full and verified against the image: Anne Rosine Kontze, born 28 Feb, baptised 1 March 1830 (Nro 15, image 1212, permalink) — daughter of Georg Friedrich Kontze, Tagelöhner (day-labourer) of Hümme, and Marie Christine geb. Kuhlmann, recorded “gebürtig von Eberschütz”; the godparents tie to the linen-weaver Christoph Kuhlmann of Eberschütz. Georg Friedrich Kontze is a third Kontze father in Hümme alongside Johannes and (Johann) Heinrich. A further attestation surfaced at Nro 5, image 1210: the grandmother of an illegitimate Thiele child was Anne Marie geb. Kontze, late wife of the late day-labourer Johannes Thiele — an older-generation Kontze woman married into the Thiele family.

Dedicated marathon scan (22 June 2026) pulled a second indexed baptism and a significant new fact. At Seite 32 (Nro 123, image 1240, permalink): Wilhelmine Friederike Kontze, baptised Sept/Oct 1834, daughter of Johannes Kontze, Leineweber, and Marie Magdalene geb. Uffelmann — with the explicit note that “this child was born three months after the father’s death.” So Johannes Kontze the Leineweber died ~mid-1834, leaving a posthumous daughter. This is the same Johannes Kontze named in the 1837 confirmation entry (father of Heinrich, b. 1823); the two wives — Catharine Elisabeth geb. Lichtefeld (mother of Heinrich; appears as a godmother in 1831, image 1218) and Marie Magdalene geb. Uffelmann (mother of Wilhelmine Friederike, 1834) — make him a twice-married man who died in 1834. The godmother of Wilhelmine Friederike is Marie Elisabeth geb. Kontze (the Laubert wife), tying the sibship together. A third Kontze child of the known Laubert × geb. Kontze couple also turned up in passing: Friedrich Laubert, baptised ~Oct 1832 (Seite 15, image 1223) — a Laubert, Kontze only as the mother’s maiden name.

All ten Kontze baptisms pulled — completed 23 June 2026

The full sweep is done: every one of the Kontze baptisms in the Taufen 1830–1863 index has now been read against the original image and cited. The register turned out to be well-ordered at image = Seite + 1208; the “shuffle/duplicate chaos” seen on 22 June was a stale-canvas artifact of the viewer (after a page-number jump it shows the previous leaf until a zoom action forces a redraw). Forcing a redraw after each jump resolved it. The ten baptisms fall into five Kontze father-households:

The five father-households: (1) Johannes Kontze the Leineweber — 1st wife Catharine Elisabeth geb. Lichtefeld (son Heinrich, b. 1823), 2nd wife Marie Magdalene geb. Uffelmann (Conrad 1833; Wilhelmine Friederike 1834, posthumous) — died ~mid-1834. (2) A second, distinct Johannes Kontze (Leineweber u. Tagelöhner) × Marie Christine geb. Hofedietz “von Naumann” — son Friedrich, 1841 (alive then, so not the same man). (3) Georg Friedrich Kontze, Tagelöhner / Bauaufseher, × Marie Christine geb. Kuhlmann of Eberschütz — Anne Rosine 1830, Heinrich Friedrich 1843, Wilhelm 1848. (4) Friedrich Kontze, Leineweber u. Tagelöhner, × Wilhelmine geb. Büngener — Conrad 1844, Christoph Heinrich 1847, Elise Amalie 1852 (likely the Friedrich Kontze whose 1843 marriage is already on file). (5) Wilhelm Kontze, Leineweber, × geb. Busch — Maria 1852 (plausibly the Wilhelm confirmed 1841, son of Heinrich). Together with Heinrich Kontze (× Marie Elisabeth geb. Büngener) from the confirmation register, this is a substantial, multi-household Kontze population in Hümme across the whole 1830–1863 register.

“Naumann” is a real place after all. The S.81 baptism records Marie Christine Hofedietz as “von Naumann” in a clear hand — so “Naumann” is a genuine place-name used in this parish, which reopens the “Naumann vs Stammen” question for Johannes Kontze’s origin in the 1837 confirmation entry (it may well read “Naumann” after all). A definitive re-read of 219529 img 16 is still the way to settle that single entry.

Method note for future reads of 219532: it is ordered at image = Seite + 1208 (title page img 1206; baptism Nro 1 / Seite 1 = img 1209; alphabetical K index leaf = img 1476). The viewer’s page-number box is stale-canvas prone — after typing an image number, click a zoom +/− button to force a redraw before reading the Seite header, or the screenshot will show the previous leaf. A full image→Seite map is saved alongside this log at germany/219532_image-seite-map.md.
