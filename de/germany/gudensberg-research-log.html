Gudensberg — Parish Research Log

51.180 N · 9.450 E · Kirchenkreis Fritzlar · ~42 km S · compiled 21 June 2026
ELIMINATED

Place-name candidate for “Deissen” / “Deisse.” The 1850 confirmation class was read (partial coverage); no Konze-pool or window male. First flagged as an on-site Tier-3 target, then found on Archion.

Confirmation-register sweep — “any Carl, 1833–1841”

Part of the June 2026 widened confirmation-register sweep across the Kreis Hofgeismar — looking for any boy named Carl/Karl born 1833–1841, surname open, who would surface in the 1847–1855 confirmation classes. This is the parish-specific row; the cross-parish ledger, the full list of boys named Carl that turned up, and the method/reliability notes live on Parish search coverage.

Classes 1848 through 1855 read in full — Charles’s entire realistic window plus margin. The forename Carl recurs — Carl Schäfer (1849), Carl Wilhelm Tiltner (1850), Carl Werner Jungeut (1852), Carl Christian Weinrich (1855), plus middle-name Carls — but never on an *n-s-n / Konze surname, and no 26-Jan-1835 Carl. Because Dissen & Grifte children were confirmed here (“Amt Gudensberg”), this register also covers those filials’ births 1833–1840. The 1855 class (read live 22 Jun 2026, permalink p/ea4e5b32da) closes the sweep — 1848–1855 uniform negative. The strongest of James Gunnis’s three “Deissen” candidates — and the only one with an online confirmation book for the window.

Konze / Kontze surname — present in Gudensberg?

No. No Konze / Kontze / *n·s·n surname appears in any Gudensberg record read to date. The Konfirmationen register 244437 ↗ lists every child confirmed regardless of forename, and its classes 1848–1855 were read boy-by-boy in full (births ~1833–1841) — the surnames are uniformly local Gudensberg / Amt-Gudensberg families (Schäfer, Tiltner, Jungeut, Weinrich, Götte, Becker, Bracke, Martin, Groß, etc.); not one Konze/Kontze among them. Because Dissen & Grifte children were confirmed here (“Amt Gudensberg”), those filials’ 1833–1840 cohorts are covered too, and their baptism books (Dissen 253403 1834–1841; Grifte 253444 1833–1841, both read in full) likewise record no Konze surname. So for Charles’s window the Konze/Kontze surname is absent from the Gudensberg cluster.

Update (27 June 2026): the residual gap below is now closed — the Gudensberg own Taufen book 244439 alphabetical name-index has been swept (next section). The Trauungen/Beerdigungen volumes remain unscanned but are low-priority given a clean baptism-index and confirmation-class negative.

Own baptism register 244439 (Taufen 1830–1844) — name-index swept (27 Jun 2026)

The earlier “residual on-site scan” caveat is now resolved. Gudensberg’s own town baptism register is “komplett transkribiert” and carries a built-in alphabetical name-index at the end of the reel (Bild ~508–525; columns Männlich | Seite | Weiblich | Seite, ordered by Seite within each letter). The index was read by eye for the full Konze/Gans pool (the index leaves are not served with usable OCR despite the transcription tag). The baptism body runs one opening per Seite at Seite = Bild − 248 (Bild 267 = Seite 19 = 1831). Result: clean negative for the entire pool.

Rigor note (Ganß vs. Groß). The first G-index entry — “…Carl…, Seite 19” — looked like “Ganß” at low magnification, which would have been a genuine pool hit. Verified at the baptism page (Bild 267 = Seite 19, p/9ac4dd7943 ↗) it is “Groß — Ferdinand Carl Hermann”, male, baptised 11 Sept 1831, father the local Amts-Schäfer (sheep-master) Groß × Caroline geb. Finckel/Vinckel, godfather Ferdinand Borchmann zu Oberkirchen — Groß, not Ganß, i.e. not a pool surname (the classic Kurrent Ganß/Groß confusion; always verify a “G…ß” index hit at the entry). The only other Carl in G is “Karl Goebel” (Seite 239, ~1844). So there is no Gans family at all in Gudensberg town — unlike the Weser-corridor parishes (Oedelsheim, Heisebeck, Bodenfelde).

Marriage & burial registers — name-indexes swept

To close the parish on every rite, the two in-window vital registers were also surname-scanned via their built-in alphabetical indexes. Both are negative for the entire Konze/Gans pool. A recurring red herring across all three Gudensberg indexes is “Jungkurt / Jungcurt” (a local Jung+Kurt family) — not to be confused with the pool surname Jungkonze.

With baptisms (1830–1844), marriages (1830–1863) and burials (1830–1851) all swept and all negative, the Konze/Gans pool is absent from every Gudensberg vital register — well beyond the 1833–1841 birth window. There was no Konze, Kontze, Konsen, Jungkonze, Gans, Ganß, Ganson or Conson family resident in Gudensberg town in this era.
