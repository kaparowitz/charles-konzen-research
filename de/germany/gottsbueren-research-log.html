Gottsbüren — Parish Research Log

51.552 N · 9.468 E · reg. 219049 (Taufen) · 219046 (Konfirmationen) · ~3.9 km SE of Deisel · compiled 21 June 2026, confirmation sweep added 22 June 2026, baptism-window sweep added 26 June 2026
ELIMINATED · claimed but unconfirmed

Jan–Feb 1835 baptisms searched live on Archion: no Konze-pool male, no illegitimate male. A Stammen 1833 marriage names Christian Ludwig Kontze as born in Gottsbüren — but Gottsbüren’s own books are entirely Kontze-free (no Kontze in baptisms 1804/06/08, marriages 1795–1806, or burials 1805–Jan 1825 read entry-by-entry; none 1827–33). Only the maternal Garland family is native here. A claimed but unconfirmed Konze tie (Andreas Kontze an in-migrant), not a documented Konze village.

Confirmation sweep added 22 June 2026. The separate Konfirmationen 1831–1932 book (reg. 219046) was read live for the “any Carl, born 1833–1841, surname open” window — the read this parish previously lacked (it had been cleared on its baptism/marriage/burial books alone). Every confirmation class across the window was read: the book runs 1844–1845 then 1848–1856 (no class held 1846/47), and classes 1848 through 1855 were all swept — the prime cohort for Charles, the 1849 and 1850 classes (boys born 1834–1835), plus 1853–1855, read in full. The result matches every other parish: the forename Carl recurs (~15 boys across these classes) but never once on a Konze-pool or *n-s-n surname, and no Carl matches Charles. The surnames are uniformly local — Schröder, Schäfer, Gerland, Hücking, Tolle, Naumann, Niemeyer, Köhler, Bremer, Engelhard. Per-image Archion permalinks are minted (below).

Reliability. Reads are by eye in Archion's hi-res viewer. The forename Carl (vs. Conrad/Johann) is dependable in Kurrent; exact birth-year digits and surname spellings are best readings, not certified transcriptions, and warrant a second eye (Peter Schräder / on-site). The verdict — no Carl on a Konze-pool surname — does not hinge on any single uncertain digit.

Window now swept end to end (1844–1856). A second pass (22 Jun 2026) added the 1853, 1854 and 1855 classes (above), so every confirmation class across Charles's whole 1833–1841 window — and beyond — has been read. The Konfirmationen section is bound badly out of order and duplicated (e.g. the 1845 leaf appears at both Bild 1775 and 1788; 1852 fragments recur at Bild 1773, 1776, 1785–87; the run is non-monotonic, e.g. Bild 1773 = 1852 but 1774 = 1844).

1846 & 1847 — apparently no class held. Across a broad leaf-by-leaf sweep of the shuffled section, the confirmed classes run 1844, 1845, then 1848, 1849 … 1856 — a clean 1845→1848 gap with no 1846 or 1847 leaf surfacing. This parallels the register's earlier multi-year gap (1831→1834) and most likely means no confirmation class was held at Gottsbüren in those two years (a pastoral vacancy or biennial schedule). To be confirmed on-site; but with 1844–1845 and 1848–1856 all read, no boy born 1831–1842 with a Konze-type surname exists in this book.

Baptism-register window sweep — Taufen 1830–1867 (reg. 219049), read 26 June 2026

The parish's own Taufregister had previously been read only for the narrow Jan–Feb 1835 window (Charles's stated birth month) plus the 1804/06/08 spot pages. This pass widened the read to cover Charles's whole plausible birth window and, via the book's own alphabetical index, the entire 1830–1867 run for any Konze-pool surname. Calibration: one written Seite per image, Seite = Bild − 793; Taufen title card = Bild 791; Nro runs continuously (not reset yearly); ≈45–55 baptisms/year (the parish covers filial hamlets, so long Aschener / Gerland / Dettmar clans dominate). Reads are by eye in Archion's full-screen hi-res viewer.

What was checkedResult
Alphabetical surname index — the book's own "mit Register" baptism index, Bild 1053 (A) → 1076 (Z), read leaf-by-leaf A–Z (every surname, via the viewer's AI "Erkannter Text" panel, hits verified against the image). Re-read with image numbers + permalinks 26 June 2026 (Session 127). ⚠️ The index is bound out of order, with duplicate and one blank leaf — so the letters are scattered (e.g. Bild 1062 is a D | K spread, not a clean opening); the earlier "K = Bild 1062R" note was right for K only by coincidence.No Konze-pool surname anywhere in 1830–1867.
K section — Bild 1062 (p/00940fbb92) + 1063 (p/04f2eea26d): Köster, Kurth, King, Köhler, Kaufmann, Kirschknecht, Kämpfe, Kümmel, Kümpel, König, Kunst, Kraft, Kluthe, Kindbe, Bury — no Konze / Kontze / Konsen / Konzen.
G section — Bild 1059 (p/7c7f01d0ca) + 1060 (p/e60d78dc37; duplicate G leaf at 1067): Gerland (native clan), Götze, Grabe, Gante, Gödchen, Gemmern, Garte, Grek, Gödecke — no Gansen / Gonsen / Gonson / Gannsen / Gans / Ganß.
Jungkonze: there is no I/J leaf in the index at all (alphabet runs …H → K → M, no I/J page; no "Jung-" form on any leaf) ⇒ none.
Conson: the alphabet jumps B→D (no C page), so C-variants would file under K — cleared. Index start (A) anchor: p/bd8ea39bc5 (Bild 1053). Only regex false-positive was "Blankenberg, Kons." (B, an abbreviation, not the surname). Corroborates the parish's other Kontze-free books.

Forename Carl appears three times, all on local families: Carl Friedrich Wilhelm Gerland (Nro 129, father Lorenz Wilhelm Gerland, mother née Grünewald), Carl Friedrich August Gerland (Nro 134, Gerland), and illegitimate Carl Heinrich Wiechard (Nro 139, mother's surname Wiechard). None Konze-pool; all born 1833.
January 1835 — the exact 26-Jan birth window (Seiten 49–50, Nro 196–201) read in fullNegative. The only January-1835 males are Heinrich Ludwig Baumann (b. 21st, father Joh. Heinrich Baumann, Zimmermeister) and Johann Ludwig Wiegand (father George Friedrich Wiegand, Leineweber) — both well-documented local families. No male born 26 January, no Carl, no Konze-pool surname. The remaining births are female (Euler, Schweinsberg, Dettmar) or an illegitimate girl (Marie Caroline, mother Wimmel).
1835, February–December (Seiten 51–64) scannedOne Carl-child only: illegitimate "Johann Carl," born 15 Feb 1835 (baptised 22 Feb), mother Gertrude Elisabeth Schröder (Seite 52, Nro 205). A genuine near-miss — an illegitimate Carl in early 1835 — but the date is wrong (15 Feb ≠ 26 Jan), Schröder is a common local Gottsbüren family, and there is no Konze connection. All other 1835 births carry local surnames (Lips, Blankenberg, Aschener, Gante, Reineke, Schweinsberg, Büttler, Gerland, Kurth, Schäfer, Dettmar).
1834 (autumn) and January 1836 spot-checkedSame local pattern (Kurth, Gerland, Schäfer; illegitimate males with Carl only as a middle name, e.g. "Theodor Carl Friedrich…"). No boy with rufname Carl on a Konze-pool surname; no match.

Net effect. Gottsbüren is now eliminated on the baptism track as well — previously it had been cleared on its confirmation, marriage and burial books and on the narrow 1835 baptism window only. The book's own index shows no Konze-type surname was ever baptised here across 1830–1867, and the exact 26-January-1835 birth window contains no matching male. The single early-1835 illegitimate Carl (Johann Carl Schröder, b. 15 Feb 1835) is a near-miss on forename alone — logged for completeness, but a very low probability of being Charles (wrong birth date, local Schröder surname, no Konze tie); not being actively pursued.

Reliability & scope. Index and entry reads are best-readings by eye, not certified transcriptions, and warrant a second eye (Peter Schräder / on-site) — but the no-Konze-surname verdict rests on the alphabetical index, not on any single uncertain entry. A complete entry-by-entry read of 1836–1841 was not done (the index already rules out the Konze surname in those years); it remains an optional follow-up.
