# Katherine Carolina Johnson, born Friedrike Catharina Caroline Amalia Gansen (L5N2-98F) — Life Sketch

> Drafted 12 June 2026 for the FamilySearch Life Sketch field. Grounded in the sources attached
> to the profile and the project page children/katherine-carolina.html.

---

Katherine Carolina Johnson was born 5 April 1870 in St. Louis, Missouri, and baptized Friedrike Catharina Caroline Amalia Gansen, the third of seven children of Charles Johnson Sr. — a Hessian-born immigrant and Civil War artillery sergeant — and Auguste Kreichelt, born in Clausthal, Hanover. She was baptized 22 May 1870 at St. Marcus German Evangelical Church (register p. 285, no. 6644) by Pastor Heinrich Braschler. Her four given names mirror her four sponsors — Friedrich Pillmann, Catharina Albrecht, Carl Kreichelt (her mother's brother), and Amalia Münke — a tightly intermarried circle of Kreichelt kin and soon-to-be kin: Pillmann had married just seven weeks earlier, and Catharina Albrecht would marry Carl Kreichelt ten months later.

Her baptism is also a famous record in the family's surname story: Pastor Braschler wrote "Gansen" here, then "Gonsen" for her sister Auguste two years later — the same scribe, two different spellings, evidence that the unstable surname came from her father's own Hessian speech and not the pastor's pen.

She grew up in the family's German Evangelical world on the south side of St. Louis, appearing in the 1880 census household on Jackson Street. As the family's name settled into its American form she became Katherine Carolina Johnson — "Katie" to her family. On 19 July 1887, at age seventeen, she married John T. Ruddy Sr. (1865–1935) in St. Louis, and they had three sons: Eugene J. (1887–1965), John Joseph Jr. (1889–1967), and John William E. Ruddy (born 1890). Her father's 1898 pension declaration lists her as "Kate Ruddy, born 8 April 1870" — a three-day drift from the church record.

The censuses of 1880 through 1940 find her in St. Louis all her life. She was twenty-six when her mother died in 1896 and thirty-three when her father died in 1903, one of the four children who survived them. Her obituary notes her membership in the Super Forest Woodmen Circle group 208. Katherine died 4 November 1949 in St. Louis, at age seventy-nine, and was buried 7 November 1949 at Resurrection Cemetery, survived by her brother Charles Johnson Jr. and her sister Bertha.
