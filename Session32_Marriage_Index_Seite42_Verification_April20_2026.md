## Critical Analytical Update: The Missing Marriage

### J.H. Konze × Wilhelmine Hildebrand — Where Did They Marry?

The marriage is now confirmed ABSENT from both Deisel church registers:
- **Register 218821** (1736-1796): Searched marriage section 1789-1796 — NEGATIVE (Session 31)
- **Register 218824** (1796-1831): Marriage index has NO Johann Heinrich Konze entry — NEGATIVE (this session)

Since the marriage must have occurred before January 1835 (when Entry 195's child was born), the possible explanations are:

1. **Marriage in Hildebrand's home parish**: Most common in German church practice — marriages were typically recorded in the bride's parish. If Wilhelmine Hildebrand was from a different village, the marriage would be in that village's church register.

2. **Marriage after 1831**: Register 218824 only covers to 1831. The next Deisel register (if one exists covering 1831-1835) would need to be checked. However, no such register has been identified in the Archion catalogue.

3. **Civil marriage record**: After Napoleonic-era reforms, some Kurhessen areas had civil registration alongside church records, though this varied by period.

**Assessment**: Option 1 (bride's parish) is most likely. This means identifying Wilhelmine Hildebrand's origin village is now a priority research task.

### The "Margaretha geb. Hildebr[and/un]" Sighting

On Seite 43 (year 1825), one entry mentions "Margaretha geb. [?]ildebrun" — this appears as a mother's maiden name (not the bride herself). If this reads "Hildebrand," it could indicate Hildebrand family presence in the Deisel area. This connection warrants further investigation.
