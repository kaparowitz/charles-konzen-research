### Critical Finding: Two Wilhelmine Hildebrands Confirmed

Image 31 contains the entry **Johannes Frenning, Schneider × Wilhelmine geb. Hildebrand** — a child born ~1833. This is clearly the same couple as Entry 198 on Image 40 (where the father was previously read as "Johannes Funning" — "Frenning" is the correct reading). This PROVES that Entry 198's Wilhelmine Hildebrand is a DIFFERENT woman from Entry 195's Wilhelmine Hildebrand (who married J.H. Konze).

Combined with Session 34's finding of the 1817-born Wilhelmine Hildebrand who married Johann Heinrich Thöne in 1849, there are now at least **TWO and possibly THREE** women named Wilhelmine Hildebrand in Deisel:

1. **Wilhelmine Hildebrand #1** — married J.H. Konze #2 (Kaufmann), mother of Entry 195 child (born Jan 1835). Marriage not found in any Deisel register. Likely born ~1800–1815.
2. **Wilhelmine Hildebrand #2** — married Johannes Frenning (Schneider), mother of children on Images 31 and 40 (Entry 198). Active in Deisel from at least 1833.
3. **Wilhelmine Hildebrand #3** — born 18.12.1817, daughter of Andreas Hildebrand and Maria Elisabeth née Birke. Married Johann Heinrich Thöne in 1849 as "eheliche Tochter" (unmarried). May be the same person as #1 or #2, but the 1817 birth date makes her only ~12 at the earliest Konze children and only ~15 at the Frenning child (Image 31) — young but not impossible for #2.

## Critical Negative Result: No J.H. Konze #2 × Hildebrand Children Before 1835

The most significant negative finding of this scan is the **complete absence of any J.H. Konze #2 × Wilhelmine Hildebrand children in the 1830–1834 range**. Every single image from 2 to 39 was examined, and no "Konze, Kaufmann" or "Konze × Hildebrand" combination was found until Entry 195 on Image 40 (January 1835).

### Implications

1. **Entry 195 may be their firstborn child**: If the couple married around 1833–1834, a January 1835 birth for their first child would be consistent.
2. **The couple may have lived elsewhere before 1835**: J.H. Konze #2 may have been a merchant (Kaufmann) who relocated to Deisel shortly before 1835. This would explain both the absence of earlier children AND the absence of a marriage record in Deisel.
3. **Earlier children may have been born in the bride's home parish**: If Wilhelmine Hildebrand #1 was from a different village (not Deisel), the couple may have initially lived there.
4. **The marriage likely occurred elsewhere**: Combined with the negative results from all three Deisel marriage registers (Sessions 33–34), the couple almost certainly married outside Deisel — possibly in Wilhelmine's home parish.
