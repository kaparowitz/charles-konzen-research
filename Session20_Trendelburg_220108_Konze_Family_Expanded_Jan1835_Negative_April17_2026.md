## Headline

Session 20 delivers two landmark results from Register 220108 (Trendelburg Baptisms 1830-1863):

1. **Carl Ludwig Konze identified as Trendelburg Ackerbürger** — the first Konze entry in the register (Entry 25, Seite 7, Image 1385) reveals that Carl Ludwig Konze, farmer-citizen at Trendelburg house Nr. 34, fathered a son Carl Wilhelm Konze (b. 15 September 1831, baptized 25 September 1831, d. 30 October 1869). The mother is Wilhelmine née Albrecht. The godfather is **Carl Wilhelm Hofeditz, Richter (judge) zu Sielen** — establishing a direct Konze–Hofeditz social connection in Trendelburg parish, consistent with the Deisel pattern where Andreas Konze b.1795 married Maria Sophia Hofeditz.

2. **January 1835 Charles Gannsen search: DEFINITIVE NEGATIVE** — a page-by-page scan of entries 118–131 covering November 1834 through March 1835, combined with a full review of the alphabetical index G section, confirms that **no child surnamed Konze, Gannsen, Gans, or any phonetic variant was baptized in Trendelburg during the period matching Charles Gannsen's 26 January 1835 birth date**. Charles Gannsen was not baptized in Trendelburg 220108.

Session 20 also completed the full K-section index catalog (3 Konze + 2 Jungkonze male entries), mapped the G-section index (no Gannsen/Gans entries), identified a Jungkonze godfather reference in 1834, and established image-to-Seite navigation calibrations for future sessions.

### Significance

- **Carl Ludwig Konze** is now identified as a Trendelburg-resident Konze-surname male with landholding status (Ackerbürger = farmer with civic rights). He lived at Trendelburg house Nr. 34.
- **Wilhelmine née Albrecht** — the Albrecht surname appears repeatedly in Trendelburg baptisms (e.g., Johann Heinrich Albrecht in entry 28/Image 1405, the Albrecht stillbirth in entry 131/Image 1416). The Konze–Albrecht marriage places the Konze family within Trendelburg's established civic families.
- **Carl Wilhelm Hofeditz, Richter zu Sielen** as godfather is a critical genealogical connection. The Hofeditz surname is the same as Maria Sophia Hofeditz, wife of Andreas Konze b.1795 in Deisel. A Hofeditz serving as godfather to a Konze child in Trendelburg suggests either (a) the Trendelburg Konze and Deisel Konze families share a Hofeditz connection through intermarriage or kinship networks, or (b) Carl Ludwig Konze and Andreas Konze b.1795 are related (possibly brothers, cousins, or uncle-nephew), with the Hofeditz family acting as godparent across both branches.
- **Carl Wilhelm Konze** (b. 1831, d. 1869) lived to age 38. His death at 38 is relatively young but not unusual for the period. He is a potential Charles Gannsen candidate only if the birth year is wrong — but 1831 vs. 1835 is a 4-year discrepancy that makes this match improbable.

## Move 4 — Jungkonze Godfather Reference in 1834

Entry 95 (Image 1420, year 1834, Seite 28) records a baptism with the godfather listed as **"Johannes Jungkonze, Knecht zu Stammen"** (farmhand at Stammen). This is significant because:

- It places a Jungkonze-surname individual in active social connection with Trendelburg families as late as 1834.
- "Knecht" (farmhand/servant) suggests Johannes Jungkonze was of modest social standing, contrasting with Carl Ludwig Konze's "Ackerbürger" civic status.
- Stammen is a village ~5 km southwest of Trendelburg, within the same parish catchment.
- This individual may be the same Ludwig Jungkonze "Schneider" (tailor) identified in the 1829 Trendelburg baptisms (Session 17), now appearing under the given name "Johannes" — or a different family member.
