## Headline

Session 22 searches the Friedrichsfeld Kirchenbuch (Register 218959) for Charles Gannsen's baptism. The search produces a NEGATIVE result for a male Konze/Kontze/Gannsen/Gans child born around January 26, 1835. However, it yields an unexpected POSITIVE discovery: a **Kontze colonist family** living in Friedrichsfeld in January 1835, with a female child (Entry 28) baptized in the same month as Charles Gannsen's birth.

Key outcomes:

1. **NEGATIVE for Charles Gannsen**: No male child with surname Konze, Kontze, Gannsen, Gans, or similar appears in the Friedrichsfeld baptism register for January-February 1835 (or the full year 1835).

2. **FOURTH Konze/Kontze father identified**: Entry 28 records the baptism of "Maria [Catharina?] Kontze," born early January 1835, daughter of **"[?]ard Kontze, Colonist, gebürtig von Friedrichsfeld."** The father's first name ends in "-ard" (Bernhard, Leonhard, Conrad, or Gerhard). This individual is DISTINCT from the three Konze fathers identified in Trendelburg Register 220108 (Carl Ludwig/Ackerbürger, Johann Christoph/Schieferdecker, Karl Wilhelm/Einlieger).

3. **Friedrichsfeld baptisms performed at Trendelburg**: All entries in the Friedrichsfeld register list the baptism location as "Trendelburg," confirming Friedrichsfeld was a filial community within the Trendelburg parish.

### Critical observations

1. **Sex = Female**: This child is NOT Charles Gannsen (who was male). Entry 28 records a daughter, not a son.

2. **Father's first name**: Ends in "-ard" — candidates include Bernhard, Leonhard, Conrad, Gerhard, or Eckhard. This does NOT match any of the three Konze fathers from Register 220108 (Carl Ludwig, Johann Christoph, Karl Wilhelm).

3. **Occupation = Colonist**: Friedrichsfeld was a planned colonial settlement (Huguenot/Waldensian colony) established in the 18th century. The father's designation as "Colonist" indicates he held a settler's plot in the village. The spelling "Kontze" (with "t") is a variant of Konze/Konse.

4. **"Gebürtig von Friedrichsfeld"**: The father is described as "originating from" or "a native of" Friedrichsfeld — meaning he was likely BORN there, not a recent migrant. This suggests the Kontze family had been in Friedrichsfeld for at least one generation before 1835.

5. **Baptism at Trendelburg**: Despite the child being born in Friedrichsfeld, the baptism took place in Trendelburg. This confirms Friedrichsfeld was a filial parish within the Trendelburg Kirchspiel (parish district). The Friedrichsfeld KB 218959 appears to be a DUPLICATE or PARALLEL record of baptisms that should also appear in the Trendelburg register 220108.

### Implication for Register 220108

If Friedrichsfeld entries were ALSO recorded in the Trendelburg register 220108, then Entry 28 (Maria [?] Kontze) should appear somewhere around Seite 7-10 of Register 220108 (year 1835, January). In Session 20, the January 1835 pages of 220108 were searched and showed entries #157-161 with no Konze child. However, if the Friedrichsfeld register uses a SEPARATE numbering sequence (starting with entry #28 for a child born in early 1835, whereas 220108 was at entry #157-161 in January 1835), the two registers clearly have independent numbering.

**This means**: The Friedrichsfeld KB 218959 is a SEPARATE parish register, not a subset of 220108. Some baptisms recorded in 218959 may NOT appear in 220108, and vice versa. This could explain why Charles Gannsen's baptism was not found in 220108 — if he was born in Friedrichsfeld, his entry might only appear in 218959.

BUT: The 1835 Friedrichsfeld entries (#28-33) contain no male Konze/Kontze/Gannsen child. So even if 218959 is a separate register, Charles is not in it.

## Structural Discovery: Friedrichsfeld as Filial Parish

The fact that ALL Friedrichsfeld baptisms were conducted at Trendelburg church confirms:

1. **Friedrichsfeld had no independent church** — it was served by the Trendelburg pastor
2. **Two parallel registers existed**: 218959 (Friedrichsfeld-specific) and 220108 (main Trendelburg parish)
3. **Independent numbering**: Friedrichsfeld entries are numbered starting from 1 each year, separate from the Trendelburg register's numbering
4. **Cross-referencing needed**: Entries in 218959 may or may not be duplicated in 220108. The relationship between the two registers needs further investigation.
