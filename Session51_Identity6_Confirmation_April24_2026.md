# Session 51 — April 24, 2026
## Identity 6 Confirmed as Klaus Andreas Kontze; Wilhelmine Kontze (b. 1800) Identified

---

## Headline

Session 51 made a **single high-leverage finding** that resolves multiple open questions from Session 50: a baptism entry on Image 1660 of Deisel KB 218824 records the May 20, 1800 birth of **Wilhelmine Kontze**, daughter of **Klaus Andreas Kontze** (the man whose surname was originally written as "Schildknecht" then crossed out and corrected to "Kontze") and **Catharina Elisabeth née Thilin**, at **House Nr. 29 in Deisel**.

The match on House Nr. 29 confirms that **Identity 6 (the "Andreas Konze of Deisel No. 29" introduced provisionally in Session 50) and Klaus Andreas Kontze are the same man.** This in turn forces a re-reading of Entry 152's mother: the partially-illegible maiden name read as "Gräfe" in Session 49 is almost certainly **Thilin** instead. And it provides a strong identification for **Wilhelmine geb. Konze** — the mother of the Marie Köster baptized in Deisel in November 1828 (Entry 155 of burial register 218851) — who at age 28 fits cleanly with this Wilhelmine Kontze born in 1800.

Two priorities (P3 = Identity 6, P4 = Maria Elisabeth Brandt) were completed via direct file edits to `Andreas_Konze_Identity_Disentanglement.md`. Three priorities (P2 baptism lookup, P5 Wilhelmine baptism, P5 Marie Elisabeth baptism) were partially executed on Archion, with the Wilhelmine baptism found and the Johannes (1810) baptism still to be located. Four priorities (P6, P7, P8, P9) were deferred.

## Priority 2 (P2) and Priority 5 (P5) — PARTIAL: Wilhelmine Kontze 1800 baptism found

### Method

Deisel KB 218824 (Church Register 1796–1831, 1893 images) was opened on Archion and Image 1660 was examined at zoom levels 11–13. This is the same image Session 36 had identified as containing the 1799 baptism of **Catharine** Kontze (daughter of Klaus Andreas Kontze × Catharina Elisabeth née Thilin) — a finding that established Klaus Andreas Kontze in the Deisel record.

### The new finding — Wilhelmine Kontze, b. 20 May 1800

A second baptism entry on the same Image 1660 (or its facing page within the 1660 spread, January–April 1800 section) was read at zoom 12 and 13:

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Child | **Wilhelmine** Kontze |
| Father | **Klaus Andreas Kontze** (surname originally written "Schildknecht," crossed out, corrected to "Kontze" — same correction pattern as the 1799 Catharine entry) |
| Mother (Ux. = uxor = wife) | **Catharina Elisabeth née Thilin** |
| Birth | 20 May 1800 (recorded as "nat. 5.20 h. 2 postmerid" = born 5/20 at 2 PM) |
| House Nr. | **29** |
| Godparent (Gevatterin) | **Anna Catharina, vng. (= wife of) Johann Henrich Kontzen** |

The godparent is the same Anna Catharina (widow of Johann Henrich Kontze, son of Andreas I) who served as godparent at the 1799 Catharine baptism. Her recurring presence confirms the family continuity.

### Significance for Identity 6

**Identity 6 = Klaus Andreas Kontze.** The Image 1660 Wilhelmine entry provides three independent points of agreement with Entry 152:

| Datum | Image 1660 Wilhelmine | Entry 152 Johannes |
|-------|------------------------|--------------------|
| Father's first name | Klaus Andreas | Andreas |
| Mother's first name | Catharina (Elisabeth) | Catharina (Catharine) |
| House number | **29** | **29** |
| Mother's maiden name | **Thilin** | partially illegible, Session 49 read as "Gräfe" |

The match on House Nr. 29 is decisive — Deisel was a small village and house numbers were stable surname-equivalents. The wife's first name is the same, and the husband is named Andreas in both. The mother's maiden name discrepancy resolves itself: **the Session 49 reading "Gräfe" should be re-examined and is most likely "Thilin."** This re-read can be confirmed in Session 52 by returning to Entry 152 in burial register 218851.

### Significance for Wilhelmine geb. Konze (Entry 155)

**Wilhelmine geb. Konze** — the mother of Marie Köster (b. 1 Nov 1828) in Entry 155 — is most likely **this Wilhelmine Kontze (b. 20 May 1800)**, daughter of Klaus Andreas Kontze.

| Wilhelmine Kontze (b. 1800) | Wilhelmine geb. Konze (Entry 155 mother) |
|---|---|
| Born May 1800 | Bore daughter Marie 1 November 1828 |
| Age 28 in 1828 | Childbearing — fits |
| Father Klaus Andreas Kontze, House Nr. 29 | Married Ludwig Köster (Ackermann) |
| Konze surname | Konze maiden name |

The Konze-Köster marriage pattern — already documented in the Konrad Konze × Anna Catharina Köster line — extends here: Klaus Andreas Kontze's daughter married into the Köster family. Wilhelmine's possible second marriage to J.H. Schildknecht (Session 36 Image 16) is also consistent with the Klaus Andreas / Schildknecht surname alias documented in the 1799 baptism entry — Wilhelmine would have known the Schildknecht side of her own family name.

### What was NOT found in Session 51

1. **The 1810 baptism of Johannes Konze.** Navigation of KB 218824 to find October 1810 was complicated by an irregular images-per-year ratio in the register. Pages observed:
   - Image 1660 — 1800 (Jan–Apr)
   - Image 1670 — 1803–1804
   - Image 1690 — 1804 (later in year)
   - Image 1715 — header reads "Getauft im Jahr **1815**" (both pages)
   - Image 1700 — header appears to read "1821" (re-read recommended; may be mis-identified)

   The non-monotonic apparent year sequence between images 1700 and 1715 suggests the register may have non-chronological binding or that one of the year headers was misread. **A targeted scan of images ~1697–1710 should locate the October 1810 baptism of Johannes Konze.** This is held over to Session 52.

2. **Marie Elisabeth Konze's baptism.** Not yet located. Likely born 1800–1812, possibly a sister of Wilhelmine (i.e., another daughter of Klaus Andreas Kontze × Catharina Elisabeth Thilin). Search recommended in the same image range.
