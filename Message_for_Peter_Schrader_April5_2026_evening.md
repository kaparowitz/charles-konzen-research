## What I Did NOT Find — and Why This Matters

**Heinrich Wilhelm Konze (b. 12.2.1832) — NOT in 1846 or 1847.** I checked all 22 entries in 1846 and all entries in 1847. He is absent.

**Maria Sabine Konze (b. 10.1.1834) — NOT in 1848 or 1849.** I checked all 21 entries in 1848 and all 26 entries in 1849. She is absent.

**No Konze entries at all from 1849 through 1855.** I scanned every year through 1855 (~160+ individual entries). Zero Konze of any kind.

Meanwhile, other Friedrichsfeld families (Graff, Aulenbürger, Brauer, etc.) continued to confirm their children at Trendelburg throughout this entire period. The parish was functioning normally — the Konze family simply was no longer there.

## The Key Conclusion

**The entire Andreas Konze family departed Trendelburg/Friedrichsfeld between mid-1843 and early 1846.** After Justus Ernst's confirmation in June 1843, no Konze child ever appears again in the Trendelburg confirmation register.

This is a roughly 2.5-year window. By June 1843, Andreas's second wife Maria Sophia had been dead for nearly 8 years. The children were motherless. Andreas may have already begun his journey toward Mainz (where he died in 1854 as a factory worker). The younger children — Heinrich Wilhelm (~11–14), Maria Sabine (~9–12) — would have needed to be placed somewhere.

This creates a significant gap in the documentary record and helps explain why tracing Charles Gannsen back to this family has been so difficult. After the 1843 confirmation, Justus Ernst (age ~13) disappears from all known German records.

## Questions for You

2. **Do you have any records for Heinrich Wilhelm Konze between his 1832 birth and his 1858 death?** He was not confirmed at Trendelburg (expected ~1846) — was he confirmed elsewhere? His death record might indicate where he was living.

3. **Is there a Trendelburg/Friedrichsfeld departure register (Abgangsregister) or similar record** that might document when Andreas or the children formally left the parish?

4. **Could you check the Trendelburg Baptisms 1830–1863 register** for any Konze births in 1835? Maria Sophia died 15 October 1835 — a child conceived November 1834 (after August's birth) could have been born mid-1835. This would be a direct match for Charles Gannsen's stated birth year.

## Significance for Charles Gannsen

Justus Ernst Konze (b. 14 Nov 1829) remains the strongest candidate:
- Confirmed at Trendelburg 1843, then untraced
- Only surviving male son with no recorded death
- His birth year (1829) is ~5–6 years earlier than Charles's stated 1835, but census ages were often inaccurate
- The family's sudden departure from Friedrichsfeld between 1843–1846 creates exactly the kind of documentary gap that would make an emigrant hard to trace

The alternative possibility — an unrecorded child born ~1835 before Maria Sophia's death — would be an even closer match for Charles's stated age. The baptism register is the key to confirming or ruling this out.
