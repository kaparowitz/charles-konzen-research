# Session 89 — Stammen Kontze marriage + burial; a new Konze location (Gottsbüren)

**Date:** 6 June 2026 · live Archion (signed in). Goal: close the Stammen Kontze branch — find the Christian Ludwig Kontze × Anne Gertrud Temme marriage (→ Christian Ludwig's origin/parents) and the burials.

## 1. MARRIAGE — found (the key result)
**Stammen Trauungen 1830–1937 (reg. 220078), image 566, entry 14, year 1833:**
- **Groom: Christian Ludwig Kontze**, Tagelöhner — *"des verstorbenen Andreas Kontze und seiner ebenfalls verstorbenen [ersten] Ehefrau Marie Elisabeth, geborene Garland, **gebürtig von Gottsbüren**,"* age **26 years 6 months** → **born ~November 1806**.
- **Bride: Anne Gertrud Temme** of Stammen — daughter of the late Johann Conrad Temme × late Anne Catharine née Mei[?]; age 32y6m → born ~Nov 1800.
- Proclamations at **Carlshafen** (12/19/26 May 1833); **married 9 June 1833**.

**The load-bearing datum:** Christian Ludwig Kontze was **born in Gottsbüren** (Reinhardswald edge, Amt Sababurg / Kreis Hofgeismar — ~4 km from Deisel), son of **Andreas Kontze × Marie Elisabeth née Garland**. So a Konze family lived in **Gottsbüren in the early 1800s**.

## 2. BURIAL — stillborn confirmed
**Stammen Beerdigungen 1830–1936 (reg. 220072), image 843, entry 58, Haus Nr. 21:** *"…Kontze, des Tagelöhners Christian Ludwig Kontze und seiner Ehefrau Anne Gertrud geborene Temme, ehelicher **todtgeborner Sohn**"* — born Stammen **14 März 1835**, buried **16 März 1835, abends 5 Uhr**. Matches exactly.

## 3. The couple LEFT Stammen
The Stammen burial register's alphabetical index (1830–1936) was checked: **neither Christian Ludwig Kontze nor Anne Gertrud (née Temme) Kontze is in it.** The only indexed Kontze is "Elise geb. Decker" (a later, separate entry). ⇒ The couple did **not die in Stammen** — they relocated after 1835 (the stillborn was their only Stammen child). **Where they went is open — emigration is a live possibility, and would make this couple relevant to the American side.**

## 4. ⚠ CORRECTION — Gottsbüren is a Konze location (the June sweep missed it)
The 4 June sweep marked **Gottsbüren "no Konze presence"** — but that was a sample of the **1830s** baptism book (Taufen 1830–1867). Christian Ludwig was born ~1806 and his father Andreas Kontze lived there in the **early 1800s**, in registers that book doesn't cover. Both facts are consistent: a Konze family in Gottsbüren ~1800–1810, gone by the 1830s (Christian Ludwig had moved to Stammen). **Gottsbüren is therefore added to the Konze cluster**; the "no Konze" statement is corrected to "no Konze in the 1830s sample; Konze present in the early 1800s (Andreas Kontze × Marie Elisabeth Garland)."

## 5. A new (≈7th) Andreas Kontze
**Andreas Kontze of Gottsbüren** × **Marie Elisabeth née Garland** (both dead by 1833; he had at least a "first wife," so possibly remarried) — father of Christian Ludwig (b. ~1806). This Andreas is **not** matched to any of the six Andreas identities in `Andreas_Konze_Identity_Disentanglement.md` (none was placed in Gottsbüren with a Garland wife). He is a new node — likely a son/grandson of one of the documented Deisel/Trendelburg Andreas men who settled in nearby Gottsbüren, but that link is not yet established.

## Effect on the distribution claim
The cluster is now **six adjacent Diemel-valley/Reinhardswald villages — Trendelburg · Deisel · Friedrichsfeld · Stammen · Hümme · Gottsbüren — all within ~5–6 km**, with the surname still absent from the wider ring. The hyper-local conclusion is **strengthened, not weakened** (one more adjacent village in the pool); only the specific "Gottsbüren = none" line needed correcting.

## ⚠ UPDATE (later 6 June, Session 90) — Gottsbüren's own registers show NO Kontze; claim hedged
Reading Gottsbüren's pre-1830 combined book (**Kirchenbuch 1767–1826, reg. 219040**, "ohne Register"):
- **Christian Ludwig's 1806 baptism was NOT found** — the full 1806 baptisms (plus spot-checks of 1804 and 1808) contain **no Kontze/Konze/Contze**.
- **No Kontze marriage** in 1795–1806 either.
- BUT **Marie Elisabeth Garland's family is native and well-documented in Gottsbüren** — a "Garland/Gerland" blacksmith family (patriarch David Garland, d. by 1797; daughters married locally, e.g. Anna Elisabeth Garland × Nicolaus Blankenberg 1805).
**Reinterpretation:** **Andreas Kontze was an in-migrant**, not a Gottsbüren native — he married into the local Garland family; the marriage and Christian Ludwig's baptism are **not in Gottsbüren's registers as read**. So the 1833 "gebürtig von Gottsbüren" is a single stated-origin datum **not corroborated by Gottsbüren's own books**.
**Corrected status of Gottsbüren:** *not* a confirmed Konze village — it is the **stated birthplace of one Kontze man (Christian Ludwig), unconfirmed in its registers**, with the maternal (Garland) family native there. The confident "sixth Konze village" framing from earlier today is withdrawn; site files corrected. The confirmed Konze cluster remains **five villages: Trendelburg · Deisel · Friedrichsfeld · Stammen · Hümme.**
**Still unread (where the answer likely is):** the Gottsbüren **burial section** of 219040 and **219043 (1827–1834)** — Andreas/Marie Elisabeth's deaths (Andreas's age/origin would be stated there); marriage years 1801–02 and 1807+; and the real question — **which parish Andreas Kontze came from** (Deisel/Trendelburg remains the natural candidate, since that is where the surname is native and where an "Andreas Kontze" recurs).

## ⚠ FINAL UPDATE (Session 91, later 6 June) — Gottsbüren burials 1805–1825 read: NO Kontze
An entry-by-entry read of Gottsbüren KB 219040's burial section, images 653–697 (≈ Oct 1805 – Jan 1825, every entry-opening name checked; all four "Andreas" burials zoom-verified and eliminated):
- **No Andreas Kontze burial. No Marie Elisabeth (Garland) Kontze burial. No Kontze burial of any kind.** (Combined with the 1827–33 negative: Gottsbüren's burials are Kontze-free across 1805–1833 except the unread sliver Feb 1825–end 1826.)
- The Garland burials, by contrast, are numerous (incl. Johann Justus Gerland, Tagelöhner, †23 Apr 1822 age 39y8m; Hans George Gerland, Tagelöhner, †30 Sep 1824 age 51y3m — Marie Elisabeth's probable kin).
**Conclusion: Gottsbüren's registers are entirely Kontze-free — baptisms, marriages, AND burials (1795–1833 read).** The Andreas Kontze family did not live or die in Gottsbüren. Christian Ludwig's 1833 "gebürtig von Gottsbüren" most plausibly reflects his **mother's** home village (the native Garland family) — perhaps a birth at the maternal grandparents' — while the Kontze household itself was based in another parish. **Andreas Kontze's actual home parish (Deisel/Trendelburg the natural suspects) is the open question**; his burial will be wherever the family actually lived.


## ⚠ UPDATE (Session 92) — the "Andreas of Trendelburg b.1787" hypothesis is REFUTED
Tested whether Christian Ludwig's father "Andreas Kontze" = the Trendelburg Andreas Kontze (bap. 1787, d. 1830). Trendelburg KB 220096:
- The **8 March 1807 marriage** of that Andreas is to **Wilhelmine Grossbernd**, and the entry calls him a **bachelor** ("hinterlassener ehl. Sohn", NOT *Witwer* — the scribe flags widowers elsewhere on the same page). A 20-year-old bachelor in 1807 cannot be the already-widowed father of a child born ~Nov 1806.
- No "Andreas Kontze × Garland" marriage and no first-wife (Marie Elisabeth/Garland) burial exists in Trendelburg (marriage index + 1803–08 entries; burial index + 1806–08 entries all read), nor in Deisel (218824 burial index, full).
**So Andreas Kontze (Christian Ludwig's father) remains an unplaced, separate man** — not the Trendelburg Andreas, not in Deisel. His and Marie Elisabeth Garland's home parish is still unidentified (and is NOT Gottsbüren, Trendelburg, or Deisel). The Stammen/Gottsbüren branch is real but currently floats free of the documented tree.
