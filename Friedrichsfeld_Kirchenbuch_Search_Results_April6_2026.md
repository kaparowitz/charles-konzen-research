## Analysis and Implications

### 1. The Friedrichsfeld register IS the correct source for Kontze baptisms

This search definitively answers the question raised by the Trendelburg Taufen search (which found ZERO Konze entries): the Kontze family's baptisms were recorded in the **separate Friedrichsfeld Kirchenbuch**, not the Trendelburg baptism register. The Kontze family appeared as WITNESSES in the Trendelburg register but had their own children's baptisms recorded in Friedrichsfeld.

### 2. Confirmation of Peter Schrader's data

Both Maria Sabina and August's birth dates match Peter's records exactly:
- Maria Sabina: 10 January 1834 ✓
- August: 15 October 1834 ✓

This confirms Peter was working from this same Friedrichsfeld register (or a Familienbuch derived from it).

### 3. No child born ~1835 to Andreas Kontze

**This is the most significant negative finding.** The index confirms there is no Kontze/Konze entry between page 13 (August, October 1834) and page 80 (an unbaptized Konze child from the ~1860s). The page-by-page scan of 1835 entries (images 1340–1343) confirmed zero Kontze entries for the entire year.

Since Maria Sophia Hofeditz died on 15 October 1835, any child she bore would have been born before that date. No such child exists in this register.

**This rules out Hypothesis #3** (that Charles Gannsen was an unrecorded legitimate child baptized in the Friedrichsfeld register). He was NOT baptized here.

### 4. Illegitimate births WERE recorded in this register

The entry for Maria Koch (image 1339, page 14) and the Dölfer/Senne child (image 1340) prove that illegitimate births were documented in this register, complete with paternity declarations. If Charles had been born illegitimate to a Friedrichsfeld mother, there should be a record — unless the mother was NOT from Friedrichsfeld.

### 5. Heinrich Wilhelm Kontze entry (page 5)

The index reveals a **Heinrich Wilhelm Kontze** baptized on page 5 of the register (approximately 1830–1831). This is very likely the Heinrich Wilhelm who:
- Was confirmed (presumably) in the Trendelburg register
- Married Ulrike Melusine Pappenheim (b. 06.06.1832) on 15.06.1856 in Stammen
- Died 19.02.1858 in Trendelburg

This entry has NOT yet been examined in detail (it's on image ~1327, in the earliest pages of the register).

### 6. Two unbaptized Konze children (pages 80 and 88)

The index records two "Konze, ungetauft" (unbaptized) entries from the 1860s — one male (p. 80) and one female (p. 88). Note the spelling shifts to "Konze" (without the 't'). These are likely children of a LATER generation of the Konze family who remained in Friedrichsfeld. These entries have NOT yet been examined in detail.
