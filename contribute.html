Contribute or correct

If you have something to add, correct, or contradict — here’s how to get it into the research.

This site is a working document. Descendants, researchers, archivists, and curious readers are all encouraged to push back. Every conclusion on the site is held in an explicit Confirmed / Working / Open / Eliminated state precisely so that new evidence can move things between states without rewriting the surrounding case.

Send everything to:
jedsjohnson@gmail.com
Replies in English or German both fine. Please put “Charles Research —” at the start of your subject line.

Four contribution pathways

If you are a descendant or family member

Add or correct your branch of the family

The Family Tree tab and the All people index show ~80 named individuals across Charles’s descendants and his St. Marcus / Kreichelt social network. If your line is not represented, or a date / name / relationship is wrong, please send:

  • Your relationship to Charles (e.g. “great-great-granddaughter via Erwin Carl Konsen / Charles Johnson Jr.”).
  • Names, dates, places — with sources where possible. A FamilySearch profile ID (e.g. LZXC-6GB) is ideal but not required.
  • Any photographs, documents, or family stories you would be willing to share. The September 2025 discovery of the Charles Sr. portrait is the standing example of how much one descendant photograph can change the project.
If you are a genealogist or local historian

Submit a primary-source lead or a candidate-family elimination

The Open Questions page lists the four primary sources still unrecovered (Charles’s German baptismal entry, his 1859 trans-Atlantic passenger manifest, the Hessian Auswanderer-Beleg, and the 1855 Stammrolle entry). The parish map shows every Hessian parish that has been searched and the result. Useful contributions:

  • Pointers to a parish, register, or archival series we have not searched.
  • A reading of a Kurrent or Sütterlin entry on a register page we have flagged as illegible.
  • A counter-argument to one of the eliminations — if you think we have ruled out a parish or family that should still be in play, please show us why.
  • A pointer to a private collection (Stammbuch, family Bible, descendant photographs) outside the digitised public archives.
If you have spotted an error

Corrections, large or small

Errors of fact, date, name spelling, transcription, citation format, broken links, or scholarly judgement — all welcome. Please include the page URL and, ideally, the exact phrase as it appears so we can find the spot quickly. Substantive corrections are credited in the Changelog and, where appropriate, in the relevant evidence card’s notes section.

If you are an archivist

Holding-archive contact

If you represent an archive whose records are referenced on this site (Archion / Landeskirchliches Archiv Kassel, Hessisches Staatsarchiv Marburg, NARA, FamilySearch, St. Marcus, etc.) and you would like a citation corrected, an image replaced with a higher-quality scan, or an item removed for rights reasons, please reach out and we will action your request promptly. Where possible we will replace the image with a link to your catalogue rather than removing the citation entirely.

What to expect when you write

This is not a 24/7 institutional inbox. Expect a reply within a week. If your contribution is substantial enough to update the site, you will be asked whether you would like to be credited by name in the relevant card or simply listed in the Changelog as “contribution from a descendant” / “tip from a researcher”.

By submitting material you confirm that you have the right to share it and that it may be used in the research, with appropriate attribution. Photographs and primary-source images you submit will be hosted on this site under the same CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 licence used for the project text, unless you specifically request otherwise.

Charles Research — a multi-month transatlantic genealogical investigation into Charles Konzen (recorded in U.S. records as Gannsen / Johnson; 1835–1903), Hessian-born Civil War sergeant of Battery B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery, and his probable Trendelburg / Deisel origins in Churhessen (a working conclusion — the German baptism remains unrecovered).
