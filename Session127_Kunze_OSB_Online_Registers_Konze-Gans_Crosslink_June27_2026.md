## 1. Ortssippenbuch Sielen (the original question)

- **Title:** *Ortssippenbuch Sielen 1714–1831*, data by the late **Rolf Nowak** (d. 24.11.2004),
  edited & published by **Klaus Kunze**, HeiKun-Verlag, Uslar **2009**.
- **Coverage:** "apparently complete" for the surviving Sielen church books **1714–1831**. The
  compiler did **not** have the years after 1831.
- **Helpful?** Partly. It **stops at 1831 — four years before Charles's 26 Jan 1835 birth** — so it
  cannot contain his baptism, and it does not extend the 1830–1876 Archion window already swept
  (reg. 220042). Its real value: it is the cleanest test of whether a **Konze/Kontze/Gans family was
  ever rooted in Sielen** before Charles. Our Archion index check (Session 125/this session) found
  **no Konze surname in Sielen 1830–1876**; a complete 1714–1831 reconstruction showing the same
  absence would essentially close Sielen as a Konze locality.
- **Catch:** the **Sielen name-register is NOT online** (only the descriptive intro page is;
  `sielen/register.htm` and `…/sielenregister.htm` are empty). To search it you need the **printed
  book** or a query to Kunze (heikun-verlag@klauskunze.com).
- URL: https://klauskunze.com/heikun/sielen/sielen.htm

## 3. The headline observation — and the rigor caveat

**Observation:** in several books the **same family number appears under BOTH a Konze-form and a
Gans-form** in the index:
- **Helmarshausen** family **67** = Konze *and* Gans; family **302** = Konze *and* Gans.
- **Karlshafen** family **287** = Konze *and* Kontze.
- (Plus dense Contze/Contzen/Kontzen variant-stacking on single families in Gieselwerder, e.g.
  fams 152, 153, 171, 253 carry both Contze and Contzen.)

**Why this is potentially big:** it is a published genealogist's index independently treating
**Konze ↔ Kontze ↔ Contze ↔ Contzen ↔ Conze ↔ Konzen ↔ Gans ↔ Ganß ↔ Ganson** as one family's
variant spellings — exactly the equivalence at the heart of the Gannsen origin hypothesis. And
**Lippoldsberg literally indexes a "Ganson" family (186)** — the American-looking spelling — beside a
large Gans/Ganß population.

**⚠️ CAVEAT — do not overstate.** A family number under a surname in an OSB name-register means only
that the surname *appears in that family's entry*. A number under **both** "Konze" and "Gans" is
consistent with two very different things:
1. the family head's surname is **spelled both ways** in different records (a true variant link — the
   hoped-for finding); **or**
2. the head is "Konze" and a **wife/mother is née "Gans"** (or vice-versa) — i.e. two *distinct*
   surnames **intermarrying**, NOT a spelling variant.
The index alone **cannot** distinguish (1) from (2). Only the **actual family entry** (which shows
"Konze gen./alias Gans" for a variant vs. "Konze ∞ N. née Gans" for intermarriage) settles it. So
this is a strong, promising lead — **not yet proof**.

## 4. Are the full family records online? No — index only.

The **family entries (with birth/marriage/death dates) are NOT online** for any of these — only the
registers. Example: **Helmarshausen OSB = 4,500 families, 1521–1950** (church books from 1645;
"between 1645 and 1950 all persons should be captured"), **630 pp, €60**, ISBN 978-3-933334-21-3 —
but online there is only the register + one specimen family. To read family 67/302's dated records
you need the book.

**Known coverage periods:** Helmarshausen **1521/1645–1950** · Karlshafen **1699–1945** · Sielen
**1714–1831**. (Lippoldsberg / Gieselwerder / Oedelsheim periods per their books — not yet captured.)

Note: Helmarshausen is **already eliminated on the "OSB track"** in the project — its Konze families
(67/96/302/406) + Jungkontz (297/507/468) were checked and fall outside the 1830s baptism window
(pre-1830 or in marriage/burial registers). So the *Helmarshausen* Konze=Gans cross-link, while
striking, has already been worked; the fresher targets are **Oedelsheim (flagged OPEN)**,
**Lippoldsberg (Ganson 186)**, and **Gieselwerder**.

## 5. How to get the dated records without buying everything

1. **FamilySearch Library** — Kunze's site explicitly notes his books are in the **Family History
   Library catalog**. Several may be readable for free at a FamilySearch affiliate library /
   Family History Center. *Check FHL catalog by author Klaus Kunze before purchasing.*
2. **Email Klaus Kunze** (heikun-verlag@klauskunze.com) with the specific family numbers — he often
   answers targeted lookups.
3. **Buy the most decisive book(s).** Priority by relevance to Charles (b.1835, Konze/Gans):
   - *Helmarshausen* (1521–1950, €60) — has the Konze=Gans 67/302 cross-link and covers 1835
     (but OSB track already eliminated — confirm what was actually consulted).
   - *Gieselwerder* — heaviest Contze/Contzen + Gans concentration.
   - *Lippoldsberg* — the only book indexing **"Ganson."**
   - *Karlshafen* (1699–1945) — Konze=Kontze fam 287; covers 1835 (but Karlshafen already
     eliminated both confessions, baptism + confirmation).
