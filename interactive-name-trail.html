Charles Research · Interactive

One man, many names

The same person appears in St. Louis records under at least nine different surname spellings — and gives nearly a dozen different birth years. Tap any marker to see the record behind it.

Each dot is one dated record, 1862–1903. Vertical position groups records by who supplied the name; colour groups the spelling family. The form Charles signed himself (Gannsen) stays remarkably stable, while the spellings clerks captured by ear drift wildly — and the single closest match to the candidate German surname, Konzen, appears only once, on his daughter's 1890 death record.

Tap a marker above to read the record.

Every record that states Charles's age implies a different birth year — spanning ~1825 to ~1841, a 16-year spread. Only one source gives an exact date: the St. Marcus burial register, 26 January 1835, the project's working birth date (shown as the gold star).

Tap a marker above to read the record.

Data transcribed from the project's own Surname across the records and Birth date across the records pages. Spelling families are a presentation aid, not a source claim; confidence labels and the “who supplied it” grouping follow the site's evidence ratings. Gannsen is the form Charles signed himself; Johnson is the adopted American name carried from 1871. This explorer is a reading aid — the underlying records and their images remain the authority.

Charles Research — a multi-month transatlantic genealogical investigation into Charles Konzen (recorded in U.S. records as Gannsen / Johnson; 1835–1903), Hessian-born Civil War sergeant of Battery B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery, and his probable Trendelburg / Deisel origins in Churhessen (a working conclusion — the German baptism remains unrecovered).

      
Compiled by Jed Johnson with Peter Schräder and James Gunnis.
