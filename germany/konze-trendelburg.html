The surname’s documentary home — Kontze/Konten attested from 1749 in the registers (and to 1745 as “Andreas Kontzen von Zeyhl,” godfather). The oldest dated individual is a Kontze woman born ~1702.

The Rathsverwandter (town-councillor) Andreas Kontze line — the Stammtafel (pedigree-table) branch

The 1795 marriage names the groom’s father as Rathsverwandter Andreas Kontze of Trendelburg; the earlier reconstruction derived Johann Friedrich from Andreas Kontze I, the Deisel Küster. Resolved (Session 97, 7 Jun 2026): the Trendelburg Konfirmationen register settles it — Ostern 1777: “Johann Friedrich, des Rathsverwandten H. Andreas Kontzen ehel. ältester Sohn, alt 13½ Jahr” (220096 S.225 · img 1105). Age 13½ at Easter 1777 lands precisely on the 11 Dec 1763 marriage-margin birthdate, and the entry names the Rathsverwandter as father directly, in Trendelburg, eighteen years before the marriage. The Deisel-Küster derivation is withdrawn (see errata). The Rathsverwandter himself is now exactly dated — and is almost certainly the “Andreas, son of Nies Sr.” who married Anna Christina Konzen in 1760, and the same man as the 1787 godfather “Andreas Kontze the Elder of Stammen.”
