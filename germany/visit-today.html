Visit Today — Trendelburg, Deisel, & Hofgeismar

A practical guide to Charles's home region — castles, fairy-tale routes, and where to find the parish records
GERMANY & ORIGINSNorthern Hesse · Deutsche Märchenstraße

★ At a Glance

All three of Charles's home places — Trendelburg, Deisel, and Hofgeismar — are still living villages on the Deutsche Märchenstraße (German Fairy Tale Route), about 30 km north of Kassel in northern Hesse. Trendelburg's hilltop castle is a working hotel advertised as the inspiration for the Brothers Grimm's Rapunzel; Hofgeismar's medieval Altstadt and Rathaus are intact; Deisel is today an Ortsteil of Stadt Trendelburg. The Trendelburg–Hofgeismar–Sababurg cluster is one of the route's densest, and is the natural base for parish-record genealogy in the Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis.

Where to go

★ Burg Trendelburg — castle hotel ("Rapunzel's Tower")

The 700-year-old hilltop castle — the same silhouette in the Merian, Reißert, and Bokelmann views on the main Germany & Origins tab — has operated as a romantic castle hotel since 1996. Hotel Burg Trendelburg has 22 individually designed rooms (some with antique four-poster beds), a restaurant, vaulted cellar, castle chapel, and registry office. The bergfried (keep) is promoted as the inspiration for Rapunzel's tower; visitors can climb the 130 steps to the top for views over the Diemel valley and the Reinhardswald. Sunday "Rapunzel" performances in the courtyard, May Fairy Tale Festival, late-November "Bewitching Lights."

Links: burg-hotel-trendelburg.com · visit.kassel.de — Trendelburg Castle · Deutsche Märchenstraße — Trendelburg · TripAdvisor

Trendelburg town & Stadt Trendelburg (incl. Deisel)

The walled town of Trendelburg sits along the Diemel river. Deisel is today an Ortsteil (district) of Stadt Trendelburg, about 3 km east. The Trendelburg Stadtpforte (city gate), shown in the 1869/1901 photograph on the main Germany & Origins tab, still stands beneath the castle's keep. Walking, cycling (the Diemelradweg and the Reinhardswaldweg both pass through), and canoeing on the Diemel are the main activities. The Diemel valley between Trendelburg, Hofgeismar, and Bad Karlshafen is one of the densest stretches of the Fairy Tale Route.

Links: Stadt Trendelburg (official city site) · Trendelburg (Wikipedia) · Rapunzelturm — TripAdvisor

Hofgeismar & the Sababurg ("Sleeping Beauty's Castle")

Hofgeismar — 10 km south of Trendelburg — preserves its medieval Altstadt, the half-timbered Rathaus, the Altstädter Kirche, and several other 16th–17th century buildings. The town's other claim is the nearby Sababurg in the Reinhardswald: the romantic castle promoted by the German tourism office as the setting of the Grimm Brothers' Sleeping Beauty (Dornröschen). The Sababurg sits in a 1,000-year-old wild park and operates today as a hotel and event venue. Hofgeismar is also the natural base for tracking down genealogy in the parish records of the Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis.

Links: Stadt Hofgeismar (official city site) · Hofgeismar (Wikipedia) · Sababurg (Wikipedia)

How to do genealogy on site

Genealogy & archival research — Hessian State Archive (HStAM)

Civil and ecclesiastical records for the Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis — including Trendelburg, Deisel, Helmarshausen, Friedrichsfeld, and the surrounding villages — are held by the Hessisches Staatsarchiv Marburg (HStAM), a 90-minute drive south of Trendelburg. Many parish registers are also digitized on Archion.de (subscription required); the Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis Trendelburg parish register is Archion KB 220096. The full transatlantic emigration index for 1850s Hessian villagers is published in ISTG (Immigrant Ships Transcribers Guild) Vol. 16.

Links: Hessisches Landesgeschichtliches Informationssystem (LAGIS) · Archion.de — German parish registers · Hessisches Staatsarchiv Marburg

How to get there

The German Fairy Tale Route (Deutsche Märchenstraße)

Trendelburg, Deisel, Hofgeismar, the Sababurg, the Reinhardswald, and Bad Karlshafen all lie on the Deutsche Märchenstraße — a 600-km tourism route from Hanau (the Grimm Brothers' birthplace) north to Bremen. The Trendelburg–Hofgeismar–Sababurg cluster is one of the route's densest. Driving Kassel → Trendelburg → Hofgeismar → Sababurg as a day trip is the standard tourist itinerary; many visitors stay overnight at Burg Trendelburg or the Sababurg hotel. The nearest major airport is Frankfurt (FRA), about 2 hrs 15 min by car or 2 hrs 30 min by ICE train to Kassel-Wilhelmshöhe followed by a 30-minute regional train or drive north.

Links: Deutsche Märchenstraße — official site (English) · Kassel tourism (visit.kassel.de)
