Wahmbeck — Parish Research Log

Weser right bank, Kirchenkreis Uslar · Landeskirche Hannovers · Kirchenbuch 1790–1845 reg. 359497 · ~14 km N of Deisel · compiled 27 June 2026
⛔ NO 26-JAN-1835 BOY, NO CARL ON A POOL SURNAME — no match for Charles

Why Wahmbeck. Wahmbeck is one of Lippoldsberg’s cross-Weser neighbours in the Kirchenkreis Uslar (Landeskirche Hannovers), part of the Weser-ring sweep that followed the Lippoldsberg Gans clan up and down the river. Unlike most of the ring it has no Klaus Kunze Ortssippenbuch, so there was no surname-register pre-screen — the window had to be read directly off the register. The provisional expectation was that no pool surname would appear; that turned out to be only half right.

What was read. The 1835 Geburts-Register of the combined Kirchenbuch 1790–1845, reg. 359497 was read leaf-by-leaf in the logged-in Archion viewer. After a calibration correction (the first year-map was off by one), the 1835 birth section is Bild 93 (Seite 92, entries 1–6 = the January head) → Bild 94 (7–21) → Bild 95 (22–29), with 1836 beginning at Bild 96. The wider window leaves (births to 1838 at Bild 100; the deaths run at Bild 130; marriages 1839 at Bild 235) were scanned to anchor the calibration. This book has no AI text recognition; OCR was ordered but had not processed, so the read is by-eye Kurrent.

Result — negative for Charles. The full January 1835 head reads: 1 Elend (m, 7 Jan) · 2 König (f, 18 Jan) · 3 Böger (f, 24 Jan) · 4 Bunzendahl (f, 27 Jan) · 5 Caroline Charlotte Gans (f, 8 Feb, illegitimate — mother Caroline Bremer, alleged father George Christian Gans “aus Gestelwarder” [?Gieselwerder]; child d. 1836) · 6 Friedrich Wilhelm Carl Schrader (m, 13 Feb). No male was born on or near 26 Jan 1835 (the late-January births are all girls), and the only male “Carl” in the head is a Schrader, not a pool surname. So Charles (Carl Gannsen, b. 26 Jan 1835) has no matching baptism in Wahmbeck.

One thing it did change. The Gans pool surname is present at Wahmbeck in the window — the single illegitimate Gans girl above — which overturns the earlier provisional “no Gans at Wahmbeck” and adds Wahmbeck to the Weser-ring Gans cluster. But it is one illegitimate girl who died the next year, not a baptising Gans household producing a boy in early 1835. Wahmbeck is closed as a candidate for Charles. (Caveat: by-eye Kurrent read of the register images; confirm against the queued Archion OCR or a Kurrent specialist per project standard.)

Remaining follow-ups

  • Certify the 1833–1841 window against the queued OCR. Archion OCR for reg. 359497 was ordered (“Texterkennung beauftragt — in Warteschlange”) but had not processed at the time of the read. When it lands, the full birth window (not just 1835) should be machine-verified, or read by a Kurrent specialist (Peter Schräder), to upgrade the elimination from “window read by eye” to fully certified. Lane: OPEN — Archion OCR queued; the 1835 head (Charles’s year) is already read and negative.
  • The illegitimate Caroline Charlotte Gans (8 Feb 1835). Mother Caroline Bremer; alleged father George Christian Gans “aus Gestelwarder” — almost certainly Gieselwerder, tying the Wahmbeck Gans presence back to the Gieselwerder/Lippoldsberg clan. Worth a cross-check against the Gieselwerder and Oedelsheim Gans households, though the child is female and died in 1836 (not Charles).
  • Confirmation track — also negative. The Wahmbeck confirmations were scanned for the pool and show the same single illegitimate Gans girl (8 Feb 1835, d. 1836) and no Konze/Kontze/Contze; Wahmbeck stays eliminated on the confirmation track as well as the birth track. A clean leaf-by-leaf confirmation read could be logged here when convenient.Lane: corroborated — Archion (by eye)
