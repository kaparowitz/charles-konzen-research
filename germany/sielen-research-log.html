Sielen — Parish Research Log

51.622 N · 9.465 E · ~6.8 km NE of Deisel · compiled 21 June 2026 · confirmation sweep re-verified 22 June 2026 · baptism sweep added 26 June 2026 · baptism name-index checked 26 June 2026

Sielen is now negative on all three tracks (baptism, confirmation, early-KB burials). Stays eliminated.

Baptism name-index check — Konze-pool surnames, 1833–1841

Read live 26 June 2026 in the same register, 220042 ↗. Because the book is “Bem.: mit Register,” it carries its own alphabetical baptism name-index at the end of the reel (Bild ~2094–2100), laid out two letters per opening with each page split into Männlich / Seite / Weiblich / Seite. The index entries are ordered by Seite (i.e. by date of baptism), so the 1833–1841 window = Seite 1–52 sits at the head of each letter. The K, I·J and G sections were read directly for the Konze-pool surnames; the alphabet runs …G, H, I·J, K, L… with no separate C page, so any C-variant (Conson/Konsen) would file under K.

Kunze / Nowak Ortssippenbuch Sielen 1714–1831 (free full-text PDF)

Klaus Kunze (HeiKun-Verlag) hosts the late Rolf Nowak’s Ortssippenbuch Sielen 1714–1831 as a free full-text PDF (OSB Sielen.pdf ↗) — an apparently complete family reconstruction of Sielen for 1714–1831. It stops four years before Charles’s 1835 birth, so it cannot hold his baptism, but it is the cleanest test of whether a Konze/Kontze/Gans family was ever rooted in Sielen. (It is not in the FamilySearch catalogue — the free online release stands in for a library copy.) The complete book (≈1,700 family entries) was text-searched 27 June 2026 (full PDF now archived in the project). Result: across 1714–1831 the Konze/Kontze surname appears in only a handful of entries — always a woman (or her daughter) marrying in from outside — and there is NO Gans / Ganß / Ganson anywhere in the entire book (0 occurrences). No Konze/Kontze household was ever resident in Sielen. Decisively, the two Konze families that touch Sielen are both rooted in the project’s core Konze locales — Trendelburg and Hümme:

The book’s own name-register lists “Kontze” / “Konze” on only a few pages and carries no Gans entry at all. Bottom line: the Nowak/Kunze OSB independently confirms Sielen had no native Konze or Gans family 1714–1831 — corroborating the elimination — while documenting that the Konze name entered Sielen only by marriage, out of Trendelburg (Johann Heinrich Konze, Ackermann) and Hümme (Johann Christoph Konze, Richter zu Hümme). Full text + PDF archived: sources/OSB_Sielen_1714-1831_Nowak-Kunze.pdf, notes/sielen_osb_1714-1831_fulltext.txt.
