Gottstreu — Parish Research Log

Weser left bank · 1722 Waldensian colony, Kirchspiel Lippoldsberg · Ev. Kirche von Kurhessen-Waldeck, Kirchenkreis Hofgeismar-Wolfhagen · own register Archion reg. 219073 · ~12 km E of Deisel · compiled 27 June 2026
⛔ NO POOL SURNAME IN THE BAPTISMS — no match for Charles

Why Gottstreu. Gottstreu was the last genuinely un-searched parish in the Lippoldsberg neighbour ring — a 1722 Waldensian colony village (the twin of Gewissenruh), founded for French Protestant refugees by Landgrave Karl of Hesse-Kassel. It belongs to the Kirchspiel Lippoldsberg (Gewissenruh + Gottstreu + Lippoldsberg + Vernawahlshausen). Crucially, it has no Klaus Kunze Ortssippenbuch — the HeiKun catalogue covers the neighbouring villages but not the Waldensian colonies — so there was no surname-register pre-screen, and the register had to be read directly.

Key discovery. Gottstreu keeps its own church-book series on Archion; it is not folded into Gewissenruh (reg. 219001) or the Lippoldsberg books. Under Hessen → Landeskirchl. Archiv Kurhessen-Waldeck → Kirchenkreis Hofgeismar → Gottstreu: KB 1722–1833, KB 1830–1927 = reg. 219073 (the window volume), KB 1928–2010. This register had never been searched — a real gap in the neighbour ring, now closed.

What was read. In the logged-in Archion viewer, reg. 219073 (combined volume, 639 images; section divider at Bild 262): one written Seite per image, Seite = Bild − 266. The whole 1835 baptism year was read leaf-by-leaf at Bild 274–275 (Seiten 8–9), and the alphabetical Tauf-Register index (Bild 359–364) — which covers the entire book, 1830 to ~1855 — was checked at the pool letters. The Konfirmationen 1847–1855 (Bild 381–383) were swept as a second, independent track.

Result — negative on both tracks. The 1835 year held seven children (Summa 7); the only January births were twin girls (Bock). No male was born on or near 26 Jan 1835, no Carl/Karl, and no pool surname — the sole 1835 boy was Wilhelm Friedrich Schweinsberg, b. 26 March (a “26” echo, but wrong month and a colony surname). The alphabetical index confirms it whole-book: C = only “Clute”; G = Graff/Günckel/Gerth… (no Gans/Ganß/Ganson); K = only “Kunkel” (no Konze/Kontze). The Konfirmationen 1847–1855 are likewise clear — the prime 1849 class (exact for a child born 26 Jan 1835) has a single boy, again Schweinsberg b. 26 March 1835. So Charles (Carl Gannsen, b. 26 Jan 1835) has no matching baptism in Gottstreu.

Reading. Gottstreu’s surname pool is a tight Huguenot/Waldensian set (Bock, Maret, Busch, Bohrmann, Bertalot, Schweinsberg, Jouvenal/Jouquenal, Steinbach, Kunkel, Pfeifer, Lott…), mirroring the Gewissenruh colony — the Konze/Gans pool simply is not part of it. With Gottstreu closed, the entire Lippoldsberg neighbour ring is now searched (Oedelsheim, Gieselwerder, Vernawahlshausen, Gewissenruh, Gottstreu, Bodenfelde, Bursfelde/Glashütte, Wahmbeck), all negative for a Carl-on-pool baptism in the window. (Caveat: by-eye Kurrent read of the register images, per project standard.)

Remaining follow-ups

  • Mint per-image permalinks. If a tighter citation is wanted, mint Archion permalinks for reg. 219073 at the key leaves: Bild 274 (1835 Taufe), Bild 359 / 361 / 363 (Tauf-index C / G / K), and Bild 381 (1847–49 Konfirmationen). The viewer links above already open the register at those images. Low priority — the surname-pool result is already decisive on two independent tracks.
  • Pre-window tail (1722–1833) and the off-prime 1851 class. The earlier Gottstreu book (reg. for 1722–1833) and the 1851 confirmation class (births ~1837, off the 26-Jan-1835 cohort) were only partially / not opened — low value, since the colony surname pool contains no Konze/Gans regardless of year.Lane: optional — Archion (digitised).
  • Ring complete. With Gottstreu eliminated, no un-searched parish remains in the Lippoldsberg neighbour ring (Wülmersen needs no separate sweep — its records sit within the already-searched Deisel / Trendelburg parish). This log closes that thread.
