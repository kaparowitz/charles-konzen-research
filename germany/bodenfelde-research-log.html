Bodenfelde — Parish Research Log

Weser left bank, Kr. Northeim · Landeskirche Hannovers, Kirchenkreis Uslar · Kirchenbuch 1794–1839 reg. 359334 · compiled 27 June 2026
⛔ NO GANS BAPTISM IN 1835 — no match for Charles

Why Bodenfelde. It is the one un-searched neighbour of the Lippoldsberg Gans clan that actually carries the surname: Klaus Kunze’s Bodenfelde Ortssippenbuch register lists a large Gans population (15 family numbers) plus one Contze (fam 178) — see the corrected Sources row below; the surname is dense here, not rare. Bodenfelde sits directly across the Weser from the Lippoldsberg / Gottstreu / Wülmersen cluster, in the Kingdom of Hannover (now Niedersachsen), so its records are in the Landeskirche Hannovers archive rather than Kurhessen-Waldeck. The Gans clan plainly moved up and down this stretch of river, so a Bodenfelde baptism for a Carl Gans born about 26 Jan 1835 was a live possibility.

What was read. The entire 1835 baptism year was read leaf-by-leaf in the logged-in Archion viewer — Kirchenbuch 1794–1839, reg. 359334, images 334–340 (pages 506–512), roughly forty baptisms, every entry’s family surname checked. The 1834/1835 and 1835/1836 year boundaries were also scanned.

Result — negative. There is no Gans baptism anywhere in Bodenfelde in 1835, and none at the 1834-end or 1836-start openings checked. Strikingly, the forename Carl/Karl is one of the most common names in the parish — nearly every male entry is a “Carl” (Carl August Braune, Carl Christian Conrad Ehle, Carl Friedrich Meier, Carl Friedrich Wilhelm Röpeler, Carl Wilhelm Eduard Jürgens, and more) — but not one of them is a Gans. The closest near-miss to Charles’s date is Carl August Braune, born 29 Jan 1835 (three days off), surname Braune, not Gans. So Charles (Carl Gannsen, b. 26 Jan 1835) has no matching Gans baptism in Bodenfelde.

Reading. The Gans surname in the Kunze OSB is therefore a historical Bodenfelde presence (16th–19th c. across all record types), not an active baptising household in the mid-1830s — the same pattern as Vernawahlshausen and unlike Lippoldsberg. Bodenfelde is closed as a candidate for Charles. (Caveat: handwriting read from the register images; spellings/days to be double-checked against the originals per project standard.)

Bodenfelde (Landeskirche Hannovers) is on Archion but the combined volume 359334 has no AI "Erkannter Text", so it was read by eye and carries no per-image permalink — the viewer links open the register at the cited image (an exact locator). Image numbers are per-microfilm-reel. The 1830s baptisms may exist as both an Erst- and a Zweitschrift (1835 also appears at images ~320–324), both read the same (negative) — see the follow-ups.

Remaining follow-ups

  • Optional — close 1834 and 1836 fully. The whole of 1835 is read (negative); the 1834 middle and the rest of 1836 were only boundary-checked. A full line-read would confirm the Gans surname is absent across the entire 1834–1836 window. Low priority — 1835 (Charles’s year) is already complete and negative.
  • Done — Familienbuch checked (27 Jun 2026): no Gans household head in Bodenfelde. The Bodenfelde Familienbuch (reg. 359344) has an alphabetical family-head index at the end (“Familienhäupter / Fam.-Nr.”). The entire G section lists only Giesing (Fam. 15), Götte (5, 25, 71) and Grote (46) — no “Gans” family. The surname appears once in the whole index, as a maiden name: “Henne, Heinrich … geb. Gans” (Fam. 2) — a Gans daughter who married into the Henne family. So by the 19th century “Gans” was not an active household surname in Bodenfelde, only a maiden name — confirming the empty 1835 baptisms and the “historical, not baptising” reading. Caveat: the index column order is irregular; a Ga/Ge sub-column was hard to resolve, so a confirming pass (or the Kartei Männer reg. 359346) could double-check no Gans head was missed.
  • Reconcile the 1835 image map (Erst-/Zweitschrift). The 27 Jun re-read found the 1835 Taufen at images ~320–324 (header “1835”), ~14 images earlier than this log’s original “334–340”. The combined volume almost certainly holds an Erstschrift and a Zweitschrift of the 1830s baptisms (1835 appears twice); both copies read the same (negative). Worth fixing the image anchors so future leaf-pulls land correctly.
  • Finding aids not yet mined (low priority). The Bodenfelde Taufen-Konfirmationen 1837–1852 and Konfirmationen 1848–1875, plus the Kartei Männer Gans / Contze-178 family cards (reg. 359346), would pin the Gans / Contze generations directly — left for a follow-up pass.Lane: OPEN — Archion (digitised)
  • Other un-searched Lippoldsberg neighbours (new-neighbour sweep, June 2026). Bursfelde + Glashütte (Hann.-Münden/Hemeln; Kunze OSB lists Gans fams 110, 113, 125, 136 — the only native Gans cohort of the ring still unchecked on register images) and Wahmbeck (Kirchenkreis Uslar) are not yet swept on Archion — top priorities. Gottstreu and Wülmersen (Huguenot/Waldensian colony hamlets) are not Gans-flagged in Kunze, so lower-yield.
