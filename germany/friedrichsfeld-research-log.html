Friedrichsfeld — Parish Research Log

51.586 N · 9.369 E · ~3.9 km NW of Deisel · compiled 21 June 2026 · updated 22 June 2026 (Lüdicke roster checked)
WORKING HYPOTHESIS · leading lead

The Andreas Kontze line (FS PZF8-X19, b. 1 Oct 1795 Trendelburg, d. 1854 Mainz). KB 218959 Entry 195 documents Justus Ernst Konze, b. 14 Nov 1829; three Peter-documented children were added to FamilySearch in May 2026.

June 2026 reconciliation: with cousin Carl Wilhelm (1831) eliminated, Justus Ernst (FS P87Z-ZDC) is the sole Konze male of his generation who leaves no adult German death, marriage, or emigration record under any indexed variant — untraced after his 1843 Trendelburg confirmation. A lead, not a confirmed identity.

Confirmation-register sweep — “any Carl, 1833–1841”

Part of the June 2026 widened confirmation-register sweep across the Kreis Hofgeismar — looking for any boy named Carl/Karl born 1833–1841, surname open, who would surface in the 1847–1855 confirmation classes. This is the parish-specific row; the cross-parish ledger, the full list of boys named Carl that turned up, and the method/reliability notes live on Parish search coverage.

Online lane — emigration & US records (searched 21 June 2026)

A fresh open-web pass on the two online lanes — German/European emigration indexes and US arrival/settlement records — returned no new trace of Justus Ernst Konze under any spelling (Konze / Kontze / Konzen / Gannsen / Gonnson). This confirms and extends the project’s 21 June reconciliation: he is absent from every searchable emigration and death index, the documentary signature of a man who either died young in an un-indexed parish burial or emigrated quietly under a new surname.

Lüdicke Deisel-emigrant study — checked (22 June 2026) ★

The library follow-up below — Martina Lüdicke’s “Verschwunden ist der Strand …” (Verein für hess. Geschichte u. Landeskunde, 1994), the published name-level study of Deisel emigrants to America — was reached through its Google Books in-book index. Result: the surname Konze occurs exactly once in the 92-page study, on p. 89, in the appendix roster headed “Auswanderung aus Deisel nach Amerika nach 1876.” Under the sub-heading ohne Jahresangabe (no year given) the line reads:

KONZE, 2 Brüder  (“Konze, two brothers” — Deisel → USA, date not recorded)

What it means (stated carefully): this is the first published, name-level record of Konze emigration from Deisel to America — direct documentary support for the Deisel-Konze → United States migration that the whole Charles hypothesis rests on. But the date excludes Charles himself: the table is “nach 1876,” two decades after Charles reached St. Louis (1850s) and served in the Civil War. The “2 Brüder” are therefore not Charles — they are later Deisel-Konze emigrants, plausibly younger kin following an established family chain. The flip side is an equally useful negative: Konze appears only on p. 89 (the post-1876 supplement); the study’s core 1831–1866 emigrant tables list no Konze at all — consistent with the project’s standing finding that Charles left in the 1850s un-indexed, or under a changed surname.

Source: Martina Lüdicke, “Verschwunden ist der Strand …”: Studien zur Auswanderung nach Amerika aus Kurhessen 1831–1866, dargestellt am Beispiel des Dorfes Deisel (Kreis Hofgeismar) (= Die Geschichte unserer Heimat, Bd. 14; Hofgeismar 1994), p. 89, table “Auswanderung aus Deisel nach Amerika nach 1876.” Located via Google Books in-book search (“1 page matching Konze,” p. 89). Full roster context needs the printed volume — held at Heidelberg UB (HEIDI) and listed in the Deutsche Digitale Bibliothek.

The “2 Brüder” traced in US records (22 June 2026) ★★

Followed Lüdicke’s lead into FamilySearch (Tree + US Historical Records). The Deisel Konze clan demonstrably emigrated to America in the 1880s–1900s — the line is now documented with named, dated individuals. The closest fit to Lüdicke’s undated “2 Brüder” is a pair of brothers, sons of Ludwig Theodor Konze × Maria Christiane Schaefer of Deisel, who both settled in New York City:

Carl Konze (FS GGYY-XXF; s/o Gottlieb Konze × Schneider)7 Jun 1881m. 22 Nov 1923 Manhattan

Relevance to Charles (carefully): this confirms the Deisel-Konze → America migration with documented people, not just a roster line — three Deisel Konze households put sons on the boat (→ NYC, Milwaukee/Chicago). But none is Charles, his child, or his sibling: born 1871–1887 (vs Charles 1835) and settled NYC / Milwaukee / Chicago, not St. Louis. They are great-nephews / collateral cousins if Charles is a Deisel Konze. The recurrence of a “Carl Konze” (b. 1881) emigrating to Manhattan alongside Charles “Carl Konzen” (b. 1835 → St. Louis) shows the forename rode with the line. Net: strong circumstantial reinforcement that the Deisel Konze clan was an emigrating family — but no direct documentary bridge to Charles’s household. Detail + IDs in Konze_Deisel_Emigrants_Trace_June22_2026.md.

Highest-value next step: a Y-DNA match between a living descendant of Heinrich Philipp Konze (NYC) or Philipp Ludwig Konze (Chicago) and a Charles descendant would tie Charles to this exact Deisel branch. (Data note: the FS Tree gives the NYC brothers’ father Ludwig Theodor Konze a 1839–1870 lifespan yet lists post-1870 children — his death year, or the mother-line, needs an Archion check.)
