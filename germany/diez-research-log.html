Diez — Parish Research Log

50.369 N · 8.016 E · Hessen-Nassau (Lahn) · ~135 km S · compiled 21 June 2026 · updated 27 June 2026 (Archion confirmation-track sweep, widened to 1846–1856)
ELIMINATED

Diez is the Lahn-valley town that the 2020 AncestryProGenealogists reading floated as a Hessen-Nassau alternative for the marriage register’s “Deisse, Kreis Cassel.” It sits ~135 km south of the Hofgeismar Kirchspiel-complex that anchors the working Trendelburg/Deisel hypothesis, and it carries none of the Konze/Kontze surname pool. Already eliminated on geography in 2020; now also closed on the confirmation track.

On 27 June 2026 the Diez Konfirmandenregister 1817–1875 (Zentralarchiv der EKHN, Dekanat Diez) was swept image-by-image across the full window for a birth in 1833–1841 — the confirmation classes of 1846–1856. At the observed confirmation ages of 13–15, that span brackets every birth year 1833–1841 (the 1846 class still holds 1833-born children; the 1856 class opens with an 1841-born boy, the Usener entry, before passing into 1842 births); the prime classes for a 26 Jan 1835 birth are 1849 and 1850. Result: negative. The surname pool is uniformly local Nassau-Lahn (Rott, Pohl, Pilgram, Fink, Müller, Thön, Lang, Langenberger, Roll, Klee, Graf, Schäfer, Raab, Saul, Wolf, Möbus, Walthaus, Marschinus, Daniel, Kraft, Bartholomä, Lotz, Gauhaus, Gemund, Diensch, Löber, Wagner, Freckenhäuser, Usener…). There is no Konze / Kontze / Gansen / Gonsen surname anywhere; the recurring forename Carl/Karl appears only on local families (e.g. Carl Fink, Carl Kraft, Carl Danus in the prime 1849 class — all wrong surnames), and the 1849–1850 cohorts that exactly match a January-1835 birth contain no Carl on a Konze-type surname.

Remaining follow-ups

None outstanding — Diez is now searched on the confirmation track (1847–1855, above) in addition to the earlier toponym/geography elimination. Fully closed within the candidate window.
