Deisel — Parish Research Log

51.568 N · 9.418 E · Kreis Hofgeismar · compiled 21 June 2026 · follow-ups worked 22 June 2026
STATED ORIGIN

Deisel is the “Deisse, Kreis Cassel, Churhessen” named in Charles’s 1866 marriage register — the single place Charles himself put on record. Mid-19th-century population 1,118 (97.94% Evangelical/Lutheran). Three “geb. Konzen” mothers are attested in Deisel 1828–1829 (Maria Elisabeth, Wilhelmina, Carlotta), and a substantial Konze/Kontze householder population is documented across the Deisel registers.

Every Deisel register with a checkable Seite/image anchor was opened live in the Archion viewer and confirmed correct against the original image (Citation Audit, 21 June 2026). No baptism for a male Carl/Karl Konze born 26 January 1835 has been located in the Deisel Taufregister 218842 — the core open gap. The Taufregister’s own alphabetical name index (img 272) was read on 22 June 2026 and lists no Konze son named Carl/Karl anywhere in 1830–1861, strengthening this negative; the 1849 confirmation class (boys Nro 19–22) likewise contains no Carl/Charles Konze.

Follow-ups — resolved 22 June 2026

Worked live in the Archion viewer (Jed’s subscription). Permalinks and image numbers are in the Sources-checked table above.

  • No “Carl Konze” baptism in Deisel 1830–1861. The Taufregister’s own alphabetical name index (218842, img 272) lists every Konze son in the register — Heinrich (Nro 185), Heinrich (Nro 222) and Ludwig (Nro 268, the 1836 baptism) — and no Konze child named Carl/Karl. The “Carl/Karl” entries in the K-index belong to a separate Karl family (e.g. Karl, Heinrich Philipp Nro 221; Karl, Heinrich Nro 250), not Konze. Clean documentary negative.Verified — Archion 218842 img 272
  • The “indexed Heinrich Konze b. 14 Nov 1834” is actually Heinrich LANGE. Register Nro 185 (1834 Oct/Nov spread, 218842, img 38) is the baptism of Heinrich Lange, b. 14 Nov 1834, son of Joh. Christoph Lange (Ackermann) and his wife “geb. Konze.” The K-index reference reflects the mother’s Konze maiden name, not a Konze child — so this record does not bear on the Charles = Konze line.Verified — Archion 218842 img 38
  • 1849 Deisel confirmation, boys Nro 19–22 — clean negative. The four boys are Georg Christian Knecht (b. 25 Feb 1835, mother geb. Konze), Georg Weber (b. 11 Mar 1835), Friedrich Schrage (b. 4 Jul 1834) and Johann(es) Kloppmann (b. 7 Jan 1835); see 218836, img 936. None is a Carl/Charles Konze. Entry 19 (Georg Christian Knecht) is the closest near-match to Charles — a Konze-descended boy born within a month of Charles’s stated 26 Jan 1835 — but he is a Knecht, named Georg, and confirmed at Deisel.Verified — Archion 218836 img 936

Remaining follow-ups

  • Two-Gottlieb reconciliation — still open. A Gottlieb Konze b. c.1817 (Deisel confirmation 1831) and a Gottlieb Konze b. ~1842 (marriage “Entry 264”) cannot be the same man, so the working model is two individuals (father and son); the 1871 HStAM Karten P II 9658/3 house-plan Gottlieb is most plausibly the younger/surviving one. Direct Archion verification is blocked: both the confirmation film (218836) and the Trau- und Sippregister (218860) are scan-jumbled and duplicated in the relevant ranges (22 Jun 2026 — the marriage Nro sequence around 264 falls in 1859–60 and the legible neighbours — Hose Nro 266, Brandau 267, Knapp 268, May 269, Köster 270/271 — are not Konze, so “Entry 264 = Gottlieb Konze” needs re-checking against the register’s own name index or the offline house-plan).Lane: OFFLINE — Deisel registers / Archion (scan-jumble) + HStAM Karten P II 9658/3 (offline)
