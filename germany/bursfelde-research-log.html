Bursfelde + Glashütte — Parish Research Log

Weser east bank · Hann.-Münden, Ortsteil Hemeln (Lkr. Göttingen) · Kunze OSB Bursfelde und Glashütte · ~9 km SE of Lippoldsberg · compiled 27 June 2026
◻ OPEN — native Gans cohort, but the 1833–41 register is not digitised

Why Bursfelde. Of all the Lippoldsberg Gans clan’s neighbours, Bursfelde + Glashütte holds the one native Gans cohort of the ring still unchecked on register images. Klaus Kunze’s Ortssippenbuch Bursfelde und Glashütte (Uslar 2003 / 2. Aufl. 2010, ~1,065 families) lists Gans in four families (110, 113, 125, 136) — a real, if modest, native population — plus the near-shapes Ganz (136) and Gaus (130). The two settlements sit on the east bank of the Weser and are administratively part of Hann.-Münden, Ortsteil Hemeln (Lkr. Göttingen), so their records are in the Hannover province rather than Kurhessen-Waldeck. A Gans baptism for a boy born about 26 Jan 1835 is a live possibility here — which is exactly why this candidate cannot yet be closed.

What was found. Archion’s only Bursfelde holding is the Kirchenbuch 1639–1746, filed under Fürstenhagen, Kirchenkreis Uslar — entirely before the 1833–1841 window. No 1830s Bursfelde/Glashütte volume appears under Kirchenkreis Uslar at all. Because the settlements belong to Hann.-Münden/Hemeln, any window register would more likely sit under Kirchenkreis Hannoversch-Münden / Hemeln — not yet checked — or else remain on-site / OSB-only.

What the Kunze OSB shows. The online surname register was rendered in the browser (the page is JavaScript-rendered) and string-matched for the pool: Gans = families 110, 113, 125, 136; no Konze / Kontze / Contze / Contzen anywhere in the register. So the pool’s Gans half is present natively, but its Konze half is not — and the OSB family number proves only that the surname appears in that family’s entry, not the baptism dates.

Status — open. Bursfelde cannot be eliminated for Charles: the four Gans families’ 1833–1841 baptisms have not been read, because the window register is not digitised on Archion under Uslar. The next move is to locate the volume under Hann.-Münden/Hemeln, or read it on-site / from the printed OSB family pages.

Bursfelde + Glashütte (Hann.-Münden, Ortsteil Hemeln) is an open candidate: the period register is not digitised on Archion under Kirchenkreis Uslar, so no images were read. Verdict awaits the 1833–1841 baptism volume (Hann.-Münden/Hemeln, on-site, or the printed OSB family pages). Image numbers/permalinks will be added once the window register is located.

Remaining follow-ups

  • Locate the 1833–1841 register under Hann.-Münden / Hemeln. Bursfelde + Glashütte are administratively Hann.-Münden (Ortsteil Hemeln), so the window volume, if filmed, would sit under Kirchenkreis Hannoversch-Münden / Hemeln on Archion — not yet browsed. This is the single step that would let the candidate be decided. Lane: OPEN — Archion (check Hann.-Münden/Hemeln); else on-site / OSB-only.
  • Read the four Gans families’ baptisms (OSB fams 110, 113, 125, 136). If the register is not digitised, the printed Kunze OSB family pages (or the Archion Familienbuch / Kartei for Hemeln, if any) carry the dated entries — enough to test whether any Gans boy was born ~26 Jan 1835.
  • Cross-link the Wahmbeck illegitimate Gans (8 Feb 1835). Wahmbeck’s lone Gans birth names an alleged father “George Christian Gans aus Gestelwarder” (?Gieselwerder); worth checking whether the Bursfelde/Glashütte Gans families connect to the same east-bank Gans network that the Wahmbeck and Gieselwerder entries point to.Lane: OPEN — open web + Archion.
