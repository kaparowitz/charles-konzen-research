Key Contextual Reading

Political backdrop · 19th-century Hessian emigration history
GERMANY & ORIGINSReading list · 9 external sources

★ At a Glance

A reading list for the political backdrop and the Hessian emigration history that explains why Charles's particular generation crossed the Atlantic when it did. The 1848 revolutions, the seven-week 1866 Austro-Prussian War, and the Hessian state-archive emigration registers (LAGIS, Arcinsys) are the three pillars.

Key contextual reading on 19th-century Hessian emigration

The political backdrop

  • German Confederation 1815–1866 — the loose state-system Hesse-Kassel belonged to before 1866
  • Revolutions of 1848 — caused major out-migration; the "Forty-Eighters"
  • Austro-Prussian War 1866 — the seven-week war that ended Kurhessen
  • Franco-Prussian War 1870–71 — preceded the Empire's founding

Emigration history & archives

  • LAGIS Hessen · Auswanderer index — the Hessian emigration register
  • Arcinsys · Hessisches Hauptstaatsarchiv — Marburg state archive (HStAM, HHStAW)
  • German Americans · Wikipedia overview
  • FamilySearch · Hesse genealogy guide
  • Wikipedia · Hesse-Kassel state article

Charles Research — a multi-month transatlantic genealogical investigation into Charles Konzen (recorded in U.S. records as Gannsen / Johnson; 1835–1903), Hessian-born Civil War sergeant of Battery B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery, and his probable Trendelburg / Deisel origins in Churhessen (a working conclusion — the German baptism remains unrecovered).
