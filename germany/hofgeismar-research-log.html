Hofgeismar — Parish Research Log

51.496 N · 9.393 E · regional centre · ~8.2 km south of Deisel · compiled 21 June 2026
ELIMINATED

Both Hofgeismar town parishes (Altstädter and Neustädter) searched on all three tracks — marriage (Sessions 63–64), confirmation, and now baptism (Session 126, 27 June 2026). The town baptism indexes (Altstädter Taufen 1830–1843 = Archion 219265; Neustädter Taufen 1830–1863 = 219364) contain no Konze/Kontze/Konzen/Konsen/Gansen/Gonsen/Conson surname for their full span; the only phonetically-adjacent name is a distinct local Janson burgher family with no son baptised Carl/Karl in 1833–41. Surname-pool incompatible with Konze. Eliminated.

Confirmation-register sweep — “any Carl, 1833–1841”

Part of the June 2026 widened confirmation-register sweep across the Kreis Hofgeismar — looking for any boy named Carl/Karl born 1833–1841, surname open, who would surface in the 1847–1855 confirmation classes. This is the parish-specific row; the cross-parish ledger, the full list of boys named Carl that turned up, and the method/reliability notes live on Parish search coverage.

Baptism-index sweep — town parishes, 1833–1841 (Session 126, 27 Jun 2026)

Closes the previously-deferred town baptism track. Both town Taufen registers carry an alphabetical name index (“mit Register”) spanning the whole register; the K/G/I·J/C letter-sections were read at high zoom (boys + girls) — this answers the surname question for the entire 1833–1841 window in one pass. Full forensic detail, calibrations, and the January-1835 leaf read are in the session log.
