Stammen — Parish Research Log

51.566 N · 9.446 E · ~2.0 km east of Deisel · compiled 21 June 2026
ELIMINATED · Konze documented

1835 baptisms searched live on Archion. One Konze-pool entry — a stillborn male Kontze, b. 14 March 1835, Haus Nr. 21 (first-pass “Rudolph, b. 20 Mar” corrected at full zoom; see errata), child of Christian Ludwig Kontze (Tagelöhner) × Anne Gertrud née Temme: a distinct Stammen branch, not the Trendelburg Carl-Ludwig line; not Charles (stillborn). Kontze also appears 1833 as a godparent, and two Jungkontze mothers appear in 1835.

Decisive offline test remaining: Justus Ernst Konze’s siblings Heinrich Wilhelm (†1858) and Elisa Melusine (†1865) both died at Stammen, so the un-indexed Stammen burial register (220072, ~1843–1875) is the page-by-page read for Justus Ernst’s own fate — a Konze death there would eliminate the leading hypothesis; its absence would harden it.

Confirmation-register scan — “Carl” + Konze pool (1833–1841)

Per Jed’s request (22 June 2026), the un-indexed Stammen Konfirmationen 1831–2013 (Archion register 220066) was read class-by-class for the years 1833–1841, looking for any confirmand named Carl/Karl whose surname falls in the Konze phonetic pool (the n-s-n / n-z-n skeleton, or Konze / Kontze / Konzen / Jungkontze). The register opens (image 415) on the combined-volume title page; the Konfirmations-Buch für die Gemeinde Stammen [& Lamerden], Band 1 begins 22 May 1831 (image 416), records start image 417, and each written page (Seite) carries an explicit “Jahr 18__” header. Classes are small (4–14 confirmands) and several years split across Pfingstsonntag / Pfingstmontag.

RESULT No “Carl Konze.” All nine years (1833–1841, ~64 confirmands) were read entry-by-entry: no confirmand named Carl/Karl carries a Konze-pool surname. Seven boys named Carl appear, all under unrelated surnames (Leimann, Müller ×2, Schacht, Hollenbrecht, Schäfer, Henne). The only Konze-cluster surnames present anywhere in the window are Jungkontze (Conrad Ludwig, in the 1831 class — context, just outside) and Kuncke (two girls, 1834 & 1835), plus a bride-mother “geb. Kontze” (1834) and a mother “geb. Kuncke” (1839). The 1840 and 1841 classes (initially blocked by the Archion single-viewer limit) were re-read on a free session and are likewise clear.
