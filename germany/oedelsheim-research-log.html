Oedelsheim — Parish Research Log

Oberweser, Landkreis Kassel · on the Weser opposite Gieselwerder · Konf. reg. 219943 · compiled 26 June 2026
GANS FAMILY PRESENT, but EMIGRATION LEAD WEAKENED (likely an unused permit) — OPEN

New Landkreis Kassel target surfaced from Klaus Kunze’s Ortssippenbuch. Oedelsheim (Oberweser, on the Weser directly across from Gieselwerder) is not among the 36 parishes previously searched. Kunze’s printed OSB surname register attests the full Konze pool here — Kontze, Kontzen, Konzen, Jungkonz, Gans, Ganß — across the 16th–19th c. That prompted a confirmation-register read on Archion (26 Jun 2026).

Confirmation read — NEGATIVE for the Konze pool. Oedelsheim keeps its own Konfirmationen 1831–1972 (Archion reg. 219943; confirmation section title leaf Bild 1323). The reel is heavily shuffled and duplicated (parallel/fair-copy pages — e.g. 1846/47 appears at both Seite 44–45 and 50–51). The reel carries the classes twice — a scrambled draft section (Bild 1340–1348) and an ordered fair copy (Bild 1349–1359). The entire window 1846–1855 has now been read, including the prime 1849 class (Charles’s exact 1835-birth cohort; 14 children, 6 Knaben / 8 Mädchen per Pfr. Theobald) and the full 1851, 1852, 1853, 1854 and 1855 classes off the fair copy. No confirmand in any class carries a Kontze / Konzen / Jungkonz / Gans / Gansen / Ganson surname — only local Oedelsheim names (Thon, Gronemann, Niemeyer, Gerland, Roelcke, Wißmann, Probsthe, Friederichs, Ellermeyer, Wieneland, Cropp, Dittmar, Allendorf, Wolff, Schadrock, Reitz; and in 1851–55: Schaufuß, Bruns, Ostermann, Greiff, Quentin, Weber, Lange, Alroth, Rülcke, Lindner, May, Jung, Plaß, Wagner, Theurer, Brand, Zeller, Graf, Gronau, Plöger). The forename Carl recurs only as a middle name on local surnames. So although the surname lived in the parish historically, it appears nowhere in the 1846–1855 confirmand lists — the same OSB-vs-confirmation split seen at Gieselwerder. (Contrast Lippoldsberg, where the Gans surname DOES appear among confirmands.) Verdict: COMPLETE NEGATIVE across the full window.

Baptism track added (26 Jun 2026, Session 126) — PARTIAL, stays OPEN. Oedelsheim’s Taufen 1830–1877 (Archion reg. 219946, title Bild 646) was opened to check the 1833–1841 window. Two obstacles: the reel is shuffled (image order does not track year — e.g. an 1831 leaf sits at Bild 705, after an 1836/37 leaf), and the alphabetical index’s K(ontze) leaf (Bild 918) is too faded to read (ink loss + bleed-through; the AI transcription returns zero K entries). Sampled baptism leaves spanning 1832–1837 — including Charles’s exact Jan–Feb 1835 birth window — show no baptized Kontze/Konze/Gans child; the Carls present are all on local surnames (Heinrich Carl Schraeder b. Oct 1834; Friedrich Carl Wessel Hausnr 21 b. ~25 Feb 1835; Carl Dettmar a father). However, unlike the cleanly-negative parishes, the Gans surname IS present in Oedelsheim in the 1830s — recurring as godparents (“Carl August Gans”, “Andreas Gans”), the baptized children’s fathers being Lindner/Schnadhorst/Süg/Koch. So the baptism track cannot be declared a clean negative on sampling alone. Stays OPEN — a definitive result needs a shuffle-aware full-window leaf sweep and/or a Kurrent specialist reading the faded K-index leaf (Bild 918), or Kunze’s printed Oedelsheim OSB. Detail: Session126_Lippoldsberg_Oedelsheim_Vernawahlshausen_Baptism_Index_1833-41_June26_2026.md.

⭐ FULL 1834–1836 SWEEP (26 Jun 2026, Session 127) — the Gans surname is ALIVE here. The complete chronological 1834–1836 run (Bild 673–693) was read leaf-by-leaf, anchoring on each written “Jahr” header (the reel is shuffled). This overturns the “pool left before the 1840s” hypothesis: there is a documented Gans baptism in 1835 — Andreas August Gans, b. 25 Feb 1835, bapt. 15 Mar 1835, Oedelsheim Hausnr 2 (Bild 679/Seite 28; permalink archion.de/p/075cc0099a ↗; image-verified). Father Georg Friedrich Gans, Drechslermeister (turner); mother Johanne Marie Elisabeth née Kohlschein; godfather Carl August Gans, son of the Gastwirth (innkeeper) Andreas Gans. So Oedelsheim holds at least three Gans men in the 1830s (Georg Friedrich the turner; Andreas the innkeeper; Carl August the innkeeper’s grown son), with “Gans” recurring as godparents through 1834–36. But still NO Carl Gans: across the fully-read window there is no boy with rufname Carl/Karl on a Gans surname and no Gans child born 26 Jan 1835 (January 1835 read in full — closest boy is George Heinrich Westerman b. 24 Jan 1835, a non-Gans). Charles is therefore not the 1835 Oedelsheim Gans baptism on the record — but with a living Gans family in exactly the right place and decade, Oedelsheim is now a priority for a full Gans-family reconstruction (all years; marriages 1830s; burials; emigration ~1850s; Kunze’s printed OSB). Detail: Session127_Oedelsheim_1834-36_Gans_Family_Found_June26_2026.md.

⭐⭐ MULTI-HOUSEHOLD GANS POPULATION (26 Jun 2026, Session 128). Extending the Gans sweep into 1837–1839 shows the Gans family is several households, not one — comparable in weight to the Hümme Kontze cluster: (A) Georg Friedrich Gans, Drechslermeister (turner), Hausnr 2 × Johanne Marie Elisabeth née Kohlschein → Andreas August (1835) + Sophie Rosine Charlotte (1837); (C) Friedrich Wilhelm Gans, Tagelöhner, Hausnr 11 × Anna Christine née Herwig → Friedrich Wilhelm Gans (b. Jan 1839, Bild 707/Seite 56, image-verified); (B) Andreas Gans, Gastwirth (innkeeper) → son Carl August Gans (adult godparent, b. ~1810–18); plus a George Andreas Gans, Stellmacher (godparent). Still no Carl Gans child (Gans sons found = Andreas August, Friedrich Wilhelm; the only “Carl Gans” is the ~1810s-born innkeeper’s son). So Charles is not a documented 1835 Oedelsheim Gans baptism — but this is now the project’s strongest Gans→Gannsen lead, pointing at a Gans son emigrating ~1850s rather than an 1835 baptism. Full picture + follow-up plan (finish 1830–33 & 1840–77 sweep; Gans marriages & burials; Carl August Gans’s family; passenger lists): Oedelsheim_Gans_Family_Reconstruction_June26_2026.md.

⭐⭐⭐ EMIGRATION RECORD FOUND (26 Jun 2026, Session 128). The LAGIS “Hessische Auswanderer” database (Hessisches Landesarchiv) holds a direct, citable record: “Gans, Georg Friedrich, Oedelsheim, 1834” (lagis.hessen.de/resolve/de/ha/110864) — destination Nordamerika, 4 persons emigrating, Vermögen 110, source HStAM Best. 16 Nr. 3216, S. 199 (Marburg). It sits in a Weser-valley Gans emigration cluster (Lkr. Kassel): Oedelsheim 1834, Heisebeck 1840 & 1846, Gieselwerder 1846. Since the Oedelsheim turner Georg Friedrich Gans stayed (baptising children 1835–37), this 1834 emigrant is a separate Gans household that actually left. Working hypothesis (strong, unproven): Charles Gannsen (b. 26 Jan 1835, no German baptism ever found) was a child of this emigrant family, born 1835 after they left Germany — which would finally explain the missing baptism, and fits the read evidence (all Jan-1835 Oedelsheim baptisms searched, no Carl Gans). Decisive next steps: scan HStAM Best.16 Nr.3216 S.199 (names the four); find a Georg Friedrich Gans / “Gannsen” family arriving ~1834–35 on the US side and place Charles with it.

✅ INDEPENDENTLY VERIFIED (26 Jun 2026, Session 20E). A second pass opened the live LAGIS record (ID 110864) field-by-field and confirms every cited detail: name Gans, Georg Friedrich; Herkunftsort Oedelsheim (Ortskennziffer 633030070, Gemeinde Wesertal, Lkr. Kassel); destination Nordamerika (Geonames 7729890); Datierung 1834; Zahl der Auswanderer 4; Vermögen 110; Nachweis HStAM Best. 16 Nr. 3216, Seite 199 (Hessisches Staatsarchiv Marburg, "B Files up to 1867"). Findability caveat: the structured Herkunftsort filter returns 0 for "Oedelsheim"; the record is reachable only via global full-text search "Gans Oedelsheim", so re-access by ID 110864. The "4 persons" are the family at departure (1834); if Charles belongs here he is a child born ~Jan 1835 after departure (at sea / US side), which fits the absent German baptism. Citable lead is solid; the actual file (S. 199) still needs pulling to name the four.

Remaining follow-ups

  • Confirmations 1846–1855 done — complete negative; nothing left here.
  • ⭐ Decisive test of the emigration hypothesis (27 Jun 2026 research pass). The U.S.-side records that would place Charles with the 1834 emigrant Gans family are all login-gated (Ancestry / FamilySearch) and were not resolvable on the open web; note also that the standard index Germans to America only begins 1840, so an 1834 arrival pre-dates it. The single most decisive action is to pull Sgt. “Gannsen / Johnson”’s Civil War pension & compiled service file (Battery B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery; unit organised St. Louis Jan 1862) from NARA / Fold3 — a pension file typically states birthplace and parents’ names, which would confirm or kill the Oedelsheim-Gans origin outright. Then: (a) order HStAM Best. 16 Nr. 3216, S. 199 from Marburg (Arcinsys) to name the four 1834 emigrants; (b) search Ancestry/FamilySearch (Jed’s login) for a Gans/Ganß/Ganss household of ~4 arriving Bremen/Le Havre → NY/Baltimore/New Orleans ~1834–36, and an 1850/1860 census Carl/Charles Gans b.~1835 in Missouri.
  • Optional close-out: Kunze’s printed Oedelsheim OSB (~€40) — shows whether the Konze/Gans families died out / emigrated before the 1840s.
  • Faded K-index leaf set up on Archion (27 Jun 2026): the baptism index’s Kontze/Konze page is reg. 219946 Bild 918 (K left / L right) — permalink archion.de/p/0f5fc4997a. The left “K” column is severely ink-faded: the AI transcription returns 0 K entries and by eye only the column header + a faint top cluster are legible. Needs a Kurrent specialist or image processing to confirm/exclude a Kontze baptism — the last open thread on the Oedelsheim baptism (Gans) track.

Priority for fresh effort now sits with Lippoldsberg (Gans appears among its confirmands).
