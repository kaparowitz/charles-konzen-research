Schöneberg — Parish Research Log

51.578 N · 9.357 E · ~4.4 km west of Deisel · pre-1822 undigitised · compiled 21 June 2026 · confirmation sweep 23 June 2026 · baptism Taufen-index sweep 27 June 2026
ELIMINATED

Grandjot territory. Pre-1822 records remain undigitised. The post-1822 baptism register’s full alphabetical Namensregister has now been read surname-by-surname (27 June 2026, reg. 220012): the G section is exclusively Grandjot and Gaide (no Gans / Gansen / Gonsen / Gannsen); the C page is blank (no Conson / Contze); and the K section’s male side is only Knorre / Küchenbecker / Krohne — no male Konze and no boy named Carl on any Konze- or Gans-pool surname. Every boy named Carl in the index sits on a local surname (Grandjot, Gaide, Küchenbecker, Jaeger, Scholle, Tiebert, Saße…).

One newly-surfaced family (verified at the record, 27 June 2026): the K Weiblich sub-column carries a recurring surname — written “Ronze” but filed by the parish under K (it is absent from the R page), so the indexer read it as “Konze” — for three girls (Maria Caroline S.61, Maria Christine S.65, Johanna Maria Louise S.70). Pulling the Seite 61 entry (p/366a4094ec, Bild 1650, entry Nro 289) confirms a real household: a Schäfer (shepherd) named Konze/Ronze and his wife Anna Catharine geb. Leck, child Maria Caroline baptised 1862, with a godmother “Marie Caroline Schäfer geb. Konze.” This is a genuine K-filed family in Schöneberg in the 1860s — a data point for the Konze-by-town map — but the three children are female and born 1862–1870, decades after and unrelated to Charles (b. 1835), so the verdict is unchanged. The Kurrent capital is genuinely ambiguous between K and R; a definitive reading (Konze vs Ronze vs Kunze) is worth one confirmation from Peter Schräder. See the Taufen-index table below.

Confirmation sweep complete (23 June 2026, Session 114). The 1847–1853 Konfirmationen classes in reg. 220012 were read leaf-by-leaf. No Carl is born in 1834 or 1835, and the 1848 and 1849 classes — the natural cohort for a boy born 26 Jan 1835 — contain no Carl at all. The four Carls that do appear (b. 1833, 1836, 1836, 1839) all carry local surnames (Siebert, Küthen, Grandjot, Wagner), none a Konze / *n·s·n* variant. This clears the previously jumble-blocked “Not yet” cell and strengthens the verdict.

Result: across the full Taufen Namensregister there is no Konze / Kontze / Konsen / Konzen as a male surname, no Gans / Gansen / Gonsen / Gannsen anywhere (G = Grandjot & Gaide), and no Conson / Contze (C blank). Boys named Carl are plentiful but every one sits on a local surname (Grandjot, Gaide, Küchenbecker, Jaeger, Scholle, Tiebert, Saße, Müller…), none a Konze- or Gans-pool name — matching the confirmation sweep. This upgrades the baptism track from an unread “index only” cell to a complete surname-level index read, and Schöneberg stays ELIMINATED for Charles. The single residual is the ambiguous “Ronze/?Konze” female surname (S.61/65/70) flagged above for record-level verification.
