The Konze / Kontze family, by town

Every documented Konze / Kontze / Kontzen / Contze / Contzen / Konzen individual — including the earliest register spellings Konde and Konten (pre-1770 scribal variants, preserved here as written) — in the Diemel-valley cluster where the surname occurs — one entry per person, grouped by parish and family, with exact birth, death, and marriage dates and the Archion register + Seite (page) reference for each. The people-level companion to the Hessian parish map. FamilySearch IDs are filled where known; where blank, the person is not yet on FamilySearch and can be added.

Köster is excluded (a separate, ubiquitous local surname). Sources: Archion registers (cited per row), Peter Schräder’s Stammtafeln (“Stammtafel”), HETRINA military index, and Sessions 11–94. Exact dates newly read from the registers on 6–10 June 2026 are bold. Where no marriage or children are documented, the row says so explicitly. Last updated 13 June 2026 (Session 102: Deisel missing-vitals sweep — 14 rows resolved via the FS communal tree, the 1774–1814 Christoph merge register-verified, the Konze burial K-leaf 1830–76 read in full, two false-Konze households (Koch, Knapp) weeded out; plus a Trendelburg vitals pass — 6 rows resolved from the FS tree, and the 220114 burial Namensregister read and found to be missing its A–K leaves, so the register-anchored 1830–77 deaths have no index route. See the per-town pages.)

Charles Research — a multi-month transatlantic genealogical investigation into Charles Konzen (recorded in U.S. records as Gannsen / Johnson; 1835–1903), Hessian-born Civil War sergeant of Battery B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery, and his probable Trendelburg / Deisel origins in Churhessen (a working conclusion — the German baptism remains unrecovered).

      
Compiled by Jed Johnson with Peter Schräder and James Gunnis.
