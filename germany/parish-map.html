Hessian Parish Map

Every parish the project has investigated for Charles Konzen's German baptism — colour-coded by candidate status. Trendelburg-area, Kreis Hofgeismar, Churhessen.

The 1866 St. Marcus marriage register names Charles's home as "Deisse, Kreis Cassel, Churhessen" — the village of Deisel, in the Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis. Every parish within reasonable walking distance has been searched — together with a wider adjacent ring, the cross-border Catholic parishes, and the far-south place-name alternatives; this map shows them all, with the Konze surname pool at the centre and the eliminated lines around the perimeter. (Click a far-south card — Mainz, Diez, the Tier-3 "Deissen" parishes — to fly the map there.) Click any marker — or any parish card below — to fly the map to that location. Where the Konze name lives — June 2026 distribution sweep

A surname sweep across the ring (Session 88) shows the Konze/Kontze pool is hyper-local: a single tight Diemel-valley cluster — Trendelburg · Deisel · Friedrichsfeld · Stammen · Hümme, all within ~5 km — with no Konze presence in any surrounding parish sampled or index-searched. (Nearby Gottsbüren is named as one Kontze man's 1833 birthplace but holds no Kontze in its own registers — a claimed, unconfirmed edge of the cluster.) Charles's two stated origins sit exactly inside the only place the surname exists. See the changelog, or the Konze family listed by town.

Which Konze son left — June 2026 FamilySearch-tree reconciliation

A cross-read against the collaborative FamilySearch shared Family Tree — which holds an independently register-sourced reconstruction of the whole Trendelburg/Deisel Konze clan — corroborated the project's hand-built framework and sharpened the candidate picture by negative evidence. Running every documented Konze male of Charles's generation through the searchable German death and emigration indexes, all but one are fully accounted for in Germany (they stayed, married, and died in the parish complex). The one same-named exception, Carl Wilhelm Konze (b. 15 Sep 1831, Trendelburg), was surfaced and then eliminated — documented dying at Trendelburg on 30 October 1869. The sole male of the cohort who leaves no adult German footprint at all is Justus Ernst Konze (b. 14 Nov 1829, Friedrichsfeld) — a lead, not a confirmed identity. The two decisive tests now lead are both offline: a page-by-page read of the un-indexed Stammen burial register (Archion 220072) and an individual emigration / release file in HStAM Bestand 180 Hofgeismar. See errata · 21 June 2026 and the negative-results audit.

Stammen Eliminated · June 2026

✦ Konze family documented here
51.566 N · 9.446 E · reg. 220069
~2.0 km east
1835 baptisms searched live on Archion. One Konze-pool entry found — a stillborn male Kontze, b. 14 March 1835, Haus Nr. 21 (the first-pass reading “Rudolph, b. 20 Mar” was corrected at full zoom — see errata), child of Christian Ludwig Kontze (Tagelöhner) × Anne Gertrud née Temme — a newly-documented distinct Stammen branch, not the Trendelburg Carl-Ludwig line; not Charles (stillborn). Gottsbüren Eliminated · June 2026

✦ Konze claimed · unconfirmed
51.552 N · 9.468 E · reg. 219049
~3.9 km SE
Jan–Feb 1835 baptisms searched live on Archion: no Konze-pool male, no illegitimate male. A Stammen 1833 marriage names Christian Ludwig Kontze as born in Gottsbüren — but Gottsbüren's own books are entirely Kontze-free — no Kontze in baptisms (1804/06/08), marriages (1795–1806), or burials (1805–Jan 1825 read entry-by-entry; none 1827–33 either); only the maternal Garland family is native here. The Andreas Kontze family lived — and died — elsewhere. So Gottsbüren is a claimed but unconfirmed Konze tie (Andreas Kontze an in-migrant), not a documented Konze village. See changelog.
