Gottsbüren — Parish Research Log

51.552 N · 9.468 E · reg. 219049 (Taufen) · 219046 (Konfirmationen) · ~3.9 km SE of Deisel · compiled 21 June 2026, confirmation sweep added 22 June 2026
ELIMINATED · claimed but unconfirmed

Jan–Feb 1835 baptisms searched live on Archion: no Konze-pool male, no illegitimate male. A Stammen 1833 marriage names Christian Ludwig Kontze as born in Gottsbüren — but Gottsbüren’s own books are entirely Kontze-free (no Kontze in baptisms 1804/06/08, marriages 1795–1806, or burials 1805–Jan 1825 read entry-by-entry; none 1827–33). Only the maternal Garland family is native here. A claimed but unconfirmed Konze tie (Andreas Kontze an in-migrant), not a documented Konze village.

Confirmation sweep added 22 June 2026. The separate Konfirmationen 1831–1932 book (reg. 219046) was read live for the “any Carl, born 1833–1841, surname open” window — the read this parish previously lacked (it had been cleared on its baptism/marriage/burial books alone). The prime cohort for Charles, the 1849 and 1850 classes (boys born 1834–1835), was read in full; 1848, 1851 and 1852 were also read. The result matches every other parish: the forename Carl recurs (≈10 boys across these classes) but never once on a Konze-pool or *n-s-n surname, and no Carl matches Charles. The surnames are uniformly local — Schröder, Schäfer, Gerland, Hücking, Tolle, Naumann, Niemeyer, Köhler. Per-image Archion permalinks are now minted (below).

Reliability. Reads are by eye in Archion's hi-res viewer. The forename Carl (vs. Conrad/Johann) is dependable in Kurrent; exact birth-year digits and surname spellings are best readings, not certified transcriptions, and warrant a second eye (Peter Schräder / on-site). The verdict — no Carl on a Konze-pool surname — does not hinge on any single uncertain digit.

Residual. The Konfirmationen section is bound out of order and duplicated (e.g. the 1845 leaf appears at both Bild 1775 and 1788; a 1852 leaf is displaced into the 1846/47 slot at Bild 1776). The 1846/1847 and 1853–1855 classes (children born 1832–33 and 1838–41 — the edges of the window, away from Charles's 1835 anchor) were not individually pinned in this pass; they sit somewhere in the shuffled leaves and are flagged for the on-site trip. The prime 1849–1850 cohort is fully read, so the negative is saturated for Charles's own birth window.
