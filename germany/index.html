Germany & Origins

The Hessian-side investigation. Charles named "Deisse, Kreis Cassel, Churhessen" on his 1866 marriage register. Every parish within walking distance has been searched; the Trendelburg / Friedrichsfeld Konze pool of Churhessen is the surviving working hypothesis.

RegionHesse-Kassel · Churhessen
Parishes searched36 across 95 sessions
StatusKonzen surname — candidate (unconfirmed) · May 2026

This is the section landing for the German-side investigation. The full narrative, latest findings feed, and integrated cross-references are on the home-page Germany & Origins tab; the deep-dive pages and standalone diagrams are linked below.

★ May 2026 candidate (unconfirmed)

KONZEN is a leading candidate for Charles's German surname

The Kontzen-family form documented continuously in the Trendelburg parish complex of Churhessen, ~5 km north of Charles's stated home village (Deisel, per his 1862 enlistment record). Nine distinct American renderings of the surname, recorded by at least five independent scribes across nine attestations between 1866 and 1896, all converge on this German source — including the 1890 daughter's-death-record reading Konzen (with z), the single American attestation of the full Hessian z-form. Held in the project's Working state pending recovery of the specific German baptismal entry.
