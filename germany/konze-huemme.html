Hümme Konze documented · line fully reconstructed

The Hümme Kontze line is now fully read (burials 1830–74 + marriages 1830–91 + baptisms 1830–63, complete indexes). The old note “the last Konze male emigrated in the 1840s” was imprecise — six adult Kontze men stayed into the 1870s–1930s. Exactly ONE man vanishes without trace: Christoph Kontze, b. 13 Feb 1814 — the presumed emigrant, now named. The baptism register (219532) was swept leaf-by-leaf on 23 June 2026 — all ten Kontze baptisms it indexes are now cited with images (table at the foot of this page); they supply primary birth dates, add two children not previously listed, and confirm five Kontze father-households.

★ The Stammen swineherd’s son — the Andreas×Garland branch resolved

Breakthrough (7 Jun 2026): the 1837 burial of Johannes Kontze names his parents — “des verstorbenen Schweinehirten Andreas Kontze und der verstorbenen Marie Elisabeth geb. Gehrland” — i.e. the SAME unplaced couple (Andreas Kontze × Marie Elisabeth Garland) named in Christian Ludwig’s 1833 Stammen marriage. The family’s base was Stammen (Andreas was the swineherd there); Johannes b. Stammen 1788, Christian Ludwig stated-b. Gottsbüren ~1806.

Baptism register (Taufen 1830–1863, 219532) — children’s vitals added 23 Jun 2026

The Hümme baptism register was swept leaf-by-leaf on 23 June 2026 and all ten Kontze baptisms in its alphabetical index pulled and read against the image. (The reel reads cleanly at image = Seite + 1208; the earlier “shuffled/duplicated” impression was a stale-canvas artifact of the viewer.) These are the children’s primary birth/baptism records — previously their dates on this page came from marriage- and burial-margins or the FS tree. Two children below were not previously listed (Friedrich 1841; Heinrich Friedrich 1843), and several dates are refined. The two day-level discrepancies have been resolved by re-reading the register: Conrad b. 25 May 1844 (the page’s “16 May” was wrong, now corrected) and Christoph Heinrich b. 23 March 1847 (the page was right; an earlier “20 Mar” transcription was the slip). The tables and trees on this page now carry the corrected dates. Full transcription & method in the Hümme research log and the image→Seite map (germany/219532_image-seite-map.md).

“Naumann” is a real place. The S.81 entry records Friedrich’s mother as “Marie Christine Hofeditz von Naumann” in a clear hand — confirming “Naumann” as a genuine place-name in this parish’s usage. That reopens the contested “Naumann vs Stammen” reading of the linen-weaver Johannes Kontze’s origin in the 1837 confirmation entry. Five father-households are now confirmed by baptisms: two distinct men named Johannes (the Ackermann d.1834 ×Uffelmann, and the Tagelöhner b.1810 ×Hofeditz), Georg Friedrich ×Kuhlmann, Friedrich ×Büngener, and Wilhelm ×Busch — plus Johann Heinrich ×Büngener from the marriage/burial books.
