Heisebeck — Parish Research Log

51.606 N · 9.506 E · ~7.4 km NE of Deisel · compiled 21 June 2026
PRIORITY GAP — NOW DIGITISED · candidate found

Map-changing finding (21 June 2026). The Heisebeck baptism volume that covers January 1835 — long recorded as undigitised and held only on-site at the Landeskirchliches Archiv Kassel — is in fact now on Archion as Taufen 1830–1887 (register 219175). It was opened live and the January 1835 window read.

Entry 76 is a male illegitimate child named “Carl Friedrich August”, born 29 January 1835 at 7 p.m., Heisebeck Oberdorf No. 33, baptized 8 February 1835. Mother: Dorothea Henriette Charlotte Wehmann, the unverehelichte (unmarried) daughter of the Wehmann (Köster/sexton) family; no father named, so the child carries the mother’s surname. The remarks column is empty — unlike his neighbours (entries 75 and 79 both read “confirmed 1849”) he shows no confirmation, death, or marriage note, consistent with a child who left Heisebeck before confirmation age (~1849): the emigrant pattern.

This is the strongest single match the project has surfaced for the illegitimate-birth scenario the Y-DNA keeps live: male, given name Carl, born late January 1835, illegitimate, in the parish flagged as the #1 priority gap. It is a candidate, not a confirmed identity — the recorded birth is 29 Jan (Charles: 26 Jan, a 3-day discrepancy) and the surname is Wehmann, not Gannsen/Konze. The neighbouring window males (entry 75 Georg Müller b. 25 Nov 1834, entry 77 Johann Wilhelm Wenkel b. 23 Feb 1835) are legitimate and non-matching.

Baptism-register index check — K / I-J / G (26 June 2026)

The full alphabetical name-index of the Taufen register (reg. 219175) was read for the in-pool surnames. K (Bild 1507–1508) = Koch, Klinge, Küster, Klemme, Kassau — no Konze/Kontze. I/J (Bild 1506) = Jacobi, Jaspe, Jericke — no Jungkonze. G = Gerland, Gans/Ganß, Gerling. The G section spans Bild 1503–1505 (Seite ≈ Bild − 1237; Bild 1283 = Seite 46 = Jahr 1839, verified). K and I/J are clean negatives; Gans is the live result — see the dedicated section below.

GANS — IN-POOL SURNAME, MAJOR HEISEBECK FAMILY

Correction (26 June 2026) to the first index pass. The earlier note here read only the late tail of the G index (Bild 1505, Seiten 145–253) and wrongly concluded Gans appeared only from ~1866. The full G section spans Bild 1503–1505, and the early Gans baptisms sit on Bild 1504. The Ortssippenbuch Heisebeck-Arenborn (Klaus Kunze) confirms the scale: its surname register lists “Gans” against ~33 family numbers — a deeply-rooted, multi-century native Heisebeck surname.

Gans baptisms IN the 1833–1841 window (Seiten ~5–60): Christine Wilhelmine Gans (f, S.41, ~1838); Eduard Gottlieb Gans (m, S.46, bapt. 16 Jun 1839, father Heinrich Wilhelm Gans, Schneider × Dorothea; p/efb5066fa2); Johann Friedrich Gans (m, S.56, ~1840–41). At least two Gans households: Heinrich Wilhelm Gans (Schneider) × Dorothea, and Tagelöhner Ernst Wilhelm Ganß (whose line produced the illegitimate Wilhelm Ganß S.165/1866 and Adolf S.196). Late-G index p/8ed0eccb8b.

Bearing on Charles: still no Gans son baptised ~26 Jan 1835 — the first Gans of either sex is S.41 (~1838) and Seite 19 (=1835) carries no Gans, so the Heisebeck Gans children all begin ~1838. Charles is therefore not a Heisebeck Gans baptism in the window. But Heisebeck is now a strong Gans-pool locus in the Diemel/Weser corridor (in-pool: Gans ≈ Gannsen), warranting OSB mining, the 1836–38 Gans marriages, and the pre-1830 baptism volume for the parents’ generation. Detail: Session127 log.

With the blank baptism-remarks column (Heisebeck back-references confirmations into the baptism remarks), this confirms he was not confirmed in Heisebeck — the "left before confirmation age / emigrant" pattern.   • US forward-trace COMPLETE — NEGATIVE (27 June 2026, FamilySearch logged-in). Searched FamilySearch historical records for Carl/Charles Wehmann b. 1833–1837, United States. Findings: (1) Missouri-filtered (77 hits) — the only St. Louis “Charles Wehmann” is a LATER man (Enlisted/Officer Muster Rolls, military service 1917–1919; St. Louis city directory 1893) — born ~1880s–90s, not our Charles (d. 1903); no Carl/Charles Wehmann b.~1835 Hesse resident in St. Louis matching Charles. (2) The one Carl Wehmann b. 1835 Germany died in Brooklyn, NY on 18 Jul 1901 with named parents Carl & Anna Wehmann (NY City Municipal Deaths) — legitimate, so NOT the illegitimate Heisebeck child, and Brooklyn not St. Louis. (3) Other Carl Wehmann arrivals (Germans to America Index) landed at NY / New Orleans ~Oct 1860, b. 1834. Structural note: an 1859 Bremen emigrant leaves no Bremen manifest (pre-1874 Bremen lists were destroyed). Net: the illegitimate Heisebeck Carl Wehmann does not trace to Charles; with Charles firmly recorded as Gannsen/Gonson/Johnson in St. Louis and the surname Wehmann→Gannsen phonetically implausible, the candidate is a same-profile coincidence, not a corroborated identity — downgraded from a live lead. Residual (optional, low value): Ancestry census-image review + NARA AAD “Wehmann” for completeness.Lane: DONE — gated FamilySearch, 27 Jun 2026
  • Ortssippenbuch surname register checked — no Konze line in Heisebeck (23 June 2026). The published Ortssippenbuch Heisebeck und Arenborn (Klaus Kunze, Uslar 2000; ISBN 978-3-933334-08-4 — a full evaluation of the local church books) has its complete name/place register online at klauskunze.com. It lists no Konze / Konzen / Kontze / Gansen / Gonsen / Jansen surname; the established Heisebeck families (Schulze, Klinge, Thiele, Wienecke, Helwig, Schäfer) are all present, so the index really is the surname list, not a stub. Reading: the Konze surname was not native to Heisebeck, consistent with the standing candidate here being the illegitimate Wehmann child (mother's surname), not a Konze. Caveat: a compiled secondary index, not the original register — corroborating context only.Lane: DONE — open web, 23 Jun 2026
