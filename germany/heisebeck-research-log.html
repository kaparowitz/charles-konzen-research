Heisebeck — Parish Research Log

51.606 N · 9.506 E · ~7.4 km NE of Deisel · compiled 21 June 2026
PRIORITY GAP — NOW DIGITISED · candidate found

Map-changing finding (21 June 2026). The Heisebeck baptism volume that covers January 1835 — long recorded as undigitised and held only on-site at the Landeskirchliches Archiv Kassel — is in fact now on Archion as Taufen 1830–1887 (register 219175). It was opened live and the January 1835 window read.

Entry 76 is a male illegitimate child named “Carl Friedrich August”, born 29 January 1835 at 7 p.m., Heisebeck Oberdorf No. 33, baptized 8 February 1835. Mother: Dorothea Henriette Charlotte Wehmann, the unverehelichte (unmarried) daughter of the Wehmann (Köster/sexton) family; no father named, so the child carries the mother’s surname. The remarks column is empty — unlike his neighbours (entries 75 and 79 both read “confirmed 1849”) he shows no confirmation, death, or marriage note, consistent with a child who left Heisebeck before confirmation age (~1849): the emigrant pattern.

This is the strongest single match the project has surfaced for the illegitimate-birth scenario the Y-DNA keeps live: male, given name Carl, born late January 1835, illegitimate, in the parish flagged as the #1 priority gap. It is a candidate, not a confirmed identity — the recorded birth is 29 Jan (Charles: 26 Jan, a 3-day discrepancy) and the surname is Wehmann, not Gannsen/Konze. The neighbouring window males (entry 75 Georg Müller b. 25 Nov 1834, entry 77 Johann Wilhelm Wenkel b. 23 Feb 1835) are legitimate and non-matching.

With the blank baptism-remarks column (Heisebeck back-references confirmations into the baptism remarks), this confirms he was not confirmed in Heisebeck — the "left before confirmation age / emigrant" pattern. No Carl/Charles Wehmann b. ~1835 of Hessian origin surfaced via open-web search; manifests, census, and Find A Grave individual entries sit behind FamilySearch/Ancestry logins. Untried concrete lane: the free NARA AAD "Germans to America" index (used decisively in Session 107 for the Konze/Gansen pool) was never run on the Wehmann surname — recommended next step, alongside FamilySearch/Ancestry under login.Lane: OPEN — gated US sites + NARA AAD "Wehmann"
  • Ortssippenbuch surname register checked — no Konze line in Heisebeck (23 June 2026). The published Ortssippenbuch Heisebeck und Arenborn (Klaus Kunze, Uslar 2000; ISBN 978-3-933334-08-4 — a full evaluation of the local church books) has its complete name/place register online at klauskunze.com. It lists no Konze / Konzen / Kontze / Gansen / Gonsen / Jansen surname; the established Heisebeck families (Schulze, Klinge, Thiele, Wienecke, Helwig, Schäfer) are all present, so the index really is the surname list, not a stub. Reading: the Konze surname was not native to Heisebeck, consistent with the standing candidate here being the illegitimate Wehmann child (mother's surname), not a Konze.
