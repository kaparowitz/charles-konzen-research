Fulda (Stadt, ev.) — Parish Research Log

50.555 N · 9.681 E · Stadt Fulda, Kirchenkreis Fulda · ~90 km SE of Deisel · compiled 26 June 2026
ELIMINATED

Tier-3 toponym lead (James Gunnis, 26 June 2026): a reading of the marriage-record place “Deissen” as Dassen — a ~50-person village in a Catholic district whose few Protestants would have been served by the Evangelical town church in Fulda. James flagged a specific baptism in the Fulda EV Taufen register (“image 852, 1838 … Karl Friedrich Gastmann?, father Michael, a Feldwebel”) as worth checking.

The entry is real and now verified against the original image (Archion reg. 168326, Bild 852, Seite 64, Eintrag 298): Karl Friedrich, legitimate first son, born the 26th (Feb) 1838 at the Petersgasse in Fulda, baptised 3 March 1838; father Michael Gastmann, Feldwebel (sergeant) in the 1st Company of the 2nd Infantry Regiment; mother Anna Catharina. The surname reads clearly with an initial G — confirming James’s “Gastmann” over Archion’s machine-OCR “Hartmann.”

Not Charles. Three independent mismatches: the surname is Gastmann (a soldier’s family — not a Konze / Gansen / *n·s·n name); the place is Kreis Fulda, not the “Kreis Cassel” Charles gave his 1866 pastor; and the birth is 1838, a year outside the strongest U.S. evidence (26 Jan 1835) though inside the wide 1833–1841 envelope. The 1851–1853 Fulda confirmation classes were scanned (below) and hold no Gastmann — consistent with a garrison family that moved on with the regiment. Logged as a documented near-miss, like the Dissen and Liebenau “Carl August” entries.

Surname check: the machine “Erkannter Text” panel rendered the father as “Hartmann,” but the handwriting’s initial is unmistakably a capital G, and the body reads Gastman(n) — James’s reading is correct. Birth/baptism days were read off the leaf and would bear a second-pass confirmation by a Kurrent reader; the year (1838), the given name (Karl Friedrich), the legitimacy, and the father’s name + military rank are secure.

Confirmation scan — “any Gastmann, 1851–1853”

A boy born Feb 1838 would normally be confirmed at Easter ~1852 (age 14). Fulda (Stadt) EV Konfirmationen 1831–1910, Archion reg. 168320 ↗ (206 images; no machine text-recognition available — read by eye). The 1862/1863 classes sit at Bild 52; working back, the 1852 class is on Bild 37 (left page) and the 1853 class opens on Bild 37 (right page, year-header “1853”) and runs onto Bild 38.

The scan covered the prime 1852 class and the adjoining 1853 class (to catch a late confirmation of a Feb-1838 birth). No Gastmann confirmand appears, and the only “Carl Friedrich” in the window is Conrad Ahl’s son, not Michael Gastmann’s. The neighbouring classes (1850–1851, 1854) were not exhaustively swept; given the surname/Kreis/year mismatches the family was not pursued further here. The absence is what a garrison family produces: a Feldwebel of the 2. Infanterie-Regiment would have been posted away from Fulda, and the son confirmed wherever the regiment then stood.

Forward trace — the regiment, and the indexed databases

Because the father was a career soldier, the family’s movements track the regiment, not the parish. The unit named in the baptism — the Kurhessen 2. Infanterie-Regiment (1. Compagnie) — is the line regiment renamed “2. Infanterie-Regiment (Landgraf Wilhelm)” in 1845; after the 1866 Prussian annexation its personnel formed Prussian Infanterie-Regiment Nr. 82. It was a Kurhessen line regiment whose battalions/companies rotated through the electorate’s garrison towns — Kassel, Hanau, Marburg, Hersfeld, Ziegenhain, Fulda — with the 1st Company demonstrably at Fulda in 1838; the regiment saw action in 1848/49 (I. Bataillon at Frankfurt; II. Bataillon at Düppel / Jütland). A Feldwebel’s family would therefore disperse across whichever garrison parishes the unit occupied next — the records are real but scattered, and almost all sit in un-indexed Kurhessen church books on Archion.

The “Carl Gestmann” 1867 immigration lead — examined

The one near-name hit was run to ground. Carl Gestmann, male, age 30 (b. ~1837), occupation Farmer, arrived New York 18 May 1867 aboard the ship Atalanta (departed Le Havre & London), destination Brooklyn. Two records, same man: FamilySearch Germans to America Index (ark KD3K-Y9Z) and the underlying Ancestry New York Arriving Passenger & Crew Lists (coll. 7488, rec. 6188660; NARA M237).

For the matchAgainst / unresolved
Surname Gestmann ≈ Gastmann (vowel variant); birth ~1837 ≈ 1838; male; from “Hessen.”Place of origin on the manifest reads only “Hesia / Jessia” (= Hessen) — a region, no town; “Germans to America” Hessen entries are as often Hesse-Darmstadt as Kurhessen. Occupation Farmer sits awkwardly with a town-dwelling sergeant’s son (Petersgasse, Fulda). The manifest image (Atalanta, list #397) shows him travelling alone in a block of unrelated Hessian passengers — no wife, no children, no Gastmann kin; no parents, no Fulda tie.

Verdict on the lead: name/age/region-plausible but unconfirmable — the generic “Hessia” origin and the farmer occupation stop it short of the Fulda Gastmann boy, and nothing ties it to Fulda or to Michael Gastmann. It also has no bearing on Charles: Charles was already in the U.S. (Battery B enlistment Dec 1862; St. Louis marriage 1866), so a man landing in 1867 cannot be him. Held as a possible-but-unproven destination for the candidate; only the original manifest image (fellow passengers / a clearer origin) could move it either way.

Net of the chase: the man leaves no confirmable trace in the major indexed databases — consistent with a garrison family whose later records are only in un-indexed Kurhessen parishes. A genuine forward trace (marriage, death) would mean reading the EV garrison-parish registers of the towns the 2. Inf.-Rgt. occupied after 1838 (Hanau, Kassel, Marburg, Hersfeld …) leaf-by-leaf on Archion. Low value, since the man is already excluded as Charles — recorded here so the lead is fully closed and not re-opened.

Garrison-parish sweep on Archion — 27 June 2026

Acting on the “read the EV garrison-parish registers leaf-by-leaf” follow-up, the Kurhessen garrison towns of the 2. Inf.-Rgt. (Kassel, Hanau, Marburg, Hersfeld, Ziegenhain) were worked directly on Archion. Two structural facts shaped the search: Archion has no cross-register name or full-text search (the search is place-based; the advanced filter only narrows by record type, and “komplett transkribiert” gives only a per-page recognised-text panel), and the pre-1866 Kurhessen church-book indexes are routinely bound out of alphabetical order and duplicated. The one decisive simplification: the garrison soldiers’ families were enrolled in dedicated military congregations, of which only one covers Charles’s / the Gastmann era.

Net: the single most diagnostic garrison register — the Kassel Garnisongemeinde marriage book, the only military register covering the window — shows no Gastmann marriage, and Hanau’s reformed town church holds no Gastmann baptism in 1837–1842. With Karl baptised at Fulda (1. Compagnie, 1838) and no Gastmann marriage at the Kassel garrison, the family was attached to whichever company-station the regiment occupied rather than to the Kassel garrison congregation. The remaining EV garrison-era records for Marburg / Hersfeld are post-1866; for Hanau / Ziegenhain they sit in the civilian town parishes, whose shuffled, search-less indexes make a complete leaf-by-leaf read disproportionate for a lead already excluded as Charles. The most efficient way to localise the family further would be the Kurhessen military personnel records (Hof- u. Staats-Handbuch / Ranglisten / Stammrollen), which would name the company’s station year-by-year. This bears on the Gastmann near-miss only, not on Charles.

Baptism-index sweep — the Konze surname pool (27 June 2026)

The Session-127 work was keyed to James’s single flagged Gastmann entry. This pass reads the Fulda EV baptism index itself — the complete alphabetical Namensregister of reg. 168326 — for the whole Charles surname pool, to test whether any Konze / Gansen-type child was baptised at the Fulda town church across 1830–1863, not merely whether the Gastmann boy is Charles. Result: negative. No pool surname appears anywhere in the index, so none in the 1833–41 birth window.

Method: the index leaves (Bild 1071–1088) were read via the reel’s AI “Erkannter Text” panel. The body reel is heavily duplicated/disordered (Bild does not map linearly to Seite — e.g. Bild 851 = Seite 63 / Dec 1837, 852 = Seite 64 / 1838, 888 = Seite 66 / Apr 1838, 925 = Seite 137 / 1849, 1060 = Seite 272 / 1863), so the printed “Seite / Jahr” header governs, not arithmetic. The index runs main A–Z (Bild 1071–1082) then a “Fortsetzung” overflow (1083–1088) then end-of-reel test cards (1089–1091); every pool letter was read in both the main run and its continuation.

Two near-tokens were chased to the original images and cleared: (a) the OCR stray “Komse 63” (dropped into the H block) → Seite 63 (Bild 851) is Oct–Dec 1837 (Frieß/Schmidt, Sergeant Giebel, Henninghausen, Lange) with no such surname — a pure OCR artifact; (b) “Kunze, Wilhelmine Ernestine Friederike Josephina, Seite 100” — the single closest K-name to the pool, but female and Seite 100 falls ≈1843–44 (bracketed by Seite 66 = 1838 and Seite 137 = 1849), so outside the 1833–41 window and the wrong sex for Charles. “Kunze” is not the Konze/Kontze pool spelling. Net: the Fulda EV baptisms hold no Konze-pool child at all — the elimination now rests on the whole register, not just the one flagged entry.

Remaining follow-ups

  • Forward-trace of the Gastmann garrison family: the EV garrison-parish registers were swept (27 June 2026, above) — the Kassel Garnisongemeinde marriage book (the only pre-1866 dedicated garrison register) shows no Gastmann marriage, and Hanau (Marienkirche) holds no Gastmann baptism 1837–1842. Marburg / Hersfeld military registers are post-1866; Ziegenhain / Hanau have none separate. What remains is a direct entry-read of the Kassel garrison Taufen (1843–51 / 1852–65, indexes fragmentary) and the civilian town parishes of whichever company-station the regiment occupied. Best localised first via Kurhessen military rolls (Hof- u. Staats-Handbuch / Ranglisten / Stammrollen). Low priority — does not bear on Charles. Lane: Archion garrison-parish registers / Kurhessen military records
  • Optional: re-read Bild 852 with a Kurrent specialist to lock the exact birth day/month and the mother’s maiden name. Not needed to eliminate the candidate. Lane: Archion re-read

Net: the Dassen→Fulda EV toponym lead is closed as a Charles match. The Gastmann baptism is a clean, well-sourced near-miss; the parish is now documented and cited.
