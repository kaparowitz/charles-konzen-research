Gottsbüren Konze documented

Claim now REFUTED: the 1833 marriage called Christian Ludwig “gebürtig von Gottsbüren,” but his actual baptism — 20 Nov 1806, in STAMMEN (220063 img 1988) — was found on 7 Jun 2026. The family (swineherd Andreas Kontze × Maria Elisabeth Gerlandt) lived in Stammen throughout 1783–1828; no Kontze event ever occurred in Gottsbüren’s registers. Even the bride’s father (“weyland Christoph Gerlandts,” 1783) carries no Gottsbüren tag, and her siblings lived around Stammen. The Gottsbüren tie is reduced to a single erroneous (or loosely-meant) phrase in the 1833 entry. One Jungkonze-born woman did die here in 1855.

Charles Research — a multi-month transatlantic genealogical investigation into Charles Konzen (recorded in U.S. records as Gannsen / Johnson; 1835–1903), Hessian-born Civil War sergeant of Battery B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery, and his probable Trendelburg / Deisel origins in Churhessen (a working conclusion — the German baptism remains unrecovered).
