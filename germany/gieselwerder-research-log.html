Oberweser / Gieselwerder — Parish Research Log

51.581 N · 9.522 E · reg. 219016 (Taufen) · reg. 219013 (Konfirmationen) · ~7.3 km east of Deisel · compiled 21 June 2026 · confirmation sweep added 24 June 2026 · per-year confirmation + baptism image permalinks minted 28 June 2026
REOPENED — KONZE-POOL LEAD

⭐ Update (Session 134, 27 June 2026) — Gieselwerder is NOT clean of the Konze pool. The full Taufen name-index (reg. 219016 is “mit Register”) was read for the first time and lists a real Kuntze / Kontze family plus a large Kunze clan baptising children here across Charles’s era. There is still no male Carl/Karl on a Konze-pool surname born 1833–1841 (so no proven Charles match), but the parish is upgraded from “eliminated” to an active Konze-pool locus with an open lead: Heinrich Ernst Kuntze ×(1) Sophie Dorothea geb. Warnecke → a stillborn son März 1834 (Seite 17), then ×(2?) Sophie Elisabeth geb. Reitz → Johann Georg Wilhelm Kuntze, b./bapt. Feb 1837 (Seite 36). The apparent remarriage leaves a child-gap around early 1835 — and Charles (b. 26 Jan 1835, no German baptism ever found) would fit that gap if a son were born ~1835 and not baptised here. See the new baptism-index row below and the Session 134 log.

⭐ Update (Session 137, 28 June 2026) — household worked further; lead still open. Two follow-ups from Session 134 were actioned. (1) Klaus Kunze’s online Gieselwerder OSB name-register (os_gi/register.htm) was pulled: it confirms a genuinely abundant Konze pool here — Kuntze (17 family refs), Kuntzen (6), Kontzen (6), Contze (11), Contzen (8), Conze (1), plus a very large Kunze clan — corroborating the “active Konze-pool locus” upgrade. (The online register is a surname→family-number index only; the per-family reconstructions are in Kunze’s printed OSB.) (2) The Archion books needed to date the Heinrich Ernst Kuntze remarriage were located: Beerdigungen 1830–1898 = reg. 219022 and Trauungen 1830–1903. The burial reel was opened and sampled (Bild 424 and 429 both read “Jahr 1832”), confirming it is shuffled/duplicated (Erst-/Zweitschrift) and carries no alphabetical index (the reel ends on entry leaves, not a register). So the decisive reads — Sophie Dorothea Warnecke’s death (~1834–35) and the Heinrich Ernst Kuntze × Reitz marriage (~1835–36) — require a leaf-by-leaf Kurrent read of a shuffled reel and are flagged for Peter Schräder / the September archive trip (or an Archion OCR request). Until those dates are read, it stays unknown whether the 1835 child-gap could hold a son born ~26 Jan 1835.

⭐ Update (28 June 2026, cont.) — direct image reads; lead now hinges on one fact. The 1834 stillborn was re-read at the image (Taufen Seite 17, Nro 86, Bild 1263): Heinrich Ernst Kuntze × Sophie Dorothea geb. Warnecke, b. 30 März 1834, “todtgeboren” (male) — and the godparent/Bemerkungen columns are blank, with no note of the mother’s death. So Warnecke was alive at the März 1834 stillbirth; her death therefore falls in the 1834–36 window before the Feb-1837 Reitz son. Both the burial reel (219022) and the marriage reel (219028) were then opened and worked: both are shuffled with unreliable year-numerals; the marriage book is “mit Register” with an alphabetical groom index at Bild ~178–192 (U/V at Bild 188, W at Bild 189 — the very common local “Warneke” dominates), and on the K leaf (Bild 178) the groom column under “K” is BLANK — no Kuntze marriage is recorded in Gieselwerder at all, meaning Heinrich Ernst Kuntze married into the parish from elsewhere (marriages were kept in the bride’s parish). Bottom line: the lead still turns on whether Warnecke died in/around childbirth ~Jan 1835 (which would support a son b. 26 Jan 1835 → Charles) or died otherwise (an ordinary widower interval). But that date can no longer be got from Gieselwerder’s marriage book; it needs either (a) the Warnecke burial in reg. 219022 — whose alphabetical index is not in the online reel, so a leaf-by-leaf Kurrent read of a shuffled reel — or (b) identifying the bride’s home parish and reading the Kuntze marriages there. Handed to Schräder / the archive trip. (Bild numbers are the authoritative locators; the viewer’s permalink button did not mint this pass.)

Oberweser’s Evangelical records are held under Gieselwerder. Jan–Feb 1835 baptisms: all males legitimate, no Konze-pool surname, no illegitimate male.

Confirmation sweep (24 June 2026): the Konfirmationen book (reg. 219013) was read leaf-by-leaf for the entire 1847–1855 classes (births ~1832–1841), covering the prime 1849 and 1850 cohorts of children born around Charles’s 26 Jan 1835. Every confirmand was checked for the forename Carl/Karl and for any Konze / *n-s-n / *n-z-n surname. Result: negative. The whole window holds exactly one Carl — Carl Friedrich Wilhelm Marunde, b. 21 March 1839 (1853 class), a local Marunde family, wrong surname and wrong birth year — and no Konze, Kontze, Gansen, Gonsen or any *n-s-n surname appears anywhere. The surname pool is entirely the recurring local Oberweser families (Marunde, Roll, Reuß, Nickel, Hennur, Vella, Schölla, Grafmann, Grovenmann, Lindner, Otto, Daus, Reitz, Müller, Masserschmidt). Unlike the other Hofgeismar confirmation reels, reg. 219013 is cleanly ordered (≈ Seite = 2 × image − 3914).

The ~early-1835 child-gap between the 1834 stillborn (Bild 1263) and the 1837 Reitz son (Bild 1282) is the heart of the open lead: whether it could hold a son born ~26 Jan 1835 turns on dating Warnecke’s death — not recoverable from Gieselwerder’s shuffled, unindexed burial reel (219022) or its marriage book (219028, no Kuntze groom indexed), so handed to Schräder / the archive trip.

Remaining follow-ups

Now active (Session 134):

  1. Done 28 Jun 2026 (Session 138) — the Heinrich Ernst Kuntze household was reconstructed in reg. 219016 and the Warnecke→Reitz remarriage confirmed real: same father, same Haus Nro 96, two wives — Sophie Dorothea geb. Warnecke (stillborn son, 30 März 1834, Nro 86, Bild 1263) then Sophie Elisabeth geb. Reitz (Johann Georg Wilhelm Kuntze, bapt. Feb 1837, Nro 175, Bild 1282). Those are the only two Kuntze baptisms; the 1835–36 gap holds no Kuntze baptism, so no son born ~26 Jan 1835 was baptised here (consistent with, not proof of, the Charles reading). The same 1837 leaf (Nro 174, Bild 1282) carries a Gans × Warnecke baptism — a Kuntze–Gans cross-link by intermarriage via the Warnecke family (per Klaus Kunze, Konze and Gans are two separate families, not one surname). Cite: Archion reg. 219016 (Gieselwerder Taufen 1830–1881), Bild 1263 & 1282 (image-verified, Session 138).
  2. Reconstruct the broader Kunze/Kontze clan (the Seite 67–252 block) — fathers, any Carl line, emigration gaps. Partly done: the OSB surname index and the LAGIS emigrant list are captured (items 4–5 below; only Carl on a pool surname = Carl Kunze 1854, out of window), but the per-family reconstruction needs the printed Gieselwerder OSB (Klaus Kunze) — still outstanding.
  3. ★ Decisive next read — Gieselwerder burials (reg. 219022) for Sophie Dorothea Warnecke’s death ~1834–35 and marriages (Trauungen 1830–1903) for the Heinrich Ernst Kuntze × Reitz remarriage ~1835–36. These bound whether the 1835 child-gap could hold a son b. ~26 Jan 1835. Both books located 28 Jun 2026; the burial reel is shuffled/duplicated and unindexed, so this is a Kurrent read for Schräder / the archive trip (or an Archion OCR request).
  4. Done 28 Jun 2026 — Klaus Kunze’s online Gieselwerder OSB name-register pulled: confirms Kuntze (17) / Kuntzen (6) / Kontzen (6) / Contze (11) / Contzen (8) / Conze (1) + a large Kunze clan. (Index only; per-family detail needs the printed OSB.)
  5. Done 28 Jun 2026 (Session 138) — LAGIS Hessische Auswanderer searched (Herkunftsort Gieselwerder, 108 persons). The 1846 Gans family emigrated to North America: Henrich Ferdinand Gans (b. 1817, Leinenweber) + wife + daughter, Georg Wilhelm, Johann Georg, Christine Charlotte, Christine (Feb 1846; source HStAM Best. 180 Hofgeismar Nr. 871; cite). A Warnecke (Georg Wilhelm Henrich) emigrated 1840, and Henrich Wilhelm Gans in 1840. No Kuntze/Kontze/Konze/Contze emigrant is recorded from Gieselwerder — so if the Kuntze family (or a ~1835 son) left, it was not via a documented Auswanderungskonsens (LAGIS is not exhaustive).
