## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Following the April 9 discovery that the Friedrichsfeld Kirchenbuch entries #12 and #29 document a Maria Magdalena née Kontze — whose brother **Johann Friedrich Kontze, Schuhmachermeister, lived in "Gelmanshausen/Helmarshausen, Amt Carlshafen"** — a full search of the Helmarshausen Taufen 1830-1856 register was conducted to determine whether Charles Gannsen could have been born into this previously unknown Kontze branch.

**Result: Helmarshausen contains NO Konze/Kontze baptism entries for the entire 1830-1856 period.**

The built-in alphabetical name index (Register) of the Kirchenbuch was read in full for the K section. No Konze, Kontze, Kunze, or similar variant surnames appear. A separate C section does not exist — the alphabet jumps directly from B to D, consistent with the German tradition of filing C under K. Spot-checks of the January–May 1835 baptism pages independently confirmed the absence of any Konze entry in Charles Gannsen's claimed birth window.

**Conclusion: Helmarshausen is eliminated as a birthplace for Charles Gannsen via the Kontze line.** The "Kontze branch in Helmarshausen" identified through the Friedrichsfeld godparent entry is real, but none of its members were baptizing children in Helmarshausen itself during this period — they were either older residents recorded before the register begins (1830), or the family had only one generation in Helmarshausen before moving elsewhere.

## 2. BACKGROUND — WHY HELMARSHAUSEN

### The "Gelmanshausen" Transcription Error

The April 9 Friedrichsfeld complete search produced this key finding from entries #12 and #29 (pages 5 and 10):

> **"Johann Friedrich Kontze, Schuhmachermeister zu [Gelmanshausen / Helmarshausen], Amt [Liebenau / Carlshafen], der Mutter Bruder"**

On re-examination of the Kurrent script, the correct reading is **Helmarshausen, Amt Carlshafen** — not "Gelmanshausen, Amt Liebenau" as initially transcribed. Helmarshausen is:

- Located ~8 km northeast of Trendelburg on the Diemel river
- Historically in the Amt Carlshafen (Hofgeismar district) of Kurhessen
- A well-documented parish with its own Kirchenbuch on Archion

This correction made the Helmarshausen Taufen register an immediate priority, because it opened the possibility that the entire Kontze family branch — including potentially Charles Gannsen — had its origin in Helmarshausen rather than Trendelburg.

### Complete K-Section Surnames Observed

The full male-child K section (and verified cross-checked on the female side) lists only the following surnames, in rough order of appearance:

Kraft, Kaph, Kayster, Kassmann, Kussmann, Kolbe, Kothe, Kuffmann, Klapp, Knipsfeld, Kella, Kalbe, Kaufmann, Kiethe, Kaiser.

None of these are Konze, Kontze, Kunze, Cuntze, Cuntz, or any close variant. The K section ended before reaching Ko- surnames alphabetically in the expected "Kon-" position — specifically, there is **nothing** between Kolbe/Kothe and Kraft that could be a Konze entry.

### No Separate C Section

German church registers of the 19th century commonly file C-surnames (Cuntze, Contze, Cuntz) under K. This register follows that tradition: the alphabet runs A → B → D with no intervening C page. Any Cuntze/Contze variant would therefore have appeared in the K section, which has been fully read.

## 6. INTERPRETING THE FRIEDRICHSFELD GODPARENT ENTRY

The Friedrichsfeld entries that originally pointed to Helmarshausen remain valid. Johann Friedrich Kontze was a real Schuhmachermeister in Helmarshausen in 1832–1834 — he is a documented, named individual. The question is why he and his sister Maria Magdalena leave no baptismal trace in the Helmarshausen register itself.

### Explanation: Generational Timing

The most likely explanation is simply that **both siblings were already adults by 1830**, when the Helmarshausen Taufen register begins:

- Johann Friedrich was old enough to be a **Schuhmachermeister** (master shoemaker) in 1832–1834 — implying a birth date no later than ~1805–1810.
- Maria Magdalena was already married to George Wilhelm Schleiffer/Pfründer by 1832 and bearing children at Friedrichsfeld.
- Their own baptisms would have been recorded in an **earlier Helmarshausen register (pre-1830)** that is either not yet on Archion or has been lost.
- Any children Johann Friedrich himself fathered in Helmarshausen would appear in the 1830–1856 register — but the absence of such entries suggests he either had no surviving children in this period, moved out of Helmarshausen before starting a family, or his wife's births fell into a gap.

### Alternative Explanation: Mobile Tradesman

Master shoemakers were often itinerant, apprenticing and later practicing in multiple towns. It is possible Johann Friedrich was only briefly resident in Helmarshausen in the early 1830s (long enough to be recorded as godfather at Friedrichsfeld) and moved on before any of his own children were baptized there.

Either way, the absence of **any** Kontze baptism in Helmarshausen 1830–1856 rules out the possibility that Charles Gannsen was born into a Helmarshausen-resident Kontze household during this period.

## 7. IMPLICATIONS FOR THE CHARLES GANNSEN INVESTIGATION

### Hypothesis Status Update

**Hypothesis 1 — Justus Ernst Konze (b. Nov 14, 1829, Deisel) = Charles Gannsen: STRENGTHENED AGAIN.**
With both Friedrichsfeld (April 9) and Helmarshausen (April 10) now excluded as possible January 1835 birthplaces, the hypothesis that Charles is an earlier-born Konze with a shifted birth year becomes increasingly the leading theory. Justus Ernst Konze remains the best candidate: documented, born in Deisel (which is within the Trendelburg ecclesiastical orbit), and part of a Konze family.

**Hypothesis 2 — Illegitimate child of an unmarried Konze woman: SOMEWHAT WEAKENED.**
An illegitimate birth to a Konze woman would have been registered under the mother's surname. The Helmarshausen K section contains no such entries. An illegitimate birth remains possible but would have to be in yet another parish.

**Hypothesis 3 — Charles was a later legitimate child born after Andreas's family left Friedrichsfeld: STILL OPEN.**
If Andreas Konze and Maria Sophia left Friedrichsfeld after October 1834 (the death of baby August), Charles could theoretically have been born in a third location between late 1835 and ~1840. But the claimed January 26, 1835 date is still biologically impossible given August's October 15, 1834 birth — so if this hypothesis holds, the birth date is significantly wrong.

**Hypothesis 4 — Charles was from the Helmarshausen/"Gelmanshausen" Kontze branch: ELIMINATED.**
This hypothesis, which appeared promising as of April 9, is now closed. No Konze baptisms exist in the Helmarshausen Taufen register 1830–1856.

### Revised Priority List

1. **Search the Deisel Kirchenbuch** for Justus Ernst Konze's full birth entry, family context, and siblings — Hypothesis 1 is now the clear leading theory.
2. **Search Trendelburg civil/church records** 1833–1840 for any Konze entries the family might have registered after leaving Friedrichsfeld.
3. **Search Mainz civil records** 1835–1854 for any Konze births (period when Andreas was documented as living/dying there).
4. **Look for pre-1830 Helmarshausen registers** (if they exist on Archion or at the Landeskirchliches Archiv Kassel) to identify Johann Friedrich and Maria Magdalena Kontze's own parents — this would establish whether the Helmarshausen Kontzes and Andreas of Trendelburg share a common ancestor one generation back.
5. **Re-examine the Carlshafen area** — Helmarshausen was in Amt Carlshafen, and neighboring parishes in the same Amt (e.g., Carlshafen itself, Gottsbüren, Gieselwerder) should be surveyed for Kontze entries.
