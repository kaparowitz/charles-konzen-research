Bibliography

A formal index of every primary source, archive, database, and secondary work cited in the project. Citations follow NGSQ short form; see Methodology · Citation style for the long-form template.

The investigation rests on three classes of primary sources (military, parish, and U.S. vital), one class of derivative aggregator (Ancestry, FamilySearch, Find a Grave), and a small set of secondary literature on the political and demographic context of mid-nineteenth-century Hesse-Kassel. Each source on this page is hyperlinked to its imaged or indexed presentation on this site, where one exists.

1 · U.S. military and pension records

Compiled Service Record of Charles Gannsen. Pvt./Sgt., Battery B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery (Union) · Record group 94, M405, file G11-422819443E · NARA, Washington, D.C. Mail-order packet received May 2026; 25 pages of carded muster rolls and special-orders entries. Imaged on this site at Sources › NARA. Where Fold3 readings disagree with the NARA originals, the NARA reading is canonical.
Civil War Pension File of Charles Gannsen. F41-523680269E · NARA, Washington, D.C. Full pension file: declaration, affidavits of seven affiants — August Derr, Christian Meyer, Henry Stoll, Michael Nolan, Henry Niemeier, Theodore Gottlob, and "John Heitzenberger" (as written by the affidavit clerk; surname-family identified as Hitzelberger — individual identity remains open); medical examinations; address-history sequence; rejection and re-filing correspondence. (Adam Herweck appears elsewhere as the 1866 marriage witness, not as a pension affiant.) Imaged at Sources › Pension File.
Fold3 — Missouri Volunteer Union Service Records. Database with images · fold3.com Used as the working transcription layer for U.S. Civil War service records. Page-level citations on individual evidence cards point to specific Fold3 page IDs (e.g. PG 21, PG 19, PG 20). See Sources › Fold3.
National Park Service — Civil War Soldiers and Sailors Database. U.S. Department of the Interior · nps.gov Used for negative searches on wedding-witness names ("Philipp Weber," "John Heitzenberger") to confirm civilian vs. veteran status. ("Heitzenberger" reframed as a misreading of Hitzelberger; see errata · 3 May 2026)

2 · German parish registers

Trendelburg ev. Pfarramt — Bestattungsregister 220096. Trendelburg burials, 1773–1818 · Archion register 220096 · img 1029 (1795 Andreas / Johann Friedrich Kontzen cluster; Archion permalink — login required) K-section transcribed Session 11 (April 2026); the parish-internal cross-reference note "siehe auch K. Contze, Cortze, Cordes, Contz" establishes the Kontze / Contze / Konze interchangeability that grounds the surname-pool argument.
Trendelburg ev. Pfarramt — Taufen 1830–1863, Register 220108. Archion register 220108 Alphabetical index lists "Konze, Johann Friedrich Wilhelm — Seite 243" — first Konze father-surname baptism confirmed in post-1831 Trendelburg. The cited Seite 243 is image 1623 (Archion permalink — login required). Session 18 discovery; Session 20+ executing the Seite 243 transcription.
Friedrichsfeld ev. Pfarramt — Kirchenbuch 218959. Archion register 218959 Andreas Kontze line. The Friedrichsfeld baptism of 14 November 1829 documents Justus Ernst Konze, son of Andreas. Multiple sessions transcribed in April 2026; HTR by Peter Schräder, Session 22.
Deisel ev. Pfarramt — Kirchenbuch 218842 & 218824. Archion registers 218842 · 218824 The Deisel baptism register (218842) holds the January 1835 entries 194 and 195 — Entry 195 re-read 4 May 2026 as a Köster day-labourer's child (see Entry 195 source page; see errata · 25 May 2026 for Entry 195 retraction propagation). Three "geb. Konzen" mothers attested 1828–1829 (Maria Elisabeth, Wilhelmina, Carlotta). No candidate Konze male b. 1835 in this parish. Searched April 2026.
Helmarshausen, Hofgeismar, Sielen, Hümme, Schöneberg parish registers. Archion · individual register IDs cited on each town page Full-index searches concluded with no Konze-pool male b. ~1835 in any. See the Hessian parish map and towns index.
Heisebeck ev. Pfarramt — Kirchenbuch (pre-1845). Landeskirchliches Archiv Kassel · undigitised The single substantive open candidate parish. On-site research at the Landeskirchliches Archiv Kassel pending. Logged as a priority gap in Open Questions.
St. Marcus German Evangelical Church (St. Louis, Mo.) — marriage, baptism, and burial registers. Microfilmed records · also indexed on FamilySearch and Ancestry The pivotal U.S.-side parish record set: 1866 marriage register (Pastor Wall, "Gonnson"); seven Charles & Auguste baptisms 1866–1880 across Pastors Wall, Braschler, Hoffmann, Nollau; 1896 wife's death record (Pastor Eilt Heeren Eilts, "Conson"); 1903 Charles burial (#3393).

3 · Hessian emigration and military registers

Hessisches Staatsarchiv Marburg — Auswanderer-Belege. Bestand 17 et al. · accessed via Arcinsys · arcinsys.hessen.de Hessian emigration permits. Used to corroborate Johannes Grandjot's 1855 Schöneberg-to-Bremen emigration (Beleg ID 19743) and as a negative-search target for Charles in 1858–1859. No Konze 1859 Beleg located — this absence is itself convergent indirect evidence for the working "illegal-emigration to evade Kurfürst conscription" hypothesis (a young man who left without registering would not have generated a Beleg); see the Stammrolle research log.
Hessisches Staatsarchiv Marburg — Stammrollen (military registers). Bestand 4h · partly digitised Conscription class 1855 (males b. 1835) for the Trendelburg / Hofgeismar district. Searched without locating Charles; some volumes remain undigitised. Logged in Open Questions.
Landesgeschichtliches Informationssystem Hessen (LAGIS). lagis-hessen.de Regional records portal for historical Hesse. Used for parish boundaries, emigration data, and contextual reference.

4 · U.S. vital, immigration, and census records

U.S. Federal Census · 1860, 1870, 1880, 1900. Indexed on FamilySearch and Ancestry 1860 and 1870 enumerations of Charles & Auguste's household have not been located despite phonetic-variant searches; logged in Open Questions. 1880 enumeration captures the surname as "Jansen." 1900 records "1859" as immigration year.
New York Arriving Passenger and Crew Lists, 1820–1957. Ancestry collection Searched 1858–1860 for any Konze-pool surname-variant arrival; no Charles match. Used to corroborate Johannes Grandjot's 25 September 1855 NYC arrival.
Massachusetts Vital and Town Records, 1620–1988. Ancestry collections 2495, 2511 Source of the 6 October 1855 Plymouth, MA marriage record of Carl Louis Herwig (Lewes Herweg / Lewis Harwig) to Catherine Sharda — pivot record for the Grandjot-Herwig U.S. trail.
Find a Grave. findagrave.com Used to confirm Jacob Reck's burial at Old Saint Marcus (Memorial #249141012, with full Westliche Post obituary, 30 May 1898) and adjacent identifications. Charles's own marker is not extant — Old Saint Marcus markers were removed; the cemetery is now St. Marcus Commemorative Park.

5 · Genealogical aggregators

FamilySearch.org — Family Tree and indexed record collections. The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints · familysearch.org Editable family tree spine for the project. Person IDs (e.g. LZXC-6GB for Charles, LZKV-9F5 for Auguste, PZF8-X19 for the Stammtafel-Andreas Kontze) are linked inline throughout the site. Many May 2026 confirmations (Erwin Spraul KLP1-N62, Jacob Reck KLPB-52X, etc.) tie back to FS-wired records.
Ancestry.com — derivative U.S. records and indexes. ancestry.com Used for U.S. censuses, vital records, passenger lists, and the Germans to America Index. Cited inline at the collection-and-record level (e.g., "collection 2495 record 14571973").
Archion — German Protestant church-record portal. Verband kirchlicher Archive · archion.de Primary access point for digitised Hessian Kirchenbücher. Subscription-based. Register-level citations include 220096, 220108, 218842, 218824 et al.

6 · Secondary literature and reference works

AncestryProGenealogists. Research Journal — Charles Gannsen. Paul A. Schmidt & Brian A. Podoll · February – June 2020 · 11-page research journal · imaged on this site at Foundation (2020) The 2020 commissioned research that opened the investigation. Decoded "Deisse" → Deisel; ruled out Ludwig Konze 1836 and Karl Konze 1839; established the Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis as the working area.
Mills, Elizabeth Shown. Evidence Explained: Citing History Sources from Artifacts to Cyberspace. 3rd ed., revised. Baltimore: Genealogical Publishing Company, 2017. Source-rating taxonomy and citation conventions framework reference.
Board for Certification of Genealogists. Genealogy Standards. 2nd ed. Nashville: Ancestry, 2019. The Genealogical Proof Standard reference for the project's confidence taxonomy. Methodology details on this site at /methodology.html.
Stadt Trendelburg — historical-statistical works on Deisel. Cited in Peter Schräder's correspondence, 20 November 2025 Mid-19th-century demographic baseline: total population 1,118 · Evangelical/Lutheran 1,095 (97.94 %) · Catholic 5 (0.45 %) · Jewish 17 (1.52 %). Establishes the Deisel religious composition that constrains the parish-search.
The New International Encyclopedia. First edition, 1905; entry on Hesse-Cassel Cited for the operative deposition date of Elector Friedrich Wilhelm I (transferred as a prisoner to Stettin on 23 June 1866) — the political event that anchors the Reading B interpretation of the firstborn's name.

7 · Working artifacts and metadata exports

Wikidata QuickStatements batch import — wikidata/quickstatements.txt. wikidata/quickstatements.txt · README A QuickStatements-formatted batch of Wikidata claims for Charles Konzen (b. 26 Jan 1835 Trendelburg-area; d. 16 Dec 1903 St. Louis), his Civil War service, his marriage to Auguste Kreichelt, and the seven children. Intended for one-time import via QuickStatements toolforge once the canonical Wikidata Q-numbers are minted. Held as v1.1.0 deposit metadata.
Citation metadata files — .zenodo.json and CITATION.cff. Repository root · refreshed 29 May 2026 (v1.1.0) Zenodo deposit metadata (CC BY-NC-SA 4.0) and Citation File Format metadata for the project as a whole. Bumped from v1.0.0 (8 May 2026) to v1.1.0 (29 May 2026) to reflect the Claims Dashboard, Andreas Konze identity disentanglement, Doc A–O crosswalk, and the pre-deploy maintenance harness. The Zenodo DOI is held in placeholder form pending mint.
Working research drafts (March–April 2026) — superseded. In-repo working documents · not surfaced on the live site Two large in-repo working documents track the candidate-family elimination chain in real time: Kontze_Family_Tree_and_Research_Paths.md (137 KB, last updated 26 April 2026) and the associated session notes. Note: these working drafts predate the 4 May 2026 retraction of the Entry 195 reading (which had been treated as a "Justus" Konze baptism candidate, now read as a Köster day-labourer's child who died unbaptised) and the 17 May 2026 finalisation of the surname canon at eight forms (expanded to nine on 21 June 2026 with the 1895 Gould’s-directory Gannsen). Several claims in the working documents are now superseded by current canon — see Claims Dashboard for the live state and errata · 25 May 2026 rigor pass for the propagation. The drafts are retained in the repository for reproducibility of the elimination process but are not deployed.
Maintenance harness (deploy-time gates) — scripts/check_canon.sh, scripts/check_links.py, scripts/build_search_index.py. Wired into deploy.sh sanity checks · v1.1.0 Three pre-deploy gates protect the live site against the propagation-cascade bug class: check_canon.sh greps the entire site for stale canonical phrases (surname counts, phonetic-decoded place-name variants, session counts, unhedged witness-surname framings) and aborts the deploy on any fail-class hit; check_links.py audits 297 pages for broken file paths and missing anchors; build_search_index.py regenerates search-index.json on every deploy so new pages become searchable without manual editing. Source under scripts/ in the repository.
