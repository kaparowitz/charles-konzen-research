### ⭐ Same family number under BOTH a Konze-form and a Gans-form
- **70** = Kuntze 70 **and** Ganß 70
- **154** = Kuntze 154 / Kontzen 154 **and** Ganß 154
- **167** = Kuntze 167 **and** Ganß 167
- **65** = Contze 65 **and** Ganß 65

This is the Konze↔Gans equivalence at the heart of the Gannsen hypothesis appearing in
**Gieselwerder specifically**, in a published genealogist's index. ⚠️ Caveat (per Session 127):
a shared family number can mean a **spelling variant** *or* an **intermarriage** (a Kuntze man
∞ a Gans woman). The index can't distinguish; only the dated family entries can — and those are
**not online** (printed OSB / Kunze email).

## 2. LAGIS Hessische Auswanderer — Herkunftsort = Gieselwerder (108 persons)

Searched the live LAGIS emigrant database (Ortskennziffer Gieselwerder **633030030**).

### The 1846 Gans family → Nordamerika (the cluster from the Session-134 follow-up)
A whole Gans household leaving **Feb 1846 for North America**:

- Detail record (Henrich Ferdinand, ID 108717): Auswanderungsziel **Nordamerika**, 02/1846,
  Vermögen 80; source **HStAM Best. 180 Hofgeismar Nr. 871, S. 19** (Entlassung aus dem
  Staatsverband). Cite: <https://lagis.hessen.de/resolve/de/ha/108717>
- Separately: **Gans, Henrich Wilhelm**, 1840.

### ⭐ A Warnecke also emigrated — 1840
**Warnecke, Georg Wilhelm Henrich, Gieselwerder, 1840** — the **maiden surname of Heinrich
Ernst Kuntze's first wife** (and, see below, of a Gans wife too). The Warnecke family is the
hinge linking Kuntze and Gans in this parish.

### Decisive negative
The full 108-person alphabetical list runs Henne → Scholle with **no I/J/K/L/M/N/O/P/Q/R
surname at all** → **NO Kuntze / Kontze / Konze / Contze emigrant recorded from Gieselwerder.**
So if a Kuntze (or a ~1835 Kuntze son) left, it was **not via a documented Auswanderungskonsens.**
(Caveat: LAGIS is not exhaustive; undocumented/"illegal" emigration was common.)

## 3. Archion reg. 219016 (Taufen 1830–1881) — Heinrich Ernst Kuntze household, image-verified

Logged-in viewer. Calibration confirmed live: **Seite = Bild − 1246.** Stale-canvas after every
page jump — forced redraw with a zoom +/− each time.

### Child 1 — stillborn, March 1834 (Bild 1263 = Seite 17, Nro 86) ✓ image-verified
"**86. Gieselwerder Nro 96 — März … Todtgeboren — Männlich**", parents "**Heinrich Ernst Kuntze**
und dessen Ehefrau **Sophie Dorothea geborn Warnecke**", godparent lines blank (as expected for
a stillbirth). Confirms Session 134.

### Child 2 — Johann Georg Wilhelm Kuntze, bapt. Feb 1837 (Bild 1282 = Seite 36, Nro 175) ✓ image-verified
"**175. Gieselwerder Nro 96 — Februar … — Johann Georg Wilhelm Kuntze**", parents "**Heinrich
Ernst Kuntze** und dessen Ehefrau **Sophie Elisabeth geb. Reitz**", godfather Johann Christoph
Seitze/Veitze (Arbeitsmann). Late confirmation/death annotation in the margin (~1912).

### ⭐ Conclusion: the remarriage is real
Same father, **same house (Nro 96)**, **different wife** — Warnecke (1834) → Reitz (1837). So
Heinrich Ernst Kuntze **remarried between March 1834 and Feb 1837**, leaving a **child-gap across
1835–1836**. The baptism index shows **no Kuntze baptism in that gap** (confirmed Session 134).
A son born 26 Jan 1835 would fall squarely in the gap *but was not baptised in Gieselwerder* —
consistent with, but not proof of, the Charles reading.

### ⭐ Cross-link found on the SAME 1837 leaf (Bild 1282, Nro 174) ✓ image-verified
"**174. … Marie Louise Elisabeth Gans** (weiblich, † )", parents "**Hans Henrich Gans**, Ackermann,
und dessen Ehefrau **Maria Dorothea geborn Warnecke**." → a **Gans man married to a Warnecke woman**,
in the same parish/era where **Kuntze married a Warnecke woman**. The **Warnecke family inter-marries
both Kuntze and Gans** here — a concrete mechanism behind the OSB's shared Kuntze/Ganß family numbers
(i.e. at least partly *intermarriage*, via Warnecke, not only spelling-variant).

⚠️ **Rigor caveat:** "Warnecke/Warneke" is a **very common Gieselwerder surname** (it dominates the
W leaf of the marriage groom-index). So Kuntze×Warnecke and Gans×Warnecke may simply reflect how
ubiquitous Warnecke is locally rather than a unique Kuntze–Gans bridge. The cross-link **raises, not
proves**, the Konze=Gans-by-marriage reading.

## 5. Net verdict

Gieselwerder is **further strengthened as an active Konze-pool locus**, but **still no direct
Charles match**:

- The **Kuntze ↔ Gans link is now concrete** — both surnames marry into the **Warnecke** family in
  this parish (Kuntze × Warnecke 1834; Gans × Warnecke 1837), matching the OSB's shared Kuntze/Ganß
  family numbers (65, 70, 154, 167).
- A **Gieselwerder Gans family emigrated to North America in 1846** (Henrich Ferdinand Gans et al.);
  a **Warnecke** emigrated 1840. **No Kuntze** emigrant is recorded.
- The **Heinrich Ernst Kuntze household** has a real **1835–36 child-gap** (Warnecke→Reitz remarriage)
  with **no Gieselwerder baptism** — the Charles-shaped hole remains open but unproven.

**Decisive next step (Kurrent / Peter Schräder / on-site):** date the gap via reg. 219028 (Kuntze×Reitz
marriage, ~1836) + the Beerdigungen book (Warnecke death, ~1834–35); and resolve the OSB family
numbers 65/70/154/167 (variant vs. Warnecke-intermarriage) from the printed Gieselwerder OSB.
