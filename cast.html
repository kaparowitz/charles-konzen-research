Cast

The cast of the nine stories — protagonists, kin, scribes, and offstage historical figures.

A small cast moves through the six stories. They sort into a handful of natural groups. Each entry below carries the person's role, dates where known, a one-line biography, the stories they appear in, and a link to a fuller profile if one exists.

The protagonists

The two people the entire investigation orbits.
CK
Charles Konzen
1835 – 1903
The subject
The subject of the investigation
Hessian-born immigrant. Probably emigrated alone in 1859. Battery B veteran. St. Louis labourer. Married Auguste Kreichelt January 1866; fathered seven children; died December 1903.
In stories 01 · 02 · 03 · 04 · 05 · 06 · 07 · 08 · 09
AK
Auguste Kreichelt
1847 – 1896
Wife
Charles's wife · mother of all seven children
Born Clausthal, Hannover; emigrated 1853 with the family; married Charles 7 January 1866 St. Marcus; mother of Friedrich Wilhelm, Maria Josephine, Katherine Carolina, Auguste, Louise, Erwin Carl, Bertha; died 1896 St. Louis.
In stories 01 · 02 · 03 · 04 · 05 · 06 · 07 · 08 · 09

Their children

The seven Konzen-Kreichelt children whose baptismal and death records anchor the surname-trail and family-pattern stories.
FW
Friedrich Wilhelm
b. 20 June 1866
Son · 1st child
Firstborn · the Elector-namesake
Born three days after the Elector of Hesse-Kassel was deposed. Christened 2 September 1866 with the same first names. The political world Charles had left ceased to exist that summer.
In 04
KC
Katherine Carolina
b. 1870
Daughter · 3rd child
Daughter · the Gansen baptism
22 May 1870 St. Marcus baptism — Pastor Braschler's hand recorded the surname as Gansen. Two years later the same hand wrote it differently. Pillmann reappears as sponsor at this baptism.
In 05 · 06
AG
Auguste (daughter)
1872 – 1890
Daughter · 4th child
Daughter · the Gonsen baptism · the 1890 Konzen death (single American attestation of the Hessian z-form)
23 June 1872 baptism (Pastor Braschler wrote Gonsen). Died 1890 at age 18 — her death record reads "Auguste Somers geb Konzen", the only American record carrying the full Hessian z-form documented in the Trendelburg parish since 1773.
In 06 · 07
EK
Erwin Carl Konsen
b. 1875
Son · 6th child
Sixth child · the only Konsen baptism
Baptised 3 October 1875 St. Marcus — the only Gannsen baptism preserving the Hesse-style surname spelling Konsen. Sole sponsor: Erwin Spraul, his namesake.
In 07 · 08

The comrade

The single Hessian sergeant whose four-year St. Louis overlap with Charles is the densest documentary record of a male bond in the project.
AH
Adam Herweck
1831 – 1897
Comrade · Battery B
Battery B Sergeant · the recruiter · senior groomsman
Born Lampertheim, Hessen. The 9 December 1862 captain's letter places him on St. Louis recruiting service the week Charles enlisted. Same Pvt → Sgt rank trajectory. First-named witness on the 1866 wedding register.
In 02

The Hessian and Hannoverian kin

Auguste's family. Every named godparent at every Gannsen baptism traces to her side — never Charles's.
GK
Georg Kreichelt
1819 – 1883
Father-in-law
Auguste's father · Battery B veteran
Born Clausthal, Hannover. Emigrated September 1853. Enlisted Missouri 1861 in the same Battery B as Charles. Walked Auguste down the aisle at the 1866 wedding. Sponsor at three Gannsen baptisms.
In 03 · 09
HK
Henriette Kreichelt
1820 – 1894
Mother-in-law
Auguste's mother
Born Clausthal as Louise Friederike Henriette Weiss. Mother of the bride at the 1866 St. Marcus wedding. Sponsor at two Gannsen baptisms (1866 firstborn + 1873 Louise — the granddaughter named directly for her).
In 03 · 09
AU
August Kreichelt
1844 – 1877
Brother-in-law
Auguste's eldest brother · Battery B comrade of Charles
Born Clausthal, Hannover. Battery B veteran alongside Charles 1862–1865 — almost certainly the doorway through which Charles met Auguste. Sponsor at the 1872 baptism of his namesake niece. Died young at age 32.
In 03 · 09
Ca
Carl Kreichelt
1846 – 1926
Brother-in-law
Auguste's younger brother
St. Louis saloonist, later real-estate investor. Had ten children baptised at St. Marcus 1871–1887 — the kin-default convention rigorously honoured throughout, in contrast with Charles's missing kin.
In 09
BK
Bertha Barbara Kreichelt
1859 – 1892
Sister-in-law
Auguste's youngest sister
First Kreichelt born in America. Sponsored two Gannsen baptisms (1873 Louise + 1880 Bertha — the namesake niece). Died age 32 in St. James, Phelps County, Missouri.
In 09

The St. Marcus parish circle

The Soulard German-Evangelical parish-circle sponsors at the seven Gannsen baptisms — Pillmann, Ebeling, Reck, and Münke recur across the family-pattern story.
FP
Frederick Pillmann
1846 – 1917
Baptism sponsor
Soulard butcher · two-time sponsor
The only person to sponsor more than one Gannsen baptism — 1866 firstborn + 1870 Katherine Carolina. Married Fredericka Albrecht 1870, becoming Kreichelt-by-marriage.
In 04 · 05 · 09
WE
Wilhelmina Ebeling
1810 – 1872
Baptism godmother
Soulard parish neighbour · 1866 godmother
Wilhelmine Ebeling Leue. Senior parish-circle neighbour at NW Ann + Elizabeth, walking distance from Charles & Auguste's South 7th Street residence. Carries the matched-pair feminine form of the Elector's name.
In 04
AM
Amalia Münke
1844 – 1905
Sister-in-law (marriage)
Sister-in-law · 1870 sponsor · namesake
Maria Emma Mincke. Wife of August Kreichelt (Auguste's brother and a Battery B veteran). The 1870 child's middle name Amalia is her direct namesake.
In 09
CA
Catharina Albrecht
1850 – 1913
Sister-in-law (marriage)
Sister-in-law via Carl Kreichelt
Married Carl Kreichelt 1871. Sister of Fredericka Albrecht (who married Frederick Pillmann). Sponsor at the 1870 Katherine Carolina baptism — the child's namesake middle name.
In 05 · 09
ES
Erwin Spraul
~1848 – 1889
Baptism godfather
Anheuser-Busch Superintendent · sole 1875 godfather · namesake
Born ~1848 Baden. By 1880 the Superintendent at Anheuser-Busch. The only non-Kreichelt-side sponsor at any Konzen baptism — Charles's pick of a successful fellow immigrant.
In 08

The collaborator and the discoverer

Two living people whose work made several of these stories possible.
PS
Peter Schräder
collaborator since late 2025
Researcher · living
German genealogist · Trendelburg
A native of Trendelburg with deep local archive expertise. Proposed the illegal-emigration-to-avoid-conscription hypothesis in December 2025 — the working explanation for why no kin from Charles's side appear at any baptism.
In 09
MJ
Maximilian J.
contacted September 2025
Descendant · living
Descendant who found the portrait
A Konzen descendant in Independence, Missouri. Found the only known portrait of Charles in a moving box he was unpacking; emailed Jed 18 September 2025. The project's first face after a hundred years.
In 01

The scribes

Anonymous figures whose hands record what they heard — and whose discrepancies are the evidence.
PB
Pastor Braschler
active 1870 – 1872
Parish scribe
St. Marcus pastor
Recorded the 1870 Katherine Carolina baptism (Gansen) and the 1872 Auguste baptism (Gonsen). The two-vowel discrepancy in his hand is the project's strongest single piece of vocal-versus-scribal capture evidence.
In 06
PH
Pastor Hoffmann
active 1875
Parish scribe
St. Marcus pastor (1875)
Recorded the 1875 Erwin Carl baptism — the only Gannsen baptism preserving the Konsen spelling. A different hand from Braschler's, capturing the same Hessian -en form fifteen years before the 1890 death record.
In 07
LR
Louis Roth
studio active 1890 – 1907
Portrait photographer
St. Louis portrait photographer
Operated 2006 South Broadway, 0.7 miles from Charles's DeKalb Street home. Took the only known portrait of Charles, datable by studio operating dates to between his wife's 1896 death and his own in 1903.
In 01

Offstage and historical

Figures who shape the stories without appearing in them directly.
F1
Friedrich Wilhelm I
1802 – 1875
Historical · the Elector
Deposed Elector of Hesse-Kassel
The last reigning Kurfürst of Hesse-Kassel — the German state of Charles's birth. Deposed by Prussian troops 23 June 1866 and transferred to Stettin. Charles's firstborn carries his first names.
