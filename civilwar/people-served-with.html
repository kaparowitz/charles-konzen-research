People Charles Served With — Battery B Comrades

2nd Regiment, Missouri Light Artillery, Battery B · 1862–1865
CIVIL WAR · COMRADESWedding witnesses · 1891 affiants

★ At a Glance

Charles's Civil War service didn't end when he was discharged in December 1865 — the men he served alongside in Battery B kept showing up in his life for the next thirty-eight years. Three Battery B veterans filed sworn affidavits supporting his 1890 pension claim. One was the senior witness at his wedding two weeks after discharge. His future father-in-law and brother-in-law were also Battery B men. The civilian-life social network that sustained Charles for the rest of his life was forged in a Missouri artillery battery during the war.

The wedding party — Battery B at St. Marcus, 7 Jan 1866

The single most consequential fact about Charles's Civil War service is that he served alongside the family he would later marry into. The 7 January 1866 St. Marcus marriage register entry names four wedding witnesses — and Battery B accounts for at least three people in the wedding party.

⚜ Senior witness · Battery B Sergeant

Adam Herweck

Born 16 July 1831 in Lampertheim, Hessen. Same rank trajectory as Charles (Pvt → Sgt). His NARA M405 Compiled Service Record places him as a Sergeant on detached recruiting service at St. Louis in November–December 1862 — the exact time and place of Charles Gannsen's enlistment. The Captain of Battery B wrote in a 9 December 1862 letter from New Madrid: "Adam Herweck, of my Company, [is on] recruiting service at St. Louis." Documentary confirmation that Adam recruited Charles. Named first in the witness column of the 7 Jan 1866 marriage register.
FamilySearch K4DW-PXM · m. Marguerite Wamsganz 21 Jul 1868 · d. 11 May 1897 Edwardsville IL
 ★ Father of bride · Battery B Veteran

Georg Kreichelt

Auguste Kreichelt's father. Walked his daughter down the aisle on 7 January 1866 — eighteen days after Charles's discharge. Hannoverian-immigrant carpenter (Clausthal, emigrated 1853 with family). Battery B veteran of the same unit Charles served in.
FamilySearch LZXC-NH9
 ★ Brother of bride · Battery B Veteran

August Kreichelt

Auguste Kreichelt's elder brother. At the 1866 wedding. Decades later (1875) sponsors at the baptism of Charles's son Erwin Carl Konsen — the surviving Hesse-spelling of the family name.
FamilySearch 2LXC-NDM (placeholder; cross-link from family tree)

Battery B comrades who gave 1890–91 pension affidavits

The pension file contains affidavits from three Battery B comrades plus a third-party St. Louis civilian who personally vouched for Charles's service and disabilities. The two senior Battery B affiants are independently confirmed by the War Department's records check on Pension Page 35.

Battery B · 1st Sergeant

August Derr

Charles's direct NCO at the 1863 New Madrid station — the most senior soldier-witness available, with documentary supervisory authority. Provides two separate affidavits: Page 24 attests the hernia from "heavy lifting"; Page 26 covers hearing loss (cannonading) and rheumatism (exposure).
1857 South 14th Street · age 56 in 1891 · Pension file pages 24 + 26
 Battery B · Private

Christian Meyer

Fellow soldier in Battery B, 2nd Mo. Light Artillery. Reconnected with Charles around 1888 and provided the May 1891 affidavit attesting Charles's "loss of hearing" originated from heavy cannonading at New Madrid in September 1863, and that his rheumatism originated from exposure during the same New Madrid station period in November 1863.
1803 Allen Avenue · age 48 in 1891 · Pension file page 22
 Cross-light-artillery comrade

Henry Stoll

24-year acquaintance of Charles since 1867. Affidavit 29 September 1890 testifies to ongoing right-side rupture pain, two-month rheumatism flare-ups, and partial deafness. Per the Pension Mutual-Aid Network theme, Stoll appears to have been a fellow Missouri Light Artillery veteran — the same drafting agent (Fred. W. Fout) handled his 1889 own-pension claim and Charles's 1890 claim.
1232 S. 3rd Street · age 50 in 1890 · Pension file page 16
 1st Mo Light Artillery (sister regiment)

Michael Nolan

★ Newly identified May 2026. Co-worker of Charles since 1867. October 1891 affidavit testifies Charles "lost considerable time" to rheumatism flare-ups, suffered continuously from rupture, and grew steadily harder of hearing. Per the Pension Mutual-Aid Network reconstruction, Nolan was a 1st MO Light Artillery veteran — sister regiment to Charles's 2nd MO L.A.
Age 49 in ~1891 · Pension file page 18

Other named members of Battery B (not part of Charles's affidavit network)

The unit's officer corps and other Battery B names that appear in muster rolls and the Wikipedia / NPS S&S record:

  • Captain commanding Battery B (1862-63) — author of the 9 December 1862 letter from New Madrid mentioning Adam Herweck's recruiting role at St. Louis. Cross-reference: NARA M405 Roll 0329, page 33.
  • Officer corps — Battery B was raised in the fall of 1861 in St. Louis and commanded through the Civil War by a succession of officers per the unit's Wikipedia history.
  • NPS Soldiers and Sailors System — full roster searchable at nps.gov/civilwar; filter on 2nd Regiment, Missouri Light Artillery, Company B.

The Pension Mutual-Aid Network

Reading the seven affidavits as independent depositions understates the case. They are the product of a coordinated mutual-aid filing run through one U.S. Claim Agent (Fred. W. Fout), notarized by a small recurring set of St. Louis notaries, and timed to the 1890 Dependent and Disability Pension Act. Of the six personal witnesses now identified, four were Missouri Light Artillery veterans — three from Charles's own 2nd MO L.A. (Derr, Meyer, Stoll) and one from the sister 1st MO L.A. (Nolan). Several filed their own pensions in the same 1889-1891 window in which they swore for Charles.

The narrative phrasings ("heavy cannonading at New Madrid," "rupture from heavy lifting") recur word-for-word across affidavits — the agent's drafting hand at work. → Read the full Pension Mutual-Aid Network theme
