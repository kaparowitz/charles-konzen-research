Fort Wyman / "64th Illinois" Mystery — Research Log

A May 2026 forensic investigation: pension claim vs. War Department roll search vs. NARA muster cards vs. free-source unit-history checks · who was Charles drilling at Fort Wyman in 1865?
CIVIL WARLast updated 6 May 2026

★ At a Glance

Charles's 1890 pension declaration claims he was injured by heavy lifting at Fort Wyman, Rolla, Missouri while detailed as drill sergeant for the 64th Illinois Infantry in Heavy Artillery tactics. The carded record places Charles at Rolla, Missouri from 27 March through 1 July 1865 (~3 months) under G.O. No. 82 / S.O. No. 14, Hd Qrs Dept of the Mo. Charles arrived at Rolla after his ~11-month New Madrid Tour 2 (24 April 1864 – 27 March 1865) ended. Fort Wyman was the main Union fortification at Rolla, so this is consistent with the pension claim's "Fort Wyman" wording — but the literal word "Wyman" does not appear on any muster card. On 1 July 1865 Charles transferred from Rolla to Department HQ St. Louis under S.O. No. 159; he was relieved 4 November 1865 by S.O. No. 92. The unit-being-drilled question is still open; the strongest candidate is the 49th Wisconsin Volunteer Infantry (at Rolla 13 March – August 1865), which overlaps Charles's 27 March – 1 July Rolla window almost exactly.

Status as of 6 May 2026

Place: CORROBORATED with caveat. The cards explicitly place Charles at "Rolla, Mo." from 27 March – 1 July 1865 under G.O. No. 82 / S.O. No. 14, Hd Qrs Dept of the Mo. Fort Wyman was the main fort at Rolla, so the pension claim's "Fort Wyman" wording is consistent with the carded record. The literal word "Wyman" does NOT appear on any muster card — the previous "first verbatim Wyman hit anywhere" finding was a transcription error and is retracted.

Rolla detachment: 27 March – 1 July 1865 (~3 months), following the ~11-month New Madrid Tour 2 (24 April 1864 – 27 March 1865).

July–November 1865: Department HQ in St. Louis (NOT Rolla). Special Order No. 159, 1 July 1865, transferred Charles from Rolla to Department Headquarters in St. Louis. He served there until 4 November 1865, when S.O. No. 92 relieved him.

Reduction Sgt → Pvt: 15 March 1865 — late in New Madrid Tour 2, before the Rolla transfer. Charles served the entire Rolla and Dept HQ details as a Private.

Date in pension declaration: still contradicted. Charles's declaration says "July 1862" — before his 2 December 1862 enlistment. The actual carded Rolla service is 27 March – 1 July 1865.

Unit being drilled: still unknown. 49th Wisconsin remains the strongest candidate (Rolla 13 March – August 1865, overlapping Charles's 27 March – 1 July Rolla window almost exactly).

The puzzle

Charles Gannsen's 1890 Declaration for Invalid Pension stakes his right-inguinal-hernia disability claim on a single sentence:

"… in the line of duty at Rolla, in the State of Missouri, on or about [July] 1862, while detailed as drill Sergeant to instruct the 64th Illinois Infantry in the tactics of Heavy Artillery, by heavy lifting in Fort Wyman he was injured on the right side."

The Pension Bureau awarded $12 / month on this claim in November 1891 despite an internal contradiction surfaced by their own due-diligence search: the 30 June 1891 War Department roll-check returned "the name Charles Gannsen has not been found on rolls of any Co. in 64 Ill Vol" (NARA Pension Page 36). Charles was awarded the pension anyway — but the question of who he actually drilled at Fort Wyman has remained open.

What's now confirmed (corrected May 2026 evening)

Confirmed
Place — "Rolla, Mo." (Fort Wyman by default), 27 Mar – 1 Jul 1865 (~3 months)

Verified verbatim across both packets — Fold3 PG 5/6 and NARA PG 11 Cards B/C: "On Det Serv at Rolla Mo. G.O. N° 82 H.Q. Dept of the Mo March 27, 65" (Fold3) / "S.O. No. 14, March 24/65" (NARA). Charles was at Rolla from late March 1865 through 1 July 1865 — about 3 months. Fort Wyman was the main Union fortification at Rolla, so the pension claim's "Fort Wyman" wording is consistent with the cards' "Rolla" reading — but the literal word "Wyman" does not appear on any muster card.

Confirmed
Three governing Special Orders (corrected)

G.O. No. 82, 27 March 1865 — Rolla detachment (Fold3 PG 5/6, NARA PG 11 B/C). NOT S.O. No. 14, NOT 24 March, as previously stated.
S.O. No. 159, 1 July 1865 — transfer from Rolla back to Department HQ in St. Louis (Fold3 PG 4, NARA PG 12 A/B). NOT to Wyman HQ at Rolla; the previous "Wyman Hd Qrs Rolla Mo July 15" reading was a transcription error.
S.O. No. 92, 4 November 1865 — "Relieving Priv. L. C. Gannsen, 2nd Mo. Lt. Art., from duty at Hd Qs Dept of the Mo," by command of Maj. Gen. John Pope, signed J. W. Williams, A.A.G. (Fold3 PG 23/24, NARA PG 04/05). Received at Battery HQ "Novbr 10/65." Note that this order relieves Charles from duty at Department HQ in St. Louis (where he had been since 1 July) — not from Rolla, where he had been earlier in the year.

Confirmed
Reduction in rank: 15 March 1865

Verbatim on Fold3 PG 4 and NARA PG 12 A/B: "Served as Sgt until Mar 15, 65 when reduced to Ranks Per [G.O.] No. 82." The reduction happened in March 1865 under G.O. No. 82 — the same general order that detached Charles to Rolla on 27 March 1865. There is only one G.O. No. 82 of Hd Qrs Dept of the Mo. for this period; the order combined the rank-reduction (effective 15 March) and the Rolla detachment (effective 27 March). The "1–4 November 1865" reduction date previously inferred from S.O. No. 92 is wrong; that order described Charles already as "Private L. C. Gannsen" because the reduction had happened seven months earlier. Charles served all of his subsequent 1865 Rolla and Dept HQ St. Louis details as a Private, not a Sergeant.

What remains disputed

Disputed
Date — pension declaration says July 1862

Charles's 1890 declaration places the injury "on or about July 1862," before his 2 December 1862 enlistment — geographically and chronologically impossible. His own 1898 handwritten address-history letter (Pension Page 28) writes the year as "July 1863" — also problematic (he was at the St. Louis garrison forts then). The actual NARA-documented Rolla service is March – November 1865 — almost certainly the period when the heavy-lifting incident actually occurred.

Disputed
Unit — the 64th Illinois was nowhere near Rolla

The 64th Illinois ("Yates Sharpshooters") served under Sherman in Tennessee, Georgia, and the Carolinas in 1865 — they marched to the sea, fought at Bentonville, and were discharged in Louisville. Never at Rolla. The Pension Bureau's 30 June 1891 negative reply on the 64th Illinois rolls is therefore correct on its terms — Charles was never attached to that regiment.

Disputed
Heavy-lifting incident location

Charles's pension declaration locates the injury at Fort Wyman, Rolla. But the comrade affidavits sworn in 1891 by 1st Sgt. August Derr and Pvt. Christian Meyer — both fellow Battery B veterans — locate the disabilities at New Madrid, Sept–Nov 1863: hearing loss from heavy cannonading, rheumatism from cold and exposure, and the rupture from heavy lifting at the same station. Two contradictory accounts of the same disability cluster, both inside the pension file. The carded muster cards do not adjudicate.

Investigation log

Round 1 6 May 2026 — Initial Fold3-only reading

Working from only the Fold3 download of the M405 carded service records, the spring–April 1865 detached-service muster card (Fold3 PG 11) was read as "Fort Madison Marines, since April 23/65." The destination was interpreted as either the U.S. Marine Hospital in St. Louis, the Marine Barracks at Jefferson Barracks, or possibly Fort Madison, Iowa. Fort Wyman did not appear on this card. Earlier project analysis concluded that the muster cards did not corroborate the pension claim's "Fort Wyman" detail.

Outcome: Pension claim's Fort Wyman wording appeared unsupported by the carded record. The hernia / right-side / 64th Illinois detail looked like pension-file confabulation.

Round 2 6 May 2026 (initial reading — partly retracted) — NARA mail-order packet (G11-422819443E) examined

A 13-page NARA mail-order Compiled Service Record packet, ordered in 2018 and previously misfiled in the project as a placeholder PDF, was re-identified and read page-by-page. The NARA scan is dramatically clearer than the Fold3 download on the spring–fall 1865 cards. Three cards resolved more cleanly than in Fold3, but the initial agent-transcription of two of them was wrong — corrected in Round 6 below.

Initial (incorrect) finding: NARA PG 11 Card B was read as "On det. serv. at Rolla Mo. S.O. No. 14 March 24/65." NARA PG 12 Card A was read as "On det. serv. at Wyman Hd Qrs. Rolla Mo. By Order No. 159 July 15." Both are incorrect. The actual readings (verified Round 6) are G.O. No. 82, March 27, 1865 for the Rolla detachment, and S.O. No. 159, July 1, 1865, Dept HQ St. Louis for the July transfer. There is no "Wyman" word on any card.

What WAS correctly read in Round 2: NARA PG 04 reproduces Special Order No. 92 in full — 4 November 1865, by command of Maj. Gen. Pope, signed J. W. Williams A.A.G., directing Pvt. L. C. Gannsen to report back to his battery. That reading stands.

Round 3 6 May 2026 — Digit-drop hypothesis: 144th Illinois

First working hypothesis on the unit Charles drilled: a clerk on the 1890 declaration dropped the leading "1" of "144th", leaving "44th", which was then mis-recorded as "64th". The 144th Illinois Volunteer Infantry was a one-year regiment (organized Alton, 21 October 1864; mustered out 14 July 1865) — the right kind of green regiment to need an artillery sergeant's drill instruction.

Free-source check: Frederick Dyer's Compendium and the Illinois GenWeb regimental page agree the 144th was assigned only to "St. Louis District / Alton" on prison-guard duty — never Rolla. The Alton Jaeger Guards reenactor history specifies the regiment was "organized to guard the prisons in Alton and St. Louis." Reece's Report of the Adjutant General of the State of Illinois company-by-company rosters (Companies A–K) examined for any "Rolla" or "Fort Wyman" or "Detached: 2 Mo. Lt. Arty." annotation.

Outcome: Zero hits across all 11 roster pages. The 144th Illinois regimental-history transcript reads: "Notwithstanding diligent effort was made to obtain historical mention of the services of the Regiment, none was sent into the office and hence it was not in our power to say anything authentic concerning the campaign of the Regiment." The 146th Illinois (parallel digit-drop candidate) never went to Missouri at all. 144th case closed.

Round 4 6 May 2026 — Free-source unit-history sweep

Broader search: which units WERE at Rolla / Fort Wyman in March – November 1865? Sources consulted: NPS Soldiers and Sailors System battle-units pages; Frederick Dyer's Compendium; Illinois GenWeb regimental pages; FortWiki / hmdb.org Fort Wyman markers; civilwararchive.com Missouri / Indiana / Illinois pages. Candidate Illinois infantry regiments (46th, 64th, 47th, 24th, 17th, 144th, 146th) and cavalry regiments (4th, 6th, 7th, 8th, 17th IL Cav) tested individually. 64th U.S. Colored Infantry tested as the literal-number candidate. 64th Indiana / 64th Iowa tested as state-mis-transcription candidates.

Finding: The 49th Wisconsin Volunteer Infantry emerged as the strongest candidate. NPS battle-unit page UWI0049RI reads: "Organized at Madison, Wis., December 24, 1864, to March 5, 1865. Left State for St. Louis, Mo., March 8, 1865, thence moved to Rolla, Mo., March 13. Garrison and guard duty at Rolla till August and at St. Louis, Mo., till November. Attached to 2nd Sub-District, District of St. Louis, Dept. of Missouri." Arrived Rolla 13 March 1865 — eleven days before Charles's S.O. 14 of 24 March. Brand-new green one-year regiment in the right administrative jurisdiction. Geographic + chronological + organizational fit on every dimension.

Other candidates eliminated: 46th IL (Cumberland → Texas in 1865), 64th USCI (Vicksburg / Mississippi), 17th IL Cavalry (left Rolla January 1865), 64th IN (never fielded as infantry), 64th IA (no such unit; Iowa fielded only 48 infantry regiments).

Round 5 6 May 2026 — 49th Wisconsin roster check + Battery B location complication

Definitive free-source check: the official Wisconsin Adjutant General's Roster of Wisconsin Volunteers, War of the Rebellion, 1861–1865, Vol. II (Madison: Democrat Printing, 1886), pages 847–867, the 49th Wisconsin section. Searched the full ~8,300 lines of the regimental section for: "Gannsen / Gansen / Gonnson / Gannson"; "drill instructor"; "2d Mo." / "2nd Mo." / "Missouri Light"; "Fort Wyman."

Finding: Zero hits in the 49th Wisconsin section for any of the search terms. The only artillery-related annotation in the regiment is one Field & Staff transfer-in ("From Corp. Co. M, 1st Wis. Heavy Art.") — a 1st Wisconsin transferee, not a 2nd Missouri Light Artillery instructor. The state roster does not document Charles's attachment to the 49th Wisconsin.

Material complication: NPS battle-unit page UMO0002RAL for the 2nd Missouri Light Artillery places Battery B at Franklin, Missouri from 19 November 1864 through 1 June 1865 — not at Rolla. "Moved to Franklin November 19-21, and duty there till June 1, 1865. Ordered to St. Louis June 1 and equipped as Cavalry. Moved to Omaha, Neb., June 11-20." Franklin is ~60 miles east of Rolla. So Charles was personally detached away from his battery to Rolla — the simple "Battery B was at Rolla and Charles drilled the 49th Wisconsin alongside" picture is wrong. Charles was a singular detached private assigned to drill duty by departmental Special Order, not part of a battery presence at Rolla.

Outcome: The 49th Wisconsin remains the strongest candidate by co-location at Rolla, but the documentary link from either side is missing.

Round 6 6 May 2026 (evening), user re-read of Fold3 cards — Major transcription corrections

The user pointed out that the agent's earlier transcriptions of Fold3 PG 4, 5, 6, and 13 (and the parallel NARA PG 11 / 12 cards) contained multiple errors. A focused re-reading of the original PDFs, cross-comparing both packets, confirmed the user's corrected readings:

  • The Rolla Special Order is No. 82, dated 27 March 1865 — not S.O. No. 14 dated 24 March (Fold3 PG 5/6, NARA PG 11 B/C: "On Det Serv at Rolla Mo. G.O. N° 82 H.Q. Dept of the Mo March 27, 65").
  • The July Special Order detached Charles to Department HQ in St. Louis, not to "Wyman HQ Rolla." S.O. No. 159 is dated 1 July 1865, not 15 July (Fold3 PG 4, NARA PG 12 A/B: "On Det Serv at Dept Hd Qr St Louis Mo. S.O. No 159 July 1, 65"). The earlier "Wyman" reading was a transcription error; no card contains the word.
  • The reduction Sgt → Pvt happened on 15 March 1865, not November 1865, per G.O. No. 82 — the same general order that detached Charles to Rolla on 27 March 1865 (only one G.O. No. 82 in this window). Fold3 PG 4 / NARA PG 12 A/B: "Served as Sgt until Mar 15, 65 when reduced to Ranks Per [G.O.] No. 82." Charles served all his subsequent 1865 details as a Private, consistent with S.O. No. 92's reference to him as "Private L. C. Gannsen."
  • The promotion to Sergeant date is preserved on Fold3 PG 13, not just NARA — earlier transcription missed this (Fold3 PG 13: "Promoted from Corporal to Sergeant Nov 7, 1863.").
Revised picture: Charles's late-war detached service is two distinct postings, not one continuous Rolla / Wyman period. (1) Rolla, Mo., 27 March – 1 July 1865 (~3 months) under G.O. No. 82 / S.O. No. 14. (2) Department HQ in St. Louis, 1 July – 4 November 1865 (~4 months) under S.O. No. 159, relieved by S.O. No. 92.
Implication for the 49th Wisconsin candidacy: The overlap window with the 49th Wisconsin tightens but the candidacy strengthens on the dimension that matters most. Charles arrived at Rolla on 27 March 1865 — exactly two weeks after the 49th Wisconsin arrived (13 March 1865) — and remained through ~early July, almost exactly the 49th's Rolla garrison window (Rolla 13 March – August 1865 per NPS). This is the cleanest possible co-location for a drill-instructor detail to a green one-year regiment. The only reason the documentary link is still missing is that neither packet records the recipient unit on the carded text — the Special Orders themselves (NARA RG 393) would name the unit.
Implication for the pension claim's "Fort Wyman" wording: The pension declaration says Charles was injured "at Fort Wyman." Fort Wyman was the main Union fortification at Rolla, so the claim is consistent with the cards' "at Rolla, Mo." reading — but the literal word "Wyman" doesn't appear on the muster cards. The previous claim that NARA PG 12 carried a "first verbatim Wyman hit anywhere" is retracted.

Definitive next steps

  1. Order the manuscript text of Special Orders Nos. 82 (27 Mar 1865), 159 (1 Jul 1865), and 92 (4 Nov 1865), Headquarters, Department of the Missouri — NARA RG 393, Part 1. The orders themselves will name the recipient regiment for Charles's drill detail (likely embedded in the body of G.O. No. 82). Available via NATF Form 86, ~$30 each. The most diagnostic single record possible. Note revised order numbers per the 6 May 2026 user correction.
  2. Order the post returns of Fort Wyman / District of Rolla, March – early July 1865 — NARA RG 393, Part 1, Entry 6232 (Returns from U.S. Military Posts). These list garrison units month by month and would name the regiments at the post during Charles's narrowed 27 March – early July 1865 Rolla window. Same paid-order path, ~$30.
  3. Order the post returns of Department HQ, St. Louis, July – November 1865 — same RG 393 series. Charles's NEWLY-IDENTIFIED 1 July – 4 November 1865 posting at Department HQ St. Louis is itself a 4-month detached service for which we have only the cards' summary remarks. The post returns would document daily / weekly assignments at Department HQ during that window, including any drill or instructor detail.
  4. HathiTrust browse of War of the Rebellion Series I, Vol. XLVIII, Part II (correspondence, March–November 1865, Department of Missouri) — search "Fort Wyman", "District of Rolla", "S.O. 82", "S.O. 159", "S.O. 92" for any published verbatim of the orders. Free.
  5. Missouri Digital Heritage / Soldiers' Records at sos.mo.gov/mdh — pull Charles Gannsen's Missouri service-record card image directly; check for any "detached" remarks not visible on the Fold3 or NARA scans. Free.
  6. FamilySearch image-only Missouri muster-and-descriptive rolls for Battery B, 2nd Mo. Lt. Arty., March–November 1865. Free; would be definitive evidence of where the battery and individuals were on a given date.
