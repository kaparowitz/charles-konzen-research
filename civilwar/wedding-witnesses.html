The Four Wedding Witnesses

St. Marcus Marriage Register · 7 January 1866 · Pastor G.W. Wall officiating
CIVIL WAR · MARRIAGE18 days after Charles's discharge

★ At a Glance

The St. Marcus marriage register entry for Charles Gannsen × Auguste Kreichelt — image 72 of the 1865-1867 register at FamilySearch — lists four witnesses to the right of the bride and groom's origin information ("aus Deifse, Kreis Cassel, Churhessen, und Clausthal, Königr. Hannover"). The pastor's signature line below them reads Cop: G.W. Wall. Of the four, one is a confirmed Battery B comrade (Adam Herweck), one is from a recurring Gannsen-baptism family (Anna Goetz), and two have not yet been individually identified.

The four witnesses, in register order

⚜ Senior witness · Battery B

Adam Herweck

Named first in the witness column. Charles's Battery B comrade and most likely his recruiter into the unit (per the 9 Dec 1862 Captain's letter from New Madrid). Born 16 July 1831 Lampertheim, Hessen. Same rank trajectory as Charles. See the dedicated Adam Herweck profile for the full deep-dive.
FamilySearch K4DW-PXM
Open ID · multiple Philipp Webers

Philipp Weber

Second witness. Surname & given name common in St. Louis German community of the 1860s; specific identification not yet pinned down (multiple Philipp Webers in 1860 St. Louis directories — one a brewer, one a labourer, one a butcher).
No FamilySearch tree person yet matched
★ Recurring Gannsen-baptism family

Anna Goetz

Third witness — likely the senior bridesmaid. The Götz / Goetz family appears at multiple Gannsen child baptisms — Maria Agnes Götz sponsored Maria Josephine Gannsen in 1868. Anna Goetz also witnessed at the 1875 marriage of Albert Schlange in the same St. Marcus register entry block. The Goetz family connection runs deep across the entire 1860s–1870s Gannsen narrative.
Cross-link: Maria Agnes Götz profile
Open ID · spelling uncertain

Sophie Lehm (Lehrm/Lehmann?)

Fourth witness. Surname spelled "Lehm" in the register; possibly Lehrm or a Lehmann variant. Specific identification not yet established.

Battery B at the wedding — the broader picture

The four witnesses are only part of the story. The wedding party — bride's family, groom's friends, formal witnesses — accounts for at least three Battery B veterans:

  • Charles Gannsen himself — Battery B veteran; served as Sergeant 1863–65, discharged 20 Dec 1865 as a Private.
  • Adam Herweck — Battery B Sergeant, named first in witness column.
  • Georg Kreichelt — bride's father, Battery B veteran. Walks Auguste down the aisle.
  • August Kreichelt — bride's brother, Battery B veteran. Decades later sponsors Charles's son Erwin Carl Konsen at the 1875 baptism.

Why this matters. Battery B was not just Charles's wartime unit — it became his civilian-life social network. The people who served with him at New Madrid in 1863 were the people at his wedding in 1866, the people who sponsored his children in 1868–1880, and the people who swore affidavits supporting his 1890 pension claim. The U.S. Civil War, for Charles, structured the next thirty-eight years of his life.

Source

St. Marcus Marriage Register 1865-1867 — Image 72 of 246, entry No. 676 (the 1 January – 25 January 1866 cluster). FamilySearch image group 007769184. The full entry reads: "Carl Gonnson, aus Deifse, Kreis Cassel, Churhessen, und Clausthal, Königr. Hannover · Adam Herweck · Philipp Weber · Anna Goetz · Sophie Lehm · Cop: G.W. Wall".
