Service Timeline — Enlistment to Death

2 December 1862 – 16 December 1903 · 41 years from enlistment to death
CIVIL WAR · PENSION · LIFEAll cited to primary documents

★ At a Glance

Three years that anchor everything we know about Charles. He enlisted in St. Louis on 2 December 1862 as L. Charles Gannsen, served Battery B of the Missouri 2nd Light Artillery to discharge in December 1865, married Auguste Kreichelt the next month — daughter of Battery B veteran Georg Kreichelt who himself had served an early-war 3-month enlistment in the same unit before Charles arrived, and lived on the resulting pension — eventually $12 / month — for the last twelve years of his life. Two primary sources carry the entire factual record: the one-page Compiled Service Record (Doc O) and the 40-page pension file F41-523680269E.

The full timeline

2 December 1862
Enlisted in St. Louis as L. Charles Gannsen, age 23, Private. Place of birth recorded as "Freundburg, Kurhessen". Unit on the original enlistment card: Co. G, 2nd Light Artillery, State of Missouri.→ Compiled Service Record (Doc O)
1862–1865
Served through the Civil War in Battery B of the 2nd Missouri Light Artillery. Promoted Private → Corporal → Sergeant. Acquired the disabilities of record — right inguinal hernia, chronic rheumatism, and partial hearing loss — that would underwrite his post-war pension claim.Pension medical examinations · Surgeon's Certificate (Page 33)
20 December 1865
Discharged at the rank of Private (he had served as Sergeant from 7 Nov 1863 until 15 March 1865, when he was reduced to Private mid-detachment at Rolla). Returned to St. Louis and took up residence at 1518 South 7th Street in the Soulard / Frenchtown German district. Charles's own 1898 pension declaration writes the address as "1516 So. 7th St."Pension Page 29 (handwritten address history)
7 January 1866
Eighteen days after discharge: marries Auguste Kreichelt at St. Marcus German Evangelical Church, St. Louis. Bride's father Georg Kreichelt and brother August Kreichelt — both fellow veterans of Battery B — at the ceremony. Recorded as "Carl Gonnson, aus Deisse, Kreis Cassel, Churhessen."St. Marcus marriage register, 1866 · → Wedding witnesses
1866–1880
Seven children baptized at St. Marcus German Evangelical Church: Carl Heinrich (1866), Maria Josephine (1868), Emma Rosalia (1870), Catharina Auguste (1871), Erwin Carl Konsen (1875 — the surname-preserving baptism), Auguste (1877), Charles Frederick (1880). Sponsor network dominated by Auguste's Kreichelt-Albrecht-Mincke kin.
1890
Files his pension claim under the new Dependents' Pension Act (June 27, 1890). Charles's claim cites the right hernia, rheumatism, and hearing loss originating from heavy cannonading and exposure at New Madrid, Missouri, in September–November 1863. Witnesses: Christian Meyer and August Derr (1st Sergeant of Co. B), both fellow Battery B veterans.Page 14 · Page 22 (Meyer) · Pages 24+26 (Derr)
November 1891
Pension Certificate No. 684,507 issued. $12.00 per month under the 1890 Act. Disability of record: Right hernia & Rheumatism; also loss of hearing. Pension agency: Topeka, Kansas. Pension paid through the Topeka office for the remainder of Charles's life until 1900.Page 09 (issued certificate)
1898
Charles writes a handwritten letter to the Pension Bureau supplying his complete address history from 1865 onward — the spine of the residence timeline. Lists six St. Louis residences and the dates of each. The Pension Bureau also takes a Surgeon's Certificate of Examination this year confirming continued disability.Page 29 · Page 33
18 October 1900
Admitted to the Danville Branch, National Home for Disabled Volunteer Soldiers in Danville, Illinois. Pension agency transferred from Topeka, Kansas to Chicago, Illinois.Page 06 (Form 103)
16 December 1903†
Died at age 68 in St. Louis of stomach cancer (carcinoma). Last recorded address: 168 Sidney Street. Buried 19 December 1903 at Old St. Marcus Cemetery (later converted to a city park; markers removed). The Danville Branch Governor returned his pension certificate to the Pension Office on 4 January 1904; the U.S. Pension Agent's "Pensioner Dropped" notice followed on 18 January 1904.Death certificate no. 10619 · Page 03 · Page 39
