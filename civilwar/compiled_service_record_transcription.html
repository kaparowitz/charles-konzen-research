How to read this page. Each of the 25 PDF pages is shown below in its original order, with the scanned image on the left and a structured transcription on the right. The cards are 19th-century clerk transcriptions in cursive and many fields are partially illegible — illegible portions are marked with [illeg.]; uncertain readings appear in [brackets]. The chronology runs backwards through the packet: the latest event (muster-out, Aug 1865) is on page 2; the earliest (enlistment, Dec 1862) is on page 21.

This is the final muster-out card — the chronologically last event in the packet despite being filed near the front. Two reading notes. (1) This roll is dated late August 1865 and its "muster-out to date" field reads August 20, 1865, but the project's canonical discharge date is 20 December 1865 — the date carried by the later separation action (Special Order No. 92, Nov 1865) and read as "to date Dec. 20, 1865" on the cleaner NARA scan (an earlier Fold3 reading had this field as Dec. 7). Charles's return to St. Louis and his 7 January 1866 marriage are consistent with a December 1865 separation. (2) The "enl'd Nov 11, '63" in the Remarks line is part of the rank / Gen.-Order-28 pay entry on this card; Charles's promotion to Sergeant is dated 7 November 1863 on the dedicated promotion card (Page 8). See the service timeline.

Page 24 — Special Order No. 92, Department of the Missouri, 12 June 1865 SPECIAL ORDER

Original endorsed order · Maj. Gen. Pope's headquarters
Header
Head Quarters, Department of the Missouri

SPECIAL ORDERS, No. 92 — Extract
Body
"3 ___ The following named enlisted men are hereby relieved from special service at these Head Quarters, and will report forthwith to their respective Company Commanders for duty, to wit: Private C. D. Gannsen, 2 Mo. Arty. The Q. M. Department will furnish the necessary transportation."
Issued by

Signed
Sm. [Samuel] Williams, Assistant Adjutant General
Endorsement / file
"Lindell Office, 2 Mo. Arty" (or similar — refers to the regimental office at the Lindell Hotel/Barracks complex in St. Louis)

The only non-card document in the packet — and arguably the most historically rich. Pope's order returns Pvt. Gannsen from special HQ duty back to his battery. The transcription of "C. D." (rather than "Charles") is consistent with the index card on page 22 and the endorsement on page 23.

Reading discrepancy (flagged June 2026): this Fold3 scan is transcribed here as S.O. No. 92, 12 June 1865, signed Sm. Williams, naming "C. D. Gannsen." The cleaner NARA mail-order scan (PG 04) of what appears to be the same order reads it as 4 November 1865, signed J. W. Williams, naming "C. L. Gannsen." The date and signer-initials are therefore contested between the two scans and unresolved; both are recorded pending a side-by-side image re-read. (Either way, the order only relieves Charles from special/detached service — it is not the source of his rank reduction, which is independently dated 15 March 1865 by G.O. No. 82.)

Charles Research — a multi-month transatlantic genealogical investigation into Charles Konzen (recorded in U.S. records as Gannsen / Johnson; 1835–1903), Hessian-born Civil War sergeant of Battery B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery, and his probable Trendelburg / Deisel origins in Churhessen (a working conclusion — the German baptism remains unrecovered).
