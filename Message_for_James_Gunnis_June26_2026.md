Hi Jim,

Thanks for this — I chased the Dassen/Fulda lead all the way down, and it was a good one to run.

**The entry is real.** I pulled it on Archion (Fulda Stadt ev. Taufen 1830–1863, Bild 852,
Seite 64, entry 298) and read it off the original: *Karl Friedrich*, legitimate first son,
born the 26th (Feb) 1838 on the Petersgasse, baptized 3 March 1838. Father **Michael
Gastmann**, Feldwebel in the 1st Company of the 2nd Infantry Regiment; mother Anna
Catharina. And you nailed the surname — the initial is clearly a **G** ("Gastmann");
Archion's machine text had guessed "Hartmann," which is wrong.

I don't think he's our Charles, though, for a few reasons that stack up:

- The surname **Gastmann** doesn't fit the Konze / Gansen (*n·s·n) family I keep landing on.
- It's **Kreis Fulda**, not the "Kreis Cassel" Charles gave his pastor in 1866.
- Born **1838**, a bit off the best U.S. record (26 Jan 1835).
- And the timing: Charles was already here by Dec 1862 (enlisted in Battery B) and married
  in St. Louis in 1866 — so he can't be a man who first lands in the U.S. later.

I kept going to be sure. Since Michael was a career Feldwebel, I traced the unit — the
Kurhessen **2nd Infantry Regiment** ("Landgraf Wilhelm" from 1845); its companies rotated
through the garrison towns, with the 1st Company in Fulda in 1838. A soldier's family would
have moved on with the regiment, which fits the Fulda confirmations: I read the 1852 and
1853 classes and there's **no Gastmann**. FamilySearch and Ancestry turn up no marriage or
death for him either. The one near-name hit is a "Carl Gestmann," farmer, ~b.1837 "Hessen,"
who landed in New York (Brooklyn) on the *Atalanta* 18 May 1867 — but he's travelling alone,
the origin is just "Hessen" with no town, and nothing ties him to Fulda, so I'd call that
unproven.

Really appreciate you stretching the place-name like this — it's exactly the kind of lateral
