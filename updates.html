May 2026

3 May 2026 · eveningSite-wide audit
Implemented the 10-recommendation site-wide improvement pass:
  • Renamed the main HTML to index.html (fixes 60+ broken back-nav links across all deep-dive pages).
  • Repaired umlaut filename mismatches (maria-agnes-gotz → maria-agnes-goetz, plus broken Reck profile link).
  • Made all SVG embeds responsive (width:100%; max-width:Npx) so the Stammtafel and St. Louis residence map scale to mobile.
  • Added print stylesheet to main HTML and every shared CSS file — pages now print as readable research notes (tabs/footer hidden, links auto-expand to URLs).
  • Injected Open Graph + Twitter Card metadata into 87 standalone HTML files so shared links unfurl with the correct page title and description.
  • Built this changelog page with category filters.
3 May 2026 · middaySite restructure
Major tile-grid pattern rolled out across five tabs (Civil War, Charles in America, Schrader, Immigration Journey, Germany & Origins). Each tab is now an overview with deep-dive page links instead of long inline content. Tab renames: "Overview" → "Latest Findings"; "At a Glance" → "Overview".
→ civilwar/ · Charles in America · immigration/ · germany/ · Peter's email timeline
3 May 2026 · morningNew page
Added Christina Hahl profile page — the structural bridge between Misch and Gottlob families, mother of Katherine Misch, 2nd wife of Theodore Gottlob Sr. Resolves the "Uncle Frederick Gottlob" naming chain documented on Frederick Charles Johnson's FS tree.
→ Christina Hahl profile
2 May 2026New page
Added Auguste Kreichelt and Katherine Misch individual pages, each with parents+siblings tree and (for Auguste) the seven Gannsen children below. Linked from the Family Tree tab.
→ Auguste Kreichelt · Katherine Misch
1 May 2026Quentin breakthrough
Quentin investigation completed Pass 1–10. Definitive identification: Quentin Geo. bakery, 345 W. Row, Cincinnati 1855-56 = Peter Schräder's Friedrichsfeld emigrant Georg Karl Quentin. Sister Auguste = Eliza Quentin in 1860 Cincinnati census. Charles Quentin (Wisconsin Senator) confirmed as the chain-migration mechanism via his 1851 Reisebilder book.
→ Quentin family file
3 May 2026Theodore Gottlob
Theodore Gottlob Sr. confirmed as Civil War Sergeant of 9th Illinois Infantry (Co. A, 1861-1864, 3+ years, saw Vicksburg). Two attached FS sources: Leavenworth NHDVS 1893 admission record + complete two-enlistment muster history. Now the 6th confirmed Civil War veteran in Charles's St. Louis circle.
→ Theodore Gottlob profile · Pension Mutual-Aid Network
3 May 2026Cleanup
Removed redundant content from the Schräder tab: the Stammtafel SVG (now the tile grid carries that information), the inline correspondence-highlights timeline (full version on the dedicated email-timeline page), and the Y-DNA pivot card. 44.5 KB shaved off the main file.

April 2026

27–30 Apr 2026Hildebrand cohort + LAGIS / St. Marcus
Sessions 75–78 of the Hildebrand investigation arc. Found the Wilhelmine Maria Hildebrand 1853 manifest (President Smidt → Philadelphia, 5-person family, exhausted at 5). Eliminated the leading Carl Kinzer 1859 candidate (manifest image read as Krieger). LAGIS Hessen emigration index queried under every name variant — negative for Charles. 30 April: LAGIS confirms four 1853 Friedrichsfeld emigrants → Nordamerika.
→ Investigation Session Notes
12 Apr 2026Schräder verdict
Peter Schräder's "not a legitimate Konze son" verdict. After checking his Konze database against Deisel emigration lists, only one Konze appears in the relevant list and it is not Charles. The Konze hypothesis is now contested — but not closed: the 1875 St. Marcus Erwin Carl Konsen baptism still preserves the surname.
→ Konze family file
11 Apr 2026Burial-index breakthrough
Charles Gannsen's burial confirmed at Old Saint Marcus Cemetery — Burial #3393, 19 Dec 1903. Jakob Reck (1837-1898) also buried at Old St. Marcus. The Charles–Jakob Reck pairing — five years apart in the same consecrated ground — is the strongest physical evidence yet that Charles's parish-circle social bonds persisted from baptism font to graveyard.
→ Old St. Marcus Cemetery
5–8 Apr 2026Andreas Konze
Peter sends the Konze / Hofeditz overview chart. Unusual case: handicapped eldest daughter, grandfather a council member of Trendelburg, the father (Andreas Konze) "lost without a trace" — recorded as such in baptism marginalia, factory-worker in Friedrichsfeld, dying in Mainz 1854. This kicks off the Konze deep dive.
→ Konze family file

March 2026

16–19 Mar 2026Y-DNA thread
Y-DNA testing of a descendant's line was reviewed as a possible route to Charles's paternal origin. The results were explored as a Jewish-paternal-line hypothesis through spring 2026. This thread was set aside in May 2026 (its basis was a weak surname-resemblance argument), then independently reactivated in June 2026 when the Big Y-700 result placed Charles's patriline in a subclade whose nearest matches are Ashkenazi — a working line of inquiry, not a conclusion; see the DNA evidence page and Open Questions · deep paternal origin. The project's surname conclusion still rests on documentary evidence.
→ Stern / Kleeburg family file
3–4 Mar 2026Grandjot · 24-hour candidate
Peter found Carl Ludwig Grandjot (b. Schöneberg 1836, godfathered by Johannes Herwig — the Trendelburg → Schöneberg → emigrant chain). For 24 hours this looks like the answer. Eliminated 4 March: Grandjot died in Schöneberg 1901, never emigrated.
→ Grandjot family file

February 2026

24–27 Feb 2026Trendelburg pivot
Peter finds the Herwig family in Trendelburg, who emigrated 1854. Adam Herweck — wedding witness at Charles's 1866 marriage — served in the same Civil War unit as Charles. Peter's 26 Feb "Trendel-burg" insight reconciles the 1862 enlistment record's "Freundburg, Kurhessen" as a clerk's mishearing — the German word Freund ("friend") substituted for the unfamiliar Trendel first syllable.
→ Freundburg mystery deep-dive

Earlier · 2025

August 2025 onwardActive research phase
Active research phase resumes with Peter Schräder. 100+ emails over 9+ months, surfacing and eliminating 13 candidate families across the entire Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis (Christian and Jewish, Trendelburg and Deisel and Wehrden and Hofgeismar, surname-resemblance and chain-migration and aristocratic-origin theories).
→ Full email timeline
June 2020Foundation
AncestryProGenealogists Research Journal completed (Paul A. Schmidt & Brian A. Podoll, Feb–Jun 2020). Identified Deisel as the parish behind the St. Marcus marriage entry's "Deisse, Churhessen"; surfaced two Konze candidates in the Deisel baptism register; indexed the U.S.-side foundation documents (Doc A–O).
→ Foundation tab

Charles Research — a multi-month transatlantic genealogical investigation into Charles Konzen (recorded in U.S. records as Gannsen / Johnson; 1835–1903), Hessian-born Civil War sergeant of Battery B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery, and his probable Trendelburg / Deisel origins in Churhessen (a working conclusion — the German baptism remains unrecovered).
