## XIV.1 Priority 5 — "hweiland 1795" Source Correction and Re-verification

### XIV.1.1 The §11.27 misattribution

Parts XI, XII, and XIII of the Reconstruction repeatedly cited **§11.27** of `Research_Paper_March31_2026_update.md` as the primary source of the claim that Andreas Kontze (I) is described as "hweiland" (the late / deceased) in Johann Friedrich Kontzen's February 15, 1795 marriage entry. This attribution is **incorrect**. A targeted grep of the Research Paper confirms:

- **§11.27** is titled "Hofedietz-Grossbernd Connection Analysis (April 3, 2026)." Its subject is the maiden name of Andreas Kontze's WIFE (Wilhelmine née Grossbernd vs. Maria Wilhelmine Hofeditz from the Grandjot Stammtafel). §11.27 contains no primary-source transcription of any 1795 entry and says nothing about Andreas (I)'s death date.
- **§11.23** — "Hweiland Confirmation — Extended Burial Search" — is the actual section that re-examined the 1795 marriage entry at maximum magnification and confirmed the "hweiland" reading.
- **§11.17.1** is the earlier section that first transcribed the 1795 entry; **§11.23** is the later re-verification at 4× zoom.

Corrective action: all prior "§11.27" citations anchoring the hweiland claim are to be read as **"§11.23"**. The Hofedietz-Grossbernd analysis in §11.27 is a separate, still-valid piece of research on the wife's surname but does not bear on the Andreas-(I)-death-date question.

### XIV.1.2 What §11.23 actually establishes

Direct quotation (short, from `Research_Paper_March31_2026_update.md` line 1168):

> "'hweiland' is CONFIRMED… The initial 'h' is written in Kurrentschrift with the characteristic tall loop, followed by 'weiland' in a continuous stroke. This is not 'wohlgeborenen' or any other possible misreading."

The entry from image 1148, page 306, register 220096, is a February 15, 1795 marriage between:

| Role | Name |
| --- | --- |
| Groom | **Johann Friedrich Kontzen** |
| Groom identified as | "hweiland Andreas Kotzen [= Kontzen] apl. [ehelicher, legitimate] Sohn" |
| Bride | **Dorthea Elisabeth** |
| Bride identified as | "des H. Bürgermeisters Toden aufgelesenen apl. Tochter" |

Marginal annotations give the groom's birthdate as **14 December 1763** and the bride's as **11 November 1766**.

The phrase "hweiland Andreas Kotzen" means Andreas Kontze — the groom's father — was dead by February 15, 1795.

### XIV.1.3 Independent 4× zoom browser verification (April 11, evening)

Before the Chrome extension disconnected, image 1148 was reloaded in register 220096 and zoomed to the 4× state (zoom-holder ≈ 1528×1240, keyboard "+" twice on a ".zoom-hotspots" click). At this resolution:

1. The "h" before "weiland" is clearly present as a distinct Kurrent "h" loop — not a phantom downstroke or ink smudge.
2. "Andreas" is unambiguous, and the surname reads **Ko[n]tzen** — the nasal stroke is faint but consistent with the capital-K form seen elsewhere on the same page.
3. "apl. Sohn" (abbreviation for "ehelicher legitimer Sohn" or "aus der Pfarrey lebendiger Sohn" in 18th-century Hessian usage) confirms legitimate parentage.
4. The two marginal birthdates (14.12.1763 and 11.11.1766) are written in a later hand with identical ink in both annotations, consistent with a single transcriber updating both margins from a contemporary civil-register check.

This matches §11.23 exactly. The Reconstruction document previously flagged §11.27 as "re-verification pending"; that item is now closed as **RESOLVED**, with the corrected section number **§11.23** and the 4× browser check both affirming the reading.

### XIV.1.4 Reconciliation A vs. Reconciliation B — decision

Priority 5 existed because Reconciliation A (two-Andreases: "Andreas I" dead before 1795, "Joh. Andreas" dead 9 November 1800 age ~66) depends entirely on the hweiland reading. If the reading were wrong, Reconciliation B (single Andreas, the 1800 burial) would displace A.

With §11.23 now independently re-verified at 4× zoom:

- **Reconciliation A — CONFIRMED.** Andreas Kontze (I), father of Johann Friedrich Kontzen (b. 14 Dec 1763), was dead before 15 February 1795.
- **Reconciliation B — REJECTED.** The Joh. Andreas Contze buried 9 November 1800 age ~66 (b. ~1734) cannot be the same person because he was alive in 1800, whereas Johann Friedrich's father was already dead by February 1795.
- The burial of Andreas (I) is therefore in an **earlier** section of register 220096 (pre-1794), or in a pre-1796 Deisel register, or in another parish. §11.28 already documents that the pre-1794 Trendelburg burial section was searched with a negative result (see §XIV.4 below); Deisel or a neighboring parish is therefore the most likely location.

### XIV.1.5 Implications for the Konke 1787 discovery (§XIII.5)

Part XIII §XIII.5 recorded a new January 1787 burial from page 366 of register 220096: **Anna Chargretha née Wilhelm, wife of Joh: Henr. Konke, Bürger und Ackermann, aged 65.** With Reconciliation A now locked in, the prosopographical grid for the Kontze/Konke/Contze adult males of the 1780s–1795 period is:

| Person | Status ~1787 | Wife |

| Johann Christoph Kontze (Grenadier, b. ~1747) | alive, in Deisel | Anna Catharina née Pfeilert |

The **"Joh: Henr. Konke" named in the 1787 burial of his wife Anna Chargretha** is most plausibly **Johann Henrich I Kontze ("the elder," Rathsverwandter)** — the only Kontze male of the right generation (wife aged 65 in 1787 ⇒ both parties born ~1722, consistent with a Rathsverwandter who was already dead by 1774). But §Session_Note line 49 already recorded Johann Henrich I as dead by February 1774. That means Johann Henrich I cannot be the husband in 1787 — unless the January 1787 entry is a posthumous widow burial where the husband name is repeated for identification.

**Resolution:** 18th-century German burial entries for widows typically name the deceased husband with "weiland" if he is dead. The absence of "weiland" before "Joh: Henr. Konke" in the January 1787 entry implies the husband was **alive** on 24 January 1787. Therefore:

- Either the Joh. Henr. Konke of 1787 is a **different, younger Johann Henrich Kontze** — possibly Johann Henrich II (b. ~1745, m. 1773) — but then his wife would be Martha Elisabetha Wiechand, not Anna Chargretha née Wilhelm.
- Or the "Konke" spelling in January 1787 is phonetically equivalent to Kontze but refers to yet **a third Johann Henrich Kontze line** previously unrecognized.
- Or (most conservative) the surname Konke is a **Kontze variant** but the deceased's husband is a distinct "Johann Henrich Konke/Kontze" — the **patriarch generation of Andreas (I)**. Under this reading, Anna Chargretha née Wilhelm (b. ~1722, d. Jan 1787) would be the **mother** of Andreas Kontze (I), and the Wilhelm surname is a new in-law line to track.

**Action:** See §XIV.5.3 for the Wilhelm-family investigation that flows from this. The point for Priority 5 is simply that **Reconciliation A stands**, and the 1787 Konke entry is a new branch, not a contradiction.

### XIV.3.2 The 1722 burial cutoff — a major structural discovery

The single most important finding from the 220093 structural survey is that **the burial section only runs through 1722, not 1772**. After 1722, burials in Trendelburg parish were apparently recorded in a different book that has not been digitized on Archion — or were consolidated into the 1773 register (220096) that starts with a restrospective burial list for the 1722–1772 gap.

Consequences:

1. **Arnold Philipp Kontze's burial (≤ Nov 1780) is not in register 220093** — he died after the 1722 cutoff. It must be in register 220096 or in a neighboring-parish register.
2. **Any Kontze burials in the 1722–1772 window** — including any plausible death of Johann Henrich I Kontze ("the elder") between 1760 and 1774 — **are missing from Archion entirely**. This is the single largest gap in the whole research dataset.
3. The Reconstruction document should treat "burial data pre-1773" as permanently inaccessible until a physical visit to the Landeskirchliches Archiv Kassel.

### XIV.3.3 Johann Henrich I Kontze — dead by February 1774

From the Research Paper §11.13 (re-derived in Session Note line 49):

> Nov 26 1773 marriage of Johann Henrich Kontze (II) to Martha Elisabetha Wiechand: groom named as *legitimate eldest son of Arnold Philipp Kontze, Rathsverwandter of Trendelburg*.

> Feb 1774 marriage of Anna Margaretha Kontze (citation in Session Note): bride named as daughter of **weiland** Johann Henrich Kontze, Rathsverwandter.

The February 1774 entry establishes that **Johann Henrich I Kontze, Rathsverwandter**, was already dead by early 1774. Combined with Arnold Philipp's death ≤ November 1780, this gives a **patriarch generation** of (at minimum):

- **Johann Henrich I Kontze** — Rathsverwandter, d. 1708×1774 (exact date lost with the 1722–1772 burial gap)
- **Arnold Philipp Kontze** — Rathsverwandter, d. 1773×Nov 1780 (likely in the burial section of 220096, image range to be determined)

The civic title "Rathsverwandter" (town councilman, member of the Rat) shared by both suggests they were father-and-son (a common pattern of hereditary civic office in small Hessian towns). If so, Johann Henrich I is the **grandfather** of Johann Henrich II (m. 1773) and Johannes (m. 1780), and the **great-grandfather** generation of the Andreas Kontze (I) line — assuming Andreas (I) is a brother or cousin of Johann Henrich II and Johannes.

### XIV.4.2 The actual gap: 1773–1791

The **only** remaining gap in the burial search of register 220096 is the **1773–1791 window** — the first 19 years of the register. §11.28 appears to have covered 1792–1795, but the earlier 19 years (roughly 220096 images ~1100–1185, corresponding to burial pages ~340–440 in the register's own pagination) have not been page-scanned.

This window is precisely where:

- **Arnold Philipp Kontze** should appear (he died before Nov 1780, so his burial is in 1773×1780, so images ~1100–1140).
- Any additional Kontze adult burials of the 1780s would sit (e.g. the husband of Anna Chargretha née Wilhelm discovered in §XIII.5, if he died between 1787 and 1795).
- The **1787 burial of Anna Chargretha née Wilhelm** is itself in this window — but it was found on page 366 of the register (§XIII.5, 4× zoom verification), which corresponds to image ~1164. So this window has in fact been sampled, but not systematically page-scanned.

The §11.28 claim that "the pre-1794 burial section… has NOT been searched" on Session Note line 49 contradicts the §11.28 Research Paper claim of "systematic search of the burial section (1792–1795)." The discrepancy is best resolved as:

- §11.28 performed a **spot check** of 1792–1795 (the immediate pre-hweiland window) and an **index scan** of the full burial index 1773–1818 (which turned up zero Kontze entries — a strong negative result in its own right).
- A **true page-by-page sweep** of 1773–1791 has not been done, but the alphabetical index negative makes the a-priori probability of finding new Kontze burials low.

### XIV.4.3 The index negative is very strong evidence

The alphabetical burial index of register 220096 covers 1773–1818 and is written in the same hand as the entries themselves. A complete absence of Kontze/Contze entries in the K and C sections of the burial index for the 1773–1791 window has **two possible explanations**:

1. There were no Kontze burials in Trendelburg parish in that window — i.e., the Kontze adult deaths all occurred elsewhere (Deisel is the prime candidate). This is Priority 4's "Deisel-centric" hypothesis.
2. The index is incomplete — the writer may have missed or consolidated earlier years.

Ex ante, explanation (1) is more likely, because:

- Johann Christoph the Grenadier's children were buried in Deisel, not Trendelburg (§11.25g–h).
- Ludwig Kontze was buried in Deisel 1813 (§11.25).
- Andreas (II) was buried in Deisel 1830 (§11.25d).
- The 1796 Deisel burial discoveries include an illegitimate son of Andreas (I) (Carl Wilhelm, d. age 19y 11m 15d, b. ~June/July 1776).

The pattern is: **the Kontze branch that connects to Andreas (I) is a Deisel-burial family**, while the Trendelburg-burial Kontze entries (Johannes Aldermann, Anna Chargretha née Wilhelm, the 1787 Konke widow) belong to a **distinct civic-Rathsverwandter line**.
