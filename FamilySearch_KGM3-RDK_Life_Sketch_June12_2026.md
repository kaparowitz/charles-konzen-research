# Bertha Jansen / Bertha Johnson (KGM3-RDK) — Life Sketch

> Drafted 12 June 2026 for the FamilySearch Life Sketch field. Grounded in the sources attached
> to the profile and the project page children/bertha.html.

---

Bertha Johnson was born on Christmas Day, 25 December 1880, in St. Louis, Missouri, and baptized Bertha Jansen, the seventh and youngest child of Charles Johnson Sr. — a Hessian-born immigrant and Civil War artillery sergeant — and Auguste Kreichelt, born in Clausthal, Hanover. (Her father's 1898 pension declaration gives the date as 20 December, a five-day drift from the church record.) She was baptized 4 September 1881 at St. Marcus German Evangelical Church (register p. 574, no. 10269) by Pastor Johannes Nollau. Alone among the seven children she had a single named sponsor: her aunt and namesake, Bertha Barbara "Babette" Kreichelt, her mother's youngest sister, then twenty years old.

Her baptism is the final entry in the family's seven-baptism record at St. Marcus, and the clerk's "Jansen" spelling is the documented bridge between the family's German surname and the American "Johnson" it became. Even her father's 1898 pension declaration still rendered her name in a German form — "Bertha Gannson."

Bertha was fifteen when her mother died in 1896. In 1898, at seventeen, she married Georg Reinhard Heinrich Adolph "Reinhardt" Rothschild (1864–1945), also known as George. They had six children: Walter (1899–1900), Augusta (1901–1925, married Harry Fabry), Irene Clara (1903–1971, married Anthony April), Elsie Marie (1906–1956, married Harold Jehne), George Joseph (1908–1977, married first Mary Rickey, then Rose Hart), and Roy (1910–1910) — like her mother, she buried two children in infancy. Bertha and Reinhardt divorced in 1920. On 25 October 1922 she married Edward F. Offer (1880–1923), son of Fredrich Offer and Margaret Raridon; he died barely a year later. On 26 August 1925, in Wellston, she married Fred Polkinghorne (1881–1967) — shortly after the death of her daughter Augusta, and a few months after the death of Fred's first wife, Lillian Bayless — a marriage that lasted the rest of her life.

The censuses keep her in greater St. Louis all her life: Wellston, Central Township, Normandy. She outlived all six of her siblings. In her final illness she spent ten weeks at the home of her daughter Irene Rothschild April at 1524 Wellston Avenue, and died 20 August 1959 in Warrenton, Warren County, Missouri, of bronchopneumonia complicated by Parkinson's disease — the last of the seven children. She was buried 24 August 1959 at Lake Charles Park Cemetery in Bel-Nor, St. Louis County, closing a family story that had begun at the St. Marcus font in 1866.
