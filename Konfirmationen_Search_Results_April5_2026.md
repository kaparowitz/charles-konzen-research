## Analysis: The Konze Family Departed Trendelburg/Friedrichsfeld Between 1843 and 1846

The pattern is unambiguous:

- **1842:** Maria Carolina Konze confirmed at Trendelburg — family present in Friedrichsfeld
- **1843:** Justus Ernst Konze confirmed at Trendelburg — family still present
- **1846–1855:** ZERO Konze confirmations at Trendelburg — family absent

This means the Andreas Konze family LEFT the Trendelburg parish area (specifically Friedrichsfeld) **sometime between mid-1843 and early 1846**. This is a window of roughly 2.5 years.

### Key contextual factors:

1. **Maria Sophia Hofeditz died 15 October 1835** — the children had been motherless for 8+ years by 1843.
2. **Andreas Konze "secretly" abandoned the family** — Peter Schrader's description says he left "secretly" and died in Mainz on 6 October 1854 as a factory worker. The timing of his departure is unknown, but **the confirmation gap suggests he may have left as early as 1843–1845**, taking or scattering the younger children.
3. **Justus Ernst was ~14 at his 1843 confirmation** — old enough to be apprenticed or to work. After confirmation, he may have left on his own.
4. **Heinrich Wilhelm was ~11–14 in the 1843–1846 window** — still dependent on family. His absence from the 1846 confirmation strongly suggests the family relocated.
5. **Maria Sabine was ~9–12 in the 1843–1846 window** — also dependent. Her absence from 1848 confirmations corroborates family departure.

### Implications for Charles Gannsen:

This finding is highly significant. If Charles Gannsen is Justus Ernst Konze (b. 1829), his last documented appearance in Germany is his 1843 confirmation at Trendelburg. After that, the trail goes cold — the entire Konze family vanishes from the Trendelburg parish records. The next step would be to search for the family in:

- **Mainz church records** (where Andreas died in 1854)
- **Other Hesse-Kassel parish records** (if the children were placed with relatives)
- **Emigration records from the 1845–1860 period** (Hamburg passenger lists, LAGIS Hessische Auswanderer)

The discovery that the family departed the area **earlier than previously assumed** (1843–1846 rather than the 1850s) could shift the emigration search window earlier as well.

## Observations on Confirmation Register Format

- **Years 1842–1849:** Confirmations held at **Pfingsten (Pentecost)**, typically late May or early June
- **Years 1852–1855:** Confirmations shifted to **Ostern (Easter)**, typically March or April
- **Typical class size:** 18–26 confirmands per year
- **Typical confirmation age:** 14 years (some entries show ages 13–15)
- **Register columns:** Entry number, name of confirmand, parents' names/occupation/residence, birth date, age (years/months/days), date of confirmation, grade (gut/genügend/nicht gut)
- **Friedrichsfeld entries:** Multiple Friedrichsfeld residents confirmed at Trendelburg each year, confirming Friedrichsfeld was part of the Trendelburg parish for the entire period
