Glossary

Definitions of the German archival and genealogical terminology used across the project — for readers approaching this site without a background in 19th-century Hessian record-keeping.

The site is bilingual by accident: it reports findings in English, but the underlying records — Kirchenbücher, Stammrollen, Auswanderer-Belege — are German, and the standard descriptive vocabulary is German too. This page defines the terms that recur most often.

Jump ABCDE FGHIK LNPRS TV

A

Auswanderer-Beleg DE /ˈaʊsˌvandərɐ bəˈleːk/
Lit. "emigrant document." A formal emigration permit issued by the local Hessian authority, certifying that a subject of military age had discharged conscription obligations and had no outstanding civic debts. Required by law in 19th-century Hesse-Kassel; in practice, often evaded. Many Auswanderer-Belege survive in Arcinsys, the catalogue of the Hessian state archive at Marburg. cf. Open Questions, Priority 3.
Auswanderung DE
Emigration. Auswanderungswelle = "wave of emigration"; refers in this project to the great mid-19th-century German emigration to the United States.

B

Bestattung / Bestattungsregister DE
"Burial / burial register." The third book in a typical Hessian Lutheran Kirchenbuch, after Taufen (baptisms) and Trauungen (marriages). The Trendelburg Bestattungsregister 220096 is one of the project's pivotal sources; it contains the K-section index entries for the Kontze / Kontzen surname pool 1773–1818.

C

Churhessen DE
The pre-1866 orthography for the Electorate of Hesse-Kassel. Modern German: Kurhessen. Charles's 1866 St. Marcus marriage register uses the Churhessen form, dating the language of the record to the pre-Prussian-annexation period. See also Hesse-Kassel context.
Compiled Service Record (CSR) EN
A U.S. National Archives compilation of the carded muster-roll entries, special orders, and miscellaneous service papers documenting one Civil War soldier's enlistment, postings, and discharge. Charles's CSR (file G11-422819443E) is imaged on the site at Sources › NARA.

D

Deisel DE
The village ~5 km south of Trendelburg in the Kreis Hofgeismar. Charles's stated home on the 1866 St. Marcus marriage register — recorded as "Deisse" by Pastor Wall, an English-speaking listener's capture of the spoken German place-name.

E

Evangelisch DE
"Evangelical." In the German context of the 19th century, the umbrella term for both Lutheran and Reformed Protestant churches; at the parish level the dominant denomination of Hesse-Kassel. Charles's American church — St. Marcus German Evangelical Church in St. Louis — preserved the term.

F

FamilySearch EN
The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints' free genealogical platform. Hosts the project's editable family tree and indexes a large fraction of the U.S. vital and census records. Person IDs (e.g. LZXC-6GB for Charles, LZKV-9F5 for Auguste) are used inline throughout the site.
Fold3 EN
An Ancestry-owned database of digitised U.S. military records, especially Civil War service and pension files. Used in this project as the working transcription layer; where Fold3 readings disagree with the NARA originals, the NARA reading is canonical.
"Freundburg" EN
The non-existent place-name written by an English-speaking recruiting clerk on Charles's 2 December 1862 enlistment register. Phonetically decoded as Trendelburg: the German "Tr-" cluster heard as "Fr-," the meaningless syllable "-endel-" normalised to the familiar German Freund- ("friend"), the place-suffix -burg preserved. See research log.

G

geb. (geboren) DE
"Born" — used in genealogy to introduce a maiden name: "Auguste Somers geb. Konzen" = "Auguste Somers, née Konzen." The German equivalent of the French née.
Genealogical Proof Standard (GPS) EN
The Board for Certification of Genealogists' five-element framework for sound genealogical conclusion: reasonably exhaustive research; complete and accurate citations; analysis and correlation of the collected information; resolution of conflicting evidence; and a soundly reasoned, coherently written conclusion. Codified in Genealogy Standards, 2nd ed. (Nashville: Ancestry, 2019). The project's confidence taxonomy on Methodology is designed against this standard.

H

HTR (Handwritten Text Recognition) EN
Automated transcription of historical handwritten text. Used by Peter Schräder for first-pass readings of Friedrichsfeld KB entries before manual correction.

I

illegitim / unehelich DE
"Illegitimate / out of wedlock." A baptismal entry for an illegitimate child in a 19th-century parish record typically notes the mother's name only, often with the marginal note illegitim or unehelich; the father's name may be supplied by a later legitimisation entry or be absent entirely. The project's multi-register Jan 1835 illegitimacy scan (April 2026) searched these entries specifically and surfaced no candidate baby for Charles.

K

Kirchenbuch DE /ˈkɪʁçənˌbuːx/
Lit. "church book." A parish register, kept by the local pastor and recording the three principal sacraments — baptisms (Taufen), marriages (Trauungen), and burials (Bestattungen). For 18th- and 19th-century Hessian Lutherans, the Kirchenbuch is the closest thing to a vital-records register; civil registration began in Hesse only in the 1870s. Most Hessian Kirchenbücher relevant to this project are digitised on Archion.de.
Kirchenkreis DE
"Parish circle." A super-parish administrative unit in the German Protestant church, roughly equivalent to a deanery or rural decanal district. Charles's stated home village Deisel (per his 1862 Civil War enlistment record and the 1866 St. Marcus marriage register) sat in the Kirchenkreis Hofgeismar, which also contained the parishes of Trendelburg, Friedrichsfeld, Helmarshausen, Sielen, Hümme, Schöneberg, and Heisebeck — the cluster the project has searched. The specific German baptismal entry that would localise Charles's home parish has not yet been recovered (see Open Questions, Priority 1).
Konze / Kontze / Kontzen / Contze / Contzen / Konzen DE
The surname pool documented continuously in the Trendelburg parish complex from the mid-18th century. Treated as orthographically interchangeable by the parish itself (cross-reference note in burial-register 220096: "siehe auch K. Contze, Cortze, Cordes, Contz"). Konzen is the project's May-2026 working conclusion for the form Charles inherited. See The Konzen puzzle.
Kreis DE
"Circle / district." A 19th-century Hessian county-equivalent administrative unit, between the village and the state. Charles's stated origin "Kreis Cassel" refers to one of the larger administrative districts of the Electorate; "Kreis Hofgeismar" is the more granular district that contains his actual village.
Kurfürst DE
"Elector." The Holy-Roman-Empire-era title of one of the seven (later more) princes who elected the Holy Roman Emperor. After 1806 the function ceased to exist, but the ruler of Hesse-Kassel preserved the title for prestige. Friedrich Wilhelm I, the last Elector of Hesse, was deposed in September 1866 when Prussia annexed the territory — three days after Charles & Auguste's firstborn was christened with the same first names, in St. Louis.
Kurhessen DE
Modern spelling of Churhessen, the Electorate of Hesse-Kassel. Used inside the 1862 enlistment register's "Kurhessen" reading. See Churhessen.
Kurrent / Sütterlin DE
Two cursive German handwriting scripts. Kurrentschrift (Kurrent) was in general use through the 19th century. Sütterlinschrift (Sütterlin) is a 1911 simplification of Kurrent that was the dominant script in German schools 1915–1941. Both are essentially illegible to modern readers without training. All 19th-century Hessian parish entries on this project are written in Kurrent.

L

LAGIS DE
Landesgeschichtliches Informationssystem Hessen — a free regional records portal for historical Hesse. Used in this project for parish boundaries, regional emigration data, and contextual reference.
ledig DE
"Unmarried, single." Common notation in 19th-century Hessian parish entries, often paired with the subject's profession.

N

NARA EN
The (U.S.) National Archives and Records Administration, headquartered in Washington, D.C. Holds the original Compiled Service Records and Pension Files for U.S. Civil War soldiers. Charles's CSR and Pension File were obtained as mail-order packets from NARA in May 2026.
NGSQ EN
The National Genealogical Society Quarterly, a leading peer-reviewed U.S. genealogical journal. Its short-form citation style is the basis for citations on this site.

P

Pfarramt / Pfarrer DE
"Parish office / pastor." The Pfarramt is the administrative office of a Lutheran parish; the Pfarrer is the parish minister, the keeper of the Kirchenbuch.

R

Register DE/EN
In the Archion.de context, a numbered parish register: e.g., Register 220108 = Trendelburg Taufen 1830–1863. The site cites these by Archion register ID.

S

Seite DE
"Page." Used to disambiguate from Nro. ("number," referring to the entry number on a page) and Bild (the digital image number in the Archion viewer). The project's image-to-Seite map for Trendelburg Register 220108 (1 Seite ≈ 1.15 images; image = 1375 + viewer-option) is documented in the project's session conventions.
Stammrolle DE
A 19th-century Hessian military register, listing all males of conscription age in a given year-cohort. For Charles, the relevant Stammrolle would be the 1855 cohort (males b. 1835). Some volumes for the relevant district remain undigitised at the Hessisches Staatsarchiv Marburg. cf. Open Questions, Priority 4.
Stammtafel DE
"Family table" — a structured pedigree chart of one ancestral line, typically on a single sheet. Peter Schräder's Stammtafel-construction work for the Trendelburg-Friedrichsfeld Konze branches is the spine of the project's German-side analysis.
St. Marcus EN
St. Marcus German Evangelical Church in St. Louis, founded 1843. Charles & Auguste's parish: the site of their 1866 marriage, the seven baptisms 1866–1880, and Charles's 1903 burial at Old Saint Marcus Cemetery (now St. Marcus Commemorative Park; markers removed).

T

Taufe / Taufen DE
"Baptism / baptisms." The first book in a Lutheran Kirchenbuch. The Trendelburg Taufen 1830–1863 (Register 220108) is the central source for the project's Konze-line searches.
Trauung / Trauungen DE
"Marriage / marriages." The second book in a Lutheran Kirchenbuch. Used to corroborate parental relationships and witness networks.
Trendelburg DE
The fortified village ~5 km north of Deisel that gives the project its working geographic anchor. Trendelburg's Kontze-pool burial register and post-1830 baptismal register are central evidence; the parish complex is the leading hypothesis for Charles's actual baptism.

V

verschollen DE
"Disappeared / lost without trace." A standard archival annotation in 19th-century Hessian state records for a subject whose departure was not formally recorded — typically used in Stammrolle and church-membership registers for males who had emigrated without filing the paperwork. The annotation pattern "nach Amerika" ("to America") similarly tags an unfiled emigration.

If a term you encountered on the site isn't here, please let me know and I'll add it.
