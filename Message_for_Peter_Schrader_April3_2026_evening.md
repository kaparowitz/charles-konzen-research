## 1. Hofedietz-Grossbernd Connection — RESOLVED (§11.27)

I've conducted a thorough analysis of whether "Hofedietz" was a Hausname for the Grossbernd family, or whether the Grandjot Stammtafel's "Marie Wilhelmine Hofeditz" is a different person from Andreas Kontze's wife Wilhelmine née Grossbernd.

**Conclusion: Hofedietz and Grossbernd are two distinct families.**

The key evidence:
- The 1810 baptism record (register 220096, page 173) clearly reads "Wilhelmine geb. Grossberndin" — a contemporary primary source that takes precedence over the compiled Stammtafel
- Both families lived in Vöhlein but appear independently with separate family members on the same register pages
- The Grossbernd family has a well-documented lineage tracing to Bartold Grosbert (b. 1595) in Sielen — they were traditionally smiths and wagon builders
- The Hofedietz family appears independently in Stammen (Christian Hofeditz, Ackermann) and Deisel (burial entries on image 1845)
- No record uses linking language (vulgo, genannt, alias) between the two names
- The Stammtafel's "Marie Wilhelmine Hofeditz" (b. 1784, d. 1850 Schöneberg) is most likely a different Wilhelmine

Andreas Kontze's wife was legally **Wilhelmine née Grossbernd**. The 1807 marriage record should be read as Grossbernd.

The Hofedietz family nonetheless remains significant: their presence alongside the Kontze family in Deisel (1813), and the 1827 marriage of Andreas Konze (widower) to Maria Doris/Sophia Hofedietz, confirms continued Kontze-Hofedietz intermarriage.

## 6. HETRINA Database Search — MAJOR DISCOVERY (§11.32)

I've now completed a comprehensive search of the LAGIS HETRINA database (Hessian Troops in America) for Johann Christoph Kontze. I searched all six spelling variants (Kontze, Konze, Contze, Cunze, Kuntze, Kunze) in the persons register, and also searched by place of origin for all soldiers from Deisel (39 persons) and Trendelburg (27 persons).

**Johann Christoph Kontze was NOT found** in the HETRINA under any spelling. However, two significant discoveries emerged:

**"Jungkonze, Henrich" (b. ~1765, Trendelburg)** — Regiment von Bose (BOS 4), rank Common, mentioned 1784. Source: HStAM 10 c No. 485; HETRINA II, 5989 [page 99]. The compound Hausname "Jungkonze" ("Young Konze") confirms the Konze family was well-established in Trendelburg with at least two distinct branches. The "Jung-" prefix implies an elder "Alt-Konze" household also existed.

**Kunze, Philipp (b. ~1747, Trendelburg)** — Regiment von Bose (BOS 1), rank Common, mentioned 1775 and 1784. Born roughly the same generation as Johann Christoph. Potential relative.

Both Trendelburg Konze/Kunze men served in the Regiment von Bose. No Kontze/Konze from Deisel appears in HETRINA at all. Johann Christoph's "Grenadier" title may reflect Seven Years' War service rather than American Revolution deployment.
