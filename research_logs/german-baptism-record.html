German Baptism / Birth Record — Research Log

Identity of Charles's biological parents · ~30-session sweep · all confidently-identified candidates eliminated
EVIDENCE NOT FOUND Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis · 1834-1836 window

★ What this record would tell us if found

The identity of Charles's biological parents — the central question of the entire investigation. A baptism entry in the right Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis parish would carry:

  • Father's name — resolving the Konze-vs-other-surname question. Establishes whether the Konsen surname recorded at the 1875 St. Marcus baptism of Erwin Carl Konsen reflects a Konze paternal line, a Gansen line, or another spelling.
  • Mother's name and maiden name — testing the Wilhelmine Hildebrand hypothesis (the project's working candidate for Charles's mother).
  • Godparents (Pathen) — typically maternal/paternal kin, naming siblings, uncles, and aunts in a single entry. The Hessian baptism convention names 2-4 Pathen, often the parents' own siblings.
  • Exact birth date and birthplace — settling the 25 Jan / 26 Jan birthdate ambiguity (death certificate vs. St. Marcus burial record vs. pension affidavit).
  • Marginalia — German parish books often record later events in the margin: confirmation, marriage, emigration, death. A single marginal "ausgewandert nach Amerika 1859" annotation would close the entire emigration question.
  • Legitimate vs. illegitimate — relevant for any birth record: illegitimate Hessian children often name only the mother. Many illegitimate Hessian children have only the mother named, with the father added separately if paternity was acknowledged.

Why this record should exist

Lutheran and Reformed parishes in Kurhessen kept comprehensive baptism registers (Taufregister). Every Christian birth in the 1834-1836 window — Charles's claimed birth window — should appear in some parish's register. The Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis parishes are mostly digitised on Archion (archion.de), with the notable exception of Heisebeck (held offline at the Landeskirchliches Archiv Kassel). The completeness of the baptism record requires only that Charles was a Christian-baptised child in this region.

Eliminated leading candidates

eliminated Entry 195 · Deisel KB 218842 · 23 Jan 1835

Promoted to leading hypothesis April 2026 after the project's HTR-assisted reading produced "Justus, son of Joh. Heinrich Konze (Kaufmann) and Wilhelmine geb. Hildebrand" with ~85% reading confidence. Eliminated 4 May 2026 when Peter Schräder re-read the original Kurrent script: the entry is for a 3-day-old male child of a Köster Tagelöhner (day-labourer), not a Konze Kaufmann. The child died unbaptised; the field interpreted as "Justus" actually reads "männlich" (male). See Entry 195 source page.

eliminated Carl Ludwig Lucas · Trendelburg · 1835

Trendelburg-baptised illegitimate male, born February 1835. Ruled out 13 April 2026 by Peter Schräder when the marriage record was found in Germany — the man stayed and married locally.

eliminated Friedrich August Hund · Trendelburg baptism #125 · 1835

Trendelburg-baptised illegitimate son of Christine Niemeyer; father Carl Ludwig Hund accepted paternity. Ruled out 31 March 2026 after Peter Schräder's name-reading resolved the entry.

eliminated Carl Ludwig Grandjot · Schöneberg · 13 Oct 1836

Schöneberg-baptised, godfather Johannes Herwig (Trendelburg → US 1854 emigrant). Ruled out 4 March 2026: died 12 February 1901 in Schöneberg — never emigrated.

eliminated Justus Ernst Konze · Trendelburg · 1829 (and other candidates)

Eliminated 12 April 2026 when Peter Schräder concluded "Charles is not a legitimate son of the Konze family" after checking his Konze database against the Deisel emigration list. Only one Konze appears in that emigration list and it is not Charles.

The Y-DNA thread (set aside)

In spring 2026 the project briefly explored whether Charles's paternal line might be Jewish, which would explain the absence of a Christian-parish baptism. That line of inquiry has been set aside (May 2026); the German-baptism search continues on documentary grounds in the Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis parishes.

Next paths to chase

  • Heisebeck on-site research at Landeskirchliches Archiv Kassel — the one digitisation gap. Heisebeck baptisms 1834-36 have not been searched directly.
  • Hesse-Kassel Jewish-community registers — Hofgeismar / Trendelburg / Deisel / Schöneberg Jewish births if such records survive for 1834-36.
  • Schöneberg pre-1822 records — Session 66 found these exist in some form; full 1810-1822 baptism walk could surface a Wilhelmine Hildebrand birth which would unlock the maternal line and indirectly point at Charles's parish via her marriage record.
  • Tax registers of Deisel / Trendelburg ~1750 — every citizen, including non-Christians; could surface a Gansen / Konsen family in the parish cluster.
