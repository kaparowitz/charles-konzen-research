1870 U.S. Federal Census — Research Log

Charles & Auguste's young-family enumeration · household not located · in-laws found mistranscribed
EVIDENCE NOT FOUND Address per pension: 1518 S 7th St → 718 Gates Ave Search documented 4 May 2026

★ What this record would tell us if found

The only census enumeration of Charles's young family with first-born children alive. Specifically:

  • Household composition — whether Friedrich Wilhelm Gonnson (b. 1866, d. 1867) had been replaced by a second living child by mid-1870. The known surviving child by mid-1870 is Friedrike Catharina Caroline Amalia (Katherine Carolina), baptised 22 May 1870 — about ~3 months old at census time.
  • Address on 1 June 1870 — would settle whether they were still at 1518 South 7th Street or had moved to 718 Gates Avenue. The pension file documents both addresses but not the exact transition date.
  • Charles's 1870 occupation, real-estate value, and personal-property value — would establish his economic position in his fifth American year.
  • Nearby households on the page — would reveal his immediate neighbours and witness-network anchors. The Battery B veteran cluster (Adam Herweck, August Derr, Christian Meyer, Henry Stoll, Michael Nolan, Theodore Gottlob) would surface if any of them were neighbours.
  • Auguste's birthplace as recorded — would confirm or refute the canonical reading that she was born in Clausthal, Kingdom of Hannover, ~1840. The 1900 census records her as Missouri-born (likely transcription error), but the 1870 entry would be ~30 years closer to her actual birth.

What level of detail to expect on origin

The 1870 schedule had a free-text "Place of birth" column (column 11) plus the new "Father of foreign birth / Mother of foreign birth" flags (columns 11-12). For Charles, the place-of-birth field could read at any of three granularities:

  • "Germany" — broadest. By 1870 the post-1871 unification language was already filtering down even though German unification wasn't quite complete; many enumerators wrote "Germany" by default.
  • State-level — "Hesse," "Hessen-Cassel," "Hessen Cassel," "Kurhessen," or sometimes "Prussia" (which had annexed Kurhessen in 1866). For Charles, this would be the highest-probability outcome by 1870 — he had been in St. Louis ~11 years and may have given the enumerator a more specific answer than in 1860.
  • Specific town or village — rare but transformative. By 1870 Charles's English would have been good enough to communicate a place name like "Trendelburg" or "Deisel" to an English-speaking enumerator without it getting mangled (as it was in 1862's "Freundburg Kurhessen").

Equally informative: the same column for Auguste. The 1900 census records her as Missouri-born; if the 1870 entry says Hesse-Kassel or another German state, that would be the closer-to-truth record and would correct one of the wife-side biographical fields.

Charles & Auguste's household was missed by the 1870 enumerator. This is consistent with three observations:

  • The 1865-1869 pension address (1518 S 7th Street) and the 1871 directory address (718 Gates Avenue) imply a move during the 1870 calendar year — possibly during the June–October enumeration window, when families in transit were systematically under-counted.
  • 1870 St. Louis ward boundaries split between Wards 4 and 5 along the central spine that runs through both addresses. A move across a ward boundary near census day 1 June would mean neither ward's enumerator captured them.
  • Across 10 adjacent pages around the Kreichelts and exact-match wife & husband searches, no plausible mistranscription surfaces — the household is not in the indexed data at all under any spelling tried.

Alternative hypotheses (less likely but not ruled out): (a) Charles was working away from St. Louis on enumeration day and Auguste + infant were enumerated alone under an unrecognised surname; (b) the household is on a still-unsearched ward / image — the page-walk methodology has covered Ward 03 around the Kreichelts but not Wards 4 / 5 of 1870 St. Louis.

Session update · 20 June 2026 — FamilySearch index sweep & methodology correction

A fresh attempt on the FamilySearch indexed 1870 U.S. census (collection 1438024), driven by the address evidence. The household was still not located, but the session overturned several assumptions that had quietly invalidated parts of the earlier search, and it re-pointed the ward target.

★ Four corrections that change how the search must be run

  • The 1870 federal census is NOT in FamilySearch Full-Text. Full-Text covers St. Louis city directories (715 "Kreichelt" hits) and a handful of census collections (e.g. 1929/1935 Oklahoma), but not the 1870 U.S. census. The "Full-Text when available" next-path is therefore closed for this record. (Directory full-text, however, is a live avenue — see below.)
  • The 1870 index has no household / relationship links. The 1870 schedule recorded no relationships, and FamilySearch did not reconstruct them. Sanity check: "Charles" b.1828–42 in St. Louis = 3,279 hits, but "Charles + spouse Aug*" = 0. Every spouse-anchored query returns 0 regardless of truth — which voids the spouse-"Charles"/wife-pair logic used in the earlier Pass 2 & Pass 3. Households can only be reached by searching one person and opening the record to read the co-residents.
  • On census day they were at 718 Gates, recorded as "Johnson," not at 1518 S 7th. The pension address-chain dates 718 Gates to 1869–1874, so 1 June 1870 falls there; the 1871 Edwards' Directory lists the head as "Johnson, Charles, conductor, St. Louis R.R., r. 718 Gate." The 1870 enumerator may well have written a phonetic German surname (cf. 1880 "Gonson"), but the anglicised "Johnson" + occupation conductor is now an explicit thing to search.
  • Auguste was Hannover-born, not Hesse. She was born ~1840 in Clausthal, Kingdom of Hannover. The earlier Pass 2 filtered her birthplace as "Hesse-Cassel," which would exclude the true entry. Her 1870 birthplace should read Hanover (or possibly Prussia).

Ward re-analysis (per the user's STLGS / Edwards' 1870 ward-boundary lead)

Using the 1870 ward boundaries from Edwards' Directory: Ward 3's south edge ran along Lafayette (east of Linn) and its north edge along Rutger/Hickory; Ward 2 lay just south of Lafayette. The family's south-side cluster (1518 S 7th and the Gates-area addresses) sits around Lafayette Ave — i.e. astride the Ward 2 / Ward 3 line — and the in-laws (Georg Kreichelt) were already confirmed in Ward 3. Wards 4 and 5 are ruled out as primary targets: Ward 4's north edge is Plum/Cerre and Ward 5 is the Market/Clark corridor, both well north of these addresses. The prior log's "Wards 4/5" guess should be replaced with Ward 2 + the unwalked majority of Ward 3 (the April walk covered only ~10 pages around the Kreichelts at image 476).

★ Breakthrough — the city directories place "Charles Gonson" at 718 Gate, in a railroad-workers' building

A FamilySearch Full-Text sweep of "718 Gate" across the St. Louis directories (these are fully OCR-indexed) recovered the family's own entries and, crucially, their neighbours:

1876 directory"Gonson Charles, driver, 718 Gate" — our Charles, under the same surname the 1880 census uses, at 718 Gate.
1872–73 directory"Johnson Charles, conductor, r. 718 Gate" (plus a second "Johnson Charles, conductor, T.W. & W.R.R.") — the anglicised rendering, streetcar conductor.
1868–69 directory718 Gate housed a cluster of St. Louis R.R. (horse-car) men: McDonald Allan J. (driver), McDonald John D. (conductor), McDonald Joseph (driver), and William Chisholm (driver). Same building, same employer.
1877–82 directories718 Gate continues as a working-men's address: Walter Mulligan (1877–78), brewer Adam Ehl & midwife Selina Ehl (1880), conductor William D. Hall (1880–82), conductor Jacob Rose (1872–73).

Why this matters: 718 Gate was evidently a multi-tenant building tied to the St. Louis Railroad car service, and Charles — as a streetcar conductor/driver — lived there from ~1869 well into the 1870s. The 1870 census household is therefore almost certainly enumerated alongside other St. Louis R.R. families. Finding any 718-Gate neighbour in the 1870 census pins the exact page where Charles's mistranscribed/omitted household sits.

Status of the neighbour-anchor: the only "Chisholm" in 1870 St. Louis is an unrelated Canadian family in Ward 9 (William Chisholm the railroad driver had moved on), so it does not pin the page. Adam Ehl (the 718-Gate brewer) is in the 1880 census at 1903 Linn Street, ED 100 (saloonkeeper) — i.e. residents of this cluster sit in the Soulard / Linn-Street district, the deep-south-central grid that in 1870 falls in Ward 1–2 (south of Lafayette). This nudges the likely 1870 ward south of the Kreichelts (Ward 3), toward Ward 2. "Gate" street still isn't placed on any period map (residents' directory vs. census addresses don't agree, so it may have been an alley or a renamed stub). The neighbour-anchor was then run to exhaustion and failed: Jacob Rose is not in St. Louis in the 1870 census (he reached 718 Gate only by 1872–73), and the McDonalds / Chisholm of the 1868–69 directory don't surface at 718 Gate in 1870 either. In other words, none of the documented 718-Gate neighbours was demonstrably co-resident in June 1870 except Charles himself — so there is no neighbour to pin the page. The sole remaining route is a manual image page-walk of the Soulard/Linn blocks of 1870 Ward 2 (and the south end of Ward 3), scanning for a railroad-worker household headed by a German "Charles/Carl" ~35 with wife Auguste and toddler Josephine.

First-pass walk attempt (21 June 2026): opened the 1870 Ward 1 image set on Ancestry to scope the walk. Two hard facts emerged. (1) The 1870 schedule has no street/house-number column (street addressing of the census only began in 1880) — so there is no way to jump to "Gate" or "Linn" street; the household can only be found by reading names line-by-line. (2) Scale: St. Louis Ward 1 alone is 678 images (~40 names each ≈ 27,000 people), and Ward 2 is comparable — so a blind name-by-name walk of the candidate area is a multi-thousand-page manual task, not completable in a single sitting. Conclusion: a productive walk needs the neighbourhood narrowed first.

★ “Gate Street” located — Carondelet Avenue, Benton Park (1870 Ward 1)

A FamilySearch Full-Text search of the directory fire/police call-box lists (1875–76, 1880, 1882 St. Louis directories) repeatedly records the box at “Carondelet av. and Gate,” listed in sequence with “Carondelet av. and Pestalozzi,” “McNair av.,” “Lynch,” and “Cherokee and Lemp's Brewery.” An 1864–65 directory likewise has a “Gate av.” corner entry. This finally places the lost “Gate” street as a cross-street of Carondelet Avenue in the deep-south Benton Park / Marine Villa brewing district (~2700–2900 south) — not in Soulard proper. In the 1870 ward grid that block is Ward 1 (everything south of the Barton → Carondelet-av → Victor → Shenandoah line). The cast fits the place perfectly: a horse-car conductor/driver (Charles) and a brewer (Adam Ehl, later 1903 Linn) living in the Carondelet-Avenue / Lemp's-Brewery streetcar district, on the family's documented southward drift (1518 S 7th → 718 Gate → 2409 S Broadway).

Refined walk target: the 1870 Ward 1 images covering the Carondelet Av / Pestalozzi / Lynch / Benton Park blocks (the enumerator walked street-by-street, so these cluster on consecutive sheets).

Anchor attempts (21 June 2026, exhausted): tried to pin the exact Ward-1 sheets via a household known to be at/near Gate in 1870 — Adam Ehl is not in the 1870 census (St. Louis), the 718-Gate McDonalds can't be isolated among ~12,000 Irish McDonalds, and no other Gate resident is both indexed and demonstrably present in June 1870. On Ancestry, filtering “Augusta b.1840 + spouse Charles” down to St. Louis Ward 1 surfaces only unrelated couples (the “Krausen” servant, Augusta Loutwick/spouse Louis, Augusta Renecke/spouse Nicholas) — confirming once more that our household is not indexed with “Charles” as the inferred spouse (his given name was mistranscribed too).

Two remaining routes, both manual: (1) a no-spouse scan of every German-born “Augusta” ~30 in 1870 Ward 1, opening each household to look for a streetcar-worker “Charles/Carl” + a ~2-yr-old Josephine; or (2) a direct image walk of the Ward-1 Benton Park / Carondelet-Av sheets. Failing both, an AI handwriting index of the 1870 census (as already exists for the directories that cracked this case) remains the cleanest eventual route.

Image-walk executed (21 June 2026) — anchor found, household not yet located. The decisive anchor for the walk is William J. Lemp (the Western/Lemp Brewery owner), enumerated in 1870 Ward 1, NARA roll M593_810, page 92B, dwelling 139, image 4273861_00192 (occupation “Brewery,” $225k real estate) — his brewery sat at Cherokee & Carondelet, ~two blocks from Gate (Carondelet Av & Pestalozzi), so Charles should be in the same enumeration. Using Ancestry's per-sheet index panel (which lists every person's name/age/birthplace/occupation without handwriting-reading), I walked pages 91–99 (dwellings 134–200, ~360 households) straddling the brewery. Every household is a German/Swiss/Irish/Scandinavian working family — laborers, stone-cutters, masons, coopers, butchers, brewery hands — but no Charles + Auguste + Josephine cluster appears.

Key tell from the walk: across the whole Carondelet-Av/brewery stretch there is not a single streetcar conductor or driver — yet 718 Gate housed a whole cluster of St. Louis R.R. men (the McDonalds, Rose, Chisholm, and Charles himself). So 718 Gate's railroad-worker building is not in the brewery blocks beside Lemp; the streetcar cluster — and Charles — sits beside the St. Louis Railroad horse-car barn/stables.

Car-barn located (21 June 2026): the 1866–67 St. Louis directory lists “St. Louis Railroad Co. Stables, W. Carondelet av. near Arsenal” (southern division) — i.e. north along Carondelet Av from Lemp's brewery, toward the lower dwelling numbers / earlier census sheets. The streetcar-worker cluster (and 718 Gate) should sit near there.

Walk extended toward the stables — 16 sheets cleared (pages 84–99, dwellings 95–200, ~640 households). Heading north/back from Lemp the trades shift (teamsters Grewoer/Baha/Gerhause/Krisner; a wagon-driver; then mixed English/Irish — policeman, physician, plasterers, cigar-maker) but still no streetcar-conductor cluster and no Charles + Auguste + Josephine. The only “Charles Johnson” reached is the previously-eliminated German laborer at dwelling 109 (wife Minnie; daughters Barbara/Anna/Louisa; record M4XG-STX).

Entire subdivision walked — 24 sheets (images 4273861_00175–00199, dwellings 37–200, ~900 households): complete negative. The full first enumeration subdivision of 1870 Ward 1 has now been read end-to-end via the index panel. It contains exactly the expected neighbourhood — the Lemp brewery and its German workers; the wealthy Carondelet-Av merchant blocks (the DeMenil physician $400k, Anheuser the brewer, Bowman jewelers); and several scattered street-railway men (Frank Faggley “driver on light car,” — Conner “conductor,” Adam Shoer “car conductor,” John Sochrim “RR conductor”), confirming this is the right kind of district. But there is no Charles Gonson/Johnson with an Auguste and a Josephine anywhere in the subdivision.

What this means. The best-reasoned target subdivision — the one containing Gate Street's Carondelet-Av blocks, the brewery, and the only conductor cluster — does not contain the household. The remaining possibilities are: (a) the family sits in a different enumeration subdivision of Ward 1 (the roll runs to 678 images; this subdivision was only ~25 of them) or just over the line in Ward 2 near the Arsenal stables — i.e. a blind search of hundreds more sheets with no anchor to target; (b) the 1870 enumerator genuinely missed the household (the original “not in the indexed data” hypothesis); or (c) it is present but with given names mangled past recognition even in the images. Conclusion of the page-walk: the targeted, anchored walk is complete and negative; further walking is unanchored and open-ended. The clean finish remains an AI handwriting/full-text index of the 1870 census (the same tool class that cracked the directories), which would surface the household by content regardless of surname/given-name distortion. The case is documented to a precise, resumable state: man (Charles Gonson/Johnson, streetcar conductor), street (Gate = Carondelet Av near Pestalozzi, Benton Park, 1870 Ward 1), anchor (Lemp, dwelling 139, image 4273861_00192), car-barn (Carondelet Av near Arsenal, likely Ward 2), and the entire Ward-1 brewery subdivision (dwellings 37–200, 24 sheets) now cleared.

Revised conclusion (two-platform negative)

The household is not retrievable from the indexed 1870 census on either FamilySearch or Ancestry — not as a Charles+Augusta couple (both spouse directions), not via the child Josephine with both parents named, and not under any recognizable surname variant (Gonson/Gonnson/Gansen/Konsen/Jansen/Johnson). Because Ancestry's household-linked, ward-aware index also fails to surface them, the earlier "given-name pools too large" limitation is no longer the whole story: the family is simply not present in the index as a recognizably-named St. Louis household. The two live explanations are (a) the enumerator missed the household (consistent with the 1869–70 move to 718 Gates), or (b) the surname and/or given names were mistranscribed into forms no name search reaches. Either way, the record is now recoverable only by a manual page-walk of the Ward 2 / Ward 3 images (or a fresh OCR/full-text index of the 1870 census if one is published).

Charles Research — a multi-month transatlantic genealogical investigation into Charles Konzen (recorded in U.S. records as Gannsen / Johnson; 1835–1903), Hessian-born Civil War sergeant of Battery B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery, and his probable Trendelburg / Deisel origins in Churhessen (a working conclusion — the German baptism remains unrecovered).

      
Compiled by Jed Johnson with Peter Schräder and James Gunnis.
