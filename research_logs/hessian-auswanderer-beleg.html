Hessian Auswanderer-Beleg — Research Log

State-archive emigration registration · Hessisches Staatsarchiv Marburg · all searches negative · re-searched 25 May 2026
EVIDENCE NOT FOUND Marburg HStAM Best. 16 / Best. 17b

★ What this record would tell us if found

The Hessian state archive's emigration registry recorded — for legal emigrants — a structured set of biographical fields:

  • Home parish (Heimat) — would resolve the central geographic question of the entire investigation: which Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis village did Charles actually come from. Trendelburg? Deisel? Schöneberg? Hümme? Helmarshausen? Friedrichsfeld? An entry would name it.
  • Age and birth year — recorded by the state at the moment of registration, free from American clerks' approximations.
  • Profession — which would establish his pre-emigration trade (Schuhmacher / Tagelöhner / Bäcker / Schmied?). Comparable Belege for adjacent emigrants record this field consistently.
  • Religion — Lutheran / Reformed / Catholic / Jewish — a field worth recording for any emigrant.
  • Destination country — recorded as "Amerika" or specific U.S. state if known to the registrar.
  • Date of registration — usually 1-3 months before actual departure.
  • Reference to the underlying parish or local emigration list — chaining backward to even earlier records.

Its absence is itself diagnostic. If Charles is not in any Auswanderer-Beleg, that suggests his emigration was un-registered with the Hessian authorities — consistent with an illegal / unauthorised departure. See the Hessian Stammrolle research log for the linked draft-evasion hypothesis.

Why this record should exist (or not)

Kurhessen (Electorate of Hesse) required emigrants to register with the state before departing, especially if they had not yet fulfilled military obligation. The registers (Auswanderer-Belege) are held at Hessisches Staatsarchiv Marburg, primarily under Bestand 16 (Innenministerium) and Bestand 17b (regional administrative records). The 1853 Friedrichsfeld emigrant cohort (Quentin family) and the 1855 Schöneberg emigrant Johannes Grandjot are both fully recorded in this series, demonstrating that the system was functional. Charles's absence implies either: (a) he registered under a name spelling not yet tried; (b) his registration is in a Bestand not yet searched; or (c) he emigrated illegally without registering.

Search log — passes attempted

negative LAGIS Hessen-Auswanderer database

LAGIS Hessen-Auswanderer indexes ~80,000 documented Hesse emigrants. Surname variants searched:

  • Konze, Kontze, Konsen, Contze — zero 1850s emigrants matching profile
  • Gansen, Gannsen, Gansin — zero matches in the 1855-1860 window
  • Gonson, Gonnson, Gonsen — zero matches
  • Jansen, Janson — multiple unrelated matches; none with Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis origin in 1859

The LAGIS index is not exhaustive — many Belege exist only on paper at Marburg.

negative Marburg HStAM Best. 16 Nr. 3237

Periodische Übersichten der erfolgten Auswanderungen, Band 1, 1850–1853 — the printed periodical overview of legal emigrations. Spot-checked for the Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis 1850-1853 cohort. Charles is too late to appear (he emigrated 1859, this volume covers through 1853). The next volume (1854-1860+) would cover his window but has not been ordered.

collateral Comparable Belege found for adjacent emigrants

The same Marburg system carries detailed entries for emigrants who DID register, demonstrating the archive's coverage:

  • Johannes Grandjot (Schöneberg, 1855) — Beleg ID 19743. Profession: Schuhmacher. Date: 08/1855. Destination: Amerika. Attached as a Memory on his FS profile (PCXP-H6X).
  • 1853 Friedrichsfeld cohort — Auguste Quentin · Georg Karl Quentin (Bäcker / Konditor) · Friederike geb. Quentin × Karl Frickhöfer · Sophie Quent × Frickhöfer. All recorded in HStAM Best. 16 Nr. 3237, p. 110.
  • Meier Meierhof (Deisel, 1854) — surfaced in Marburg's 1854 emigrant lists (Peter Schräder discovery, 14 Oct 2025).

These adjacent finds set the floor of what the system records. Charles's absence under the same coverage is informative.

Re-search — 25 May 2026

All three online avenues were re-worked on 25 May 2026. The headline result is unchanged — no Auswanderer-Beleg for Charles — but the re-search closes one previously open gap and pins down specific archival signatures.

negative LAGIS / HESAUS re-search — full surname sweep

The LAGIS Hessische-Auswanderer database (HESAUS) has grown to 222,782 records — up from the ~80,000 indexed at the April 2026 pass — so a fresh sweep was warranted. Every surname in the project's variant pool was searched against the person-name field (truncated):

  • Konze — one genuine hit: Ludwig Konze, Deisel, October 1864, who emigrated to Frankfurt/Main, not America (HStAM Best. 180 Hofgeismar Nr. 841, Bl. 49; assets 600 fl.). This is the only Konze in the database and is not Charles.
  • Kontze · Kontzen · Contze · Contzen · Konzen · Konsen — zero each.
  • Gansen · Gannsen · Gonson · Gonnson · Gonsen · Jansen — zero each.
  • Janson — 34 hits, none matching: a Kassel-city Janson family (1860), Rheinhessen Jansons (1783–1841), and Maria Janson, Hofgeismar 1861 (already eliminated). No male of the surname pool born ~1835 emigrating ~1859 from the Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis.

The expanded database confirms the April 2026 negative: no member of the Konze / Gansen / Gonson / Konsen surname pool matching Charles's profile registered as a Hessian emigrant.

structural finding Arcinsys / HStAM Best. 16 — the periodical-overview gap is now closed

The April 2026 pass left the post-1853 periodical overview as an open gap (“successor volume not yet ordered”). The Arcinsys finding aid now resolves it. The series Periodische Übersichten über die stattgefundenen Auswanderungen (HStAM Best. 16) runs in four volumes:

  • Band 1 = Nr. 3237 (1850–1853)
  • Band 2 = Nr. 3238 (1854–1855)
  • Band 3 = Nr. 3239 (1856–1858)
  • Band 4 = Nr. 3243 (1858–1861) — the volume covering Charles's 1859 emigration window.

Band 4's Arcinsys record carries the note „Namen erfasst in der Datenbank HESAUS“ — its names are indexed in the same HESAUS database searched above — and the volume is digitised (Nutzungsdigitalisat online). The official Kurhessen periodical emigration overview for 1858–1861 is therefore already covered by the LAGIS surname sweep, which returned no Charles match. The gap the previous log flagged is closed: Charles is absent from the registered-emigrant overview for his window — consistent with the unregistered / unauthorised-departure hypothesis.

negative Ancestry & FamilySearch — no dedicated Hesse-Kassel emigration index

Ancestry's card catalogue holds no Hesse-Kassel emigration index — the authoritative one is HESAUS, which Ancestry does not carry. An immigration-category search of the Konzen surname pool (b. ~1835) surfaced only unrelated Rhineland Konzen (Antwerp departures, 1852) and Metz-area Konzen / Konzer — nothing matching Charles's 1859 / St. Louis profile. FamilySearch's immigration & naturalization records for the surname (1,586 entries) are likewise all U.S.-side passenger lists and naturalisations — none matching Charles — and FamilySearch carries no Hesse-Kassel emigration index of its own. (Two collateral, non-matching finds: Heinrich Philip Konze, born Deisel, naturalised New York 1906; and Louis Konze, naturalised St. Louis 1874.) The authoritative Hessian emigration index remains HESAUS / LAGIS.

Next paths to chase

  • HStAM Best. 16 periodical overview — now resolved. The 1858–1861 volume (Nr. 3243, Band 4) is digitised online and its names are indexed in HESAUS; it is covered by the 25 May 2026 surname sweep and need not be ordered separately.
  • HStAM Best. 180 Hofgeismar — district-office release files. The Hofgeismar Kreis / Landratsamt "Aufnahme und Entlassung der Untertanen" series holds individual emigration applications and release-from-citizenship files that are not fully indexed in HESAUS. (The Ludwig Konze 1864 Beleg sits here, Nr. 841.)
  • HStAM Bestand 17b — regional administrative emigration files. Regierung-Kassel paperwork that may not be aggregated into the periodical overview.
  • Direct paid query to Marburg — sent 25 May 2026. A letter requesting a search of Best. 180 Hofgeismar and Best. 17b for the Konze / Konsen / Gansen pool, 1856–1861 — and for any record of an unauthorised departure — was sent to the Hessisches Staatsarchiv Marburg (marburg@hla.hessen.de) on 25 May 2026 (Marburg_HStAM_Auswanderer_Query_May25_2026.md). Awaiting the archive's cost estimate / reply.
  • Re-search once a Stammrolle / parish entry locks in the surname — if either of the other two German-side searches surfaces Charles's actual home-parish surname, the Beleg search can be redone with the correct variant.
