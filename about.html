About this project

Who runs it, why, how to contact us, and how to cite the work.

This is a descendant-led, source-rigorous transatlantic genealogy investigation. It is not affiliated with any university or genealogical society. All sources are listed; all conclusions are held in the project’s explicit Confirmed / Working / Open / Eliminated taxonomy.

Commissioned researchers — AncestryProGenealogists (2020)

Paul A. Schmidt & Brian A. Podoll

Professional genealogists who conducted the original February–June 2020 commissioned Hesse research — the AncestryProGenealogists Research Journal that opened the investigation. They proposed the Deisel reading of the marriage record’s “Deisse,” searched the Deisel baptisms (1834–1841) for the Gonnson / Johnson surname forms, surfaced and ruled out two Konze candidates, and dismissed the Freudenberg / Schierstein reading of “Freundburg.”

Engagement completed 2020; the investigation has been carried forward by the researchers above.

Why this site exists

Charles never named his exact German parish on any U.S. record. The closest he came was “Deisse, Kreis Cassel, Churhessen” on his 1866 marriage register — phonetically the village of Deisel, in the Kreis Hofgeismar of the former Electorate of Hesse-Kassel. The specific German baptismal entry recording his birth has never been recovered, despite multi-year searches across every digitised parish register in the surrounding 5 km × 5 km area.

This site exists to (a) make the existing evidence base auditable to other researchers, (b) document each candidate parish that has been searched and what was searched there, and (c) hold open a public locus where descendants can find and contribute to the work. The site replaces ad-hoc PDFs and email threads with a citable, versioned, permanent record.

Declared interests

Family relationship. Jed Johnson is a direct descendant of Charles. Where evidence is ambiguous the site notes the ambiguity in the surrounding text rather than collapsing it; the project’s working / confirmed / open / eliminated taxonomy is intended to insulate against the descendant’s natural pressure toward a tidy answer.

Research compensation. Peter Schräder is a paid genealogist; his German-side work has been compensated by Jed Johnson on a per-project basis since November 2025.

Funding. No external funding. Archion subscriptions, NARA mail-order fees, and other primary-source costs are paid out of pocket by Jed Johnson.

How to cite this site

Recommended — NGSQ-style Johnson, Jed, with Peter Schräder and James Gunnis. "Charles Research: A Genealogical Investigation." Version 1.1.0, last modified 29 May 2026. https://charles-research.netlify.app/. Accessed [DATE OF ACCESS]. Chicago author-date Johnson, Jed, with Peter Schräder and James Gunnis. 2026. "Charles Research: A Genealogical Investigation." Version 1.1.0. Accessed [DATE]. https://charles-research.netlify.app/. Plain prose Charles Research (v1.1.0, 29 May 2026), by Jed Johnson with Peter Schräder and James Gunnis, accessed [DATE], at https://charles-research.netlify.app/.

For the formal one-page summary suitable for footnoting in a journal article, cite the Abstract. For specific evidence claims, cite the relevant Key Evidence card by its number and date; the Claims Dashboard is the canonical inventory of project-level claims with their evidence status. A persistent DOI for the project is in progress (see Zenodo deposit, planned May 2026).
