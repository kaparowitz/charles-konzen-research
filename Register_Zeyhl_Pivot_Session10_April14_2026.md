## 1. Key finding — Zeyhl is NOT a standalone parish in Kirchenkreis Hofgeismar

Pulled the complete parish list for **Kirchenkreis Hofgeismar** (the church district containing Trendelburg) direct from Archion's Landeskirchliches Archiv der Evangelischen Kirche von Kurhessen-Waldeck. The full set of parishes is:

> Arenborn, Bad Karlshafen (four sub-congregations), Beberbeck, Burguffeln, Calden, Carlsdorf, **Deisel**, Eberschütz, Ehrsten, Ersen, Friedrichsfeld, Fürstenwald, Gewissenruh, Gieselwerder, Gieselwerder–Oedelsheim, Gottsbüren, Gottstreu, Grebenstein, Griemelsheim, Haueda, Heisebeck, Helmarshausen, Hofgeismar (five sub-congregations), Hohenkirchen, Holzhausen, Hombressen, Hümme, Hümme–Stammen, Immenhausen, Kelze, Knickhagen, Lamerden, Langenthal, Liebenau, Lippoldsberg, Mariendorf, Meimbressen, Mönchehof, Münchehof, Niedermeiser, Obermeiser, Oedelsheim, Ostheim, Schachten, Schöneberg, **Sielen**, Stammen, **Trendelburg**, Udenhausen, Vaake, Veckerhagen, Vernawahlshausen, Westuffeln, Wilhelmshausen, Wilhelmsthal, Zwergen.

**There is no "Zeyhl" parish.** Therefore **Zeyhl is a filial/hamlet annexed to one of the above mother-parishes** — or a dialect/archaic spelling for one of the listed places. Given geographic proximity to Trendelburg and the fact that Andreas Sr. appears casually at a Trendelburg baptism as godparent, the candidate set is small:

## 3. Hypothesis for "Zeyhl" as a place-name variant

Reviewing the Kurrent-script reading from Register 220093 image 765 (November 1745 Trendelburg baptism, godparent entry), "Zeyhl" does not phonetically map cleanly to any Kirchenkreis Hofgeismar parish. Three possibilities remain open:

1. **Zeyhl = a filial / Vorwerk / estate** within Sielen or Deisel parish boundaries. Historic Hessian gazetteer lookups (Meyers Orts- und Verkehrs-Lexikon; GOV Geschichtliches Ortsverzeichnis; Hessisches Historisches Ortslexikon) should be consulted for a hamlet spelled Zeyhl / Zehl / Zeyl / Zell in the Trendelburg-Hofgeismar corridor.
2. **Zeyhl = scribal shorthand for another listed parish.** Unlikely for "Sielen" (initial S in Kurrent is unmistakable) but plausible for a small Ortsteil abbreviation.
3. **Zeyhl = outside Kirchenkreis Hofgeismar.** If Andreas Sr. moved across the Weser into Westphalia (Paderborn/Höxter side), the register would be in a different archive (Paderborn Archdiocese for Catholic, or Landeskirchliches Archiv Westfalen for Protestant Weserbergland).
