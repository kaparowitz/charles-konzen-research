# Maria Josephine Gonnson / Josephine Herr (LTSG-XWW) — Life Sketch

Josephine Maria Johnson was born Maria Josephine Gonnson on 20 February 1868 in St. Louis, Missouri, the second of seven children of Charles Johnson Sr. — a Hessian-born immigrant and Civil War artillery sergeant — and Auguste Kreichelt, born in Clausthal, Hanover, and the first of their children to survive infancy. She was baptized 7 June 1868 at St. Marcus German Evangelical Church (register p. 224, no. 5857). Her sponsors were Jacob Reck, a Pfalz-born family friend later buried near her father at Old St. Marcus Cemetery, and his first wife Josephine née von Delien — whose name the infant carried — along with Maria Agnes Götz and her grandfather Georg Kreichelt. The clerk wrote the surname "Gonnson," as at her brother's 1866 baptism.

On 8 April 1886, at age eighteen, she married Edward "Ed" (Eduard) Herr (1866–1947), a St. Louis-born German-American. They had three sons — Edward (1886–1887), August "Jacob" (1887–1888), and Conrad (1889–1905) — and in her short married life Josephine knew the same grief her parents had known, losing her first two boys in infancy.

Josephine died 5 May 1890 in St. Louis, twenty-two years old, and was buried 7 May 1890 at Old St. Marcus Cemetery. The St. Marcus death register entered her as "Josephine, née Konzen, wife of Eduard Herr" — preserving the Hessian form of the family surname and becoming key evidence in tracing the family's origins. Her death opened the family's hardest year: her sister Auguste died that same December, and their mother buried two grown daughters in 1890. Her surviving son Conrad died at fifteen in 1905; Edward outlived her by fifty-seven years.
