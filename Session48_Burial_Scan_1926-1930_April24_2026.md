## Notable Observations

### Two Konze Burials in 1929

Both Konze entries are from 1929 — a remarkable coincidence after no confirmed direct Konze burials in the 1919–1928 period (with the exception of Entry 953 in 1923, which was the wife of Johann Ludwig Konze). The 10-year gap between the last male Konze death (Heinrich Conze Ackermann, d. 1919) and Philipp Georg Konze (d. 1929) confirms the generational gap hypothesis from Session 47. Philipp Georg, born 1840, was of the older generation but his exceptional longevity (88 years) pushed his death into the late 1920s.

### New Patriarch Names — Philipp Georg and Andreas

The given names "Philipp Georg" are entirely new in the Konze patriarch registry. All previously identified patriarchs were named Heinrich, Ludwig, Johann Ludwig, or some combination thereof. The appearance of "Philipp Georg" and "Andreas" suggests a distinct branch of the Konze family that used different naming patterns. House No. 38 is also new — previously identified Konze house numbers were 50, 94, 100, 141, and others.

### Heinrich Ludwig Konze — Next Generation

Käte Konze's father, Heinrich Ludwig Konze at No. 156, represents a younger generation of Konze patriarchs. With a daughter born in 1909, Heinrich Ludwig was likely born around 1870–1885, making him a contemporary of the "younger Heinrichs" identified in earlier sessions. His dual given name "Heinrich Ludwig" could reflect naming traditions from both the Heinrich and Ludwig branches of the family.

### Köster Family Dominance in Connected Entries

Of the 10 connected family entries, 5 involve the Köster family — an extraordinary concentration. Three Köster entries involve wives née Köster (married to non-Köster men), while two involve born Köster women. The Köster-Thiele intermarriage pattern is notable: Heinrich Thiele married Marie née Köster, while Elise Thiele married Ludwig Köster. These two families were deeply intertwined.

### 1928 — Unusually Low Death Count

Only 8 deaths in 1928 (5 male, 3 female) — the lowest annual total yet observed. For comparison: 1923 had 19, 1924 had 19, and 1929 had 13+. The low 1928 count may reflect a healthy year or a demographic shift in Deisel's population.

### Register Format Change

Starting from 1926 (Image 173), the year header format changes: the preprinted "Jahr 18" is struck through and replaced with handwritten "9/26", "927", "928", etc. — reflecting the transition from 18xx to 19xx years. The month range headers also become more descriptive (e.g., "Januar bis August", "November und Dezember").
