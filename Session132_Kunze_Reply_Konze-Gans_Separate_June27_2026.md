# Session 132 — Klaus Kunze's reply: Konze ≠ Gans, Ganson = Jansen, no 26 Jan 1835 birth

**Date:** 27 June 2026
**Trigger:** Klaus Kunze (HeiKun-Verlag, genealogist of the Oberweser OSBs) answered Jed's
27 Jun query about the Konze/Gans cross-links in his online Ortssippenbuch registers
(see Session 127). His reply resolves several open Session-127 items **without buying the books.**

---

## The exchange

- **Jed → Kunze (27 Jun, 07:10):** asked whether (1) Helmarshausen families Nr. 67 and 302 appear
  under *both* "Konze" and "Gans" because the name is spelled two ways (variant link), or (2) because
  a Konze married a née Gans (intermarriage); whether he treats Konze/Kontze and Gans/Ganß as one
  name or two; and whether Lippoldsberg's "Ganson" (186) is a local variant of Gans.
- **Kunze → Jed (27 Jun, 13:18):** answered all three.

## What Kunze said (harvested)

1. **"Kontze und Gans sind ganz verschiedene Familien."** Konze and Gans are **completely different
   families** — *not* spelling variants of one name. ⇒ The dual family numbers in the Helmarshausen
   index (67, 302 under both) reflect **intermarriage** (caveat option 2 from Session 127), **not** a
   "Konze = Gans" variant equivalence.
2. **"genannt"/alias double-names were not common here** ("nicht die große Mode"). An illegitimate
   son might later take his father's surname, but **no hyphenated/alias double surnames**. ⇒ removes
   the "Konze genannt Gans" reading.
3. **"Ganson"** (the American-looking spelling, Lippoldsberg 186) is **derived from / a variant of
   Janson = Jansen**, NOT of Gans. It appears **only once, in the 18th century.** ⇒ Ganson is a red
   herring for the Gans pool; if it matters at all it points to a **Jansen/Janson** line.
4. **Variant pools are internal, not cross-pool:** "Ganß" is just a spelling of Gans; likewise
   **"Conze, Contzen, Konte etc." are spelling variants of each other** (the Konze pool). The two
   pools do not merge.
5. **Birth date 26.1.1835 = Fehlanzeige** ("nichts") **in ALL his villages** — i.e. across
   Helmarshausen, Lippoldsberg, Karlshafen, Gieselwerder, Oedelsheim and Sielen at once.
6. His OSB **name-registers point to page numbers**, so a surname landing on the same family number
   under two headings can be **coincidental pagination** — "da gab es keinen Zusammenhang."
7. A **Gans ∞ Contze marriage in the 17th century** exists but "nützt Ihnen nichts" (too early).

## Why this matters for the project

- It **refutes, from an expert source, the Konze = Gans equivalence** that Session 127 flagged as
  "at the heart of the Gannsen origin hypothesis." Konze and Gans are distinct Oberweser families;
  the index cross-links are intermarriage, not variant spelling. The American surname cluster
  (Gannsen / Gansen / Gonsen …) therefore does **not** get a documentary bridge to Konze via Gans.
- The **"Ganson" lead** (Lippoldsberg 186) is closed for the Gans pool; redirects to Jansen/Janson.
- A clean **negative on 26 Jan 1835 across all six Kunze-OSB parishes** — useful elimination,
  consistent with the Archion index sweeps already on file for these parishes.
- **Resolves Session-127 open items** §6: the Helmarshausen 67/302 variant-vs-intermarriage question
  (→ intermarriage) and the Lippoldsberg "Ganson" question (→ Jansen, one 18th-c. occurrence).

## Caveat on the negative

Kunze's "Fehlanzeige" is keyed to the **exact date 26.1.1835**, which is itself a US-reported figure
and may be off by a year or two. His coverage is the **Oberweser/Weser** parishes; the project's
documentary Konze evidence (the "geb. Konzen" mothers) sits in the **Diemel valley**
(Deisel / Trendelburg / Hümme), largely **outside** his OSB coverage (Helmarshausen excepted).
