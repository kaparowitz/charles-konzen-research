## Headline

Session 25 achieves the most significant breakthrough in the search for Charles Gannsen's birth record. **High-resolution re-reading of Deisel Register 218842 (Image 40) reveals that BOTH Entry 194 AND Entry 195 are Konze family births** — not one, but TWO male Konze children born in Deisel in January 1835, the exact month and year of Charles L. Gannsen's stated birth date (January 26, 1835).

Session 24 had partially read these entries but failed to identify the father's surname as "Konze" — misreading the Kurrent capital K as a D. The user (Jed) corrected this reading, identifying both entries as Konze and noting that Entry 195's parents are **Heinrich Konze and Wilhelmine Hildebrant**, a known couple. This correction was verified against the clearly readable wife's maiden name "geb. Hildebrand" in Entry 195.

## Implications for Charles Gannsen (b. January 26, 1835)

### Which entry could be Charles?

**Entry 195 is the stronger candidate** for several reasons:

1. **Birth date**: Entry 194's child was baptized January 18, suggesting a birth around Jan 5–10 — about 2–3 weeks BEFORE Charles's stated birth date of January 26. Entry 195's exact birth date needs verification but could be closer to January 26.

2. **Child name**: Entry 194's child is named "Ludwig Friedrich" — these are not the names associated with Charles. Entry 195's child name is still unclear and NEEDS VERIFICATION — it could potentially be "Carl" or "Justus" or another name that could become "Charles."

3. **Notes column**: Entry 195 appears to have a marginal note that needs reading — this could contain death/emigration information or other critical data.

4. **Father's occupation**: Heinrich Konze was a Kaufmann (merchant), which is a higher-status occupation than Ackermann (farmer). This is consistent with the resources needed for emigration to America.

5. **Known family**: The user recognized "Heinrich Konze and Wilhelmine Hildebrant" from other research, suggesting this family has been encountered in other records.

### However, Entry 194 cannot be ruled out:
- The child "Ludwig Friedrich" could have used a different name in America
- The birth date discrepancy (2–3 weeks) could reflect imprecise records or a deliberate alteration
