## 1. Lippoldsberg — full Gans baptism reconstruction 1831–1836 (reg. 219778)
Read every baptism year 1831–1836 leaf by leaf (1831 = Bild 1416–1419; 1832 =
1420–1424; 1833 = 1425–1428; 1834 = 1429–1434; 1835 = 1435–1442; 1836 =
1443–1448), each closed by its year-end "Summa" tally.

**Five Gans households (day-labourer/weaver clan):**
- **Johann Wilhelm Gans** (Leineweber) × Anna Maria née Spierlmann → **Carl Eduard Gans** b. ~1 May 1831 (Bild 1417), **d. 1832**.
- **Benjamin Gans** (Tagelöhner) × Charlotte née Gans → **Heinrich Wilhelm Gans** b. ~1 Jun 1831 (Bild 1417) → 1846 confirmand.
- **Joh. Christian Gans** (Leineweber/Tagelöhner) × Marie Wilhelmine → **Heinrich Gans** (twin, co-twin stillborn, Bild 1419, Nov 1831) + **August Gans** b. 12 Jun 1835 (Bild 1439), d. 26 Jun 1835.
- **Johannes Gans** (Tagelöhner) × Justine Henriette → **Konrad Gans** b. 25 Jun 1832 (Bild 1422) → 1846 confirmand; + **Justine W. F. C. Gans** (girl) b. 1 Mar 1835 (Bild 1437), d. 13 Apr 1835.
- **Johann Daniel Gans** (Tagelöhner) × Karoline née Haßler → **Friedrich Wilhelm Gans** b. 24 Jun 1835 (Bild 1440) → 1849 confirmand.

**Bearing on Charles:** 1833, 1834 and 1836 have **no Gans baptism at all**. The
forename Carl appears in the clan exactly once — **Carl Eduard Gans (1831, d. 1832)**;
no replacement Carl was ever baptised. **No Carl Gans b. 26 Jan 1835**, and no Gans
in the Jan–Feb 1835 opening. Lippoldsberg = real Gans clan, no direct Charles match.
Detail: `germany/lippoldsberg-research-log.html`.

## 2. Bodenfelde — cross-Weser Gans check (Landeskirche Hannovers, Kr. Northeim)
The one un-searched neighbour whose Kunze OSB carries **Gans/Ganß**. Records are in
the Hannover archive (Kirchenkreis Uslar), not Kurhessen-Waldeck.

- **Baptisms — Kirchenbuch 1794–1839, reg. 359334.** The **entire 1835 baptism year**
  read leaf by leaf (images 334–340, pages 506–512; ~40 baptisms), plus the 1834-end
  and 1836-start boundaries. **No Gans baptism anywhere in 1835** — striking because
  "Carl" is one of the most common forenames in the parish (Carl August Braune,
  Carl Christian Conrad Ehle, Carl Friedrich Meier, Carl Friedrich Wilhelm Röpeler,
  Carl Wilhelm Eduard Jürgens …). Nearest near-miss = **Carl August Braune, b. 29 Jan 1835**
  (3 days off Charles's date; surname Braune, not Gans).
  *(Note: a later re-read placed the 1835 Taufen at images ~320–324 — the volume holds an
  Erst- and a Zweitschrift of the 1830s; both copies read the same, negative.)*
- **Familienbuch — reg. 359344.** Alphabetical family-head index at the end. The whole
  **G section = Giesing (Fam. 15), Götte (5/25/71), Grote (46) — no "Gans" household head.**
  The surname appears once, as a maiden name: **"Henne, Heinrich … geb. Gans" (Fam. 2)** —
  a Gans daughter married into the Henne family. (Index column order irregular; a Ga/Ge
  sub-column was hard to resolve, so a confirming pass / the Kartei Männer reg. 359346
  could double-check.)

**Conclusion:** No Carl Gans b. 1835 in Bodenfelde; by the 19th c. "Gans" was not an
active household there, only a maiden name. Bodenfelde closed as a candidate. Detail:
`germany/bodenfelde-research-log.html`.

## 3. Oedelsheim emigration lead — confirmed, and the decisive next step
The LAGIS "Hessische Auswanderer" record was re-opened field-by-field and confirms:
**"Gans, Georg Friedrich, Oedelsheim, 1834"** (LAGIS ID 110864 →
https://lagis.hessen.de/resolve/de/ha/110864) → destination **Nordamerika**,
**4 persons**, Vermögen **110**, source **HStAM Best. 16 Nr. 3216, S. 199** (Marburg).
The record names only the head + the count of four.

Open-web research (28 Jun pass) for the US side returned **dead ends behind paywalls,
not negatives**: Ancestry/FamilySearch passenger and census indexes are login-gated, and
*Germans to America* only begins 1840 (an 1834 arrival pre-dates it). No free-web hit for
a Gans family of 4 arriving 1834–36, a Carl/Charles Gans b. ~1835, or a Missouri
Gans/Gannsen household. Surname-distribution note: modern "Gansen" clusters in the
Rhineland, *not* Hesse — consistent with "Gannsen" being a US-anglicised Hessian "Gans/Ganß".

**Decisive next steps (in order):**
1. **Pull Sgt. "Gannsen / Johnson"'s Civil War pension & compiled service file** (Battery B,
   2nd Missouri Light Artillery, organised St. Louis Jan 1862) from NARA / Fold3 — a pension
   file usually states birthplace and parents, which would confirm or kill the Oedelsheim-Gans
   origin outright. **This is the single most decisive action.**
2. Order **HStAM Best. 16 Nr. 3216, S. 199** from Marburg (Arcinsys) to name the four 1834 emigrants.
3. Ancestry/FamilySearch (Jed's login): a Gans/Ganß/Ganss household of ~4 arriving Bremen/Le Havre →
   NY/Baltimore/New Orleans ~1834–36; and an 1850/1860 census Carl/Charles Gans b. ~1835 in Missouri.

Detail: `germany/oedelsheim-research-log.html`.

## 4. Logged-in Ancestry/FamilySearch pass — Oedelsheim "born-in-US" branch CLOSED
With Jed logged into Ancestry + FamilySearch, the US side was finally checkable.

**Reframing from Jed's own FamilySearch profile (LZXC-6GB, "Charles Johnson Sr."):**
- Charles **immigrated 1859** (1900 census) and **emigrated alone** (pension file: only
  comrades/friends vouch, no relatives). So he was **German-born ~1835**, not US-born.
- Leading origin (project working conclusion): **Konzen of Deisel**, Kreis Hofgeismar,
  Kurhessen — son's 1875 St. Marcus baptism "Erwin Carl **Konzen**"; daughter's 1890 death
  "geb. Konzen"; 1866 marriage "aus **Deisse** [=Deisel]"; 1862 enlistment "Freundburg"
  (decoded Trendelburg). The Konze surname is hyper-local to a 5-parish Diemel cluster
  (Trendelburg, Deisel, Friedrichsfeld, Stammen, Huemme).
- Profile note **"Deisel Village Hausname 'Gans'"**: Deisel residents historically carried
  the informal Hausname **"Gans"** — which neatly reconciles the American "Gannsen/Gansen"
  with a legal surname Konzen. (Hausname Gans ≠ a separate hereditary Gans family.)

**Closing the Oedelsheim/Weser-Gans-emigration branch (the task Jed picked):**
- The Ancestry "1834" hit for **Georg Friedrich Gans** is **not** a US arrival manifest —
  it is Kurt Günther, *Hessian Emigrants to America* (Filby coll. 7486, p. 375; same
  emigration event as LAGIS). It records the family leaving as **"Wife & 3 children"** in
  1834 — a complete unit, no room for a not-yet-born Charles.
- Charles **arrived 1859, alone** → he is not one of the 3 children who left in 1834, and the
  "born in the US ~1835" variant is impossible.
- **No Carl/Charles Gans b. ~1835 (Germany)** appears in U.S./Missouri census records.
- The on-site pension file names **no German birthplace or parents**, so it can't revive the lead.

**Net:** the Weser-valley Gans-emigration theory is **ruled out**. Effort should return to the
project's standing leading hypothesis — **Konzen of Deisel** — and the genuinely missing records:
Charles's **1859 arrival manifest** (likely New Orleans → up the Mississippi) and his **German
baptism** in the Deisel/Trendelburg cluster or the *undigitised* Landeskirchliches Archiv Kassel
registers (pre-1845 Heisebeck, pre-1822 Schoeneberg). Optional completeness: order HStAM Best. 16
Nr. 3216 S. 199 (Marburg) to name the four 1834 emigrants.
