Hümme — parish south of Trendelburg

Searched · negative · marriage register 1815–1840 verified in Kurrent script; no Konze marriage match

Historical overview

Hümme is a small village in the Esse valley, between Trendelburg and Hofgeismar, with documented mention from the 12th century. By the 19th century it was a typical Hessian agricultural village of ~400–500 inhabitants. The village had its own Lutheran parish church and parish records, but the population was small enough that emigration patterns are easily traced.

The marriage register volume 1815–1840 is on Archion. The Kurrent-script verification (Session 61) involved cross-checking the difficult late-Kurrent handwriting against transcriptions to ensure the negative finding for the Konze marriage was accurate.

Hümme passed to Prussia along with Kurhessen in September 1866 and became part of Stadt Hofgeismar in 20th-century administrative consolidations.

Significance to the investigation

Hümme was one of seven parishes searched for a Joh. Heinrich Konze × Wilhelmine Hildebrand marriage — a parent pair drawn from the Entry 195 reading later retracted (corrected 4 May 2026). The marriage register 1815–1840 was searched and Kurrent-verified (Session 61, 25 April 2026); no Konze × Hildebrand marriage record exists. The Kurrent verification was important because earlier readings of the Hümme register had returned ambiguous results that needed careful handwriting analysis to confirm as negative.
