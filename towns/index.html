Towns of the Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis

A gazetteer of the ten places in the German-side investigation — northern Kurhessen
10 TOWNS · KURHESSEN · NORTH OF KASSEL
Looking for where the search stands? This page is a geographic reference — what each place is, where it sits, and the per-town index of research notes. All search status — candidate rankings, open gaps, and eliminated parishes — lives on the Hessian Parish Map, which tracks all 29 investigated parishes and is kept current.

The setting — the Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis

The investigation has concentrated on a small cluster of villages and parishes in the Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis — the Lutheran ecclesiastical district covering the northwestern corner of former Kurhessen, just north of Kassel. The core places

These ten are the central places of the investigation — each has a full page with geography, parish history, register holdings, and visiting links. Hover a tile to highlight its pin on the map above. The wider search ring below lists every other parish on the map. For the current research status of any parish, see the Hessian Parish Map.

Trendelburg

Hilltop castle town on the Diemel; the parish church for Deisel and surrounding villages. Documented Kontze residence 1773–1830s — multiple generations of Rathsverwandte.

Deisel

The village Charles named at his 1866 St. Louis marriage ("aus Deisse, Kreis Cassel, Churhessen" — "Deisse" decoded phonetically to Deisel). Three "geb. Konzen" mothers attested 1828–1829; churched from Trendelburg.

Friedrichsfeld

Small colonist village just west of Trendelburg. Home of the documented Andreas Kontze line (b. 1795 Trendelburg).

Helmarshausen

Former monastery town northeast of Deisel, on the Diemel just above Bad Karlshafen. Famed medieval scriptorium.

Hümme

Parish village south of Trendelburg, between Deisel and Hofgeismar.

Hofgeismar

Seat of the Kirchenkreis and the district's administrative centre, with two parishes — Altstadt and Neustadt.

Schöneberg

Parish near Hofgeismar. Post-1822 volumes digitized on Archion; pre-1822 registers held at the Landeskirchliches Archiv Kassel.

Heisebeck

Parish at the northeastern edge of the search ring. Marriages 1808–1921 digitized on Archion; baptisms after 1831 undigitised, held at the Landeskirchliches Archiv Kassel.

Sielen

Small village northeast of Trendelburg, mentioned in several Konze-related register entries.

Kassel

The Kurhessen capital. Site of the Landeskirchliches Archiv and the city archive; the state archive HStAM is in Marburg.

The wider search ring — every other parish on the map

Beyond the ten core places, the investigation searched a wider ring of adjacent Kreis-Hofgeismar parishes, the cross-border Catholic parishes of Prussian Westphalia, and a set of far-south place-name alternatives. Each now has its own page with a research summary and a per-parish research log. Status reflects the Hessian Parish Map.

Diemel valley & adjacent Kreis Hofgeismar

Stammen

~2 km east. A distinct Stammen Kontze branch documented; 1835 entry a stillborn male — not Charles. The burial register (220072) is a key open offline test.

Gottsbüren

~4 km SE. A claimed but unconfirmed Kontze tie — the village’s own books are entirely Kontze-free. 1835 negative.

Haueda

~6 km WNW. Filial with its own Taufbuch from 1830; 1835 males all legitimate. Eliminated.

Lamerden

~5 km NW. Jan–Feb 1835 baptisms read by eye — negative. Eliminated.

Eberschütz

~5 km NNE. One illegitimate male verified not a match. Eliminated.

Hombressen

~7 km SW. Legitimate males only; the one illegitimate birth female. Eliminated.

Oberweser / Gieselwerder

~7 km east. Oberweser records held under Gieselwerder; 1835 negative. Eliminated.

Ostheim

~9 km WSW. One stillborn male (not a candidate); otherwise negative. Eliminated.

Bad Karlshafen

~9 km north. Both confessions searched; one illegitimate male legitimized 1838 — not Charles. Eliminated.

Niedermeiser

~10 km SW. No male baptism in the 1835 window at all. Eliminated.

Hofgeismar-Kelze

~11 km SW. Huguenot colony parish; only window baptism female. Eliminated.

Gewissenruh

~12 km NE. Tiny Huguenot parish; no January 1835 baptism at all. Eliminated.

Liebenau (+ Grimelsheim)

~13 km SW. Open — Archion holds only to 1777; the 1835 books are not digitised anywhere.

Grebenstein

~14 km south. One illegitimate January birth, female; no Konze-pool male. Eliminated.

Veckerhagen

~15 km SE. One out-of-window illegitimate male, unrelated. Eliminated.

Immenhausen

~16 km south. All 1835 males legitimate. Eliminated.

Marzhausen

~40 km SE (Kirchenkreis Witzenhausen). Checked for completeness — negative. Eliminated.

Cross-border Catholic parishes (Prussian Westphalia)

Herstelle / Würgassen

~8 km NNW. First cross-border parish (Erzbistum Paderborn), searched via Matricula — all ehelich. Eliminated.

Dalhausen

~10 km NW. A striking 26 Jan 1835 near-miss (F. W. Sievers) — legitimate, died in Germany 1908. Eliminated.

Beverungen

~11 km north. Five window males, all ehelich, each accounted for in Germany. Eliminated.

Tier-3 “Deissen” & far-south toponym checks

Gudensberg

~42 km S, near Kassel. Tier-3 “Deissen” place-name candidate; 1850 confirmation class read — negative.

Dissen

~40 km S. Tier-3 “Deissen” place-name candidate; searched — negative.

Grifte

~37 km S (Edermünde). Tier-3 “Deissen” place-name candidate; searched — negative.

Diez

~135 km S (Lahn). Hessen-Nassau toponym alternative for “Deisse”; negative.

Dies

~135 km S (Rhein-Lahn). Hessen-Nassau toponym alternative; negative.

Schierstein

~170 km S (Wiesbaden). Tested the “Freudenberg / Schierstein” Rhine reading; negative.

Mainz

~175 km S. Where Andreas Kontze died 1854 — the family’s southern settlement, not a birth parish. Eliminated.

Surname variants in the Trendelburg parish

The same family appears under multiple spellings

Archion's surname index for the K-section explicitly cross-references all of the variants below as one family:

Konze · Kontze · Kontzen · Contze · Konzen — the five Hesse-attested forms, with parish cross-reference also adding Cortze · Cordes · Contz. The American records add Konsen (1875 baptism, closest phonetic capture but not itself Hesse-attested) and a direct attestation of the full Hessian z-form Konzen on the 1890 daughter's death record.

(plus the distinct Jungkontze "junior" Hausname branch)

Tools used across all parish searches

The same handful of databases and archives are queried for every village. Each individual town page lists town-specific links inline; this is the consolidated reference list.

Parish records

Archion

The German Evangelical Church's parish-register portal. Subscription-based; covers most digitized Hesse-Nassau parishes.

archion.de ↗
Emigration register

LAGIS Hessen Auswanderer

The Hessian state's online catalogue of documented emigrants from Kurhessen and Hessen-Darmstadt (~1830–1900).

LAGIS Auswanderer ↗
State archive

Arcinsys Hessen

The Hessian state archives' online finding aid. Holdings include HStAM Marburg (Kurhessen records) and HHStAW Wiesbaden.

arcinsys.hessen.de ↗
Civil registers

Standesämter (post-1874)

Imperial Germany introduced civil registration on 1 January 1875 (Prussian Personenstandsgesetz of 9 March 1874). Birth/marriage/death records from 1874+ are in local Standesamt offices, with copies in regional state archives.

FamilySearch wiki ↗
Population schedules

Kurhessen Volkszählung

Kurhessen conducted population counts in 1846, 1855, 1858, 1861, and 1864. Most pre-1871 schedules are at HStAM Marburg.

HStAM Marburg ↗
German newspapers

Casseler Zeitung

Cassel/Kassel-based papers from the 1820s onward carried death notices, emigration announcements, and police reports of "auswandering ohne Genehmigung."

Universität Kassel digitized newspapers ↗
Cemeteries

BillionGraves & Find a Grave

Most surviving 19th-century Hessian-Lutheran burials were eventually cleared as cemetery rotations advanced; surviving stones are spotty.

Find a Grave ↗ BillionGraves ↗
Bremen passenger lists

Bremen Passenger Lists Database

Almost all original Bremen passenger lists were destroyed during WWII. Pre-1920 manifests survive only via U.S. arrival records.

passengerlists.de ↗
