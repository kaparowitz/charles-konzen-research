Deisel — Ortsteil von Trendelburg

★ The contested candidate village · "of Deisse, Kreis Cassel, Churhessen" — Charles's own 1866 St. Marcus marriage record
★ CONTESTED CANDIDATE Trendelburg parish Kreis Hofgeismar

Project status★ Contested candidate. Charles's 1866 St. Marcus marriage register entry names "Deisse" as his place of origin. Two Konze male births in January 1835 surfaced here. Schräder's 12 April 2026 verdict closes the legitimate-Konze-son hypothesis but leaves the surname-link unexplained.

The 1866 St. Marcus marriage register entry — the documentary anchor

"Carl Gonnson, aus Deisse, Kreis Cassel, Churhessen, und Clausthal, Königr. Hannover"

— St. Marcus German Evangelical Church, St. Louis, Missouri · marriage register 1865–1867, image 72, entry No. 676 · 7 January 1866 · officiating pastor Rev. Geo. W. Wall

This is the only documentary instance where Charles himself names a German place of origin. The 2020 AncestryProGenealogists journal proposed reading Deisse as a phonetic / clerical-shorthand for Deisel, a real village in Kreis Hofgeismar (the "Cassel" reference being administrative — the village fell under the broader Kreis Cassel jurisdiction at the time). That reading anchored seven months of subsequent investigation. The Hannoverian half of the entry refers to Auguste Kreichelt's birthplace at Clausthal in Hannover.

Historical overview

Deisel is a small agricultural village in the Diemel valley, ~2 km south of Trendelburg. It has no separate Lutheran church — historically and to this day, Deisel residents attended church and recorded their baptisms, marriages, and deaths in the Trendelburg parish register. The village had perhaps 150–200 households in the early 19th century, organized around a small core of Ackerbürger families with names that recur across the parish-register pages: Kontze, Hildebrand, Hofeditz, Köster, Schildknecht, Bonhagen, Steitz, Hund.

By the 1820s–1830s, several Konze branches had established farms in Deisel. A January 1835 Konze baptism (Entry 194) names Andreas Konze, Ackermann as the father. The neighbouring Entry 195 in the same volume (KB 218842) was at one point read by machine HTR as a Konze emergency baptism, but Peter Schräder's 4 May 2026 re-reading of the original Kurrent script corrected it: the father's surname is Köster (a Tagelöhner / day-labourer), not Konze; the child received no recorded first name and died three days after birth, without baptism. Entry 195 is therefore eliminated as a Konze record and is not connected to Charles. The "two Konze fathers" framework — and later the "three Konze fathers" framework — predates this correction and rested partly on the retracted Entry-195 reading.

Deisel was annexed by Prussia along with the rest of Kurhessen in September 1866 and became an Ortsteil under the Provinz Hessen-Nassau administration. In the 20th century the village merged administratively into Stadt Trendelburg.

Religious composition · Peter Schräder's data (20 Nov 2025)

From a book on Deisel that Peter Schräder studied (cited in his 20 November 2025 email): the religious composition of Deisel in roughly the mid-19th century:

Total population1,118 people
Evangelical / Lutheran1,095  (97.94 %)
Catholic5  (0.45 %)
Jewish17  (1.52 %)

Deisel was overwhelmingly Evangelical — 98% — but with two small non-Lutheran communities. Peter's reading on 20 November 2025: "perhaps we're searching in the wrong community" — meaning if Charles wasn't Evangelical, he wouldn't appear in any of the Lutheran parish records on Archion. With only ~5 Catholics and ~17 Jews in Deisel, the non-Evangelical population is small enough that both groups could be exhaustively checked:

  • Catholic check (negative): Peter checked the Catholic records — those five Catholic families would have been registered at the nearest Catholic parish, west of the Weser in Westfalia (e.g. Wehrden, the town that surfaced in connection with the Person/Brasun candidate-family thread). The Catholic check came back negative: no Charles, no Gonnson, no plausible match. The "Catholic baptism explains the missing Lutheran record" theory was ruled out.
  • Jewish check (set aside): Peter pulled the LAGIS summary of the Jewish cemetery in Trendelburg (which served Deisel) — the transcribed gravestones contain no Gonson family name. The 1740 Trendelburg "Protected Jews" list and the ~1750 Deisel/Trendelburg tax registers (covering every citizen, all faiths) were reviewed during the now set-aside Jewish-paternal-line inquiry.

The negative Catholic check is significant: it means Charles is almost certainly not from one of the five Deisel Catholic families. Combined with his absence from all searched Lutheran registers (Trendelburg, Deisel KB 218842, Friedrichsfeld, Helmarshausen, Hümme, both Hofgeismar parishes, post-1822 Schöneberg), the surviving non-trivial leads are: (a) Heisebeck Lutheran register pre-1845 (undigitized), (b) pre-1822 Schöneberg Lutheran (undigitized), or (c) the Jewish-paternal-line inquiry (a thread the project has set aside).

Source: Peter Schräder email "the research" · 20 November 2025 · Stadt Trendelburg historical-statistical book about Deisel.

Significance to the investigation

Status (May 2026): contested. Peter Schräder's 12 April 2026 verdict, after checking his own Konze database against Deisel-area emigration lists, closed the legitimate-Konze-son hypothesis: "Charles is not a legitimate son of the Konze family." Only one Konze appears in Deisel emigration lists matching Charles's age window, and it isn't him. The 1875 St. Marcus baptism record of Charles's son spelled Erwin Carl KONSEN — using the Hesse surname — remains the surname-preservation anchor and is unexplained without a Konze biological tie.

The two January 1835 baptisms — Entries 194 + 195

  • Entry 194 · "Ludwig Friedrich" · b. early Jan 1835 · father Andreas Konze, Ackermann
  • Entry 195 · 23–24 Jan 1835 · re-read 4 May 2026 as a Köster day-labourer's child — no recorded name, born and died within three days, never baptised. Eliminated as a Charles candidate.

Other Konze residents in Deisel

  • Justus Ernst Konze — b. 14 November 1829 (Trendelburg/Deisel); confirmed Pfingsten 1843 in Deisel. Last documented at 1843 confirmation; emigration unknown. The closest in age and naming pattern to Charles, but search for his post-1843 trace is incomplete.
  • Wilhelmine geb. Konze × Ludwig Köster (Ackermann) — children 1828–31 in Deisel
  • Maria Elisabeth geb. Konze × Christian Köster III — son d. 1835 in Deisel
  • Carlotta geb. Konze — the third geb.-Konze mother attested in Deisel, 1828–1829 (see the Deisel research log)
