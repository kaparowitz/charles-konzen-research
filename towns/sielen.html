Sielen — small village northeast of Trendelburg

Mentioned in a few Konze-related register entries · not yet exhaustively searched · part of Trendelburg parish complex

Historical overview

Sielen is a small Hessian village northeast of Trendelburg, with a population of perhaps 200–300 in the 19th century. It does not have its own Lutheran parish church — Sielen residents attended church in Trendelburg, and their baptisms, marriages, and deaths appear in the Trendelburg parish register volumes with a "Sielen" village-of-origin notation.

The village passed to Prussia along with Kurhessen in September 1866 and became an Ortsteil of Stadt Trendelburg in modern administrative consolidations.

Significance to the investigation

Sielen is referenced in a few Konze-related register entries from the Trendelburg books — typically as a village-of-residence for collateral relatives of the main Trendelburg / Deisel Konze line. It has not been exhaustively searched as an independent parish (because it does not have its own register volume). Searches in Trendelburg KB 218842 and 220XXX series automatically cover any Sielen residents whose entries were filed in Trendelburg.

If a future research lead surfaces a Sielen-specific Konze branch, the relevant search would be in the Trendelburg parish books filtered for Sielen-village-of-origin entries.
