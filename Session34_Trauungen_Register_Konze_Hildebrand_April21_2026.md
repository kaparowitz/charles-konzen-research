## Critical Analytical Update: The Wilhelmine Hildebrand Question

### Finding: Wilhelmine Hildebrand Was Unmarried in 1849

The most significant finding of this session is that Wilhelmine Hildebrand is described as **"eheliche Tochter"** (legitimate daughter) in her 1849 marriage to Johann Heinrich Thöne. In German church record conventions, this designation means she had **never been married before**. Had she been a widow of Johann Heinrich Konze, the entry would read **"Witwe des verstorbenen Johann Heinrich Konze"** (widow of the deceased J.H. Konze).

### Implications for the J.H. Konze × Hildebrand Research

This finding creates several possible interpretations:

#### Scenario A: Different Wilhelmine Hildebrand (MOST LIKELY)
The Wilhelmine Hildebrand who married Thöne in 1849 (b. 18.12.1817) is a **different person** from the Wilhelmine Hildebrand who was the mother of J.H. Konze's children. The "Hildebrand" surname was clearly present in Deisel (father Andreas Hildebrand, Ackermann), and multiple women named Wilhelmine Hildebrand could have existed — perhaps an aunt and niece, or cousins.

**Supporting evidence:**
- Born December 1817, she would have been only **12–13 years old** when the first Konze children were reportedly born (~1830) — extremely young even by 19th-century standards
- She was unmarried ("eheliche Tochter") in 1849, with no mention of previous marriage or widowhood
- The J.H. Konze who fathered children with "Wilhelmine Hildebrand" is documented in register 218824 from as early as 1830

**Counter-evidence:**
- She was from Deisel ("von hier")
- The surname match is exact
- Only one Wilhelmine Hildebrand appears in the Trauungen index

#### Scenario B: Same Person, Never Formally Married to J.H. Konze
The Konze children (including Entry 195) may have been born to an **unmarried** Wilhelmine Hildebrand. In German church records, illegitimate children were typically noted differently, but the Deisel register formats may not always make this explicit. If J.H. Konze and Wilhelmine Hildebrand never married, the children would have been illegitimate — and Wilhelmine could later marry Thöne as an "eheliche Tochter" of her parents.

**Supporting evidence:**
- The J.H. Konze × Hildebrand marriage is absent from ALL Deisel registers
- If they were never married, this explains the complete absence of a marriage record

**Counter-evidence:**
- The children in register 218824 appear to be recorded as legitimate (standard format with both parents named)
- Age problem: born 1817, she would have been ~12 at the first child's birth

#### Scenario C: J.H. Konze × Hildebrand Married Elsewhere
The marriage may still have occurred in another parish (possibly Hildebrand's home village, if this is a different branch of the Hildebrand family), and J.H. Konze may have died before 1849, with the 1849 bride being a younger relative of the same name.

### The Andreas Hildebrand Connection

Entry 187 identifies the bride's father as **Andreas Hildebrand, Einwohner und Ackermann** in Deisel. This connects to the earlier Session 32 finding of "Margaretha geb. [?]ildebrun" on Seite 43 of register 218824 — the Hildebrand family was clearly established in Deisel. Future research should:
1. Search the Deisel Taufen registers for children of Andreas Hildebrand to determine if there were multiple daughters named Wilhelmine
2. Search the Deisel burial registers for a J.H. Konze death before 1849
3. Check whether the Entry 195 baptism (Jan 1835) identifies the mother as "Wilhelmine Hildebrand" or "Wilhelmine Konze née Hildebrand"

### Entry 185 — New Konze Branch Identified

The Philipp Konze entry reveals a previously unknown Konze family branch in Deisel:
- **Father**: Johann Christoph Konze — **Bürgermeister** (mayor) of Deisel
- **Mother**: Maria Elisabeth née Schrage
- **Son**: Philipp Konze (b. ~August 1825), Ackermann

This is distinct from:
- Johann Heinrich Konze #1 (son of Jost/Christoph Contze, married Maria Donner 1824)
- Johann Heinrich Konze #2 (father of Entry 195 child, married Wilhelmine Hildebrand)
- Andreas Konze (father of Entry 194 child)

The Johann Christoph Konze branch, with its Bürgermeister status, suggests the Konze family held significant social standing in Deisel — consistent with the "merchant" (Kaufmann) occupation noted for J.H. Konze in other entries.
