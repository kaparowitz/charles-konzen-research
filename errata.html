Errata & Revisions

A reverse-chronological list of prior assertions on this site that have been revised, corrected, or withdrawn. Each entry names the original claim, the corrected reading, and the evidence that prompted the revision. The Confirmed / Working / Open / Eliminated taxonomy elsewhere on the site is the live state of the work; this page is its history.

Genealogical claims revise. The nine-attestation surname trail recorded here was not assembled in a single sitting — it is the survival, after thirteen candidate families and several mistaken readings, of what remained when each new piece of evidence was confronted with the prior interpretation. We list the revisions formally so that anyone citing an earlier version of this site, an earlier email from one of the authors, or an earlier Family Tree pull-down can locate the change and the warrant.

Entries are tagged major when the revision changes a public-facing conclusion, minor when it corrects a transcription, date, or attribution, and withdrawn when a hypothesis was retired without a replacement.

Major17 May 2026 (late evening) · primary-source re-read · 1890 daughter's death record · supersedes 5 May 2026 errata

The 1890 death record of daughter Auguste reads "Auguste Somers geb Konzen" (with z) — not Konsen. Eight distinct American spellings, the only American attestation of the full Hessian z-form.

Before (5 May 2026 errata canon)

The 5 May 2026 errata (er-2026-05-05) had concluded after re-examination that the 1890 death record reads "Auguste Somers geb Konsen" (with s), matching the same scribe's 1875 baptism reading and yielding the project's "Konsen attested twice 15 years apart" framing. The seven-distinct-American-spellings canon (Gonnson · Gansen · Gonsen · Konsen · Jansen · Gonson · Conson) treated 1875 and 1890 as two attestations of Konsen.

After (17 May 2026 re-examination)

A late-evening 17 May 2026 user re-examination of the 1890 death-register image confirms the reading is Konzen with a z, not Konsen with an s. The reading has now been called Konzen → Konsen (5 May) → Konzen (17 May) across two months, reflecting the difficulty of distinguishing z from s in mid-19th-century German Kurrentschrift. The current canonical reading is Konzen; the 5 May 2026 errata above is hereby superseded. Three substantive cascade effects: (1) The American spelling canon expands from seven to eight: Gonnson · Gansen · Gonsen · Konsen · Jansen · Gonson · Konzen · Conson. (2) The 1890 record becomes the single American attestation of the full Hessian z-form Konzen — the form documented in the Trendelburg parish complex from 1773. Maiden-name attestations on death records are among the most carefully captured forms in genealogical practice, which makes this a strong piece of evidence. (3) The "Konsen attested twice 15 years apart" framing is retired; replaced by "Konsen 1875 (phonetic s-form) and Konzen 1890 (Hessian z-form) — two parallel orthographic variants of the same Hessian root in continuous family use 1875–1890." The 17 May 2026 P0-4 errata fix that had stripped "Konzen" from the U.S.-spelling enumeration on methodology.html and faq.html (on the basis that Konzen didn't appear in any U.S. record) is therefore also superseded; Konzen has been restored to both pages with a note pointing at the 1890 attestation.

Evidence: St. Marcus Death Register, p. 18–19, entry 1961, 31 December 1890 — image filed at evidence_images/death_1890_auguste_konsen.webp (filename retained; image content re-read). Major17 May 2026 (late) · surname-pool framing · "Konsen" as a Hesse spelling

"Konsen" with an s is not attested in any Hesse parish register — it's the American phonetic capture closest to the Hesse cluster, not itself a Hesse-cluster member

Before

The "Hesse records · pre-1859" chip set on the name-evolution flow (index.html and index.html#charles) listed three forms — Konze · Konsen · Konzen — conflating the American-side Konsen (with s) with the Hesse-parish cluster. The Konsen Puzzle page (germany/konzen-puzzle.html) went further and called Konsen "the German Hesse spelling found in the Deisel and Trendelburg church books," and Step 2 of the name-evolution prose described the 1875 baptism as "the only St. Louis document that preserves the original Hessian spelling on American soil."

After

The five attested Hesse parish-register spellings are Konze · Kontze · Kontzen · Contze · Konzen (all with z/c/tz, never with s), per Trendelburg KB 220108 alphabetical index ("Konze, Johann Friedrich Wilhelm"), Trendelburg burial register 220096 K-section (Kontze / Kontzen entries 1773–1818), and the parish's own cross-reference note "siehe auch K. Contze, Cortze, Cordes, Contz" adding three more orthographic variants. "Konsen" with an s appears only in two American records (1875 St. Marcus baptism by Pastor Hoffmann, Swiss-born; 1890 daughter's death record). It is therefore the closest American phonetic capture to the Hesse cluster — preserving the Hessian K-initial and the -en suffix — but is not itself one of the documented Hesse spellings. Chips on both pages now show the five Hesse-attested forms with the parish cross-reference note as a footnote, and an explicit "Konsen with an s is NOT in this cluster" callout. Step 2 prose now says "closest American match to the documented Hesse-parish cluster" rather than "preserves the original Hessian spelling." The Konsen Puzzle page now says "closest American match" and adds an explicit "no Trendelburg or Deisel parish register carries that exact form" caveat.

Evidence: Bibliography.html entry for Trendelburg burial register 220096; methodology.html §3 source-rating taxonomy and §6 naming conventions; index.html#germany surname-pool table; glossary.html entry for the Kontze surname pool. Triggered by: user catch — reader could not recall seeing "Konsen" in any Hesse parish record, and flagged that the Hesse-records chip set showed only three forms when five are documented. Affected pages: index.html (chip set + Step 2 prose), index.html#charles (chip set + Step 2 prose), germany/konzen-puzzle.html (lead-paragraph framing).
