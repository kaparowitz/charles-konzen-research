# Register 220096 Burial Index — K Section Breakthrough
## April 11, 2026 (evening)

## CRITICAL FINDING

The prior research paper §11.33 (line 1783) claimed "Systematic search of Trendelburg register 220096 burial section (1792-1795) and burial index (1773-1818) found NO Kontze entries." **This claim is INCORRECT.** Direct reading of image 1222 of Register 220096 (the J-K-L-M section of the alphabetical burial index for 1773-1818) reveals multiple Kontze and Jungkontze entries that the prior search missed.

## The breakthrough methodology

Previous attempts to read Archion's handwritten pages through the browser automation bridge failed because the default viewer renders a small, low-resolution thumbnail. This session discovered that the Archion viewer's `.zoom-hotspots` element accepts programmatic click events, and a single dispatched click event doubles the zoom level from the default (382×310 pixels) to 2x (764×620 pixels). At 2x zoom the tile loader fetches higher-resolution tiles, making the Kurrent script readable via computer-use screenshot + zoom tools. Without this discovery, the remainder of this file would not exist.

## K section of the 1773-1818 burial index (image 1222, lower-left column)

Three Kontze entries appear consecutively at the bottom of the K section, immediately after "Kollers Andreas 393":

1. **Kontze [Ernst or Arnd] Philipp — page 361**
2. **Kontze Andreas — page 396** (ditto-marked under Kontze)
3. **Kontze Johannes — page 414** (ditto-marked under Kontze)

**Caveat on the first-name reading:** The first name on entry 1 is written in Kurrent as a 4-5 letter word starting with a capital letter that could be read as either "E" or "A." Possible readings include:

- "Ernst Philipp" (plausible German double-name)
- "Arnd Philipp" or "Arnt Philipp" (where Arnd/Arnt is the medieval short form of Arnold)
- "Arndt Philipp" (Arndt as a standalone given name)

If this reads "Arnd Philipp," it is the same person as **Arnold Philipp Kontze, Rathsverwandter of Trendelburg**, named in three 1773-1780 marriage entries (Nov 26 1773, Feb 17 1774, Nov 30 1780) as already documented in §11.13 of the research paper. The biographical fit is strong: Arnold Philipp was known dead by November 1780 (the November 30 1780 marriage names his children as his survivors), and a burial at manuscript page 361 (approximately one-third of the way through the burial section for 1773-1818) is consistent with a death in the late 1770s to early 1780s.

**A firsthand visual check of the actual burial entry at manuscript page 361 is needed to resolve the Ernst-vs-Arnd ambiguity and to capture date, cause of death, age, and any survivor information.**

"Jungkontze" (literally "Young-Kontze") is a Kontze family Hausname / alternate surname that distinguishes a particular branch from the senior Kontze line. The burial index entries here expand that documentation substantially.

**Note on maiden name Wallroth:** the "geb. Wallroth" reading gives a new in-law surname not previously recorded. Wallroth is a Hessian surname; this connection should be noted for follow-up research.

## C section cross-reference note (image 1221, top of left column)

The top of the C section contains a handwritten cross-reference note reading approximately:

> *"(siehe auch K. Contze, Cort[ze], Cord[es], Cont[z])"*
> *("see also under K. Contze, Cortze, Cordes, Contz")*

This note explicitly directs readers consulting the C section to look under K for Contze / Cortze / Cordes / Contz variant spellings. It confirms that the indexer recognized surname-spelling variants and consolidated them under K rather than splitting them across C and K. Anyone searching only the K section for "Kontze" would catch the three entries listed above, but a researcher who searched only for "Contze" under C without reading this cross-reference would find zero results — which is plausibly what happened in the prior §11.33 sweep.

## Methodological note

This breakthrough was possible only because:
- A programmatic click via `dispatchEvent(new MouseEvent('click',...))` on the `.zoom-hotspots` element in Archion's viewer triggers a 2x zoom without requiring full-screen mode
- The 2x zoom causes the tile loader to fetch higher-resolution tiles
- Computer-use screenshot + zoom then captures the higher-resolution rendering at sufficient fidelity to read Kurrent
- The effective reading resolution at 2x is approximately equivalent to reading a ~1000x800 image crop of the register page

Further zoom levels (4x, 8x) may be achievable by repeated click events. This should be tested in the next session to enable even more confident readings.

## Calibration of image-to-manuscript-page mapping

Image 1217 (index dropdown value 236) was found to contain manuscript pages **438 (left) / 439 (right)**, with top-of-page headers reading "Begraben im Jahr 1817" and "Begraben im Jahr 1818."

The exact formula for register 220096 is therefore:

> **Left page number  = 2·(image number) − 1996**
> **Right page number = 2·(image number) − 1995**

Inverse: for manuscript page P, image n = (P + 1996)/2 if P is even (left-hand page) or (P + 1995)/2 if P is odd (right-hand page).

This calibration invalidates the naive "1 image ≈ 2 pages starting at image 981" mapping that was used in Part I above. ## §II.1 Manuscript page 361 — Arnold Philipp Kontze (d. 1780)

**Image 1178, right-hand page.** Header: "Begrabene 1779" (left page) → "Summa 16 Begraben 1779" → "1780" heading on right page.

Transcription of the entry (Kurrent, best-effort reading):

> **[Apr.] 5. Arnold Philipp Kontze, Rathsverwandter und Ackermann, + am 3. April Nachmittags um 1 Uhr, alt 71 Jahr.**

Translation:
- Buried ~April 5, 1780
- Died ("+") April 3, 1780, afternoon ~1 o'clock
- Aged 71 years
- Occupation at death: Rathsverwandter und Ackermann (town councilman and farmer)

**Identity confirmation.** This is unambiguously the same **Arnold Philipp Kontze, Rathsverwandter** named in the three 1773–1780 Trendelburg marriage entries (November 26 1773, February 17 1774, November 30 1780). The November 30 1780 marriage entry described his children as his survivors, which is now explained: he had died on April 3, 1780, several months before his surviving children's marriage record was written.

**Derived birth year.** 1780 minus 71 years = **born ~1708/1709**. This places him in the generation one to two steps senior to Andreas Contze (below).

**Resolution of the index ambiguity.** The index entry that I transcribed on image 1222 as "Kontze [Ernst or Arnd] Philipp — p. 361" is now definitively the "Arnd Philipp" reading, where Arnd = the medieval short form of Arnold. The "Ernst Philipp" alternative can be retired.

**Consequence for the research paper.** §11.13 of the research paper (the three Rathsverwandter marriage entries for Arnold Philipp Kontze) is now complete. The brick wall on Arnold Philipp's death/burial is **solved**. Trendelburg register 220096, manuscript page 361, entry for ~April 5, 1780.

## §II.2 Manuscript page 396 — Herr Andreas Contze (d. 1800)

**Image 1196, left-hand page.** Header: "anno Begraben 1800 zu Trendelburg."

Transcription:

> **9. Herr Andreas Contze, Rathsverwandter [ahier?] gestorben am 7ten Nachmittag[s] zwischen 1 und 2 Uhr, alt 78 Jahr weniger 3 Monat und 4 Tage.**

Translation:
- Buried November 9, 1800 (the "9" entry under the November 1800 section; surrounding entries confirm the month)
- Died in Trendelburg ("ahier") on November 7, 1800, afternoon between 1 and 2 o'clock
- Aged 78 years minus 3 months and 4 days
- Occupation / title: **Herr** (the honorific "Herr" is rare in the Trendelburg register and signals elevated social status) **Rathsverwandter** (town councilman)

**Derived birth date.** November 7, 1800 minus 78y 3m 4d = **approximately August 3, 1722**.

**Identity analysis.** This is a second Rathsverwandter Kontze / Contze in Trendelburg — confirming that the Kontze family held the Rathsverwandter role across multiple generations (Arnold Philipp 1773-1780, Andreas 1800). The 14-year age gap between Arnold Philipp (b. ~1708) and this Andreas (b. ~1722) is consistent with them being **brothers** (less likely cousins or nephew/uncle).

**Conflict with §11.27 of the research paper.** The prior research paper claimed Andreas Kontze (I) — the father of Johann Friedrich Kontze — was "hweiland" (already deceased) by an earlier date and was not buried in Trendelburg. That claim must now be re-examined:

1. If the prior "hweiland" dating was before 1800, this 1800 burial is a different Andreas (an older brother of Arnold Philipp, perhaps the patriarch of the family).
2. If the prior "hweiland" dating is actually later than assumed, this IS Andreas Kontze (I) and §11.27's "not buried in Trendelburg" conclusion must be retracted.

Either way, **an adult Rathsverwandter Herr Andreas Contze, age 78, WAS buried in Trendelburg on November 9, 1800.** The §11.33 claim that the burial index contained no Kontze entries is fully refuted.

## §II.3 Manuscript page 414 — Johannes, child of Johann Christoph Contze (d. 1808)

**Image 1205, left-hand page.** Header: "Begrabenen 1808" (just below "Begraben 1807" concluding the 1807 section).

Transcription:

> **Jan. 4. Johannes, Joh. Christoph Contze [occupation/regiment] und Grenadiers [in dem] Leib Grenadier Bataillon[s] Söhnlein, gestorben am 1[...] abends gegen 7 Uhr, alt 3 Jahr 6 Monat weniger 2 Wochen.**

Translation:
- Burial entry for January 4, 1808
- **Johannes** — a male child ("Söhnlein" = little son)
- Father: **Johann Christoph Contze, Grenadier in the Leib-Grenadier-Bataillon** (the Hessian bodyguard grenadier regiment)
- The child died on the 1st [of January 1808] in the evening around 7 o'clock
- Age: 3 years, 6 months, minus 2 weeks → born approximately **mid-to-late July 1804**

**This is NOT an adult Johannes Kontze.** The index entry "Kontze Johannes — p. 414" refers to this child burial, not to any known adult Johannes.

**New family member discovered.** The father **Johann Christoph Contze, Grenadier in the Leib-Grenadier-Bataillon**, is previously undocumented in the family reconstruction. He was:

1. Alive in January 1808 in or near Trendelburg
2. A serving **Grenadier** in the Leib-Grenadier-Bataillon — a Hessian (probably Hessen-Kassel) elite infantry unit
3. Father of a son **Johannes Contze** born ~mid-1804

**HETRINA parallel.** §11.32 of the research paper already documents "Jungkonze, Henrich" of Trendelburg in the HETRINA (Hessen-Kassel military) records. Johann Christoph Contze is a second Kontze-family Hessian soldier from the same parish. Both point to a militarized Kontze cohort in late-eighteenth- and early-nineteenth-century Trendelburg.

2. **§11.13 is now completed:** Arnold Philipp Kontze's burial is located and dated.

## §II.7 CORRECTION appended later the same evening — J-section is Jungeorge, not Jungkontze

1. **The J-section of the burial index is Jungeorge (NOT Jungkontze).** Re-reading the individual burial pages (385 Feb 23 1797, 398 May 9 1801) established beyond reasonable doubt that the surname is **Jungeorge** — terminal letters "-r-g-e" not "-t-z-e" — a distinct Hessian surname (literally "Jung + Georg"), unrelated to the Kontze family. §II.5 point 5 above is retracted. The "seven Jungkontze burials" are actually seven Jungeorge burials in a separate family.
2. **Page 364 contains two additional UNINDEXED Kontze child-burials:** (a) Martha Elisabeth, dau of Johann [Henrich?] Kontze, Bürger und Ackermann, d. Apr 11 1783 aged 3y 4.5m, buried Apr 14 1783; (b) Wilhelmina, dau of Johannes Kontze, Bürger und Ackermann, d. Jun 14 1783 aged 1y 5m 2d 12min, buried Jun 16 1783.
