Story 03

The brother in the same battery

1862 – 1866 · St. Louis & New Madrid

Two months before he proposed to Auguste, Charles had spent three years in the same artillery battery as her brother. Her father served too. The wedding eighteen days after the unit's December 1865 discharge brought four Battery B veterans to the same St. Marcus altar — the groom, the senior groomsman, the bride's father, and the bride's brother.

Battery B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery, was a German-immigrant unit. The roll included Charles (Hessian, just-arrived), Adam Herweck (Hessian, the recruiter), Georg Kreichelt (Hannoverian, age 43), and August Kreichelt (Hannoverian, age 18 — Georg's son and Auguste's brother). Four men, four different German regional origins, fighting together at New Madrid in 1863 and Vicksburg in 1864.

How exactly Charles met Auguste isn't documented anywhere directly. But Battery B accounts for nearly all the male introductions in his St. Louis life. Adam Herweck recruited him in. Georg and August Kreichelt served beside him. Auguste was Georg's daughter and August's sister. The introduction was structurally inevitable — the parish (St. Marcus) had been the Kreichelt family's Lutheran congregation since their 1853 emigration, and three Hessian-Hannoverian men returning from war would naturally have ended up at the same altar.

The wedding tells the rest. On 7 January 1866 — eighteen days after Battery B's discharge — Charles married Auguste at St. Marcus. The four Battery B men gathered in one room: Charles (groom), Adam Herweck (senior groomsman, first-named witness), Georg Kreichelt (walking his daughter down the aisle), August Kreichelt (the bride's brother). It was an unusually concentrated Battery B reunion — almost certainly the densest Hessian-Hannoverian-American Lutheran cluster of veterans in St. Louis that month.

The integration that followed plays out across the next fourteen years of baptisms. August stood as sponsor at the 1872 baptism of his namesake niece. Georg sponsored at three baptisms (1866, 1868, 1872). Henriette, the matriarch, sponsored at two (1866, 1873). What Story 09 documents — every kin sponsor on Auguste's side, never Charles's — is what this story sets in motion. The introduction was the war. The integration was the parish.

Sources: NARA Battery B Compiled Service Records, 1862–1865; St. Marcus marriage register, 7 January 1866; FamilySearch L5FW-572 (August Kreichelt) and LZXC-NH9 (Georg Kreichelt). Full August Kreichelt profile.
