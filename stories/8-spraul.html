Story 08

The brewing-trade godfather

3 October 1875 · St. Louis

The seven Konzen baptisms follow a pattern: Kreichelt sponsors, Kreichelt-by-marriage sponsors, Kreichelt-side parish circle. Six of them. The seventh — the 1875 Erwin Carl baptism — breaks the pattern entirely.

There are no Kreichelt sponsors. There are no Albrecht-side or Pillmann-side relatives. The sole named godfather is Erwin Spraul, a non-relative. The child is named directly after him — Erwin Carl Konsen.

Spraul was 27 years old at the baptism, born around 1848 in Baden, Germany. He had married Anna Maria Grassner in St. Louis in 1868 and lived at 1340 South 9th Street in Soulard. By 1880 he held the trade title of Superintendent at the Anheuser-Busch Brewing Association — the most senior position at one of the largest German-immigrant employers in St. Louis. Charles was a labourer; Spraul ran a brewery floor.

Why him? The most direct answer comes from Jed in December 2025: Charles chose Spraul "because he was a successful fellow immigrant" — sharpened by the obituary to successful at the very top of the brewing trade. Charles, the labourer's labourer, picked the most respected fellow-Hessian-by-circumstance he knew as the namesake-godfather for his sixth child. There is a two-generation echo in this: Charles's grandson Frederick Charles Johnson (b. 1905) later worked as a Bottler at Anheuser-Busch, picking up the brewing-trade thread three decades after Erwin Spraul's death.

The same baptism carries the closest American phonetic capture of the Hessian spelling. Of the seven children, only this one is recorded as Konsen — the K-initial and -en suffix the previous story traces, the closest phonetic rendering to the Trendelburg parish-register Kontzen / Konzen cluster, and the phonetic-s sibling of his sister Auguste's 1890 death record reading Konzen — the verbatim Hesse-parish form and the closest American match overall. Two off-script choices in one event: the godparent and the surname. The 1875 baptism is the family's most explicit declaration of Hessian identity in St. Louis, and the only Konzen-family baptism without a Kreichelt sponsor at the font.

Sources: St. Marcus baptism 3 October 1875 (Erwin Carl Konsen); Erwin Spraul obituary, 19 June 1889 St. Louis; FamilySearch KLP1-N62. Full Erwin Spraul profile.
