## Critical New Analysis: The Two Johann Heinrich Konze Problem

Session 30 discovered a Konze marriage entry that creates a significant puzzle:

### The §25 Marriage (Register 218824, Seite 42, ~1824)
| Field | Reading |
|-------|---------|
| Groom | Johann Heinrich V. Kontze |
| Status | lediger Gesell (unmarried journeyman) |
| Bride | Maria Donner |
| Groom's father | Jost Kontze |

### Entry 195 Parents (Register 218842, Image 40, January 1835)
| Field | Reading |
|-------|---------|
| Father | Joh. Heinrich Konze, Kaufmann |
| Mother | W. Wilhelmine geb. Hildebrand |

### The Discrepancy
The §25 groom married **Maria Donner** in 1824, but Entry 195's mother is **Wilhelmine Hildebrand** in 1835. Three scenarios explain this:

**Scenario A: Same man, two marriages**
- J.H. Kontze married Maria Donner in 1824
- Maria Donner died (or the marriage ended)
- J.H. Kontze then married Wilhelmine Hildebrand (before 1835)
- The son Ludwig (b. 1821, confirmed ~1835 with mother "geb. Köster") would then need a THIRD wife — unless "Köster" is a misreading or the confirmation entry refers to a different mother
- **Problem**: This requires THREE wives (Köster for Ludwig b.1821, Donner m.1824, Hildebrand by 1835)

**Scenario B: Two different Johann Heinrich Konzes**
- J.H. Konze (son of Jost Konze) married Maria Donner in 1824
- A DIFFERENT J.H. Konze married Wilhelmine Hildebrand and fathered Entry 195's child
- The confirmation register's "Joh. Heinrich Konze, Ackermann" (Ludwig's father) could be either man
- **Supporting evidence**: Two Konze family branches are already documented in Deisel (Andreas Konze and Jost Konze lines)

**Scenario C: Same man, Köster reading is wrong**
- If the confirmation register mother's maiden name is NOT Köster but something else, then the problem reduces to two wives (Donner → died → Hildebrand)
- **Against**: User Jed confirmed "Köster" reading
- **But**: The confirmation entry mother could be the maternal grandmother or another relationship

### Assessment
**Scenario B (two different J.H. Konzes) is currently most likely.** The Deisel records already show multiple Konze family branches. A "Johann Heinrich" born to the Jost Konze branch (who married Maria Donner in 1824) would be distinct from a "Johann Heinrich" of the Andreas Konze branch or another line (who married Wilhelmine Hildebrand).
