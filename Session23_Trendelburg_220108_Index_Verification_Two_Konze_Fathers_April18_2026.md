## Headline

Session 23 completes the verification of all contested K-section index entries in Register 220108 (Trendelburg Baptisms 1830-1863), delivering three landmark results:

1. **"Korte, Karl Wilhelm Ludwig — 202" RESOLVED** — Re-reading the K-section index at Images 1644-1645 at high resolution definitively confirms the Seite 202 entry is surname **Korte**, not Konze. The Session 21 ambiguity ("Konze or Korte?") is eliminated. Physical pages at Seiten 201-204 (Images 1579-1582) were scanned and contain no K-surname baptism entries.

2. **"Konze, Karl Heinrich — 211" TRANSCRIBED** — Entry 762, Image 1589, Seite 211. Born 4 November 1854. Father: **Johann Christoph Konze**, Einwohner und Ackermann at Trendelburg Nr. 109. Mother: **Maria Elisabeth, née Kleine**. Godfather: Johann Christoph Friedrich Kleine from Deisel. This confirms Johann Christoph Konze as a **SECOND Konze father** in Trendelburg, entirely distinct from Carl Ludwig Konze (Ackerbürger at Nr. 34, wife Wilhelmine née Albrecht).

3. **"Konze, Joh. [Friedrich] — 60" CORRECTED** — Verified in earlier part of this session (pre-compaction). The actual entry reads **Johann Heinrich Konze**, born 1 March 1837. The index-maker misread "Heinrich" as "Friedrich." Father: Carl Ludwig Konze, Ackerbürger. Mother: Wilhelmine née Albrecht. This is a second confirmed child of Carl Ludwig Konze.

## Move 2 — Seite 202: "Korte, Karl Wilhelm Ludwig" — DEFINITIVELY NOT KONZE

The K-section continuation (Image 1645, Männlich column) was re-read at enhanced zoom. The entry at Seite 202 clearly reads:

### Resolution

**The "Konze or Korte?" ambiguity from Session 21 is resolved: the surname is KORTE.** This entry has no connection to the Konze family and is removed from the Konze research thread.

**Deisel Connection**: The godfather for Karl Heinrich Konze is from **Deisel** — the same village where Andreas Konze b.1795 (married Maria Sophia Hofeditz) was active. The mother's maiden name **Kleine** also suggests a Deisel family connection. This creates a second Konze–Deisel link alongside the Carl Ludwig Konze–Hofeditz godfather connection.

**Relationship Hypothesis**: Johann Christoph Konze could be:
- A brother or cousin of Carl Ludwig Konze
- A son of Andreas Konze b.1795 (if born ~1820s in Deisel, married Maria Elisabeth Kleine, moved to Trendelburg by 1854)
- An unrelated Konze from elsewhere in the region

The 23-year gap between Carl Ludwig's first known child (1831) and Johann Christoph's first known child (1854) suggests they are of different generations. Johann Christoph could be a son or nephew of Carl Ludwig, or a member of the Deisel branch.
