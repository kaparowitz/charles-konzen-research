### 2.2 bap Karl Wilhelm Konze 1844 FF.jpg — 1844 Baptism Record

**Entry #130, Friedrichsfeld parish**

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Child | Carl Wilhelm Konze |
| Mother | "Elisa Melusina, called Adolphine Konze" |
| Mother described as | "Tochter des **verschollenen** Andreas Konze" (daughter of the DISAPPEARED Andreas Konze) |
| Godfather | Israel Ludwig Lotze, farmer |
| Child's death | Died January 2, 1845 |

**Key significance:** The word "verschollenen" is a German legal term meaning "disappeared without a trace" or "missing, presumed dead." By 1844, Andreas Konze had already been gone long enough to be formally described this way in an official church record. Since his second wife died in October 1835, Andreas likely disappeared **between late 1834 and 1844**, with the likeliest window being **1835–1840**.

## 4. CONNECTION TO THE CHARLES GANNSEN BRICK WALL

### 4.1 Why Andreas Konze matters

Charles Gannsen/Johnson (b. ~1835, d. 1903 St. Louis) claimed origins variously as "Hessen" and "Trendelburg." The Konze family was based in the Trendelburg-Friedrichsfeld-Deisel area. The timeline aligns remarkably:

- **Charles's birth year (~1835)** falls within Andreas Konze's second marriage (Maria Sophia Hofeditz died October 1835). The last recorded child, August, was born in 1834.
- **Andreas disappeared** sometime after 1835, becoming "verschollenen" by 1844.
- **Andreas died in Mainz (1854)** as a factory worker — he had left the region entirely.
- **Charles emigrated to the US** — if he was Andreas's son (legitimate or illegitimate), he could have been taken along or sent away.

### 4.2 Possible scenarios

**Scenario A: Charles is an unrecorded child of Andreas and Maria Sophia (2nd wife)**
- Maria Sophia died October 15, 1835. If Charles was born in early 1835 (before her death), he could be a child whose baptism wasn't recorded — or was recorded in a parish we haven't checked.
- The fact that August (1834) and Maria Sabine (1834) were born the same year already shows an unusual pattern. A child in early 1835 would be biologically possible but tight.

**Scenario B: Charles is an illegitimate child of Andreas, born after he left**
- Andreas left the family "secretly." He could have fathered a child elsewhere.
- Peter explicitly suggested this in his April 6 email: "perhaps born somewhere else as an illegitimate son of Andreas."
- This would explain why Charles's baptism has never been found in Friedrichsfeld or Deisel.

**Scenario C: Charles is a child of one of Andreas's sons**
- Heinrich Wilhelm (b. 1832) or Justus Ernst (b. 1829) could theoretically have fathered a child, but they would have been very young (~3–6 years old) in 1835 — too young. This scenario is not viable for the birth year of ~1835.

**Scenario D: Charles is connected to a different branch of the Konze/Kontze family in Deisel**
- Our Archion search found multiple Johann Christoph Kontzes in Deisel with different wives. The Konze family was large and multi-branched.
- The Schildknecht/Kontze dual-identity pattern (documented in the 1799 baptism) adds another layer of complexity.

### 4.3 The surname question: Konze → Gannsen?

The transformation from "Konze/Kontze" to "Gannsen" remains the central puzzle. However:
- Historical variants include Contzin, Contze, Konze, Kontze — showing the name was fluid
- In American immigration records, phonetic spelling was common
- "Konze" spoken with a Hessian dialect accent could potentially be heard/recorded differently
- Charles may have deliberately changed his name upon emigration

### 4.4 Strengthening evidence

The following circumstantial evidence supports a Konze-Gannsen connection:

1. **Geographic match:** Konze family is centered in Trendelburg-Friedrichsfeld-Deisel — exactly where Charles claimed to be from
2. **Timeline match:** Andreas's disappearance window (~1835–1843) overlaps with Charles's birth year (~1835) and potential emigration
3. **Behavioral pattern:** Andreas abandoned his family — a man willing to leave secretly could also father unrecorded children
4. **Unusual family circumstances:** The Konze family had multiple anomalies (handicapped child, two births in one year, father as own child's godfather, secret abandonment) — consistent with a family under social stress
5. **No baptism found in Deisel for Charles:** Our full Archion search of KB 218824 (1796–1831) found no "Andreas Kontze" baptism and no "Charles/Karl Kontze" birth — but the register only goes to 1831, four years before Charles's likely birth

4. **Is there a Friedrichsfeld baptism register for 1834–1840?** This is the critical window. A child named Karl/Charles born to Andreas Konze (or born illegitimately and attributed to the family) in this period could be our Charles Gannsen.

5. **Can we find Andreas Konze in Mainz records (1840s–1854)?** His death record in Mainz as a factory worker might reveal whether he had any children or companions with him.
