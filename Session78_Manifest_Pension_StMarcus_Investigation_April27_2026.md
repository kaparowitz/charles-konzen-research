## Part 1 — Carl Kinzer Manifest: Surname is KRIEGER, not Konzen

### 1A. The original manifest

Viewed the original ship Resolute manifest (Liverpool→NY, arrived 20 December 1859), the page containing the indexed "Carl Kinzer" record. The Ancestry indexer's transcription is the issue: the actual handwritten surname is **KRIEGER**, not Kinzer.

### 1B. Manifest excerpt (relevant rows)

| Line | Name | Age | Sex | Occupation | Origin | Destination |
|------|------|-----|-----|------------|--------|-------------|
| (n) | John Warwick | 65 | M | Shoemaker | — | — |
| (n+1) | Joseph Williams | 24 | M | — | Wales | — |
| (n+2) | Ann Williams | 24 | F | — | — | — |
| (n+3) | Ann Dunlap | 22 | F | — | England | — |
| **(n+4)** | **Carl Krieger** | **22** | **M** | **Miller** | **Germany** | **—** |
| (n+5) | Heinrich Hauser | 32 | M | Laborer | Hanover | — |
| (n+6) | James Kovan | 20 | M | — | Ireland | — |

### 1C. Conclusion — Carl Krieger is NOT Charles Gannsen

- Surname is clearly Krieger, not Konzen / Köster / Gannsen / Kontze.
- Occupation is Miller, while Charles Gannsen worked as cotton press worker / watchman in St. Louis (different trade entirely).
- Origin is just "Germany" with no Hessen specifier; the surrounding line for Heinrich Hauser specifically says "Hanover," indicating the manifest did capture province-of-origin where the passenger gave it. Carl Krieger gave only "Germany" — could be from anywhere.

The leading 1859 candidate from Session 77 is therefore eliminated. Charles Gannsen's 1859 immigration arrival remains undocumented in any indexed Ancestry passenger collection.

## Part 3 — Erwin Carl Konzen 1875 Baptism — Godparent Identified

### 3A. The St. Marcus baptism record

Located the actual Erwin Carl Konzen baptism entry in the digitized St. Marcus Evangelical Church register:

- **Source:** St. Marcus Evangelical Church records, 1847–1981, FamilySearch Film 007769183, Image 488 of 847, page 453 of register
- **Entry #8854 / 282** (1875 baptism series)
- **Name:** Erwin Carl Konsen [or Konzen — handwriting is K-on-sen / K-on-zen]
- **Born:** 27 August 1875 A.D.
- **Baptized:** 3 October 1875 A.D.
- **Parents (Eltern):** Carl Konsen and his wife Auguste née Kreichelt
- **Godparent (Pathen):** **Erwin Spraul** (followed by what appears to be "der Vater des Kindes" — possibly indicating Erwin Spraul stood in proxy for an absent godfather, or a notation about the relationship; pastor J. Hoffmann officiating as on every entry)

### 3B. Significance of the godparent

Charles Jr. (= Erwin Carl Konzen) is named after his godfather **Erwin Spraul**. This is the standard 19th-century German Lutheran custom: the child takes the godparent's first name.

**Spraul is NOT a Deisel / Hessen surname I have seen in any prior research file.** It does not appear in:
- The Deisel Kirchenbuch families documented through KB 218842 / 218836 / 218851 / 218860
- The Hildebrand or Köster networks
- The Buecher St. Marcus marriage or burial indices

So Erwin Spraul is an independent St. Louis acquaintance — possibly a Civil War comrade, neighbor, or fellow St. Marcus parishioner — not a relative from Hessen-Kassel.

### 3C. Surname spelling clue: "Konsen"

The handwritten surname on this baptism could be read as either "Konzen" or "**Konsen**" (with an "s" rather than a "z"). This is potentially significant because:
- "Konsen" → "Gansen" or "Gannsen" with phonetic K↔G softening (German "K" is hard but a clerk hearing "Gansen/Gannsen" might write "Konsen")
- "Konsen" → "Konze/Konzen" with simple s↔z variation
- The visual ambiguity in this single church-clerk's hand makes both readings plausible

This adds a third surname-spelling layer to Charles's identity:
- 1862 enlistment: **Gannsen**
- 1866 marriage: **Gonnson**
- 1875 baptism (this record): **Konsen** or **Konzen**
- Late life: **Johnson** (Anglicized)

### 3D. Other St. Marcus sources attached to Charles Jr.'s FamilySearch profile

## Part 4 — Updated Conclusion on Justus → Charles Gannsen

### 4A. Hypotheses status after Sessions 75–78

| St. Louis Hildebrand or Köster relative is documented | **NEGATIVE** — pension file witnesses, St. Marcus burials, and St. Marcus baptism godparents all show no extended-family network from Deisel |

### 4B. What this means

After 78 sessions of research, the Justus-=-Charles identity hypothesis still hinges on:
1. The matching January 1835 birth date (KB 218842 Entry 195 vs. Charles's pension file)
2. Charles's marriage record naming Deisse / Deisel as origin
3. The complete absence of Justus from every Deisel parish record (baptism after 1835, confirmation, marriage, burial)
4. Charles's German surname KONZEN documented in three independent St. Marcus records

…but is NOT corroborated by any of:
- LAGIS Hessian state emigration index (no Justus Köster, no Justus Konze)
- Indexed NY passenger lists (no clear match in 1854–1859)
- Hildebrand chain migration (1850 Rockford / 1853 Philadelphia destinations don't link to St. Louis)
- St. Louis pension witness network (no Deisel-origin sponsors)
- St. Louis St. Marcus godparent network (godparent Spraul is not a Deisel surname)

The most plausible remaining theory: **Justus left Deisel as a teenager between ~1849 and 1854 for an undocumented intra-German destination (apprenticeship, schooling, urban relocation), was confirmed there, married or remained single, and emigrated as an adult after 1855 directly from a German port without a Hessian Auswanderungspatent.** He arrived in St. Louis without a family or village network already there — which is why no Deisel surname surfaces among his pension witnesses or his children's godparents.
