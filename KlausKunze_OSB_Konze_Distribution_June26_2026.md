# Klaus Kunze Ortssippenbücher — Konze/Gans surname mining (26 June 2026)

**Why:** Widened Search Plan Tier 3 asks for the *wider Kreis Kassel ring* — any
**un-searched** Landkreis Kassel parish that actually carries the Konze pool.
Jed pointed to **klauskunze.com** (HeiKun-Verlag; Klaus & Heike Kunze, Uslar):
printed *Ortssippenbücher* (OSB) for the upper-Weser / Reinhardswald area, several
with **online surname registers**. Data-mined those registers for the pool
(Kontze / Konze / Kontzen / Konzen / Jungkonz(e) / Gans / Ganß / Ganson / Gonse).

**Method:** opened each OSB's online *Registerseite* (surname index) and string-matched.
The registers are alphabetical surname lists with page numbers — they prove **presence
of the surname in that parish's book (16th–19th c.)**, not the individual dates.

## Headline
Two **previously-unsearched Landkreis Kassel parishes carry the full Konze pool** —
**Oedelsheim** (Kontze/Kontzen/Konzen/Jungkonz) and **Lippoldsberg** (Kontze/Jungkontze
+ **Ganson**, a direct American-spelling match). Both sit in the Weser corridor just
**north of Gieselwerder**, extending the Diemel-valley cluster downriver. This is the
first material widening of the Konze geography since Session 88 declared the pool
"hyper-local."

**On "Gans" as a candidate:** Gansen/Ganson fit the American **Gannsen** at least as well
as Konze does phonetically (n-s-n skeleton; "Ganson" is literally in the surname canon).
So Gans/Gansen/Ganson are now treated **in-pool** for these reads, not just Kontze.
Caveat: Gans is a common surname (less diagnostic than the rare Kontze); one Gans family
(Meimbressen, Salomon Gans, b.1850, Jewish) was already eliminated.

## Update (26 Jun 2026) — Lippoldsberg confirmations DO carry Gans

A corrected re-read of Lippoldsberg's confirmation register (reg 219775, ordered leaves
Seite 40–51) found **three Gans confirmands**: Heinrich Wilhelm Gans (b.1831) and Konrad
Gans (b.1832), conf. 1846; Friedrich Wilhelm Gans (b.24 Jun 1835), conf. 1849. So for
Lippoldsberg the OSB pool surname **does** surface in the confirmation window — unlike
Gieselwerder and Oedelsheim (both now fully read = complete negative). None of the three is
a Carl and none was born 26 Jan 1835, so Charles is not directly among them, but the dense
Gans presence makes **Lippoldsberg the top priority for the Gans = Gannsen branch** (next:
the Gans baptisms). Detail in Tier1_Search_Log_June2026.md (Session 20) and
germany/lippoldsberg-research-log.html.
