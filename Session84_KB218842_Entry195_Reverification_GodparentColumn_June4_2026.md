### 2. Surname = KÖSTER — now corroborated by the baptism register itself
The father's surname in Entry 195 reads **"Köster"** directly in the baptism register, independent of the 1850 confirmation-class sibling (Ernst Wilhelm Köster). This strengthens the Session 71 conclusion from "single-source" to "two independent sources."

### 3. Child's given name — "JUSTUS" RETRACTED
At maximum zoom the **child-name column reads only "Männl: Köster"** (sex + surname) — there is **no legibly recorded given name.** This is typical for a Nottaufe of a weak newborn. The prior "Justus" reading is not supported and is retracted.
**Consequence:** the long-standing "Justus ≠ L. Carl / Charles" objection **dissolves** — the German record assigns no given name that could conflict with the American "L. Carl."

### 4. No death notation for Entry 195 (corrects an AI-HTR trap)
Entry 195's only remark is "getauft am 23. 35." There is **no death cross** on its row, and its **godparents column is a blank diagonal slash** (no godparents — consistent with Nottaufe).
**Important:** Archion's AI text-recognition mis-attributed two items from the next row to Entry 195 —
- the godparent **"Anna Kath. Meinebrosse geb. Weike"**, and
- the death cross **"† … 29 … 1835"** —
both of which actually belong to **Entry 196 (Sophie Weike, b. 28 January 1835)**, an infant who died the day after birth. Anyone reading the raw AI transcript could wrongly conclude Entry 195's child died in infancy. It did not. The "no death → emigration" inference therefore still holds.
