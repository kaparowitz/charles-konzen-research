# Auguste Gonsen / Auguste Somers (L5N2-M8Y) — Life Sketch

> Drafted 12 June 2026 for the FamilySearch Life Sketch field. Grounded in the sources attached
> to the profile and the project page children/auguste.html.

---

Auguste Johnson was born 4 March 1872 in St. Louis, Missouri, and baptized Auguste Gonsen, the fourth of seven children of Charles Johnson Sr. — a Hessian-born immigrant and Civil War artillery sergeant — and Auguste Kreichelt, born in Clausthal, Hanover, and was named directly after her mother. She was baptized 23 June 1872 at St. Marcus German Evangelical Church (register pp. 352–353, no. 7519) by Pastor Heinrich Braschler. Her sponsors were all immediate family — her grandparents Georg and Louise (Henriette) Kreichelt and her uncle August Kreichelt, a Battery B veteran who had served alongside her father and whose name she also carried in its feminine form — making hers the most "in-family" baptism of the seven. The clerk wrote the surname "Gonsen," two letters apart from the "Gansen" the same pastor had written for her sister two years earlier.

On 12 July 1890, at age eighteen, she married James E. Somers (b. 1869), a St. Louis-born man, and that year lived at 2408 DeKalb Street in St. Louis. Their only child, James Victor Somers, was born and died that same year. Auguste herself died 31 December 1890, barely five months after her wedding, at age eighteen, of idiopathic peritonitis — the St. Marcus death register's "Unterleibsleiden," an abdominal ailment — likely the result of a difficult pregnancy and delivery. She was buried 2 January 1891 in St. Louis, the second grown daughter her mother buried in that hardest of years, after her sister Josephine in May.

Her St. Marcus death-register entry, "Auguste Somers geb. Konzen," is the single American record of the full Hessian z-form spelling of the family surname — the form documented in the Trendelburg parish registers — and stands today as keystone evidence connecting her father's family to its origins in Hesse.
