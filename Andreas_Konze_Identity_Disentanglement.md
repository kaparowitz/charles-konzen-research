# Disentangling the Multiple Andreas Konze Identities

**Compiled: April 4, 2026 | Updated: April 5, 2026** | Based on §12.9, §13.1, §3.3–3.6 of the Research Paper (April 4, 2026), Peter Schrader's corrections (April 3–5, 2026), and FamilySearch Data Entry workbook

---

## The Problem

The April 4 research paper (§12.9) identifies disentangling the multiple Andreas Konze identities as **"the single most critical open question for the Konze family reconstruction."** At least **five distinct men** named Andreas Kon(t)ze appear in the Trendelburg and Deisel church records spanning approximately 1718–1830. Earlier sections of the paper (§3.3, §3.4, §3.6) incorrectly attributed the Hofeditz marriages to the wrong Andreas, an error Peter Schrader corrected on April 3, 2026.

This document separates all five identities, lays out what is confirmed for each, and flags what remains unresolved.

**APRIL 5, 2026 — MAJOR NEW FINDINGS (Peter Schrader email "Andreas Konze"):**

Peter Schrader describes this family as "unusual" and highlights several anomalies:

1. **Grandfather was a Ratsherr (council member) in Trendelburg** — mentioned at Andreas's 1795 baptism. This indicates the family held a position of civic prominence, placing them among Trendelburg's leading citizens.

2. **Married his sister-in-law after first wife's death.** After Wilhelmine Hofeditz died (7 Oct 1825), Andreas married her sister Maria Sophia Hofeditz (16 Dec 1827). This was not unheard of in the era but was noteworthy enough that Peter flags it.

3. **Two children born in 1834 — unusually close spacing.** Maria Sophia gave birth to two children in the same year (1834). Normal birth spacing in this period was approximately 2 years. This rapid succession is "unusual" per Peter.

4. **Andreas was godfather of his own son August, born October 1834.** Peter emphasizes this was "very unusual, because this makes no sense." A father serving as godfather to his own child violates the purpose of godparentage (providing a spiritual guardian *other* than the parents). This may indicate: (a) Andreas was already estranged from the family; (b) a clerical or transcription error; (c) other extraordinary circumstances in the household.

5. **Andreas secretly abandoned his family and died in Mainz in 1854 as a factory worker.** The death record was found in "Trendelburg-Friedrichsfeld" records but records his death in Mainz. He left his family without notice ("secretly"). This abandonment is a significant biographical detail — it shows a pattern of family instability and departure.

**Children of the second marriage — COMPLETE DATA (from Peter's "Andreas Konze.pdf", April 5, 2026):**

| Child | Born | Died | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Maria Carolina | 5 Mar 1828 | Unknown | Survived |
| Justus Ernst | 14 Nov 1829 | Unknown | **Survived — only surviving male son with no recorded death. KEY CANDIDATE.** |
| Heinrich Wilhelm | 12 Feb 1832 | **19 Feb 1858** | **DIED 1858 — RULES HIM OUT as Charles Gannsen (who enlisted 1862, died 1903)** |
| **Maria Sabine** | **10 Jan 1834**, Friedrichsfeld | Unknown | **First of two children born in 1834. Exact date confirmed from PDF.** |
| **August** | **15 Oct 1834**, Friedrichsfeld | **17 Oct 1834** | **DIED AT 2 DAYS OLD. Father Andreas was godfather — "very unusual" per Peter. Rules out August entirely.** |

**Second wife's death:** Maria Sophia Hofeditz died **15 October 1835**. This means no further children could be born to her after this date.

**CRITICAL OBSERVATION — Timing with Charles Gannsen (UPDATED with PDF data):** Charles Gannsen was born approximately **1835** (various records give 1835–1837). Maria Sophia Hofeditz's last child August was born **15 October 1834** and died two days later. Maria Sophia herself died **15 October 1835**. A birth between roughly July–October 1835 is biologically possible (conceiving ~November 1834 after August's birth). However, **Peter's compiled PDF lists NO child between August (Oct 1834) and Maria Sophia's death (Oct 1835)**. This means either: (a) no such child was baptized in Trendelburg, or (b) the record was missed, or (c) Charles was born elsewhere.

**Key elimination from PDF:** Heinrich Wilhelm (b. 1832) **died 19 February 1858** — he cannot be Charles Gannsen. August (b. & d. October 1834) died at 2 days old. This leaves **Justus Ernst (b. 14 Nov 1829) as the ONLY surviving male son** with no recorded death date. He would be ~5–6 years older than Charles's stated birth year of 1835 — a significant but not impossible discrepancy given census age inaccuracies.

**Also from the first marriage:** Karl Wilhelm (b. 19.1.1824, godfather Ludwig Konze, farmer from Friedrichsfeld) died 6.8.1824 in infancy. Elisa Melusine's godmother was Maria Sophia Hofeditz — the future second wife was already connected to the family as early as 1822.

Peter's comment — "Charles is an unusual case, perhaps he belongs to this unusual family" — explicitly connects this family to the Charles Gannsen investigation.

**The critical confusion resolved:** §3.3 of the research paper originally attributed the Hofeditz marriages to the Stammtafel Andreas (b. 1787). This was wrong. The December 16, 1827 marriage entry in register 220120 — "Andreas Konze (Witwer) × Maria Doris/Sophia Hofedietz" — is this man, the 1795 Andreas, marrying his deceased first wife's sister. The name "Doris" in the original transcription should read "Sophia" (Maria Sophia Hofeditz).

**Father "Friedrich Konze" — open question:** His father Friedrich Konze may connect to the Deisel Registerband entries (§11.34.2), which list two Andreas Kontze births with father "Friedrich" (Entry 94: b. 2 Jan 1717; Entry 167: b. 19 Jun 1768). If so, the 1795 Andreas is a grandson of this earlier Friedrich line. This would make his father a brother or half-brother of Andreas I (Küster) or Johann Friedrich Kontze. Peter Schrader requires additional Archion access to resolve this. **April 5 update:** The grandfather being a Ratsherr may help narrow the "Friedrich Konze" identity — the Trendelburg council records (Ratsprotokoll) could potentially be searched for a Konze council member in the 1770s–1790s.

**Why he matters:** His separation from Identity 3 was the key correction of April 3, 2026. The children found in the Trendelburg confirmation entries (§3.4 — Anna Christine Konze c. 1798–1803, Maria Wilhelmine Konze b. 1807, Christine Wilhelmine Konze c. 1805) are now **unattributed**: they are too old to be this man's children (he was born 1795), but whether they belong to the Stammtafel Andreas (b. 1787) or to yet another Konze man remains an open question.

**NEW (April 5): Why he matters for Charles Gannsen.** Peter Schrader explicitly suggests Charles may belong to this "unusual" family. The key parallels are: (a) Andreas left his family secretly — a pattern of departure that could run in the family; (b) his sons from the second marriage (Justus Ernst b. 1829, Heinrich Wilhelm b. 1832, August b. 1834) would have been teenagers/young adults in the 1850s — the same period Charles emigrated; (c) the family was based in the Trendelburg-Friedrichsfeld area, consistent with Charles's stated origins; (d) the "unusual" nature of the family (rapid births, self-godparentage, secret abandonment) is consistent with the kind of disrupted family history that produces hard-to-trace emigrants.

## What §3.3 Got Wrong and How It Was Corrected

**The original error (§3.3, written March 26–27):** When the burial register for the Stammtafel Andreas (Identity 3) was read, it mentioned his first wife as "Wilhelmine." The researcher assumed this Wilhelmine was Wilhelmine née Hofeditz, creating a narrative where one Andreas married Hofeditz first, then married Anna Christina Heidt in 1818 after his first wife died.

**Peter Schrader's correction (April 3, 2026):** Peter's examination of the "Andreas Konze.pdf" records proved that the Hofeditz marriages belonged to a completely different man — Andreas Konze born 1795, son of Friedrich (Identity 4). The Stammtafel Andreas's first wife "Wilhelmine" was actually **Wilhelmine née Grossbernd** (confirmed by baptism records of their children, §11.15.2–3).

**Cascade of corrections:**
- §3.3 — The Hofeditz attribution is wrong. The burial register's "Wilhelmine" = Grossbernd, not Hofeditz.
- §3.4 — The confirmation-era children (Anna Christine, Maria Wilhelmine, Christine Wilhelmine) cannot belong to Identity 4 (too old). They may belong to Identity 3 or an unknown sixth man.
- §3.5 — Anna Christine Konze's marriage to Carl Wilhelm Hofedietz (1820) is still valid, but her father is no longer securely identified.
- §3.6 — The Dec 16, 1827 marriage entry "Andreas Konze (widower) × Maria Sophia Hofedietz" = Identity 4, not Identity 3.
