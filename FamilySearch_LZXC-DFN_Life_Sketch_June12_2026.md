# Louise Jansen / Louise Johnson / Louise Foster (LZXC-DFN) — Life Sketch

> Drafted 12 June 2026 for the FamilySearch Life Sketch field. Grounded in the sources attached
> to the profile and the project page children/louise.html.

---

Louise Johnson was born 26 December 1873 in St. Louis, Missouri, and baptized Louise Jansen, the fifth of seven children of Charles Johnson Sr. — a Hessian-born immigrant and Civil War artillery sergeant — and Auguste Kreichelt, born in Clausthal, Hanover. She was baptized 22 February 1874 at St. Marcus German Evangelical Church (register p. 402, no. 8178). Her sponsors were her grandmother and namesake, Louise Henriette Kreichelt née Weis, and her aunt Barbara "Babette" Kreichelt, then just thirteen. Her baptism entry is the first appearance of the "Jansen" spelling in the family record — the J-form that marked the start of the drift from the family's German surname toward its eventual American name, Johnson.

She grew up on the south side of St. Louis, appearing in the 1880 census household on Jackson Street, and as the family's name settled she became Louise Johnson. About 1896 she married a Mr. Foster in St. Louis — her father's 1898 pension declaration lists her as "Louise Foster, born December 1873." On 20 May 1903 she married William Fletcher (1878–1954) in St. Louis, and their daughter Helen L. Fletcher was born in 1910; the records also note a marriage to a Mr. Beck about 1905. The censuses then trace her far from her St. Louis beginnings — Tacoma, Washington, in 1910 and 1920, and San Joaquin County, California, in 1930 — and she and Fletcher divorced before 1940.

Through her St. Louis years she remained close to her brother, Charles Johnson Jr.: she stood as baptismal sponsor for all three of his sons — Wilhelm Thomas (1896), Henry Charles (1903), and Frederick Charles (1905) — the family's most faithful recurring sponsor of the next generation. She was twenty-two when her mother died in 1896 and twenty-nine when her father died in 1903, one of the four children who survived them. Louise died 17 February 1947 in St. Louis, at age seventy-three, and was buried 20 February 1947 at Laurel Hill Memorial Gardens in Pagedale, St. Louis County.
