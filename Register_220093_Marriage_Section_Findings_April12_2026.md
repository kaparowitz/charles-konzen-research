## 1. Summary

A systematic search of the marriage section of Register 220093 (covering 1708–1772) yielded **nine Kontze-related marriage entries** spanning 1752–1771. These entries revolutionize the understanding of the Kontze family's pre-1773 structure by identifying **three Generation 0 patriarchs** and establishing critical father-son linkages that reshape the entire family model.

The most significant discovery: **Arnold Philipp Konze was a WIDOWER** when he married Anna Dorothea Uffelmann in 1754 — meaning he had a first wife (unknown) who is the mother of his older children, including Joh. Henrich (eldest son, married 1773).

## 2. Complete Marriage Entries Found

### Marriage A: [February 24, 1752] — Anna Catharina Kontze

- **Groom:** [Name partially cut off — needs re-verification]
- **Bride:** Anna Catharina Kontze, **des Nies Kontzen Sen. hinterlassene jüngste Tochter** (the late Nies Kontze Senior's remaining youngest daughter)
- **Key finding:** Introduces **Nies Kontze Senior** as a Generation 0 patriarch, deceased by 1752. "Nies" is a diminutive of Johannes or Nicolaus. He was designated "Senior," implying there was a "Junior" contemporary.

### Marriage B: [May 9, 1754] — Maria Elisabeth Kontze × Dionysius Pfaffenrieder

- **Groom:** Dionysius Pfaffenrieder, Wittwer (widower)
- **Bride:** Maria Elisabeth, des **Joan Henrif Kontzen** allhier Tochter (daughter of Joan Henrif Kontze of Trendelburg)
- **Key finding:** Identifies **Joan Henrif Kontze** as a second Generation 0 patriarch, alive in May 1754 with a marriageable daughter.

### Marriage C: [July 24, 1754] — Arnold Philip Konze × Anna Dorothea Uffelmann — **THE BIG ONE**

- **Groom:** Arnold Philip Konze, **Wittwer** und Gemeinde-Vorsteher, Ackermann (WIDOWER, community leader/overseer, farmer)
- **Bride:** Anna Dorothea Uffelmannin, des Raths Verwandten Joannes Uffelmanns jüngste Tochter (youngest daughter of councillor Joannes Uffelmann)
- **Location of marriage:** In Kassel (not Trendelburg)
- **Critical findings:**
  1. Arnold Philipp had a **FIRST wife** who died before July 1754 — she is the mother of any children born before 1754
  2. He already held civic office: Gemeinde-Vorsteher (community leader/overseer)
  3. The marriage took place in **Kassel**, not Trendelburg — unusual, suggesting a connection to the bride's family there
  4. The bride's father Joannes Uffelmann was a Rathsverwandter (council member) — another council-family alliance
  5. Arnold Philipp's eldest son Joh. Henrich (married 1773) was likely born in the 1740s–early 1750s, making him a child of the FIRST wife, not Anna Dorothea Uffelmann

### Marriage D: [~October 1755] — Maria Elisabeth née Kontze × Joannes Schaub

- **Groom:** Joannes Schaub, lediger Gesell, Weher (unmarried journeyman, weaver)
- **Bride:** Frau Maria Elisabeth geb. Kontzin, des Nies [Kontzen...] (Maria Elisabeth née Kontze, described as "Frau" indicating she was a widow remarrying)
- **Key finding:** Another daughter of **Nies Kontze**. She was a widow ("Frau") at this marriage.

### Marriage E: [December 21, 1761] — Anna Christina Kontze × Joannes [Nagel?]

- **Groom:** Joannes [Nagel?], Wittwer (widower)
- **Bride:** Anna Christina Kontze, **des Nies Kontzen, Ackermanns hinterlassene Tochter** (daughter of the late Nies Kontze, farmer)
- **Key finding:** Confirms Nies Kontze's occupation as **Ackermann** (farmer) — matching the family trade. Third known daughter of Nies Kontze.

### Marriage F: [February 5, 1762] — Philip Jordan × Anna Catharina Kontze

- **Groom:** Philip Jordan, lediger Geselle (unmarried journeyman)
- **Bride:** Anna Catharina Kontze, **Joan Henrif Kontzen Tochter** (daughter of Joan Henrif Kontze)
- **Key finding:** Joan Henrif Kontze is **NOT described as deceased** — he was still **alive** in February 1762. Second known daughter.

### Marriage G: [August 5, 1762] — Philip Konze × Martha Elisabeth Stein

- **Groom:** Philip Konze, lediger Geselle und Sergeant (unmarried journeyman and [former] Sergeant), **Joan Henrif Kontzen nachgelassener Sohn** (surviving son of the late Joan Henrif Kontze)
- **Bride:** Martha Elisabeth Steirin, des Accis-Einnehmer Joannes Steids Tochter (daughter of excise tax collector Joannes Steid)
- **CRITICAL TIMELINE:** Joan Henrif Kontze was **ALIVE** in February 1762 (Marriage F) but **DEAD** by August 1762 (this entry: "nachgelassener"). Joan Henrif Kontze **died between February and August 1762.**
- **Key finding:** Philip Konze is confirmed as **Joan Henrif Kontze's SON** — a former Sergeant who married into the tax collector's family.

### Marriage H: [August 10, 1762] — Christoph [Dornhage?] × Catharina Kontze

- **Groom:** Christoph [Dornhage?], lediger Geselle (unmarried journeyman)
- **Bride:** Catharina Kontze, **des Andreas Kontze hinterlassene Tochter** (daughter of the late Andreas Kontze)
- **Key finding:** Introduces **Andreas Kontze** as a **THIRD Generation 0 patriarch**, deceased by August 1762. At least one known daughter.

### Marriage I: [January 25, 1771] — Henning Albrecht × Anna Margaretha Kontze — **P5 RESOLVED**

- **Groom:** Henning Albrecht
- **Bride:** Anna Margaretha, **des Rathsverwandten Philip Kontzen älteste Tochter** (eldest daughter of Rathsverwandter [council member] Philip Kontze)
- **Key finding:** Directly resolves Priority 5 from Part XXI. Philip Kontze — the former Sergeant who married Martha Elisabeth Stein in August 1762 (Marriage G) — had risen to the rank of **Rathsverwandter** (council member) by 1771. His eldest daughter Anna Margaretha married Henning Albrecht, confirming the Albrecht–Kontze marriage that was known from the 1776 baptism entry (Part XXI, §XXI.2.1).
- **Significance:** Philip Kontze is now confirmed as both (a) the SON of Joan Henrif Kontze and (b) the FATHER of Anna Margaretha who married Henning Albrecht. This establishes a three-generation chain: Joan Henrif Kontze → Philip Kontze → Anna Margaretha Kontze (m. Albrecht).

## 3. The Three Generation 0 Patriarchs

| # | Patriarch | Occupation | Death Status | Known Children |
|---|-----------|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| 1 | **Nies Kontze Senior** (= Johannes or Nicolaus) | Ackermann (farmer) | Dead by Feb 1752 | Anna Catharina (m. 1752, "youngest daughter"), Maria Elisabeth (m. ~1755, widow), Anna Christina (m. 1761) |
| 2 | **Joan Henrif Kontze** | Unknown (civic status implied) | Died Feb–Aug 1762 | Maria Elisabeth (m. 1754), Anna Catharina (m. Feb 1762), **Philip Konze** (son, m. Aug 1762) |
| 3 | **Andreas Kontze** | Unknown | Dead by Aug 1762 | Catharina (m. 1762) |

**Key question:** What is the relationship between these three men and **Arnold Philipp Kontze** (the fourth patriarch, already documented from 220096 marriages of 1773 and 1780)?

**Working hypothesis:** These four men — Nies, Joan Henrif, Andreas, and Arnold Philipp — were likely siblings or father-and-sons, all active in Trendelburg in the mid-18th century. The "Senior" designation on Nies suggests generational differentiation. Joan Henrif's son Philip Konze rose to Rathsverwandter status, as did Arnold Philipp — suggesting they were of the same social class.

## 4. Arnold Philipp Konze — First Wife Discovery

The July 1754 marriage entry describes Arnold Philipp as a **"Wittwer"** (widower). This means:

- He had a **first wife** who died before July 1754 (name unknown)
- His eldest son Joh. Henrich Kontze (married 1773, described as ~25–30 at marriage) was likely born ~1743–1748, making him a child of the **first wife**
- His second son Johannes (married 1780, described as "2ter Sohn") may also be from the first wife, or possibly from Anna Dorothea Uffelmann
- Anna Dorothea Uffelmann (married 1754) is the **second wife** and mother of any children born after July 1754

**Next research target:** Search Register 220093 baptism section for Kontze children in the 1740s–1770s to determine which children belong to the first wife vs. Anna Dorothea Uffelmann.

## 5. Philip Kontze — Career Arc

| Date | Event | Title/Status |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Before Aug 1762 | Father Joan Henrif Kontze dies | — |
| Aug 5, 1762 | Marries Martha Elisabeth Stein | "lediger Geselle und Sergeant" (former Sergeant) |
| Jan 25, 1771 | Daughter Anna Margaretha marries Henning Albrecht | "Rathsverwandter" (council member) |
| Oct 24, 1796 | Daughter Anna Margaretha marries Johannes Albrecht | "Bürger" (citizen) — described in Register 220096, Marriage #5 |

Philip rose from military service (Sergeant) to council membership (Rathsverwandter) in under 9 years — a significant social advancement. His wife Martha Elisabeth was the daughter of the excise tax collector (Accis-Einnehmer) Joannes Steid, which may have facilitated his civic advancement.

**Note on the 1796 entry:** The Part XXI document records a daughter "Anna Margaretha" of "Johann Philipp Kontze" marrying Johannes Albrecht in 1796. If this is the same Philip Kontze, he had TWO daughters named Anna Margaretha (one married 1771, one married 1796 — a 25-year gap), or the 1796 bride was a granddaughter. Alternatively, the "Johann Philipp Kontze" of 1796 could be a different man — possibly the Johann Philipp Kontze (b. ~Oct 2, 1768, d. Feb 21, 1831) documented in the burial section of Register 220099 (Part VIII), who was the son of Rathsverwandter Andreas Konze and Anna Christina geb. Konze.

## 6. Joan Melchior Wichard — Supplementary Finding

On the same image as the 1752 marriage entries, an entry records the marriage of **Joan Melchior Wichard**, son of **Bürgermeister George Wichard** — confirming the Wiechardt family held the mayor's office (Bürgermeister) in Trendelburg. This is the same Wiechardt family whose daughter Martha Elisabetha married Joh. Henrich Kontze in 1773 (Register 220096, Marriage #1). The civic alliance between the Kontze and Wiechardt families spanned at least two generations.

## 7. Revised Generational Framework

### Pre-Register Generation (~1690–1720): The Founders

- **Nies Kontze Senior** (Ackermann, d. before 1752) — at least 3 daughters
- Possibly related to a "Nies Kontze Junior" (implied by "Senior" designation)

### Generation 0 (~1710–1740): The Patriarchs

- **Joan Henrif Kontze** (d. Feb–Aug 1762) — at least 2 daughters + son Philip
- **Andreas Kontze** (d. before Aug 1762) — at least 1 daughter
- **Arnold Philipp Kontze** (Gemeinde-Vorsteher/Ackermann, 1st wife d. before 1754, 2nd wife Anna Dorothea Uffelmann m. 1754 in Kassel; d. 1774–1780) — sons Joh. Henrich (1st) and Johannes (2nd)
- **"Elder Johann Henrich Kontze"** (Rathsverwandter, d. before Feb 1774) — daughter Anna Margaretha (m. Lotz 1774)

**Possible relationship:** Joan Henrif, Andreas, Arnold Philipp, and Elder J.H. Kontze could all be sons of Nies Kontze Senior. Alternatively, Joan Henrif and Elder J.H. could be the same person — but the 1774 Lotz marriage describes the bride's father as "weiland Johann Henrich Kontze, Rathsverwandter," while Joan Henrif Kontze died in 1762 without the Rathsverwandter title. These are more likely different individuals.

### Generation 1 (~1740–1770): The Established Families

- **Joh. Henrich Kontze the Younger** (eldest son of Arnold Philipp) — 3 marriages (1773, 1783, 1797)
- **Johannes Kontze** (2nd son of Arnold Philipp) — 2 marriages (1780, 1806)
- **Philip Kontze** (son of Joan Henrif) — Sergeant → Rathsverwandter; married 1762
- **Johann Friedrich Kontze** (b. 1763, d. 1823) — later documented in 220099 burials; son of an "Andreas Konze, B. u. Ackermann" — possibly the Andreas Kontze of Generation 0
