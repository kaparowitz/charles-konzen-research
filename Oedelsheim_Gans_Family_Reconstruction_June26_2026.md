## ⚠️ IMPORTANT CORRECTION (27 Jun 2026) — the "emigration" is most likely an UNUSED PERMIT
**Read this before the breakthrough section below.** Checking the parish records
(KB 1810–1830, reg. 219940) substantially **undercut** the emigration story:
- The LAGIS / Günther–Filby entries are **PERMIT records** — *Entlassung aus dem
  Untertanenverband* (release from subject status), i.e. **applications/intent to
  emigrate**, filed in the Marburg Innenministerium files. **Getting a permit ≠
  actually leaving.** Many Hessians applied and then stayed.
- The **only Georg Friedrich Gans I can document in Oedelsheim is the turner
  (Drechsler) × Maria Elisabeth née Kohlschein — and he demonstrably STAYED:**
  son **Georg Friedrich Wilhelm Gans baptised 28 Mar 1821** (Bild 536; with a death
  note **†12 Feb 1845, in Oedelsheim**), plus **Andreas August (1835)** and **Sophie
  Rosine Charlotte (1837)**. A man baptising children in Oedelsheim in 1835 and 1837
  did **not** emigrate in 1834.
- **Therefore the 1834 "emigration to America (wife + 3 children)" was almost
  certainly the turner's UNUSED permit** — he applied for release but stayed. Unless
  a *second*, separate Georg Friedrich Gans existed (none found yet), **there was no
  actual 1834 Gans emigration**, and the US-arrival lead is a **spurious permit, not
  a real departure.**

**Net effect on the hypothesis:** the clean "Gans family emigrated 1834 → Charles"
story is **substantially weakened, probably wrong.** What survives: Oedelsheim still
has a real, large Gans population in the right place/era; the only remaining (thin)
route to Charles would be **a single Gans youth — a son named Carl — emigrating
*alone* later**, but (a) I found **no Carl Gans** baptism in 1834–1841, (b) the
turner's pre-1834 children aren't yet fully listed, and (c) "the family stayed in
Oedelsheim" sits awkwardly with the US-side "no family found" only if Charles left
solo. **Treat the Oedelsheim-Gans thread as an open, weakened lead — not the answer.**
The honest lesson: emigration-database hits must be checked against the parish
records before being believed, because they are permits.

## a Gans family emigrated Oedelsheim → North America in 1834
The **LAGIS "Hessische Auswanderer"** emigration database (Hessisches Landesarchiv)
holds a direct, citable record:

> **Gans, Georg Friedrich — Oedelsheim, 1834** (LAGIS ID 110864; permalink
> `https://lagis.hessen.de/resolve/de/ha/110864`).
> **Auswanderungsziel: Nordamerika** (North America). **Datierung: 1834.**
> **Anzahl Auswandernde: 4** (a family of four). Vermögen 110.
> Source: **Hessisches Staatsarchiv Marburg, Best. 16 Nr. 3216, S. 199** — the
> Innenministerium files on "Auswanderungen / Entlassung aus dem Untertanenverband"
> (emigration permits / release from subject status).

This sits inside a **Weser-valley Gans emigration cluster** (all Landkreis Kassel,
the project's search zone): **Oedelsheim 1834**, **Heisebeck 1840 & 1846** ("Frau
des Henrich Wilhelm Gans"), **Gieselwerder 1846** (Christine Charlotte Gans; Georg
Wilhelm Gans; "Frau des Henrich Ferdinand Gans"). The Gans surname was leaving this
stretch of the Weser **en masse, 1834–1846, for North America.**

### Why this likely cracks the Charles mystery
- The **Oedelsheim turner** Georg Friedrich Gans (Drechslermeister, Hausnr 2)
  **stayed** — he kept baptising children in Oedelsheim in 1835 and 1837. So the
  **1834 emigrant Georg Friedrich Gans is a *separate* household** (a relative /
  namesake) that actually **left for North America**.
- The family of **4 left in 1834**; **Charles was born 26 Jan 1835** — i.e. **the
  year *after* this family emigrated.** A child born in early 1835, just after the
  family reached (or was reaching) America, **would have no German baptism** — which
  is exactly the wall this project has hit ("the German baptism remains unrecovered").
  And it fits the read evidence: I searched **all of January 1835 in Oedelsheim and
  found no Carl Gans baptism** — consistent with Charles being born *abroad*, not in
  Oedelsheim.
- "**Gans**" → anglicised "**Gannsen / Ganson**" is the project's own surname canon.

**Working hypothesis (strong, not yet proven):** *Charles Gannsen was a son of the
Georg-Friedrich-Gans household that emigrated from Oedelsheim to North America in
1834, born 26 Jan 1835 after the family left Germany — hence no German baptism.*

### US-side corroboration (Ancestry, Jed's login) — and a sharpened hypothesis
A **second, independent** index confirms the emigration and adds the family size:
> **Georg Friedrich Gans** — *U.S. and Canada, Passenger and Immigration Lists Index,
> 1500s–1900s* (Ancestry coll. 7486, rec. 129797). **Arrival 1834, America.**
> **Family Members: Wife & 3 children.** Annotation: *"All from counties of the
> province Nether-Hesse, northeastern part of the former territory of Hesse-Cassel."*
> Source: **Kurt Günther, "Hessian Emigrants to America," *Germanic Genealogist*
> no. 15 (1978), pp. 374–377, p. 375** (Parts 2–3: 1833–1834).

So both LAGIS (Marburg file) and Filby/Günther record the **same** Georg Friedrich
Gans emigrating to America **1834** from **Niederhessen / Hesse-Cassel**, with a
**wife and 3 children**. (Related: *Johann Georg Gans*, arrival **1846**, matches
the Gieselwerder/Heisebeck 1846 Gans cluster; and a *George W. Ganse, born
Hesse-Cassel, naturalised 1852 Cleveland, Ohio* — a possible relative/settlement clue.)

**Sharpened working hypothesis.** The emigrant Georg Friedrich Gans is **not** the
Oedelsheim *turner* (who stayed and only began having children in 1835) — he is a
**separate Gans household that left in 1834 with a wife and 3 children**. **Charles
(Carl) was most likely one of those 3 children** — **born in Kurhessen ~1832–1834**
and emigrating as a toddler in 1834. This fits the American evidence far better than
a 1835 birth: the family's belief that he was **"born in Kurhessen"**, the **death
certificate's "born 1834"**, and the surname **Gans → Gannsen**. The "26 Jan 1835"
is a single post-mortem family estimate and may be a year off.

**Decisive next steps (sharpened):**
1. **Find the emigrant family's 3 children's baptisms** in Oedelsheim/Niederhessen
   **~1826–1834** — i.e. a Georg Friedrich Gans father whose wife is **not**
   Kohlschein (the turner's wife) — and look for a **son Carl**. Search **KB
   1810–1830 (reg. 219940)** + the 1830–34 part of **219946**, and nearby filial
   parishes if "Oedelsheim" is the Amt rather than the exact village.
2. **Get Kurt Günther, *Germanic Genealogist* no. 15 (1978) p. 375** — the article
   may **name the wife and the 3 children** directly.
3. **US census:** trace this Georg Friedrich Gans family in the **1840/1850 census**
   (Ohio? — cf. the Hesse-Cassel Ganse in 1852 Cleveland; or Missouri, where Charles
   ended up) and identify **Charles among the children**; tie to the Battery B soldier.

### Reality check (Jed) + honest status — 26 Jun 2026
Two facts Jed flagged, and where they leave us:
- **"Charles said he was born in Germany."** ✓ Consistent. It rules out the
  born-at-sea / born-in-America variant and supports **born in Kurhessen, emigrated
  as a child** — exactly the sharpened hypothesis.
- **"No family has been found" (in the US).** This is the real obstacle, and it
  cuts both ways. Tested this session on the US side:
  - **1850 census, "George Gans" b. Germany ~1805 → only one George W. Gans, and he
    was *Pennsylvania*-born (not ours).** "Charles/Carl Gans" b. ~1834 Germany in
    1850 → **zero.** So the family does **not** surface in the 1850 census under a
    plain "Gans" + German birth.
  - **Günther / Filby give only "wife & 3 children" — the children are NOT named**
    (these emigration compilations record head + counts).
- **Net:** the **1834 Gans emigration is a strong *circumstantial* lead** — right
  surname (**Gans → Gannsen**), right place (**Oedelsheim**, the Weser-Konze zone),
  right era, **German birth** — and it would neatly explain why the project, which
  only ever searched *Konze / Gannsen / Gonson / Johnson*, **never found the family
  (it would be under "Gans")**. **But it is not yet tied to Charles by evidence**, and
  "no family found" still stands: the Gans family isn't surfacing in the 1850 census
  under "Gans" either. So either the surname was heavily anglicised/the family
  dispersed, **or** Charles emigrated apart from this family. **Treat this as the
  leading new hypothesis, not a conclusion.**

## The Charles question — answered: NO
Across the **fully-read 1834–1839 window** there is **no boy with rufname
Carl/Karl on a Gans surname**, and **no Gans child born 26 Jan 1835**:
- The Gans sons found are **Andreas August** (1835) and **Friedrich Wilhelm** (1839).
- The Gans child closest to Charles's 26-Jan-1835 birthdate is **Andreas August
  Gans, b. 25 Feb 1835** — wrong forename and 30 days later; and being the turner's
  1835 child, a same-mother "Carl b. 26 Jan 1835" in that household is impossible.
- The only person actually named "**Carl … Gans**" is the adult **Carl August
  Gans** (the innkeeper's son, ~b. 1810–18) — ~20 years too old to be Charles.

**Conclusion:** Charles is **not** a documented Oedelsheim Gans baptism of 1835.
If he connects to this Gans population at all, the link runs through **emigration
of a Gans son in the ~1850s**, not a 26-Jan-1835 baptism here. A long-shot
alternative worth flagging only: that Charles's US-recorded "26 Jan 1835" is a
near-miss for **Andreas August Gans (25 Feb 1835)** — but the Andreas→Charles/Carl
name change makes this weak.

left.** This is the pattern one would expect if a Gans son (→ "Gannsen") emigrated
