Open Questions

An explicit inventory of what the project has searched for and not yet found — with what would resolve each gap. World-class research is transparent about what it does not know.

A research site that lists only its findings is incomplete. This page lists the documents the project has actively searched for and not yet located, the candidates that remain open, and the embargoed materials whose existence has been documented but whose contents are not yet imaged on the site. Each entry names the specific evidence that would resolve it — so a future researcher (or a descendant who happens to walk into the right archive) knows exactly what to look for.

Priority gaps — directly load-bearing on the central thesis

Charles's German baptism record

Priority 1
Last attempted: Session 24 · 18 April 2026 · multi-register Jan-1835 illegitimacy scan

The single most-wanted document in the entire project. Charles's 1866 St. Marcus marriage register names "Deisse, Kreis Cassel, Churhessen" — phonetically Deisel — as his place of origin. The Trendelburg parish complex's Konze / Kontze / Contzen baptismal registers have been searched at maximum zoom for January 1835 with no Charles / Carl entry surfacing under the leading-hypothesis surname.

What would resolve this

A Trendelburg / Deisel / Friedrichsfeld / Helmarshausen / Hofgeismar baptismal entry dated late January 1835 (or up to several weeks before or after) for a male child whose surname is in the Konze pool, with a father or godfather name that matches one of the documented Konze branch heads. Alternative resolutions: an illegitimate-birth entry whose father is later legitimised; a parish jurisdiction the project has not yet identified.

Heisebeck post-1831 baptisms — now digitised; an illegitimate “Carl” candidate

Open · candidate found
Status (updated 21 June 2026): the post-1831 baptism volume is no longer undigitised — Archion now carries Heisebeck Taufen 1830–1887 (register 219175), which covers the target window. It was read live and the January 1835 entries transcribed. (The earlier-searched marriages 1808–1921 / KB 219169 / 219190 remain negative, Sessions 66–67.) The companion gap, Liebenau 1835, is likewise now digitised and was searched and eliminated (see the Liebenau research log).

The January 1835 window yielded entry 76: a male illegitimate child named “Carl Friedrich August,” born 29 January 1835 (baptized 8 February 1835, Heisebeck Oberdorf No. 33), mother Dorothee Henriette Charlotte Wehmann, the unmarried daughter of the Wehmann (Köster/sexton) family; no father is named, so the child carries the mother’s surname. Crucially, his remarks column is empty — unlike his immediate neighbours (entry 75 Georg Müller and entry 79 both read “confirmed 1849”), he shows no confirmation, death, or marriage note, consistent with a child who left Heisebeck before confirmation age (~1849): the emigrant pattern. Permalink: archion.de/p/34e1ddfe6e (reg. 219175, image 1256).

This is the strongest single match the project has surfaced for the illegitimate-birth scenario the Y-DNA keeps live — male, given name Carl, born late January 1835, illegitimate, in the parish flagged as the #1 priority gap. It is a candidate, not a confirmed identity: the recorded birth is 29 January (Charles’s stated date is 26 January — three days off) and the surname is Wehmann, not Gannsen/Konze. The other Heisebeck window males are legitimate and non-matching (entry 75, Georg Müller, b. 25 Nov 1834; entry 77, Johann Wilhelm Wenkel, b. 23 Feb 1835).

What would resolve this

Follow-ups worked 22 June 2026. The entry-76 reading was re-verified live on Archion (219175, image 1256): birth "den neun und zwanzigsten … [Januar], sieben Abends" (29 Jan 1835, 7 p.m.), and the remarks column is confirmed blank. The Heisebeck confirmation register (Archion 219172) was searched for the b.1835–36 cohort and contains no Carl Friedrich August Wehmann — corroborating an early departure (the blank baptism-remarks column is the decisive proof, since Heisebeck back-references confirmations there). An open-web US forward-trace returned no match; manifests, census, and Find A Grave entries are behind FamilySearch/Ancestry logins, and the free NARA AAD “Germans to America” index has not yet been run on the Wehmann surname (recommended next step). Reconciling an illegitimate Wehmann birth with the documented Konze Y-DNA signature (unknown father) remains the open analytic question. See the full Heisebeck research log.

DNA evidence — awaiting an informative match

Tested · no confirming match
Status: Testing done — Y-DNA via FamilyTreeDNA Big Y-700 (haplogroup I2-M223; terminal subclade I-Y38863); autosomal via AncestryDNA and MyHeritage. See the genetic-evidence section in the methodology. Results to date are consistent with a Hessian origin but do not yet confirm the Konze line.

The Big Y-700 places Charles's patriline on terminal subclade I-Y38863 (branch formed ~1400 CE; members' common ancestor ~1450 CE; three tested members with origins in Germany, Poland, and the Russian Federation). The recent-ish branch age means a Konze Y-match would be genuinely probative — but none of the few current members carries a Konze surname, which with so small a sample neither confirms nor refutes the conclusion. The autosomal estimates (a descendant test, run through both AncestryDNA and MyHeritage) are roughly half Germanic — Southern Germanic Europe 33%, Northwestern Germany 16%, Netherlands 5% at Ancestry; Germanic 36.4% + Dutch 12% at MyHeritage — consistent with the German origin, but a descendant's admixture averages all ancestral lines, cannot isolate the Konze line, and resolves no finer than a broad region that merely includes Hesse; no autosomal match has yet been tied to a documented Trendelburg–Deisel Konze descendant. The genetic evidence therefore corroborates the broad origin without promoting the surname or parish conclusions off Working.

What would resolve this

Any one of: (1) a Y-DNA match to a documented male-line descendant of the Trendelburg–Deisel Konze / Kontzen family — direct confirmation of the surname conclusion; (2) an autosomal match to a documented descendant of a candidate Konze family within the genealogical timeframe; (3) autosomal matches corroborating the Kreichelt / Grandjot / Herwig lines independently of the parish paper trail. Recruiting a known Konze-line male in the Trendelburg area to Y-test is the highest-yield next step.

Deep paternal-line origin — a possible Ashkenazi-Jewish patriline

New · DNA-raised
Raised by: Y-STR / Big Y match review, 30 May 2026 — see the DNA evidence page.  YFull (independent) corroborates the tree placement: the YFull v14 tree places the line on the same subclade I-Y38863 (formed ~750 ybp, common ancestor ~700 ybp / ≈1300 CE) among east-central-European samples it flags as Ashkenazi — corroborating the FamilyTreeDNA reading on a separate tree.

Charles's closest paternal-line Y-DNA matches are Ashkenazi-Jewish lineages from east-central Europe — both men sharing his exact terminal subclade (I-Y38863) and the closest parent-clade (I-BY2640) match are Ashkenazi, three independent lines converging on his ~1400–1450 CE. This points to a Jewish origin in the deep male line — beneath, and not contradicting, the documented Hessian-Evangelical Konze family. It bears on two existing threads: it offers a possible reason the Konze baptism has never been found (an undocumented-paternity event, so the biological patriline is not the documented family) and it could connect to the January-1835 illegitimacy hypothesis (an unknown biological father carrying this line). This is not a new idea for the project: a Hessian-Jewish paternal-line inquiry ran from August 2025 and was set aside in May 2026 when its surname-resemblance basis (Gans → Gannsen) and tested candidates failed — the Meimbressen Gans / Segelbaum line and the Christian Gans of Lippoldsberg were both eliminated, and the Stern / Kleeburg line was never tied to Charles. The Y-DNA now supports that inquiry's direction on an entirely independent (biological) footing, while leaving those specific eliminations intact. The documented local Jewish presence — the 1740 Trendelburg "Protected Jews" (Seligmann Ganß and neighbours) — becomes worth re-examining as context, though the ~1450 CE branch age may predate it.

What would resolve this

(1) A documented record of illegitimacy, conversion, or an in-marrying paternal ancestor in the Trendelburg–Deisel Konze line; (2) closer Y-matches (lower genetic distance, more Big Y testers) that date and localise the Jewish-origin branch; (3) triangulating the several small Ashkenazi-surname autosomal matches already present in the Family Finder list (including one sharing the closest Y-match's surname) to test whether they cluster on a shared segment and a common ancestor. The interpretation is held as a working line of inquiry, not a conclusion.

1859 trans-Atlantic passenger manifest

Priority 2
Last attempted: May 2026 · ISTG Vol. 16 + Zenodo CHUP Bremen dataset + live Ancestry sweep (Hamburg, NY Castle Garden, New Orleans); 1860 census negative

Charles's 1866 St. Marcus marriage register states he was in St. Louis "since seven years" — a self-report placing arrival in 1859. A May 2026 sweep reframed the target: the 1860 U.S. Federal Census, queried under every spelling and birth year, contains no German-born Charles / Carl b. ~1835. That absence does not settle the arrival year, because it has two competing explanations: either he had not yet arrived on Census Day (1 June 1860) — which would make the self-reported "1859" ~1–2 years early, a common late-life rounding — or he was already in St. Louis and was simply missed by the enumerator or buried under a mistranscription (the reading held by the companion 1860 census question below). Either way the working window is roughly 1859 – summer 1862, up to his August 1862 enlistment; the census negative alone cannot narrow it to post-1860. The Bremen departure corpus that would have recorded it is largely destroyed (1875 fire / WWII): the ISTG Vol. 16 transcriptions (1853–1860, ~8,873 passengers reviewed) and the Zenodo CHUP Bremen dataset (735,545 records — 1858, 1859, 1861, 1862 empty) both return nothing, and a live Ancestry sweep of the Hamburg, New York / Castle Garden, and New Orleans indexes (ten surname variants × three collections, plus Carl-forename and K*nz* / G*ns* wildcard passes) produced zero matches. The closest name-shape match in the entire surviving index, Carl Ganns (b. 1835), was eliminated — Prussian-Rhineland residence, Brazil destination.

What would resolve this

A departure or arrival entry for a single male b. ~1835 from Hesse-Cassel / Churhessen under any rendering in the surname pool — Konze, Kontze, Contze, Konzen, Kontzen, Konsen, Gansen, Gonsen, Gonnson, Jansen, and under the German forename Carl. The indexed-database paths are now exhausted; the remaining avenues are all image-by-image: (1) the NARA M237 New York arrival microfilm read line-by-line across the full 1859 – summer 1862 window (U.S. arrival manifests survive even where the Bremen departure manifests don't); (2) Bremen-to-NY sailings of 1860–1862 outside the original ISTG 1858–1860 focus, which the census negative — if it reflects a true late arrival rather than a miss — moves up in priority; (3) the Baltimore, New Orleans, and Boston passenger collections; (4) Hamburg under a wider age window, and the Antwerp / Le Havre lists.

Hessian Auswanderer-Beleg (emigration permit)

Priority 3
Last attempted: April 2026 via Hessisches Staatsarchiv Marburg / Arcinsys

The Electorate of Hesse-Kassel required emigrants to register their departure with the local authority. For a young man of military age in 1859, the Auswanderer-Beleg would document his consent to emigrate, his place of origin, and his profession. The Konze / Kontzen surname pool has been searched in Arcinsys; no 1859 Beleg for a Charles / Carl matching the profile has been found.

What would resolve this

An Arcinsys hit on a Beleg dated 1858–1859 in the Trendelburg / Hofgeismar district, named subject male b. 1835, surname in the Konze pool. Alternatively, an unfiled-departure entry (a young man who left without registering) would be definitively resolved by the Stammrolle (below).

Hessian Stammrolle (military register)

Priority 4
Last attempted: April 2026 LAGIS query

Hessian males of military age were entered into a Stammrolle on or near their 20th birthday — for Charles, that would be January 1855. The register would record his parents, place of origin, and either his eventual conscription or his recorded emigration. Located registers for the Trendelburg complex have been searched without a definitive match; some volumes for the relevant district remain undigitized.

What would resolve this

An entry in the Trendelburg / Hofgeismar / Cassel-Land Stammrolle for the conscription class of 1855 listing Charles or any Konze-pool male b. 26 January 1835. A negative-but-definitive result (full register imaged, name absent) is also useful — it would force the search outward to adjacent districts.

1860 U.S. Census — Charles in St. Louis

Open
Last attempted: April 2026 · phonetic-variant search across the surname search pool

Per his 1866 marriage record, Charles was already in St. Louis by 1859. He should appear on the 1860 census (taken 1 June – August 1860) as an unmarried young man, born Hesse, occupation likely tradesman or laborer. No matching household has surfaced. The most parsimonious explanations: a mistranscription in the search index (against the surname variability already documented post-1866), an enumerator miss, or a boarding-house entry under an employer's surname. The continued absence narrows the search to image-by-image walks of the relevant St. Louis wards rather than further indexed-search attempts.

What would resolve this

An 1860 St. Louis enumeration with Charles age 25, born Hesse / Hesse-Cassel, under any phonetic variant of Konze/Gonsen/Konsen/Jansen/Gannsen/Conson. Image-by-image search of the relevant St. Louis wards is underway.

1870 U.S. Census — Charles & Auguste household

Open
Last attempted: 4 May 2026 · evening · three-pass targeted sweep

The 4 May 2026 sweep located Georg Kreichelt's household (Charles's father-in-law) at St. Louis Ward 03 — mistranscribed as "Reichel" — but Charles & Auguste's own household was not located across ten consecutive census images around the Kreichelts (~80 households). The address change between 1518 South 7th Street (1865–1869) and 718 Gates Avenue (1871) is the leading explanation: the household was likely missed by the enumerator during the move.

What would resolve this

A 1870 census image showing Charles (age ~35), Auguste (age ~27), and infant child(ren) in any St. Louis ward under any of the surname phonetic variants. Alternatively, a definitive negative result from a full ward-by-ward image walk would close the gap.

Resolved gaps — closed by evidence, kept here for transparency

The following lines were once "open questions" on the project; each has now been closed. They are listed here so a researcher reviewing past site states does not re-open them.

★ Charles's surname conclusion

Working — not closed

The convergent surname-trail analysis (see Key Evidence Card 1) concludes Konzen, the Hessian -en form documented in the Trendelburg / Friedrichsfeld parish complex. This is held at Working, not Confirmed: no German baptismal entry naming Charles has been recovered, so the conclusion is an inference to the Konze surname pool rather than a closed gap. It is listed here only to record that it was briefly promoted (4 May 2026) and returned to Working state — see errata and the claims dashboard. The open condition that would close it is in the gaps above (Priority 1, the German baptism).

★ Theodore Gottlob's Civil War service

Closed · 3 May 2026

Closed by the National Home for Disabled Volunteer Soldiers register: Sgt., Co. A, 9th Illinois Infantry, 1861–1864, including Vicksburg. Adds a sixth confirmed Union veteran to Charles's pension witness pool.

Carl Louis Herwig's U.S. trail

Closed · 3 May 2026

Closed by the 6 October 1855 Plymouth, MA marriage record (Lewes Herweg / Lewis Harwig and Catherine Sharda) — pivot from groom-side to bride-side search cracked the long-cold trail.

Trendelburg Register 220108, Seite 243 — Konze baptism

Closed · 31 May 2026

Recovered and verified directly on Archion (Register 220108, image 1623). Seite 243, entry № 953 — the last entry on a faintly-exposed page two earlier sessions could not confirm — is the Nottaufe (emergency baptism) of Johann Friedrich Wilhelm Konze: born 23 June 1861 at Trendelburg auf dem Berge (house № 227), emergency-baptised at home 28 June 1861. Father: Karl Wilhelm Konze, Einlieger (lodger); mother: Marie geb. Lauterbach, von Deisel; godfather: Johannes Köhler, Ackerbürger. The remarks column is blank — no death note and no later church-confirmation recorded. The father (b. ~1831) is already eliminated as Charles's father; the entry's value is a documented Trendelburg–Deisel Konze link (the mother from Deisel) and confirmation of a male-line Konze family continuing in Trendelburg after 1830.
