## Move 1 — Register 218842 Taufen 1830-1861 scan

Navigated to viewer, confirmed 287 options total. Register structure:

- Options 3-4: first 1830 baptisms in tabular format (columns: Nro., Geburt, Taufe, Geschlecht/Namen des Kindes, Eltern, Taufpathen, Bemerkungen)
- Options ~5 through ~265: chronological baptisms 1830-1861
- Options 269-283: alphabetical surname index at the end, organised by house number then page

Index scan results (options 269-274):
- Option 270: H/D range (Dietz, Hildebrand)
- Option 271-272: transition from H through L (Leymann, Lauterbach, Losse at 272)
- Option 273: M/N transition
- Option 274: N (Niemeyer, Nagel)
- Option 279: S (Schildknecht, Schrage, Schäfer, Starcke, Stein, Stieglitz)
- Option 283: V/W (Wiegand, Weisenbach, Wiese, Weller)

**Key finding**: The K section (between H and L) shows no clearly identifiable "Konze" or "Kontze" family-head entries. At subscriber zoom the left-page K section of option 270-271 shows surnames like Hildebrand, Dietz, and others but nothing legibly reading "Konze" as an independent family name. This argues against two hypotheses:

1. Justus Ernst Konze was not baptised late in 218842 (delayed from 14 Nov 1829 into early 1830) — no Konze father-surname family appears in the 1830-1861 Deisel baptism index.
2. No 1835 unrecorded Konze child was baptised in Deisel's separated Taufen register — the alternative Charles Gannsen origin hypothesis (Path β: Deisel Konze child born 1835, emigrated as "Charles Gannsen") loses primary-source support from 218842.

**Limitation**: The alphabetical index scan was at subscriber zoom level, not microscope-level. A dedicated Session 18 re-scan at max zoom of the K section is warranted before declaring the hypothesis dead. House numbers and Kurrent flourishes can conceal a Konze entry.

## Move 3 — Image 1746 eodem entry high-zoom read

Navigated back to 218824 option 97 and attempted maximum zoom. Findings:

- **Right-page top segment is December 1829** (not January 1830 as initially assumed from the "1830" header location). The header actually reads "[Monat] ... Getaufte im Jahren 1829" as the closing of 1829; the "1830" header appears lower on the page where the year-end summary transitions to 1830.
- The eodem entry with "et uxoris Carlotta geb. Konze" sits in the late-1829 tail, likely entry number Z.28–Z.30 of 1829.
- Child's given name at subscriber zoom is still obscured. Legible fragments suggest it may start with a consonant cluster such as "Chr" (e.g., Christian) or "H" (e.g., Heinrich). The name does NOT legibly read "Justus Ernst".
- Death marginalia on this entry reads "27. Juni 1891" — the child lived ~62 years.

**Conclusion**: The "Carlotta geb. Konze" entry is unlikely to be Justus Ernst's baptism, because (a) the father's surname is not Konze (only the mother's maiden name is), and (b) the given name doesn't match. However, this entry DOES provide a third 1828–1829 Konze-mother data point (Maria Elisabeth geb. Konzen; Wilhelmina geb. Konzen; Carlotta geb. Konze).

### Structural observations

1. Trendelburg parallels Deisel in post-1831 record-keeping: combined KB until ~1831, then separated Taufen/Trauungen/Beerdigungen/Konfirmationen series beginning 1830-1831.
2. The **Kirchenbuch 1773-1818 = Register 220096** also exists and may contain Andreas Konze b.1.10.1795 if the Konze family crossed between Deisel and Trendelburg parishes, though Deisel's 218821 remains the primary target.
3. **Register 220108 Taufen 1830-1863** is the precise Trendelburg baptism register where Charles Gannsen b.26 Jan 1835 would be recorded if his baptism occurred in Trendelburg rather than Deisel. Given Charles's later US documentation gives "aus Deisse" — which a genealogist can plausibly read as either Deisel (village 3 km from Trendelburg) or a clerk's rendering of Trendelburg itself (old spellings vary), this register is now a **Session 18 primary-source priority**.
4. **Register 220099 Kirchenbuch 1819-1831** covers Justus Ernst Konze's 14 Nov 1829 hypothetical baptism if his birth was in Trendelburg parish rather than Deisel. This register is the session's most promising new lead given that Session 14–17 exhaustively scanned Deisel 218824 without finding Justus Ernst with a Konze father-surname.
