### The Catharina Elisabeth Schildknecht geb. Konze entry — significance

Image 1830 contains an entry reading approximately:

> "Catharina Elisabeth **Schildknecht** geb. **Konze** [...] + 29 menſ. praet. Nachmittag 2 Uhr alt 34 Jahre 5 Monaten"

**Reading uncertainty.** The maiden name in this entry is **either "Konze" or "Köſter"** — both are short Kurrent words with similar silhouettes in this scribe's hand. A Jed-side Kurrent expert read is needed to settle which name is correct.

**If "Konze":** This documents a previously unknown Konze daughter who married a Schildknecht. Born ~1795 (34y 5m before death), she could be a sister of Joh. Christoph Konze, Joh. Heinrich Konze, or Wilhelmine Konze — all Konze men/women of the same generation. The Konze-Schildknecht alliance becomes a multi-marriage pattern: Klaus Andreas Kontze ALIAS Schildknecht (1799/1800) + Wilhelmine Konze × J.H. Schildknecht 1832 + Catharina Elisabeth Konze × Schildknecht (this entry, ~1815–1820 marriage).

**If "Köſter":** Less significant for the Konze investigation, but still adds a Schildknecht-Köster intermarriage.

The death year is likely 1827–1830 based on the image position in the burials section. If she died age 34y 5m, she was born approximately 1793–1796 — same generation as the Konze daughters Wilhelmine (b. 1800) and the unnamed Marie Elisabeth Konze, but slightly earlier.

- Baptisms 1796–1830 (S. 1–194) — 97 image-spreads
- Confirmations 1796–1830 — page count unspecified

Total expected: 97 + ? + 28 + 3 + 48 + ? + index = ~190+ image-spreads = ~190+ images.

Total post-title-page images: 1893 − 1697 = 196 images.

The math works approximately if confirmations and stillbirths are short (~3–5 image-spreads each). But the section ordering observed in Sessions 51, 53, 55, 57 doesn't match the title-page order cleanly:

- 1697 title → baptisms start
- 1737 = 1827 baptisms (NOT 1810 as Session 52 estimated)
- 1820 = late 1820s baptisms (still in section)
- 1822, 1826 = section dividers (X symbols)
- 1830 = burials (late 1820s)
- 1840 = burials 1808

The discontinuity at 1830 (where burials suddenly appear) suggests the marriages section may have been INSERTED between baptisms and burials, possibly at images **1822–1828** (a small range matching the 28-image-spread expectation for marriages).

**Recommended Session 58 navigation target:** Image **1825** (between the two "X" dividers at 1822 and 1826).
