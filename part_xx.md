#### XX.3.2 — Two "Johann Henrich Kontze" men identified

The P1 scan revealed that TWO men named "Johann Henrich Kontze" were active in Trendelburg simultaneously:

- **Joh. Henrich Kontze "der Junge" (the younger)** = our target, married Martha Elisabetha Wiechardt (1773), then Anna Catharina Othermann (1783). Son of Arnold Philipp Kontze (Patriarch A).
- **Joh. Henrich Kontze "der Alte" (the elder)** = a separate, older man. His wife appears as godmother "Anna Catharina, Johann Henrich Kontzen Ehefrau aus Reizfell" in July 1780 (page 41).

This "elder" Johann Henrich Kontze may be from a different generation or branch. His wife's name "Anna Catharina" is notably the same as Joh. Henrich the younger's SECOND wife (Anna Catharina Othermann, m. 1783) — but they are different women.

#### XX.3.3 — Johann Philipp Kontze identified

A third Kontze male, "Johann Philipp Kontze," was discovered during the P1 scan. He appears in entries from the 1770s as a distinct individual from both Johann Henrich (elder) and Johann Henrich (younger). His exact relationship to the known patriarchs requires further investigation.

#### XX.3.4 — Kontze as godparents (pages 40–43)

- **Page 41, entry 16 (July 1780):** Godmother "Anna Catharina, Johann Henrich Kontzen Ehefrau aus Reizfell" — wife of the ELDER Joh. Henrich Kontze.
- **Page 41, entry 23 (July 1780):** Godfather includes "[first name uncertain] Kontze" — a Kontze male.
- **Page 43, entry 17 (June 1781):** Godmother "Martha Elisabeth, Johannis Jungkontzin" — Martha Elisabeth associated with Johannes Jung-Kontze (the younger). This could be a wife or daughter of Johannes Kontze the younger.

#### XX.3.5 — Anna Margaretha geb. Kontzin married to David Lotz (Jan 1781)

Page 42 (image 1005), entry January 10, 1781:

- **Child:** Anna Catharina
- **Father:** David Lotz, von Stockhausen, Conductor und Marwaldverwalter(?)
- **Mother:** Anna Margaretha, geb. **Kontzin** (née Kontze)

**Significance:**

1. A Kontze DAUGHTER named Anna Margaretha married David Lotz von Stockhausen, a "Conductor" (administrative official). This represents a Kontze daughter marrying into the administrative/official class — a step up from the artisan marriages typical of the family.
2. The husband is "von Stockhausen" — from outside Trendelburg.
3. This is a THIRD "Anna Margaretha Kontze" — distinct from the one who married Christoph Näckel (1797) and the one who married Johannes Albrecht (pre-1799). Given the 1781 date, she is from an earlier generation.
4. She could be a daughter of the ELDER Johann Henrich Kontze, of Johann Philipp Kontze, or of another Kontze man active in the 1750s–1760s.

### XX.4 — Wiechardt and Grosbernd family discoveries

#### XX.4.1 — Christian Wiechardt: Patriarch identified

The P1 scan produced critical information about Christian Wiechardt, the likely father of Martha Elisabetha Wiechardt (Joh. Henrich Kontze's first wife):

**Page 42, entry 31 (Dec 25, 1780):** Child Johann Christian; father Johannes [surname uncertain]; mother Anna Christina geb. **Wiechardin** (née Wiechardt). Godfather: "der GroßVatter Christian Wiechard sen." — the maternal grandfather Christian Wiechardt senior.

**Page 42–43, Feb 4, 1781:** Father: Christian Wiechard, **Bürger und Braumeister** (citizen and brewmaster). Mother: Anna Christina, geb. **Groffin** (née Groff). Child: Johann Friederich (b. Jan 28, 1781). Godfather: "der Mutter Bruder Joh. [?] Groff, Bürger u. Ackermann allier" — the mother's brother.

**Synthesis:**

- Christian Wiechardt was a **brewmaster** (Braumeister), an important civic trade
- His wife was **Anna Christina née Groff**
- Known children:
  1. Martha Elisabetha Wiechardt (married Joh. Henrich Kontze, 1773)
  2. Anna Christina Wiechardt (married Johannes [?], Dec 1780)
  3. Johann Friederich Wiechardt (b. Jan 28, 1781)
- Described as "sen." (senior), suggesting a son of the same name existed

#### XX.4.2 — Andreas Wiechardt: Merchant

Page 41 (Nov 1780): Andreas Wiechard, Bürger und **Handelsmann** (citizen and merchant), with wife Engel Catharina geb. Sandfortin. A separate Wiechardt branch — merchant rather than brewmaster.

#### XX.4.3 — Grosbernd family: Wainwrights

Page 41 (Nov 1780): Jost Henrich Grosbernd, Bürger und **Wagner** (wainwright), with brother Johannes Grosbernd, also Bürger und Wagner. Confirms the Grosbernd family was in the wagon-making trade — the same family whose daughters later married both Andreas Contze and Johannes Contze.

Page 43 (Apr 1781): "Joh. Michael Grosbernd" appears as godfather — another Grosbernd male confirming the family's prominence.

### XX.5 — Double Contze-Grosbernd marriage confirmed

Pages 158 and 172 confirm that BOTH Generation III Contze brothers married Grosbernd women:

- **Johannes Contze** × Maria Elisabeth geb. Grosbernd (pages 158, 179)
- **Andreas Contze** × Wilhelmina geb. Grosbernd (page 172)

This double marriage into the same family strongly suggests the Grosbernd women were sisters or closely related. The Grosbernd family's trade as wainwrights (Wagners) places them in the same artisan-civic class as the Kontze Ackermanns.
