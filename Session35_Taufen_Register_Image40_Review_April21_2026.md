## Critical Analytical Update: The Hildebrand Network in Deisel

### Three Hildebrand Women on One Page

The full-page reading of Image 40 reveals an extraordinary concentration of Hildebrand-linked families:

1. **Entry 195**: Mother = Wilhelmine geb. **Hildebrand** (wife of J.H. Konze)
2. **Entry 196**: Mother = Elisabeth geb. **Hildebrand** (wife of Ludwig Werth)
3. **Entry 198**: Mother = [?] geb. **Hildebrand** (wife of Johannes [?], needs verification)

All three are married women who were born Hildebrand and married into different Deisel families. This confirms the Hildebrand family was large and well-established in Deisel.

### Connection to Session 34 Findings

Session 34 identified the 1849 Wilhelmine Hildebrand (who married Thöne) as the daughter of **Andreas Hildebrand** (Ackermann) and **Maria Elisabeth née Birke**. If the Hildebrands on Image 40 are siblings or cousins of this Wilhelmine, the family network is extensive. The key question is whether there was an OLDER Wilhelmine Hildebrand (born ~1800–1810) who was Entry 195's mother — distinct from the younger Wilhelmine (b. 1817) who married Thöne.

The presence of **Elisabeth geb. Hildebrand** (Entry 196) as a married woman in January 1835 suggests she was born at least ~1815 or earlier. She may be a sister of the Entry 195 mother.

### Implications for the "Two Wilhelmine Hildebrands" Hypothesis

This data strengthens the hypothesis that the Hildebrand family had **multiple daughters** of marriageable age in the 1830s. The Entry 195 mother (Wilhelmine geb. Hildebrand) could easily be an older sister or cousin of the 1817-born Wilhelmine who married Thöne in 1849.
