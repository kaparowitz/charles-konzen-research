Start Here — For Researchers

A front door for collaborating genealogists. The working conclusion and where it stands, the core evidence, what has already been searched, and the threads still open — each linked and grouped by purpose.

If you're picking this project up to evaluate the evidence or carry the search forward, start here. The handful of pages below are the load-bearing ones; everything else on the site supports them.

Where things stand

Charles (~26 January 1835 – 16 December 1903). His German surname appears across fourteen U.S. records, 1862–1896, in nine spellings — Gannsen (the form he signed himself), Gonnson, Gansen, Gonsen, Gonson, Conson, Jansen, Konsen, and Konzen — before the family settled on the adopted Anglicisation Johnson (see surname records). His birth is held as ~26 January 1835 in Kurhessen on a working basis — the date rests on a single post-mortem parish burial entry, and no German baptism has yet been found. The origin is a working conclusion pointing to the Trendelburg / Deisel area of the Kirchenkreis Hofgeismar; the most-wanted document remains Charles's baptism register.
