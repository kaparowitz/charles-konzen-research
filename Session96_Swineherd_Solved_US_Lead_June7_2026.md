## A. ★★ Stammen KB 220063 — the swineherd family completely reconstructed
- **Marriage found, 23 Mar 1783, Stammen:** "Andreas Konze, Schweinehirt(?), weyland **Arnold Philips Konzen**, gewesenen Einwohners zu **Trendelburg**, ehelich nachgelassener Sohn × Maria Elisabeth, weyland **Christoph Gerlandts** ehelich nachgelassene Tochter" (img 2063).
  - ⇒ **The swineherd is a SON of Arnold Philipp Konze of Trendelburg** (d. 1780). With Arnold Philipp's two Uffelmann wives (×1 d. 1754; ×2 Anna Dorothea d. 1785), the 1787 godfather entry "Andreas Kontze von Stammen, **des Vaters Halbbruder**" is RESOLVED: Andreas (b. ~1758, of ×2) and Joh. Henrich the younger (b. ~1740, of ×1) were half-brothers. Arnold Philipp's children now: Joh. Henrich (×1), Johannes, + of ×2: **Andreas the swineherd, Johann Philips, Joh. Christoph, Anna Catharina (× Joh. Henrich Engel, Stammen 21 Nov 1784)**.
- **TWELVE children, 1784–1809, all baptized in Stammen** (three new): George Henrich (1784†), **Johann Christian (b. 1 Jul 1785 — untraced, worth following)**, Johannes (b. 26 Jul 1788; margin "✝1837 zu Hümmen" — Hümme identity locked), Catharina Elisabeth (1791), Maria Sophia (1793; × J.C.A.W. Müller ~1815; d. 26 Dec 1829 — exact age match), Johann Conrad (1796–1812), Anna Catharina (1799), **Johann Henrich (b. 20 May 1800 — untraced, worth following)**, Johann Ludwig (1802–04), Maria Charlotta (1805†), **Christian Ludwig (b. 20 Nov 1806, bap. 23 Nov 1806 IN STAMMEN — margin "✝ 11/4 1883")**, Sophia Maria (1809–10).
- **Both parents died in Stammen** — mother 20 Nov 1817 ("des Schneiders Andreas Kontze Ehefrau, geb. Gerlandt," aged 57); father **31 Dec 1828** (bur. 3 Jan 1829, "Andreas Kontze, Schneider, alt 70") — he turned tailor in old age (the swineherd post passed to Düsterdiek by 1814). The "died elsewhere/1831–33" hypothesis is refuted.

## B. ★ US-side hunt for Christoph Kontze (b. 13 Feb 1814) — best lead is provocative
- **Headline hit:** Brig *President*, Bremen → Philadelphia, **arrived 26 Aug 1839**: "**Chs Aug(s) Kontze**, age 25 [b. ~1814], farmer" traveling with "**Child Kontze, age 4, male [b. ~1835]**" — NO wife in the party. Origin column reads either "**Warren C[ount]y Miss[issippi]**" (Ancestry's transcriber; a Vicksburg "L Konntze" appears in an 1878 directory) or "C[ur]hessen." FS arks 1:1:QV9Y-JSKQ / 1:1:K8C3-FT1; original image read.
  - Right age for Christoph, rare exact surname, unmarried — but given name "Chs Aug(s)" (Charles August), and **a Kontze boy b. ~1835** in tow. NOT claimed as Charles (Charles's own record-trail starts St. Louis ~1859 and he consistently said b. 1835 Kurhessen, arr. ~1850s) — but this is the single most Charles-adjacent datum any US search has produced. **Follow-ups queued:** Warren Co. MS 1840/1850 censuses, Vicksburg German records, the 1878 "L Konntze" original.
- Eliminated: Christopher "Kontze" of Hardin Co., Ohio (= Koutz, b. ~1808, married by 1838). Noted: Christian Kontze (Atlanta 1850); Frederick Kontze (Illinois land 1847–48); Henry Kontze (Ripley Co. IN 1840).
- **Hümme→NYC line documented on FS Tree**: Friedrich Kontze GPK7-JYP (b. 1816, the Stammen-born Johannes's son!) — "death after 1870, New York City"; researcher **GeneDana** (Feb 2024 notes citing the exact Hümme pages we read) is an active collaboration lead.
- Explicit negatives: NO Christoph/Christopher Kontze/Konze in Missouri/St. Louis in any spelling; FS catalog "Auswanderung Hofgeismar" search erroring — retry later; HStAM Auswanderungsakten remains the decisive offline source.
- St. Louis "Konitz" family noted (one 1877 marriage to an **H. W. Temme** — curious echo of Kontze×Temme; parked).
