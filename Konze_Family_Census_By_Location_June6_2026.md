## Coverage-status key
- **★ INDEX-VERIFIED** — the register's own alphabetical index (or a complete burial/marriage index) was read for the K-section; effectively exhaustive for that book.
- **● DOCUMENTED** — individuals established across multiple sessions / Peter's Stammtafeln, but not every register page confirmed.
- **◐ SAMPLED** — transcript or visual sampling across decades; rules out a *cluster*, not every individual.
- **○ TO READ** — register exists but the K-content has not been swept.

## 4b. GOTTSBÜREN  *(stated origin of one Kontze man — NOT a confirmed Konze village)*
**Registers:** KB 1767–1826 (219040, read) · KB 1827–1834 (219043) · Taufen 1830–1867 (219049). **Status: ○ Konze claimed, UNCONFIRMED in Gottsbüren's own registers.**
- The 1833 Stammen marriage states **Christian Ludwig Kontze** was *"gebürtig von Gottsbüren,"* son of the late **Andreas Kontze × Marie Elisabeth née Garland**. **But Gottsbüren's own books contradict an easy reading:** the 1806 baptisms (+ spot-checks 1804/1808) contain **no Kontze**, there is **no Kontze marriage 1795–1806**, and an entry-by-entry read of the burials (~Oct 1805–Jan 1825, plus 1827–33) found **no Kontze burial either** — Gottsbüren's registers are entirely Kontze-free. The **Garland (mother's) family IS native** to Gottsbüren (blacksmith David Garland, d. by 1797; daughters married locally).
- ⇒ **Andreas Kontze was an in-migrant** who married a Gottsbüren Garland woman; the marriage and Christian Ludwig's baptism are not in Gottsbüren's registers as read. So Gottsbüren is **the stated birthplace of one Kontze man, not a documented Konze settlement** — and where Andreas actually came from (Deisel/Trendelburg the natural candidate) is the real open question. A new (≈7th) **Andreas Kontze**, not yet matched to the disentangled identities.
**To make airtight:** ○ only burials Feb 1825–1826 remain unread in Gottsbüren (sliver); ○ the real task is now **identifying Andreas Kontze's home parish** — his burial lies wherever the family actually lived (Deisel/Trendelburg suspected).

## Summary — why this supports the conclusion
The Konze surname is documented continuously and densely in **Trendelburg and Deisel** (back to ~1718/1763), present in adjacent **Stammen, Hümme, Friedrichsfeld** (and named as one man's birthplace at **Gottsbüren**, though unconfirmed in its registers), and **absent from every surrounding parish** checked (Gottsbüren, Grebenstein, Immenhausen, Helmarshausen, Hofgeismar, Sielen, Schöneberg, Bad Karlshafen, Gieselwerder, Veckerhagen + the Catholic Westphalian parishes). Charles's two independent self-reports (Deisel 1866, Trendelburg 1862) fall inside this single ~5 km cluster. **The name and the place are corroborated; the open question is only which specific Konze household Charles belonged to** — and the DNA suggests the biological paternal line may sit outside the documented family entirely.
