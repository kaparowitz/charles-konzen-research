Life Timeline — Birth to Burial

26 January 1835 (Kurhessen) → 16 December 1903 (St. Louis) · 68 years
CHARLES IN AMERICA38 years in St. Louis · 18 events documented

★ At a Glance

From an unknown port of arrival to a sergeant's stripes, a German Evangelical wedding, seven children, and a working watchman's life on the north side of St. Louis. Charles's American life ran 44 years — from 1859 immigration to his 1903 death. The 16-year gap before 1859 (childhood, confirmation, possible apprenticeship, emigration prep) sits inside the German-side investigation; everything below is anchored in U.S. primary records.

The full timeline

26 January 1835
Born in Kurhessen — Deisel/Trendelburg area, Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis. Day and month from the St. Marcus burial register (entry no. 3393); the year is corroborated by the censuses. (The pension file gives only inconsistent ages, not this date — see birth date across the records.)St. Marcus burial register #3393 (Doc N)
~1843–1859
A 16-year documentation gap. Childhood, confirmation, possible apprenticeship, and emigration all sit inside this window.Inferred — no records yet found
1859
Immigrated to the United States. Census-reported year; departure and arrival ports remain unknown. Hamburg or Bremen are most likely for a Kurhessen emigrant.1900 U.S. Federal Census
2 December 1862
Enlisted as L. Charles Gannsen in the Missouri 2nd Regiment, Battery B, Light Artillery (U.S. Army). Birthplace stated: "Freundburg, Kurhessen."Civil War enlistment record
1862–1865
Served through the Civil War, rising from Private to Corporal to Sergeant (promoted 7 November 1863), then reduced back to Private on 15 March 1865 mid-detachment. Discharged 20 December 1865 as a Private. Sustained injuries documented in the pension file: hernia, hearing loss, rheumatism.Pension medical examinations · NARA Compiled Service Record
20 December 1865
Discharged. Returned to St. Louis, taking up residence at 1518 South 7th Street (Soulard / Frenchtown). Charles's own 1898 pension declaration writes the address as "1516 So. 7th St."Pension Page 29
7 January 1866
Married Auguste Kreichelt at St. Marcus German Evangelical Church, St. Louis. Recorded as "Carl Gonnson, aus Deisse, Kreis Cassel, Churhessen." Officiated by Rev. Geo. W. Wall. Bride's father George and brother August had also served in Battery B.St. Marcus marriage register, 1866
1866–1880
Seven children born and baptized at St. Marcus. The eldest, Friedrich Wilhelm Gonnson, died as an infant. Two daughters (Maria Josephine, Auguste) died young, at 22 and 18. Four children — Katherine, Louise, Erwin Carl, and Bertha — lived into the 20th century. → The seven childrenSt. Marcus baptisms · FamilySearch
1869–1874
Moved to 718 Gates Street, working as a railroad conductor (per 1871 Edwards' St. Louis Directory: "Johnson, Charles, conductor, St. Louis R.R., r. 718 Gate"). His own 1898 pension letter confirms this same address.1871 St. Louis Directory · Pension Page 29
1874–~1877
Moved to 2409 South Broadway, where the family lived "about 3 years."Pension Page 29
~1877–1880
Brief residence at 2412 South 2nd Street before the move to north St. Louis.Pension Page 29
1875
Son Charles Jr. baptized at St. Marcus as Erwin Carl Konsen — preserving the Hesse-style surname KONSEN in the church record. The strongest direct link between the American family and the German Konze line.St. Marcus baptism register, 1875 · → Erwin Carl Konsen profile
1880
Living at 1534 Jackson Street in the south-central Ward 9 district (Kosciusko) with wife Auguste and five surviving children; occupation: cotton-press worker.1880 U.S. Federal Census, ED 137
1890–1900
Settled at 2408 DeKalb Street (Kosciusko, south-side St. Louis — between Soulard and the river, modern zip 63104; the same south-side German-immigrant corridor as the family's earlier addresses on S. 7th, S. Broadway, and S. 2nd, not the north-side Hyde Park district as earlier site copy stated). The pension file consistently records this single address across declarations, depositions, and the Surgeon's certificate of examination through 1898; the 1890 Veterans Schedule entered it as "2405 DeKalb (rear)" and the 1895 City Directory as "2407 DeKalb · Watchman" — adjacent buildings on the same lot. Listed in St. Louis directories as a watchman through these years.Pension Pages 11, 14, 32, 33 · 1890 Veterans Schedule · 1895 directory
November 1891
Pension Certificate No. 684,507 issued — Charles awarded $12.00 per month under the Act of June 27, 1890. Disability of record: Right hernia & Rheumatism; also loss of hearing.Pension Page 09 (issued certificate)
1896
Wife Auguste died.FamilySearch profile LZKV-9F5
June 1900
Recorded as a boarder in the Peter Kuhn household at 170 Sidney Street, St. Louis Ward 9. Listed as: "Charles Johnson, boarder; Nov 1836; age 63; widowed; immig. 1859; naturalized; retired."1900 U.S. Federal Census, ED 129
18 October 1900
Admitted to the Danville Branch, National Home for Disabled Volunteer Soldiers in Danville, Illinois. Pension agency transferred from Topeka, Kansas to Chicago, Illinois.Pension Page 06 (Form 103, signed by Treasurer W. C. Tuttle)
16 December 1903†
Died in St. Louis of stomach cancer (carcinoma), age 68. Last residence of record on the death certificate: 168 Sidney Street. Buried 19 December 1903 at Old St. Marcus Cemetery. The Danville Branch Governor returned his pension certificate to the Pension Office on 4 January 1904; the U.S. Pension Agent's "Pensioner Dropped" notice followed on 18 January 1904.Death certificate no. 10619 · Pension Page 03 · St. Marcus burial records

Charles Research — a multi-month transatlantic genealogical investigation into Charles Konzen (recorded in U.S. records as Gannsen / Johnson; 1835–1903), Hessian-born Civil War sergeant of Battery B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery, and his probable Trendelburg / Deisel origins in Churhessen (a working conclusion — the German baptism remains unrecovered).
