St. Louis Residences — 1865 to 1903

Six documented addresses across 38 years · LaSalle Park / Soulard → Kosciusko (with one 1880 north-side detour to Baden)
CHARLES IN AMERICAReconciled across pension file, 2 censuses, 2 directories

★ At a Glance

Charles's residences across thirty-eight years in St. Louis (1865–1903), reconciled across three independent record sets: (a) his own handwritten address-history letter inside the Civil War pension file (Page 29, c. 1898); (b) the 1880 and 1900 federal censuses; and (c) the 1871 and 1895 St. Louis city directories. Six of the seven verified addresses cluster on the south side of St. Louis, between downtown and the river: 1518 S. 7th in LaSalle Park (1865–69), then 2409 S. Broadway, 2412 S. 2nd, 2408 DeKalb, and 168/170 Sidney all in modern Kosciusko (1874–1903). The 1880 census places the family at 1534 Jackson Street — in the south-central Ward 9 district, where the period Jackson Street ran between Columbus and DeKalb across Sidney St (not the modern north-side Jackson Street in Baden). The two DeKalb Street numbers visible in the secondary records (2405 rear, 2407 front) are adjacent buildings on the same lot.

St. Louis residences — mapped

Charles's residences across thirty-eight years in St. Louis (1865–1903), reconciled across three independent record sets: (a) his own handwritten address-history letter inside the Civil War pension file (Page 29, c. 1898); (b) the 1880 and 1900 federal censuses; and (c) the 1871 and 1895 St. Louis city directories. The earliest addresses cluster in the historically-German Soulard / LaSalle Park district south of downtown; the family then settled long-term on the Kosciusko blocks closer to the river — DeKalb Street and Sidney Street — where Charles spent most of his post-1890 life. The two DeKalb Street numbers visible in the secondary records (2405 rear, 2407 front) are adjacent buildings on the same lot — though the pension file itself uses "2408 DeKalb" as the canonical address through the entire 1890–1898 period. Address sources

The address timeline above has been reconciled across the following primary sources. Where two records gave conflicting house numbers (e.g., 2405 / 2407 / 2408 DeKalb), the pension file is treated as the canonical record because it carries Charles's own sworn signature.

  • Civil War pension file F41-523680269E (40 pages) — Charles's handwritten address-history letter of c. 1898 (Page 29) is the spine of the timeline; declarations on Page 11 (1891) and Page 14 (1890), the Pension Bureau docket on Page 32, and the Surgeon's Certificate of Examination on Page 33 all confirm "2408 DeKalb St." through the 1890s. Page 06 records the 18 Oct 1900 admission to the Danville Soldiers' Home. → Open the full pension-file viewer (with page-by-page transcription)
  • 1880 U.S. Federal Census · ED 137, Sheet 4D, Dwelling 15, Family 37 — "Charles Gonson household, 1534 Jackson St." (corrected from this site's earlier mistranscription as "1534 Jackson"). → FamilySearch · 1880 census record ↗
  • 1900 U.S. Federal Census · ED 129, Sheet 5B, Dwelling 59, Family 103, Peter Kuhn household — Charles a boarder at 170 Sidney St. → FamilySearch · 1900 census record ↗
  • National Homes for Disabled Volunteer Soldiers, Historical Register (NARA M1749) — "Charles Gannson," Danville Branch, admitted 1900; birthplace Germany, enlisted/discharged St. Louis 2 Dec 1862 – 20 Dec 1865, Co. B — service dates matching Charles exactly. → Ancestry · Soldiers' Home register ↗
  • 1890 Veterans Schedule · "Charles Gonnsen, sergeant; Co. B, 2nd MO Vols" — secondary source recording "2405 DeKalb (rear)" for the 1890 reading. The 1890 federal population census schedule was destroyed by fire; only the Veterans Schedule survives.
  • St. Louis City Directories (mined via FamilySearch Full-Text, June 2026) — the only contemporaneous sources for his early addresses, naming him at 718 Gate year by year under four surname spellings, plus the 1895 DeKalb entry:
    • 1871 Edwards' Directory p. 352 — "Johnson, Charles, conductor, St. Louis R.R., r. 718 Gate"
    • 1872–73 — "Johnson Charles, conductor, r. 718 Gate" — direct record ↗
    • 1874 — "Konsen Charles, driver, r. 718 Gate" (newly found — the Konsen spelling, matching the 1875 baptism) — direct record ↗
    • 1876 — "Gonson Charles, driver, 718 Gate" (newly found — the Gonson spelling, matching the 1880 census; extends the tenure to 1876)
    • 1895 / 1895–96 — "Gannsen Charles, watch, r. rear 2407 DeKalb" (+ Charles Jr., lab.)
    All reproducible via FamilySearch Full-Text: → "718 Gate" · → "Gannsen". The full annual directory run is also browsable in the FamilySearch catalog.
    Note on 1518 S. 7th (1865–69): no directory or census entry for Charles at that address has been found under any spelling — it is attested only by his c. 1898 pension recollection ("1516 So. 7th"), so its house number and dates are approximate.
  • Death Certificate no. 10619 · St. Louis Health Department — "Last residence 168 Sidney" at death on 16 Dec 1903.
  • FamilySearch profile LZXC-6GB · Charles Johnson Sr. — 41 attached source records cross-link the residences above, the seven children, and the spouse Auguste Kreichelt (LZKV-9F5).

Open questions in this section

  • What occupations did Charles hold between his 1865 discharge (1518 S. 7th St.) and the 1871 directory (railroad conductor at 718 Gates)?
  • Why does the 1900 census list him as a boarder in the Peter Kuhn household — was Kuhn a relation, a former Battery B comrade, or simply a landlord?
  • Are any earlier (1860, 1870) U.S. censuses identifiable for Charles under any of the surname-variant spellings (Gonnson, Gonson, Konsen, Johnson)?
  • Where, if anywhere, do Charles's seven children's post-1900 lives lead back to evidence of his German origin?
