## Headline

Session 56 continued the burial register 218851 scan into 1832 (Images 1794–1795), reaching September 1832 deaths. **The most consequential finding** is Entry 45 on Image 1794: **Maria Elisabeth Schildknecht geb. Thiele** (b. 3 September 1793 at 11 AM, d. February-March 1832, age 38), wife of Ackermann Johann Christoph Schildknecht. This confirms two structural facts about the Deisel surname network:

1. **"Thiele/Thile/Thilin" is a real Deisel maiden name** — independently attested in Maria Elisabeth Thiele (b. 1793) AND Caroline Thilin (m. Andreas Grebe, 1810 baptism Image 1685) AND Catharina Elisabeth Thilin (Klaus Andreas Kontze's wife, 1799/1800 baptisms). The "-in" ending in "Thilin" is the German feminine genitive marker; the family's actual surname is Thiele/Thile.
2. **A SECOND Schildknecht-Thiele couple** parallel to Klaus Andreas (alias Schildknecht) Kontze × Catharina Elisabeth née Thilin: **Johann Christoph Schildknecht × Maria Elisabeth née Thiele**. The Schildknecht and Thiele families had at least two intermarriages, suggesting close family ties.

**Not found:** The specific Ludwig Köster (Ackermann, husband of Wilhelmine née Konze) death entry in Jan-September 1832 (the window scanned this session). Entries 34–41 (likely January 1832) were not fully scanned — they may be on an image not yet visited (between Images 1793 and 1794). The window of possibility for Ludwig Köster's death has been narrowed to either January 1832 (if in this register) or 1828–1829 (if in the earlier KB 218824 register).

**Significance.**

1. **Confirms Thile/Thilin as a Deisel surname.** Now THREE attestations: (a) Catharina Elisabeth née Thilin (Klaus Andreas Kontze's wife, 1799/1800 baptisms); (b) Caroline née Thilin (Andreas Grebe's wife, 1810 baptism); (c) Maria Elisabeth née Thiele (Joh. Christoph Schildknecht's wife, this entry). The varying spelling — Thilin / Thiele / Thile — reflects scribal variation; the family name is Thiele/Thile.

2. **Establishes a Schildknecht-Thiele intermarriage pattern.** Two Schildknecht men (Klaus Andreas a.k.a. Kontze, and Johann Christoph) both married Thiele women. Either they are brothers/cousins, OR the Schildknecht family routinely intermarried with the Thiele family.

3. **Strengthens Klaus Andreas Kontze's "Schildknecht" alias plausibility.** If "Schildknecht" was Klaus Andreas's birth surname (or a household name), the broader Schildknecht-Thiele alliance documented here becomes the immediate context for Klaus Andreas's own marriage to Catharina Elisabeth Thilin.

4. **Maria Elisabeth Thiele was born September 1793.** Could Klaus Andreas Kontze's daughter Marie Elisabeth (the hypothetical daughter b. ~1805–1812 who may have married Christian Köster III) be related to or named after this Maria Elisabeth Thiele? A naming-tradition link is plausible but not provable from this entry alone.
