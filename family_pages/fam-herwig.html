2. Carl Ludwig Herwig line — Herwig

Trendelburg → Massachusetts (1854)
ELIMINATEDInvestigation: late February – March 2026

★ At a Glance

ELIMINATED Original investigation late Feb – March 2026 · downstream Herwig-family US trail located May 2026

Carl Ludwig Herwig of Trendelburg — pursued for several months as a strong candidate. Eliminated when the family's documented destination was confirmed to be Massachusetts, not Missouri. The trail of the missing Herwig son no longer dead-ends, but it leads to Plymouth, Mass. and Jersey City, NJ — not St. Louis; no Herwig in St. Louis traces to the Trendelburg family.

Why this matters less for Charles: the geographic mismatch (Massachusetts → New Jersey) decisively rules out the Herwig branch. Peter pivoted from the Herwig line to other Trendelburg candidates. Retained for documentary completeness — and now substantially expanded with the May 2026 US-side findings.
★ May 2026 update — full Herwig US trail reconstructed: The 1854 Bremen → NYC voyage of Johan Herwig + Elisabeth + 4 children on the ship "Sonter Johnny" was located on Ancestry's NY Customs Passenger Lists; the family Anglicized to Herig within months and was tracked from 1855 NYC Ward 13 → 1860 NYC Ward 7 (Lower East Side) → 1885–1915 Jersey City NJ Ward 7 across six US censuses. The eldest surviving Herwig son Carl Louis Herwig (b. 27 Nov 1830 Trendelburg) emigrated separately and is now confirmed in the U.S. for the first time: married Catherine Sharda 6 Oct 1855 in Plymouth, Massachusetts, with his father indexed as "John Herweg / John Harwig" (= Johannes Herwig) — two independent Ancestry indexes corroborate. The "Massachusetts attribution" in Peter's chart is therefore correct for at least one son; St. Louis remains a non-destination for any documented Trendelburg Herwig. See the research log below.

2. Carl Ludwig Herwig line — Herwig family, Trendelburg → Massachusetts (1854)

Latest attachment 26 March 2026 · "Carl Ludwig Herwig" · status: eliminated
Peter's genealogical content
  • Carl Louis (Ludwig) Herwig · PCXT-X3M — born 27 Nov 1830 Trendelburg (the parish confirmation entry recorded 1831 in error). Spouse Catherine Sharda · PZXN-32N; father Johannes Herwig · LRZG-NML; mother Catherine Elisabeth Grandjot · LRZG-LPS.
  • Mother's maiden name Grandjot, of the French Huguenot colony at Schöneberg.
  • Godfather of the eldest surviving Herwig son was Charles Louis Grandjot (the mother's brother).
  • Adam Herweck — sponsor at Charles's 1866 St. Louis marriage and Civil War unit-mate — connects via this same Trendelburg Herwig family.
  • The Herwig–Grandjot couple emigrated to the U.S. in 1854 with 4 children, destination Massachusetts. Two of their six children are unaccounted for at emigration.
Why this line was rejected

The family's documented destination is Massachusetts, not Missouri. The trail of the missing son dead-ends; no Herwig in St. Louis traces to the Trendelburg family. Peter pivoted from the Herwig branch to the maternal Grandjot line.

Downstream US-side research (May 2026)

Eliminated for Charles Gannsen, but reopened in May 2026 to settle two long-cold questions: where did the Herwigs actually go in the U.S.? and can the missing eldest son Carl Louis be located? Both are now answered.

Finding 1 (2 May 2026) — 1854 Sonter Johnny manifest located; family Anglicized to "Herig" and settled NYC → Jersey City NJ

An Ancestry pass on Jeanette/Annette Herwig (the uniquely-named eldest daughter, b. 4 Jul 1833 Trendelburg = PCXT-27M) returned six consecutive US censuses spanning 60 years, every one under the Anglicized surname "Herig". The single discovery that unlocked everything: she was indexed as "Annette" on the 1854 ship manifest and as "Jennette / Jeannette / Janet Herig" in the censuses below.

Net of finding 1: Peter's "Massachusetts" attribution was a faithful reading of the 1854 Bremen manifest, but the parents and four 1854-manifested children pushed on to NYC Ward 13 within six months of arrival, then NYC Ward 7 by 1860, and across the Hudson to Jersey City, Hudson County, NJ by 1885 where they remained at least until 1915. The surname Anglicized from Herwig to Herig within months — which is why every prior search under the original German spelling missed them. Two separate 1885 Jersey City Herwig households (Jeanette + a "Charles/Mary/Charles/Bertha/Theodore" household = parents + Carl Peter + Theodor) confirm the wider family clustered there.

Finding 2 (3 May 2026) — Carl Louis Herwig (b. 27 Nov 1830 Trendelburg) located: married 6 Oct 1855 Plymouth, Massachusetts

The eldest surviving Herwig son was not on the 1854 Sonter Johnny manifest (the manifest "Carl Herwig 1842" is his younger brother Carl Peter, b. 1841). A direct search on his FS-recorded wife Catherine Sharda (PZXN-32N) returned a 6 October 1855 Plymouth, MA marriage record indexed in two independent Ancestry collections derived from the same Plymouth Town Vital Records original:

Why this is unambiguously Carl Louis Herwig (PCXT-X3M): the bride's name "Catherine Sharda" exactly matches the wife already on his FS profile; his father John Herweg / Harwig is the unambiguous Anglicization of Johannes Herwig (LRZG-NML, b. 22 Aug 1802 Trendelburg, the 1854 Sonter Johnny emigrant); marriage age 24 in October 1855 places groom's birth in November 1830 — Carl Louis was born 27 Nov 1830 Trendelburg, so he was 24 turning 25; "Germany" is consistent with Hesse-Kassel; and Plymouth, MA is the very destination declared on the 1854 manifest. Plymouth was a real intended destination — at least one Herwig actually used it.

Two candidate prior NY arrivals for Carl Louis (neither yet decisive)
  • Carl Herrig · age 20 · b. abt 1831 · Bremen → NY 23 Sep 1851 · ship Young · place of origin "Germany"
  • Luis Harney [Luis Harweg] · age 22 · b. abt 1830 · Bremen → NY 5 Apr 1852 · ship Ocean · destination written as "St Luis"

The 1851 Young arrival has the better birth-year match (1831 vs 1830). The 1852 Ocean arrival has the perfect 1830 birth year but the "St Luis" destination annotation more plausibly fits the Hanover-born St. Louis confectioner Louis J Herwig (a confirmed name-twin via Find a Grave memorial 87945136; d. 26 Aug 1870 Pagedale St. Louis Co. MO; not the Trendelburg Carl Louis). Original-image inspection of both manifests is the obvious next step.

Verdict, after May 2026

The St. Louis elimination stands: the Trendelburg Herwig family's actual US trail is Plymouth, MA (Carl Louis 1855) and NYC → Jersey City, NJ (parents + 4 siblings 1854–1915). No Herwig settled in St. Louis under any traceable spelling. The May 2026 work substantially fills out the family's US story and partially restores the "Massachusetts" attribution that had been provisionally written off on 2 May, but it does not change the verdict on Carl Ludwig/Louis Herwig as a Charles Gannsen candidate.

Open follow-ups
  1. Pull the 6 Oct 1855 Plymouth marriage record image (collection 2495 record 14571973) for groom occupation, town of last residence, witnesses, and officiating minister.
  2. Search 1855 Mass. State + 1860/1865/1870 federal/state censuses for "Lewis Harwig" + Catherine in Plymouth and surrounding Plymouth County towns (Carver, Kingston, Duxbury, Marshfield).
  3. Identify Christian Sharda (Catherine's father) in 1850 Plymouth federal census — the surname may be Anglicized from German Schardau / Schardt.
  4. Pull original 1851 Young and 1852 Ocean manifest images to disambiguate Carl Louis's actual NY arrival.
  5. Search Plymouth-area death/cemetery records for Lewis/Louis/Lewes Herweg/Harwig — the FS profile records him "Deceased" without a date.
  6. Locate sister Caroline Phillipine Herwig (b. 1837) and brother Johannes Grandjot 1855 emigration — both still undocumented in indexed Ancestry collections.

See the parallel Grandjot family page (fam-grandjot.html) for the same May 2 + May 3 findings expressed from the Grandjot in-law perspective, plus Peter's three original charts.
