Johannes Grandjot — verification check on the 1855 emigrant brother

Sibling of focal candidate ★ Carl Ludwig · Schuhmacher · Bremen → NYC · 25 September 1855
VERIFICATION CHECK CLOSED · NOT CHARLESOpened & closed 4 May 2026

★ At a Glance

CLOSED · NOT CHARLES Johannes Grandjot · verification check opened & closed 4 May 2026

Johannes Grandjot (b. 14 Sep 1828 Schöneberg, FS PCXP-H6X) is the only documented transatlantic emigrant in ★ Carl Ludwig Grandjot's nuclear family. He left Schöneberg in August 1855 as a registered Schuhmacher (shoemaker), boarded a Bremen-line ship, and arrived in New York on 25 September 1855 traveling with a co-passenger named Lisette Grandjot (relationship to Johannes not stated on the manifest). He appears in the 1860 NY Ward 5 District 2 census as "Jos Grand" alongside one "Mary Grand" (relationship unstated), and his trail then goes cold — apparently with no surviving descendants under the Grandjot surname.

Verdict: ★ Carl Ludwig Grandjot was the focal candidate (eliminated 4 March 2026 — stayed in Schöneberg). Johannes is decisively not Charles Gannsen — he was 7 years older, kept the Hugenot surname intact, settled NYC not St. Louis, and is independently documented in 1860 Lower Manhattan with a separate biography. The verification check is closed. Surviving NY Grandjot descendants (Suffolk County) trace to a separate 1882 Schöneberg emigration by Louis Grandgot, not to Johannes.

Sub-investigation · Johannes Grandjot's 1855 emigration

Opened 4 May 2026 · two corroborating records identified · status: active verification check (not a Charles candidate)
★ 4 May 2026 update — two independent records corroborate the emigration: (1) the Hessisches Staatsarchiv Marburg Auswanderer-Beleg (ID 19743, attached as a memory on his FS profile) records his profession as Schuhmacher, dated 08/1855, destination Amerika; (2) the New York Arriving Passenger and Crew Lists, 1820-1957 on Ancestry index a "Joseph Grandjos" b. ~1828 from Bremen arriving NYC 25 Sep 1855, with co-traveler Lisette Grandjot (relationship undocumented on the manifest). The given name "Joseph" is a transcription artifact — the German script abbreviation "Joh:" (for Johannes) misread by the indexer as "Jos.". The 5-week Aug 1855 → Sep 1855 interval matches a Bremen → NYC voyage of that era exactly. A duplicate index entry in the U.S. and Canada Passenger and Immigration Lists Index spells the surname correctly: "Joseph Grandjot" with "Grandjot, Lisette" listed as the other passenger.
★ Correction · 4 May 2026 (later same day): An earlier draft of this page identified Lisette as Johannes's wife and tied her to a Peter Schräder note that "his wife later hanged herself in America." Both attributions were errors. Re-reading Peter's email of 5 Mar 2026 shows the suicide-by-hanging he described was Carl Ludwig Grandjot's wife (Katherine Sophia, FS PCNW-JB7), in Schöneberg, Germany — not Johannes's wife in the U.S. The 1855 manifest lists Lisette Grandjot only as a co-traveler, with no relationship code. Lisette is therefore not confirmed as Johannes's wife; she is simply a fellow Schöneberg-Grandjot emigrant who took the same Bremen → NYC voyage. The wife-candidate framing below has been retracted.

Name-script analysis · why "Joseph" is almost certainly a misread "Johannes"

German parish and ship-manifest scripts of the mid-19th century commonly abbreviate Johannes as "Joh:" or "Joh." with a colon or period. In Kurrent / Sütterlin handwriting, the lowercase h is a tall narrow loop that can be mistaken for an s by a transcriber unfamiliar with the script — yielding "Jos:", which an Ancestry indexer would then expand to Joseph. The duplicate index entry preserves the surname Grandjot correctly while the primary entry misreads it as Grandjos, which is consistent with a single-source indexing pass that read both names in the same line of script.

Three corroborating signals make the identification probable:

  • Birth year 1828 — matches Johannes Grandjot's documented baptism year (14 Sep 1828) with no other plausible candidate in the FS Grandjot record set
  • Bremen → NYC routing — matches the standard Hessian emigration corridor that the Hessian state archive registered him on
  • August → 25 Sep 1855 timing — the 5-week interval between Hessian-side emigration registration and NYC arrival is the canonical Bremen-line transit window
"Lisette Grandjot" — co-traveler · relationship undocumented

The co-traveler is recorded simply as Lisette Grandjot on the manifest index. Lisette is the French diminutive of Élisabeth — characteristic Schöneberg-Hugenot female naming. The 1855 manifest does not record a relationship code (wife / sister / sister-in-law / niece / fellow emigrant), and Johannes's FamilySearch profile has no spouse attached. Without a relationship indicator on the manifest or a corroborating German-side record, Lisette should be treated as a co-traveler of unknown relationship, not a wife. (An earlier note on this page treated her as the "wife candidate" for the suicide event Peter described in his correspondence; both inferences have been retracted — see the amber correction callout above.)

4 May 2026 · US-records pass

An Ancestry sweep across U.S. census, immigration, and obituary collections returned three substantive leads:

SourceMatchSignificance
1860 U.S. Federal Census "Jos Grand" + "Mary Grand", household at New York Ward 5 District 2 (Lower Manhattan) — both b. ~1828, listed birthplace France, Male / Female Plausible match for Johannes. Schöneberg was a French Hugenot colony — Schöneberg Grandjots commonly self-identified as French in 19th-century US records, even though they were born in Hesse. The "Grand" surname is the Anglicized truncation of "Grandjot" the indexer arrived at — same pattern that produced "Grandjos" and "Grandgot" elsewhere. The 1860 census schedule did not consistently record relationship-to-head-of-household, so "Mary Grand" alongside "Jos Grand" is a co-resident of unstated relationship — possibly wife, possibly sister, possibly Lisette anglicized, possibly someone else entirely. (An earlier draft asserted Mary was Lisette anglicized and was Johannes's wife; that was inference layered on inference. Both retracted.)
U.S. Newspapers.com Obituary Index, 1800s–current — Retracted. An earlier draft cited an "open lead" for a "Lisette Grand-I" entry in this index. A direct exact-match query of the index on Ancestry (4 May 2026) returns zero records under the surname Grandjot, so the prior reference was a misinterpretation of a fuzzy-match result, not a real index entry. The wife-suicide framing was itself based on a misreading of Peter's 5 Mar 2026 email — the suicide he described was Carl Ludwig's wife in Germany, not anyone in the U.S. — so even if an obituary did exist, the predicate for searching for it has dissolved.
NYC Grandjot descendants — separate follow-up 1900 Manhattan: Louis Grandgot + Mary Grandgot, both b. ~1861-63 Germany, m. 1885 (later arrivals, 1869-1882). 1920 Manhattan AD 14: Harry / Clara / Slyvia / Harold "Dranjo" [indexed as Grandjot] (US-born). 1940 Suffolk NY: Harry Granajot. Multiple Grandjot lineages persist in NYC across 1900–1950 under the surname mutations Grandgot, Granajot, and Dranjo. The 1920 Manhattan family (Harry as head of household, US-born children Slyvia/Harold) is the strongest candidate to trace whether they descend from Johannes — Harry's parents on his birth or marriage record would tell. A separate sub-investigation may be warranted; not pursued here.

Negative results in the same pass: no 1870 or 1880 census match for Johannes/Joseph Grand or Grandjot in NY-state or surrounding states. He may have died, moved further inland, or remained in NYC under a yet-unsearched surname mutation. Ward 5 District 2 in 1860 covered the area roughly bounded by Broadway, Canal, the East River, and Houston — a heavily German/French immigrant district that became a destination for the broader Schöneberg-Carlsdorf cluster of arrivals.

Lineage closure · the Suffolk-NY Grandjots are NOT Johannes's descendants

Net: the NY Grandjot/Grandgot/Granajot/Dranjo lineage — surviving into the 1970s in Suffolk County and Manhattan — descends from Louis Grandgot's 1882 emigration, not from Johannes's 1855 emigration. The two NY Grandjot lines are unrelated. Louis (b. 1861) was almost certainly born to a different Schöneberg-Carlsdorf Grandjot couple than Johannes's parents Jean Daniel × Marie Elisabeth Martin (Louis's exact German parentage is the open lead for tracing the 1882 line, not pursued here). Johannes's American trail goes cold after the 1860 Ward 5 District 2 census — he has no traceable descendants in the 1870, 1880, 1900, or 1910 NY censuses under the Grandjot, Grandgot, Granajot, Grandjean, Grand, or Dranjo surnames. Plausible explanations: he and his household died, dispersed inland, or survived under a yet-unsearched surname mutation.

Verification-check verdict: Johannes Grandjot is decisively not Charles Gannsen — he is documented in 1860 Lower Manhattan at age 32, listed as French-born, while Charles Gannsen is independently documented in St. Louis in the same window with a separate biography. The NYC line of Grandjots (descendants of Louis 1882) is also independently traceable and unrelated to Charles. The verification check is closed.

Open follow-ups
  • Pull the actual 1860 NY Ward 5 District 2 census image for Jos & Mary Grand — confirm household composition, relationship-to-head if recorded, occupation (should read "Schuhmacher / shoemaker"), real-estate value, year of arrival, and any children present
  • Pull the actual Bremen → NYC manifest image (25 Sep 1855 arrival) to verify ship name and any relationship code recorded for Lisette Grandjot on the original document (the indexed record does not preserve a relationship field)
  • Search 1865 NY State Census and 1870 US Federal Census for Joseph or Mary Grand / Grandgot / Dranjo at NYC addresses
  • Trace Harry Granajot/Dranjo (1920–1940 NYC) backwards via the 1900 census and 1880 census — independent line, but worth confirming it is the Louis Grandgot 1882 lineage and not connected to Johannes
  • If a German-side Schöneberg parish marriage record can be located naming Johannes's spouse (or confirming he was unmarried at the time of emigration), update his FamilySearch profile accordingly

Retracted earlier follow-ups: "Pull the Lisette Grand-I obituary" — the Newspapers.com Obituary Index returns zero exact-match results for surname Grandjot (verified 4 May 2026), so the cited index entry was a misread fuzzy match. "Add Lisette to Johannes's FS profile as a spouse" — the manifest does not record a relationship between them and the wife framing was an inference now retracted.
