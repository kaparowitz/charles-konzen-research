10. Wilhelmine Hildebrand line — Hildebrand family · Deisel · Heisebeck · maternal Konze line + chain migration

Discussed throughout; key context April 2026 · status: open
Genealogical content
  • Wilhelmine Hildebrand — read (in the now-retracted HTR reading of Entry 195, Deisel KB 218842) as the Konze mother; that reading was corrected on 4 May 2026 (Entry 195 is a Köster record), so this identification no longer stands.
  • Ludwig Hildebrand family (Ackermann · wife Catherine née Jordan) — emigrated 1850 to Rockford, Illinois (NOT St. Louis).
  • Anna Margaretha Hillebrand — wife of grenadier Johann Christoph Contze, 1814.
  • Three distinct Wilhelmine Hildebrands appear in the Deisel/Trendelburg records, complicating identification.
Status

Premise retracted (4 May 2026). The Hildebrand line entered the investigation only through the Entry 195 HTR reading, now corrected to a Köster record. With no Konze × Hildebrand marriage and no documentary tie to Charles, the line is no longer an active lead. The Ludwig Hildebrand → Rockford chain-migration sub-hypothesis is likewise unsupported once the Entry-195 connection is removed.

Charles Research — a multi-month transatlantic genealogical investigation into Charles Konzen (recorded in U.S. records as Gannsen / Johnson; 1835–1903), Hessian-born Civil War sergeant of Battery B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery, and his probable Trendelburg / Deisel origins in Churhessen (a working conclusion — the German baptism remains unrecovered).
