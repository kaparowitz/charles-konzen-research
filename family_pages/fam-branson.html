Branson → Person family on FamilySearch

Per Peter's Stammtafel Heinrich Branson chart (embedded below in the research log) and the FamilySearch tree, the Deisel Branson branch transitioned to the surname "Person" between 1812 and 1838 — sons of Carl Ludwig Branson (b. 1812) used the new surname while their elder sister kept Branson, and the parental generation already had at least one sibling using Person. This naming-mutation pattern within a single Deisel family is the reason the Branson and Person investigations are interlinked.

Why the surname change matters: The Deisel Branson branch's transition to "Person" within two generations (1812–1838) is the documentary precedent that licenses Peter's phonetic-constraint methodology — surnames mutate dramatically across generations even within the same German village. Charles's own name drift (Konsen → Gonnson → Gannsen → Johnson) follows the same pattern. The full Stammtafel Heinrich Branson chart from Peter is in the research log below; it traces both the Branson and the Frisse marriage branches.

14. Heinrich Branson line — Branson / Brahsuhn / Frisse / Bauer, Wehrden / Schönhagen / Amelunxen

Latest 11 February 2026 · "new overview" · status: eliminated
Peter's genealogical content
  • Christina Elisabeth Branson b. 16 Jan 1808, daughter of Joh. Heinrich Peter Brahsuhn × Victoria Honsel; d. 3 Sep 1863, "left behind one husband, one child majorenne and four minorenne."
  • m. 25 May 1832 Wehrden to Johann Heinrich Frisse b. 1 Jan 1806, son of Johannes Frisse; d. 23 Mar 1879.
  • Children of Frisse–Branson: Maria Sophia (b. 1832), Joh. Carl Joseph (b. 1835), Maria Elisabeth Constantine (b. 1838), Franz Johann (b. 1840 → emigrated U.S., d. 1909 Philadelphia), Heinrich Friedrich Ferdinand (b. 1843), Carl Joseph (b. 1846, d. 1866), Maria Josephina Gertrud (b. 1848).
  • Branson lineage traced back to Carl Ludwig Branson b. 1735, sister Angela Lucia Branson.
  • Linked theory: Karl Ludwig Person sr. b. 1777, widower 12+ years, d. 1839 — proposed possible father of an illegitimate Charles.
Why this line was rejected

All Bransuhns/Frisses were Catholic; Charles was Lutheran/Evangelical. Children either died young, stayed in Germany, or are accounted for in the U.S. (Franz Johann to Philadelphia, not St. Louis). Trail ran cold mid-February 2026.
