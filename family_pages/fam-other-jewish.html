13. Trendelburg Protected Jews 1740 — Ganß / Gumbrecht / Meyer

Trendelburg · Eberschütz · Hofgeismar
SET ASIDE — Jewish-paternal-line inquiryEarliest leads: 31 August 2025 · set aside May 2026
Reactivated by DNA evidence — 30 May 2026. A FamilyTreeDNA Big Y-700 result (on two–three tested members) indicates Charles's direct paternal line is Ashkenazi-Jewish in deep origin (a working inference, not a settled conclusion) — independent biological support for the direction of the Hessian-Jewish inquiry this page documents, though not for its original surname-resemblance basis (Gans → Gannsen). The 1740 Trendelburg "Protected Jews" register (Seligmann Ganß and neighbours) is the documented Jewish presence in Charles's own locality and is therefore worth re-examining as context — but no record yet links any name on it to Charles, and the Y-line's common ancestor (~1450 CE) may well predate this list. This thread had been set aside in May 2026 when its surname-resemblance basis and tested candidates did not hold up; the working surname conclusion (Konzen, of the Trendelburg-area parish complex) still rests on documentary evidence. See DNA evidence and Open Questions · deep paternal origin.

13. Trendelburg "Protected Jews" 1740 — Seligmann Ganß · Salomon Gumbrecht · widow Jacob Meyer (Trendelburg / Eberschütz / Hofgeismar)

Latest attachment 19 March 2026 · "puzzle pieces" · status: open
Peter's genealogical content
  • Documents on Jewish residents of Trendelburg and Eberschütz, including a Jewish citizen list from Trendelburg ~1740.
  • Solomon Gandt (Trendelburg) and Jeanette Gans (Eberschütz) flagged as surname-resemblance candidates.
  • "Protected Jews" of Trendelburg/Deisel from 31 Aug 2025 thread: Seligmann Ganß of Trendelburg, Salomon Gumbrecht of Trendelburg, the widow of Jacob Meyer of Deisel.
  • 1812 lists of Jewish males in Deisel, Trendelburg, etc.
Status

Set aside (May 2026). No specific named candidate was developed; these names were kept on a watch list because the surnames "Gandt" and "Gans" are phonetically close to "Gannsen." The Jewish-paternal-line inquiry this list belonged to is not currently being pursued. The ~1750 tax registers (every citizen, all faiths) are noted as a possible future step.
