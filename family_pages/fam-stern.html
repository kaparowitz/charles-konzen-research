9. Joseph Sterne / Minna Kleeburg née Stern line

Hofgeismar Jewish family — a candidate line of inquiry now set aside
SET ASIDE — Jewish-paternal-line inquiryExplored spring 2026 · set aside May 2026
Reactivated by DNA evidence — 30 May 2026. A Big Y-700 result (on two–three tested members) indicates Charles's direct paternal line is Ashkenazi-Jewish in deep origin — a working inference, not a settled conclusion — giving independent biological support to the direction of this Hessian-Jewish inquiry, which was explored in spring 2026 and then set aside. The Stern / Kleeburg family of Hofgeismar remains an exploratory regional candidate only — it was never tied to Charles by a record, and the DNA does not promote it: the Y-evidence speaks to deep ancestry, not to a specific family. The working conclusion on Charles's surname (Konzen, of the Trendelburg-area parish complex) rests on documentary evidence. See DNA evidence and Open Questions · deep paternal origin.

★ At a Glance

SET ASIDE — Jewish-paternal-line inquiry Explored spring 2026 · set aside May 2026

A set-aside line of inquiry. During spring 2026 the project explored whether Charles's paternal origin might lie with a Hessian-Jewish family — the Stern / Kleeburg family of the Hofgeismar area being the geographically plausible cluster. That inquiry has been set aside: it was never tied to Charles by a documentary record, and the project's working surname conclusion (Konzen, of the Trendelburg-area parish complex) rests on documentary evidence.

Why this matters for finding Charles: This was an exploratory thread, not a documented identification — no record connects Charles to the Stern / Kleeburg family. The Trendelburg "Protected Jews" list (1740) and the ~1750 Deisel/Trendelburg tax registers remain on file as a potential future avenue. See also fam-other-jewish.html (Trendelburg Protected Jews 1740).

9. Joseph Sterne / Minna Kleeburg née Stern line — Hofgeismar Jewish family

Latest attachment 18 March 2026 · "graveyard" · status: set aside May 2026
Peter's genealogical content
  • Survey of the Jewish graveyard at Trendelburg (attached 18 March 2026) with names & dates from gravestones.
  • Stern family of Trendelburg — divorce proceeding flagged in the records; Jed ordered the divorce papers from the relevant archive.
  • Deisel/Trendelburg tax registers ~1750 list every citizen including Jewish residents, complementing the church-only Archion search — noted as a possible future avenue.
  • Joseph Sterne and his son Paul Sterne (paternal line traced to Soetern, Birkenfeld, and Hoppstädten in Oldenburg) were examined during this inquiry; no documentary link to Charles was established.
  • Minna Kleeburg née Stern — b. 15 April 1832 — appears on the LAGIS Hofgeismar Jewish-cemetery database (wife of Simon Kleeburg).
Why this line was explored, and why it is set aside

In spring 2026 the project explored whether Charles's paternal origin might lie with a Hessian-Jewish family, which would explain the absence of a Christian-parish baptism. The Stern family of Hofgeismar was the geographically plausible candidate cluster. The thread was never documented as Charles's specific line, and the project has set it aside (May 2026); the working surname conclusion rests on documentary evidence. This page is retained in case future documentation reopens the question.
