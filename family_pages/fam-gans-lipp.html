17. Joh. Daniel Gans × Karoline Haake line — Gans / Lippolsberg, Christian (m. 1829 Deisel)

14–15 December 2025 · "Haake and Gans" · status: eliminated
Peter's genealogical content
  • Johann Daniel Gans b. Lippolsberg 1787, m. 1829 Karoline Haake in Deisel.
  • Daughter (1831) and son (1833) born in Deisel; family later returned to Lippoldsberg. Lippoldsberg Gans clan emigrated as the Hellmuth family (the unmarried Gans mother married a Hellmuth in the U.S.).
  • Illegitimate Karl Gans b. 1844, mother a Gans, emigrated U.S. 1846 — too young.
  • Naturalization analysis: Charles declared origin "Prussia" in 1868 (consistent with Hesse annexed by Prussia 1866).
  • Godparent analysis of Charles's St. Louis baptisms: early baptisms used apparent strangers (Jacob Reck & Josephine née von Delian; Friedrich Wilhelm Pillman & Catharina Albrecht); later baptisms used Kreichelt in-laws.
  • Erwin Spraul (1875 godparent) was born in Baden — likely a friend, not kin.
Peter's working theory

"People of Deisel call themselves Gans (= goose) — and Charles calls himself Gans-son, son of Deisel." The surname could be a place-of-origin nickname rather than a true family name, which is why no Gans baptism shows up.

Why this line was rejected

The Lippoldsberg Gans clan resurfaced in the U.S. as Hellmuth, not Gannsen. The 1844 illegitimate Karl Gans is too young. The detailed Haake/Gans Deisel work was the closest fit but produced no specific named match. The godparent strangers (Reck, Pillman, von Delian) never produced a German origin link.
