1. Joh. Friedrich Kontze line — Konze / Kontze / Konsen

Trendelburg & Deisel · Hofgeismar Kirchenkreis
ELIMINATED as a legitimate line · illegitimate descent & surname link OPENVerdict: 12 April 2026

★ At a Glance

ELIMINATED as a legitimate line · illegitimate descent & surname link OPEN Active focus Apr 2026 · verdict 12 April 2026

Once the leading hypothesis for Charles's German paternal line. Peter Schräder mapped the Joh. Friedrich Kontze line in Trendelburg / Deisel and noted the family's "unusual case" pattern (handicapped daughter, secret births, abandoned father Andreas Konze who left in 1854 and died in Mainz). On 12 April 2026 Peter checked his Konze database against Deisel emigration lists and concluded "Charles is not a legitimate Konze son" — only one Konze appears in the relevant emigration list, and it is not Charles. Charles is therefore eliminated as a biological descendant of this family. The page is preserved because the 1875 St. Marcus baptism of Erwin Carl Konsen still points back to this name cluster — that surname-preservation puzzle is open even though biological descent is closed.

Why this matters for finding Charles: The 1875 St. Marcus baptism of Erwin Carl Konsen preserved the German Konsen / Konze spelling — the only Hesse-style spelling of the family name in the entire American record. This is the strongest surname-preservation evidence in the project. Biological descent from this family is rejected, but the name link from this family cluster to Charles remains unresolved.

Peter's chart — received via email

1. Joh. Friedrich Kontze line — Konze / Kontze / Konsen, Trendelburg & Deisel

Latest attachments 5–8 April 2026 · "Andreas Konze" / "Re: Andreas Konze" · status: open / contested
Peter's genealogical content
  • Andreas Konze — grandfather a council member (Rathsverwandter) in Trendelburg, mentioned at Andreas's baptism. After his first wife died he married her sister.
  • Andreas's second wife bore two children in 1834 (unusual — normal pause is 2 years); their son August was born October 1834 with Andreas himself listed as godfather (highly irregular).
  • Andreas's eldest daughter (b. 1822) was handicapped — confirmed at age 15 with poor grades "because of missing skills." She died in Stammen giving birth to an illegitimate child; the child died with her. She had hidden the pregnancy and never named the father.
  • Andreas himself left his family secretly in 1854 and was found later in Trendelburg-Friedrichsfeld records as a factory-worker, dying in Mainz in 1854. His Mainz death record (verified on Ancestry) describes him as single with no family.
  • Three of Andreas's grandchildren (children of the handicapped daughter) were recorded at baptism as "children of the lost-without-trace Andreas Konze."
  • Daughter Maria Caroline Konze (from his second marriage) was godmother to her half-niece in 1850 — so she remained in the area.
  • Confirmation records for Andreas's second-marriage children have a gap until 1880, frustrating tracing.
Peter's working theory

Peter felt the unusual circumstances of this family — handicapped daughter, secret births, abandoned father — fit Charles's "unusual case" pattern. His hypothesis: Charles may have been an illegitimate son of Andreas Konze, born somewhere outside Deisel, raised briefly in Deisel or Trendelburg, then emigrated.

Why this line is contested

On 12 April 2026 Peter checked his Konze database against Deisel emigration lists and concluded: "Charles is not a legitimate Konze son." Only one Konze appears in the relevant emigration list and it is not Charles. The line is not closed — the 1875 St. Marcus baptism preserving the surname KONSEN remains unexplained — but Charles is no longer placed inside the Konze tree as a legitimate biological descendant. The elimination rests on a single Deisel emigration-list check, not a positive disproof of descent; the closest-fitting individual — Justus Ernst Konze (b. 14 November 1829, untraced after his 1843 confirmation) — therefore remains an open, unresolved thread rather than a positively excluded one, and the illegitimate-descent hypothesis is likewise not falsified.

3 May 2026 update — children added to FamilySearch

★ 3 May 2026 update: Three previously-Archion-only children of Andreas + Maria Sophia have been added to the FamilySearch tree this session — Justus Ernst Konze (P87Z-ZDC), Maria Sabina Kontze (P878-ZSG), and August Kontze (P878-BL1, the 2-day emergency baptism) — alongside the previously-existing Heinrich Wilhelm (PZFD-1X4). A research note documenting Peter's account of Elisa Melusine's disability, Stammen death, and her three "lost-without-trace Andreas Konze" grandchildren has also been attached to her FS profile (PZF6-H42).

4 May 2026 update — Entry 195 (KB 218842, 23 Jan 1835) eliminated

★ 4 May 2026 update — Entry 195 not a Konze record at all. Peter Schräder re-read the original Kurrent script and reports that Entry 195 (KB 218842, image 40, January 1835), which the project had been treating as the strongest single-record Konze candidate for Charles's birth registration, is in fact not a Konze record. Per Peter's email of 4 May 2026: the child's surname was Küster / Kuester (Köster), Tagelöhner (day-labourer); the previously-transcribed "Justus" is actually the German word "männlich" (male) — used when an infant dies before baptism; the boy was born and died three days later without a baptism. The Section 7 Entry 195 ⇄ Entry 261 collision (the Justus Köster confirmation 1849) is also resolved: those two entries belong to different Deisel Köster households. Net effect on this page: the Konze biological-descent verdict (12 April 2026) was already eliminated; Entry 195 is now eliminated as well, reinforcing that Charles is not in the Deisel Konze paper trail. See the corrected Section #6 on Key Evidence, the eliminated banner on Entry 195 source page, and the 4 May 2026 entry on the Peter Schräder correspondence timeline.
