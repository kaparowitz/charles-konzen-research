## Part 1 — Ludwig Hildebrand Household: 1850 Census Negative; 1860 Census FOUND

### 1A. 1850 US Federal Census — NO match

Searched the 1850 US Federal Census collection (Ancestry collection #8054):
- Query: Ludwig / Lewis Hildebrand, born ~1808, Hessen Germany, lived Winnebago Illinois → 0 in Winnebago
- Query: Catherine Hildebrand, b. ~1811, Germany → 7 results, none in Illinois
- Query: All Hildebrand surnames in Winnebago Co. IL → only Pennsylvania-born Hildebrands (no German-born)

**Conclusion:** The Ludwig × Catherine Jordan Hildebrand family is NOT enumerated in the 1850 US Federal Census. Reason: LAGIS confirms departure January 1850; transit took ~6–10 weeks; arrival ~March/April 1850. The 1850 census enumeration date was June 1, 1850 — they should have been counted. The most likely explanations are: (a) they arrived after the local enumerator passed through their initial settlement area, (b) they were missed entirely, or (c) they are indexed under a misspelled surname not surfacing in standard search variants.

### 1B. 1860 US Federal Census — FAMILY FOUND: Monroe Township, Ogle County, Illinois

The full Hildebrand household, located in **Monroe Township, Ogle County, Illinois (Post Office: Lindenwood)** — about 15 miles south of Rockford, just over the Winnebago / Ogle county line. Indexed by Ancestry as "Hillabrand" (a misspelling of Hildebrand):

| Name | Age (1860) | Calc Birth | Notes |
|------|-----|-----|-----|
| Lewis Hillabrand | 43 | 1817 | Father, Germany-born (WikiTree says Ludwig b. 1808; census age likely off by ~9 yrs — typical) |
| Catherine Hillabrand | 50 | 1810 | Mother, Germany-born (WikiTree says Catherine b. 1811) |
| Fredrick Hillabrand | 23 | 1837 | Son, Germany-born (= Friedrich, the early-confirmed of KB 218836 1850 Entry 1) |
| Theodore Hillabrand | 22 | 1838 | Son, Germany-born |
| Godfery Hillabrand | 16 | 1844 | Son = Gottlieb (LAGIS 1845, WikiTree 1844) |
| Laura Hillabrand | 14 | 1846 | Daughter |
| Louis Hillabrand | 11 | 1849 | Son = Ludwig Theophil (LAGIS 1849) |
| Charles Hillabrand | 8 | 1852 | Son, US-born |
| John Hillabrand | 5 | 1855 | Son, US-born |

**Total: 9 individuals — parents + 7 children. NO unaccounted teenager.**

Notable observations:
- LAGIS-listed Sophie Amalie (b. 1847) is NOT in the 1860 household — she likely died young (between 1850 emigration and 1860 census).
- Henry Hildebrand (per WikiTree) is also missing from the 1860 census household — possibly died young or already left home (would have been ~25 in 1860 if eldest).
- Charles William and John (per WikiTree) are present as Charles age 8 and John age 5 — both US-born after 1850 arrival.

**Conclusion (Hypothesis B testing):** The 1860 Hildebrand household contains NO extra male teenager who could be Justus Köster (b. 1835, age 25 in 1860 — if he were living with the family). Justus did NOT emigrate with the Hildebrands to Rockford in 1850, nor did he join them in their Ogle County household by 1860.

This eliminates Hypothesis B from Session 75: "Justus emigrated 1850 with the Hildebrands to Rockford, then relocated to St. Louis before 1862 enlistment" — the Rockford/Ogle Hildebrand household never includes Justus. (He might still have come to America with them and immediately moved on without being enumerated in their household, but no evidence supports that.)

## Part 2 — October 1853 Emigration: PASSENGER LIST FOUND

### 2A. Manifest match: ship "President Smidt," arrival 14 January 1854, New York

The LAGIS October 1853 Wilhelmine Maria Hildebrand family emigration was located in the NY Arriving Passenger Lists collection (Ancestry #7488). Departure October 1853 from Bremen → arrival 14 January 1854 New York on the ship **President Smidt**.

The Ancestry index calls her "Marie Hildebrand" (her use-name = Wilhelmine Maria from LAGIS).

### 2B. Manifest entries (rows 97–101)

Read directly from the original manifest image:

| Line | Name | Age | Sex | Origin | Destination |
|------|------|-----|-----|--------|-------------|
| 97 | Marie Hildebrand | 33 | F | **Deisel** | **Philadelphia** |
| 98 | Marie | 11 | F | (ditto) | (ditto) |
| 99 | Amalie | 7 | F | (ditto) | (ditto) |
| 100 | Ludwig | 5 | M | (ditto) | (ditto) |
| 101 | Theodor | 2 | M | (ditto) | (ditto) |

The manifest **explicitly names "Deisel"** as the place of origin and **"Philadelphia"** as the destination. The five-person family group (mother + 4 children) matches the LAGIS entries exactly.

Key new fact: **destination = Philadelphia.** Not Rockford. Not St. Louis. The 1853 Hildebrand cohort from Deisel went to Philadelphia, PA.

### 2C. Adjacent manifest entries (rows 95–103)

The manifest does NOT show any extra Hildebrand or Köster traveler between rows 97–101. The line immediately following Theodor (row 102) is "Nicolaus Krauss, 36, M, Baker, Rinchgau, Buffalo" — entirely unrelated.

**Conclusion:** Justus Köster did NOT travel with the October 1853 / January 1854 Wilhelmine Maria Hildebrand cohort. There is no extra male teenager attached to that family group.

### 2D. Implications

The 1853 Hildebrand cohort is now fully characterized: a single mother (Wilhelmine Maria, 33, possibly widow) traveling with four young children (ages 2–11) from Deisel to Philadelphia. Their household composition rules them out as the chain-migration vehicle for Justus.

Philadelphia is in eastern Pennsylvania, ~840 miles from St. Louis. There is no obvious Philadelphia-to-St. Louis link connecting Charles Gannsen to this family.

## Part 3 — Stephen Hildebrand on DeKalb Street (1860 St. Louis): NOT from Hessen

- **Birth Place: BADEN** (NOT Hessen / Kurhessen)
- Home: St Louis Ward 1, St. Louis, Missouri
- Post Office: St Louis
- Occupation: Day Laborer (note: 1860 city directory said "shoemaker" — possibly two different individuals or directory error)
- Personal Estate Value: $25
- Wife: Anna M Hildebrand, age 32
- Children: Anna M (9), John (7), Adolph (4), Henry (2), Catharina (2 months)

### 3B. Conclusion

**Stephen Hildebrand of DeKalb Street is from Baden, southwest Germany — NOT Deisel / Kurhessen.** This conclusively rules him out as a Deisel-Hildebrand chain-migration link to Charles Gannsen on the same street. The DeKalb Street coincidence is just that — a coincidence.

This finding is consistent with the Buecher St. Marcus burial index from Session 76, which showed all three Hildebrandt burials at St. Marcus came from non-Hessian origins (Osnabrück, Lübeck, Baden). Baden is the same origin as Stephen.

## Part 4 — 1854-1859 Passenger Lists: Plausible Carl Kinzer; No Definitive Justus Match

Searched NY Arriving Passenger Lists for Justus Köster b. ~1835 Germany — 12 results, none matching Charles Gannsen's profile:

**No Justus Köster passenger arrival from a German port in the 1854–1859 window matches Charles Gannsen.**

### 4B. "Carl Konzen" search: 17 results — top candidate identified

Searched NY Arriving Passenger Lists for Carl Konzen b. ~1835 Germany — 17 results. Top-of-list candidate:

**Carl Kinzer**:
- Age: 22
- Birth Date: abt 1837 (off by 2 years from Justus's January 1835)
- Place of Origin: Germany (no specific province)
- Departure Port: **Liverpool, England**
- Destination: USA
- Arrival Date: **20 December 1859**
- Arrival Port: New York, NY
- Ship: **Resolute**
- Ethnicity: German

**This is a plausible — but unverified — match for Charles Gannsen's "1859 immigration":**
- ✓ Age within 2 years (22 vs. 24)
- ✓ German
- ✓ Arrived December 1859 (matches the 1900 census claim of immigration 1859)
- ✗ Liverpool departure (not Bremen — but German emigrants often transited through Liverpool)
- ✗ Surname Kinzer (not Konzen / Köster — but indexer transliteration of handwritten German "K" surnames into "Kinzer" could mask original "Konzen" or "Konze")
- ? Place of origin only "Germany," no Hessen specifier (passenger list didn't capture it)

This needs the manifest image consulted to determine the original handwritten surname spelling and any further origin/destination clues.

### 4C. "Konzen × Hesse Germany × 1854" search: 254 results

| Justus was confirmed in another German parish 1849–1851, then emigrated as adult | **OPEN — not yet investigated** (Trendelburg-area parishes other than Deisel) |

### 5B. The DeKalb Street coincidence is dissolved

Stephen Hildebrand of 1860 DeKalb Street is from **Baden**, definitively NOT Deisel. The geographic coincidence with Charles Gannsen's later DeKalb Street address has no genealogical significance.

### 5C. Charles Gannsen's German surname confirmation deepens

While not the original task, Session 77's broad searches reaffirm the surname puzzle:
- LAGIS Auswanderer index: 0 records for Justus Köster, 0 records for Justus Konze, 1 record for "Konze, Ludwig, Deisel, October 1864" (too late).
- Ancestry NY Passenger Lists: no Hessen-specific Justus match.
- St. Marcus burial index (Session 76): 3 St. Marcus Konzen records confirm Charles's German surname KONZEN.
- The 1860 census of every "Hildebrand" in St. Louis, Winnebago, and Ogle counties contains no person directly traceable to Deisel.

The surname mystery and the emigration-route mystery converge on the same conclusion: Charles Gannsen / Carl Konzen emigrated from Hessen by an undocumented route that left no fingerprint in either the Hessian state archive index or the standard NY arrival manifests indexed by Ancestry. The Carl Kinzer 1859 Liverpool→NY arrival is the strongest single candidate but is not conclusive without manifest image inspection and additional cross-references.
