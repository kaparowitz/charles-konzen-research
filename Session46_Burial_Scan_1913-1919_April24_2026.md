## Headline

Session 46 continued the systematic page-by-page scan of the post-1876 Deisel burial register (Register 218854) from Image 140 through Image 162, covering years 1913–1919. **Key results**: (1) **3 direct Konze/Conze burial entries found** — Heinrich Carl Conze (born 1825, died 1914, age 88), Marie Amalie Conze née Hillebrand (born ~1858, died 1914, wife of Heinrich Conze Schuhmacher), and Heinrich Conze Ackermann (born 1835, died 1919); (2) **No "geb. Konze/Kunze" references found** in this stretch — a notable gap compared to the 1907–1912 scan; (3) **Multiple connected family entries** — Meinbreße (Images 142, 161), Hillebrand (Images 160, 161), Jarre (Images 141, 160, 161, 162), Fanning (Image 147); (4) **WWI-era deaths begin** — Lazarett (military hospital) references appear from 1915, "im Krieg Gefallenen" (war dead) noted in year summaries; (5) **1918 Spanish Flu year uncertain** — the register may have had very few civilian entries for 1918, or entries are on pages that were ambiguously dated; (6) **Cumulative scan now covers 1876–1919** across Images 5–162.

## Notable Observations

### WWI Impact on Deisel Burials
- **1915 year summary** explicitly notes "im Krieg Gefallenen" (war dead), with 15 total deaths
- Entry 838 (1915) references a "Lazarett" (military hospital) death
- The 1916–1918 period shows relatively sparse civilian entries, possibly because:
  - Young men were away at war (fewer civilian deaths in working-age cohort)
  - War deaths were recorded separately or in military registers
  - The register may include only returned remains or locally buried war dead

### Absence of "geb. Konze" References
- Unlike the 1907–1912 scan which found 7 "geb. Konze/Kunze" references, this 1913–1919 scan found **zero**. This may be due to:
  - Smaller sample of total deaths reviewed
  - Random fluctuation in which family members died
  - Possible that Konze women married into families living outside Deisel by this period

### Image Numbering Anomalies
- The Archion register has duplicate images for the same double-page spreads (Images 145/146, 148/149, 153/159, 154/155 are duplicates)
- Blank template pages appear at Images 150, 152, 156 (unused pages in the physical register)
- This means the effective scan from Image 140 to 162 covers approximately 15 unique content pages
