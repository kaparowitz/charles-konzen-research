## New Findings

### 1. Deisel Marriage Index — NO Kontze Marriages

I located the marriage index for the Deisel KB 1796–1831. It spans images 1821–1827 (with X-card separators at images 1822 and 1826), covering letter sections P, R, S, T, W, and Z. The K section is **entirely absent** — meaning no Kontze marriages were recorded in Deisel parish during 1796–1831.

This is a significant negative finding. While the Kontze family was living and dying in Deisel throughout this period, their marriages apparently took place in other parishes (possibly Trendelburg, where we already know Andreas Kontze married in 1782).

### 2. MAJOR DISCOVERY: Two 1796 Burial Entries

While navigating the marriage index area, I discovered a page of burial entries from **1796** (the first year of the register). The year is confirmed by the year-end summary: "17 sind in diesem Jahr bisher begraben" followed by "Begraben im Jahr 1797" on image 1828. Two Kontze entries appear on this page:

**Entry §14: Johann Conrad, son of Johann Christoph Kontze**
- Died 1796 (January)
- Age: 18 years, 1 month, 20 days → **born approximately November/December 1777**
- Father: **Johann Christoph Kontze** — this is the "Grenadier" from the burial index!
- A partially legible descriptor follows "Kontze" — needs further paleographic analysis

**Entry §16(?): Carl Wilhelm, illegitimate son of Andreas Kontze**
- Died 1796 (approximately May/June)
- Age: 19 years, 11 months, 15 days → **born approximately June/July 1776**
- Father: **Andreas Kontze** — specifically described as father of an illegitimate ("unehelicher") child
- Died at 10 o'clock in the evening

**Analysis:** Carl Wilhelm was born approximately 6 years BEFORE Andreas Kontze's 1782 marriage to Anna Margaretha Thiele, meaning Andreas fathered a child out of wedlock as a young man (~18 years old). This was not uncommon in 18th-century Hessen. Johann Christoph Kontze is now confirmed as a Deisel resident with family — his son's 1796 burial places him there definitively.

## What This Means

The 1796 discoveries push the documented Kontze family presence in Deisel even further. We now have Kontze individuals born as early as ~1760 (Anna Catharina) and dying in Deisel from the very first year of this register (1796). The family includes at least two distinct patriarchs — Andreas Kontze and Johann Christoph Kontze (the Grenadier) — both with children documented in Deisel in the 1770s.

The absence of Kontze marriages in Deisel, combined with the extensive burial presence, suggests the family resided in Deisel but married in neighboring parishes — a pattern consistent with their known ties to Trendelburg.
