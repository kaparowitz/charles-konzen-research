## Method

Class-by-class read of every confirmation class 1847–1855 (the cohort in which a
boy born 1833–1841 — working anchor **26 Jan 1835** — would be confirmed). Each
boy's given name scanned for **Carl/Karl**; the surname of every Carl checked
against the Konze / Kontze / Gansen / Gonsen / *n·s·n* canon. Boys-first then
"Mädchen / B. Töchter" in each class; year-end "Summa … N Knaben, M Mädchen"
tallies used to confirm completeness.

## Result — NEGATIVE

Across the full 1847–1855 window the forename **Carl recurs ~12 times but never on
a Konze / Kontze / Gansen / Gonsen / *n·s·n* surname**, and **no boy was born
26 Jan 1835**. The surnames are all local Immenhausen families — Heinemann,
Bonnet, Hornung, Engelhard, Jäger, Homburg, Waldeck, Brandau, Richter, Wittig,
Carl. The **prime 1849 and 1850 classes** — the exact cohort for the working
birthdate — hold five Carls between them, all on non-matching surnames; the 1851
and 1855 classes hold no Carl at all.

One point of interest: the 1849 **Carl Jäger** has mother *geb. Naumann* (a
name in the project's Naumann place-name thread), but the child's surname is
**Jäger** and his father is Georg Jäger, a local Tagelöhner — not a Konze.

**Immenhausen is now searched on BOTH tracks** — baptism (reg. 219586, 1835) and
confirmation (reg. 219580, 1847–1855) — negative on both. Surname reads are
by-eye Kurrent, to be certified by Peter Schräder.
