### Task 15: Map Seite Numbers to Image Numbers
- **Status**: COMPLETED
- **Method**: Navigated to three calibration points in the register to read entry numbers and Seite (page) numbers:
  - Image 1790 = Seite 6, entries 18–23
  - Image 1848 = Seite 64, entries 335–342
  - Image 1850 = entries 349–354
- **Formula**: The index "Seite" numbers correspond to sequential entry numbers (laufende Nummer), NOT page numbers. Approximately 6 entries per image page.
- **Key insight**: The physical page number (Seite, printed on the register page) follows the formula Image = 1784 + Seite. But the alphabetical index uses ENTRY NUMBERS, not page numbers, despite labeling them "Seite."

## Analysis: The Search for J.H. Konze #2

### What we now know

Johann Heinrich Konze #2 — the father listed in Entry 195 (January 1835 baptism of a child with Wilhelmine geb. Hildebrand) — has NO confirmed death record in the Deisel Beerdigungen 1830–1876.

The two male Konze entries in the burial index have been resolved:
1. **Entry 358**: Conrad Konze (son of Andreas), born 1780, died 1841. Wrong person.
2. **Entry 380**: Could not be identified as a Konze entry — index discrepancy.

### Where is J.H. Konze #2's death record?

Several possibilities remain:

**A. He died after 1876.** The burial register only covers through 1876. If J.H. Konze #2 was born around 1800–1810 (a reasonable estimate for someone fathering children in the 1830s), he could have lived to age 66–76+ and died after 1876. A later burial register would need to be searched.

**B. He died elsewhere.** He may have left Deisel at some point — perhaps emigrating (like his possible son Charles Gannsen) or moving to another parish.

**C. He is the "Johannes Konze" referenced in Entry 379.** Catharina Elisabeth née Jordan was the widow of "Ackermann Johannes Konze." If J.H. Konze #2 had a first wife (Catharina Elisabeth Jordan) who died in 1842, and he then married Wilhelmine Hildebrand... but this chronology doesn't work because Entry 195 (child with Hildebrand) is from 1835, seven years BEFORE Catharina Elisabeth Jordan's death in 1842. Unless the "Johannes Konze" in Entry 379 is yet another person.

**D. His entry was misread or his surname was spelled differently.** The index might list him under a variant spelling (Kontze, Contze, etc.) or in a different section.

### The Konze family tree expands

This session revealed at least THREE distinct Konze men who can now be placed:

1. **Andreas Konze** — father of Conrad Konze (revealed through Entry 358)
2. **Conrad Konze** (1780–1841) — son of Andreas, married Anna Catharina née Köster
3. **Johannes Konze, Ackermann** — married Catharina Elisabeth née Jordan, died before 1842

Combined with previous findings:
4. **Johann Heinrich Konze #1** — married Maria Donner 1824 (Session 34)
5. **Johann Heinrich Konze #2** — married Wilhelmine Hildebrand, father of Entry 195 (Session 26)
6. **Johann Christoph Konze, Ackermann** — former Bürgermeister of Deisel, father of Philipp Konze (Sessions 34, 36)

## Technical Notes

- **Register 218851**: Deisel Beerdigungen (Burials) 1830–1876, Images 1784–2069
- **Entry-to-Image mapping**: Index "Seite" = sequential entry number (laufende Nummer). ~6 entries per image page. Entry 358 → Image 1851 (confirmed). Entry 379 → Image 1854 (confirmed).
- **Page number formula**: Physical Seite printed on page = Image number − 1784. (Seite 6 = Image 1790, Seite 116 = Image 1900)
- **Index accuracy**: The index-to-entry mapping was confirmed for entries 358, 359, and 379. Entries 380 and 461 did not match, likely due to misread index numbers rather than systematic error.
- **Kurrent paleography note**: "Conradt" was initially misread as "Joannes" in the alphabetical index — a reminder that Kurrent capital letters (especially C/J and similar letterforms) require very high zoom for reliable reading.
