## Key Findings

### 1. Ludwig Kontze — Burial Entry Transcribed (Image 1845, page 43)

I located and transcribed Ludwig Kontze's burial entry:

- **Death:** April 29, 1813, at 9 o'clock in the evening
- **Age:** 39 years, 10 months → **born approximately June 1773**
- **Important reference:** The entry mentions "Rosine Grebin geb. Kontzin" — meaning *Rosine Greb née Kontze*. This is a previously unknown Kontze woman who married into the Greb family. The feminine form "Kontzin" confirms her birth surname was Kontze.
- **Place name:** A partially legible place name ending in "-feld" appears in the entry — needs further paleographic analysis.
- **Hoffeditz connection:** Directly below Ludwig's entry on the same page are Hoffeditz family entries, reinforcing the Kontze-Hoffeditz geographic proximity in Deisel.

Ludwig was born c. June 1773, placing him in the same generation as Andreas Kontze (I) who married in Trendelburg in 1782. He could be a brother, cousin, or other close relative.

### 2. Anna Catharina Kuntze — NEW Discovery (Image 1865)

While searching for the Andreas entry, I discovered a previously unknown Kontze family member:

- **Death:** December 29, 1826 (buried January 1827)
- **Age:** 66 years, 11 months, 8 days → **born approximately January 18, 1760**
- **Name spelling:** "Kuntze" (with u) — a common variant
- **Status:** Described as an elderly person using her maiden name, suggesting she was unmarried or widowed

Born c. 1760, Anna Catharina belongs to the generation BEFORE Ludwig (b. 1773) and Andreas. She could be their mother, aunt, or older sister. This pushes the documented Kontze presence in Deisel back to at least the 1760s.

### 3. Amalia Kontze — Infant Burial (Image 1867)

Another discovery: an infant burial entry:

- **Age:** 9 months, 2 days
- **Parent:** A Kontze father (first name partially legible, possibly Andreas)
- **Approximate death year:** 1828

### 4. Andreas Kontze — Index Re-Verified (Page 82)

I re-examined the burial index and confirmed "Kontze, Andreas — page 82." I also discovered additional Kontze index entries that were missed in the initial search:

- **Johann Friedrich Kontze** — pages 42 and 51 (two separate entries!)
- **Johann Conrad Kontze** — early page
- **Unknown Kontze** — page 85

In total, **at least 7–8 Kontze family members** are recorded in the Deisel burial register 1796–1831.

Unfortunately, the actual register entry for Andreas at page 82 could not be definitively read at the Archion viewer's resolution — the Kurrentschrift in that section is particularly dense. Higher-resolution images or in-person examination would be needed.

## What This Means

This is a breakthrough. Rather than a single marriage connection (Johannes Kontze marrying Anna Margaretha Thiele of Deisel in 1780), we now see an **entire extended Kontze family** living and dying in Deisel parish from at least the 1760s through the 1830s. Combined with Charles Gannsen's identification as "aus Deisse" in the 1866 St. Louis marriage record and the "Gans" Hausname connection, Deisel is now firmly established as the Kontze family's primary parish — likely their home after they disappeared from the Trendelburg registers around 1817.
