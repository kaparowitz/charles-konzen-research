★ Who Peter is

Peter Schräder is a Hessian genealogist who has been the principal collaborator on this investigation. The active research phase began in August 2025 and has run continuously since. This page captures the chronology of the back-and-forth so that any prior topic can be located quickly.

Peter's working operating principles

  • Geographic constraint. Pre-train Hesse: an 1859 emigrant from the north would travel Trendelburg → Karlshafen on the Weser → Bremen, NOT to Le Havre. Filters U.S.-arrival records.
  • Phonetic constraint. Charles answered an English-speaking clerk in German. "Trendelburg" became "Freundburg" / "Trendelb-urg" / "Deisse." "Konsen" became "Gonnson" / "Gannsen." Many surname matches require allowing for this distortion.
  • Charles is findable. A recurring methodological theme in Peter's correspondence (paraphrased): each elimination is progress, because the real Charles — a documented person — still exists in the records somewhere, under some spelling.
  • Dead-end discipline. Peter is rigorous about confirming eliminations through to a death record or alternative U.S. trace, not just absence of evidence.

6 Dec 2025 · ★★★ "Trendelburg / Freudenberg" — the Freundburg breakthrough
★ One of Peter's most important contributions to the project. Peter argues that the place-name "Freundburg" on Charles's enlistment card is a phonetic transcription artifact: when an English-speaking U.S. military clerk asked Charles where he came from, Charles answered in German "Trendelburg". The methodology — phonetic mutation under English-speaking clerks — also underwrites the project's tolerance for "Konsen → Gonnson → Gannsen → Johnson" surname mutation. incoming · pivot
