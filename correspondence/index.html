Schräder Correspondence

The collaboration trail with the German-side researcher.

The German-side primary research is led by Peter Schräder, an independent genealogist based in Trendelburg, Hesse. Their joint reasoning across the Konze-Kontzen surname pool, the parish complex of Churhessen, and the “Freundburg, Kurhessen” phonetic decoding is documented in a long-running correspondence dating from late 2025. The full thread lives on the home-page Schräder Correspondence tab; this page indexes the standalone milestones.

26 February 2026

The “Freundburg, Kurhessen” phonetic decoding

Peter’s original observation that the otherwise-meaningless place-name on Charles’s 1862 enlistment register is best decoded as Trendelburg — the German Tr- cluster heard as English Fr-, the syllable -endel- normalised to the familiar German Freund-. This is the geographic anchor of the working hypothesis.

7 April 2026

Andreas Konze identity disentanglement

Peter’s analysis of the “multiple Andreas Konze” problem in the Mainz / Trendelburg surname pool. Read the full reply.

For background on Peter’s role in the project and the terms of the collaboration, see About this project. For the latest open question Peter is investigating, see Open Questions.
