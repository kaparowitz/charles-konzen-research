## English summary (for your own reference — do not send)

The email asks HStAM Marburg's research service (Auftragsrecherche) to look in **Bestand 4h Stammrollen, conscription class of 1855, Kreis Hofgeismar** for a male born on or around **26 January 1835** in any of the Hofgeismar-area parishes (Trendelburg, Deisel, Friedrichsfeld, Helmarshausen, Hümme, Hofgeismar Altstadt/Neustadt, Sielen, Schöneberg, Heisebeck) with a surname from the Konze/Kontze/Kontzen/Contze/Contzen/Konzen/Konsen pool — or, alternatively, **any 1855-cohort entry annotated "nicht erschienen" (failed to appear), "desertiert", or "heimlich ausgewandert"** that fits the birth-date and place profile, since the absence of an Auswanderer-Beleg points strongly toward an illegal departure to evade the draft. Includes the 1862 US enlistment physical description (5'5", fair, blue, blond, farmer) as a cross-reference, and asks for a scan of the relevant page if a hit is found or a written confirmation of a null-result if not.

## Practical notes

- **Cost:** HStAM publishes its current fee schedule on the landesarchiv.hessen.de site under *Gebühren*. A focused single-year Stammrolle lookup is normally in the €30–€80 range; expect a *Kostenvoranschlag* (cost estimate) back before they execute the search.
- **Turnaround:** typically 4–10 weeks, longer in summer.
- **What a positive result delivers:** Charles's full birth name (resolves the "L." middle-initial), exact birth date (resolves the 25 vs 26 January ambiguity), parish of registration (localises the home village), parents' names, occupation at 20, height/eyes/hair (cross-checks the 1862 US descriptive book), and — most valuably — the conscription disposition. A *desertiert* / *nicht erschienen* annotation would directly explain the project's persistent failure to find any Auswanderer-Beleg.
- **What a documented negative result delivers:** equally useful — it eliminates the Kreis Hofgeismar Stammrolle as a remaining live lead and lets the project either widen the parish search radius or pivot to the next archival channel.
