# Session 44 — April 22, 2026
## Continued Scan of Deisel Beerdigungen 1876–1956 (1904–1907)

---

## Headline

Session 44 continued the systematic page-by-page scan of the post-1876 Deisel burial register (Register 218854) from Image 108 through Image 118, covering entries 659–710 and years 1904–1907. **Key results**: (1) **6 direct Konze burial entries found** — including 3 major adult patriarch/wife deaths and genealogically rich detail about parentage; (2) **2 new Konze patriarchs identified**: Ferdinand Konze (Ackermann, wife Wilhelmine née Weisenbach) and Gottlieb Konze (Gastwirth from Trendelburg); (3) **Entry 675 connects to Johann Christoph Konze** — Heinrich Ludwig Konze (d. 1905) was identified as son of Ackermann Ferdinand Konze; (4) **Entry 683 reveals illegitimate birth**: Friedrich Wilhelm Konze was "durch nachfolgende Ehe legitimiert" — born out of wedlock to Ludwig Konze and Amelie née Meinbreße; (5) **Entry 708 completes the Ferdinand Konze family**: his widow Wilhelmine née Weisenbach died in 1907; (6) **Format change observed**: starting ~1905, entries include village name "Deisel" and "Land" designations with more detailed parentage information.

## Notable Observations

### 1. Entry 683 — Illegitimate Birth of Friedrich Wilhelm Konze
The notation "durch nachfolgende Ehe legitimierter Sohn" is socially significant. Friedrich Wilhelm Konze was born before his parents (Ludwig Konze and Amelie Meinbreße) were married. He was later legitimized when they married. This provides rare social history detail — premarital births occurred even in small farming communities.

### 2. Ludwig Konze's Two Marriages (or Two Ludwigs)
Entry 683 names Friedrich Wilhelm Konze's mother as **Amelie née Meinbreße**, married to Ludwig Konze. But Entry 562 (Session 43) identified Ludwig Konze's widow as **Maria née Küffmann**. This means either: (a) Ludwig Konze married twice — first Amelie Meinbreße, then Maria Küffmann — or (b) there were two distinct Ludwig Konzes. The house numbers differ slightly (136 for Maria Küffmann, 142 for Friedrich Wilhelm), which could support either interpretation.

### 3. Format Change Starting ~1905
Beginning around Image 111 (1905), entries become more detailed, consistently listing both parents of the deceased (including mothers' maiden names), whether parents are "verstorben" (deceased), and the village designation "Deisel" or "Land" before house numbers. This new format provides significantly more genealogical data per entry.

### 4. Johann Christoph Konze Connection
Entry 676 identifies Heinrich Konze (d. 1905) as the son of **Johann Christoph Konze "Ste"** — linking back to the Johann Christoph Konze burial found in the 1830–1876 register during Session 36 (Image 38). This creates a confirmed two-generation Konze lineage: Johann Christoph Konze → Heinrich Konze.

### 5. Gottlieb Konze from Trendelburg
Entry 669 is the first Konze entry originating from OUTSIDE Deisel. Gottlieb Konze came from Trendelburg (~5 km south of Deisel) and worked as a Gastwirth (innkeeper). This raises the question of whether the Deisel and Trendelburg Konze families were related.
