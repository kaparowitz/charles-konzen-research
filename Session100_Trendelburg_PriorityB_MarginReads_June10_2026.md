## Daughters' marriages — why the index can't resolve them (10 Jun 2026)

The three daughters of grenadier Joh. Christoph Contze × Anna Catharina (Pfeil) who married
out — **Maria Wilhelmina (b. 1801), Maria Carolina Joannette (b. 1809), Anna Margaretha
(b. 1811)** — would marry ~1820s–1830s, i.e. in **220099** (Trauungen 1819–1831) and
**220120 = Trauungen 1830–1892** (imgs 204–393).

- **220120 has an alphabetical Trau-Register at imgs ~376–388**, but it is **alphabetised
  by the GROOM's surname (Männlich) only**; the bride (Weiblich) is listed on the groom's
  row, *not* under her own maiden name. Verified on img 376 (H | J/"C zugl. K" opening):
  e.g. groom "Jordan, Carl Ludwig" → bride "Berg, Maria Christine"; the Weiblich column is
  not maiden-name-sorted.
- **Consequence:** a Contze *bride* cannot be found by surname in this index. Without the
  groom's name there is no index shortcut — the only route is a **year-by-year scan of the
  marriage entries** (220099 1819–1831 + 220120 1830–1892), reading each bride's "geb. ___".
  Not run this session.
- Same caveat as the burials: per-page recognised text is not free here, and the viewer's
  page-canvas is unstable in these end-of-book index runs.
