People who knew Charles

Witnesses, comrades, and neighbors documented in the Civil War pension file F41-523680269E
11 people · 7 personally acquainted civilians + 3 Battery B comrades + 1 wedding witness (Battery B)

Charles's documented social circle, 1862–1898

Every person who swore an affidavit, witnessed a signature, or stood as wedding sponsor for Charles Gannsen between his Civil War enlistment in 1862 and the Pension Bureau's 1898 family-roster reply is catalogued here. The list is taken from the 40-page Civil War Pension File and the St. Marcus Marriage Register entry of 7 January 1866. Each profile collects what we know about the person — their age, their St. Louis address, when they first met Charles, what they swore to, and whatever later civic record (city directory, census, FamilySearch tree) we have been able to pin down.

Battery B comrades

Three men of Battery B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery — Charles's company. All three independently confirmed by the U.S. National Park Service's Soldiers and Sailors System and the War Department's records check inside the pension file.

✓ ID Confirmed
Battery B · Sergeant · wedding witness

Adam Herweck ⚜

Senior groomsman at Charles's 1866 wedding. Same rank trajectory as Charles (Pvt → Sgt). Born 16 July 1831 Lampertheim, Hessen. Married Marguerite Wamsganz 21 July 1868 St. Louis; relocated to Edwardsville Illinois between 1873 and 1880; died 11 May 1897 there. Two earlier children of Adam & Margaretha (Emma 1870, Margaretha 1873) traced at St. Marcus — sponsors were Wamsgans-side relatives, NOT Charles Gannsen. Third child Charles C Herweck (b. 1874) was likely baptized in Edwardsville; the namesake-Charles-Gannsen hypothesis is now weakly disfavored.
FamilySearch K4DW-PXM · Wedding register: 7 Jan 1866

Battery B · 1st Sergeant

August Derr

Charles's direct NCO during the 1863 New Madrid station — the most senior soldier-witness available. Two affidavits in 1891 corroborating the rupture, hearing loss, and rheumatism. Resided 1857 South 14th Street, St. Louis.
Pension file: Pages 24, 26 · Age 56 in 1891

Battery B · Private

Christian Meyer

Battery B comrade who reconnected with Charles around 1888 and provided the May 1891 affidavit about cannonading and exposure at New Madrid in fall 1863. Resided 1803 Allen Avenue, St. Louis.

Baptism sponsors — Friedrich Wilhelm Gonnson (1866 firstborn)

Four named sponsors at the 1866 St. Marcus baptism of Charles & Auguste's firstborn child. The default Lutheran-Evangelical structure: both maternal grandparents (Georg and Henriette Kreichelt) plus a male and female parish-circle sponsor (Friedrich Pillmann and Wilhelmine Ebeling Leue, both now identified to FamilySearch tree persons). → See the baptism page.

Sponsor · Auguste's father · Battery B

Georg Andreas Kreigelt

Charles's father-in-law and Battery B comrade. Born 17 January 1819 Clausthal, Hannover; christened 14 February 1819. Hannoverian miner. Emigrated September 1853 (Bremen → New Orleans → St. Louis). Enlisted Missouri 1861 in the same Battery B as Charles. Walked Auguste down the aisle at the 7 January 1866 wedding. Civil War pension awarded 24 March 1883; died six months later 20 September 1883. Buried Old Picker's Cemetery, St. Louis. Sponsor at TWO Gannsen baptisms (1866 firstborn + 1872 Auguste).
FamilySearch LZXC-NH9 · 1819–1883 · Clausthal-born

Sponsor · Auguste's mother

Louise Friederike "Henriette" Weiss

Charles's mother-in-law. Born 18 February 1820 Clausthal; christened 5 March 1820 as Louise Friederike Henriette Weiss. Married Georg Kreigelt 8 April 1844 Clausthal. Mother of Auguste. Sponsor at TWO Gannsen baptisms (1866 firstborn + 1873 Louise — the granddaughter named directly for her). Outlived Georg by eleven years; died 15 August 1894 St. Louis City; buried Old Picker's Cemetery alongside Georg.

Sponsor · 1866 + 1870 · the recurring outsider

Frederick Wilhelm Pillmann

The only non-Kreichelt sponsor at the 1866 firstborn baptism. At the time he was unmarried and not yet kin to the Kreichelts; he would marry Fredericka Albrecht (Catharina's sister) on 5 April 1870 — making him formally kin to Carl Kreichelt, who married Catharina in 1871. Sponsor again at the 1870 Katherine Carolina baptism. The only person to sponsor TWO Gannsen baptisms. Born 1846 Germany; St. Louis butcher; d. 1917; buried Bellefontaine Cemetery. Carries the live "illegal Kassel emigration" lead per Peter Schräder's database.
FamilySearch L17N-8Q6 · sponsor 1866 + 1870
✓ ID Confirmed
Sponsor · Soulard German neighbor

Dorothea Wilhelmine Ebeling Leue

Fourth sponsor at the 1866 firstborn baptism. Identified May 2026 as Wilhelmine Ebeling Leue (1810-1872), wife of Heinrich Andraes Daniel Leue. Born Fallersleben, Germany; emigrated to St. Louis; lived NW corner of Ann Avenue + Elizabeth Street (south-side Soulard German immigrant district, walking distance from the Gannsens' 1518 South 7th Street residence). Died 19 December 1872, Webster Groves, St. Louis. The only non-Kreichelt-side sponsor at the firstborn baptism — a senior parish-circle neighbor, not a relative. Identification confirmed via exact death-date match + matched daughter-death-years cross-check.
FamilySearch M6G9-FJR · 1810–1872 · Fallersleben-born

Baptism sponsors — Josephine Maria (1868)

Three named sponsor names at the 1 June 1868 St. Marcus baptism — counting Jacob & Josephine as a couple, four people. Georg Kreichelt and Jacob Reck are fully identified; Jacob's first wife Josephine and Maria Agnes Götz remain individual-ID open. → See the baptism page.

All four resolve to a single tightly-intermarried Kreichelt ⟷ Albrecht ⟷ Pillmann ⟷ Mincke kin network on Auguste's side. Sponsor 1872 · Auguste's brother · Battery B

August Kreichelt

Auguste's eldest brother. Born 8 December 1844 Clausthal Hannover; christened 8 April 1845 as Carl Friedrich "August" Kreigelt. Battery B veteran of the 2nd Missouri Light Artillery — served alongside Charles. Husband of Maria Emma Mincke ("Amalia Münke") with four children. The 1872 niece Auguste Gonsen is named after both her mother and this uncle. Died young at 32 on 24 November 1877; buried Holy Ghost Cemetery, St. Louis. His death is the turning point in the contraction of the Kreichelt-Battery-B social network around Charles.

Sponsor 1873 · "Barbara" · age 13

"Barbara" / Bertha Barbara Kreichelt

Auguste's youngest sister, then age 13 — a junior co-sponsor with her mother Henriette. Recorded by the 1873 parish clerk simply as "Barbara Kreichelt"; canonical FamilySearch name is Bertha Barbara Kreichelt with documented Alternate Name Babette. Earlier transcriptions on this site mislabeled her as "sister-in-law" — corrected May 2026 to "blood sister." She would sponsor again in 1880 (recorded then as "Babette") at Bertha Johnson's baptism, the namesake niece. Born 28 November 1859 St. Louis (first Kreichelt born in America); died at age 32 in St. James, Phelps County, Missouri (1892); buried Holy Ghost Cemetery alongside her brother August.

Baptism sponsors — Erwin Carl Konsen (1875)

The 3 October 1875 St. Marcus baptism of Charles & Auguste's sixth child Erwin Carl Konsen (later "Charles Johnson Jr."). The only Gannsen baptism with no Kreichelt sponsor at all and the only one preserving the Hesse-style surname spelling Konsen. Sole named sponsor: Erwin Spraul (with Charles Sr. listed by the parish clerk as a co-sponsor — an unusual breach of standard Lutheran practice). The child is named directly after his godfather. → See the baptism page.

✓ ID Confirmed
Sole sponsor 1875 · namesake · Anheuser-Busch Superintendent

Erwin Spraul

Sole named sponsor at the 1875 Erwin Carl Konsen baptism — the direct namesake of Charles & Auguste's sixth child. Born ~1848 Baden, Germany (per the June 1889 obituary; FS tree currently records 1841). Married Anna Maria Grassner 12 November 1868 St. Louis (one son Frank b. 1869). Superintendent of the Anheuser-Busch Brewing Association at his death — the most senior trade title at one of St. Louis's largest German-immigrant employers. Lived at 1340 S 9th Street in Soulard / Frenchtown, walking distance from the brewery and ~2 miles from the Gannsen residences. Died 19 June 1889 St. Louis aged 41 of post-operative complications from a left-shoulder abscess (Dr. Mudd performed surgery). Predeceased Charles by 14 years. Per Jed (Dec 2025): Charles chose Spraul "because he was a successful fellow immigrant" — now sharpened by the obituary to "successful at the very top of the brewing trade." Erwin's biological line ended in 1894 with son Frank's death; godson Erwin Carl Konsen carries the name forward. Two-generation Anheuser-Busch employment bridge: Charles's grandson Frederick Charles Johnson (Charles Jr.'s son, b. 1905, FamilySearch KLBX-VR1) later worked as a Bottler at Anheuser-Busch.
FamilySearch KLP1-N62 · ~1848–1889 · Anheuser-Busch Supt.
Co-sponsor 1875 · "the father of the child"

Charles Gannsen (himself)

The 1875 St. Marcus baptism register lists "the father of the child" — i.e. Charles Gannsen himself — as a co-sponsor alongside Erwin Spraul. This is unusual: the father is not normally also the godfather of his own child. Two competing readings: (a) parish-clerk shorthand for "no second sponsor present," or (b) a deliberate choice by Charles to mark the child as the carrier of his Hessian surname identity — the only Gannsen baptism with the spelling Konsen. Either way, this is the only Gannsen baptism with no maternal-Kreichelt-side sponsor. The strongest direct surname link between Charles in St. Louis and the Konze line in Hesse. → See the Charles in America tab on the main site for the full life-of-Charles overview.
FamilySearch LZXC-6GB · 1835–1903 · subject of the entire investigation

Baptism sponsors — Bertha (1880)

The 4 September 1881 St. Marcus baptism of Charles & Auguste's seventh and youngest child Bertha Jansen (b. 25 December 1880). The only Gannsen baptism with a single named sponsor. The child is named directly after her aunt — the third Gannsen baptism with a named-after-sponsor namesake (after Katherine Carolina ← Catharina Albrecht in 1870, and Erwin Carl Konsen ← Erwin Spraul in 1875). → See the baptism page.

✓ ID Confirmed
Sole sponsor 1880 · "Babette" · namesake aunt

"Babette" / Bertha Barbara Kreichelt

Auguste's youngest sister, then age 20. Recorded by the 1880 parish clerk as "Babette Kreichelt" — the German diminutive form of Barbara, also documented as her family-circle Alternate Name on FamilySearch. Same person as the "Barbara Kreichelt" who appeared at the 1873 Louise baptism (then age 13, junior co-sponsor with her mother). The 1880 child Bertha Johnson is her direct namesake — Bertha Barbara → Bertha. Died at age 32 in St. James, Phelps County, Missouri (1892); buried Holy Ghost Cemetery alongside her brother August. The simplification from three or four sponsors (1860s and early 1870s baptisms) down to one sponsor in 1880 reflects the contraction of the Kreichelt-Battery-B network: brother August had died in 1877 (age 32), father Georg's health was declining (he would die three years later), and the broader family social orbit was shifting away from St. Marcus.
FamilySearch L5FH-BRP · 1859–1892 · sponsor 1873 + 1880
