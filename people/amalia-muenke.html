Researcher view (sources, citations, bottom-line)

Maria Emma Mincke (1844, St. Louis-born), recorded in the 1870 St. Marcus baptism register as "Amalia Münke", was the wife of August Kreichelt (Auguste's eldest brother and Battery B veteran) — making her sister-in-law to both Auguste and Charles. She sponsored Katherine Carolina's 1870 baptism and was the only sponsor at that baptism who was already Auguste's sister-in-law at the time. The infant's middle name Amalia is her direct namesake. The "Maria Emma Mincke" → "Amalia Münke" name reconciliation was confirmed by Jed in December 2025.

Why this matters for understanding Charles: Amalia is a St. Louis-born sponsor whose Mincke line goes back to an early German-immigrant generation in the city. Her 1870 sponsorship alongside her not-yet-married brother-in-law Carl Kreichelt and fiancée Catharina Albrecht stitches the Mincke / Kreichelt / Albrecht / Pillmann families into the same single-baptism sponsor pool — among the most densely in-law-networked sponsorship rosters across the seven baptisms.

Maria Emma Mincke ("Amalia Münke" as Pastor Braschler recorded her in the 1870 baptism register) is the sister-in-law of Auguste Kreichelt and therefore of Charles Konzen. She married August Kreichelt — Auguste's eldest brother and a Battery B veteran — and stood as sponsor at the 1870 baptism of her niece Katherine Carolina Gansen. The Mincke family is documented in St. Louis records from the 1850s onward. Cross-reference Auguste's father's Kreichelt clan family tree for the full sibling network.

Quick facts & name reconciliation

★ Name reconciliation · "Amalia Münke" ≈ Maria Emma Mincke

The 1870 St. Marcus baptism register for Katherine Carolina names this sponsor as "Amalia Münke". The FamilySearch tree person who matches her — wife of August Kreichelt (Auguste's brother) — is recorded under the canonical name "Maria Emma Mincke" (L52N-1PL). The two names refer to the same person. Both surface-level differences are explainable:

  • Amalia ≈ Emma. "Amalia" and "Emma" are common interchangeable forms in 19th-c. German naming (Emma derives from Amal- roots in Old High German). The 1870 St. Marcus parish clerk likely heard "Emma" and wrote the more formal "Amalia" — or vice versa.
  • Münke ≈ Mincke. The umlauted vowel "ü" in Münke is approximated as "in" (or "i") in unaccented English-language American records — Mincke. Both spellings appear in St. Louis records.

Confirmed by Jed × Peter Schräder, 18 December 2025: the "spelling-variant ambiguity" was resolved against the FamilySearch tree person Maria Emma Mincke L52N-1PL, who marries the Battery B veteran August Kreichelt and bears him four children, three of whose 1870s baptism dates are consistent with the St. Marcus social network and the 1872 baptism of Charles & Auguste's daughter Auguste (where August Kreichelt himself sponsored).

Connection to Charles Gannsen & Auguste Kreichelt

★ Sponsor at the 22 May 1870 baptism of Katherine Carolina

Maria Emma Mincke is the fourth named sponsor at the 22 May 1870 baptism of Charles & Auguste's third child at St. Marcus German Evangelical Church (entry no. 6644, register page 285, recorded as "Amalia Münke"). The other three sponsors are Friedrich Pillmann, Catharina Albrecht, and Carl Kreichelt.

By 1870 Maria Emma had been married to August Kreichelt for several years (their daughter Louise Henriette was born in 1869) and was therefore already sister-in-law to Auguste Kreichelt — the only one of the four sponsors who held that formal in-law status at the time of the baptism. The middle name Amalia in Katherine Carolina's full baptismal name Friedrike Catharina Caroline Amalia Gansen is her direct namesake.

The Mincke ⟷ Kreichelt marriage — first of the three sponsor-marriages

Maria Emma's marriage to August Kreichelt (Auguste's older brother) is the first of the three Albrecht/Pillmann/Mincke ⟷ Kreichelt marriages that resolve the 1870 baptism's seemingly-strange sponsor pool into a single kin network. The marriage date is not yet pinned to a primary source, but the 1869 birth of their first child Louise Henriette establishes that it occurred no later than ~1868. In practical terms:

  • Maria Emma had been a Kreichelt by marriage for at least two years when she stood as sponsor in May 1870.
  • Friedrich Pillmann had married Fredericka Albrecht just seven weeks earlier (5 April 1870).
  • Carl Kreichelt would marry Catharina Albrecht ten months later (5 March 1871).

Tragedy of August Kreichelt's early death · 1877

August Kreichelt — Battery B veteran, Auguste's brother, the man who walked his sister down the aisle at her 1866 wedding to Charles Gannsen — died young in 1877 at about 33. Maria Emma was widowed at age 33 with three surviving young children (her second child Heinrich Wilhelm "August" had already died in infancy in 1872). She remarried George Henry Zuver after August's death, but raised the four Kreichelt children. None of the August-Kreichelt-and-Maria-Emma children are documented as appearing at any later Gannsen baptism — consistent with the family's mid-1870s relocation away from the immediate St. Marcus social orbit after August's death.

Maria Emma herself died in 1905 in St. Louis, two years after Charles Gannsen's December 1903 death.

📂 Full research log — Deep-rooted Mincke family in St. Louis, broader-investigation significance

The deep-rooted Mincke family in St. Louis

The Minckes are one of the older German-American families in St. Louis. Maria Emma's parents Heinrich Mincke and Margaretha Ludwig married in St. Louis on 14 November 1839 — when the city's German-immigrant community was still small. By the time of Charles Gannsen's 1859 arrival, the Minckes were already a settled, well-connected, second-generation American household. Maria Emma herself was born in St. Louis in 1844, fifteen years before Charles's arrival.

This family-depth contrasts with the Kreichelts and Pillmanns (both first-generation 1850s immigrants) and with Charles Gannsen himself (a singleton 1859 immigrant). The Mincke family's century-deep St. Louis roots are part of why August Kreichelt's marriage into them was a strategic ascent for the recently-immigrated Kreichelts — and why Maria Emma's status as the sponsor with the longest American tenure mattered at the 1870 baptism font.

Significance to the broader investigation

  1. The "Münke / Mincke" surname puzzle is settled. Until December 2025 the 1870 baptism's "Amalia Münke" was treated as a candidate Charles-side German-origin lead. The reconciliation against FamilySearch L52N-1PL closes that lead: she is American-born St. Louis, married to Auguste's own brother, with no Hessen connection.
  2. Naming pattern echo. Katherine Carolina's middle name Amalia mirrors Maria Emma's recorded sponsor name "Amalia Münke" — confirming the standard Lutheran sponsor-namesake practice and providing additional cross-evidence that the "Amalia ≈ Maria Emma" identification is correct.
  3. August Kreichelt's death as a turning point. The mid-1870s death of August removed the eldest Kreichelt brother from the St. Louis Battery-B-veteran social orbit and may explain why the Gannsen baptism sponsor pool drifted further toward the Kreichelt women in the 1875 (Erwin Carl Konsen) and 1880 (Bertha) baptisms. Maria Emma's family was effectively decoupled from the active Gannsen social network after 1877.
