Henry Stoll

Pension witness · 24-year acquaintance · Soulard district
Limited record Pension witness · 29 Sept 1890 Knew Charles since 1867

★ At a Glance

✓ ID Confirmed
BATTERY L COMRADE-IN-REGIMENT · PENSION WITNESS

A Swiss-born St. Louis pension witness who attested 24 years of acquaintance with Charles — and, as identified in May 2026, a same-regiment comrade in the 2nd Missouri Light Artillery.

Henry Stoll (FS LR6F-J67 · b. 18 March 1835 Schaffhausen, Switzerland · m. Wilhelmine Eich 19 January 1861 St. Louis) gave the 29 September 1890 General Affidavit attesting 24+ years' acquaintance with Charles since 1867 — covering ongoing rupture pain, rheumatism flare-ups laying Charles up "two months at a stretch," and partial deafness throughout the 1867-1890 window. ★★★ Major May 2026 finding: NPS Soldiers and Sailors Database confirms Stoll served as Private in Company L (Battery L), 2nd Regiment Missouri Light Artillery — the same regiment as Charles's Battery B. He was a same-regiment comrade, not just a civilian acquaintance.

Why this matters for understanding Charles: The new Battery-L finding promotes Stoll from "civilian acquaintance" to "same-regiment veteran witness" — joining August Derr (Co. B, 1st Sgt.) and Christian Meyer (Co. B, Pvt.) on the list of 2nd MO Light Artillery comrades attesting Charles's pension claim. Three of the pension file's affidavit-witnesses thus share the 2nd MO LA regimental affiliation (with Michael Nolan adding a 1st MO LA tie from the same Jefferson Barracks cluster), strengthening the documentary network around Charles's St. Louis post-war social circle. Stoll's Schaffhausen-Swiss origin (recorded as "Baden / Germany" in U.S. censuses due to the Swiss-German border) places him in the same southern-German linguistic band as several Battery B / St. Marcus circle Germans, distinct from Charles's Hessen-Hofgeismar origin.

What he swore to

General Affidavit · 29 September 18[90]

Attests 24+ years' acquaintance with Charles Gannson, since 1867. Specifically attests:

  • Ongoing right-side pain from the rupture/injury, persistent throughout acquaintance;
  • Rheumatism flare-ups laying him up for two months at a stretch;
  • Partial deafness evident throughout the period.

Source: Pension File F41-523680269E, Page 16 (verso jurat Page 17). Notary: Theod[ore] D. Frank. Filed by Fred. W. Fout, U.S. Claim Agent.

Why he matters

Stoll's affidavit covers a 24-year window of personal observation that bridges the gap between Charles's Civil War service (1862–65) and the 1890 pension claim. He is one of three post-war-acquaintance witnesses (alongside Michael Nolan and Henry Niemeier) whose testimony combined establishes a continuous documented disability narrative from 1865 through 1890 — necessary for the pension under the 1890 Dependents' Pension Act.

Stoll's South 3rd Street address (Soulard / Frenchtown district, the original German immigrant neighborhood) suggests the acquaintance dates to Charles's early post-war years at 1518 South 7th Street and 718 Gates Street — also Soulard-district addresses.

★ Multi-database investigation (3 May 2026)

FamilySearch tree person — Henry Stoll (LR6F-J67)

Direct FS tree match identified: Henry Stoll (LR6F-J67):

  • Birth: 18 March 1835, Schaffhausen, Switzerland — Swiss, not German. Schaffhausen is the Swiss canton on the Swiss-German-Baden border, which explains why every U.S. census recorded his birthplace simply as "Baden" or "Germany" — Schaffhausen Swiss-German speech and culture were indistinguishable from neighboring Baden German for an American census-taker.
  • Death: 14 May 1905, St. Louis City, Missouri at age 70
  • Parents: Benedict Stolle × Elizabeth Sick/Serik
  • 13 sources documenting his life

Wife and children

Wife: Wilhelmine Eich (1844–1918, FS G61W-7LH). Married 19 January 1861, Saint Louis, Missouri — six years before meeting Charles Gannsen in 1867. ("Mathilde" / "Matilda" in the 1880 and 1900 censuses is a name variant for Wilhelmine — German "Wilhelmine" was often Anglicised to "Mathilda" in U.S. records.) Henry & Wilhelmine had at least six children:

  • Anna Marie Stoll (1861–1937, 9791-42T)
  • Frances Wilhelmina Stoll (1863–Deceased, GN23-LYH)
  • Jacob Peter Stoll (1865–1894, LVDM-3V6) — died at age 29
  • Charlotte Stoll (1869–1944, 9994-CWZ)
  • Henry Stoll Jr. (1872–1895, LVDM-35J) — died at age 23
  • (plus at least one more documented in the 1900 census: Emma, Fred, Thomas, William)

★★★ Civil War service identified — same regiment as Charles!

NPS Soldiers and Sailors Database confirms: Henry Stoll served as Private in Company L (Battery L), 2nd Regiment, Missouri Light Artillery — the same regiment as Charles Gannsen (who served in Co. B / Battery B). NARA M390 Roll 47. Additional note in his service record: "See also 1 U.S.R.C. Cav." — meaning Henry first served in the 1st U.S. Reserve Corps Cavalry (a Missouri short-term unit, similar to Jacob Reck's 2nd USRC Infantry) before transferring to the 2nd Missouri Light Artillery alongside Charles.

Charles and Henry Stoll did not serve in the same battery (Charles = Battery B, Henry = Battery L), but they served in the same regiment. This means they were under the same regimental colonel, attended the same regimental musters, and would have known each other's batteries even if not stationed in the same place. The 2nd Missouri Light Artillery had multiple batteries (A, B, C, D, ... L, M) deployed across different garrison stations during 1862-1865 — Charles at New Madrid, MO (with Battery B in fall 1863), Henry likely at a different post with Battery L.

This upgrades the Charles–Stoll bond from "civilian acquaintance" to "regimental comrade." The 1890 Census of Union Veterans (K8SY-Y2C) independently confirms Stoll's veteran status. Henry's affidavit timeline of "since 1867" makes sense in this new light: both men mustered out in 1865 and reconnected in St. Louis through the regimental veteran network in the post-war years, with the friendship deepening over the next 24+ years.

This is a major correction to the prior profile note "Not a Battery B veteran — civilian acquaintance only." Stoll was a fellow 2nd MO Light Artillery veteran, just in a different battery. The Charles–Stoll bond is now formally a regimental-comrade bond, alongside Adam Herweck (Battery B), August Derr (Battery B), and Christian Meyer (Battery B) — making Stoll the fourth identified 2nd MO Light Artillery veteran in Charles's documented social circle.

1880 + 1900 US Federal Censuses, St. Louis

  • 1870 Census: Henry Stoll, b. 1843 Baden, Saint Louis, MO (the "Baden" entry is the Swiss-German-border conflation noted above)
  • 1880 Census: Henry Stoll, b. 1843 Germany, wife Mathilde Stoll, children Friederich, Ida, Henry, Charles
  • 1900 Census: Henry Stoll, b. March 1842 Germany, marriage 1870 (a different marriage event recorded?), wife Matilda, children William, Emma, Fred, Thomas

The 1870-1880-1900 census birth-year drift (1843 → 1843 → 1842) is normal census imprecision; the FS tree's 18 March 1835 is anchored to documented sources.

Robert Buecher's St. Marcus indexes — Stoll-family network at the parish

Buecher's St. Marcus Marriage Index 1847–1865 returned 4 Stoll marriages, several of which document Henry's broader family network:

  • Jacob Stoll × Anna Christina Harbeck · both from St. Louis · 22 January 1860 · entry #621. Likely Henry's brother (Henry named his own first son Jacob Peter Stoll in 1865 — typical German naming-after-uncle convention). Jacob's St. Louis marriage 1860 is just one year before Henry's own 19 January 1861 marriage — the Stoll brothers were arriving and marrying in the same window.
  • Anna Maria Stoll × John Jones · both from St. Louis · 2 November 1862 · entry #751. Possibly a sister of Henry.
  • Catharine Stoll × Jacob Valet · both from Zeisenhausen / Gochtsheim, Amt Bretten, Baden · 19 August 1855 · entry #321. Earlier-emigrating Stoll branch from Baden.
  • Marie Stoll née Bücklein × Henry Schaaf · Marie originally from Canton Aargau, Switzerland · 6 July 1860 · entry #639. Marie was previously married to a Stoll (now widowed); her remarriage shows the Stoll-family Swiss connection — Aargau is geographically close to Schaffhausen, both Swiss-German cantons. Confirms the broader Stoll family had Swiss roots beyond just Henry.

Notably, Henry Stoll's own 19 January 1861 marriage to Wilhelmine Eich is NOT in Buecher's St. Marcus 1847-1865 index — so Henry & Wilhelmine were married at a different parish (or by civil registration). Most likely Holy Ghost German Evangelical Church or another north-side German Lutheran congregation.

Probable region of origin: Schaffhausen, Switzerland (with broader family in Baden + Aargau)

Henry himself: Schaffhausen, Switzerland. Broader Stoll family: Bretten, Baden (Catharine Stoll branch) and Canton Aargau, Switzerland (Marie Stoll née Bücklein). All in the southern German / Swiss-German linguistic band. Distinct from Charles Gannsen's Hessen-Hofgeismar origin (~600 km north). The Charles-Stoll connection was formed in St. Louis post-1865, possibly through the broader German-Swiss immigrant Union-veteran community.
