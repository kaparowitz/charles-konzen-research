Josephine née von Delien ✓

First wife of Jacob Reck (FS KLPB-52X) · co-sponsor at the 1 June 1868 St. Marcus baptism of Maria Josephine Gonnson · the child's middle name is her direct namesake
BAPTISM SPONSOR · 1868 ✓ SURNAME CONFIRMED · DELIEN / DELIAN ★ ORIGIN: Belgian-Flemish (Antwerp / Brabant) individual ID still open

★ At a Glance

? ID Open
SPONSOR PROFILE · INDIVIDUAL ID OPEN Latest research pass: 7 May 2026

First wife of Jacob Reck and a co-sponsor at the 1868 baptism of Charles and Auguste's second child.

Josephine is the first wife of Jacob Reck and the only one of the fifteen distinct named sponsors at the seven Gannsen baptisms whose individual identity remains open. The 1 June 1868 St. Marcus parish-clerk hand reads "Jakob Reck u. J. G. · Josephine geb. von Delien" — the surname reading is well-supported as Delien / Delian (the 2020 transcription, a 2 May 2026 high-res re-read of the same entry, and a Concordia Cemetery cross-reference) — though the individual's identity remains open, and the 7 May 2026 deep search produced the project's first solid geographic-origin lock: Delien is unequivocally a Belgian-Flemish surname (Forebears.io: 503 worldwide bearers, ~84 % Flemish Belgium; FS: 553 tree people in Noord-Brabant). The German von is the parish-clerk's transliteration of Dutch van. Josephine was therefore most likely a Catholic Brabant-Antwerp immigrant who reached St. Louis via Antwerp port, met Jacob there 1865–67, sponsored Maria Josephine on 1 June 1868, and died sometime before 27 July 1869 (when Jacob remarried) — most likely in childbirth.

Why this matters for understanding Charles: Of the fifteen distinct named sponsors, Josephine is the last open ID. The new Belgian-Flemish lock reframes the Reck-Gannsen relationship as a St. Louis-formed bond crossing the Belgian-Catholic / German-Lutheran immigrant lines — not an old-country tie. Identifying her closes the project's last sponsor question.

The 1868 baptism — the one direct documentary trace

St. Marcus Baptism Doc G — page 224, entry 5867 / 173

Child: Maria Josephine Gonnson · b. 20 February 1868 · baptized 1 June 1868
Parents: Carl Gonnson and Auguste Kreichelt
Sponsors: "Jakob Reck and his wife, Josephine, née von Delian" · "Maria Agnes Götz" · "Georg Kreichelt"

Verbatim parish-clerk hand:

"Jakob Reck u. J. G. [und Gemahlin] · Josephine geb. von Delien"

FHL Microfilm 1503034 / FS DGS 7769183, image 372. The infant Maria Josephine is named directly after Josephine — Charles & Auguste's second-born carries Josephine's first name as her middle name, signalling a closer-than-acquaintance bond. This single entry is currently the only documentary trace of Josephine's existence on the American side.

Open questions & next research steps

Open questions

  • Birth year, place, and parents — no FS tree person, civil birth record, or parish christening entry has been linked to her. The 7 May 2026 search showed multiple Brabant Delien families with daughters in 1830–1845, but none named Josephine; she likely belongs to an as-yet-unindexed Antwerp side branch.
  • Precise death date and burial location — Concordia is the leading candidate but no specific entry surfaced.
  • Where she met Jacob Reck — Josephine was not on Jacob's Louis Henry voyage (zero Delien passengers); she likely sailed separately from Antwerp.
  • Whether she had St. Louis kin in 1868 — the 1882 Delien Emma at Concordia is the strongest hint, but the connection to Josephine is not yet documented.

Productive next pulls

  1. ★★ Antwerp / Mechelen Catholic baptism registers 1830–1845 — Belgian state archives (Rijksarchief Antwerpen). Search Josephine / Josephina / Joanna Josepha Delien in Balen, Nijlen, Schoten, Essen, Goirle.
  2. ★ Antwerp port emigration records 1860–1868 — Belgian state archives. Closes the "how she reached America" question.
  3. ★ Phelps County MO marriage book — Otta A. Delien × William King, 8 April 1866 — original entry may name Otta's father as consenting party, anchoring an extended Delien family.
  4. 1860 Phelps County census — browse for Delien household; Otta would be ~14–18 in her parents' household.
  5. Concordia Cemetery pre-1870 register — request from cemetery office (4205 Bates Street, St. Louis) for 1868–69 burials under "Reck, Josephine" or "Delien, Josephine."
  6. St. Marcus burial register 1868–69 — re-check original microfilm for any missed Reck/Delien entry.
  7. Concordia 1882 Emma Delien parents — pull the full burial record for the 4-day-old Emma to identify her parents, possibly Josephine's siblings.
📂 Full research log — surname-confirmation passes, deep Ancestry/FS search, Belgian-Dutch finding, Detjen alternative

★ Surname confirmed "Delien / Delian" — three sources converge  ✓ CONFIRMED · 6 MAY 2026

Three independent sources converge on the same surname:

  1. 2020 AncestryProGenealogists Doc G transcription (Paul A. Schmidt & Brian A. Podoll, working from microfilm) — recorded the surname as "von Delian" with the bracketed [?] marking transcription tentativeness, not surname uncertainty.
  2. 2 May 2026 high-resolution image re-read from FHL film 7769183, image 372, page 224, entry 5867/173 — confirms the trailing letters as -ian / -ien, not -ius. Verbatim transcription: "Jakob Reck u. J. G. [und Gemahlin] · Josephine geb. von Delien"
  3. StLGS database search · 6 May 2026 — surfaced two confirmed Delien-surname individuals in St. Louis records (next section), validating the surname's existence in the same German Lutheran community.

The conclusion: the surname is Delien / Delian (the ending oscillates between -en and -an across different scribes; the morphology is the same). This rules out:

  • von Delius — the "-ius" Latin ending in the Westphalian Bielefeld merchant family doesn't match either reading
  • von Dahlen — the parish hand reads "Delien," not "Dahlen"; StLGS returns 0 results for Dahlen

The "von" particle is also confirmed as part of the recorded name. The 7 May 2026 deep search subsequently established that the German von here is the parish-clerk's transliteration of the Dutch surname-prefix van — see the Belgian-Flemish origin section further down this log.

★ StLGS confirms Delien surname in St. Louis German Lutheran community  ★ NEW · 6 MAY 2026

A St. Louis Genealogical Society (StLGS) database search for surname "Delien" (begins-with) returned two confirmed Delien-surnamed individuals in St. Louis records:

  1. Delien, Emma  ·  died 26 December 1882  ·  age 4 days  ·  Concordia Cemetery (C22), 4205 Bates Street, St. Louis  ·  Evangelical Lutheran Cemetery, established 1845. The cemetery is the Soulard / Lafayette-area Lutheran burial ground used by the same German-Lutheran parish-circle community as the Gannsens. This is a baby Delien in a German Evangelical Lutheran family in St. Louis 14 years after our Josephine's June 1868 sponsorship — strongly suggesting an extended Delien family was rooted in the St. Louis Lutheran community across multiple decades.
  2. DeLien, La[…]  ·  b. 11 Nov 1906, d. 9 Mar 1992, buried 12 Mar 1992  ·  St. Marcus (New) Cemetery (the modern German Lutheran cemetery succeeding Old St. Marcus)  ·  cremated, mortuary Kutis  ·  age 85 yrs. Modern descendant — confirming the family's ~110+ year continuity in the St. Louis Lutheran community.

What this validates: the AncestryPro 2020 + 2 May 2026 high-res reread surname reading is now externally corroborated by a third independent source — a St. Louis cemetery index that captures the same surname in the same German Lutheran community 14 years later. Searches for "Dahlen" and "Delius" in StLGS returned zero results.

★ Deep Ancestry + FamilySearch search  ★ 7 MAY 2026

A second pass over both Ancestry (logged in) and FamilySearch — combining tree, records, and full-text indexes — turned up one promising new lead and several productive negatives that further constrain the open identification.

→ The new lead — Otta A. Delien × William King, Phelps County, Missouri, 8 Apr 1866

Ancestry's Missouri, U.S., Compiled Marriages, 1851–1900 (Dodd / Liahona Research) returns a single Delien-surnamed Missouri marriage in our era: Otta A. Delien (Female), married William King on 8 April 1866 at Phelps County, Missouri. This is the only 1860s-era Delien woman documented in Missouri besides Josephine. Otta is plausibly Josephine's sister, cousin, or niece — both adult Delien-surnamed women in adjacent Missouri years.

Note: the 1870 Phelps County Wm R King household had wife Martha (not Otta), so the actual 1866 William King may have died, moved, or been a different William King in the county. The original Phelps County marriage book entry (not the compiled index) would be the next pull.

→ Productive negatives — what the deep search ruled out

  • Josephine was not on Jacob's ship. The Louis Henry (Bremen → NY, 20 Nov 1865) passenger list returns 0 Delien-surnamed passengers.
  • No St. Louis civil marriage of Reck × Delien before 1869. Ancestry's St. Louis Marriage Index 1804–1876 returns only Jacob × Emma Weingardt 27 Jul 1869.
  • No Delien household in 1860 St. Louis or 1860 Missouri census. Metaphone-broadened search returned Belgian "Delen" (1919 arrival) and Dulin/Dillen/Dallen sound-alikes — no exact-spelling 1860s match.
  • FS Full-Text "Delien" St. Louis 1850–1900 returned 118 hits — all OCR misreads of "lien"/"delinquent"/"defendant" in legal documents.
  • Emma Delien (1882) parents not listed in Find a Grave Memorial #203909000 or in FamilySearch Find a Grave Index.
  • FS exact-match "Delien" globally returns only three records — Emma Delien 1882 St. Louis, Elise Delien 1905 NY (b. Sweden, unrelated), and Herman "Delien" Wisconsin (FS tree contributors merged this one to Herman Paul Detjen LB26-D66, recognizing it as a transcription error for Detjen).
  • FS catalog Phelps County, Missouri has no 1866-era marriage book images — vital records start in 1883. The 1866 Otta Delien × William King marriage is only accessible offline at the Phelps County Recorder of Deeds office.

★★ MAJOR: Delien is a Belgian-Flemish surname  ★ MAJOR · 7 MAY 2026

The 7 May 2026 deep search produced one finding that fundamentally reframes Josephine's geographic origin hypothesis. Searching FamilySearch for surname=Delien exact-match in the broader Belgium / Netherlands region returns:

  • 553 FamilySearch Tree people with surname Delien (exact) in Noord-Brabant — a multi-generation lineage from the 1600s through 1900s
  • 112+ FamilySearch Vital Records for Delien individuals — concentrated in Tilburg, Goirle, Rosmalen, and Bergen op Zoom (Noord-Brabant, Netherlands) and Essen, Nijlen, and Antwerp city (Antwerp province, Belgium)

Documented Delien families in the 1830s–40s birth window:

  • Antonetta Delien (1837–1899, FS LKFK-G4P) — born Tilburg, parents Hendrik Delien × Antonia Maria Brouwers
  • Hendrika Delien (1840–1902, FS 9NHP-BBN) · Johanna Wilhelmina Delien (1838–1919, FS LKKC-3ZN) · Adriana Delien (1834–1906, FS LKKC-SBM) — all daughters of Johannes Hubertus Delien × Johanna Denissen, Tilburg
  • Wouter Norbertus Delien (1834–1892, FS GDXB-TTH) — born Goirle. Siblings (per FS): Franciscus 1834, Antonetta 1837 — no Josephine in this branch
  • Josephus Delien (1831–1876, FS LR27-NH1) — born Nijlen, Antwerp, Belgium

The deepest documented Delien is Maria Cornelius Joossen Delien (1685–1723, FS GXXN-ZXC), born Essen, Antwerp, Belgium — proving the family's roots in this border region back to at least the 17th century.

→ Forebears.io confirms: 503 worldwide bearers, mostly Belgium

Forebears.io records: surname Delien is held by approximately 503 people worldwide, ranking 613,453rd globally. Most prevalent in Belgium. Per Eupedia surname analysis, 84% of Belgian Delien bearers are in the Flemish (Dutch-speaking) Region. Within Antwerp province, FS clusters the surname in Kempen-region villages (Balen, Nijlen, Schoten, Essen).

→ What this changes about Josephine's origin

The German "von" in the parish hand was almost certainly the clerk's rendering of the Dutch "van" particle. "Josephine geb. von Delien" ≈ "Josephine née van Delien" in a German Lutheran register. The German von is not a noble particle — it's the clerk's habitual translation of the surname-prefix his Dutch-speaking informant used.

Implications: Josephine was almost certainly born Roman Catholic in Noord-Brabant or Antwerp province, converted on emigration / marriage, sailed via Antwerp port (not Bremen), and met Jacob in St. Louis. This is the project's first solid geographic-origin lock for Josephine — and it dovetails with the failure of all German-side Delien searches to find anything: the surname is not German because Josephine was not German.

★ The "Detjen" alternative reading — a distant secondary hypothesis  ★ NEW · 7 MAY 2026

The Wisconsin Herman "Delien" → Detjen tree-merge prompts a parallel question: could the 1868 St. Marcus parish-clerk's "von Delien" reading itself be a transcription artifact for "Detjen" (a Frisian / Low German surname)? In old German Sütterlin handwriting the letter combinations tj and li can look similar.

Counter-evidence: a FS exact-surname=Detjen search restricted to St. Louis 1860–1880 returns 647 records — substantial Detjen population. So the alternative is not implausible by frequency. However, the convergence on Delien is strong:

  • 2020 AncestryProGenealogists Doc G — "von Delian"
  • 2 May 2026 high-resolution image re-read — "von Delien"
  • StLGS Concordia Cemetery 1882 Emma Delien burial — independent corroboration on a separate document
  • Forebears.io Belgian-Flemish distribution — Delien is a real, identifiable surname (just not German)

Four converging sources make the Delien reading robust. The "von Detjen" alternative remains a distant secondary hypothesis worth checking only if the Belgian-Flemish trail dead-ends.
