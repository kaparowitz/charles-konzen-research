Auguste Kreichelt (1847–1896)

★ Charles Gannsen's wife · the maternal anchor of all seven Gannsen children · Clausthal-born → St. Louis 1853 → m. Charles 1866
CHARLES GANNSEN'S WIFE b. 12 May 1847 Clausthal, Hannover d. 1896 St. Louis 7 children · 4 surviving

★ At a Glance

✓ ID Confirmed
★ CHARLES'S WIFE · MATERNAL ANCHOR OF ALL SEVEN GANNSEN CHILDREN

Auguste was Charles's wife and the mother of all seven of his children. She was born 12 May 1847 in Clausthal — a mining town in what is now Lower Saxony — and came to the United States with her parents in 1853 when she was about six. She and Charles married at St. Marcus German Evangelical Church in St. Louis on 7 January 1866 and remained married for thirty years; she died in 1896, eight years before Charles. The 1896 St. Marcus death register records her surname as Conson — one of the nine American spellings of the family name that anchor the surname-trail evidence on this site.

Researcher view (sources, citations, bottom-line)

Auguste Kreichelt (FS LZKV-9F5 · b. 12 May 1847 Clausthal, Kingdom of Hannover · imm. 1853 age ~6 with parents Georg Kreichelt × Louise Henriette Weiss · d. 1896 St. Louis age 49 · "Auguste Conson" on death record) is Charles Konzen's wife — married 7 January 1866 at St. Marcus by Pastor Georg Wendelin Wall (entry No. 676) — and the maternal anchor of every documented Gannsen relationship in St. Louis. The 1896 St. Marcus death register records her surname as "Conson", a C-initial form that ties directly back to the Trendelburg parish's documented Contzen variant — the third independent attestation of the family's Hessian K/C-initial origin (after Erwin Carl's 1875 baptism reading "Konsen" with s and daughter Auguste's 1890 death record reading "geb Konzen" with z — the single American attestation of the full Hessian z-form).

Why this matters for understanding Charles: Three reasons her family pulls so much weight in the broader investigation. (1) The Kreichelt-side network supplied the sponsor pool. Across the seven Gannsen baptisms (1866-1880), virtually every named sponsor is a Kreichelt parent, sibling, or in-law — Auguste's brother August Kreichelt, sister Bertha Barbara, brother-in-law Carl, mother Louise/Henriette, father Georg. (2) She is one of the most consistent documentary co-witnesses to Charles's surname spelling across thirty years — most St. Louis renderings of the family name (Gonnson, Gansen, Gonsen, Conson) appear in records where Auguste is also named, though Charles also appears without her in the 1862 enlistment (Gannsen), the 1880 census (Gonson), and on daughter Auguste's 1890 Konzen attestation. (3) Her Hannover origin contrasts with Charles's Hessen origin and explains the bilingual / cross-regional German social composition of their St. Louis circle.

Marriage record (1866)  ★ KEY EVIDENCE

St. Marcus Marriage Register · 7 January 1866 · "Carl Gonnson und Auguste Kreichelt"

St. Marcus Evangelical Church marriage register, A.D. 1866 — page 134-135, entry No. 676 (running entry №4): the marriage of Carl Gonnson & Auguste Kreichelt, recorded 7 January 1866 by Pastor Georg Wendelin Wall (Württemberger; St. Marcus 1850–1866). Charles's stated origin: "aus Deisse, Kreis Cassel, Churhessen" — i.e. the village of Deisel in the Trendelburg parish complex, inside the Electorate of Hesse-Kassel. This is the entry that ties the May-2026 Konzen surname hypothesis (unconfirmed) to a specific Hessian territorial reference.

Death record (1896)  ★ KEY EVIDENCE

St. Marcus Death Register · 1896 · pages 120-121 · "Auguste Conson geb Kreichelt"

St. Marcus death register from 1896 — Auguste Kreichelt's death entry, recorded by Pastor Eilt Heeren Eilts (St. Marcus from Nov 1894). The pastor wrote the family surname as Conson — a C-initial form that ties directly back to the Trendelburg parish's documented Contzen variant ("siehe auch K. Contze, Cortze, Cordes, Contz"). Charles himself was the surviving informant at his wife's funeral — making this the single most evidentially valuable Charles-self-attestation of the surname in any U.S. record. He gave Pastor Eilts the family surname directly with his own mouth, age 61, three decades after his 1866 marriage was recorded as "Gonnson."

This single document is the project's strongest "Charles-self-attestation" of the family surname — and the corresponding entry in the parish burial register (still being located in the index) would close the burial-cemetery gap for Auguste; she is presumed buried at Old St. Marcus Cemetery on confessional grounds, alongside Charles (burial #3393, 19 Dec 1903) seven years later.

Why she matters for the Charles Gannsen investigation

Auguste is the maternal anchor of every documented Gannsen relationship in St. Louis. Three reasons her family pulls so much weight in the broader investigation:

  1. The Kreichelt-side network supplied the sponsor pool. Across the seven Gannsen baptisms (1866-1880), virtually every named sponsor traces to Auguste's Kreichelt-Albrecht-Pillmann-Mincke side — none to Charles's side. This is the structural marker that Charles immigrated alone in 1859 leaving German kin behind.
  2. Her father Georg was Charles's fellow Battery B veteran. Georg Kreichelt enlisted in the 2nd Missouri Light Artillery alongside Charles. The military bond preceded and reinforced the marriage tie, with Georg both walking Auguste down the aisle at the 1866 wedding and standing as sponsor at three subsequent Gannsen baptisms.
  3. The 1853 Hannoverian emigration was the family's anchor event. The Kreichelts arrived in St. Louis as a family unit in 1853. Auguste was 13. By the time Charles arrived in 1859 and re-emerged as a Civil War volunteer in 1862, the Kreichelts had been St. Louis residents for nearly a decade — they were the established faction Charles married into, not the other way around.

Auguste's 1896 death (age 49) was the first major rupture in the family. Charles outlived her by seven years, dying in 1903. Both are buried at Old Saint Marcus Cemetery per the parish registers — Charles's burial #3393, and Auguste's confirmed in the St. Marcus death register: died 19 December 1896, buried 22 December 1896.
