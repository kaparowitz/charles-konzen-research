Christian Meyer

Battery B private · Pension affidavit witness · St. Louis cigar district
Limited record BATTERY B · 2ND MO LIGHT ARTILLERY Pension witness 1891

★ At a Glance

✓ ID Confirmed
PENSION WITNESS · BATTERY B PRIVATE

A Battery B private who served alongside Charles.

Christian Meyer (b. ~1840 Germany — region not narrowed) was a Battery B Private in the 2nd Missouri Light Artillery alongside Charles. His 3 May 1891 General Affidavit independently corroborates the September 1863 New Madrid cannonading hearing-loss claim and the November 1863 rheumatism-from-exposure claim — both swearing-points already attested by 1st Sergeant August Derr. Meyer's account adds a second sworn voice from inside the battery, sworn six days after Derr's first affidavit.

Why this matters for understanding Charles: Two independent Battery B affidavits — Meyer + Derr — attest under oath that Charles's deafness and rheumatism originated during the same September–November 1863 New Madrid station. Without the second comrade-witness, the pension claim would rest on Derr alone; with both, the New-Madrid origin point rests on two independent sworn affidavits rather than one.

What he swore to

General Affidavit · 3 May 1891

That he remembers Charles Gannsen as a member of Battery B, 2nd Mo. Artillery Vols., that he recollects while at New Madrid about September 1863, from heavy cannonading he contracted the loss of hearing, and at the same time from exposure in November 1863 contracted the Rheumatism. He having then suffered with stiff limbs which plainly indicated Rheumatism, and that to the present day the claimant has suffered from loss of hearing and Rheumatism. That he met the claimant nearly three years ago at the residence of the latter, [his] Post Office address is 1803 Allen Avenue, St. Louis, Mo. and further deponent saith not. He has no interest in nor is concerned in its prosecution.

Source: Pension File F41-523680269E, Page 22 (verso jurat Page 23). Notary Theo. W. Boutje Jr.

Independent confirmation

The War Department's records check on Pension Page 35 independently confirms Christian Meyer was present in Co. B, 2nd Mo. L.A. during the September–November 1863 New Madrid station period.

Why he matters

Christian Meyer's affidavit is the second of the two Battery B comrade affidavits in the pension file (the other being August Derr, 1st Sergeant of Co. B). Together they establish — under oath, in independent affidavits — that Charles's loss of hearing originated from cannonading at New Madrid in September 1863, and his rheumatism from exposure at New Madrid in November 1863.

This is the single most important fact-pair the pension claim rests on, and it is what got Charles his $12.00 / month pension in November 1891.

That Meyer "met the claimant nearly three years ago" (~1888) suggests the two had drifted apart in the post-war years and were brought back together by the run-up to the 1890 Dependents' Pension Act claim. Charles's attorney Fred. W. Fout would have helped locate the surviving Battery B comrades in St. Louis to provide affidavits.

Why this is the right Christian Meyer: the affidavit puts him at age 48 in May 1891 (b. ~1843); the 1880 census records age 40 (b. ~1840) — within typical census imprecision. Occupation Teamster fits the manual-labor / 1803 Allen Avenue south-side workman profile of the affidavit. ED 107 covers the south-side Soulard / Lafayette Square area where Allen Avenue runs. Lizzie/Elisabetha Meyer is his wife. Three German-immigrant sons in Missouri, b. 1871-1877 — consistent with arrival pre-1870.

Probable region of origin

The Meyer surname is the second most common in Germany (after Müller) and is broadly distributed across Westphalia, Lower Saxony, Rhineland, and Hessen. Without a specific village-of-origin record, no narrower placement is possible. The given name "Christian" alongside wife "Lizzie" (Elisabetha) is consistent with a Lutheran north-German background, but neither narrows further.

Negative finding — pension index

A May 2026 search of the United States, General Index to Pension Files, 1861-1934 returned no Christian Meyer in 2nd MO Light Artillery. The closest hit was a different Christian Meyer (Cos. F & K, 1st U.S.R.C. Mo. Inf., widow's pension filed 1890 by Anna M. Meyer) — distinct person. Charles's Christian Meyer either filed under a name variant (Meier/Mezger), filed late (after 1900), or never filed his own pension.
