Charles's brother-in-law — Auguste's eldest brother — and a Civil War comrade. The two served together in Battery B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery.

Researcher view (sources, citations, bottom-line)

Carl Friedrich "August" Kreichelt (1844–1877, Clausthal) was Auguste's eldest brother, Charles's brother-in-law, and a Civil War comrade-in-arms — they served together in Battery B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery. Husband of Maria Emma Mincke. He sponsored the 1872 Auguste baptism — the infant Auguste Gonsen carries both her mother's and her uncle's name (the standard masculine-→-feminine namesake convention). He died young at age 32 in 1877, only five years after this baptism.

Why this matters for understanding Charles: August is a critical Charles-link figure: same regiment, same battery, same German-immigrant peer-group, same St. Louis post-war rebuild years. The "Charles & August served together" pattern is one of the strongest Charles-side documentary anchors. The 1872 baptism that names a daughter for him reads as a deliberate honoring of this comrade-brother bond. His early death at 32 narrowed the surviving Kreichelt male generation — meaning by 1880 Charles's primary in-law male peer was Carl, not August.

Connection to Charles Gannsen

★ Battery B comrade and brother-in-law from January 1866 onward

August was Charles Gannsen's first Kreichelt-family contact in Battery B — an older Hannoverian-born German-speaker enlisted as a private (or NCO; rank to be confirmed) in the same 2nd Missouri Light Artillery regiment that Charles joined on 2 December 1862. Together with their father Georg, the two Kreichelt brothers' Battery B service is one of the strongest documentary traces that the Charles Gannsen ↔ Auguste Kreichelt courtship was forged inside the unit during the war.

August was at Charles & Auguste's 7 January 1866 wedding at St. Marcus Evangelical Church — eighteen days after the unit's discharge. Together with his father (who walked the bride down the aisle) and the senior groomsman Adam Herweck (also Battery B), August was the third Battery B veteran in the wedding party.

★ Sponsor at the 1872 Auguste baptism · all-Kreichelt sponsorship

August stood as sponsor at the 1872 baptism of his namesake niece Auguste Gonsen, alongside his parents Georg and (likely) Louise/Henriette Kreichelt. This is the only Gannsen baptism with all-Kreichelt sponsorship — the most "in-family" of the seven baptisms. The infant Auguste Gonsen was named after both her mother and her uncle (the masculine August → feminine Auguste namesake convention, standard Lutheran practice).

This is August's only documented sponsor appearance at a Gannsen baptism. He had two children of his own at the time (Louise Henriette and Heinrich Wilhelm), and would have a third (August Jr.) the same year as the niece's baptism.

Tragic early death · 24 November 1877 · age 32

August died young — at age 32 in St. Louis on 24 November 1877. The cause of death has not yet been pulled from his death certificate. He left behind his wife Maria Emma Mincke (then 33) and three surviving young children: Louise Henriette (8), August Jr. (5), and Emma Wilhelmine (3).

Maria Emma was widowed at 33; she remarried George Henry Zuver after August's death and raised the four Kreichelt children as part of the new household. None of the August-Kreichelt-Maria-Emma children are documented as appearing at any later Gannsen baptism — consistent with the family's mid-1870s decoupling from the immediate St. Marcus Gannsen orbit after August's death.

August was buried at Holy Ghost Cemetery in St. Louis — the cemetery attached to the Holy Ghost German Evangelical Church where his wife's parents Heinrich Mincke and Margaretha Ludwig had married in 1839, and where Maria Emma's siblings were baptized. (Note that this is a separate cemetery from Old Picker's Cemetery where his parents Georg and Henriette were buried.)

📂 Full research log — Broader-investigation significance

Significance to the broader investigation

  1. The earliest Kreichelt-family Battery B link. As the first-born of the Kreichelt children, August would have been one of the first into Battery B — possibly even ahead of his father Georg's 1861 enlistment. His German-Hannoverian Lutheran identity overlap with Charles created the social bridge that culminated in the 1866 wedding. Whether the Charles-Auguste courtship was directly mediated by August is not yet documented but is consistent with the pattern.
  2. Sponsorship reflects birthorder seniority. Among Auguste's three brothers (August, Carl, Carl Wilhelm Christian Hermann), only the eldest two — August and Carl — appear as sponsors at Gannsen baptisms (August 1872, Carl 1870). The third brother Carl Wilhelm Christian Hermann does not. This birthorder pattern is consistent with German-Lutheran practice where eldest brothers typically held the senior in-law sponsor role.
  3. His death is a turning point in the sponsor pool. The 1875 Erwin Carl Konsen baptism is the first one without any Kreichelt sponsor — its sole sponsor is the Baden-born friend Erwin Spraul. August's death two years later (1877) marked the contraction of the eldest-brother Battery B social network around Charles, and the 1880 Bertha baptism reverts to a single Kreichelt sponsor (his sister Bertha Barbara "Babette"). The arc from "all-Kreichelt 1872" to "no-Kreichelt 1875" to "single-Kreichelt-sister 1880" tracks August's diminishing presence and eventual absence.
