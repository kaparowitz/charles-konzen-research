Dorothea Wilhelmine Ebeling Leue ★

A sponsor at the 1866 baptism of Charles and Auguste's firstborn — a senior Soulard / Lafayette-area German Lutheran neighbour during the family's South 7th Street years.

Dorothea Wilhelmine Ebeling Leue is now positively identified as the 1866 St. Marcus baptism sponsor previously recorded only as "Wilhelmina Ebeling." She was a senior Soulard / Lafayette-area German Lutheran neighbor of Charles & Auguste during their 1518 South 7th Street residence period, b. 1810 Fallersleben (Lower Saxony), m. Heinrich Andraes Daniel Leue 1840 in Hornburg, emigrated to St. Louis, lived at the NW corner of Ann Avenue and Elizabeth Street, d. 19 December 1872 — buried Gatewood Gardens (formerly New Picker's) Cemetery. The match was confirmed by six aligning evidence threads (surname match, given-name exact match, walking-distance Soulard geography, Lutheran religion, age-fit at 56–59, timing-fit with subsequent absence) and an FS-tree cross-check where her death date, both surviving daughters' death years, and the predeceased third daughter all align with the 1872 Westliche Post obituary.

Why this matters for understanding Charles: Three of the four 1866 firstborn-baptism sponsors were Auguste's Kreichelt-side kin; Wilhelmine was the fourth, the one non-kin sponsor — a senior parish-circle neighbor. Her identification opens up the Soulard / Lafayette German Lutheran social network as Charles's working immediate-environment in 1865–1867 — and notably, Charles's documented residences stay within this south-side German corridor: the family's later 1890–1903 addresses at 2408 DeKalb / 168 Sidney sit in modern Kosciusko, the next neighborhood south of Soulard, all within roughly a mile of Wilhelmine's Ann Avenue address.

Why this matters: the Soulard German social network in 1866

Identifying Wilhelmine reshapes the picture of the Gannsens' immediate post-marriage social world:

  • The firstborn baptism's sponsor pool was not exclusively Auguste's Kreichelt-side kin. Three of four sponsors (Georg Kreichelt, Henriette Kreichelt, Friedrich Pillmann) came from the bride's family; the fourth — Wilhelmine — was a senior parish-circle neighbor, not a relative.
  • The Soulard / Lafayette-area Lutheran network was Charles's working social environment in 1865–1867: Ann Ave + Elizabeth St (Wilhelmine), 1518 S. 7th St (Charles), and the early-1860s St. Louis directory entries that placed multiple St. Marcus parishioners in this same six-block radius. Charles remained a south-side German for most of his American life — his later 1890–1903 settlement at 2408 DeKalb / 168 Sidney is in modern Kosciusko, the next neighborhood south of Soulard, the 1880 census placed him at 1534 Jackson Street, in the same south-central district (Ward 9 — the 1880 Jackson Street ran between Columbus and DeKalb across Sidney St, not the modern north-side Jackson).
  • Wilhelmine's daughter Mina Otto, née Leue (1843–1918) was 23 years old in 1866 — a contemporary of Auguste Kreichelt (b. 1847). The two women may have been parish-circle friends, with Wilhelmine standing as godmother as a generational courtesy from Auguste's mother Henriette to her friend's mother.
  • The Westliche Post obituary network is a previously unmapped genealogical resource for this period — a German-language Lutheran-leaning daily that ran funeral notices for the parish-circle Charles and Auguste moved within. Future searches against Westliche Post 1865–1875 obituaries may locate other figures from the Gannsens' Soulard period.

Open questions & next research steps

Open questions

  • Karl Kipp's relationship — listed in the 1872 obituary as a child / step-relation but not on the FS tree under M6G9-FJR. Possibly from a first marriage of Heinrich or Wilhelmine, or a stepson by another connection.
  • Birth-year discrepancy — FS records 1810 Fallersleben; obituary gives age 65 (implying ~1807); Find a Grave records 1807–1872. A 3-year drift typical of 19c German immigrant records, but the original parish-baptism record at Fallersleben would resolve.
  • Direct Auguste-Mina friendship — the strongest hypothesis for why Wilhelmine stood as sponsor. No direct documentary evidence yet.

Productive next pulls

  1. Fallersleben parish baptism register c. 1810 — confirms exact birth year and parents' marriage.
  2. St. Louis 1865–1875 city directories — locate Heinrich Leue residences across the period, cross-reference with Charles's address history.
  3. Westliche Post obituary search 1865–1875 — surface other parish-circle figures from the Soulard / Lafayette German Lutheran network.
  4. St. Marcus 1865–1867 marriage register — check for any Leue-family witnesses or weddings that bridge the Leue and Kreichelt circles.
📂 Full research log — six-thread identification proof, FS-tree cross-check, earlier superseded hypotheses

✓ The 6-thread identification proof  ✓ CONFIRMED · 6 MAY 2026

The 1866 St. Marcus baptism sponsor recorded as "Wilhelmina Ebeling" is identified as Dorothea Wilhelmine Ebeling (FS M6G9-FJR), b. 1810 Fallersleben, m. Heinrich Andraes Daniel Leue (FS M6G9-FJZ) in Hornburg, Halberstadt, Sachsen, Preußen, Deutschland in 1840, emigrated to St. Louis, d. 19 December 1872. Six aligning evidence threads:

  1. Surname. The parish-clerk's "Ebeling" is a near-exact phonetic rendering of Ebling (her maiden name). German baptismal registers used by 19th-century pastors commonly inserted an extra unstressed -e- in surnames; "Ebeling" → "Ebling" is a one-letter normalization.
  2. Given name. "Wilhelmina" / "Wilhelmine" — exact match. The Westliche Post obituary uses "Wilhelmine" (the German form); American records use "Wilhelmina" (the Anglicized form).
  3. Geography. Wilhelmine lived at the NW corner of Ann Avenue and Elizabeth Street (per her 12-21-1872 Westliche Post obituary funeral notice). Charles & Auguste lived at 1518 South 7th Street in 1865–1867 (per Charles's pension address-history letter). Both addresses are within the same Soulard / Frenchtown German immigrant district of south St. Louis — walking distance.
  4. Religion. The Westliche Post St. Louis German-language Lutheran-leaning newspaper published her obituary, and her language ("went to sleep in Jesus") signals German Lutheran piety — same congregation network as the Gannsens at St. Marcus Evangelical.
  5. Age. Born 1810, she was 56 years old at the September 1866 baptism (or 59 if the obituary's age 65 is correct) — exactly the senior-matron age profile for a parish-circle female sponsor.
  6. Timing. She died 19 December 1872 — six years after the baptism. Her sponsorship of Friedrich Wilhelm in 1866 (the Gannsens' firstborn) and her absence from later baptisms (1868, 1870, 1872, 1873, 1875, 1880) is consistent with a senior sponsor whose health declined and who died before subsequent children were baptized.

✓ FS-tree cross-check — daughters' death years align exactly with 1872 obituary  ✓ 6 MAY 2026

The FS-tree person M6G9-FJR was cross-checked against the 1872 Westliche Post obituary's surviving-children list:

  • FS daughter Wilhelmine Dorothea Auguste Ebeling 1841–1918 (FS MGXM-PRG) ≈ obituary's "Mina (Minnie D.) Otto, née Leue" — death year 1918 matches exactly.
  • FS daughter Louise Wilhelmine Marie Leue 1844–1933 (FS 979G-GMK) ≈ obituary's "Marie (Mary Louisa) Prehn, née Leue" — death year 1933 matches exactly; middle name "Marie" matches.
  • FS daughter Johanna Dorothea Wilhelmine Leue 1840–1871 (FS KFLW-HL4) — predeceased her mother by one year, which is exactly why she's absent from the 1872 funeral notice's surviving-children list.

Three independent date alignments (mother's 1872 death, daughter Mina's 1918 death, daughter Marie's 1933 death) plus the structural prediction that pre-deceased daughter Johanna would be absent from a 1872 obituary — combined, the identification is confirmed beyond reasonable doubt.

Earlier (now-superseded) hypotheses

Before the 6 May 2026 identification of Wilhelmine Ebling Leue, the working hypothesis was that the sponsor was an unknown Albrecht-side, Pillmann-side, or Kreichelt-side female relative. None of those hypotheses now hold; preserved here for archival completeness.

The sponsor pool at the 1866 firstborn baptism otherwise consists entirely of Auguste's immediate kin (Georg and Henriette Kreichelt) plus Friedrich Pillmann (who became kin via the Albrecht/Kreichelt marriage chain four years later). On the strict pattern of "all sponsors are kin or near-kin from Auguste's side," Wilhelmina Ebeling was previously expected to belong to one of:

  • An Albrecht-side female relative — perhaps a sister or sister-in-law of Catharina Albrecht or her sister Fredericka. The Albrecht sisters in 1866 were still minors except for Catharina (16) and Fredericka (15). Possibly a married Albrecht aunt by surname.
  • A Pillmann-side female relative — a sister or sister-in-law of Friedrich Pillmann. Friedrich's documented FamilySearch family is sparse, so Pillmann female kin are not yet enumerated in the tree.
  • A Kreichelt-side female relative — possibly a sister-in-law of Auguste's mother Henriette via Henriette's maternal Peinemann line, or a connection via Georg's siblings (Georg's sister Wilhelmine Augustine Kreigelt, b. 1811 d. 1849, would be the namesake match — but she had died seventeen years before the 1866 baptism, ruling her out).
  • A non-kin parish friend whose surname has been mis-transcribed.

The matching of "Wilhelmina" with the namesake aunt Wilhelmine Augustine Kreigelt (Georg's sister) was intriguing but ultimately not the answer: she had died in 1849 in Hannover. The 6 May 2026 identification of Dorothea Wilhelmine Ebeling Leue as a parish-circle Soulard neighbor cleanly resolves the open question — confirming the fourth-sponsor pattern was a non-kin parish friend, not a relative.
