John Hebbeler

Pension declaration witness · 20-year acquaintance · 1891
Limited record Declaration witness · 9 Feb 1891 Knew Charles ~20 years (since c. 1871)

★ At a Glance

✓ ID Confirmed
PENSION DECLARATION CO-WITNESS · 20-YEAR ACQUAINTANCE

One of the two witnesses who co-signed Charles's 1891 Declaration for Invalid Pension — the formal claim instrument that opened the pension case.

John P. Hebbeler (FS M3DQ-K8M · b. January 1834 Germany — likely Westphalia / Lower Saxony · arrived US 1855 · m. Mary ~1865) co-witnessed Charles's signature on the central 9 February 1891 Declaration for Invalid Pension, sworn before Notary Robert Aubel — the formal claim instrument that initiated the pension process. His co-witness was John P. Thurbecker. Per the pension viewer's editorial note, Hebbeler's acquaintance with Charles ran roughly 20 years, dating to c. 1871 — the year the Edwards' Directory placed Charles at 718 Gates Street as a railroad conductor.

Why this matters for understanding Charles: Hebbeler's witness signature carries the formal-declaration's identification weight: he was attesting that the man signing the pension claim is the same Charles Gannson he had known for two decades. The Hebbeler + Thurbecker witness pair (916 N. 6th Street + 638 N. 7th Street, both north-side German immigrant blocks) places Charles in a known social-circle north of his own DeKalb / Sidney Street south-side residences — implying Charles maintained relationships across the city's two German-immigrant districts. His 1855 immigration date sits one wave after Charles's 1859 arrival, fitting the same Westphalian / north-German emigrant cohort.

What he witnessed

Witness signature · Declaration for Invalid Pension · 9 February 1891

Co-witnessed (with John P. Thurbecker of 638 N. 7th Street) Charles's signature on the formal Declaration for Invalid Pension sworn before Notary Robert Aubel on 9 February 1891. This is the central pension claim instrument.

Source: Pension File Page 11 + Page 12 (verso witness affidavit and filing label). Editor's note in the pension viewer specifies: "Witnesses had known Charles for 20 years (since circa 1871)."

Why he matters

Hebbeler's witness signature carries the formal-declaration's identification weight: he was attesting that the man signing the pension claim is in fact the same Charles Gannson he had known for two decades. The combined Hebbeler + Thurbecker witness pair on the 1891 Declaration — both 20-year acquaintances — established Charles's identity for Pension Bureau purposes.

The 916 North 6th Street and 638 North 7th Street addresses (Hebbeler and Thurbecker) are both in the German-immigrant district on the city's north side, between downtown and the historic Hyde Park / Bremen neighborhood. Charles, by contrast, was on the south side at 2408 DeKalb Street (Kosciusko, between Soulard and the river) by 1890 — so the witness pair was not next-door at the time of the 1891 declaration. Their 20-year acquaintance points to an earlier overlap: most likely Charles's 1869–1874 railroad-conductor years on Gates Street, his 1880 residence at 1534 Jackson Street (south-central Ward 9), or shared parish / fraternal ties.

★ Independent record verification (3 May 2026)

1900 US Federal Census, St. Louis Ward 19 ED 288 — strong match

FS Records returned a near-certain identification: John P Hebbeler, 1900 US Census, St. Louis (M3DQ-K8M). Full household at Precinct 4, St. Louis city Ward 19, ED 288, Sheet 7B:

  • John P Hebbeler · Self / Head · M · 66 · b. January 1834 Germany · arrived 1855 · married 35 years (m. ~1865) · Father's birthplace Germany, Mother's birthplace Germany
  • Mary Hebbeler · wife · F · 53 · b. Germany
  • Edw R Hebbeler · son · M · 31 · b. Missouri (~1869)

Why this is the right John Hebbeler:

  • Marriage in 1865 — exactly when Charles Gannsen returned from the Civil War. Hebbeler was building his St. Louis family at the same moment Charles was beginning his.
  • Arrived 1855 — Hebbeler immigrated about four years before Charles (1859). Both men were established St. Louis Germans by the late 1860s.
  • 20-year acquaintance in the 1891 affidavit corresponds to ~1871 — the Edwards' Directory year showing Charles at 718 Gates St as a railroad conductor. This places their first meeting in Charles's railroad-conductor phase, when Hebbeler was 37 and Charles 36 — peers in age and life-stage.
  • Birth January 1834 places Hebbeler one year older than Charles (Jan 1835). They were almost exact contemporaries.

Possible tree match flagged by FS: John Heppler (1835-Deceased) · GVYQ-BYX. A separate "John Hipler, b. 1833 Germany, 1880 Census St. Louis" entry may be the same person under an alternate clerical reading. The "Hebbeler" / "Hipler" / "Heppler" surname-cluster is consistent with a single underlying German surname rendered phonetically by different American clerks.

Probable region of origin

The "Hebbeler" surname is a Lower Saxon / Westphalian cluster name (variant of Hebbeling / Hebeler), with concentration around Münster, Osnabrück, and Westfalen. The 1855 arrival date and German-immigrant 916 N. 6th Street neighborhood (Hyde Park / Bremen district) fits the Westphalian-Catholic and -Lutheran post-1848 emigration wave.
