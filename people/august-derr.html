August Derr

1st Sergeant of Co. B · Charles's direct NCO at New Madrid · Pension affidavit witness
BATTERY B · 2ND MO LIGHT ARTILLERY · 1ST SERGEANT 2 affidavits in 1891

★ At a Glance

✓ ID Confirmed
PENSION WITNESS · 1ST SERGEANT CO. B

Charles's direct supervisor during the Civil War — the 1st Sergeant of Company B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery.

August Derr (b. ~1833 Germany — likely Saxony; mustered out 1865 age 32) was the 1st Sergeant of Company B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery — Charles's direct supervisor during the September 1863 New Madrid station. He swore two pension affidavits for Charles in 1891 (29 September and 14 May), independently corroborating Charles's hearing loss from heavy cannonading at New Madrid and his rupture-related disability. Derr's NCO rank gives his testimony the heaviest evidentiary weight of any soldier-witness in the file: he could speak with personal supervisory authority about Charles's medical history during active service.

Why this matters for understanding Charles: Derr's affidavits are the keystone of Charles's pension narrative — the only witness named in the pension file whose chain-of-command position lets him swear, under oath, that the New Madrid cannonading and the resulting deafness occurred while Charles was under his direct supervision in September–October 1863. He filed his own Civil War pension in May 1889, the same year Charles began his application — suggesting Charles's pension witnesses, including Derr, were themselves working through a coordinated post-war disability claim cycle in St. Louis.

What he swore to

First affidavit · 29 September 189[?]

Attests that Charles Gannsen, a member of Co. B, 2nd Mo. Art. Vols., while at New Madrid, Mo. "from heavy lifting received an injury in the right side. This injury [continuing] proved to be a rupture. Said Gannsen was unfit for duty on account of [same] [sometime] and that affiant has seen Gannsen on the sick list very often during service for the above said ailments. The above statement is made from personal knowledge."

Signed: August Derr, Co. B, 2nd Battery B, 2nd Mo. Light Artillery. Source: Pension File Page 24 (verso jurat Page 25).

Second affidavit · 14 May 189[?]

Confirms that Charles, at New Madrid, Mo. about September 1863 contracted from heavy cannonading, loss of hearing; further that along the same time from exposure & hardships contracted the Rheumatism. Affiant identifies himself as "1st Sergt. of same Co." — confirming his direct supervisory authority over Charles.

Source: Pension File Page 26 (verso jurat Page 27). Notary Theo. W. Boutje Jr.

Independent confirmation

The War Department's records check on Pension Page 35 independently confirms August Derr was present in Co. B, 2nd Mo. L.A. during the period in question (Sept.–Oct. 1863, while stationed at New Madrid, MO).

Why he matters

August Derr's affidavits carry the most evidentiary weight of any soldier-witness in the file because of his rank: as 1st Sergeant of Company B he was Charles's direct supervisor during the 1863 New Madrid station and could speak with personal authority about Charles's medical history during service.

The two affidavits cover all three of Charles's pension-cited disabilities: the rupture/hernia from heavy lifting (first affidavit) and the loss of hearing from cannonading + rheumatism from exposure (second affidavit) — all at the same New Madrid station in fall 1863. The pension was awarded $12.00/month on this evidence in November 1891.

★ Independent record verification (3 May 2026)

An independent multi-database research pass adds the following confirmed findings:

  • Side: Union
  • Company: B (same as Charles Gannsen)
  • Soldier's Rank In: Private
  • Soldier's Rank Out: Sergeant (NPS records "Sergeant"; Derr's own 1891 affidavit identifies him as "1st Sergeant," likely a promotion not captured in the NPS roll-up or Derr's own elevated description)
  • Alternate Name: M390 ROLL 11
  • Notes: "Company Note - Batt'y" (suggesting an entry annotation in the NARA compiled service records)

FamilySearch Records — Missouri Civil War Service Records of Union Soldiers, 1861-1865

  • Name: August Derr
  • Age 32 in 1865 (mustering out) — establishes birth year ~1833, more precise than the prior "~1835" estimate
  • Military Unit: "Second Light Artillery, De-Eh" (likely transcription of "D-Bh" or "Btty B" with OCR error — fits Battery B)
  • Affiliate: NARA M405 — Compiled Service Records of Volunteer Union Soldiers Who Served in Organizations from the State of Missouri
  • FS record FFYP-CW7

Pension

Pension filed 24 May 1889 in Missouri (per "United States, Civil War and Later Pension Index, 1861-1917"). This is the same year Charles Gannsen began his own pension application process — suggesting Charles's pension witnesses, including Derr, were themselves working through Civil War pensions at the same time. Derr also appears under the "United States, General Index to Pension Files, 1861-1934" with a 1889 entry referencing Missouri Township, Clark, Arkansas — possibly a relocation in his later years.

Negative findings — Derr is not in St. Marcus parish circle

A full pass through Robert Buecher's St. Marcus transcription set returned zero Derr entries:

  • Marriage Index 1847–1865: 0 Derr entries (out of 972 marriages)
  • Burial Index 1847–1879: 0 Derr entries (out of 1,209 burials)
  • Death Index 1880–1899: 0 Derr entries (out of 1,743 deaths)
  • Confirmation Index 1848–1870: 0 Derr entries

This means August Derr did not attend St. Marcus Evangelical Church — distinguishing him from the broader Pfälzer/Hessian Lutheran network that connected Charles to most other St. Louis acquaintances (Reck, Götz, Spraul, Pillmann, etc.). Derr's connection to Charles was purely military — formed in 1862-1865 service in Co. B, 2nd MO Light Artillery — not parish-circle.

Possible German origin — Saxony (Sachsen)

FS tree search for "August Derr" born ~1830-1838 returns the surname's strongest concentration in Saxony, Germany — specifically Bernsdorf, Kreis Chemnitz-Land and Oberlungwitz. The closest tree person August Friedrich Derr (FS 9SZ7-NP5) married in Bernsdorf 25 October 1814 — too early to be ours but documents the Saxon Derr line. Probable region of origin for our August Derr: Saxony, Germany — distinct from Charles Gannsen's Hessen-Hofgeismar origin (~350 km north of Chemnitz). Confirms the Charles-Derr bond was formed in St. Louis, not transatlantic.

Negative findings — Find a Grave

A May 2026 Find a Grave search for "August Derr" died before 1920 in Missouri returned zero matches. His burial record is either unindexed, in a non-Missouri cemetery (Clark County Arkansas? per the 1889 pension file reference), or under a name variant.
