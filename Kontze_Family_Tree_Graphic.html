KONTZE / KONZE / CONTZEN FAMILY TREE

Trendelburg & Deisel, Kurhessen — Reconstructed from Archion Register 220096
Compiled 1 April 2026 · Searched against Charles's 26 January 1835 birth window — no candidate found (Sessions 18–50, April 2026; Peter Schräder verdict 12 April 2026)
Jed Johnson & Peter Schräder · last updated 6 June 2026

Search complete — no Konze candidate identified. Between Sessions 18 and 50 of the April 2026 sprint, every documented Konze / Kontzen male in the Trendelburg parish complex (including the sons-of-Andreas window 1807–1818 that this tree's earlier "highest-priority research action" called for) was tested against Charles's 26 January 1835 birth window. Peter Schräder's verdict, 12 April 2026: “Charles is not a legitimate son of the Konze family.” The Entry 195 emergency-baptism candidate (Deisel KB 218842, January 1835) was also closed on 4 May 2026 when Peter re-read it as a Köster day-labourer's child, not a Konze (see Entry 195 source page). This tree is retained as the project's reconstruction of the Konze surname pool; Charles is not a documented descendant of any branch shown.

June 2026 update. A surname-distribution sweep confirmed the Konze pool is confined to one tight Diemel-valley cluster (Trendelburg · Deisel · Friedrichsfeld · Stammen · Hümme — all within ~4 km), and a previously-undocumented Stammen Kontze household was found (Christian Ludwig Kontze × Anne Gertrud Temme, m. 1833; only child stillborn 1835 — shown below). For the full people-by-parish listing with FamilySearch IDs, see The Konze family, by town; for the geography, the Hessian parish map.

Charles Research — a multi-month transatlantic genealogical investigation into Charles Konzen (recorded in U.S. records as Gannsen / Johnson; 1835–1903), Hessian-born Civil War sergeant of Battery B, 2nd Missouri Light Artillery, and his probable Trendelburg / Deisel origins in Churhessen (a working conclusion — the German baptism remains unrecovered).
