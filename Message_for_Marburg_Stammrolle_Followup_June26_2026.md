## Notes (for you, not for sending)

- **BESTAND CORRECTION (Arcinsys, 26 Jun 2026) — "4h" looks wrong; the rolls are Best. 331 [Amt].**
  Arcinsys checks settle this. "Bestand 4h" appears **nowhere** among HStAM conscription or
  muster holdings. The actual Kurhessen recruitment muster rolls are filed as
  **HStAM Bestand 331 _[Amt/locality]_** — yearly volumes titled *"Rekrutierungsstammrolle"*
  (e.g. **331 Frielendorf** runs Bd. 1 = 1841 through Bd. 16 = 1892, i.e. the 1850s class is
  covered where a given Amt's series survives). So Charles's 1855 roll would be in
  **Best. 331 for the Amt covering Trendelburg / Deisel (and/or Hofgeismar)**, the volume for
  that conscription year. Supporting fonds: **Best. 12** (Kurhessisches Kriegsministerium,
  1813–1867, central military authority) and, for the Napoleonic period only, Best. 75 / 77a /
  98c / 112b / 118a and Best. 330 _[Kreis]_ "Konskription." The older idea that it sits in
  **Best. 180 Hofgeismar** (Kreisamt, holds the emigration files) is a secondary possibility.
  → **Next message to Marburg should drop "4h" and ask them to check Best. 331 for the
  Trendelburg/Hofgeismar Amt (1855 vol.), with Best. 12 as fallback.** (The 26 May letter and
  my first follow-up still said 4h / Best. 180; correct this in the next exchange or when they
  reply.)

- **REFINEMENT (Nimble + Arcinsys, 27 Jun 2026) — the LOCAL rolls almost certainly don't help; pivot to Best. 12.**
  Filtering "Rekrutierungsstammrolle" to **1850–1860 across all Hessen returns only 21 items,
  of which just 2 are HStAM** — *330 Borken* (wrong Kreis) + one other; **nothing for
  Hofgeismar / Trendelburg / Deisel or any Kreis-Hofgeismar Amt.** The HStAM 331 series are
  overwhelmingly **post-1867 (Prussian era)** and survive for only a few Ämter (Frielendorf
  jumps 1841 → 1870+, i.e. its 1850s vol is missing too). So the 1855 Kurhessen class for
  Charles's home Amt **very likely does not survive in the local Best. 331/330 series at HStAM.**
  → **Best bet is now the central military fonds, Best. 12 (Kurhessisches Kriegsministerium,
  1813–1867)** — the army's own conscription/Stammrollen — with **Best. 180 Hofgeismar**
  (Kreisamt Militärersatz/Aushebung admin) and the **Stadtarchiv Hofgeismar** as secondary.
  The next Marburg message should ask them to check **Best. 12** for the 1855 Aushebung of
  Kreis Hofgeismar, note that the local Best. 331 rolls appear not to survive for that Kreis/
  year, and drop "4h" entirely.
- **One-stop option.** Your separate 25 May Auswanderer query (Best. 180 Hofgeismar /
  Best. 17b) is with the same archive — if useful you could ask them to treat both together,
  since a Best. 180 Hofgeismar pull could answer the conscription *and* the emigration
  question in one visit.
- These rolls are the highest-value remaining German source if Charles dodged the draft:
  a positive "secretly emigrated" notation would both confirm origin and explain his total
  absence from the indexed emigration lists.
