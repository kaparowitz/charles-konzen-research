## 1. Stammen — the 1835 stillborn's siblings (the real lead) — RESOLVED

**Question:** did the stillborn male Kontze (b. 14 Mar 1835, Haus Nr. 21,
child of **Christian Ludwig Kontze × Anne Gertrud née Temme**) have any
baptized siblings in Stammen?

**Method:** swept the **alphabetical name-index of Stammen Taufen 1830–1915
(reg. 220069)**. The index sits at the end of the reel, one letter per page:
**I/J = Bild 273, K = Bild 274, U/V = 285, W = 287.**

**K-leaf (Bild 274, permalink https://www.archion.de/p/35bc5b4dce/):**
- Männlich: Kuncke, Kloppmann, Krieger, Kuncke, Krieger, Kothe, Kampe.
- Weiblich: Kloppmann, Klages, Koch, Küchemann, Krone, Klages, Küchemann,
  Krieger, Klages, Krieger.
- **NO Kontze / Konze / Konsen anywhere** in the entire 1830–1915 register.

**Conclusion:** the 14 Mar 1835 child was **stillborn (todtgeboren), never
baptized**, so he appears only in the **burial** register (220072, img 843) —
not in the baptism index. The couple had **no baptized child in Stammen**, so
this child had **no baptized siblings here.** This confirms Session 89's read
("the stillborn was their only Stammen child; the couple left after 1835") —
now via the baptism index itself. **Any siblings were born wherever Christian
Ludwig & Anne Gertrud relocated after 1835** — still the open emigration
question (passenger / Auswanderer under Kontze ~1836–55; US Konze/Kontze in the
right window). The Stammen pre-1830 Kontze/Jungkontze baptisms already on file
(220063: Joh. Henrich 1800, Joh. Ludwig 1803, Maria Charlotta 1805, Christian
Ludwig 1806, Johannes Jungkontze 1808) are an **earlier generation**, not this
couple's children.
