- The father's surname is **Köster** — consistent with Session 71 confirmation register finding

### Death Year Analysis — 1879 Confirmed

The death notation in the Bemerkungen column was zoom-examined at maximum resolution to distinguish between possible years (1836, 1839, 1879). The digit sequence following "December" was examined carefully:

- **1836** would be biologically impossible (child born August 16, 1836 cannot die in the same register entry's remarks on December 24, 1836 before the entry was even completed — this was ruled out)
- **1839** (age ~3): The "3" in Kurrent is written as a rounded, curved figure resembling a reversed "B"
- **1879** (age ~43): The "7" in Kurrent is written with a distinct angled downstroke, followed by "9"

At maximum zoom, the year digit shows an **angled downstroke consistent with "7"**, not the rounded curve of "3." The sequence reads **1-8-7-9 = 1879**. Conclusion: Ernst Wilhelm Köster died December 24, 1879, at age 43.

**This resolves the session-summary concern that the death year might indicate infant death (1839) or same-year death (1836). Ernst Wilhelm lived to adulthood, consistent with his confirmed appearance in the 1850 Deisel confirmation class (KB 218836) when he would have been age 13–14.**

### Given Name Discrepancy — Flagged for Verification

The first given name in Entry 261 presents a paleographic challenge. The initial letter appears to have a **curved, closed stroke** more consistent with Kurrent "C" (= Carl) than with Kurrent "E" (= Ernst). However, the 1850 confirmation entry (KB 218836, Image 938, Entry 10) definitively identifies this same individual — same birth date (August 16, 1836), same parents (Heinrich Köster × Wilhelmine geb. Hillebrand) — as **"Ernst Wilhelm."**

Possible explanations for the discrepancy:
1. **Misread "E" as "C"** — In Kurrent handwriting, the capital "E" can have a curved initial stroke that, under imperfect zoom/scan quality, resembles "C." This is the most parsimonious explanation.
2. **"Carl Ernst Wilhelm Köster"** — The child may have been baptized with three given names, with "Carl" as the first name that fell out of use, and "Ernst Wilhelm" as the names he used going forward (including at confirmation). This was not uncommon in 19th-century German baptismal practice.
3. **Register error** — Less likely, but the baptism register could contain an error that was corrected or superseded by the confirmation entry.

**Recommendation:** Professional paleographic examination of the Entry 261 name field is recommended to definitively resolve this question. The surname "Köster" (underlined) is unambiguous and the family identity is confirmed regardless of the given-name reading.
