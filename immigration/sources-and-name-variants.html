Sources Searched & Name Variants

Every archive consulted · every spelling tried · every given-name combination tested
IMMIGRATION JOURNEY11 sources · 14 surname variants · 6 given-name combos

★ At a Glance

Over the course of the investigation, every major U.S.-arrival passenger-list collection, every Hessian state-archive emigration index, and every aggregated emigrant database has been searched under two distinct families of name spellings — the "Gannsen / Gonnson" cluster (the form Charles used in America) and the "Konze / Konsen" cluster (the form preserved at his son's 1875 St. Marcus baptism). Searches were also run under six given-name combinations covering both the "L. Charles" enlistment record and the candidate Justus / Ludwig / Lorenz reconstructions of the German baptismal name. No match has surfaced for any combination.

Sources searched

Each source below was searched under the full set of name variants and date range 1853 – 1862. The verdict column captures the outcome.

Bremen Auswanderer-Datenbank ↗The German emigrant database (Die MAUS Bremen / Bremen Chamber of Commerce digitization). Indexes Bremen departures 1832 – 1939. Searched under all surname variants. no match — Bremen passenger lists for 1832 – 1872 were largely destroyed; surviving partial indices contain no Charles-shaped entry from Hessen.
Hessisches Staatsarchiv (HHStAW / HStAM) ↗Marburg state archive emigration cards via the Arcinsys portal. Documents requested-for-emigration permits from 1820s onward. no match for Charles under any spelling. The only "Gonnson"-like entry surfaced is the Bad Nauheim probate of Count Jouffroy Gonsons of Paris (see French Count panel) — unrelated.
LAGIS Hessen — Auswanderer ↗The Hessian Land-Information-System emigration index. Indexed by surname and town. no Charles match. Surfaces Wilhelmine Maria Hildebrand's October 1853 emigration from Deisel (5-person family to Philadelphia, ship President Smidt) and the 1850 Ludwig Hildebrand emigration to Rockford IL (chain-migration angle).
Ancestry · NY Arriving Passenger Lists 1853 – 1862 ↗Collection #7488 (1820–1957). Searched under all name variants. 12 Justus Köster results · 17 Carl Konzen results · 1 Carl Krieger candidate. Best hit: Carl Kinzer (indexed) on the ship Resolute, Liverpool→NY 20 Dec 1859. Manifest image inspected: the actual handwritten surname is KRIEGER, occupation Miller, eliminated.
Castle Garden — NY arrivals 1855 – 1862 ↗The pre-Ellis Island intake gateway, indexed by Battery Conservancy. no Hessen-specific Charles match under any surname. Origins were often recorded only as "Germany" without province.
Ancestry · New Orleans arrivals 1853 – 1862 ↗Collection #7484 (1820–1945). Alternative downriver route to St. Louis. no match. Less common for north-Hessen emigrants.
Ancestry · Baltimore arrivals 1853 – 1862 ↗Collection #8748 (1820–1948). Third major port. no match.
FamilySearch · Germany emigration index ↗Aggregated index combining HEPIS, Hamburg State Archive, Bremen index. no match for Charles under any surname; surfaces only the Hildebrand cohort.
Hamburg Staatsarchiv passenger lists ↗The complete Hamburg passenger lists 1850 – 1934 (Ancestry collection #1068, digitized from Staatsarchiv Hamburg). no match — but Hamburg is the wrong port for a Trendelburg emigrant; this was a confirmation search rather than primary expectation.
NARA — index to passenger lists ↗National Archives master index for U.S. ports. Same negative result; same name-spelling problem.
Schräder geographic-constraint checkPeter Schräder independently verified that no Trendelburg or Deisel resident named Konze / Konsen / Gonnson appears in the Marburg state archive emigration cards for the 1850s under any spelling close to Charles's age window. (Captured across the Schräder Correspondence February – April 2026.)

Given-name combinations

Charles also went by several given names. Searches were run under each.

L. CharlesCivil War enlistment recorded form. The "L." has never been resolved — possibly Ludwig, possibly a clerk's mishearing. → See the "L." mystery deep-dive
Charles / Carl / Karldirect German↔English conversion. "Carl Gonnson" appears in the 1866 marriage; "Karl Konsen" was tested in German-language indexes.
Justusformerly tested as the Entry 195 child-name (KB 218842, 23 Jan 1835); the Entry 195 reading was retracted 4 May 2026 (the child had no recorded name), so this search thread is closed. → Entry 195 source page
Justus Ludwig / Lorenz Justus / Ludwig Justuscompound-name reconciliations of the U.S. "L. Charles" with the German "Justus" reading.
Erwin Carltested in the indexes — confirmed only as the SON's name (1875 baptism), not Charles himself.
Gustava former alternative reading of the Entry 195 child-name; closed, as the Entry 195 reading was retracted 4 May 2026.

The Hessian-emigrant geographic constraint

Peter Schräder's geographic constraint (15 March 2026): pre-train northern Hesse, an emigrant from Trendelburg in 1859 would travel Trendelburg → Karlshafen on the Weser → Bremen → North America, NOT to Le Havre, Hamburg, or Antwerp. The Karlshafen-Weser route is by far the most likely. This filters several name-match candidates that arrived from Liverpool / Le Havre — those are still possible but require a transit detour Charles is unlikely to have taken given his geographic origin.
