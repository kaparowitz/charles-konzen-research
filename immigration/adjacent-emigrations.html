Adjacent Emigrations & the Negative as Evidence

Two Hildebrand cohorts that didn't match Charles · what the absence tells us
IMMIGRATION JOURNEYHildebrand chain-migration angle · Bremen-records-destruction context

★ At a Glance

Two Hildebrand-cohort emigrations from Deisel surfaced in the LAGIS index — neither matches Charles, but both illuminate the chain-migration possibility. The 1850 Ludwig Hildebrand cohort went to Rockford, Illinois (a maternal-aunt route); the October 1853 Wilhelmine Maria Hildebrand cohort went to Philadelphia (manifest located, no extra teen). Beyond the Hildebrands, the absence of Charles from any indexed Bremen / Hamburg / NY / Baltimore / New Orleans manifest is itself the strongest single piece of evidence — pointing at the destroyed Bremen 1832–1872 records as the most likely explanation.

Adjacent emigrations that surfaced

Two Deisel-Hildebrand emigrations that did NOT match Charles but illuminate the chain-migration possibility:

April / May 1850 · Ludwig Hildebrand cohort → Rockford IL

Deisel KB 218836 (1850 confirmation class, Entry 1) records Friedrich Hildebrand confirmed early "weil die Eltern nach Amerika auswandern wollten" (because the parents wanted to emigrate to America). Located on the 1860 census in Monroe Township, Ogle County, Illinois — about 15 miles south of Rockford. Indexed by Ancestry as "Hillabrand."

Implication: if Charles is Justus Köster (b. Jan 1835), he could have travelled with this 1850 Hildebrand cohort as a 15-year-old maternal nephew — but Rockford is not St. Louis. A delayed Rockford → St. Louis move between 1850 and 1862 remains possible but undocumented.

October 1853 · Wilhelmine Maria Hildebrand → Philadelphia

Manifest located: ship President Smidt, Bremen → New York, arrived 14 January 1854. Five-person family (mother + 4 young children), origin "Deisel," destination "Philadelphia" — explicitly named. Adjacent rows on the manifest (95–103) show no extra Hildebrand or Köster traveller — the cohort is exhaustive at five people.

Implication: ELIMINATED as the Justus Köster vehicle. The manifest has no extra teen, and the destination is wrong.

The negative as evidence

After running every variant against every major manifest collection, the absence is itself informative:

  1. Charles either travelled under a name spelling so distorted that no soundex bridge connects it to "Gannsen / Konsen / Konze," or
  2. he travelled on a manifest that was destroyed (the Bremen passenger lists for 1832 – 1872 are largely lost), or
  3. he travelled with a family group whose surname masked his (e.g. as a minor with a maternal aunt or uncle's surname).

Option 2 (destroyed Bremen manifests) is most consistent with the geographic constraint: a Trendelburg emigrant in 1859 would naturally depart from Bremen, and Bremen's records for that exact window are gone. This is the leading explanation for the manifest gap.
