### Entry B-1: Wilhelmine Kontze (b. 1799) — CORRECTED April 4, 2026

**Location:** Register 218824, Image 1660, left page, mid-page entry (entry No. 25)
**Section:** Getaufte 1799 (Baptisms, year 1799)
**Screenshot:** Saved — full page view and multiple detail zooms of image 1660

#### Original Transcription (Kurrentschrift) — CORRECTED

> 25. **Wilhelmine**, Klaus Andreas Schi~~ldknecht~~ **Kontze**, et Ux. Ca[tharina]
> Elisabeth gnbs Thilin, nat. d 20 h 2 postmerid [17]99.
> Gevatternin, Anna Catharina, ux[oris] Johann Henrich Kontzen.

**Transcription notes:**
- **CORRECTION (April 4, 2026):** The child's name is **Wilhelmine**, not "Catharine" as previously transcribed. The earlier transcription incorrectly merged this entry with the NEXT baptism entry on the page (Catharina Elisabeth, daughter of Dragoner David Lauterbach and wife Anna Margareta née Koch). Those lines belong to a completely separate baptism (Entry No. 29/1, March 1799), not to the Kontze godparents.
- The surname "Schildknecht" is visibly crossed out in the original and replaced with "Kontze" — same ink, same hand, indicating the correction was made at the time of entry
- ~~strikethrough~~ indicates text crossed out in the original
- "gnbs" = geborene (née); "Ux." = Uxoris (wife); "nat." = natus/nata (born); "postmerid" = post meridiem (afternoon)
- The entry has only ONE listed godmother: Anna Catharina, wife of Johann Henrich Kontzen
- Marginal death annotation visible: "†181[0?]" — indicating Wilhelmine died around 1810

#### English Translation — CORRECTED

> No. 25. **Wilhelmine**, [daughter of] Klaus Andreas ~~Schildknecht~~ **Kontze** and [his] wife Catharina Elisabeth née Thiel, born the 20th [of the month] at 2:00 in the afternoon, [17]99.
>
> **Godmother (Gevatternin):**
> Anna Catharina, wife of Johann Henrich Kontze

#### Analytical Notes

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Child** | **Wilhelmine** (female) |
| **Father** | **Klaus Andreas Kontze** (surname corrected from "Schildknecht") |
| **Mother** | **Catharina Elisabeth née Thilin** (Thiel/Thill) |
| **Born** | The 20th [month uncertain — estimated February 1799 based on page position between January and March entries], 2:00 PM |
| **Died** | ~1810 (marginal death annotation "†181[0?]") |
| **Parish** | Deisel |

**Godparent identification:**
**Anna Catharina, wife of Johann Henrich Kontze** — This is the widow of Johann Henrich Kontze (b. ~March 1747, d. April 17, 1792), née Köster per the family tree. She was still alive in 1799, seven years after her husband's death. Her presence as sole godmother directly ties Klaus Andreas to the established Deisel Kontze family — a strong indicator that he was a close relative (likely nephew or son of a brother).

**Critical observation — the Schildknecht/Kontze surname correction:** The pastor initially wrote the father's surname as "Schildknecht" then crossed it out and wrote "Kontze." This is the only such correction in the register and strongly suggests Klaus Andreas was known by both surnames in the community. Possible explanations: (a) raised by a Schildknecht family but biologically a Kontze; (b) a stepchild of a Schildknecht who used his birth father's Kontze name; (c) the pastor momentarily confused him with a Schildknecht family member and corrected himself.

**Additional findings from image 1660 (right page):**
- **Anna Dorothea, wife of Klaus Christian Kontze**, appears as godmother for a Maria Goelle baptism on the same page — confirming Klaus Christian Kontze was active in the Deisel community at this time
- **Johann Christoph Sasse** married a "Catharina Elisabeth née Thilin" — the same maternal maiden name as Klaus Andreas Kontze's wife. This suggests the Thiel family had multiple daughters married into different Deisel families, and could indicate a kinship network worth investigating

### Entry D-1: Andreas Kontze (I) — The Deisel Patriarch

**Location:** Register 218821, Image 1558, right page, Entry 223
**Section:** Beerdigungen (Burials), approximately March 1791
**Screenshot:** Saved — full page view of image 1558 (Burials 1790/1791 spread) and zoomed detail of entry

#### Original Transcription (Kurrentschrift)

> № 223. **Andreas Kontze**, gewesenen Küster
> † d. 2ten [Martij] dieses Abends 9 Uhr
> Alt 72 Jahr 6 Monat
> Nr. 1

**Transcription notes:**
- "gewesenen" = former (past participle, indicating he had retired from the position)
- "Küster" = sexton, church custodian
- "Nr. 1" = house number 1 in Deisel
- The month "Martij" (March) is inferred from the position in the register and surrounding entries

#### English Translation

> No. 223. **Andreas Kontze**, former sexton [of the church],
> died the 2nd of March, this evening at 9 o'clock,
> aged 72 years 6 months.
> [Residence:] House No. 1 [Deisel]

#### Analytical Notes

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Name** | Andreas Kontze |
| **Occupation** | Gewesener Küster (former sexton/church custodian) |
| **Died** | March 2, 1791, at 9:00 PM |
| **Age at death** | 72 years, 6 months |
| **Calculated birth** | ~September 1718 |
| **Residence** | House Nr. 1, Deisel |

**Significance:** This is the **single most important discovery** of the entire research project. Andreas Kontze (I) is the patriarch of the Deisel Kontze family — father of Johann Friedrich, Johann Christoph (the Grenadier), Johann Henrich, and Konrad. His position as Küster indicates literacy and community standing. House Nr. 1 suggests the family was long-established in Deisel. His birth year (~1718) is significantly earlier than the prior estimate of ~1735, which likely referred to his son Johann Friedrich.

### Entry BP-1: Anna Margaretha Kontze — Daughter of Andreas (I) the Küster (MAJOR DISCOVERY)

**Location:** Register 218821, Image 1425, left page, December entry
**Section:** Getaufte (Baptisms), year ~1777–1778 (to be confirmed)
**Screenshot:** Saved — zoomed detail of the entry

#### Original Transcription (Kurrentschrift)

> Decembr 2. **Anna Margaretha**, **Andreas Kontzen**,
> Jetzigen **Küsters** alfier et Uxor **Anna Maria** gnbs. **Gutmann** alfier,
> Tüssferlein nat: 29 ejusdem hor: Septima Vespertina.
> Gevatternin: Anna Margaretha, [des] Johann Henrich
> Gutmanns alfier Ehefrau.

**Transcription notes:**
- "Jetzigen Küsters alfier" = current sexton from here — confirming Andreas Kontze was the ACTIVE Küster at this date
- "et Uxor" = and [his] wife
- "gnbs. Gutmann" = geborene (née) Gutmann
- "Tüssferlein" = a little daughter (diminutive)
- "nat: 29 ejusdem" = born the 29th of the same [month] (November 29)
- "hor: Septima Vespertina" = at the seventh evening hour (7:00 PM)
- "Gevatternin" = godmother
- The godmother Anna Margaretha was wife of Johann Henrich Gutmann — likely the wife's brother or relative

#### English Translation

> December 2. **Anna Margaretha**, [daughter of] **Andreas Kontze**,
> current **sexton** from here, and [his] wife **Anna Maria née Gutmann** from here,
> a little daughter, born the 29th of the same [month, i.e., November 29] at 7:00 in the evening.
> **Godmother:** Anna Margaretha, wife of Johann Henrich Gutmann from here.

#### Analytical Notes

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Child** | Anna Margaretha Kontze (female) |
| **Father** | **Andreas Kontze**, Küster (sexton) — the Deisel patriarch |
| **Mother** | **Anna Maria née Gutmann** |
| **Born** | November 29, ~1777/1778, at 7:00 PM |
| **Baptized** | December 2 |
| **Godmother** | Anna Margaretha, wife of Johann Henrich Gutmann |
| **Parish** | Deisel |

**SIGNIFICANCE — BREAKTHROUGH DISCOVERY:** This entry reveals **Andreas Kontze I's wife's maiden name: GUTMANN.** This was previously unknown and is a critical genealogical link. The wife's name is **Anna Maria née Gutmann**, from Deisel. The godmother is from the same Gutmann family (wife of Johann Henrich Gutmann), confirming a close family connection. Andreas is explicitly titled "Jetzigen Küsters" (current sexton), consistent with his burial entry (D-1) which calls him "gewesenen Küster" (former sexton). The exact year of this entry needs confirmation; based on page position (~image 1425 in the 1736–1796 baptism section), it falls approximately 1777–1778.

**Additional finding on same page:** The entry immediately above the Kontze baptism mentions "Catharina, Johannes **Schildknechts** alfier noch lebige des Tochter" (Catharina, still-living daughter of Johannes Schildknecht from here) as a godmother for another child's baptism. This confirms the Schildknecht family was active in Deisel simultaneously with the Kontze family.
