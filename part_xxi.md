

## PART XXI — MARRIAGE REGISTER SYSTEMATIC SEARCH AND FAMILY STRUCTURE REVOLUTION

*April 12, 2026 — continuation of Part XX*

### XXI.1 — Session goal

This session completed the remaining items from the XX.7 revised priority queue: (1) P6 J-section baptism page reads (pages 9, 22, 103, 133, 174), (2) P3/P5 systematic search of the entire marriage section ("Copulirte") of register 220096, and (3) incidental resolution of P2 (Andreas Contze children) and P4 (Elder J.H. Kontze identity). The marriage search yielded ten Kontze-related entries spanning 1773–1808, fundamentally reshaping the family model.

### XXI.2 — J-Section Baptism Page Discoveries

#### XXI.2.1 — Page 22–23 (image 995): Two critical entries (1776–1777)

**Entry 1 (page 22, 1776):** Baptism of a child of "Hennig Albrecht, Pfannenmeister, und Anna Margaretha geb. Kontzin." This confirms an Anna Margaretha née Kontze married into the Albrecht family before 1776. She cannot be the same Anna Margaretha born Oct 5, 1774 (an infant in 1776). She is likely a daughter of the elder Johann Henrich Kontze or of Johann Philipp Kontze.

**Entry 2 (page 23, 1777):** Baptism of **Anna Christina Kontze**, born January 6, 1777, daughter of Joh. Henrich Kontze (the younger) and Martha Elisabetha née Wiechardt. This is the fourth first-marriage child discovered, filling the gap between Anna Margaretha (1774) and Maria Elisabetha (1779).

**Updated first-marriage children list:**

1. Anna Margaretha (b. Oct 5, 1774, page 11)
2. Anna Christina (b. Jan 6, 1777, page 23) — NEW
3. Maria Elisabetha (b. Apr 28, 1779, page 38)
4. Anna Catharina (b. Feb 10, 1782, page 44)

Godfather for 1777 baptism: Christian Wiechardt (maternal grandfather), confirming the strong Wiechardt–Kontze family bond. Notably, Christian Wiechardt's wife was recorded as "Anna Margaretha" in 1777, which appears to differ from the "Anna Christina née Groff" recorded in a 1781 entry — suggesting either two Christian Wiechardts, or a remarriage.

#### XXI.2.2 — Page 103 (image 1035): Joh. Henrich, son of Johannes Jungkonze, Steindecker (1798)

Baptism of Joh. Henrich, son of "Johannes Jungkonze, Steindecker" and wife "Carolina née Ender." The child died (cross annotation). This entry was indexed under "J" because "Jungkonze" was written as one word.

**Significance:** Identifies "Johannes Jungkonze" as a slate roofer (Steindecker) with a wife Carolina née Ender. The "Jungkonze" designation (Kontze the younger) distinguishes this branch. The child shares the name "Joh. Henrich" with the patriarch, suggesting it honors a grandfather or great-uncle.

#### XXI.2.3 — Pages 9, 133, 174: No Kontze entries

These J-index cross-referenced pages contained entries for other J-surnames (Jordan, Jacob, Jungdodt) and H-surname entries (Henze, Hentze) that were confirmed NOT Kontze by careful examination of the initial letter.

### XXI.3 — Marriage Register Systematic Search: Complete Findings

The marriage section of register 220096 spans pages 286–327+ (images 1137–1158+), covering years 1773–1818. A complete page-by-page search yielded ten Kontze-related marriage entries.

#### XXI.3.1 — Marriage #1: Johann Henrich Kontze × Martha Elisabetha Wiechardt (1773)

- **Date:** November 26, 1773
- **Location:** Page 288, Image 1139
- **Groom:** Johann Henrich Kontze, **Arnold Philipp Kontzen Rathsverwandten allier ehelicher ältester Sohn** (eldest legitimate son of Arnold Philipp Kontze, town council member here)
- **Bride:** Martha Elisabetha, **Christian Wiechardt Rathsverwandten allier eheliche älteste Tochter** (eldest legitimate daughter of Christian Wiechardt, town council member here)

**Critical findings:**
- Arnold Philipp Kontze held the title **Rathsverwandter** (town council member) — a civic leadership position
- Christian Wiechardt also held the same title — this was a union between two council families
- Joh. Henrich was the **ältester Sohn** (eldest son), confirmed
- Martha Elisabetha was the **älteste Tochter** (eldest daughter)
- Arnold Philipp was ALIVE in November 1773

#### XXI.3.2 — Marriage #2: David Lotz × Anna Margaretha Kontze (1774)

- **Date:** February 17, 1774
- **Location:** Page 288, Image 1139
- **Groom:** H. David Lotz, **Wittwer** und von Stockhausen, Verwalter und Conductor auf der Abgunst allier (widower, administrator and conductor at the Abgunst estate here)
- **Bride:** Jf. Anna Margaretha, **weiland H. Johann Henrich Kontzen Rathsverwandten allier** hinterlassene eheliche Tochter (maiden, daughter of the **late** Johann Henrich Kontze, town council member here)

**Critical findings:**
- **Elder Johann Henrich Kontze identified:** Anna Margaretha's father is "weiland" (late) Johann Henrich Kontze, a Rathsverwandter — this is the ELDER J.H. Kontze, dead by February 1774
- This RESOLVES P4: the elder J.H. Kontze was a separate individual from Arnold Philipp's son, holding the same civic rank (Rathsverwandter)
- David Lotz was already a widower — Anna Margaretha Kontze was his second wife
- David Lotz later married Anna Catharina née Roedde in March 1782, making her his third wife
- Anna Margaretha was described as "Jf." (Jungfrau = maiden), confirming she was unmarried prior

#### XXI.3.3 — Marriage #3: Johannes Kontze × Anna Margaretha Thielen (1780)

- **Date:** November 30, 1780
- **Location:** Page 296, Image 1143
- **Groom:** Johannes Kontze, Ackermann, weiland Arnold Philipp Kontzen gewesenen Rathsmanns und Ackermanns allier nachgelassener ehelicher **2ter Sohn** (farmer, surviving legitimate **2nd son** of the late Arnold Philipp Kontze, former council member and farmer here)
- **Bride:** Anna Margaretha Thielen, weiland Johannas Thielen gewesenen Einwohnerts zu Deybell (Deissel) nachgelassene eheliche Tochter

**Critical findings:**
- **Arnold Philipp Kontze was DEAD by November 1780** (described as "weiland")
- Arnold Philipp's full title: "Rathsmann und Ackermann" — council member and farmer
- **Johannes was the 2nd son**, confirming Joh. Henrich as 1st son
- Birth order: Joh. Henrich (eldest) → Johannes (2nd) — possibly more sons
- Bride was from Deissel (Deybell), a nearby village
- Arnold Philipp died between November 1773 (alive at son's wedding) and November 1780

#### XXI.3.4 — Marriage #4: Johann Henrich Kontze × Anna Catharina Asthermann (1783)

- **Date:** October 5, 1783
- **Location:** Page 297, Image 1143
- **Groom:** Johann Henrich Kontze, Wittwer, Bürger und Ackermann allier (widower, citizen and farmer here)
- **Bride:** Anna Catharina Asthermannin, Conrad Asthermanns eheliche Tochter von Eberschütz

**Note:** The initial letter of "Asthermann" paleographically reads as "A" in this scribe's hand, though "Osthermann" is more common onomastically. Recorded consistently as "Asthermann/Asthermanns" in both instances.

#### XXI.3.5 — Marriage #5: Johannes Albrecht × Anna Margaretha Kontze (1796)

- **Date:** October 24, 1796
- **Location:** Page 308, Image 1149
- **Groom:** Johannes Albrecht, Bürger und Schneider (citizen and tailor), des weil. ... Jonas Albrecht hinterlassener ehelicher Sohn
- **Bride:** Anna Margaretha, des Bürgers und [trade?] **Johann Philipp Kontzen** eheliche Tochter

**Critical findings:**
- **Johann Philipp Kontze identified as father:** Anna Margaretha was the daughter of Johann Philipp Kontze, a Bürger (citizen) in Trendelburg
- This partially RESOLVES P4: Johann Philipp Kontze was a third contemporary Kontze male alongside elder J.H. Kontze and Arnold Philipp Kontze
- His relationship to the other Kontze lines remains undetermined — possibly a brother of Arnold Philipp, or a son of the elder J.H. Kontze

#### XXI.3.6 — Marriage #6: Joh. Henrich Jungcontze × Anna Catharina Steitz (1797)

- **Date:** May 14, 1797
- **Location:** Page 309, Image 1149
- **Groom:** Joh. Henrich Jungcontze, Wittwer allier (widower from here)
- **Bride:** Anna Catharina, weil. Johs Steitz Bürgers und [Bäckers?] dasier hinterlassene eheliche Tochter

**Critical findings:**
- This is the **THIRD marriage** of Joh. Henrich Kontze the younger — his second wife Anna Catharina Asthermann/Osthermann died before May 1797
- Written as "Jungcontze" (one word), confirming the J-index filing convention
- The 1798 baptism of Joh. Henrich (son of "Johannes Jungkonze, Steindecker," page 103) may represent a child from this third marriage — OR may involve a different "Jungkonze" individual

#### XXI.3.7 — Marriage #7: Christoph Näckel × Anna Margaretha Contze (1797)

- **Date:** September 24, 1797
- **Location:** Page 310, Image 1150
- **Groom:** Christoph Näckel aus Lippoldsberg, weil. Christian Näckel daselbst hinterlassener ehelicher Sohn
- **Bride:** Anna Margarethe, des Bürgers dasier Joh. Henrich Contze eheliche Tochter

Previously known from earlier sessions. The bride is a daughter of Joh. Henrich Contze (no "weiland" prefix), suggesting he was still alive in September 1797.

#### XXI.3.8 — Marriage #8: Joh. Friedrich Koch × Anna Christina Contze (1805)

- **Date:** June 3, 1805
- **Location:** Page 320, Image 1155
- **Groom:** Joh. Friedrich Koch, Soldat im Regiment Linsing, Joh. Peter Koch hiesigen Bürgers ehelicher Sohn
- **Bride:** Anna Christina, **weil.** Joh. Henrich Contze, Bürgers und Ackermanns dasier hinterlassene eheliche Tochter

**Critical findings:**
- Joh. Henrich Kontze is now "weiland" (late) — he died between September 1797 (alive at Näckel wedding) and June 1805
- The bride Anna Christina could be the same Anna Christina born January 6, 1777 (first marriage daughter, now age 28)

#### XXI.3.9 — Marriage #9: Johannes Contze × Maria Elisabeth Grosbernd (1806)

- **Date:** January 12, 1806
- **Location:** Page 321, Image 1155
- **Groom:** Johannes Contze, Wittwer und hiesiger Ackermann (widower and local farmer)
- **Bride:** Maria Elisabeth, weil. Johs Grosbernd hiesiger Bürgers und Mauerers hinterlassene eheliche Tochter (daughter of late Johannes Grosbernd, citizen and mason)

**Critical findings:**
- Johannes Contze's first wife **Anna Margaretha Thielen** died before January 1806
- His second wife is **Maria Elisabeth Grosbernd** — establishing the Grosbernd connection
- Later, Johannes' grandson Andreas Contze also married a **Wilhelmina Grosbernd**, confirming a double Contze-Grosbernd alliance across generations
- Maria Elisabeth's father was a mason (Maurer) — a skilled trade

#### XXI.3.10 — Marriage #10: Johann George Doenges × Anna Catharina Contze (1808)

- **Date:** June 19, 1808
- **Location:** Page 324, Image 1157
- **Groom:** Johann George Doenges, weil. Jonas Doenges hiesigen Bürgers und Schreinermeisterd hinterlassener ehelicher Sohn
- **Bride:** Anna Catharina, **Johs Contze** Bürgers eheliche Tochter

**Critical findings:**
- Johannes Contze is described **without "weiland"** — he is still ALIVE in June 1808
- Anna Catharina is Johannes Contze's daughter (from first wife Anna Margaretha Thielen, as the marriage was 1780 and daughter old enough to marry by 1808)

- **Andreas Contze × Wilhelmina Grosbernd:** Not in this register (1809–1811 checked). Marriage likely occurred in another parish
- **Hennig Albrecht × Anna Margaretha Kontze:** Pre-dates the marriage section start year effectively (the earliest entries are Nov 1773). May be in register 220093 (1637–1773)
- **Joh. Henrich Kontze × Martha Elisabetha Wiechardt first marriage (1773):** FOUND — entry #1 above (it was in the register after all, on a page that appeared after the 1775 entries due to register pagination)

### XXI.4 — Additional Findings from Marriage Section

**Hennig Albrecht confirmed as Bürger und Schmied (citizen and smith):** In the Aug 4, 1805 marriage of his son Johannes Albrecht (soldier), Hennig Albrecht is described as "Bürger und Schmied" (page 320). This resolves his trade — he was both a Pfannenmeister (pan-maker) in 1776 and later described as Schmied (smith).

**Wiechardt family marriages:** Multiple Wiechardt marriages found in 1808–1809, including Johann Friedrich Wichardt (son of Christian Wiechardt, Bürger und Ackermann) and Dionysius Wichardt — confirming the Wiechardt family's continued prominence.

**David Lotz timeline:** David Lotz was already a widower when he married Anna Margaretha Kontze in Feb 1774. He was "Verwalter und Conductor auf der Abgunst" (administrator and conductor at the Abgunst estate). He later married Anna Catharina née Roedde (widow of Marquard) in March 1782, and his son George Lotz (Colonist zu Friedrichsfeld) married in 1798.

### XXI.5 — Revised Family Model

#### XXI.5.1 — Generation 0 (pre-register): Three Kontze patriarchs

| Patriarch | Title | Status | Known children |
|-----------|-------|--------|----------------|
| **Elder Johann Henrich Kontze** | Rathsverwandter | Dead by Feb 1774 | Anna Margaretha (m. David Lotz 1774) |
| **Arnold Philipp Kontze** | Rathsverwandter/Ackermann | Alive Nov 1773; dead by Nov 1780 | Joh. Henrich (eldest), Johannes (2nd) |
| **Johann Philipp Kontze** | Bürger | Unknown death date | Anna Margaretha (m. Johannes Albrecht 1796) |

The relationship between these three men is unknown but they were likely brothers or father-and-sons. All three held civic status in Trendelburg.

#### XXI.5.2 — Generation 1

**Family A: Joh. Henrich Kontze the Younger (eldest son of Arnold Philipp)**
- 1st marriage: Martha Elisabetha Wiechardt (Nov 26, 1773) — 4 known children
- 2nd marriage: Anna Catharina Asthermann/Osthermann (Oct 5, 1783)
- 3rd marriage: Anna Catharina Steitz (May 14, 1797)
- Death: between Sept 1797 and June 1805

**Family B: Johannes Kontze (2nd son of Arnold Philipp)**
- 1st marriage: Anna Margaretha Thielen (Nov 30, 1780)
- 2nd marriage: Maria Elisabeth Grosbernd (Jan 12, 1806)

- Known daughter: Anna Catharina (m. Doenges 1808)

- Military career: Grenadier (1799, 1801), nachgelassener Soldat (discharged) by 1808
