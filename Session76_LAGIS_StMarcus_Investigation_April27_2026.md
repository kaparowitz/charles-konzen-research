## Part 1 — LAGIS Hessische Auswanderer Database

### 1A. Database scope and methodology

The LAGIS module "Hessische Auswanderer" is the authoritative state-archive index of 18th- and 19th-century emigrants from the historical Hessian territories (Hessen-Darmstadt, Hessen-Kassel/Kurhessen). It combines source data from the Marburg, Darmstadt, and Wiesbaden state archives, with 223,000+ individual entries indexable by name, place of origin, occupation, destination, and departure date.

Search interface: `https://www.lagis-hessen.de/de/subjects/index/sn/ha`
Result URL pattern: `https://www.lagis-hessen.de/de/subjects/gsrec/current/N/sn/ha?q={query}`

Two distinct family emigrations from Deisel surface:

**Family A — January 1850 — Ludwig Hildebrand × Catherine Jordan (Rockford IL)**
The three children indexed by LAGIS (Gottlieb 1845, Sophie Amalie 1847, Ludwig Theophil 1849) match the Rockford-Illinois Hildebrand family confirmed via WikiTree: parents Ludwig Hildebrand (b. 27 Dec 1808 Hessen, d. 4 Nov 1897) and Catherine Ann (Jorden) Hildebrand (b. 27 Jul 1811 Hessen, d. 13 Jul 1897). The full sibling list from WikiTree includes Henry, Frederick (= Friedrich, the early-confirmed of KB 218836 1850 Entry 1), Theodore, Laura, Ludwig (Jr. = Ludwig Theophil), Charles William, John, and Gottlieb. The LAGIS entries appear to be incomplete (only 3 of 8 children indexed) — likely the LAGIS source register only listed children of certain ages or those needing emigration permits. Father Ludwig and mother Catherine are NOT individually indexed in LAGIS; they emigrated as the heads of household and would appear in the source register without separate LAGIS records.

**Family B — October 1853 — Wilhelmine Maria Hildebrand b. 1822 with four children**
Four children (Maria 1843, Amalie 1846, Ludwig 1849, Georg Theodor 1851) plus mother Wilhelmine Maria (b. 1822). No husband/father is indexed under "Hildebrand × Deisel × 1853" in LAGIS. This emigration is from a different archive signature (18/4264) than Family A's HG128 signature, confirming they are separate family events.

The "Hillebrand; Hildebrand" double-spelling notation in LAGIS exactly mirrors the spelling pair Wilhelmine née Hildebrand / Hillebrand seen in KB 218842 Entry 195 (Justus's baptism) and KB 218836 1850 Entry 10 (Ernst Wilhelm Köster's confirmation). This is the same Deisel branch's spelling habit.

### 1E. Search 4: Justus Konze and Konze × Deisel — minimal hits

- `Justus Konze`: 0 results
- `Konze Deisel`: 1 unique entry (Ludwig Konze, Deisel, October 1864) — too late by ~5 years to be Charles Gannsen's emigration; biographically inconsistent.

### 1F. LAGIS conclusion

**Justus Köster (or Justus Konze) is NOT documented as an emigrant from Deisel in the Hessian state archive index.** This is strong negative evidence.

Three implications:
1. He may have emigrated through a non-state-permit channel (e.g., as an adult after the Auswanderungspatent rules of 1832 were relaxed, traveling without recording at the home Landratsamt)
2. He may have left Deisel for another Hessian parish (apprenticeship, family relocation) and emigrated from there under different jurisdiction
3. He may have died outside Deisel before emigrating

For the Charles-Gannsen-=-Justus identity hypothesis, this means the German archival paper trail for the emigration itself does not exist. Alongside the absent Deisel confirmation/marriage/burial records, the absent LAGIS emigration record shows that whatever Justus did between 1835 and his emergence in St. Louis ~1859 did not pass through standard documented channels.

## Part 2 — Robert Buecher's St. Marcus Evangelical Church Indices

### 2A. Marriage Index 1847–1865 (Buecher2.htm)

Searched the full transcribed index for Hildebrand / Hillebrand / Hilbrand variants. Single result:

> HILDEBRAND, Hermann August (from St. Louis, Mo.) × KESSLER, Elisabetha Cath. (from St. Louis, Mo.); 9 June 1864; entry # 849

Both spouses listed origin "St. Louis, Mo." — neither shows a German place of origin in this transcription. This Hildebrand has no documented Deisel connection.

### 2B. Burial Index 1847–1893 (Buecher6.htm)

Three Hildebrandt burials:

| Name | Born | Place of birth | Died | Notes |
|------|------|----------------|------|-------|
| Anna née Hildebrandt (m. Andreas Graf) | 15 Sep 1807 | **Osnabrück, Germany** | 4 Dec 1893 | St. Marcus Cemetery [#2359] |
| Maria née Hildebrandt (m. Johann Hesz) | 27 Feb 1819 | **city of Lübeck** | 3 May 1890 | New Pickert [#1899] |
| Johann Hildebrandt | 8 Dec 1827 | **Ranenburg, Baden** | 27 Jun 1887 | St. Marcus Cemetery [#1621A&B] |

**None of these three Hildebrandts is from Hessen — they are from Osnabrück (Lower Saxony), Lübeck (Hanseatic free city), and Baden (southwest German state).** No St. Marcus burial of a Deisel- or Kurhessen-origin Hildebrand surfaces in Buecher's transcribed index.

### 2C. INCIDENTAL FINDING — Charles Gannsen's daughters under the KONZEN surname

The same Buecher burial index revealed an independent confirmation of Charles Gannsen's German identity:

| Name | Born | Died | Husband |
|------|------|------|---------|
| **Josephine née KONZEN** | 22 Feb 1867 | 5 May 1890 | Eduard Herr (#1897) |
| **Auguste née KONZEN** | 8 Apr 1872 | 31 Dec 1890 | James Somers (#1961) |

These match Charles Johnson Sr.'s documented daughters:
- Josephine Maria Johnson (1868–1890, FamilySearch LTSG-XWW) — Buecher's date 22 Feb 1867 vs FS 1868 — likely transcription/profile variance
- Auguste Johnson (1872–1890, FamilySearch L5N2-M8Y) — exact birth match (8 April 1872)

Both daughters were buried under the German maiden surname **KONZEN** at St. Marcus, independently corroborating the surname documented in the 1875 St. Marcus baptism record of Erwin Carl Konzen (= Charles Johnson Jr.). This is now THREE independent St. Marcus records that show "Konzen" as Charles's German surname in St. Louis Lutheran church records (1875 baptism + 1890 burial #1897 + 1890 burial #1961).

### 2D. St. Marcus conclusion

The St. Marcus indices confirm:
1. **No Deisel-origin Hildebrand has been buried at St. Marcus** through 1893. The DeKalb-Street-coincidence Stephen Hildebrand from 1860 (Session 75 finding) is therefore not strengthened by St. Marcus evidence.
2. **Charles Gannsen's German surname KONZEN is confirmed by three independent St. Marcus records.** The "Konzen" identity is settled archivally on the American side.

### 3B. The "where was Justus 1849–1859" question

Justus's absence from EVERY documentable channel — Deisel parish records AND Hessen state emigration permits — narrows the possibilities to:

1. **He left Deisel as a teenager (apprenticeship, schooling, relatives)** to a parish that has not yet been searched, was confirmed there, and emigrated as an adult ~1854–1859 directly from a port (Bremen, Hamburg) without a Hessian Auswanderungspatent. This is consistent with the 1900 census "1859" and Charles Gannsen's known St. Louis arrival.

2. **He emigrated with the 1853 Wilhelmine Maria Hildebrand cohort to America at age 18** — possible if he was effectively a foster son or close kin, traveling as a teenager whose paperwork was bundled with the maternal Hildebrand household. The 1853 emigration is six years before Charles Gannsen's reported 1859 immigration year, but census-self-reported immigration years are notoriously unreliable.

3. **He died as a teenager outside Deisel** — possible but harder to reconcile with the Konzen surname surfacing in St. Louis with a January 1835 birthdate match.

### 3C. The Hildebrand presence in St. Louis remains unresolved

Stephen Hildebrand on DeKalb Street (1860 St. Louis directory) — same street as Charles Gannsen 30 years later — is NOT a Deisel emigrant per the St. Marcus burial evidence and not confirmable as Hessen-born without paid census access. The DeKalb coincidence stands as suggestive but unverified.
