## 1. Search Methodology

### Phase 1: Systematic page-by-page scan (images 1652–1690, years 1796–1812)
Every baptism entry was visually scanned for the surnames Kontze, Konze, Contze, Schildknecht, Gans, Gannsen, and all plausible variants. Each two-page spread was examined at full zoom.

### Phase 2: Spot-check scan (images 1695–1748, years ~1814–1828)
The register's handwriting style changed dramatically around 1814 (new pastor), becoming extremely compressed and cursive. These pages were checked at 5-10 image intervals. Detailed zooming was not performed due to the extreme difficulty of reading the compressed later handwriting.

### Important Note
**No alphabetical index exists for the baptism section of this register** (confirmed via title page). Only the marriage and burial sections have alphabetical indexes. This means every page must be scanned manually.

## 5. PRIMARY FINDING: No "Andreas Kontze" Baptism in 1796–1812

**Despite systematic page-by-page scanning of every baptism entry from 1796 through 1812, NO baptism record for a child named "Andreas" born to a Kontze/Konze/Contze/Schildknecht father was found.**

This confirms and extends the 1798–1800 finding from the prior search session. Identity 5 (Andreas Konze, d. 24 Feb 1828 in Deisel, age 29, b. ~1799) either:

1. Was born **outside Deisel** and moved there later
2. Was born under the surname **"Schildknecht"** — given the documented Schildknecht/Kontze surname confusion (entry 2.1), his baptism could be recorded under "Schildknecht" rather than "Kontze"
3. Had his age at death **recorded inaccurately** (if born before 1796, his baptism would be in the earlier register KB 218821)
4. Had his baptism record **omitted or lost** from this register
5. Was baptized in the **later years 1813–1830** under a different circumstance (though this contradicts his reported age of 29 at death in 1828)

### The Schildknecht Hypothesis (Strengthened)
The discovery of multiple Schildknecht entries in this register (§§3.1–3.6) significantly strengthens the hypothesis that Identity 5 may have been baptized under "Schildknecht." The documented pattern shows:
- Klaus Andreas Kontze was also known as "Schildknecht" (1799)
- Johann Christoph Schildknecht was closely connected to the Kontze family (godparent in 1805)
- Multiple Schildknecht individuals (Johann Conrad, Johann Christoph, and at least two daughters who married Bunkage men) were active in Deisel 1805–1812
- The name confusion in the 1799 entry shows the pastor himself was uncertain which surname applied

**Recommended:** A systematic search of ALL 1796–1812 baptisms for a child named "Andreas" born to a **Schildknecht** father is needed.

## 6. ANALYSIS: Multiple Johann Christoph Kontzes in Deisel

The baptism entries reveal a critical problem: the name "Johann Christoph Kontze" appears as father to children with at least THREE different mothers:

| Mother's Maiden Name | Children (Year) | Images |
|---------------------|-----------------|--------|
| née Koch | Maria Elisabeth (1805), Johann Christoph (1808), Ludwig (1808 Dec) | 1673, 1680, 1681 |
| née Baumann | Johann Friederich (1808 Oct) | 1680 |
| née Kellerbein | Anna Catharina (1811) | 1687 |

Three children in the same year (1808) with two different mothers is impossible for one couple, confirming **at least two men named Johann Christoph Kontze** in Deisel:

**Johann Christoph Kontze "A"** (likely the Grenadier, b. ~1747):
- First wife: Catharina Elisabeth née Baumann (d. ~1789?)
- Second wife: [someone] née Koch
- Children: Maria Elisabeth (1805), Johann Christoph (1808), Ludwig (1808)

**Johann Christoph Kontze "B"** (younger generation, possibly son of "A"):
- Wife: Catharina Elisabeth née Baumann (a different woman with the same name)
- OR: The Grenadier's first wife lived longer than previously thought
- Child: Johann Friederich (1808)
- Possibly also: Anna Catharina (1811, mother née Kellerbein — if "B" remarried)

This distinction is crucial for accurately placing Identity 5 in the family tree.
