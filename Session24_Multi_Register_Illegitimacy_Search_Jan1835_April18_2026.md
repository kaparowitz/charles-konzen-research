## Headline

Session 24 completes the first systematic cross-register scan for ALL male births around January 26, 1835 — the birth date of Charles L. Gannsen — across every available parish register in the Deisel/Trendelburg/Friedrichsfeld cluster. The search was motivated by the hypothesis that Charles may have been an illegitimate child (unehelich) registered under his mother's maiden name, which could be Gannsen/Gans or any other surname. Four registers were evaluated; three were fully scanned. **No match for Charles Gannsen was found in any register.**

Key findings:
1. **Friedrichsfeld (218959): ELIMINATED** — No births at all recorded in January 1835. The register jumps from Entry 39 (born Oct 11, 1834) to Entry 40 (born Feb 25, 1835). **CORRECTION: Session 22's claim that Image 1335 = January 1835 was WRONG — it is January 1834.**
2. **Deisel (218842): 6 male entries found** in the Dec 1834–Feb 1835 range, but none with parents named Konze, Gannsen, or Gans. Most births are early January; entries baptized later (Feb 4, Feb 15) may have late-January birth dates but parents appear to be other Deisel families.
3. **Trendelburg (220108): Previously completed in Session 24 pre-compaction** — 8 entries scanned (121–128), two illegitimate males found and eliminated.
4. **Deisel (218824): NOT APPLICABLE** — register covers only 1796–1831, cannot contain 1835 entries.
