## Significant Collateral Finding — Entry 107 (February 1843)

### Friedrich Konze × Wilhelmine Büngener (Hümme, 1843)

**Entry 107, image 460, Jahr 1843:**

- **Groom:** Friedrich Konze, Tagelöhner (day laborer), born and residing in Hümme
  - Father: **Friedrich Konze** (Ackermann, deceased) and his wife **Marie Sophie, geborene Rock** (also deceased)
  - Age: ~37 years (born ~1806)
  - Confession: Evangelical Reformed

- **Bride:** Wilhelmine Büngener
  - Father: Friedrich Büngener (deceased)
  - Mother: Wilhelmine, geborene Becker (surviving)
  - Age: ~14 years 8 months (born ~1828)

- **Civil registration:** Hofgeismar, 11 February 1843
- **Banns:** 17, 19 [?] 1843; last bann 27 März 1843
- **Marriage:** Hümme, [?] 1843

**Significance:**
- A "Friedrich Konze" (Tagelöhner) is documented in Hümme in 1843, born ~1806, with a father named Friedrich Konze (Ackermann)
- This is a NEW Konze individual distinct from all previously documented Kontze/Konze persons
- Previously documented Hümme Konze: Johannes Kontze (1831 marriage, son of Johann Christoph Kontze, mother from Deisel)
- The 1843 Friedrich Konze has a different father (Friedrich Konze Ackermann) — this may represent another branch of the Konze family in Hümme
- The groom is NOT "Joh. Heinrich Konze" and the bride is NOT Wilhelmine Hildebrand

### Entry 54 (1836) — Daughter of Johannes Kontze marries in Hümme

The bride in Entry 54 (1836, groom Johann Christoph Davis) is described as the daughter of **Johannes Kontze** (Ackermann) and **[Marie Magdalene] Apfelmann** — consistent with the 1831 Johannes Kontze × Marie Magdalene Apfelmann marriage found in Session 61. This confirms the Johannes Kontze family continued to produce daughters who married in Hümme.
