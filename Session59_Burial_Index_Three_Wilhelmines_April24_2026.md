## Headline

Session 59 located the **burial alphabetical surname index in KB 218824** at **Images 1885–1888** — a previously-undiscovered finding that provides direct lookup access to all Konze, Schildknecht, Hildebrand, and other relevant deaths recorded in the Deisel burial section (1796–1831). The most consequential finding from this index:

**"Hillebrant, Wilhelmine — Seite 23"** appears in the burial index (Image 1886, H-section). Since the burial register covers 1796–1831, this means a **Wilhelmine Hildebrand DIED in Deisel before 1831**. She therefore CANNOT be the mother of Entry 195's child (Entry 195 is dated January 1835, so the mother had to be alive then). This is a **third Wilhelmine Hildebrand** distinct from the previously known two:

| # | Wilhelmine Hildebrand | Status | Source |
|---|-----------------------|--------|--------|
| 1 | The "Entry 195 mother" Wilhelmine Hildebrand | Mother of Entry 195 child b. Jan 1835; therefore alive in 1835 | Session 25/26 |
| 2 | The 1817-born unmarried daughter | b. 18 Dec 1817, m. Joh. Heinrich Thöne 1849 ("eheliche Tochter" — too young for Entry 195) | Session 34 |
| 3 | **The pre-1831 deceased "Hillebrant, Wilhelmine" — NEW** | Died before 1831 in Deisel; Seite 23 of burial section | Session 59 |

The existence of three distinct Wilhelmine Hildebrands strengthens the hypothesis that the **Hildebrand family of Deisel was multi-generational** with the name Wilhelmine recurring across cousins/aunts/nieces. Identifying which (if any) is connected to Entry 195's mother by family relationship — and where Entry 195's mother actually came from (since she's not in the Deisel records) — remains the central open question.
