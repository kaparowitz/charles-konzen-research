## Move 1 — Deisel Kirchenbuch-Registerband 1635–1796 (Archion Register 218833), K-section images 1242–1247

Subscriber resolution produced legible Kurrent throughout. The K-section runs images 1240–1247; image 1248 begins the L-section (Losse/Lotze/Lucen/Leiber/Löwer). The section is numbered 1–217 alphabetically, with line numbers visible on every entry.

### Corrections to Session 11 transcription

Session 11 (non-subscriber) mis-identified several Köster and Kuntz entries as "Kontze." The corrections are as follows.

**Image 1243, entry 114 — NOT a Konze.** Correct reading: **Köster, Andreas, Joh. Christoph [Untermüller?] S.** (Moritz, a Größer); × 19.9.1738; ∞ 23.2.1762 m. Niemeyer a. Catharina (+6.3.1812); b.1752; F.21.2.1797. The "Köster" umlaut is clear at subscriber resolution.

**Image 1243, entry 117 — surname is Kuntz, not Kontze.** Correct reading: **Kuntz, Joh. Christoph, Andreas S.** (mother surname: Temmeha[u]sen or Gemmenhausen); × 11.2.1740; ∞.1 28.11.1765 m. Bemmer, Catharina Heidrichs (+21.6.1788); ∞.2 6.8.1789 m. Köster, a. Cath. Petsche; b.1753; †3.12.1817. Surname variant Kuntz/Konze shows up in the same family in the later entry 177 — see below.

**Images 1242 and 1244 produced no genuine Konze entries** (all Koch/Köster/Klaßmann/Kohlkage/Knapp/Klohkuge/Körner and similar K-surnames).

### Confirmed Konze entries (genuine)

**Image 1245, entry 177 — Konze, Johannes.** Father: Joh. Christoph (Kuntz/Konze, same man as entry 117). Mother: from Bemmer family (first marriage). × 21.4.1771; b.1785; †14.12.1838 in Deisel.

**Image 1247, entry 217 — Konze, a.[nna] Cath.[arina?].** Father: Joh. Christoph (B)T. — i.e. daughter of a second (or later) Joh. Christoph Konze line, with mother surname possibly Köster-beh-Petsche (consistent with entry 117's second wife). × 31.10.1793; ∞ 3.4.1814 m. [Kodemann/Köhlemann?] Heinrich; b.1808; F.3.1.1842. **Marginalia at bottom of entry reads: "(× 28.8.1781 ze [Zpp?/Zeyhl?]; +5. 13.12.1868)"** — this is likely the husband's vital data and contains what may be the elusive "Zeyhl" place name.

### Implications

1. The Deisel Kontze line is **continuous and endogamous** from at least 1740 (Joh. Christoph Kuntz b.11.2.1740) through 1793 (Anna Catharina Konze b.31.10.1793), the register's last decade. The surname variant **Kuntz / Konze** is used interchangeably in the same family across successive entries.

2. **The Session 11 "Andreas b.2.1.1717" entry (claimed at image 1242) does not appear on the page at subscriber resolution.** That reading was an artifact of low-resolution Kurrent ambiguity. The earliest clearly-Konze entry in the Registerband is entry 177 (Konze Johannes b.1771).

3. **No "Andreas Konze b.1795"** appears as a K-section entry in the Registerband. The register ends 1796; if Andreas b.1.10.1795 was baptized in Deisel at all, his baptism would be on the last baptism page of the predecessor register 218821 (Deisel KB 1736–1796). His absence from the consolidated index is a flag that either (a) the index was compiled before late-1795 additions, (b) Andreas was born in a filial parish not indexed here, or (c) the 1795 birth itself is mis-attributed to Deisel in the research tree.

4. **Possible "Zeyhl" resolution in entry 217 marginalia.** The parenthetical marginalia on entry 217 may read "× 28.8.1781 ze Z[?]" — at very high zoom the third letter resembles a Z with a vertical stem and two shoulders, but could be S, Y, or a ligature. This is the exact place-abbreviation that has stalled the Session-10 "Zeyhl" inquiry and is the most consequential lead from Move 1.

## Move 3 — Hamburg/Bremen passenger-list framework

Open-web searches confirmed the right pathways but did not return individual hits (which require FamilySearch / Ancestry account searches within the collection).

**Framework established:**

- **Hamburg passenger lists 1850–1934** are on FamilySearch free of charge (browsable) and Ancestry (indexed). For 1850–1854 the originals are arranged alphabetically by first letter of head-of-household surname, making a "K" search efficient. For 1855+ chronological by departure date.
- **Kartei 2** indexes direct lists 1850–1871 and indirect lists 1854–1865 (partial).
- **Bremen lists** for this window were largely destroyed — only 2,953 lists 1920–1939 survive in the Bremen Chamber of Commerce archive. Most 1850s Bremen emigrants must be traced via arrival-side U.S. records.
- **Hessian context:** ~20,000 Hessians emigrated to America in 1854 alone; over 9,000 from Hesse-Kassel (which includes Kurhessen / Deisel). Kurhessen emigration permits are held at HStA Marburg (Auswanderer-Akten).

**Recommended next searches (require Ancestry/FamilySearch login):**

1. Ancestry "Hamburg Passenger Lists 1850-1934" — surname Konze OR Kontze, age 20-30, origin Hessen/Kurhessen/Deisel, date window 1850-1859.
2. FamilySearch "Germany, Hesse, Marburg Emigration Records 1840-1850" for pre-1854 permit holders.
3. Germans-to-America (Glazier-Filby) NARA index 1850-1897 under Konze / Kontze / Gonze / Gannsen / Johnson.
4. St. Louis federal census 1860 and 1870 under all variants, born Hessen ~1829 ±3.
