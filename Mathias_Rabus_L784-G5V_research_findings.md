# Mathias Rabus (FamilySearch L784-G5V) — Archion Research Findings

_Prepared from Archion church-book research + the existing FamilySearch certificates. Compiled to support attaching original parish records, adding a memory, and correcting the Vitals._

## Summary of confirmed identity

Mathias (Matthäus) Rabus was **born c. 1699 in Reuth bei/unter Neuhaus** (a village in the Ettenstatt area, Landkreis Weißenburg‑Gunzenhausen, Mittelfranken, Bavaria), **son of the brewer Philipp Rabus** and Anna Barbara Dietrich. He was himself a **Bierbräuer und Wirt (brewer and innkeeper)** in Reuth. He **married Margaretha Barbara née Baum** (a widow of Joh. Rötter) on **5 July 1729 in the Evangelical‑Lutheran church at Bergen**, and **died / was buried 24 December 1777 in Bergen, aged 78** (a widower and pensioner).

This is solidly established from the 1957 parish certificates already attached to the FamilySearch profile (Trauzeugnis, Sterbezeugnis, and the children's Geburts‑ und Taufzeugnisse), plus direct reading of the Bergen church registers on Archion (the son Georg Michael Rabus, innkeeper in Bergen, is recorded buried 17 July 1779 — confirming the family in these books, and that the surname reads clearly as "Rabus").

## Key correction to the parish assumption

The FamilySearch birthplace "Reuth unter Neuhaus" caused the initial search to go to **Ettenstatt** parish, but the family's documented adult life — marriage (1729), all children's baptisms (1730s–40s), and death (1777) — is in **BERGEN** parish (Dekanat Weißenburg i.Bay.). Mathias was *born* in Reuth and *moved to* Bergen.

## Death record — transcription & translation (Bergen Bestattungen 1765–1811, Bild 36, right page)

**German (as written):**
> "Den 24. X[broo]. wurde Matthäus Rabus, margräfl[icher] Schutzverwandter [Bräuer/Wirt] allhier, mit einer Predigt begraben. aet[atis] 78 Jahr. starb an der [Brustkrankheit / Flüsse — word uncertain]. [Leichentext:] Luc[as] 2, 28–30."

**English:**
> "On 24 December [1777] Matthäus Rabus, margravial protected‑subject [brewer/innkeeper] here [in Bergen], was buried with a sermon. Aged 78 years. Died of [a chest ailment — uncertain]. [Funeral text:] Luke 2:28–30."

Year is confirmed as **1777** by chronology (these late‑December entries flow into the "Ao 1778" heading on the following leaves) and by the 1957 Sterbezeugnis (24 Dec 1777, age 78). Age 78 ⇒ born **c. 1699**.

## Marriage — status (important)

The Bergen marriage book's year mapping is **offset from the image numbers** (decorative "Anno 1728" sits on image 51, "Anno 1729" begins at the foot of image 53). Reading the actual 1729 summer leaves:

- **Image 52 = 1728** — contains the marriage of **Matthäus Petz of Ettenstatt to Anna Maria Rabus**, recorded as *"die Braut wurde in Reuth geboren,"* daughter of the **brewer Philipp Rabus**. This is **Mathias's sister** — strong independent confirmation of the family, the father (brewer Philipp Rabus), and the Reuth origin.
- **Image 54 = summer 1729** (the page you uploaded as "Bild 54"). Every entry read: 10 May (bride Barbara, m. Kaltenbuch); Joh. Christoph Vollmann; Dom. Exaudi (Adam Moringer); **Christian Moriz × Elisabetha Borns** (wed 12 July); **Melchior Spörr × Maria Margaretha Hartmann**; Andreas Schmidt (Dom. V–VII p. Trin.). **No Matthäus Rabus.**

**Conclusion:** Mathias's own marriage to the widow **Margaretha Barbara née Baum (widow of Joh. Rötter)** is not in the Bergen register's 1729 section. The likeliest explanation is that the wedding of a **widow** was held in **her** parish of residence (the Rötter family appears tied to Döllern/Kaltenbuch), and the 1957 Bergen Trauzeugnis is a derived certificate. To find the original I'd need to search the bride's parish — or re‑check the exact wording of the Trauzeugnis.
