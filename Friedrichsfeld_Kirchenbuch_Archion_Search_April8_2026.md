**The Friedrichsfeld Kirchenbuch 1830-2011 was successfully located on Archion.** It is listed as a SEPARATE parish under Kirchenkreis Hofgeismar — NOT under Trendelburg. This is why the previous Archion search (which searched for "Trendelburg") did not find it. The register contains baptisms from 1830-1868, covering the critical window for the Charles Gannsen investigation.

The known 1844 Konze entry was confirmed in the register (image 1367), validating that this is the correct source. However, systematic browsing of the 1830-1836 baptism pages was not possible due to non-linear microfilm image ordering.

### Successful Search Path

1. All Archives → **Hessen**
2. → **Landeskirchliches Archiv der Evangelischen Kirche von Kurhessen-Waldeck**
3. → **Kirchenkreis Hofgeismar**
4. → **Friedrichsfeld** (listed as its own parish, separate from Trendelburg)

**Key discovery:** Friedrichsfeld has its OWN parish entry in the Archion hierarchy, distinct from Trendelburg. This reflects the historical reality that Friedrichsfeld had its own Kirchenbuch even though it was a hamlet of Trendelburg (merged in 1970).

## 6. WHAT WAS NOT FOUND (LIMITATIONS)

### 1830-1836 Baptism Pages Not Located
Due to the non-linear microfilm order, the baptism pages covering 1830-1836 (approximately pages 1-15) could not be reliably located. The images surrounding the known anchor points jump between different years and sections unpredictably.

### Baptism Name Index Not Fully Examined
The cover page states that indexes follow each section ("Register jeweils nach den Amtshandlungen"). An index page with the letter "A" was found at approximately image 1445, but the "K" section was not located due to the non-linear image arrangement.
