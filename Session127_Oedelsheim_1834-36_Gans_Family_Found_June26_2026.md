# Session 127 — Oedelsheim 1834–1836 Baptism Sweep: an ACTIVE GANS FAMILY found
## Date: June 26, 2026

## Objective
Close the Gans question left open in Session 126 by doing the **full leaf-by-leaf
sweep of Oedelsheim's 1834–1836 baptisms** (Archion churchRegister **219946**),
hunting for any baptized Gans/Kontze child and specifically a **Carl Gans born
~26 Jan 1835** (Charles).

## Method
The reel is **shuffled** (image order ≠ year) so I anchored on each leaf's written
**"Jahr 18XX" header** (read from the AI "Erkannter Text" panel) and swept the
contiguous block leaf-by-leaf. The main chronological run for the window is
**Bild 673–693 (1834 → 1836)**, verified continuous (Bild 670=1832, 671–672=1833,
673–677=**1834**, 678–686=**1835**, 687–693=**1836**, 694=1837); the only
out-of-place leaves seen were *older* (Bild 689 = a displaced 1830 leaf; cf. S126
Bild 705 = 1831), i.e. early leaves misfiled forward — not 1834–36 leaves. Every
leaf was regex-scanned for Gans/Kontze/Konze surnames and Carl/Karl.

## ⭐ HEADLINE FINDING — the Gans surname is ALIVE in Oedelsheim in 1835
Session 126 had found "Gans" only as godparents and hypothesised (per the
Oedelsheim research log) that the OSB Konze/Gans pool had left before the 1840s.
**That is now overturned.** There is a documented **Gans baptism in 1835**:

> **Andreas August Gans** — male, **born 25 Feb 1835, baptized 15 March 1835**,
> Oedelsheim, Hausnummer 2 (Bild 679 = Seite 28; **permalink
> https://www.archion.de/p/075cc0099a**). Father **Georg Friedrich Gans,
> Drechslermeister** (master turner); mother **Johanne Marie Elisabeth née
> Kohlschein**. Godfather **Carl August Gans**, son of the **Gastwirth (innkeeper)
> Andreas Gans**. Image-verified (surname "Gans" underlined in the child column;
> father "Georg Friedrich Gans" legible in the Eltern column).

So Oedelsheim has **at least three Gans men** in the 1830s:
- **Georg Friedrich Gans**, Drechslermeister (turner) — father of Andreas August Gans (1835).
- **Andreas Gans**, Gastwirth (innkeeper) — father of Carl August Gans.
- **Carl August Gans** (the innkeeper's son) — adult by 1835 (acts as godparent ⇒ born ~1810–1818).
- (a **George Andreas Gans**, Stellmacher/wheelwright, also appears as a godparent).
"Gans" recurs as godparents on several 1834–1836 leaves (e.g. Bild 676, 682, 683).

## The Charles question (Carl Gans b. 26 Jan 1835): still NO direct match
Across the **fully-read 1834–1836 window** there is **no boy with rufname
Carl/Karl on a Gans (or Kontze/Konze) surname**, and **no Gans child born
26 Jan 1835**:
- **1834** (Bild 673–677): no Gans/Kontze child; Carls all local — **Carl Ludwig
  Wessel** (Mar 1834), Carl August, Christian Carl Tichtus (illegit.), etc.
- **1835** (Bild 678–686): the only Gans child is **Andreas August Gans (b. 25 Feb
  1835)** — wrong forename, and 30 days after Charles's 26 Jan. **January 1835 was
  read in full** (boys: George Wilhelm Gerland, **George Heinrich Westerman b. 24
  Jan 1835**, Friedrich Carl Wessel ~25 Feb) — none a Carl Gans. The "Carl August
  Gans" who appears is the innkeeper's grown son (godparent), far too old.
- **1836** (Bild 687–693): no Gans/Kontze child; Carls local (Kraut, Lindner…).

So **Charles is not, on the documentary record, the 1835 Oedelsheim Gans baptism.**

## Why this still matters a great deal
The project's leading-surname for the "…Gansen/Ganson/Gannsen" branch now has a
**concrete, living family in exactly the right place and decade**. Charles
(b. 26 Jan 1835, surname → "Gannsen") could plausibly be:
- a child of one of these Gans households recorded under a year/spelling not yet
  checked (the register is shuffled and the alphabetical **K-style/G index leaf
  is faded** — a Gans entry could be mis-shelved), or
- a Gans child whose US birth-date (26 Jan 1835) is a near-miss for **Andreas
  August Gans's 25 Feb 1835** (name mismatch makes this unlikely but not impossible),
  or a sibling in an adjacent year.

**Oedelsheim moves from "probably negative / open" to a PRIORITY parish for a full
Gans-family reconstruction.**
