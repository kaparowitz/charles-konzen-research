# Session 41 — April 22, 2026
## Continued Scan of Deisel Beerdigungen 1876–1956 (1883–1892)

---

## Headline

Session 41 continued the systematic page-by-page scan of the post-1876 Deisel burial register (Register 218854) from Image 31 through Image 57, covering entries 213–415 and years 1883–1892. **Key results**: (1) **7 direct Konze/Kunze burial entries found** — including 3 adult patriarchs/wives and 4 children/stillbirths; (2) **9 "geb. Konze" references** — Konze daughters who married into other Deisel families; (3) **New numbered patriarchs identified**: Philipp Kunze I., Philipp Kunze 2s, Philipp Theodor Kunze, Heinrich Kunze 3s, Ludwig Kunze 2s, Ludwig Theodor Kunze, Christoph Wilhelm Kunze; (4) **Multiple Hillebrand family references** tracked; (5) **J.H. Konze #2 (Johann Heinrich Konze, born ~1808, Ackermann, married Wilhelmine née Hildebrand) was STILL NOT FOUND** — the scan has now covered 1876–1892 without locating his death record. If born ~1808, he would have been 68–84 during this range. His death either occurred in the earlier 1830–1876 register (possibly missed) or he died/moved elsewhere.

### The Aschbraff Connection
Three Konze wives were née Aschbraff:
1. Karoline née Aschbraff → married Philipp Kunze I.
2. Wilhelmine née Aschbraff → married Ludwig Kunze 2s
3. Philippina Charlotte Amalie née Aschbraff → married Heinrich Kunze 3s

Plus Marie Amalie née Aschbraff → married Philipp Theodor Kunze. This suggests a close alliance between the Konze and Aschbraff families, possibly with multiple sisters marrying multiple Konze men.

## J.H. Konze #2 — Status Update

**Johann Heinrich Konze #2** (born ~1808, Ackermann in Deisel, married Wilhelmine née Hildebrand) remains the primary search target. After scanning:
- 1830–1876 register: NOT FOUND (Session 37–38)
- 1876–1892 in post-1876 register: NOT FOUND (Sessions 40–41)

**Possible explanations**:
1. His death entry was in the 1830–1876 register but was missed during the scan (the index showed only entries under "Konze" but he could be listed under a variant spelling or the entry was damaged)
2. He moved away from Deisel before death (to another Kurhessen parish)
3. He died in a hospital or institution elsewhere and was recorded in that parish
4. The "Hildebrand" wife connection is misidentified and he's actually one of the patriarchs we've found under a different wife's name
5. His death entry is still ahead in the register (post-1892) — theoretically possible if he lived past 84, but unlikely

**Note on Heinrich Konze numbering**: We've now found Heinrich Konze III (entry 373) and Heinrich Konze V (entry 168). J.H. Konze #2 could be designated as Heinrich Konze II in the parish records. His death entry, if present, would list him as "Heinrich Konze 2s" or "Heinrich Konze II." with wife Wilhelmine née Hildebrand.
