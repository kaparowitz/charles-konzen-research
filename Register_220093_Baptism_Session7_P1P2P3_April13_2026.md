## 1. P1 — Re-read page 48 (image 733) with higher zoom + pan

**Outcome:** Confirmed the Session 6 transcription. No new data extracted.

- The entry on page 48 reads, in relevant part: **"Andreas Kontze, des Nies Kontzen Sohn, die Mutter Catharina ..."** followed on the next line by **"Pate Andreas Kontze"** (godfather Andreas Kontze).
- Catharina's maiden name is **not given** in the entry as written — the surname column does not record it for the mother, so this branch of the search is effectively closed unless a marriage record surfaces (see §2).
- Re-examination of the Kontze baptism index (captured this session from image 835) shows the baby being baptized on page 48 in 1722 is one of three "Andreas" Kontze baptisms in the register (48, 117, 185). Reading the page 48 entry as naming the BABY Andreas, the phrase "des Nies Kontzen Sohn" attaches to the father (Andreas Jr., Gen 1), not the baby.
- **Revised three-generation reading of page 48:**
  - **Gen 0 — Nies (Dionysius) Kontze Sr.** — grandfather of the baby (named only via genitive "des Nies Kontzen").
  - **Gen 1 — Andreas Kontze** — father of the baby; wife Catharina.
  - **Gen 2 — baby Andreas Kontze** — baptized March 1722; the "Pate Andreas Kontze" (godfather) is almost certainly Andreas Sr., the great-grandfather patriarch — still alive in 1722.

## 2. P2 — Marriage register — Andreas Kontze × Eva Rosina Engel

**Outcome:** **Decisive hit.** The marriage index on image 860 pairs **"Andreas Kontze"** with **"Eva Rosina [Engel]"** as a married couple.

This is the most important finding of Session 7. Combined with P1, the likely narrative is:

1. Baby Andreas Kontze (Gen 2) is baptized on page 48 in 1722 (son of Andreas Kontze Jr. Gen 1 × Catharina).
2. He grows up in Trendelburg.
3. In the 1740s he marries Eva Rosina Engel (per the marriage index). Eva Rosina Engel matches a bride-family seen in the page 48 neighborhood (entry 4 on page 48 references "Eva Rosina, des Conrad Engels Tochter" — her baptism was literally ON THE SAME PAGE as his, in 1722).
4. By 1745 he is serving as godfather under the form **"Andreas Kontzen von Zeyhl"** (image 765) — having relocated to the neighboring hamlet of Zeyhl.

The Andreas × Eva Rosina pairing is the bridge that connects the 1722 Trendelburg baby to the 1745+ Zeyhl adult Kontze, and plausibly to the 18th-century Kontze presence outside Trendelburg.

Other Kontze-adjacent pairings noted on image 860: Dionysius Temme × Anna Elisabeth Schaub; Nicolaus Wilhelm Holtzgrebe × Maria Elisabeth Uffelmann — reinforcing the Kontze-Uffelmann-Schaub-Temme cluster of allied Trendelburg families.

## 3. P3 — Burial register — structure mapped, one Kontze burial identified

### 3.2 Kontze/Jungkontze burial-index hits

- **Kontze Maria [Christina?] — page 397** (index #2, image 920). This is a Kontze FEMALE burial — possibly Andreas × Eva Rosina's widow, or a daughter, or an elderly Kontze woman. This entry is in the LATE pages of the burial register, so likely a post-1760 death.
- **Jungkontze Joh. Georg Elisabeth — pages 363, 373** (index #1, image 907). "Jungkontze" literally means "Young Kontze" — a nickname applied to distinguish the younger generation. Joh. Georg × Elisabeth appears to be a married couple, both buried.
- **Jungkontze Joh. Georg — page 398** (index #2, image 920) — likely a second Joh. Georg, perhaps a son of the earlier one.

### 3.3 What's missing — Andreas Sr.'s specific burial

The name "Andreas Kontze" does NOT appear in either burial index under K. Three explanations:
1. **Andreas Sr. died before the burial register begins (pre-1708).** Unlikely — he was alive in March 1722 (godfather entry on page 48).
2. **Andreas Sr. died OUTSIDE Trendelburg parish** — possibly in Zeyhl or another nearby village, and so would appear in a different register.
3. **Andreas Sr. is listed under "Jungkontze"** — unlikely since Jungkontze entries are paired with Joh. Georg.

The most probable scenario is #2: Andreas Sr. or later Andreases relocated (to Zeyhl or elsewhere) and their death records are in a different parish register. The single unequivocal Kontze burial located in this register is "Kontze Maria [Chr...?] 397" — gender female.

### 3.4 Recommended next read

Navigate to **burial-register page 397** (approx image 945+ in the later section) to read the Maria [Christina?] Kontze burial entry — age at death, relationship, and date would establish whether she is Eva Rosina × Andreas's daughter, wife, or unrelated. Also read pages 363, 373, 398 for the Jungkontze entries — their ages and relationships would clarify whether "Jungkontze" is a branch derived from the Trendelburg Kontzes or a different family entirely.
