Session 97 — Trendelburg 220096: Taufen Index Discovery, Sweep Completion, Koch–Contzin Resolution

Date: June 7, 2026 Register: Archion 220096 (Trendelburg KB 1773–1818), logged in via Chrome Goal: (1) Check images 1223–1228 for alphabetical Taufen index; (2) sweep Taufen 1807–1818 for more children of grenadier Joh. Christoph Conze; (3) download image 1059 for the evidence archive.

Headline results

  1. The alphabetical Taufen index EXISTS — but at the END of the Taufen section (images 1095–1102), not at 1223. Images 1220–1224 hold only the deaths/burials index.
  2. The index leaf containing K(continuation)–P was never filmed (verified in both filming passes). The Contze entries indexed "s. auch K!" — including, almost certainly, Anton Wilhelm (S.151) and Anna Margaretha (S.175) — are on that missing leaf.
  3. The C section still lists EIGHT Contze baptisms with Seite refs, five of them previously unknown, three pre-1800 (the parents' generation).
  4. Konfirmationen bonus (S.224–225): two Kontze confirmations 1776/1777 naming TWO Kontze householders of the 1760s–70s — Arnold Philip Kontze and Andreas Kontze sen., both Rathsverwandte (town councilmen).
  5. Session 96's "geb. Contzin / Rgmt v. Biesenroth" mystery resolved: the couple is Joh. Friedrich KOCH (Soldat, Rgmt von Biesenroth) ⚭ Anna Christina geb. Contzin; son Joh. Henrich Koch b. 6 Aug 1806 (S.149–150). The same couple as 1809's "Hock" find — read Koch.
  6. Image 1059 PDF download blocked by repeated HTTP 503 from Archion's download endpoint (retry next session).

Task 2 — Sweep completion (images 1092–1095, S.216–222)

  • S.216–217: 1817 year-total = "11 Knaben und 15 Mädchen, in Sa. 26 Kinder"; early 1818 (Aderholz, Klages/Hase, Schäfer, Graff, Grossbernd) — no Conze.
  • S.218–219 (Feb–Jun 1818): Uffelmann, Wede, Heuser/Nolte, Evers, Geilfuss, Gudel, Grossbrand/Geise — no Conze.
  • S.220–221 (Jun–Nov 1818): Selle, Brauner, Rüssel/Stütz, Evers/Weisse, Rode (×2), Somman, Kranz/Gosmann, Jäger (Invalide, Leib-Bataillon — wife geb. NOT Conze), Krügel — no Conze. False positive zoom-checked: "Maria Elise geb. Greuze" (Rode entry; clear G-r, not Conze).
  • S.222: register's last baptism 12 Dec 1818 (Carolina Charlotta, Brand/Rode); closing note: 1818 = "16 Knaben und 14 Mädchen, in Sa. 30 Kinder"; new book begins 1819.

Sweep verdict (1807–1818 complete): exactly THREE children of grenadier/entlassener Soldat Joh. Christoph Conze ⚭ Anna Catharina geb. Pfeil in this register: Anton Wilhelm (b. 12 Sep 1806), Anna Margaretha (b. 25 Mar 1811), Johann Carl Ludwig (b. 21 May 1814). The bridge to Charles Gannsen b. 26 Jan 1835 must run through the Anschlussband (220108, Taufen 1830–1863) and/or the 1819–1829 register.

Konfirmationen bonus (image 1105 = S.224–225)

Verbatim, zoom-verified:

Ostern 1776, #5: "Andreas, des Rathsverwandten Arnold Philip Kontzens ehel. Sohn, alt 15 Jahre" → Andreas Kontze b. ~1761, very likely our later Bürger/Ackermann Andreas Conze (⚭ Wilhelmina geb. Grosbernd, son Johannes b. 1814).

Ostern 1777, #3: "Johann Friedrich, des Rathsverwandten H. Andreas Kontzen ehel. ältester Sohn, alt 13½ Jahr" → Joh. Friedrich Kontze b. ~1763/64, very likely our Ackermann Joh. Friedrich Contze.

So the 1770s Trendelburg Kontze elders: Arnold Philip Kontze and Andreas Kontze sen., both Rathsverwandte — the probable grandfather generation for grenadier Joh. Christoph Conze (b. ~1775–1785, confirmation expected ~1789–1799 in S.230ff).

S.148–151 resolution (images 1058–1059)

  • Image 1059 = S.150–151 (not 151–152), both "ferner Getaufte 1806".
  • S.149 (image 1058) bottom: "Aug: 10. Joh: Henrich, Joh: Friedrich Koch Soldat [bey…" — margin "†18 14/7 73" (d. 14 Jul 1873) — continuing onto S.150 top: "…bey dem Rgmt von Biesenroth und Ehefrau Anna Christina geb. Contzin Söhnlein, geboren am 6ten Nachmittags zwischen 2 und 3 Uhr. Gevatter: Joh. Henrich, Joh. Peter Koch hiesigen Bürgers ehel. Sohn." → Joh. Henrich Koch, b. 6 Aug 1806, bp. 10 Aug 1806, † 14 Jul 1873. Mother = Contze daughter (C-index "Anna Christina 118"?). Same couple as the 1809 "Hock" baptism (Wilhelmina, b. 19 Nov 1809) — surname is Koch.
  • The other "Rgmt v. Biesenroth" on S.151 is unrelated: godfather of the Lucas entry ("Gevatter: Joh. Valentin [Krug?], Soldat bey dem Rgmt v. Biesenroth").
  • Corrections to Session 96: the geb.-Contzin tail sits at top of S.150 (start bottom S.149), not S.152; the husband is Koch, not an unknown soldier; "Rgmt v. Wiesenroth" reads Biesenroth.
  • Anton Wilhelm's entry re-verified in full context at S.151 top: "17 [Sep]. Anton Wilhelm, Joh: Christoph Conze Grenadier unter dem Leib-Grenadier-Bataillon und Ehefrau Anna Catharina geb. Pfeilin von Homberg Söhnlein, geboren am 12ten Morgens gegen 10 Uhr. Gevatter: Anton Pfeil Landbereiter zu Carlshaven."

Session 97 continuation — C-index direct lookups (same day)

TWO MORE CHILDREN of the target couple (zoom-verified verbatim)

1. Maria Elisabeth Contze — S.107–108 (images 1037/1038, "ferner Getaufte 1799"):

"14. [Apr] Maria Elisabeth, Christoph Contze, Soldat bey dem Hochlöbl. Leib Gren: Bataillon des 1ten Garde Rgmts und Ehefrau Catharina // [S.108:] geb. Pfeilin von Homberg Töchterlein, geboren am 3ten Nachmittags um 12 Uhr. Gevatterin Maria Elisabeth, Valentin Dickhaut Schreiners zu Homberg Ehefrau." Margin: "Gestorben am 12ten Juni(?) 1834."

→ b. 3 Apr 1799, bp. 14 Apr 1799 — FIRST known child, seven years before Anton Wilhelm. Marriage Christoph ⚭ Catharina Pfeil therefore ≤1798. Note regiment formula here: "Leib-Gren.-Bataillon des 1. Garde-Rgmts."

2. Maria Wilhelmina Contze — S.123 (image 1045):

"20. Maria Wilhelmina, Joh: Christoph Contze Grenadiers unter dem Leib Grenadier Bataillon und Ehefrau Catharina geb. Pfeilin von Homberg Töchterlein, geboren am 15ten Nachmittags um 1 Uhr. Gevatterin Anna Catharina, Johs Contze Bürgers und Ackerm: dahier ehel. Tochter."

→ Godmother = daughter of Johannes Contze (Ackermann) — direct kinship bridge between the grenadier and the Johannes-Contze household. Date: b. 15 Sep 1801, bp. 20 Sep 1801 per the site's earlier exact-date pull (konze-family-by-town, S.123) — my in-session "Aug 1800" inference was wrong: I never panned to the 1045 year header, and the census file independently dates the S.120 Jungconze twins to 1801, putting S.120–123 in 1801. The verbatim text above stands; only my month/year inference is corrected.

RECONCILIATION (post-session, against the site's prior person-level census): the konze-family-by-town table already carried this family in full from earlier pulls — Maria Elisabeth (1799), Maria Wilhelmina (1801), Johannes (b. 17 Jun 1804, bp. 24 Jun 1804, S.139 — the C-index "Johannes 139", already resolved; presumably = the child d. 4 Jan 1808 aged 3½), Anton Wilhelm (1806), Maria Carolina Joannette (b. 23 Jan 1809, S.163 · img 1065), Anna Margaretha (1811). My continuation's "new finds" #1–2 were independent re-verifications with verbatim transcriptions (valuable as such), and the Koch ⚭ Contzin couple was likewise already documented (m. 3 Jun 1805, S.320; she = Anna Christina Kontze b. 6 Jan 1777, dau. of Johann Henrich Kontze). NEW-to-project from this session remain: the St. Louis identification (Session 96), the Sep-1806 date correction, the Taufen-index discovery, the two Konfirmationen (incl. the Johann-Friedrich paternity resolution), and the Biesenroth-tail resolution. Father's regiment note: the 1806 Koch baptism says "Rgmt von Biesenroth" while the 1805 Koch marriage (S.320) says "Rgmt v. Linsing" — regimental renaming/transfer, worth a footnote, not a conflict. HETRINA caveat: the site dates the grenadier father "b. ~1747" (HETRINA) — under-discussed tension with a man fathering children to 1814; flag for review (two Joh. Christophs?).
