Session 86 — Y-DNA review: YFull sample YF147577 + FTDNA Big Y

Date: 6 June 2026 · live, signed-in YFull + FTDNA Discover; YFull STR panel supplied by Jed. Sample: Charles's tested male-line descendant = YFull YF147577 (= the "new" sample on the public I-Y38863 tree); FTDNA Big Y-700 kit 1032621.

Goals (all four requested)

Confirm haplogroup position · mine the match list · interpret the Ashkenazi finding · log it in the research.

1. Haplogroup position — CONFIRMED, two labs agree

  • Terminal subclade: I-Y38863 (I2 › I-M223 line). FTDNA terminal = I-Y38863 (parent I-BY2640, per the Discover Time Tree). YFull v14.03 places YF147577 on the identical I-Y38863 (formed ~750 ybp, TMRCA ~700 ybp ≈ 1300 CE). FTDNA Discover dates the branch ~1400–1450 CE. Two independent trees, same subclade, compatible medieval coalescence.
  • STR panel (YF147577, ~780 markers, supplied 6 June): unremarkable I2-M223 modal haplotype — DYS393=13, DYS390=22, DYS19=16, DYS391=10, DYS385=16-17, DYS426=11, DYS388=13, DYS439=11, DYS389I=13, DYS392=13, DYS389II=29. No rare or null markers; a few multi-copy markers (DYS464, DYS458, DYS449) not called (normal). Adds no conflicting signal. (The raw upload could not be persisted into the repo — a transient uploads-mount issue left a 0-byte placeholder at peter_charts/STR_for_YF147577_20260606.csv; re-drop the file if you want the full panel stored. Core values are recorded here and in dna.html.)

2. Match list — mined on both platforms; all Ashkenazi, no Konze

  • YFull I-Y38863 branch = exactly 3 members: YF147577 (Charles's descendant) · YF078713 — Poland [PL-14], carrying YFull's "yid" (Ashkenazi) origin tag · YF002081. So the only two men on Charles's exact YFull branch are an Ashkenazi Pole + one further sample.
  • FTDNA Big Y matches (9, retrieved 30 May 2026 CSV — no closer matches added since):
  • Same terminal I-Y38863: Mendelson — Poland, EKA "Zadok Mendelsson (Ashkenazi) b.~1840", GD 7, 10/556 Big-Y STR diff (closest match).
  • Parent clade I-BY2640: Smith — Lithuania, EKA "Peretz Wizansky b.1856", GD 8.
  • I-S23612 cluster: Kleinmann (Austria), Lamm (Germany, EKA "Moses Lamm b.~1640"), Åström (Sweden), Richmond (unknown).
  • I-M223: Povolotskiy, Zivy (unknown origin).
  • The YFull Polish sample (YF078713) and the FTDNA Mendelson/Poland match are the same Poland/Ashkenazi signal reached on two trees.
  • No Konze / Köster / Hessian-village surname appears on either platform. The named earliest-known ancestors (Zadok Mendelsson, Peretz Wizansky, Moses Lamm) are unambiguously Jewish.

3. Interpretation

  • Charles's strict paternal line is Ashkenazi-Jewish, east-central European, beneath and separate from the documented Hessian-Evangelical Konze family. Three independent Ashkenazi lines coalesce with his ~1400–1450 CE.
  • Crucial limit: even the closest match (Mendelson) shares a common ancestor ~600–700 years back (≈15–20+ generations). The Y-DNA therefore establishes the ancestral population, not Charles's specific 1835 father — it cannot, by itself, name a biological father or grandfather.
  • Best explanation: an undocumented-paternity (non-paternity) event somewhere up the line — which would also explain the central mystery, why no Konze baptism for Charles has ever been found (the biological patriline may not be the documented Konze family). Dovetails with the long-standing January-1835 illegitimacy hypothesis (an unknown biological father) — the reason the June 2026 parish sweep was widened to unwed-mother births.
  • This is not a new theory but independent biological confirmation of the (set-aside, name-based) 2025 Hessian-Jewish inquiry — reached by genetics rather than the weak "Gans → Gannsen" surname resemblance; it does not revive the eliminated surname-Gans candidates.

4. Logged

  • dna.html: added a "YFull · sample YF147577" card (the 3-member branch, the Polish co-member, the STR-panel summary). The page's existing Ashkenazi-cluster analysis already matches these findings.
  • This session note; changelog entry cl-2026-06-06-ydna-yfull.

Actionable takeaway

The DNA lead is now essentially read out: it pins the deep paternal origin (Ashkenazi) and constrains the record hypotheses (non-paternity / illegitimacy), but it will not identify the 1835 father without a much closer Y-match. The single highest-yield DNA action remains recruiting a documented male-line Konze descendant from the Trendelburg area to Big-Y test — a match would prove the surname line; a non-match would confirm the non-paternity reading. Passive monitoring of YFull/FTDNA for a new close match (especially anyone with a Hessian or Konze paper trail) is the low-effort complement.
