Session 87 — Western / northern fringe + cross-border parishes (the unsearched left & top of the map)

Date: 6 June 2026 · live Archion (signed in) + Matricula (free/public). Trigger: map review — the upper-left, left, and northern fringe of the parish map had never been searched because it falls outside the Evangelical Kreis-Hofgeismar net (different confessions, different states). Same criteria as the June 4 sweep: a MALE baptism ~10 Jan–28 Feb 1835 (target 26 Jan), surname in the Konze pool OR illegitimate.

A. Kurhessen — west & north (Archion)

Parish Register Result
Bad Karlshafen (Lutheran) Taufen 1830–1895 (218677) NEGATIVE — 1835 window (img 530–533) clean.
Bad Karlshafen (Reformed — French + German congregations merged from 1830) Taufen 1830–1857 (218713) One window hit, then ruled out: img 403, Nr. 123 — Johann Heinrich Thielemann, male, illegitimate, b. 8 Feb 1835 (Hafenstraße 18), bapt. 22 Mar 1835, mother Elisabeth Thielemann. Marginal note: legitimized 31 Aug 1838 when Joh. Heinrich Carl Schlosshahn married the mother and declared paternity; child thereafter named Schlosshahn, locally documented to 1839. → A documented German father + legitimization + wrong date/name = not Charles (kept on record as the only window-compatible illegitimate male in the region).
Hombressen Taufen 1830–1850 (219487) NEGATIVE — window entries: legitimate males 21 Jan & 25 Feb; the one illegitimate is female (†May 1835).
Haueda (filial, own book from 1830) Taufen 1830–1928 (219154) NEGATIVE — window males all legitimate (Eble, Bolte); no unehelich, no pool surname.
Ostheim (Kirchenkreis Hofgeismar) Taufen 1830–1914 (219970) NEGATIVE — window (img 1045, Seite 27): one stillborn male Jan 1835 (unnamed, unbaptized — not a candidate by definition); G. L. Becker b. 21 Jan legitimate; no pool/illegitimate male.
Niedermeiser Taufen 1830–1877 (219877) NEGATIVE — no male baptism at all between 11 Jan and 1 Mar 1835 (entries 108–111 all female).
Liebenau (Hesse) + Grimelsheim (presumed filial) — COVERAGE HOLE. Archion holds Liebenau only to 1777 (registers 219733, 219736); nothing for 1835. Plain Ortssuche doesn't even surface the Hessian Liebenau (only Saxon/Hanoverian namesakes). 1835 cannot be checked online → needs Landeskirchliches Archiv Kassel or FamilySearch microfilm. Logged as a new open gap.

B. Across the border — Prussian Westphalia, Catholic (Matricula, Erzbistum Paderborn)

Parish Holdings Result
Herstelle, St. Bartholomäus (covers Würgassen residents) KB006-01-T Taufen 1828–1859 (DE_EBAP_10608) SCANNED — NEGATIVE. Jan–Feb 1835 (img T_0034): all entries marked ehelich (the register has an explicit ehelich/unehelich column); no pool-like surname. Closest regional "unehelich male" is outside the window (Franz, b. 16 Nov 1834, Würgassen mother).
Würgassen, St. Michael Own books only from 1894 N/A — 1835 covered by Herstelle (above).
Beverungen, St. Johannes Bapt. KB006-02-T Taufen 1803–1835 (DE_EBAP_10601) COMPLETED later 6 June — NEGATIVE. Jan–Feb 1835 (img T_0108): five window males, all ehelich, none in the pool — and every one carries a later margin note (death or local marriage) placing him in Germany; all five independently eliminated as emigrant candidates.
Dalhausen, St. Marien KB002-01-T Taufen 1822–1838 + name index (DE_EBAP_10605) COMPLETED later 6 June — NEGATIVE. The register's own K-index holds no pool-resembling surname for the entire 1822–1838 span. Window pages (T_0056–T_0058): all males ehelich. Near-miss for the record: entry 6, Friedrich Wilhelm Sievers, born exactly 26 January 1835 — legitimate (Sievers × Spindeler), margin note †23.2.1908 = died in Germany; positively eliminated.

Net result (final, end of 6 June)

  • Ten more parishes cleared negative (Bad Karlshafen ×2 confessions, Hombressen, Haueda, Ostheim, Niedermeiser, Herstelle/Würgassen, Beverungen, Dalhausen). The full ring now stands at 29 parishes investigated · 24 eliminated · 2 open gaps (Heisebeck pre-1845, Liebenau 1835 — both undigitised, both needing the Kassel archive or FamilySearch film).
  • Still no male birth on/near 26 Jan 1835 in the surname pool or as an unresolved illegitimate — anywhere, across two states (Kurhessen + Prussian Westphalia) and two confessions. The closest-dated boy found in any register (Dalhausen's F. W. Sievers, born exactly 26 Jan 1835) is legitimate and died in Germany in 1908.
  • The weak Thielemann/Schlosshahn hit (Karlshafen Reformed) is recorded and closed by its 1838 legitimization.
  • Sites updated: parish map (cards, markers, labels, counts), negative-results, changelog.
