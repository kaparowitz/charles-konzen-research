Session 96 — MyHeritage DNA Match Analysis

Date: 7 June 2026 Kit: Jed Johnson (descendant test), MyHeritage — 6,896 DNA Matches Companion kits on MyHeritage: Father (Gary Lee Johnson, 3,530.8 cM), Mother (Sandy Johnson, 3,520.0 cM), Sister (Sarah Bradshaw, 2,730.1 cM). Both parents tested → every match can in principle be assigned paternal / maternal via shared-match triangulation. This is a capability the FTDNA kit (Session 86 context) does not have.

Bottom line: the documented-surname gap seen on FTDNA persists on MyHeritage. No Konze / Kontzen, Kreichelt, Grandjot, or Herwig connection at any segment size.

2. Germany-resident matches: 327 (vs. near-zero close German signal on FTDNA)

MyHeritage's user base is far more continental-European than Ancestry's or FTDNA's, and it shows: 327 matches currently living in Germany, with several in the 30–80 cM range.

So the maternal side has its own strong south-German block (Schuon/Rabus/Leipoldt, Swabian-Baden names) that must NOT be confused with Charles's line. The Charles-relevant signal is the paternal Lipphardt–Eickenberg cluster.

3. The paternal German cluster — the finding of the session

  • Rolf Lipphardt and Birgit Haase share 51.7 cM with each other — they are related to each other as well as to the Johnsons. One ancestral couple plausibly links the whole cluster to Gary's German ancestry.
  • MyHeritage's estimate for both: 3rd cousin to Gary (Rolf: "Parent's 3rd cousin" to Jed, 36.6% probability; Birgit: 4th cousin / parent's 3rd cousin). A 3rd-cousin MRCA for Gary = his great-great-grandparents — i.e. a couple born roughly 1800–1840. That is exactly Charles Konzen's generation (b. 26 Jan 1835) or his parents'.
  • If the connection runs through Charles's line, the cluster would descend from a sibling (or close kin) of Charles who remained in Germany — descendants of such a sibling are precisely 3rd–4th cousins to Gary.
  • Geography of the cluster's surnames: Lipphardt / Kielhorn / Sasse (Lower Saxony), Eickenberg / Lüke (Westphalia). Trendelburg–Deisel sits in the Hesse / Lower Saxony / Westphalia tri-border Weser region — the cluster's geography is compatible with (though far from proof of) the northern-Hessen hypothesis. Caveat: it is equally compatible with a different German line in Gary's ancestry, or with Charles's wife's side.
  • Small Ashkenazi-surname matches (Inna Agrest, Liora Lopian, Ariel Zuchowski, Michelle Karnetsky, Veronica Paz) recur inside this paternal cluster's shared-match lists at 9–20 cM — the same "faint Ashkenazi echo" pattern documented on FTDNA (dna.html § match list), now visible in a second database.

3-CORRESPONDENCE. Prior contact from Melanie (recalled by Jed, 7 June 2026)

Melanie N (Birgit's daughter, 60.5 cM, lives in Virginia, originally from Germany) wrote to Jed a few years ago when her results first came in ("cousin 3rd to 5th removed"). Her message named two family lines:

  • Eickenberg (her maternal grandfather Egon's side) — "my maternal grandpa's cousin emigrated to the US"
  • Kammerscheid (her paternal grandfather's family, who also emigrated to the US)

What this pins down: Jed's DNA connection to Melanie runs through her mother Birgit (Birgit herself matches at 52.5 cM; brother Matthias too). So Melanie's paternal Kammerscheid line is irrelevant to this match — the link is in Birgit's two lines, Eickenberg or Lüke (or deeper behind them). The Eickenberg-cousin emigration she mentions is likely a 20th-century event and not itself the connection (our MRCA couple dates ~1800–1840), but it shows the family preserves emigration memory worth mining.

Questions now with Melanie (Jed responded 7 June 2026): the high-value answers are (1) the towns of origin of the Eickenberg and the Lüke lines; (2) whether anything in Birgit's ancestry connects to Lipphardt / Sasse / Kielhorn (the Rolf Lipphardt link, 51.7 cM to Birgit); (3) any family link to the Oberharz (Clausthal) → Kreichelt-side, or the Hofgeismar district → Konze-side.

3a. Could the cluster be Kreichelt-side rather than Konze-side? (Jed's question, same session)

Yes — entirely possible, and for Rolf Lipphardt arguably the better geographic fit. Gary descends equally from Charles Gannsen/Konzen and Auguste Kreichelt (m. 1866, St. Marcus). At 3rd-cousin depth the autosomal segment cannot say which spouse's line it travelled down: the MRCA couple could be Charles's parents or Auguste's parents (both born ~1800–1810), i.e. the cluster could descend from a sibling of Charles or a sibling of Auguste who stayed in Germany.

  • Geography leans Kreichelt for Rolf: the Kreichelts were Hannoverian, from Clausthal in the Harz (emigrated 1853). Rolf's ancestral surnames — Lipphardt, Sasse, Kielhorn — are Lower Saxony / Hannover-region names; Kielhorn in particular concentrates around Hannover–Braunschweig, well within reach of the Harz. That is at least as consistent with Auguste's line as with a Hessian Konze line.
  • Birgit is less clear: Eickenberg and Lüke read Westphalian, but her tree is 19 people / 2 generations — her deeper lines are unknown.
  • Surname searches don't decide it: Kreichelt, Kreigelt, Kreichel, Billmann, Mincke/Münke all return 0 matches — expected, given the cluster's shallow trees; absence is not evidence here.
  • Either answer advances the project: Konze-side → the long-sought German anchor for Charles; Kreichelt-side → the first autosomal corroboration of the Kreichelt reconstruction (already a stated goal on dna.html § next steps).

Discriminators, in order of cost: 1. Ask Rolf/Birgit one question: do their lines trace to the Oberharz (Clausthal-Zellerfeld mining families) or to the Hofgeismar district (Trendelburg/Deisel)? 2. Chromosome-browser triangulation (Rolf × Birgit × Melanie × Gary): if all overlap on one segment, a single identified origin settles the whole cluster. 3. Locate/test a descendant of Auguste's siblings (e.g. Bertha Barbara Kreichelt's line): a match to the cluster = Kreichelt-side; no match leans (weakly) Konze-side.

3b. Chromosome-browser triangulation (run 7 June 2026, same session)

One-to-Many comparison: Jed vs. Birgit Haase + Matthias Haase + Gary Lee Johnson (father).

  • Result: one triangulated segment — chromosome 17, 54,150,328–64,343,560 (rs6505034 → rs77204538), 12.4 cM, 4,608 SNPs — shared by all four kits. This is a true common-ancestor segment, inherited by Jed through Gary, confirming the Haase/Eickenberg connection is a genuine identical-by-descent paternal-side link, not coincidental small-segment noise.
  • Birgit's other segments with Jed: chr 4 (one) and a second chr 17 segment (~36–46 Mb region, adjacent to but distinct from the triangulated block); largest 30.9 cM. Matthias carries the chr 17 triangulated block (his 12.8 cM largest) plus a small chr 6 segment.
  • Rolf Lipphardt and Melanie N could not be included: both are absent from the chromosome-browser selection pool and its search (while present in the match list), meaning their kits have shared-segment viewing disabled in privacy settings. Whether Rolf's single 55.1 cM segment overlaps the chr 17 block therefore cannot be verified from this side — worth asking him directly, or asking him to enable segment sharing.
  • Next segment-level step: when contact is established, check whether the Lipphardt 55.1 cM segment and the chr 17 / chr 4 Eickenberg segments coincide — if yes, one ancestral couple; if no, the two German testers may connect through different lines.

4. Ashkenazi proxy check

Israel-resident matches: only 4, all 12.3–19.6 cM single/double segments (Veronica Paz, Sophie Diamant, Alina Kotliarsky, Andrew Shapiro). No close Jewish match → unchanged conclusion: Ashkenazi contribution real but small and/or deep; nothing here dates it.

4a. Genetic Groups & AutoClusters (checked 7 June 2026)

  • Genetic Groups (v2.5, both confidence levels): "Mormons in Utah, Idaho, California, Arizona, Wyoming and Colorado" and "Northeastern and Midwestern USA and Canada (Ontario)" — both American settler groups, no German regional group. No help localising Charles.
  • AutoClusters: Generate requested 7 June 2026 (report arrives by email as an HTML/zip when ready). When it lands, locate the cluster containing the Haase/Eickenberg members — its other members are additional candidates descending from the same ancestral couple, and any with deeper trees may resolve the Konze-vs-Kreichelt question without waiting on correspondence.

4b. AutoClusters results (report parsed 7 June 2026; files in autoclusters_2026-06-07/)

100 matches ≥~30 cM clustered into 17 clusters (8 matches dropped for no shared matches; 37 singletons omitted from chart).

  • Cluster 16 — the German paternal cluster: Melanie N (60.5) + Rolf Lipphardt (55.1) + Birgit Haase (52.5), and no one else. AutoClustering (shared-match overlap, independent of segment data) groups Rolf with the Eickenberg family — corroborating, alongside the chr 17 triangulation and the 51.7 cM Rolf↔Birgit share, that ONE ancestral couple likely stands behind the whole cluster. An origin answer from any member probably resolves the cluster as a unit. Matthias Haase (19 cM) fell below the chart's inclusion threshold.
  • Cluster 17 — Karl Heinrich Rabus (65.0) + Tim Vogel (42.5) + Linus Vogel (37.5). Rabus is maternal (§2) → the Vogels are by implication maternal too. Eliminates Tim Vogel from the Konzen-side candidate list.
  • Cluster 12 — Rainer Schuon (79.8) + Betty A Brown (64.0) + Julian Schmidt (35.2) — the maternal Swabian block, now bounded.
  • Cluster 2 is the West/parent's-1st-cousin family network; Cluster 7 is the Gaff/Connie H (parent's 2nd cousin) network; remaining clusters are mid-size American/British-Isles groups not yet relevant to the German question.

No cluster other than 16 contains a Germany-resident match on the paternal side — the Lipphardt–Eickenberg group is the unique German paternal signal at ≥30 cM.

7.3 Chromosome 17 block at FTDNA — the Ashkenazi echo and the German cluster may be THE SAME SEGMENT

"Download All Segments" export (1032621_Chromosome_Browser_Results_20260607.csv, 15,610 segments, in project folder). Filtering chr 17 for ≥5 cM segments overlapping the MyHeritage-triangulated block (54,150,328–64,343,560) by >1 Mb: 35 matches, topped by Gary (the paternal transmission; his chr-17 segment with Jed spans the block) and then a striking run of Ashkenazi-surname matches — P. Shafer (23.4 cM), Beatrice & Isabel Sommerfeld, Sabina Rosenfeld, Orit Lavi, Frances Harris, Ziegelheim, Karp, Baron Sherman, Hoffman, Hershkovitz, Selker, Reingold, Schatz, Hirshfeld, Shaburov, Kaplan, Sternshein, Hankin… Many share near-identical breakpoints (≈55.7→64.9 Mb) — the signature of a shared founder haplotype, not random small-segment noise. (The block is NOT the known chr-17 MAPT-inversion pile-up at 43–45 Mb.)

Implication (unverified): if these Ashkenazi-side segments lie on Jed's PATERNAL chr 17 — the same copy carrying the Birgit/Rolf triangulated block — then the German paternal cluster's common ancestor may sit on a line of Ashkenazi origin, dovetailing with the Y-line I-Y38863 hypothesis. Caveat: without bucketing, these 35 could match Jed's maternal chr 17 copy instead. Discriminators: (1) complete the MyHeritage-account connection → bucketing labels them paternal/maternal instantly; (2) check ICW Gary for the top names; (3) ask Birgit/Melanie whether they match any Sommerfeld/Rosenfeld/etc.
