Session 88 — Konze surname-distribution sweep + Stammen correction

Date: 6 June 2026 · live Archion (signed in). Goal shifted from birth-window searching to mapping where the Konze pool exists at all (Konze/Kontze/Kontzen/Contze/Contzen/Konzen + Jungkontze; Köster excluded as ubiquitous).

1. ⚠ CORRECTION — the Stammen "Rudolph Kontze" reading (Session 85/87) was wrong

High-resolution re-read of Stammen Taufen 1830–1915 (reg. 220069), image 24 = Seite 20, Jahr 1835, entry 73: - The child is a stillborn male Kontze — the name column reads "Männlich: — Kontze, todtgeboren", NO first name; baptism and godparent columns are dashes. - Born 14 March 1835 ("der vierzehnte März", 3 p.m.), Haus Nr. 21 — not 20 March, and there is no "Rudolph." - Parents confirmed: Christian Ludwig Kontze, Tagelöhner × Anne Gertrud geb. Temme. - Consequence: unchanged verdict (a stillborn cannot be Charles), but the prior "Rudolph Kontze b. 20 Mar 1835" record is retracted — corrected in Session 85, the parish map, Negative Results, and the changelog; errata entry er-2026-06-06-stammen-stillborn.

2. The Stammen Kontze branch — what the sweep established

  • Christian Ludwig Kontze (Tagelöhner, Stammen Haus Nr. 21) × Anne Gertrud geb. Temme — her father was the late Johann Conrad Temme of Stammen (d. before Aug 1833; his 2nd wife Catharine Elisabeth geb. Scheele of Eberschütz).
  • Aug 1833 (img 17, entry 45): Christian Ludwig Kontze appears as godparent — "der Schwager" — at the baptism of his wife's sister's illegitimate son (Heinrich Wilhelm Temme, †13.4.1908; alleged father Georg Heinrich Feige of Hümme denied paternity).
  • Baptisms swept continuously Aug 1833–Nov 1835 (entries 44–86) + spot-checks ≈1839/41/45/47/49: no other Kontze baptism — the stillborn of 14 Mar 1835 is the couple's only found child so far.
  • Jungkontze (the compound surname known from Trendelburg!) appears in Stammen as two mothers' maiden names in 1835: Wilhelmine geb. Jungkontze (× Pappenheim) and Anne Elisabeth geb. Jungkontze (× Schäfer).
  • The Kontze × Temme marriage was not found in Trauungen 1830–32 (entries 1–11, no Kontze) ⇒ it is either 1833+ (Trauungen 220078, img 566 ff.) or pre-1830 (Kirchenbuch 1775–1831, reg. 220063). Unchecked: Beerdigungen 1830–1936 (reg. 220072 — would hold the stillborn's burial and the couple's deaths); most baptism years 1836+.
  • Stammen register list (for follow-ups): KB 1720–1777 (220060) · KB 1775–1831 (220063) · Taufen 1830–1915 (220069) · Trauungen 1830–1937 (220078; marriages start img 559) · Beerdigungen 1830–1936 (220072) · Konfirmationen 1831–2013.

3. The Hümme Konze line — corroborated as "daughtered-out / emigrated"

  • Only Hümme's Taufen 1830–1863 (219532) is AI-transcribed; 13 pages sampled across 1830–1858 (regex incl. ausgewandert/Amerika):
  • 1830 (img 1210): a grandmother "…dessen ebenfalls verstorbenen Ehefrau Anne Marie, geb. Kontze" — an older-generation Kontze woman, already deceased.
  • ~1840 (img 1276): "Christoph Laubert Ehefrau, geborne Kontze," resident Hümme.
  • No baptism with a Kontze father in any sampled page 1830–1858, and no emigration remark on sampled pages.
  • Reading: the Kontze name is native to Hümme but by 1830–60 survives only in women's maiden names — consistent with the project's note that the last Hümme Konze male emigrated in the 1840s. Naming him + finding the "ausgewandert" remark needs the un-transcribed Trauungen 1830–1891 / Beerdigungen 1830–1874 / Konfirmationen 1831–1934 (visual scan, ~190–290 images each — flagged for a dedicated session; that emigrant male is also of interest for the American side).

5. The distribution picture (the headline)

With the prior record (Trendelburg from 1773; Deisel from ~1718; Friedrichsfeld colonists) plus this sweep: The Konze/Kontze pool is hyper-local — a single tight cluster in the Diemel valley: Trendelburg · Deisel · Friedrichsfeld · Stammen · Hümme (all within ~5 km), with no presence in any of the surrounding ring of parishes sampled (Gottsbüren, Grebenstein, Immenhausen, Bad Karlshafen, Gieselwerder, Veckerhagen — nor the previously index-searched Helmarshausen, Hofgeismar, Schöneberg, Sielen). This sharpens two things: (a) Charles's claimed origins (Deisel; "Freundburg"=Trendelburg) sit exactly in the only place the surname exists — the surname conclusion is geographically coherent; (b) any Y-DNA recruitment target must come from this cluster's lines: the Trendelburg branches, the Stammen Christian-Ludwig line (only known child stillborn — likely no male descendants unless later children exist in the unswept years), or the Hümme emigrant's American descendants if he can be named.

Open follow-ups from this session

  1. Stammen Trauungen 1833+ / KB 1775–1831 → the Kontze×Temme marriage and Christian Ludwig's own origin (he may be a son of one of the documented Trendelburg/Deisel branches — that would tie the Stammen branch into the tree).
  2. Stammen Beerdigungen 1830–1936 → stillborn's burial; the couple's deaths; any other Kontze deaths.
  3. Stammen baptisms 1836+ → later children of the couple (Y-DNA relevance).
  4. Hümme's un-transcribed marriage/burial/confirmation books → name the 1840s emigrant Konze male.
  5. Jungkontze: clarify the Stammen↔Trendelburg Jungkonze/Jungkontze relationship.
