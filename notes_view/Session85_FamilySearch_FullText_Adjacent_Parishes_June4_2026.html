Session 85 — "Complete the research" pass: FamilySearch Full-Text + unsearched adjacent parishes

Date: 4 June 2026 · live, signed-in FamilySearch (Jed S Johnson) + Archion sessions. Why: with no active candidate and the Stammrolle inquiry already emailed (awaiting reply), this pass works the remaining online-searchable leads: (A) FamilySearch's new AI full-text index — never systematically used in this project — and (B) the Kreis Hofgeismar parishes adjacent to Deisel/Trendelburg that had never been opened.

A. FamilySearch Full-Text sweep (~20 queries across the full name pool)

U.S. side — confirms the soldier, adds the "L.", but NO origin-naming document

  • The surname that hits in Missouri is "Gannsen" (not Konzen/Gonnson/Gansen). Tight, consistent cluster, all our Charles:
  • 1862 Regimental Descriptive Book (Missouri Military Records 1861–1864, ARK 61903/3:1:3QHJ-N3BK-RJM4, img 31): "Charles Gannsen, Batt'y B, 2 Reg't Mo. L. Arty… age 23, 5'5", fair, blue eyes, blond, born Freundburg, Kurhessen, farmer, enl. Dec 2 1862 St. Louis." (Already in the file.)
  • Co. B service card (ARK 61903/3:1:3Q9M-C3W4-S3WS-4, img 5062): "Gunnsen, Chas. L. or Gannsen, Chas., Sergt. Co. B… enl. Dec 2 1862 St. Louis; promoted Sergt. from Corp. Nov 7 1863; reduced to ranks Mar 15 1865; mustered out Dec 20 1865." → independently ties the middle initial "L." to the soldier (bridge toward "Charles L. Johnson").
  • General-index/reference cards, battery muster rolls, and an 1895 St. Louis directory ("Gannsen Charles, watchman, r. rear 2407 DeKalb" + "Gannsen Charles jr., lab.") — no new biographical data.
  • No naturalization / declaration of intention / probate / land record naming a German birthplace surfaced under any spelling. The only "Gannsen" naturalization (Emil Gannsen, Scott Co., Iowa, 1908) is unrelated. So the U.S. full-text path yields the "Chas. L." corroboration but no declaration with a village + birthdate.

German side — exhausted, no authentic hit (a meaningful negative)

  • Surname pool × place terms (Deisel, Trendelburg, Hofgeismar, Hessen, Cassel, Gänsewinkel) → no genuine Hesse match. "Konzen" full-text hits are a Catholic Rhineland (Eifel/Aachen) family in Iowa/Ohio; "Kontzen" → Alabama/Brazil; "Konsen" → Dutch OCR noise; "Köster + Deisel/Gänsewinkel" → 0.
  • Important corroborating negative: the full-text index does hold deep Kreis Hofgeismar material (LDS family-group records and parish abstracts for Deisel, Trendelburg, Grebenstein, Hombressen, Veckerhagen, etc.) — yet the Konze/Konzen/Kontzen surname never appears attached to the Hofgeismar district. This reinforces the Archion finding that the family does not surface in the Deisel/Trendelburg books under those spellings; it is coverage-backed, not a gap.

B. Archion — unsearched adjacent Kreis Hofgeismar parishes, Jan–Feb 1835 baptisms

Searched the parishes nearest Deisel/Trendelburg that had never been opened (target: a male birth ~26 Jan 1835, surname in the Konze pool or an illegitimate male birth of any surname, given the DNA non-paternity hint).

One new lead (weak, for evaluation — NOT a confirmed candidate)

  • Eberschütz, Taufen 1830–1897 (Archion register 218887), image 351, entry No. 101: Heinrich Wilhelm Albore (surname faint — also readable Älbore/Alborn), male, illegitimate, born 22 Jan 1835, 3 a.m.; baptized 1 Feb 1835. Child carries the mother's surname; mother "aus Bischhausen, Amt Hardenberg, im Königreich Hannover" (an outsider); an associated man (read "Wilhelm Hisk[?]") noted "unverheirathet."
  • Why it's even noted: exactly the unwed-mother / illegitimate-male category the Y-DNA result makes relevant (a biological father who is not a local Konze), and the date is 4 days off the claimed 26 Jan 1835.
  • Why it is a long shot (stated plainly, no overreach): the surname "Albore" has no relation to the Konze pool or to any American form Charles used (Gansen/Gonnson — which the project tied to Deisel's "Gänsewinkel," not Eberschütz); the given name "Heinrich Wilhelm" ≠ "Carl/Ludwig/Charles L."; and Eberschütz is neither of his two stated origins (Deisel, Trendelburg). The manuscript hand is hard to read at viewer resolution.
  • RESOLVED 4 June 2026 (full-magnification re-read of the manuscript, image 351 / Seite 50): the child-name column reads "männl: Heinrich Wilhelm Albore" — confirmed male, given name Heinrich Wilhelm, surname Albore (the mother's). Parents/remarks columns: mother of the Albore family "gebürtig aus Bischhausen, Amts Hardenberg, im Königreich Hannover"; an unmarried man read "Wilhelm Hisk[?]" noted ("unverheirathet"); a later note records a death. Verdict: NOT a match for Charles. Surname "Albore" relates to nothing Charles ever used (Gansen/Gonnson/Konze pool), the given name "Heinrich Wilhelm" is not Carl/Ludwig/Charles-L., and Eberschütz is neither of his stated origins. It is simply the only illegitimate male birth in the Jan–Feb 1835 window across the newly-searched parishes; logged as checked, now closed.

Negative (window cleanly checked)

  • Gottsbüren (Taufen 1830–1867, reg. 219049): Jan–Feb 1835 males all legitimate, none Konze-pool.
  • Lamerden (Taufen 1830–1903, reg. 219676; not AI-transcribed, read by eye): Jan–Feb 1835 — two legitimate males (Götze; Carl Wilhelm Heinrich), neither Konze-pool; rest female. No match.
  • Gewissenruh (Kirchenbuch 1830–1969, reg. 219001): no January 1835 baptism at all; only window entry female. No match.

No separate Archion register (skipped)

  • Carlsdorf, Liebenau, Gieselwerder — no independent Kurhessen-Hofgeismar baptism book on Archion under those names (filials / not held).

Second batch (later 4 June) — five more parishes scanned

(Registers here had no AI transcription; all reads were visual from zoomed scans, so exact day-digits are approximate.)

Parish Taufen register Result
Stammen 220069 Konze-pool entry — but NOT Charles. Entry 73, Seite 20: ⚠ CORRECTED 6 June (Session 88): a stillborn male Kontze ("todtgeboren"), born 14 March 1835, no first name — the first-pass reading "Rudolph, b. 20 Mar" was wrong. Father Christian Ludwig Kontze (Tagelöhner), mother Anne Gertrud née Temme. Stillborn ⇒ cannot be anyone, let alone Charles. The January page had only two female births (3 Jan, 25 Jan). Newly documented: a Kontze family in Stammen ~1835 — possibly the Carl Ludwig Kontze (b. 1804, Trendelburg) line; worth a later cross-check, but not Charles.
Veckerhagen 220213 Illegitimate male — but NOT Charles. Img 830, entry 244: Wilhelm Walter, male, "unehelich geboren," baptized ~March 1835 (birth ~late Feb/early Mar), mother Anna Martha Johanne Schäfer, no father named. Wrong date/name/place.
Marzhausen 181898 NEGATIVE — 1835 entries all married couples; no Konze/Köster, no illegitimate male.
Grebenstein 219106 NEGATIVE — one illegitimate Jan-1835 birth but female; no Konze/Köster male.
Immenhausen 219586 NEGATIVE — 1835 males all legitimate, no Konze/Köster, no illegitimate male.

No parish in either batch held a male baptism on/near 26 January 1835 in the surname pool or as an illegitimate birth. The two Konze-pool / illegitimate-male hits found (the Stammen stillborn male Kontze, b. 14 Mar — first read "Rudolph b. 20 Mar", corrected Session 88; Veckerhagen Wilhelm Walter ~Mar) are both ruled out.

Third batch (later 4 June) — final two parishes + Stammen cross-check → SWEEP COMPLETE

Parish Taufen register Result
Oberweser (held under Gieselwerder) 219016 NEGATIVE — Jan–Feb 1835 males all legitimate, no Konze/Köster, no illegitimate male.
Hofgeismar-Kelze 219634 NEGATIVE — small Huguenot colony; the only Jan–Feb 1835 baptism is female; no pool surname, no in-window illegitimate male.

Stammen Kontze cross-check — RESOLVED: Stammen reg. 220069, Seite 20, entry 73 (a stillborn male Kontze, b. 14 March 1835, Haus Nr. 21 — "Rudolph, 20 Mar" first reading corrected in Session 88) — father re-read at max zoom as Christian Ludwig Kontze, Tagelöhner, wife Anne Gertrud née Tem[me] (not "Hinn-"). This is a distinct man from the documented Carl Ludwig Kontze (b. 1804, Trendelburg, × Marie Wilhelmine Albrecht) — different given name, wife, occupation, and village. So: a newly-documented, separate Stammen Kontze family (Christian Ludwig Kontze, Tagelöhner of Stammen No. 21) — add to the family map as a distinct branch; not Charles.

Adjacent-parish sweep — COMPLETE (11 parishes)

Gottsbüren, Lamerden, Gewissenruh, Eberschütz, Stammen, Marzhausen, Grebenstein, Immenhausen, Veckerhagen, Oberweser/Gieselwerder, Hofgeismar-Kelze — all negative for a male birth on/near 26 Jan 1835 in the surname pool or as an illegitimate birth. Three new non-matching finds logged (Eberschütz Albore; the Stammen stillborn male Kontze — see Session 88 correction; Veckerhagen Wilhelm Walter). This closes the readily-searchable Archion parish space around Deisel/Trendelburg.

Net status after this pass

  • Still no confirmed candidate. The online-searchable space is now substantially exhausted: FamilySearch full-text (German + U.S.) added the "Chas. L." service card but no origin document; the closest unsearched parishes were negative except the weak Eberschütz illegitimate-male lead.
  • The decisive leads remain the ones already in motion or requiring offline work: (1) the HStAM Marburg Stammrolle 1855 inquiry — emailed, awaiting reply (likely most informative); (2) line-by-line passenger manifests (NARA M237 NY 1859–62; Baltimore/New Orleans/Boston; Antwerp/Le Havre) — a dedicated image-read, not yet done; (3) Y-DNA recruitment of a Trendelburg-area Konze male; (4) finishing the remaining adjacent parishes (Grebenstein, Immenhausen, Stammen, Marzhausen, Veckerhagen/Oberweser cluster, Kelze).
  • NOT an open lead (correction): the NARA pension file is already obtained, published on the site, and fully transcribed — it is a closed source, not an outstanding one. (It supplies the "Freundburg, Kurhessen" origin statement already in use.)
  • Dedup check (research logs): the adjacent parishes scanned here (Gottsbüren, Lamerden, Gewissenruh, Eberschütz) were previously only ever logged as "confirmed digitized — not systematically searched," and earlier notes proposed searching their 1849–51 confirmation registers for a "Justus Köster" (now moot — Entry 195 eliminated). So this 1835-baptism scan is new, non-duplicative work; the Eberschütz "Albore" entry had never been recorded before.
  • Reminder for the file (already flagged on Key Evidence Card 8): the 1862 descriptive book gives age 23 → birth ~1839, ~4 years later than the self-reported 26 Jan 1835.
