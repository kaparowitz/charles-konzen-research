Session 96 — Swineherd family solved end-to-end; "Halbbruder" puzzle resolved; a provocative 1839 US lead — 7/8 June 2026

Scope. Three pulls per Jed's "yes start": US-side hunt for Christoph Kontze (b. 13 Feb 1814 Hümme); Stammen KB 220063 for the swineherd family; max-zoom re-reads of two flagged Deisel entries. Page integrated (239 entries) preserving Jed's interim enhancements (embedded SVG town-trees via scripts/build-bytown-trees.py + many new FamilySearch IDs); the Stammen/Gottsbüren tree data was updated and all 27 trees regenerated.

A. ★★ Stammen KB 220063 — the swineherd family completely reconstructed

  • Marriage found, 23 Mar 1783, Stammen: "Andreas Konze, Schweinehirt(?), weyland Arnold Philips Konzen, gewesenen Einwohners zu Trendelburg, ehelich nachgelassener Sohn × Maria Elisabeth, weyland Christoph Gerlandts ehelich nachgelassene Tochter" (img 2063).
  • ⇒ The swineherd is a SON of Arnold Philipp Konze of Trendelburg (d. 1780). With Arnold Philipp's two Uffelmann wives (×1 d. 1754; ×2 Anna Dorothea d. 1785), the 1787 godfather entry "Andreas Kontze von Stammen, des Vaters Halbbruder" is RESOLVED: Andreas (b. ~1758, of ×2) and Joh. Henrich the younger (b. ~1740, of ×1) were half-brothers. Arnold Philipp's children now: Joh. Henrich (×1), Johannes, + of ×2: Andreas the swineherd, Johann Philips, Joh. Christoph, Anna Catharina (× Joh. Henrich Engel, Stammen 21 Nov 1784).
  • TWELVE children, 1784–1809, all baptized in Stammen (three new): George Henrich (1784†), Johann Christian (b. 1 Jul 1785 — untraced, worth following), Johannes (b. 26 Jul 1788; margin "✝1837 zu Hümmen" — Hümme identity locked), Catharina Elisabeth (1791), Maria Sophia (1793; × J.C.A.W. Müller ~1815; d. 26 Dec 1829 — exact age match), Johann Conrad (1796–1812), Anna Catharina (1799), Johann Henrich (b. 20 May 1800 — untraced, worth following), Johann Ludwig (1802–04), Maria Charlotta (1805†), Christian Ludwig (b. 20 Nov 1806, bap. 23 Nov 1806 IN STAMMEN — margin "✝ 11/4 1883"), Sophia Maria (1809–10).
  • Both parents died in Stammen — mother 20 Nov 1817 ("des Schneiders Andreas Kontze Ehefrau, geb. Gerlandt," aged 57); father 31 Dec 1828 (bur. 3 Jan 1829, "Andreas Kontze, Schneider, alt 70") — he turned tailor in old age (the swineherd post passed to Düsterdiek by 1814). The "died elsewhere/1831–33" hypothesis is refuted.
  • ⇒ The Gottsbüren claim is REFUTED: Christian Ludwig was born in Stammen; the 1833 "gebürtig von Gottsbüren" is simply wrong (or loosely meant). Gerlandt kin (mother's siblings Margretha Elisabeth × Wendel, Joh. Jacob, Joh. George) lived around Stammen; the "native-Gottsbüren Garland" derivation is doubtful. Gottsbüren page section re-pilled "claim refuted."
  • Christian Ludwig d. 11 Apr 1883 (baptism margin) — he lived 48 years past the stillbirth; his post-1835 trail is now a tractable question.
  • Stammen Jungkonze: Joh. Christoph Jungkontze (Maurer, b. ~Mar 1768, d. 16 May 1824) and brother Johannes Jungkonze × Maria Catharina Lohr — children Anna Elisabeth (1801), Johann Conrad (1805–07), Johannes jr. (b. 22 Jun 1808, margin ✝1865 — probably the 1834 "Weißbindergesell zu Stammen"), Wilhelmina (1809†), Maria Sophia (1811–12). Caveat: if Johannes jr. were Eduard-b.1822's father he'd have been 13 — the artillerist Johannes is more likely an older man; flagged.
  • Also: Maria Elisabeth geb. Konze × Lorenz Becker (~1809, children 1810/1812); Wilhelmine geb. Jungkontze × Pappenheim child d. 1828; Engel×Konze family; Gerlandt godparent web. Marriages 1789–1831 not yet scanned (Müller×Kontze ~1815, Becker×Konze ~1809, Jungkonze ~1800/01 still to pull).

B. ★ US-side hunt for Christoph Kontze (b. 13 Feb 1814) — best lead is provocative

  • Headline hit: Brig President, Bremen → Philadelphia, arrived 26 Aug 1839: "Chs Aug(s) Kontze, age 25 [b. ~1814], farmer" traveling with "Child Kontze, age 4, male [b. ~1835]" — NO wife in the party. Origin column reads either "Warren C[ount]y Miss[issippi]" (Ancestry's transcriber; a Vicksburg "L Konntze" appears in an 1878 directory) or "C[ur]hessen." FS arks 1:1:QV9Y-JSKQ / 1:1:K8C3-FT1; original image read.
  • Right age for Christoph, rare exact surname, unmarried — but given name "Chs Aug(s)" (Charles August), and a Kontze boy b. ~1835 in tow. NOT claimed as Charles (Charles's own record-trail starts St. Louis ~1859 and he consistently said b. 1835 Kurhessen, arr. ~1850s) — but this is the single most Charles-adjacent datum any US search has produced. Follow-ups queued: Warren Co. MS 1840/1850 censuses, Vicksburg German records, the 1878 "L Konntze" original.
  • Eliminated: Christopher "Kontze" of Hardin Co., Ohio (= Koutz, b. ~1808, married by 1838). Noted: Christian Kontze (Atlanta 1850); Frederick Kontze (Illinois land 1847–48); Henry Kontze (Ripley Co. IN 1840).
  • Hümme→NYC line documented on FS Tree: Friedrich Kontze GPK7-JYP (b. 1816, the Stammen-born Johannes's son!) — "death after 1870, New York City"; researcher GeneDana (Feb 2024 notes citing the exact Hümme pages we read) is an active collaboration lead.
  • Explicit negatives: NO Christoph/Christopher Kontze/Konze in Missouri/St. Louis in any spelling; FS catalog "Auswanderung Hofgeismar" search erroring — retry later; HStAM Auswanderungsakten remains the decisive offline source.
  • St. Louis "Konitz" family noted (one 1877 marriage to an H. W. Temme — curious echo of Kontze×Temme; parked).

C. Deisel max-zoom re-reads — both flags resolved

  1. Philipp bap. 10 Oct 1824 (218824 S.152) is NOT a Konze: the corrected father reads "Christoph Goebel" (capital-G match, b-ascender, no z-descender; ≥90% not-Konze), mother Wilhelmine geb. Schildknecht. So "Philipp Konze #2" (m. 14 Jun 1850; pencil b. 7 Oct 1824, after a struck "20.8.1825") has no located baptism — next: Deisel baptisms Aug–Sep 1825.
  2. Bürgermeister Christoph Konze's father = "CHRISTIAN Konze" (~90%; "Justus" excluded) → ties him to Christian Konze (b. ~1756, d. 1829) × Anna Dorothea Brönner. Full burial verbatim also confirms: b. Deisel 6 Aug 1784; Kirchenältester; × Maria Elisabeth Schrage.

D. Site integration — NOTE on concurrent edits

The page had been enhanced in the meantime (27 embedded SVG town-trees via scripts/build-bytown-trees.py; many real FS IDs added to rows, e.g. Philipp #2 = G36L-2RZ with FS-sourced death 1893 and children). My Stammen-section rewrite briefly removed the Stammen tree + two FS chips; both restored (Elisa Melusine PZF6-H42; Heinrich Wilhelm PZFD-1X4 + FS marriage 1856 Pappenheim), the script's Stammen/Gottsbüren data updated to today's findings, and all 27 trees regenerated. Page: 239 entries, tags clean.

E. Updated open questions

  1. Christian Ludwig Kontze post-1835 → d. 11 Apr 1883 — where? (margin gives no place; possible Stammen-area or emigration-return).
  2. The two untraced swineherd sons — Johann Christian (b. 1785) and Johann Henrich (b. 1800) — Trendelburg-line men of exactly fathering age in 1835, neither yet traced. NEW candidate-relevant thread.
  3. Philadelphia 1839 "Chs Aug Kontze" + boy b. ~1835 → Warren Co. MS follow-up.
  4. Philipp Konze #2's baptism (Aug–Sep 1825 Deisel window).
  5. Stammen marriages 1789–1831 (Müller, Becker, Jungkonze couples).
  6. HStAM Auswanderungsakten for Christoph (b. 1814); GeneDana contact.
