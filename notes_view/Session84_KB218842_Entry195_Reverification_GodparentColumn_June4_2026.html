Session 84 — Live Archion Re-Verification of Entry 195 (KB 218842, Image 40)

🛑 CONCLUSION CORRECTED — ENTRY 195 IS ELIMINATED, NOT A CANDIDATE. This note as first written (4 June 2026) wrongly treated Entry 195's missing given name as merely "illegible (Nottaufe)" and concluded the candidate was strengthened. That conclusion is retracted. Per Peter Schräder's expert paleographic read (retraction dated 4 May 2026, see correspondence/HStAM_Marburg_Stammrolle_Anfrage_May2026.md), the "Männl: Köster" entry with no given name, the ‡/ungetauft marker, and the empty godparent column is the standard recording of a newborn who died unbaptized — so Entry 195 is NOT Charles and is eliminated. The "no burial found → emigration" reasoning is a false inference (an unbaptized infant death is noted in the baptism register, not necessarily the burials register). The investigation currently has no active candidate. The factual observations below (dates, parents' names, sibling scan, marriage-register negatives) remain valid as data; only the "strengthened candidate" framing is void.

Date: 4 June 2026 · Researcher: Jed Johnson (+ assistant, live Archion session) Register: Deisel, "Taufen 1830–1861" = Archion churchRegister/218842, Image 40, Gemeinde Deisel · Jahr 1835 · Monat Januar · Seite 39, entries 194–198. Method: Archion AI text-recognition ("Erkannter Text" / KI-Texterkennung) for the full page, cross-checked against a max-zoom manuscript read of each cell (the AI text is explicitly flagged by Archion as machine-generated and error-prone, so every load-bearing claim below was confirmed visually).

Why this session ran

Three items flagged as never-completed across Sessions 26/61/70 were the reason: 1. The godparents column of Entry 195 had never been read. 2. The child's given name ("Justus") was repeatedly flagged as "best reading, residual uncertainty." 3. The Köster vs. Konze surname question rested on a single 1850 confirmation-class sibling, not the baptism register itself.

All three are now addressed directly from the baptism register.

Findings

1. Entry 195 core facts — CONFIRMED at source

  • Place: Deisel, Gänsewinkel, House No. 1.
  • Birth: "den zwanzigsten … nachts 1 Uhr" → 20 January 1835, ~1 a.m. (confirms the Session 70 correction; the older "23–24 January" is wrong).
  • Baptism: emergency baptism (Nottaufe); the remark column reads "getauft am 23. 35" → formally registered/baptized 23 January 1835.
  • Father: Joh. Heinrich Köster (occupation uncertain — the AI read "Kaufmann," the manuscript line looks closer to "Tagl." = Tagelöhner; leave open).
  • Mother: Wilhelmine geb. Hillebrand.

2. Surname = KÖSTER — now corroborated by the baptism register itself

The father's surname in Entry 195 reads "Köster" directly in the baptism register, independent of the 1850 confirmation-class sibling (Ernst Wilhelm Köster). This strengthens the Session 71 conclusion from "single-source" to "two independent sources."

3. Child's given name — "JUSTUS" RETRACTED

At maximum zoom the child-name column reads only "Männl: Köster" (sex + surname) — there is no legibly recorded given name. This is typical for a Nottaufe of a weak newborn. The prior "Justus" reading is not supported and is retracted. Consequence: the long-standing "Justus ≠ L. Carl / Charles" objection dissolves — the German record assigns no given name that could conflict with the American "L. Carl."

4. No death notation for Entry 195 (corrects an AI-HTR trap)

Entry 195's only remark is "getauft am 23. 35." There is no death cross on its row, and its godparents column is a blank diagonal slash (no godparents — consistent with Nottaufe). Important: Archion's AI text-recognition mis-attributed two items from the next row to Entry 195 — - the godparent "Anna Kath. Meinebrosse geb. Weike", and - the death cross "† … 29 … 1835" — both of which actually belong to Entry 196 (Sophie Weike, b. 28 January 1835), an infant who died the day after birth. Anyone reading the raw AI transcript could wrongly conclude Entry 195's child died in infancy. It did not. The "no death → emigration" inference therefore still holds.

5. Entry 194 — earlier reading looks wrong

Entry 194 (the "parallel" January-1835 male, child Ludwig Friedrich) reads at source: father "Andreas Köster, Ackermann" × wife "Elisabeth geb. Schildknecht." This contradicts the prior record in Kontze_Family_Tree_and_Research_Paths.md ("Andreas Konze × Elisabeth Pfeffkumpf"). The "Schildknecht" maiden name is notable because it ties to the "Klaus Andreas Kontze alias Schildknecht" thread. Action: re-read Entry 194 before trusting the Konze/Pfeffkumpf version.

Net effect on the Charles Konzen identification

Entry 195 is ELIMINATED (Peter Schräder, 4 May 2026): a male Köster newborn who died unbaptized — not Charles. The factual observations gathered this session stand as data, but they do NOT support Entry 195 as a candidate: - the "Männl: Köster" name cell with no given name + ‡/ungetauft + empty godparent column = the convention for an infant who died unbaptized; - "no burial in register 218851" does NOT mean emigration — an unbaptized infant death is recorded in the baptism register itself; - the surname-was-Köster reading is correct but moot, since this family is not Charles's.

Current status of the whole investigation: NO active candidate. This 4 June pass was a verification check of prior research; its value is confirming that Entry 195 was correctly eliminated and should stop resurfacing, and clearing away the false "emigration" inference that kept reviving it.

Recommended next Archion steps (all now framed under "Köster," not "Konze")

  1. Marriage of Joh. Heinrich Köster × Wilhelmine Hildebrand — re-run the marriage search under Köster (the 14 prior negative registers were searched under "Konze"). Deisel Trauungen 1830–1901 (= 218860) and pre-1830 Deisel marriages are the first targets.
  2. Other children of Köster × Hildebrand in KB 218842 — page-scan 1830–1845 for siblings; each gives an independent surname/occupation reading and may show the family leaving (last entry before emigration).
  3. Re-read Entry 194 (Andreas Köster/Konze × Elisabeth Schildknecht/Pfeffkumpf) to resolve the §5 contradiction.
  4. Occupation of Entry 195's father — settle "Kaufmann" vs. "Tagelöhner" at max zoom; it materially changes the family's social profile and which U.S. records to expect.

Session 84b — Sibling page-scan + marriage search (same day, 4 June 2026)

A. Sibling scan of KB 218842 (Deisel Taufen 1830–1861), images ~15–95 (≈1832–1841)

Method: AI-transcript filter for "Hildebrand/Hillebrand" on every page, then max-zoom visual verification of each hit (the AI scrambles columns and "Köster" is ubiquitous in Deisel, so visual confirmation of the father+mother pairing was required).

Result — only TWO children of Joh. Heinrich Köster × Wilhelmine geb. Hildebrand exist in the whole register:

Image Year Entry Child Birth House Notes
40 1835 195 (no given name recorded) — male 20 Jan 1835 Gänsewinkel Nr. 1 Nottaufe; the target record
~51 1836 261 read "Karl Wilhelm" (project had "Ernst Wilhelm") 16 Aug 1836 Deisel birth date matches the project's sibling exactly, but the given name reads Karl Wilhelm, not Ernst Wilhelm — flag
  • No earlier children (nothing 1832–1834) → the couple married ≈1834.
  • No later children; the family disappears after Aug 1836 → emigration window ≈1837–1839 (last baptism before the gap = the Aug 1836 child).
  • Corroborating: on image 46 (Nov 1835) "Wilhelmine Köster geb. Hildebrand" appears as a godmother — alive and resident in Deisel between her two births.
  • ⚠ Two readings to resolve by expert/archive: (a) whether Entry 195 carries an infant-death/burial marginal note — my own max-zoom read found only "getauft am 23.35" with the death-cross belonging to the next entry (Sophie Weike, Entry 196), but the second pass flagged a possible faint death note on/near Entry 195; treat the child's survival as probable but not certain. (b) the 1836 sibling's given name (Karl Wilhelm vs Ernst Wilhelm), which underlies the Session 73 surname-matching.

B. Marriage search under "Köster" — Deisel Trauungen 1830–1901 (register 218860)

Note: this register is the tail of the long Deisel scan; its images run 1293–~1499, so the "images 1300–1308" the old logs cited were correct. Used Archion's AI text-recognition (ordered per page; queue is slow).

Searched the marriage window implied by the ≈1834 marriage date: - 1834 marriages (images 1307–1309): several Köster families present (e.g. a groom Köster Steinhauer son of Andreas Köster; brides née Köster) but NO "Joh. Heinrich Köster," and NO Hildebrand bride at all. - 1835 marriages (image 1310): no Köster, no Hildebrand.

Conclusion: the Köster × Hildebrand marriage is NOT in the Deisel register for 1834–1835. Combined with the prior (Konze-framed) negatives across 14 parishes, the most likely explanations are: (1) the couple married in Wilhelmine Hildebrand's home parish (marriages were typically recorded where the bride resided), or (2) they married before 1833 (less likely, since no children appear before 1835).

C. Revised next steps

  1. Identify the Hildebrand home parish and search its marriage register ~1833–1834 for Joh. Heinrich Köster × Wilhelmine Hildebrand. This is now the single highest-value German lead. (Cross-check against the project's existing "Hildebrand" emigration thread, Sessions 75/77/78.)
  2. Pull the 1836 sibling baptism (KB 218842, ~image 51) at max zoom to settle Karl Wilhelm vs Ernst Wilhelm and read its house number.
  3. Get a definitive expert/HTR read of Entry 195's remarks column to settle whether the child died in infancy (the one thing that would eliminate the candidate).
  4. With the emigration window ≈1837–1839, re-focus U.S./passenger searches on 1837–1840 arrivals (earlier than some prior assumptions).
