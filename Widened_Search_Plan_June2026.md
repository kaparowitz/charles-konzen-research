# Widened Search Plan — looking for *a Carl*, not "a Konze"

**Date:** 19 June 2026 · **For:** my own Archion work (the undigitised tier is where Jim's September trip fits)

## Why widen

The negative-results audit shows the prior search had **two built-in blind spots**:

1. **Surname was filtered to the Konze pool.** Every core result reads "no **Konze-pool** male b. ~1835." The June 2026 adjacent sweep only broadened the criterion to "Konze-pool **or illegitimate** male." A *legitimate* boy named Carl with a non-Konze surname (e.g. an *n‑s‑n* ending) would have been read straight past.
2. **The window collapsed to ~Jan 1835 once I left the core.** The 11-parish adjacent sweep read **only Jan–Feb 1835**; Friedrichsfeld stopped at **1836**. Nothing covered 1833–34 or 1837–41 outside the core — yet several U.S. records point to 1837–1841.

Plus a third: most registers searched were the present-day Evangelical/Lutheran books; the **Reformed** registers and the wider **Kreis Kassel** parishes are largely uncovered.

## New standing criteria — apply to every register from here on

- **Given name is the anchor, not the surname:** any **Carl / Karl** (German form of Charles).
- **Surname OPEN** — especially endings fitting **\*n‑s‑n** (Gansen, Ganssen, Gonsen, Gonse, Gonson, Hansen, Jansen, Nansen, Lansen, Mansen, Konsen, Konzen…), **legitimate *or* illegitimate**.
- **Birth window 1833–1841** (priority core **1834–1838**). 26 Jan 1835 stays the working anchor but is no longer the boundary.
- **Read both confessions** — Lutheran *and* Reformed — wherever a parish had both.
