## Notable Observations

### Absence of Direct Konze Burials (1919–1925)

The complete absence of confirmed Konze burials in this 6-year stretch is significant but not unexpected. By 1919, the known Konze patriarchs born in the 1825–1870s range would be:

- **Heinrich Carl Conze** (b. 1825): died 1914 (Session 46)
- **Heinrich Conze Ackermann** (b. 1835): died 1919 (Session 46)
- **Heinrich Konze "Ste."** (b. ~1830s): likely died pre-1910
- **Heinrich Konze at 141** (b. ~1830s–40s): likely died ~1890s
- **Heinrich Konze Schmiedmeister** (b. ~1830s–40s): died post-1909
- **Heinrich Konze "Vt."** (b. ~1870s): possibly still alive in the 1920s
- **Heinrich Conze Schuhmacher** (b. ~1860s): possibly still alive in the 1920s

The gap suggests that most of the older generation has died before 1919, while the younger generation (born 1870s–1900s) either survived into the 1930s+ or moved away from Deisel. The potential "geb. Konze" references (women born Konze who married into other families) are also absent, unlike the 1907–1912 period which had 7 such references.

### Meinbreße Family Prominence

The Meinbreße family continues to appear frequently: 4 entries in 1921–1924 (Adolf, Friedrich, Carl Ludwig Heinrich, and Sophie née Theile wife of Heinrich). This family's persistent connection to the Konze family through Ludwig Konze's first marriage makes every Meinbreße entry a potential indirect link.

### Köster Family Connections

Three Köster entries appear in 1921–1923. The Köster family is connected to Konze through Entry 406 (Wilhelmine geb. Konze married into the Köster family). The concentration of Köster deaths in this period (Elise née Köster, Anna née Köster, and Philipp Ludwig Köster) suggests this family was aging in parallel with the Konze patriarchs.

### Year-End Totals Stable

Both 1923 and 1924 show exactly 19 deaths each. For comparison, the war year 1915 had 15 deaths (plus war dead counted separately). The post-war period shows stable civilian mortality.

### Image Navigation Issues

The Archion viewer's combobox page selector has a consistent offset of ~2 between the internal value and the displayed image number. This required using the Chrome MCP `form_input` tool for reliable navigation rather than the triple-click-type-enter approach used in previous sessions. The `find` tool successfully located the combobox element (ref_189) for direct value setting.
