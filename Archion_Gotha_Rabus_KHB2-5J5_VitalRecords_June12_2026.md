## Key discovery: the family's parish was the Augustinerkirche (St. Augustin)
Gotha town had several Lutheran congregations on Archion: **St. Augustin** (Augustinerkirche),
**St. Margarethen** (Margarethenkirche / main town church), Schlosskirche, Garnisonskirche,
and a Stiftskirche congregation. The St. Margarethen baptism index for 1803–1808 contains
**no Rabus** (R-page checked: Rauchhauer, Raßgeber, Reinhardt… no "Rab-"). The St. Augustin
Taufen 1801–1808 name index **does** list "**Rabus, … Wolfgang …**". → the family belonged to
**St. Augustin**.

5. **Groom's birth margin annotation "* 28.4.1805 Aug. fol. 161"** — the register itself cross-references

## ✗ DEATH / BURIAL (9 Apr 1872) — searched all 3 Gotha town parishes, NOT FOUND (June 13 2026)
**Conclusion:** Georg Christoph Rabus's death/burial as dated 9 April 1872 is **not present** in any of
the three realistic Gotha-town Lutheran burial registers for 1872–1875. Specifically:
- **St. Margarethen, Beerdigungen 1872–1875** (churchRegister **353478**): scanned late-March → late-April
  1872 (entries #67–83, images 12–13). The death on **9 April 1872 is entry #77 = "August Carl Ernst
  Fischer"**, a day-labourer's child — **not** Rabus. No "Rabus" anywhere in that stretch (names: Ditter,
  Hüllner, Gilbert, von Westernhagen, Storch, Angermeyer, Wenk, Lende, Gernandt, Schneider, Fischer,
  Weber, Schütz, Götz, Zahn, Schleicher …).
- **Schlosskirche, Beerdigungen 1837–1875** (churchRegister **353586**): used the register's own
  alphabetical **name index** (img 184) — the **"R" section begins at "Reinhardt"; there is no "Rab-"
  entry at all** → no Rabus burial in the court parish 1837–1875.
- **St. Augustin, Beerdigungen 1872–1875** (churchRegister **353549**): (a) chronological April-1872
  scan (img 17, entries #88–94) shows deaths on 5, 6, **8**, **11**, 11, 13, 13 April — a **gap at 9–10
  April**, and **no Rabus** (Steinhardt, Hofmann, Reinhardt, Weber, Hartung, Holz…); (b) the register's
  **name index "R" page** (img 200, left) lists Röse, Roth, Reinhardt, Rüede, Roßbach, Reuter, Reichardt,
  Rudloff, Rudolph, Remmel, Reichlein — **again no "Rabus."**
- Not searched: **Garnisonskirche** (military parish — implausible for a civilian master shoemaker).
  St. Augustin **[Nord]/[Süd]** only begin post-1876, so they can't hold an 1872 burial.

**Most likely explanations (for follow-up):**
1. The **"9 April 1872" date comes from the 1950s LDS family-group sheet**, the same source that mis-stated
   other facts — so the **day, month, or year may be wrong**. Worth scanning St. Margarethen / St. Augustin

3. Earliest tie to St. Augustin was a faint margin note on his baptism that *looked* like a death

## Image-extraction pipeline (works — used for the baptism Memory upload)
Archion blocks direct downloads and the JS bridge truncates/blocks base64, so: (1) view the page in
fullscreen so all level-11 tiles load; (2) in-page JS composites the signed tiles onto a `<canvas>` →
`toDataURL` (full-resolution JPEG); (3) stash the data URL in `window.name`; (4) navigate the **same tab**
to the FamilySearch Memories page (window.name survives cross-origin navigation); (5) in-page JS rebuilds a
`File` and injects it into the upload `<input>` via `DataTransfer` + dispatched `change`. The baptism page
was uploaded this way.
