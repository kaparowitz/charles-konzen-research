### Key Finding: Zimmermann/Wolfert Book (Critical Gap)

The 1859 Bremen→NY passenger group — **Joh (age 59), Antje (age 54), Anke (age 23)** — appears at page 41 of Zimmermann/Wolfert. Ancestry's collection 7486 indexes names and ages but does NOT capture the place-of-origin field from the original passenger lists. The physical book (available at major genealogical libraries) must be consulted to determine whether this group originated from Deisel/Kurhessen.

Note: The female names Antje and Anke are distinctly Frisian/North German and unusual for Kurhessen/Deisel. This raises a question about whether this particular group is related to our Deisel Köster family or is a coincidental surname match. Resolution requires the physical book.

### "L." Initial Mystery

Charles appears in Civil War records as **"L. Charles Gannsen."** The "L." initial remains unresolved:
- Possibilities: Ludwig, Leopold, Lorenz, Luca, or a Kurrent script misread of "J." (Justus)
- In Kurrent handwriting, capital "J" and capital "L" can be confused by untrained readers
- No German record has been found linking "Justus Köster" to an "L." given name

### Conclusion — Task #26

The Ancestry search is effectively exhausted for this research phase. The American records confirm:
- Charles Gannsen/Johnson was born approximately January 1835 in Germany (Kurhessen)
- He immigrated approximately 1859
- He lived in St. Louis, Missouri
- His marriage record (St. Marcus Church, 1866) states birthplace "aus Deisse, Kreis Cassel, Churhessen"
- The surname "Gannsen" is documented in city directories and Civil War records

No Ancestry record directly links "Justus Köster" (Deisel Entry 195) to "Charles Gannsen" (St. Louis). The connection remains circumstantial but compelling given the convergence of birth year, immigration year, origin location, and surname derivation. The Zimmermann/Wolfert physical book consultation remains the next actionable Ancestry-based step.

**Task #26: COMPLETED (Ancestry search exhausted; Zimmermann/Wolfert book requires in-person library visit)**

### Name Index Analysis (Images 1480–1499)

Beginning at Image 1480, the register contains a comprehensive **Namensregister** (alphabetical name index) covering the full KB 218860 register (all years 1831–1901). The index format: each alphabetical section lists grooms (Männlicher column) and brides (Weiblicher column) with their corresponding Seite (physical page number) in the register.

**Image 1480** — F and G sections  
**Image 1484** — **K and L sections** (critical image)

The K section, Männlicher (groom) column, was read in detail:
- All Köster groom entries appear at **Seite numbers ≤ ~95**
- Seite 95 corresponds to the mid-1870s at latest; the Volume II content begins at Seite ~129 (confirmed: Image 1422 header reads "Seite 129, Jahr 1877")
- No Köster groom entry appears at Seite ≥ 129

**Image 1490** — Q and R sections (confirming index covers Volume II)
- Q section Männlicher column shows entries at **Seite 129–177** — confirming that Volume II entries (1878-1901) ARE indexed
- R section Männlicher column shows entries spanning both early (Seite 28–29) and late (Seite 125–159) sections, further confirming comprehensive indexing
- The absence of Köster entries at Seite ≥ 129 in the K section is therefore **definitive**: no Köster groom in the 1878–1901 period

**Image 1499** — R and S sections (final image of register)

### Note on Köster Brides in the Index

The K section's Weiblicher (bride) column does show several "Köster" female entries — women with the Köster surname marrying men of other surnames. These are from the Köster family remaining in Deisel (likely Ernst Wilhelm Köster's daughters, or daughters of other Köster family branches). These bride entries do NOT affect the Justus Köster search. They are noted here for completeness.
