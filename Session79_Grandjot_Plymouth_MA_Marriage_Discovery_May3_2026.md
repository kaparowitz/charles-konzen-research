## Part 1 — Carl Louis Herwig: BREAKTHROUGH

### 1A. Search strategy

Starting from the May 2 finding that the family Anglicized to "Herig" in NJ records, I searched Ancestry US collections for `Carl Louis Herig` b. 1830 Hesse and several variants. None of the Hesse-/Hesse-Kassel-tagged returns matched (most were Berlin/Brandenburg or Hannover-born name-twins). The **Hanover-born Louis Herwig in 1860 St. Louis Ward 9** (b. ~1830, confectioner, wife Virginia, kids Caroline & Francis) was confirmed via Find a Grave (Louis J Herwig, d. 26 Aug 1870, Bethany Section St. Peter's Cemetery, Pagedale, St. Louis Co. MO; memorial 87945136) to be from **Hanover not Hesse-Kassel** — a name-twin, NOT our man.

Pivoting from the groom side to the **bride side** broke the case open. The FS profile records Carl Louis Herwig's spouse as `Catherine Sharda · PZXN-32N`. A direct Ancestry search for "Catherine Sharda" returned a 1855 Plymouth, Massachusetts marriage record at the very top of the results.

### 1B. The marriage record — two independent indexes corroborate

Both Ancestry collections that index the Plymouth Town Vital Records confirm the same marriage entry:

**Source A**: Massachusetts, U.S., Town and Vital Records, 1620–1988 — collection 2495, records 14571973 (bride) and 4579239 (groom)
**Source B**: Massachusetts, U.S., Marriage Records, 1840–1915 — collection 2511, record 6671306

| Field | Source A | Source B |
|---|---|---|
| Groom name | **Lewes Herweg** | **Lewis Harwig** |
| Groom marriage age | 24 | 24 |
| Groom birth year | abt 1831 | abt 1831 |
| Groom birth place | — | **Germany** |
| Groom's father | **John Herweg** | **John Harwig** |
| Bride name | Catherine Sharda | Catherine Shanda |
| Bride age | 22 | 22 |
| Bride birth year | abt 1833 | abt 1833 |
| Bride's father | **Christian Sharda** | — |
| Marriage date | **6 Oct 1855** | **6 Oct 1855** |
| Marriage place | **Plymouth, Massachusetts, USA** | **Plymouth, Massachusetts, USA** |

### 1C. Why this is unambiguously Carl Louis Herwig PCXT-X3M

- **Bride name match**: "Catherine Sharda" is the exact spouse already on his FS profile — this is the linking pin. The name is uncommon enough that this is not coincidental.
- **Father's name match**: "John Herweg / Harwig" is the unambiguous Anglicization of *Johannes Herwig* (LRZG-NML, b. 22 Aug 1802 Trendelburg, the 1854 Sonter Johnny emigrant who declared Massachusetts as destination on the manifest).
- **Age match**: marriage age 24 in October 1855 places groom's birth in November 1830 — Carl Louis was born 27 Nov 1830, so on 6 Oct 1855 he was 24 years 10 months (turning 25 in Nov). Exact.
- **Birth place match**: "Germany" is consistent with Hesse-Kassel (vague but compatible).
- **Geographic match**: Plymouth, MA is the very destination declared on the 1854 Sonter Johnny manifest by his parents. He went there too.

### 1D. What this rewrites

The May 2 conclusion that "the family did not actually settle in Massachusetts" needs nuance:
- The **parents and the four 1854-manifested children** indeed pushed on to NYC within months of arrival (Jeanette in NYC Ward 13 by the 1855 NY State Census, six months after the 1854 NYC arrival).
- But the **eldest surviving son, Carl Louis** evidently disembarked or stopped in **Plymouth, Mass.** long enough to marry there 6 Oct 1855 — only ~10 months after the family arrived in NYC.
- Plymouth was therefore a real intended destination that at least one Herwig actually used.

Carl Louis was not on the Sonter Johnny — the manifest "Carl Herwig 1842" is Carl Peter Herwig (b. 1841, age ~13 in 1854). Carl Louis must have emigrated separately, either before or shortly after his parents.

### 1E. Two candidate prior NY arrivals — neither yet decisive

| Index name | Age | Birth | Departure | Arrival NY | Ship | Manifest "destination" |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Carl Herrig (NARA M237; Ancestry coll 7488 rec 6183) | 20 | abt 1831 | Bremen | 23 Sep 1851 | *Young* | (blank — "Germany" only as origin) |
| Luis Harney [Luis Harweg] (NARA M237; Ancestry coll 7488 rec 241620) | 22 | abt 1830 | Bremen | 5 Apr 1852 | *Ocean* | "St Luis" |

The 1851 *Young* arrival has the better birth-year match (1831 vs 1830) but recorded only "Germany" as origin. The 1852 *Ocean* arrival has the perfect 1830 birth year but a "St Luis" destination annotation that more plausibly fits the Hanover-born St. Louis confectioner Louis J Herwig (the name-twin), and its other indexed forms include "Harney" — a transcription oddity that needs original-image inspection. Both candidates need the original manifest images pulled before either can be confirmed as Carl Louis's actual crossing.

## Part 2 — Caroline Phillipine Herwig: NEGATIVE

Searches: `Caroline Herig b. 1837 Hesse`, `Caroline Herwig b. 1837 Germany`, all priority United States, multiple Ancestry collections.

Returns surveyed (none match):
- Caroline Marie Louise Herig — b. 12 May 1837 Berlin, Brandenburg (wrong region)

- Caroline Herwig — 1880 Philadelphia, b. abt 1837 Pennsylvania (US-born)
- Caroline Herwig — 1880 Peoria IL, b. abt 1838 Germany (no surname match for husband, lived Peoria not NJ)
- Caroline Herwig [née Weller] — d. 1916 Philadelphia, b. 4 Jan 1836 Lancaster (US-born)

**Conclusion**: she or her sister Maria Magdalena (b. 1835) is the manifest "Agnes" Herwig (age ~1836) of the 1854 Sonter Johnny voyage; the other's whereabouts after the 1854 family emigration are still unknown to indexed Ancestry records. Per Peter's *3 x emmigration 1857* chart, she may have crossed in 1857 separately from her parents — but I have not yet located that crossing.

## Part 3 — Johannes Grandjot 1855 emigration: NEGATIVE

Searches: `Johannes Grandjot b. 1828`, `John Grandjot b. 1828`, `_Grandjot priority US`, `John Grandjon b. 1828`, all against the full Ancestry US collection set.

Returns surveyed (none match):
- 9 results total for "Johannes Grandjot 1828" — all are Mexican Juan Vargas/Grandos, French Grandjon/Girandot, Finnish Grandt, Australian Grandbut. Zero Hesse-origin matches.
- "John Grandjon b. 1828" returned 418 hits — overwhelmingly English Grandons and Welsh/Irish unrelated families.
- The unique Hesse-origin Grandjot in the broad result set (Albert Grandjot, b. 1895 Kassel Hesse) is a generation later and unrelated to the Schöneberg branch.

**Conclusion**: Johannes Grandjot's 1855 emigration is documented only on Peter's German-side chart. No NY/Bremen passenger list entry under any obvious spelling, no US census appearance in any state under any obvious spelling. His US life remains entirely undocumented in indexed Ancestry sources. Either:
- he Anglicized so heavily the surname is lost to indexing, or
- he came on a ship whose manifest is not yet in the indexed collections, or
- he died young in the US before the 1860 census.

The chart-mention of "his wife later hanged herself in America" suggests a real US life, just one that hasn't surfaced.
