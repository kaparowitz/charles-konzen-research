## Assessment
- **Charles is not identified among the documented in-window baptisms.** The two surnames that
  matter — a *Carl* AND a *Gans* born ~26 Jan 1835 — do not coincide in any record found: the
  Carls are non-Gans or a generation too old; the 1835 Gans is "Andreas August."
- **But the Gans→Gannsen branch now has concrete, living families in exactly the right
  corridor and decade** (Oedelsheim turner + innkeeper lines; Heisebeck tailor + labourer
  lines; plus Lippoldsberg "Ganson"). A Charles of this pool would most plausibly be a Gans
  **who emigrated**, recorded in US sources under "Gannsen/Ganson," whose German baptism is
  either (a) an as-yet-unread leaf on a shuffled reel, (b) in a household whose run we haven't
  fully reconstructed, or (c) in a neighbouring Weser-corridor parish.

## Emigration / passenger-list thread (worked this session)
- **The Bremen→US channel was already exhaustively cleared** (see `immigration/bremen-reconstruction.html`):
  1853–Aug 1862, NY (M237) / New Orleans (M259) / Baltimore (M255) / Galveston (M575), ~8,873+
  passengers, under a **Carl/Charles given-name + Konze/Gansen/-en/-on surname** filter →
  **0 Charles candidates.** The tracked surname variants were all -en/-on endings
  (Gannsen·Gansen·Gonnson·Gonsen·Gunnsen·Konze·Konzen·Konsen·Kontze·Hansen) — **plain "Gans"
  was *not* a tracked variant**, and the search assumed the forename Carl. Our new German finding
  ("Andreas August Gans" — plain Gans, non-Carl forename) fits exactly that residual gap.
- **NEW this session — plain "Gans" at New Orleans (Ancestry coll. 7484), birth ~1835 / arrival
  ~1859:** 40 results. **No clean Charles match.** The only 1858–1862 arrival is **Wilhelmine
  Gans** (f, b. ~1836, Bremen → NOLA, 29 Oct 1860). The Bremen *male* Gans both arrived **1851**,
  not 1859, with wrong forenames: **George Gans** (b. ~1834, Bremen) and **Paul Gans** (b. ~1832,
  Bremen). Other Gans arrivals are Le Havre/Havana/later. So the plain-"Gans" NOLA gap is also
  **negative** for a Carl/Charles Gans ~1835 arriving ~1859.
- **NEW — New York port (Ancestry coll. 7488), plain "Gans" b.~1835 / arr.~1859:** 1,159 results
  (NY was the big port). Relevance-top German/Bremen males near the window, **none a clean Charles**:
  **August Gans** (b. ~1834, Bremen→NY **1861**, ship *Laura*); **Georg Gans** (b. ~1837, Bremen→NY
  **1862**, ship *Ottilie*); **John Gan** (b. 1837, **1859** but Liverpool/English — unlikely German);
  plus earlier Bremen Gans (Jacob 1857, Mandel 1857, Abraham 1853). The most suggestive is **August
  Gans (1861)** — "August" is a recurring forename in the *Oedelsheim* Gans family (Andreas **August**
  Gans b.1835; Carl **August** Gans the godparent) — but forename/year don't match Charles; not confirmed.
- **George Gans manifest pulled (NOLA):** male, **age 17 (b. ~1834), Bremen → New Orleans on the ship
  _Diana_, 8 Nov 1851**; indexed origin only "Bremen" (no town). A young Gans in the right channel, but
  wrong forename + 8 yrs before Charles's stated 1859.
- **Residual emigration vectors still open:** (a) other spellings — Ganss/Gantz/Ganz/Gons — at all
  ports; (b) **Antwerp / Le Havre / Rotterdam** departures; (c) Baltimore/Galveston *plain-Gans*; (d)
  Charles under a wholly different name. Worth a closer look someday: **August Gans (NY 1861)** and
  **George/Paul Gans (NOLA 1851)** — all Bremen-channel Gans males of ~the right age, none matching
  Charles's stated forename+year, but candidates for the *family's* emigration trail.
