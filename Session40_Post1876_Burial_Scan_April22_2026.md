## Analysis

### Child Mortality
Of the 8 Konze entries found, 6 are children (ages 2–11). Only entry 31 (Heinrich Wilhelm Konze, age 33) and entry 184 (Maria Konze née Lemme, adult widow) are adult deaths. The high child mortality rate reflects general 19th-century rural conditions.

### The Heinrich Konze Numbering System
Entry 168's "Heinrich Konze V." is the most significant structural finding. German parishes assigned Roman numeral suffixes to distinguish people with identical names. Finding "V." means the Deisel parish records contained at least 5 different men named Heinrich Konze. J.H. Konze #2 ("Johann Heinrich Konze") would likely have his own designation — possibly "Johann Heinrich Konze I" or "II" etc.

### The Lemme Connection
Maria née Lemme appears in two contexts:
- Entry 48: Mother of Catharina Wilhelmine Konze (father: Heinrich Konze, Ackermann)
- Entry 184: Widow of Johannes Friedrich Konze, Ackermann

This could mean: (a) Maria née Lemme married twice — first Johannes Friedrich Konze, then Heinrich Konze; or (b) two different women named Maria née Lemme married into the Konze family.

### J.H. Konze #2 — Still Not Found
The primary search target — J.H. Konze #2 (Johann Heinrich Konze, born ~1808, Ackermann, married to Wilhelmine née Hildebrand) — was NOT found in the 1876–1882 range. If born ~1808, he would be 68–74 years old in this period. His death could occur in the 1883–1900 range (ages 75–92), requiring continued scanning from Image 31.

The Hillebrand references (especially entry 122, "Witwer von Wilhelmine née Hillebrand") suggest the Hillebrand family had multiple daughters in Deisel, so Wilhelmine Hildebrand (wife of J.H. Konze #2) may not be the only Wilhelmine Hildebrand in the records.
