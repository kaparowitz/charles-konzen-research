## 2. Cluster K — Page 90, Image 754: Dionysius Kontze Baptism

### 2.1 Transcription

**Location:** Left page of image 754 = manuscript page 90
**Year header:** "Baptizat: 1737" transitioning to 1738
**Position:** Bottom portion of page 90, below Uffelman entries

**Entry (best reading):**

> [Date: likely late 1737 or early 1738]
> Getauft **Dionysius Kontze**
> **Andras Kontze** u[nd] Ackerm[ann] [und] b. Bürger [allhier?]
> [Mutter:] **Anna Catharina** geb. **Kleina** höy [= from] **Deisel**
> [Pate/Godfather:] **Dionysius Kontze**, ein dasiger Bürger [a local citizen of Trendelburg]

### 2.2 Analysis

This is the **baptism of Nies Kontze Senior** himself (Dionysius → Denis/Denies → Nies).

**Critical findings:**

1. **Father identified: ANDREAS KONTZE, Ackermann (farmer) and Bürger (citizen) of Trendelburg.** This pushes the Kontze lineage back one more generation — Andreas Kontze is now the earliest known Kontze patriarch in Trendelburg.

2. **Mother identified: Anna Catharina née Kleina from Deisel.** This establishes the first known Kontze–Deisel connection in the baptism register. The Kleina family of Deisel is a new surname in the research.

3. **Godfather: Dionysius Kontze, "ein dasiger Bürger."** The child was named after his godfather, who was also a Kontze — likely an uncle, great-uncle, or older relative. This means there was ALREADY a Dionysius Kontze in Trendelburg before this child was born (~1737). This earlier Dionysius is a previously unknown individual.

4. **Date range:** The entry falls between "Baptizat: 1737" entries and January 1738 entries, placing Nies Kontze Senior's birth at approximately late 1737 or very early 1738. This is consistent with having marriageable daughters by the early 1750s (he was deceased by February 1752 per Marriage A).

5. **The Deisel connection:** Anna Catharina née Kleina "höy Deisel" — this is the earliest documented Kontze–Deisel family link, predating the known marriage of Johannes Kontze × Anna Margaretha Thielen of Deisel (1780) by over 40 years.

### 2.3 Genealogical Implications

**Updated lineage:**
- **Generation -1 (NEW):** Andreas Kontze, Ackermann und Bürger, Trendelburg × Anna Catharina née Kleina of Deisel
  - Son: **Dionysius (Nies) Kontze** (b. ~1737/38, d. before Feb 1752)
  - Godfather Dionysius Kontze (elder, "dasiger Bürger") — relationship to Andreas unknown but likely brother or uncle

- **Generation 0:** Nies Kontze Senior (= Dionysius) × [wife unknown]
  - Daughter: Anna Catharina Kontze (married Feb 24, 1752, Marriage A)
  - Daughter: Maria Elisabeth née Kontze (married ~Oct 1755, Marriage D — widow remarrying Joannes Schaub)

**Open question:** Is the father "Andreas Kontze" on page 90 the SAME Andreas Kontze listed as a Generation 0 patriarch (deceased before Aug 1762, per Marriage G)? The dates don't quite work — if Andreas fathered Dionysius in ~1737, he was likely born ~1700–1715, and if he died before 1762, he'd be ~47–62 years old. That's plausible. But the marriage records describe a separate "Andreas Kontze" as a Gen 0 patriarch with his own children. This Andreas could be the SAME man (Dionysius's father) or a different Andreas (Dionysius's brother or cousin). **Page 48 (earliest Andreas baptism) should clarify.**

### 3.2 Page 168 (Image 793, left page) — Two Kontze Sisters (Incidental Discovery)

**Year:** ~1757
**Entry:** Baptism of Anna (Christina) Katharina, daughter of David K[astings?], Tagelöhner (day laborer).

> Mutter: **Maria Christina** geb. **Kontzen**
> [Patin/Godmother:] **Clara Margaretha Kontzen**, "der Mutter Schwester" (the mother's SISTER)

**Later annotation:** † 31/10/1821 Trendelburg (child died October 31, 1821)

**Critical findings:**
1. **Two previously unknown Kontze women identified:** Maria Christina Kontze and Clara Margaretha Kontze, confirmed as SISTERS.
2. Maria Christina married David K[astings?], a day laborer — married out of the Kontze family.
3. Clara Margaretha was unmarried at the time of this godmother appearance (no married surname given).
4. **These sisters must be daughters of one of the Generation 0 patriarchs** (Nies Kontze Senior, Joan Henrif Kontze, Arnold Philipp Kontze, or Andreas Kontze). Their father's identity is not stated in this entry but may be determinable from other baptism entries in the priority queue.

### 3.3 Page 169 (Image 793, right page) — Arnold Philipp Kontze's Son: Joan Christian

**Year:** 1757
**Entry (transcription):**

> d 8. Maij [May 8, 1757] getauft als **Arnold Philips Kontzen** Ackermann
> Söhnlein: die Mutter ist **Anna Dorothea** geb. **Asselmann**
> das Kind war geboren d 4 [May] morgens 3 Uhr
> [Pate/Godfather:] Joannes Wegner
> des Kindts Nahm: **Joan Christian**

### Analysis:

**Child:** Joan Christian Kontze, born May 4, 1757 (3:00 AM), baptized May 8, 1757.
**Father:** Arnold Philipp Kontze, Ackermann (farmer)
**Mother:** Anna Dorothea née **Asselmann**

**CRITICAL DISCREPANCY — "Asselmann" vs. "Uffelmann":**

The July 1754 marriage entry (Marriage C) identifies Arnold Philipp's second wife as "Anna Dorothea Uffelmannin, des Raths Verwandten Joannes Uffelmanns jüngste Tochter" (youngest daughter of councillor Joannes Uffelmann). But this 1757 baptism entry reads the maiden name as "Asselmann."

Possible explanations:
1. **Kurrent script misread:** In 18th-century Kurrent, "Uff-" and "Ass-" can look very similar depending on the scribe's hand. The marriage and baptism entries were likely written by different scribes or at different times.
2. **The same person:** Given that both entries refer to "Anna Dorothea" as wife of "Arnold Philipp Kontze, Ackermann," these almost certainly describe the same woman. The maiden name should be reconciled on re-examination.
3. **"Uffelmann" is more likely correct** because the marriage entry explicitly names the father "Joannes Uffelmann, Rathsverwandter" — a specific, verifiable individual. The baptism entry's "Asselmann" may simply be a harder-to-read version of the same name.

**Genealogical placement:** Joan Christian Kontze (b. May 4, 1757) is a **child of Arnold Philipp Kontze's SECOND marriage** (to Anna Dorothea, married July 1754). He joins:
- Joh. Henrich (eldest son, from FIRST wife, married 1773)
- Johannes (2nd son, b. ~July 1755, married 1780)
- Joan Christian (b. May 1757) — **NEW**
- [Possibly more children to be discovered]

The godfather **Joannes Wegner** is a new name in the Kontze network.
