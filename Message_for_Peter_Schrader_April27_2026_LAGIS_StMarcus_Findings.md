## 1. LAGIS Confirms TWO Hildebrand Emigrations from Deisel

The LAGIS database (HStAM Bestand 180 Hofgeismar Nr. 128 = Deisel, Bd. 1) documents two separate Hildebrand family emigrations from Deisel:

**Emigration A — January 1850, "Hildebrand (1)" family** (page 53 of the Deisel Bd. 1 register)
- Gottlieb Hildebrand, b. 1845, mit Familie
- Sophie Amalie Hildebrand, b. 1847, mit Familie
- Ludwig Theophil Hildebrand, b. 1849, mit Familie
- Destination: Nordamerika

These three children, plus father Ludwig Hildebrand (b. 27 Dec 1808 Hessen) and mother Catherine Ann Jorden (b. 27 Jul 1811 Hessen), settled in Rockford, Winnebago County, Illinois (per WikiTree profile of son Gottlieb, who lived 1844–1927 in Rockford). This is the family from KB 218836 1850 confirmation Entry 1 (Friedrich Hildebrand confirmed early "weil die Eltern nach Amerika auswandern wollten").

**Emigration B — October 1853, "Hillebrand; Hildebrand" family** (separate archive signature 18/4264)
- Wilhelmine Maria Hildebrand, b. 1822, mother of the family
- Maria, b. 1843
- Amalie, b. 1846
- Ludwig, b. 1849
- Georg Theodor, b. 1851
- Destination: Nordamerika

This is a SECOND, separate Deisel-Hildebrand emigration three years later. Wilhelmine Maria Hildebrand is too young (b. 1822) to be Justus's mother, but she could plausibly be a niece, cousin, or younger sister of Wilhelmine née Hildebrand (the Entry 195 mother).

**The "Hillebrand; Hildebrand" double-spelling in the LAGIS heading is significant** — it confirms what Entry 195 / Entry 261 showed, that this Deisel family used both spellings interchangeably. This is the same spelling pair as Justus's mother.

## 2. NO Köster Emigration from Deisel Matches Justus

LAGIS searches:
- "Justus Köster" → 0 results
- "Köster Deisel" → returns 50 result-pages but no Köster-from-Deisel emigrant matches our Justus's profile (one Christian Köster Deisel 1833 — too early; one Ludwig Koester Deisel 1866 b. 1848 — wrong birth year)
- "Konze Deisel" → 1 entry only: Ludwig Konze, October 1864 (not Justus)
- "Justus Konze" → 0 results

This is strong negative evidence. The Hessian state-archive index of 19th-century emigrants does NOT document Justus Köster (or Justus Konze) as ever leaving Deisel through any of the documented emigration permit channels.

## 3. St. Marcus St. Louis — NO Deisel Hildebrand Buried There

I searched Robert Buecher's transcribed indices of St. Marcus Evangelical Church in St. Louis (where Charles Gannsen / Carl Konzen married in 1866). All Hildebrandt entries:

- Anna née Hildebrandt (married Andreas Graf): b. 1807 **Osnabrück**, d. 1893
- Maria née Hildebrandt (married Johann Hesz): b. 1819 **Lübeck**, d. 1890
- Johann Hildebrandt: b. 1827 **Ranenburg, Baden**, d. 1887

None from Hessen, much less Deisel. So the Stephen Hildebrand on DeKalb Street (1860 St. Louis directory) coincidence I flagged in Session 75 is not strengthened by St. Marcus burial evidence.

**However — a separate big confirmation** from the same St. Marcus burial index: Charles Gannsen's two daughters who died young appear under the surname **KONZEN**:
- Josephine née Konzen (m. Eduard Herr), b. 22 Feb 1867, d. 5 May 1890
- Auguste née Konzen (m. James Somers), b. 8 Apr 1872, d. 31 Dec 1890

These match Charles's daughters Josephine Maria Johnson (1868–1890) and Auguste Johnson (1872–1890). This is yet another independent confirmation that Charles's German surname was **Konzen**, recorded that way in church records.
