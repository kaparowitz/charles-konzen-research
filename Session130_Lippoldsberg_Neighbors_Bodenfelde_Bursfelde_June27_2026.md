Lippoldsberg itself is already swept negative (Taufen reg. 219778 + Konfirmationen reg.

## 1. Klaus Kunze OSB online registers — the new neighbours (open web)

Kunze's surname registers map **surname → family number** in each printed OSB. Two of
Lippoldsberg's un-searched neighbours have Kunze books with online registers. Both pages are
**JavaScript-rendered** — `web_fetch` returns only a shell, so they were rendered in the
browser and the body text string-matched for the pool
(Gans/Ganß/Ganson/Gante · Konze/Kontze/Contze/Contzen/Conze/Jungkonz).

### Bursfelde + Glashütte  (`os/bursfelderegister.htm`)
*OSB Bursfelde und Glashütte 16.–20. Jh., Kunze, Uslar 2003 / 2.Aufl. 2010, ~1065 families.
The two settlements are administratively **Hann.-Münden, Ortsteil Hemeln** — east bank, Lkr.
Göttingen.*
- **Gans = families 110, 113, 125, 136** (4 families); near-variants **Ganz 136**, **Gaus 130**.
- **No Konze / Kontze / Contze / Contzen** anywhere in the register.
- ⇒ a real but modest native **Gans** population; no Konze-form surname.

### Bodenfelde  (`bf/register-osb-bodenfelde.htm`)
*Lkr. Northeim. The descriptive page `bf/bodenfelde.htm` still says the OSB is "in Arbeit,
nicht vor 2012" — **out of date**: the "vollständige Register" page is live and populated.*
- **Gans = 15 families: 13, 95, 118, 141, 155, 170, 171, 196, 197, 229, 256, 453, 456, 480, 538.**
  Plus **Gansberg 170, Ganske 171, Gante 171 & 196** (related/adjacent Ga-surnames).
- **Contze = family 178** — a genuine Konze-pool spelling (the only one; all other "Kon…" /
  "Con…" hits are occupations or unrelated surnames: Konditor, Konrektor, Konteradmiral,
  Kontoristin, Konath, Konnies, Conerding, Conrad, Constabel, Conductor…).
- ⇒ **Bodenfelde carries the densest Gans population of any new neighbour (15 families) AND a
  Contze (178)** — i.e. both halves of the pool are historically present on the left/west
  Weser bank parish directly across from the Lippoldsberg/Gottstreu cluster. This **corrects
  and upgrades** the earlier note (Session ~126/`bodenfelde-research-log`) that had Bodenfelde
  down as merely "Gans/Ganß, Konze = false" — the Gans presence is large and there **is** one
  Konze-pool surname (Contze 178).

> ⚠️ Rigor caveat (as always with Kunze indexes): a family number proves the surname appears
> in that family's book entry, not the dates. Family **178 (Contze)** and the 15 Gans families
> are listed separately (no shared number ⇒ not an obvious Gans=Contze variant link, unlike
> Helmarshausen 67/302). Dated family entries are book-only.

**Bodenfelde is NOT a Kunze book that was never published** — earlier worry retracted. The
register is online (JS-rendered); the *family pages* with dates still require the printed OSB
or the Archion Familienbuch / Kartei.

## 2. Bodenfelde on Archion — neighbour sweep (logged-in viewer)

Bodenfelde is on Archion under **Niedersachsen → Landeskirchliches Archiv Hannover →
Kirchenkreis Uslar → Bodenfelde** (17 books). Holdings of interest:
Kirchenbuch 1794–1839 (reg. **359334**, Taufen), Kirchenbuch 1841–1852, **Taufen Konfirmationen
1837–1852**, Taufen 1853–1883, **Konfirmationen 1848–1875**, **Familienbuch 1797–1899**,
**Kartei Männer 1705–1876 (reg. 359346)** + Kartei Frauen 1705–1875, **Namensregister 1815–1836
(reg. 359342)** + Namensregister 1853–1966, Konfirmationen/Trauungen/Beerdigungen 1830–1840.

### 2a. 1835 baptisms — re-verified NEGATIVE (corroborates the existing log)
`bodenfelde-research-log.html` already recorded the **1835 Taufen = negative** (no Gans child;
ubiquitous "Carl" forename on local surnames; near-miss Carl August **Braune** b. 29 Jan 1835).
This session re-read the 1835 year independently in reg. 359334 and **confirms it**:

- The 1835 baptisms I read sit at **images ~320–324** (header "1835"; e.g. img 324-left = July
  1835, Nr 26–29). NB this is **~14 images earlier than the existing log's "img 334–340"** —
  the combined volume almost certainly contains both an **Erstschrift and a Zweitschrift** of
  the 1830s baptisms (a normal Hannover practice), so 1835 appears **twice**; both copies read
  the same. (Worth reconciling the image map; it does not change the result.)
- **January 1835 in full:** births 8 Jan (Witte, Wasmuth, "Carl August Ferdinand **Gehringer**")
  → 22 Jan (Wilhelmine Charlotte **Husemann**, Nr 5) → **8 Feb** (Hanne Sophie Pauline **Brauns**,
  Nr 6). **No baptism falls on/near 26 Jan**, and the 22-Jan and 8-Feb children are female on
  local surnames. ⇒ **no child born 26 Jan 1835**, hence no Charles here.
- **No Gans / Contze / Konze surname** in the 1835 baptisms; surname pool is the recurring
  local Solling/Weser families (Witte, Wasmuth, Gehringer, Husemann, Brauns, Schubert, Böcker,
  Giesing, Streitwolf, Quast, Fleißner, Biermeier, Spieß, Schräder…). The forename **Carl** is
  pervasive but always local (Carl August Ferdinand Gehringer; Carl Gottlieb Streitwolf b. 24
  May; Carl August Biermeier; Carl Friedr. Ludw. Spieß b. 7 Jul, d. 5 Oct 1835).

### 2b. Kartei Männer 1705–1876 (reg. 359346) — alphabetical men's card index

## 2bis. Bursfelde + Glashütte on Archion — parish identified, 1835 checked

**Key structural finding: the parish that registered 1800s Bursfelde/Glashütte is
Fürstenhagen** (Niedersachsen → Landeskirche Hannovers → **Kirchenkreis Uslar →
Fürstenhagen**), *not* a stand-alone "Bursfelde" book. A place-search for "Bursfelde" returns
only one book — *Kirchenbuch 1639–1746*, filed under Fürstenhagen — but Fürstenhagen's later
registers carry an explicit **"Ort" (village) column**, and the **"Bursfelder Glashütte"**
settlement appears there directly (verified in the 1835 baptisms). So Fürstenhagen is the
correct 19th-c. parish for Bursfelde/Glashütte.

Relevant Fürstenhagen registers: **Kirchenbuch 1832–1852 (reg. 359377)** — combined, with the
Taufen tabulated and an Ort column; **Namensregister 1793–1911 (reg. 359381)** — alphabetical
index across Taufen/Konf/Ehestand/Begräbnis; plus KB 1639–1746, KB 1746–1831, Taufen
1853–1894, Beerdigungen 1853–1905, Trauungen 1853–1907.

**1835 baptisms read (reg. 359377, images 11–12; 1835 ≈ Bild 11–13):**
- **January 1835 = Offensen children only** (Nro 1 Dorothea Charlotte Elisabeth, b. 6 Jan; Nro 2
  Johanna Friederike Charlotte, b. 10 Jan) — **no Bursfelde/Glashütte child born ~26 Jan 1835.**
- The 1835 **"Bursfelder Glashütte"** baptisms (Nro 12–13, ~April 1835) are **non-Gans** — e.g.
  child **"Georg Ludwig"**, parents a Wittler/Müller-type surname; no Carl, no Gans.
- ⇒ **No Carl Gans born 26 Jan 1835 in Bursfelde/Glashütte** on the read evidence → Charles is
  not a documented Bursfelde 1835 Gans baptism. The four Kunze Gans families (110, 113, 125,
  136) are thus **historical OSB families**, not an 1835-cohort Carl-Gans baptism here.

**Caveats / not yet done for Bursfelde (full sweep):** only the 1835 Taufen window was read.
The **Namensregister 1793–1911 (reg. 359381)** G-section would list *every* Gans across 1793–
1911 with year columns (the efficient way to pin the four Gans families' dates and spot any
emigration gap), but it is written **forename-first in a faint hand** and the G/Gans line could
not be cleanly isolated this pass. The KB 1832–1852 Ort column should also be scanned across
all years for "Bursfelde/Glashütte" + Gans. Best route to the **dated** Gans-family records =
Kunze's printed *OSB Bursfelde und Glashütte* (fams 110/113/125/136). Wahmbeck not started.

## 2ter. Wahmbeck on Archion — effectively NEGATIVE

Wahmbeck (small Weser hamlet, Lkr. Northeim) is **its own parish** on Archion (Niedersachsen →
Landeskirche Hannovers → **Kirchenkreis Uslar → Wahmbeck**, 8 books; **no Namensregister**). It
has **no Kunze OSB** and was never flagged for the Konze/Gans pool. The 1835 baptisms are in
**Kirchenbuch 1790–1845 (reg. 359497)** — a combined volume, sectioned by rite, with a tabular
*Geburts-Register* that includes a clean **"Familien-Namen" column**.

Calibration: Taufen section ≈ 2 images/year; 1833 = Bild 93, 1835 = Bild 94–95, 1836 = Bild 96.
The volume is **bound out of order / has an Erst- + Zweitschrift** (Bild 94-right reads 1836
while 95-left reads 1835), so the exact **Jan–Aug 1835 leaf (Nro 1–21) could not be isolated**.

**Read:** 1835 Nro 22–29 (Sept–Dec) + much of 1834 and 1836. The **entire local surname pool**
came through the Familien-Namen column and it is tight and local — **no Gans, no Konze/Kontze
anywhere**: Arnstmeier, Rammenstein, Niemeyer, Friese, Elend, Förtner, Morgenstern, Grote,
König, Meister, Bremer, Brockhaus, Pape, Hesse, Grobe, Hauck, Krautmann, Schröder, Vömer,
Wagner. The only **"Carl"** rufnamen in the window are **Carl Heinrich Wilhelm Morgenstern**
(b. 7 Dec 1835) and **Carl … Brockhaus** (b. 25 Feb 1836) — both local surnames, neither 26 Jan
1835, neither Gans/Konze. ⇒ **Wahmbeck effectively NEGATIVE** for the pool and for Charles.
(Caveat: the displaced Jan–Aug 1835 leaf was not read line-by-line; the surname pool is decisive,
but a residual remains until that leaf is located — likely a Zweitschrift leaf elsewhere in the
264-image volume.)
