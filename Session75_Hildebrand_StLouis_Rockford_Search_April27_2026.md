## Part 1 — The 1850 Ludwig Hildebrand Family: Destination Identified

### 1A. WikiTree match — Gottlieb (Godfrey) Hildebrand

A WikiTree profile (Hildebrand-976) documents:

- **Name:** Gottlieb Hildebrand (Anglicized: Godfrey Hildebrand)
- **Born:** 17 March 1844, **Deisel, Trendelburg, Kassel, Hessen**
- **Died:** 5 August 1927, **Rockford, Winnebago County, Illinois**
- **Father:** Ludwig Hildebrand (Anglicized: Lewis Hildebrand)
- **Mother:** Catherine Ann (Jorden) Hildebrand
- **Siblings:** Henry, Frederick, Theodore, Laura (m. Brummer), Ludwig (Jr.), Charles William, John

This is unambiguously the same family flagged in Deisel KB 218836 (1850 confirmation class, Entry 1) — Friedrich Hildebrand, son of Ludwig Hildebrand × geb. Jordan, confirmed early "weil die Eltern nach Amerika auswandern wollten." The mother's name in the German register reads "Jordan"; the WikiTree spelling "Jorden" is a trivial Anglicization. The father's occupation Ackermann fits a man capable of organizing a multi-child trans-Atlantic move.

### 1B. Settlement in Rockford, Illinois — NOT St. Louis

The destination of the 1850 Hildebrand family was **Rockford, Winnebago County, Illinois** — about 90 miles northwest of Chicago, ~350 miles from St. Louis.

This is the single most important finding of Part 1: the 1850 Deisel Hildebrand emigration did NOT terminate in St. Louis. The chain-migration "uncle paved the way" hypothesis for Justus → St. Louis via Ludwig Hildebrand 1850 does not hold in its simplest form.

### 1C. Implications for the Justus / Charles Gannsen reconciliation

| Hypothesis | Status after Session 75 |
|------------|------------------------|
| Justus emigrated 1850 with uncle Ludwig Hildebrand directly to where Charles Gannsen later surfaced | **Weakened** — Ludwig went to Rockford IL, not St. Louis MO |
| Justus emigrated 1850 with the Hildebrands to Rockford, then moved to St. Louis before 1862 enlistment | **Open** — geographically possible (Illinois → Missouri); no documentation yet |
| Justus emigrated separately, ~1859, joining a different family or alone | **Open** — fits the 1900 census claim of immigration year 1859 |
| Justus was confirmed in another German parish (apprenticeship, relatives' village) and emigrated later | **Open** — not yet investigated |

The Rockford finding does NOT eliminate Justus → Charles Gannsen. It just removes one tidy route. Two paths still fit the evidence: a delayed move from Rockford to St. Louis between 1850 and 1862, or an entirely separate 1859 emigration with confirmation having taken place somewhere outside Deisel.

### 2C. The DeKalb Street coincidence

**Charles Gannsen's documented St. Louis addresses:**
- 1880 Census ("Charles Gonson"): 1534 Jackson Street
- 1890 Veterans Schedule: 2405 DeKalb (rear)
- 1895 St. Louis City Directory ("Charles Gannsen"): 2407 DeKalb, watchman

**Stephen Hildebrand (1860):** DeKalb Street, between L'esperance and Picotte streets, in the southern German-immigrant Soulard / "German First Ward" district.

This is a notable spatial coincidence: a Hildebrand was a DeKalb Street resident in 1860, and Charles Gannsen settled on DeKalb Street by 1890. DeKalb is a long street, and there is no evidence yet that Stephen lived in the same blocks Charles later occupied (block-level addresses 2400s vs. the 1860 cross-streets need direct directory consultation to map). But both addresses fall within the historically German Soulard / Frenchtown corridor of south St. Louis — the same neighborhood in which St. Marcus German Evangelical Church (where Charles married 1866 and baptized children) sits.

This coincidence is **suggestive, not conclusive**. Stephen Hildebrand's place of origin within Germany has not been established; he may be from any of dozens of Hessian, Westphalian, or other German villages that produced shoemaker emigrants to St. Louis.

### 2D. Other regional Hildebrands

**1880 US Federal Census, Millstadt/Centerville, St. Clair County, Illinois:**
- John Hildebrand, 41, coal-digger, born Hessen
- Millstadt is ~12 miles east of St. Louis (across the Mississippi River)
- Birth year ~1839, comparable to Justus's generation
- Hessian origin
- Surname Hildebrand
- Geographic proximity to St. Louis where Charles lived

**Status:** Worth investigating in detail. Could potentially be the John Hildebrand of the WikiTree Ludwig family (one of the Rockford siblings who moved away), or an unrelated Hessian Hildebrand. The age fit is suggestive but not diagnostic.

## Part 4 — LAGIS Hessian Auswanderer Database

The LAGIS (Landesgeschichtliches Informationssystem Hessen) "Hessische Auswanderer" module is the authoritative state-archive index of 18th- and 19th-century Hessian emigrants. It contains 223,000+ individual entries combining sources from Marburg, Darmstadt, and Wiesbaden state archives.

A search for "Schindehütte, Johann Christn., Deisel, 1846" returned a hit, confirming the database does index Deisel emigrants by surname AND village.

**Status:** Direct query of the LAGIS database via the `lagis-hessen.de` interface for Hildebrand × Deisel was not completed this session (web fetch blocked at the egress layer). The LAGIS interface allows search by name, place of origin, occupation, and destination — and is the single strongest source for confirming whether Justus Köster or any specific Hildebrand from Deisel has a documented emigration record. **This is the highest-priority follow-up.**

## Part 5 — Summary and Updated Hypotheses

### 5A. What we now know

1. **The Ludwig Hildebrand × Catherine Jordan family of Deisel emigrated 1850 to Rockford, Illinois** — confirmed via WikiTree profile of son Gottlieb (b. 1844 Deisel, d. 1927 Rockford). Children: Gottlieb, Henry, Frederick, Theodore, Laura, Ludwig Jr., Charles William, John.

2. **No documented Justus Hildebrand / Justus Konze surfaces in Rockford OR St. Louis** in publicly indexed records reached this session. (Ancestry's full passenger-list and census collections were not directly queried — paywall.)

3. **A Hildebrand presence in St. Louis is documented from at least 1860** (Stephen, Adolph, Franz, Joseph, Mary) including a Stephen Hildebrand on DeKalb Street — the same street where Charles Gannsen lived 30 years later. Origins of these St. Louis Hildebrands within Germany have NOT been established.

4. **A Hessian John Hildebrand surfaces in 1880 in Millstadt, IL** (12 miles east of St. Louis), age 41, coal-digger — generationally compatible with Justus's cousins.

### 5B. Updated answer to Jed's original question

**Jed asked:** "If he emigrated 1859 he should have been confirmed. Are there any Hildebrands in St. Louis from Deisel?"

**Answer:** The "from Deisel" qualifier is the key. We now have:

- **Documented Deisel Hildebrand emigration (1850):** YES — but they went to Rockford IL, not St. Louis.
- **Documented Hildebrand presence in St. Louis from the 1860s onward:** YES — at least five Hildebrand households in 1860 alone, including one on DeKalb Street where Charles later lived. But no proof yet that any of these St. Louis Hildebrands originated in Deisel.

The honest summary: **There is no confirmed Deisel-Hildebrand–St. Louis link in any record consulted to date.** The DeKalb Street coincidence is suggestive enough to merit dedicated follow-up, but not enough on its own to anchor a chain-migration hypothesis. The 1850 Rockford terminus of Ludwig Hildebrand's family complicates the simple "uncle drew Justus to St. Louis" theory.

### 5C. Reconciling the confirmation paradox

The Deisel confirmation absence (1849–1852, all classes searched negative) plus 1859 immigration year (per 1900 census) still requires Justus to have been somewhere OTHER than Deisel between roughly 1849 and 1859 — a 10-year window of unaccounted whereabouts. The candidates remain:

1. **Apprenticeship or relatives in another Hessian or neighboring parish** — confirmed there, then emigrated 1859. Most prosaic explanation. Would require searching parishes around Trendelburg/Hofgeismar/Cassel for an 1849–1851 confirmation of a Justus Köster.

2. **Emigrated 1850 with the Hildebrand uncle to Rockford, then relocated to St. Louis before 1862 enlistment.** The 1900 census "1859" could be self-reporting error. Investigating the Rockford Hildebrand household would either find Justus there in the 1850 or 1860 censuses or definitively rule it out.

3. **Died as a child between 1835 and 1849, somewhere other than Deisel** — would require a death record in another parish; would eliminate him as Charles Gannsen.
