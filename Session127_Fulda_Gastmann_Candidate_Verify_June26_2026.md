## 9. The chase — regiment + databases (follow-up, 26 June 2026)

**Regiment identified.** The baptism's "2. Infanterie-Regiment, 1. Compagnie" is the
Kurhessen line regiment **2. Infanterie-Regiment**, renamed **"2. Infanterie-Regiment
(Landgraf Wilhelm)"** in 1845; its personnel became Prussian IR Nr. 82 after the 1866
annexation. (de.wikipedia: *Zweites Infanterie-Regiment „Landgraf Wilhelm von Hessen"*.)
It was a line regiment whose battalions/companies rotated through the Kurhessen garrison
towns — **Kassel, Hanau, Marburg, Hersfeld, Ziegenhain, Fulda** — with the **1st Company
at Fulda in 1838**. It fought in 1848/49 (I. Btl. Frankfurt; II. Btl. Düppel/Jütland).
→ A Feldwebel's family moved with the unit; later records are scattered across whichever
garrison parishes it occupied, almost all un-indexed on Archion.

**The "Carl Gestmann" 1867 lead — examined (the one near-name hit):**
Carl Gestmann, male, age 30 (b. ~1837), **Farmer**, arrived **New York 18 May 1867**,
ship **Atalanta** (Le Havre & London), destination **Brooklyn**. Two records, same man:
FS Germans to America (ark KD3K-Y9Z) + Ancestry NY Passenger Lists (coll. 7488 rec.
6188660, NARA M237). Manifest **place of origin = "Hesia/Jessia" (= Hessen)** — region
only, **no town**. Manifest image (Atalanta, list **#397**) shows him travelling **alone**
in a block of unrelated Hessian passengers (Philipp, Seif, Papst, Gärtner…) — **no wife,
children, or Gastmann kin**.
- *For:* Gestmann ≈ Gastmann; ~1837 ≈ 1838; male; Hessen.
- *Against/unresolved:* generic "Hessen" (often Hesse-Darmstadt, not Kurhessen); occupation
  **Farmer** ill-fits a town sergeant's son; no parents/party/Fulda tie.
- **Verdict:** plausible but **unconfirmable**, and **irrelevant to Charles** (he was in
  the US by Dec 1862 / married St. Louis 1866, so a 1867 arrival can't be him). Held as a
  possible-but-unproven destination for the candidate; only the original manifest image
  (companions / clearer origin) could move it.

**Outcome:** no marriage or death for Karl Friedrich Gastmann (nor further Michael Gastmann
children) in the major indexes. The man is effectively untraceable except by reading the
EV garrison-parish registers (Hanau / Kassel / Marburg / Hersfeld) on Archion by hand —
low value since he's already excluded as Charles. Lead fully closed.
