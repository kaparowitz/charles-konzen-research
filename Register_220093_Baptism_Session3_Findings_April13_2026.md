## 1. CLUSTER A — Page 1, Image 709: Arnold Philipp Kontze's Baptism

### 1.1 Context

The baptism index listed "Arnold Philipp" on page 1. This initially raised skepticism because page 1 was estimated to date from ~1637–1640, making an Arnold Philipp born then impossibly old (~115) by his 1754 marriage. However, the register's title page shows **"ANNO 1708"** — the baptism section actually begins in 1708, not 1637. The 1637 date applies to a different section of the kirchenbuch.

### 1.2 Transcription

**Location:** Image 709, right page = manuscript page 1
**Year header:** "ANNO 1708" / "Baptisati"
**Month:** Martio (March)

**Entry (transcription from Kurrent script):**

> d[en] d. E. martij ist h[errn] Andreas Contzen Söhn[lein]
> ehelich[en] ehefrau in [der] Tauf[e] gehobt, comp[ater] K. Andr[eas]
> [U]ffelmann und hieß es bei[m Tauf] Arndt [Ph]ilipp

### 1.3 Translation

> On the [date] of March [1708], Herr Andreas Contze's little son
> [born of his] lawful wife, was lifted [held] at baptism. Godfather: K[arl/Kaspar?] Andreas
> Uffelmann, and [the child] was called Arnold Philipp at [his] baptism.

### 1.4 Critical Findings

**A. This is the baptism of Arnold Philipp Kontze himself — born March 1708.**

This resolves the page-1 puzzle. Arnold Philipp was NOT born in ~1637 — he was born in **March 1708**, making him ~46 years old at his second marriage in July 1754, and dying sometime after 1757 (his last known child). This age is completely consistent with all other records.

**B. Father confirmed: ANDREAS CONTZE**

Arnold Philipp's father is Andreas Contze — the SAME Andreas who also fathered Dionysius (Nies) Kontze (discovered on page 90, April 12). This means:

- **Arnold Philipp Kontze and Nies Kontze Senior were BROTHERS** — both sons of Andreas Contze.
- The Generation 0 patriarchs are not four separate unrelated families; at least two of them (Arnold Philipp and Nies) share the same father.

**C. Godfather: Andreas Uffelmann**

The godfather at Arnold Philipp's 1708 baptism was an **Andreas Uffelmann**. This is enormously significant because:

- Arnold Philipp later married **Anna Dorothea Uffelmann** (daughter of Rathsverwandter Joannes Uffelmann) in July 1754.
- The Kontze-Uffelmann connection was already established by **1708** — nearly 50 years before the marriage.
- The godfather Andreas Uffelmann may have been an uncle, family friend, or neighbor who cemented the inter-family bond.
- This explains WHY Arnold Philipp married into the Uffelmann family — the families had been close for generations.

**D. Mother's name NOT stated**

Unlike the Dionysius baptism on page 90 (which names "Anna Catharina née Kleina of Deisel"), this entry does NOT explicitly name the mother beyond "ehelichen ehefrau" (lawful wife). However, since the father is the same Andreas Contze, the mother is almost certainly **Anna Catharina née Kleina of Deisel** — the same woman named on page 90.

## 2. CLUSTER F — Page 39, Image 728: "Contzy" Variant and the Two Andreas Contzes

### 2.1 Context

The baptism index header line read **"Kontze: v. R. 39"** — meaning "vide Registro page 39" (see Register page 39). This cross-reference indicated that Kontze entries on page 39 might be indexed under a variant spelling.

### 2.2 Location and Date

**Image:** 728 (right page)
**Year:** "ANNO 1719" visible on left page; right page continues into 1719
**Page:** ~39 (right page of the spread)

### 2.3 Transcription

**Entry 1 (daughter's baptism):**

> d[en] 13. d. Junij ist **andreas Contzy jün[ioris]** Tochterlein...
> [in der Taufe] geb[...] **Andreas Contzy** [as godfather]

**Translation:** On the 13th of June [1719], Andreas Contze the Younger's little daughter... [was baptized]. [Godfather:] Andreas Contze [Senior].

**Entry 2 (son's baptism, same page):**

> **Contzy juvenis** andreas Contzy Sohn...

**Translation:** Contze the younger, Andreas Contze's son...

### 2.4 Critical Findings

**A. Surname variant "CONTZY" confirmed**

The scribe on page 39 consistently wrote the surname as **"Contzy"** rather than "Contze" or "Kontze." This is why the index included the cross-reference "v. R. 39" — the indexer recognized that these "Contzy" entries were the same family as "Kontze" and added a pointer.

**Variant spellings now documented across the register:**
- **Kontze** — index standard
- **Contze** — page 1 (1708)
- **Contzy** — page 39 (1719)
- **Konze** — burial/marriage records in other registers

**B. TWO Andreas Contzes active in 1719: Senior and Junior**

The entry explicitly distinguishes:
- **Andreas Contzy jünioris** (= the Younger) — father of the baptized child
- **Andreas Contzy** (= Senior, unnamed but serving as godfather)

This proves that by 1719 there were at least two generations of Andreas Contze in Trendelburg:
- **Andreas Contze SENIOR** — the grandfather figure, serving as godfather
- **Andreas Contze JUNIOR** — the father, already married with children by 1719

**C. Genealogical implications**

If Arnold Philipp (b. March 1708) was the son of "Andreas Contze" (page 1), and that Andreas is the SENIOR of the 1719 pair, then:

- Andreas Contze Senior was old enough to have a married son (Andreas Junior) producing children by 1719
- Andreas Contze Senior was also old enough to have fathered Arnold Philipp in 1708
- **Andreas Contze Junior may be Arnold Philipp's OLDER BROTHER** (born before 1708, hence already married by 1719 at age ~25+)
- OR Andreas Junior could be a cousin/nephew with the same name

**The "Andreas" on page 48 of the index (Cluster G, Tier 2) may be the baptism of Andreas Junior himself** — resolving whether he's a brother, son, or nephew of our line.

## 4. THE UFFELMANN CONNECTION — NOW TRACED TO 1708

The Kontze-Uffelmann inter-family bond is now documented across three events spanning 50 years:

| Date | Event | Uffelmann Involvement |
|------|-------|----------------------|
| **March 1708** | Arnold Philipp Kontze baptized | Godfather: **Andreas Uffelmann** |
| **July 1754** | Arnold Philipp marries Anna Dorothea Uffelmann | Bride is daughter of **Joannes Uffelmann**, Rathsverwandter |
| **May 1757** | Joan Christian Kontze baptized (Arnold Philipp's son) | Mother: Anna Dorothea née **Uffelmann** (written as "Asselmann" — likely scribal variant) |

This pattern suggests the Kontze and Uffelmann families were neighbors or close associates in Trendelburg from at least the early 1700s, with godparent relationships cementing the bond long before intermarriage.
