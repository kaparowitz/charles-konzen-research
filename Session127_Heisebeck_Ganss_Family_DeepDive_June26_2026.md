# Session 127 — Heisebeck Ganß family deep-dive (+ correction to Session 125)

**Date:** 26 June 2026
**Trigger:** follow-up to the Session 125 baptism-index sweep, which had flagged a lone
"Ganß" baptism in Heisebeck (Wilhelm Ganß, 1866) and judged the Gans surname out of the
1833–1841 window. Jed asked to dig into the Ganß family and trace its origin.

## Headline — TWO findings, one of them a correction

### 1. CORRECTION: Session 125 was wrong that Heisebeck Gans is "only from ~1866"
That conclusion came from reading **only the late tail** of the G index (Bild 1505,
Seiten 145–253). The Heisebeck Taufen index (reg. **219175**) is comprehensive, and the
**G section actually spans Bild 1503–1505**; the early Gans entries sit on **Bild 1504**,
which I had skipped. Calibration holds: **Seite ≈ Bild − 1237**, and **Bild 1283 = Seite 46
= Jahr 1839** (verified live), i.e. ~6.75 Seiten/yr, so the 1833–1841 window = Seiten ~5–60.

### 2. Heisebeck is a Gans STRONGHOLD, with male Gans baptisms IN Charles's window
- The **Ortssippenbuch Heisebeck-Arenborn** (Klaus Kunze) surname register lists **"Gans"
  against ~33 family numbers** (25, 27, 45, 54, 55, 66, 74, 75, 78, 83–85, 88, 94, 96,
  107, 109–111, 134, 135, 150, 151, 153, 154, 166, 195, 218, 225–227, 237, 238, 252) —
  a deeply-rooted, multi-century native Heisebeck surname, not a late arrival.
- In the 1830–1887 Taufen register, the in-window Gans baptisms are:
  | Child | Sex | Seite | ≈Year | Father |
  |---|---|---|---|---|
  | Christine Wilhelmine Gans | f | 41 | ~1838 | (same household) |
  | **Eduard Gottlieb Gans** | **m** | 46 | **1839 (bapt. 16 Jun)** | **Heinrich Wilhelm Gans, _Schneider_ (tailor) × Dorothea** |
  | Johann Friedrich Gans | m | 56 | ~1840–41 | (same household) |
  (just after the window: Joh. Fr. Wilhelm August Gans, S.75, ~1843; Caroline Wilhelmine
  Gans, S.76, ~1843.)
- At least **two distinct Gans households**: **Heinrich Wilhelm Gans, Schneider × Dorothea**
  (the 1838–41 children above), and **Tagelöhner Ernst Wilhelm Ganß** (whose daughter b.
  29 Mar 1845 bore the illegitimate, later-legitimised Wilhelm Ganß baptised 1866 — Seite
  165). The two later illegitimate entries (S.165 1866, S.196 ~1870s) belong to the Ernst
  Wilhelm line.

## What this does and doesn't mean for Charles
- **Does NOT directly identify Charles.** There is **no Gans son baptised ~26 Jan 1835** in
  Heisebeck: the first G-surname *male* in the register is Seite 28 (~1837, a Grebe), and the
  first *Gans* of either sex is Seite 41 (~1838). Seite 19 (= 1835) carries no Gans. So no
  Carl-Gans birth in 1835, and the Heisebeck Gans children all begin ~1838 — Heinrich Wilhelm
  Gans evidently married ~1837.
- **But it materially upgrades Heisebeck as a Gans-pool locus.** "Gans / Ganß" is an in-pool
  surname (n-s-n skeleton; "Ganson" is in the surname canon), and here it is a major native
  family in the **Diemel/Weser corridor** baptising sons during exactly Charles's era. A
  Charles born 1835 to this surname-pool would have to be (a) an older child baptised before
  the family's register run begins, (b) from another Gans household, or (c) from a Gans family
  in a neighbouring parish. The OSB (centuries deep) is the tool to map the whole Gans clan.

## Follow-ups worth doing
1. **Mine the OSB Heisebeck-Arenborn** for the Gans family pages (the online register gives
   only surname → family numbers; the printed/CD OSB has the per-family detail — births,
   marriages, who had a son ~1835, links between the Schneider and Tagelöhner lines).
2. **Heisebeck marriages** (Trauungen) ~1836–38 — Heinrich Wilhelm Gans (Schneider) and
   Ernst Wilhelm Ganß (Tagelöhner): names their fathers and confirms native-vs-incomer.
3. **Pre-1830 Heisebeck baptisms** (separate, possibly on-site volume) — would hold the
   parents' own generation, i.e. anyone Gans born ~1810–1815, and potentially a Gans son b.
   ~1835 if a household's run predates this register.
4. Cross-check the **wider Gans pool** flagged 26 Jun (Lippoldsberg "Ganson", Oedelsheim,
   Vernawahlshausen) against the Heisebeck Gans — same Weser-corridor cluster.
