Each has a different occupation and social status, and each is connected to a different wife's family. The question of whether these are brothers, father-son pairs, or cousins is now a primary genealogical puzzle.

## Move 1 — Entry 953 Recovery: Johann Friedrich Wilhelm Konze (Seite 243, Image 1623)

### Context

Session 20 reported that Image 1623 (Seite 243) showed entries 947–952 with non-Konze surnames (Nolde, Rode, Dönges, Fischer, Weihe), and the K-index entry "Johann Friedrich Wilhelm — 243" appeared to be an anomaly. Session 21 re-investigated with tighter zoom and found **Entry 953 at the very bottom of the page**, cut off in the earlier, less thorough view.

### Full transcription

| **Taufe** | Nottaufe (emergency house baptism), 28 June 1861 |
| **Geschlecht** | Männlich |

| **Taufpate (Godfather)** | Johannes Köhler, Ackerbürger |
| **Seite** | 243 |
| **Image** | 1623 |

### Significance

- **Karl Wilhelm Konze** is identified as an Einlieger (lodger) — the lowest civic status among the three Konze fathers. He did not own land or a house, but rented accommodation. This contrasts sharply with Carl Ludwig Konze's Ackerbürger status.
- **Marie geb. Lauterbach von DEISEL** — the mother is FROM DEISEL. This is the first direct documentary link between a Trendelburg Konze family and the village of Deisel, where Andreas Konze b.1795 raised his family. This raises the possibility that Karl Wilhelm Konze himself came from Deisel and married a Deisel woman, or that the Lauterbach family served as a bridge between the Deisel and Trendelburg communities.
- **Nottaufe** (emergency baptism) indicates the child was in danger of dying at birth. The baptism was performed in the house, not in church. The child's fate after the Nottaufe is not recorded in the visible marginalia.
- **Karl Wilhelm Konze (father, b. ~1831)** — could this be the same Carl Wilhelm Konze who was baptized in 1831 as the son of Carl Ludwig Konze (Session 20, Entry 25, Seite 7)? The name matches exactly, and the age fits: Carl Wilhelm b.1831 would be 30 in 1861, a plausible age for a father. If so, then the 1831 Carl Wilhelm (son of the Ackerbürger) had descended to Einlieger status by 1861, and married a Deisel woman.
- **Connection to the Deisel Konze family**: If Karl Wilhelm Konze (Einlieger, 1861) = Carl Wilhelm Konze (b.1831, son of Carl Ludwig), then Carl Ludwig Konze and Andreas Konze b.1795 are linked through the Lauterbach marriage AND through the Hofeditz godparent connection identified in Session 20.

## Move 2 — Karl Heinrich Konze Baptism (Seite 211, Image 1589)

### Navigation challenges

Finding Seite 211 required overcoming two obstacles:
1. **Interleaved index pages**: The alphabetical index in Register 220108 is NOT at the end of the register but is physically interleaved with the baptism register pages. Index tab pages (each covering two letters) are inserted between register pages throughout the book. Image 1590 (L/M index) sits between register pages at Images 1589 and 1591.
2. **Spread view confusion**: In spread view, the Archion viewer pairs adjacent images, sometimes combining a register page with an index page, creating misleading Seite attributions.

The correct mapping: **Image 1589 (single-page view) = register page with Seite 211** (right page header: "Monat November und December, Seite 211", Jahr 1854).

### Significance

- **Johann Christoph Konze, Schieferdecker** — a new Konze family head in Trendelburg. His occupation (slate roofer) marks him as an artisan/tradesman, distinct from Carl Ludwig Konze's farmer status and Karl Wilhelm Konze's lodger status.
- **Marie Elisabeth née Heine** — the Heine family connection is NEW. The godfather (Johann Christian Friedrich Heine, Strumpfwirker) shares the mother's maiden name, indicating he is likely her brother, father, or uncle. The Heine–Konze marriage places the Konze family within Trendelburg's artisan trades network.
- **Karl Heinrich Konze (b. 1854)** is too young to be Charles Gannsen (b. 1835) — a 19-year discrepancy eliminates him as a candidate. However, he could be a younger cousin or nephew of Charles.
- **Marginalia annotations** suggest Karl Heinrich Konze was confirmed around 1882 (age ~28, unusually late — or the date refers to something else) and has a record extending to 1892. No clear death date was visible, suggesting he may have survived into the 20th century.

### Hypothesized relationships

1. **Carl Ludwig → Karl Wilhelm?** If Karl Wilhelm Konze (Einlieger, 1861) is the same person as Carl Wilhelm Konze (b. 1831, son of Carl Ludwig), then the family shows downward social mobility: Ackerbürger father → Einlieger son. The Deisel marriage connection (wife Marie née Lauterbach von Deisel) could indicate Karl Wilhelm moved to Deisel temporarily and married there before returning to Trendelburg.

2. **Johann Christoph Konze's origin**: His relationship to Carl Ludwig is unknown. Possible scenarios: brother, cousin, or son from an unrecorded marriage. His Schieferdecker trade suggests he was trained as an artisan, possibly outside Trendelburg.

3. **Connection to Andreas Konze b.1795 (Deisel)**: The Hofeditz godparent link (Session 20) connects Carl Ludwig Konze to the Deisel Konze family. The Lauterbach-von-Deisel marriage connects Karl Wilhelm Konze to Deisel. Johann Christoph Konze has no known Deisel connection yet.
