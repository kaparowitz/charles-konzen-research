## Notable Observations

### 1. House Number Clusters
The Konze family occupied specific houses in Deisel:
- **No. 136/136½**: Ludwig Konze (d. 1896) and Philipp Konze (d. 1897) — adjacent houses suggesting extended family compound
- **No. 102/104**: August Wilhelm Konze family
- **No. 119**: Philipp Ludwig Konze family
- **No. 16**: Heinrich Wilhelm Ludwig Konze (Gastwirth)
- **No. 43**: Friedrich Wilhelm Konze (widow)

### 2. The Gastwirth Connection
Entry 460 reveals the first non-agricultural Konze patriarch: Heinrich Wilhelm Ludwig Konze was a **Gastwirth** (innkeeper). All other known Konze patriarchs were Ackermann (farmer), Taglöhner (day laborer), Ziegenfuhr (goatherd), or Büttner (cooper). An innkeeper suggests a different economic niche and possibly higher social standing.

### 3. October 2, 1897 — Double Death
Entries 528 and 529 both died on October 2, 1897: a 5-year-old child (Heinrich Ludwig Konze, son of Philipp Ludwig Konze) and a 56-year-old widow (Wilhelmine Konze née Thiele, widow of Philipp Konze). Despite different households (119 vs 136½), the same death date suggests either coincidence or a shared cause (epidemic, accident).

### 4. Konze-Köster Intermarriage Intensifies
Multiple Köster entries appear alongside Konze entries throughout the register. The Konze and Köster families were deeply intertwined:
- Heinrich Wilhelm Ludwig Konze married Maria Amelie née Köster
- Maria née Konze married Heinrich Ludwig Köster (Kirchenältester)
- Multiple other Köster-Konze cross-references
